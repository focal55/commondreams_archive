Bush Energy Budget Guts Renewables Funding
Responsibility for balanced energy budget falls on Congress
 
WASHINGTON - April 9 - The Union of Concerned Scientists today rebuked President Bush for his FY 2002 Energy Department budget, which slashes core funding for renewable energy research and development programs by nearly 50 percent -- from $376 million to $186 million.

"The president has missed a key opportunity to live up to his campaign commitment to promote renewable energy," said Ron Sundergill, Washington Representative for the Clean Energy Program at the Union of Concerned Scientists. "Building new power plants and drilling for more oil will not solve the nation's energy problems."

DOE's clean energy programs are vital to the growth and commercialization of renewable energy technologies - solar, wind, geothermal, and biomass -- that help protect our environment, enhance reliability, create new jobs and bolster national security. By helping to reduce US dependence on electricity generated from dirty fossil fuels, the renewable energy programs are a major component of the federal government's efforts to reduce the harmful emissions that cause smog, soot, acid rain, mercury contamination and global warming.

"The administration is turning away from a balanced approach to the nation's energy policy in favor of a risky strategy that prolongs our dependence on fossil fuels," said Sundergill. "We urge Congress to pursue a responsible and balanced national energy policy that protects consumers, the environment, and the economy by increasing funding for clean energy technologies."
