Details:
In the wake of the Oklahoma City bombing, several bills were signed into law that eroded the civil liberties of American citizens. According to the ACLU, the most dangerous of these laws were the Anti-Terrorism and Effective Death Penalty Act and the Illegal Immigration Reform and Immigrant Responsibility Act, which stripped the Federal judiciary of its traditional authority to enforce the Constitution.

Now, in the aftermath of the terrorist attacks of Sept. 11, the country faces a similar situation. Legislation is moving swiftly through Congress that is aimed at terrorists, but in reality will diminish citizens' constitutional rights and harm innocent citizens and immigrants alike.

The American Civil Liberties Union will conduct a panel discussion to examine this trend, discuss the threats to civil liberties that emerge from terrorism and release a new report examining the consequences of the 1996 anti-terrorism legislation.

More details will be available in the coming weeks.
