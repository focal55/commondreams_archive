PSR Urges Senators To Condemn Bush's ABM Withdrawal Threat
Killing Treaty Endangers Americans, Raises Threat of Arms Race
 

WASHINGTON - December 12 - Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR) condemns unequivocally the move by President Bush to unilaterally withdraw from the 1972 Anti-Ballistic Missile (ABM) Treaty. On behalf of more than 20,000 doctors, nurses, public health professionals and others, PSR is urging all Senators and responsible political leaders to show their support for this vital Treaty. The ABM Treaty has been key to the prevention of nuclear war, and reductions in nuclear arms for 30 years.

Today, PSR presents 20,000 signed letters from PSR members and supporters to the Senate and to President Bush opposing withdrawal from the ABM Treaty. The letters also call on the President not to destroy a treaty that has helped keep us all secure, and not to deploy an unproven system at a cost of hundreds of billions of dollars we haven't got in our deficit and recession-plagued economy.

"A unilateral move to abandon this key Treaty will endanger Americans and reduce American security," said Robert K. Musil, Ph.D., MPH, executive director and CEO of Physicians for Social Responsibility. "This maneuver will destabilize relations with Russia at a time when we most need their help in the fight to prevent Russian nuclear materials or weapons falling into the hands of terrorists. This is a real threat the President should be working with Russia to eliminate."

PSR is also faxing Senators urging them to speak out on the risk the President is taking with our national security. "This move threatens all the arms reductions achieved to date. Russia has said with U.S. National Missile Defense in place, it will aim new, more sophisticated weapons at the United States, even as the Russian early warning system fails. Such a development makes an accidental nuclear launch a more likely and more deadly threat. Deployment of NMD will likely spark a Chinese arms buildup, in numbers of warheads and in sophistication of delivery methods," said Martin Butcher, director of security programs for PSR. "This could cause a further arms race between India and Pakistan. The President is endangering America, making nuclear war more likely here or in regional conflicts."

"The increased nuclear threat that will result from the President's own policies will be used to justify the deployment of NMD," said Butcher. "The President is raising the threat of nuclear proliferation, and thus of a catastrophic nuclear conflict and then saying the world is a dangerous place so we need missile defense. This is truly politics through the looking glass."

In its appeal to Senators, PSR says: "President Bush is moving ahead with a highly questionable and controversial policy at a time when he should be building unity in a nation at war. As a Senator, you have a duty to your constituents and to the nation to hold the President to account. Please speak out for the preservation not just of the ABM treaty, but of all arms control and non-proliferation measures. Oppose the President's plan -- tell him to stop and think before it is too late."

Robert K. Musil, Ph.D., MPH, executive director and CEO of PSR is available for interview.
