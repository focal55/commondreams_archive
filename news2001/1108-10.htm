House Energy Legislation Forces Taxpayers to Pay for Pollution
 
WASHINGTON - November 8 - Taxpayers would foot the bill for $38 billion in tax breaks and other giveaways to big oil, electric utilities and nuclear power under the House energy legislation passed in early August, according to a new report released today by the U.S. Public Interest Research Group (U.S. PIRG).

"These giveaways to polluting industries would not only waste taxpayer dollars but also would do virtually nothing to provide for America's energy future," said Anna Aurilio, Legislative Director of U.S. PIRG. "Increasing energy efficiency and producing more energy from clean, renewable sources will help reduce the vulnerability of our energy supplies, create jobs, save consumers money and protect the environment," added Aurilio.

U.S. PIRG's report-- Polluter Payday-- found that these handouts could have profound implications for American taxpayers. U.S. PIRG estimated that the tax breaks and subsidies for polluters would cost taxpayers $38 billion-only slightly less than the $38.9 billion in tax rebates mailed out to taxpayers in 2001. In fact, in 15 states-including Florida, New Mexico, and Texas-the cost of these handouts to polluters would exceed the total amount rebated to the states' taxpayers.

U.S.PIRG identified more than 100 corporations in polluting industries that would benefit from the House energy plan's handouts. U.S. PIRG's analysis shows that these corporations recorded revenues in excess of $1.6 trillion in 2000.

The group was joined at the press conference by Cena Swisher, Senior Director of the Green Scissors campaign for Taxpayers for Common Sense, a fiscal conservative group, and Lynnea Smith, a member of the Concerned Citizens of T'iistsoozi-Crownpoint, NM, representing ENDAUM (Eastern Navajo Diné Against Uranium Mining) and SADD (Students Against Destructive Decisions.) Smith spoke of her group's concerns about H.R.4's $30 million in subsidies for uranium mining, saying, "The bill will support companies such as Uranium Resources, Inc., whose operations will contaminate our precious water and will severely harm my Navajo people."

U.S. PIRG also strongly denounced the recent efforts by some Senators to attach dirty energy policies to federal anti-terrorism legislation in the name of national security.

"Those pushing for a plan to address energy and national security are partially correct," said U.S. PIRG Research Director Alison Cassady, the author of the report. "America needs a long-term national energy policy. But the House energy legislation will only deepen our dependence on oil and other polluting sources. It's just not the answer," continued Cassady.

U.S. PIRG called on the Senate to reject the fundamentally flawed House energy legislation and support proactive policies for cleaner, more affordable energy solutions that benefit consumers rather than polluters.

"The House energy plan is simply out of balance with America's current priorities. The vast majority of the bill supports dirty energy sources, while devoting barely a quarter of its subsidies and tax breaks to energy efficiency and renewable energy," added Aurilio.

U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for the State Public Interest Research Groups. State PIRGs are non-profit, non-partisan public interest advocacy groups.
