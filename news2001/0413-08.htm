Week of Actions from April 16-22, 2001, Across U.S. to Protest NAFTA Expansion Throughout the Hemisphere
Release of State NAFTA Job Loss Figures to Coincide with Local Activities
 
WASHINGTON - April 13 - Seven years after the implementation of the North American Free Trade Agreement (NAFTA), business and political leaders will promote NAFTA expansion and the creation of a Free Trade Area of the Americas (FTAA) at a summit to take place in Quebec City during April 20 and 21. FTAA is an expansion of the NAFTA model to all countries in the Western Hemisphere except Cuba. Unions, community organizations, environmentalists, students and faith based activists are organizing a week of actions in the U.S. April 16-22 in local communities to fight the negotiation of FTAA.

"NAFTA has been a disaster for working people in the U.S., Mexico and Canada. It's put corporate greed before workers' rights and environmental health, " said Fred Azcarate, Executive Director of Jobs with Justice. "FTAA would make a bad situation worse for people throughout the hemisphere."

More than 50 actions will take place across the country during this week (see highlighted local actions attached). Local activities will range from rallies at plants where workers have faced NAFTA layoffs, to campus based teach-ins and local educational forums. All activities will make connections between the FTAA and local impacts and are in solidarity with actions being organized throughout the Western Hemisphere.

At local actions, new estimates of the devastating U.S. job losses caused by NAFTA will be released on a state-by-state basis. According to a recent study by the Economic Policy Institute, NAFTA eliminated 766,000 job opportunities between 1994 and 2000 with job losses in every state.

"The people of the Americas, North and South, are demanding a new model of globalization, one that benefits working people, the environment, family farmers, and public health and safety, not just corporate and financial interests," said Kelly Ross, Director of the Citizens Trade Campaign. "Expanding the old, failed NAFTA model to the entire hemisphere takes us backwards 180 degrees in the wrong direction."

Jobs with Justice is a national community, labor, student, faith-based, environmental and family farm coalition coordinating U.S. actions. Also assisting in coordinating local activity are the Citizens Trade Campaign and the American Federation of Labor-Congress of Industrial Organizations, United Steel Workers of America, Applied Research Center, Center for Third World Organizing, Communications Workers of America, 50 Years is Enough Network, International Brotherhood of Teamsters, Longshore and Warehouse Union International, Pride at Work, Public Citizen's Global Trade Watch, Service Employees International Union, United States Student Association and US Action.

April 16-22 Week of Actions Against the Free Trade Area of the Americas
Highlighted Actions
For more information contact Jobs with Justice: 202-434-1106, info@jwj.org,

Chicago, IL:
On April 21, there will be a rally and march focusing on labor, immigrant, & environmental rights. The rally will begin at Hendricson Spring Plant where 250 workers are being laid off - 20% of jobs will move to Canada, 80% to Mexico. Leo Gerard, USWA President, will lead the event. The march will be a funeral procession featuring a mock "Race to the Bottom", ending with a rally in McKinley Park. Contact: Sarita Gupta, Chicago Jobs with Justice: (312) 787-5868 x137, chicagojwj@mindspring.com

Cleveland, OH:
Cleveland Jobs with Justice is planning three events for the FTAA week of action. On April 16, they will organize a bus tour of Cleveland with Representative Sherod Brown to highlight job loss and current working conditions in the city. On April 19, there will be a rally in downtown Cleveland. On April 20, National Offices of the United Church of Christ will hold a solidarity
prayer service in support of the actions in Quebec. On April 21st, there will be an outdoor event in solidarity with the activities in Quebec. Contact: Steve Cagan, Cleveland JwJ: (440) 333-6363, clevelandjwj@igc.org

Boston, MA:
At a press conference on April 19th, Boston JwJ will release state numbers on job loss due to NAFTA. On April 20 ten buses full of labor and community activists will leave Boston for Quebec. On April 21, there will be a rally at Harvard University in solidarity with activities in Quebec. Contact: Cassie Watters, MA JwJ: (617) 524-8778, cassiefw@aol.com

Portland, OR:
A rally will take place on April 21 at noon at Pioneer Courthouse Square with street theatre and giant puppets. After the rally, activists will march to Senators Smith and Wyden's offices to deliver a message that Portland doesn't want the FTAA. Contact: Margaret Butler, Portland JwJ: (503) 236-5573, jobswj@teleport.com

Buffalo, NY:
The Coalition for Economic Justice/JwJ and the Buffalo Activist Network are organizing a "Globalize Liberation" rally and march on April 22 at Front Park, near the historic transnational Peace Bridge. The rally will begin at 11am and will feature local labor and environmental speakers from the US and Canada. At 3pm, the group will march to downtown Buffalo, where hundreds of jobs were lost as a result of NAFTA. Contact: Maria Whyte, CEJ/JwJ: (716)892-5877
New Haven, CT:
On April 20, a coalition of students and local unions will demonstrate in support of Yale workers, demanding that the University agree to card-check and neutrality during organizing drives and against the FTAA. The rally will turn into a march in solidarity with workers across the hemisphere, protesting the FTAA. A bus will leave for Quebec after the march. The event will coincide with a Yale fundraiser to be attended by their wealthiest 2000 graduates, including George W. Bush.
Contact: Justin Ruben, Mobilization to Stop the FTAA: (203) 777-3327, justin.ruben@yale.edu

Washington, DC:
Hemispheric trade union leaders from the Union Network International as well as the AFL-CIO will participate in a rally on April 20 at 5pm at the US Chamber of Commerce in solidarity with the activities in Quebec. Contact: Treston Faulkner, Jobs with Justice:

Salem, OR:
On April 16, there will be a rally to seek City Council approval of an anti-FTAA resolution, followed by a rally on April 23 demanding an ordinance supporting "corporate accountability". Contact: Jim Cook, Mid-Willamette Valley JwJ: (503) 581-1505, cookj@opeuseiu.org

Seattle, WA:
Thousands of union members and community members from Canada and Washington State will gather on April 21 at Peace Arch Park in Bellingham for a peaceful rally on the US - Canada border to bring attention to the FTAA.
