Fathers Applaud Initiative to Help Kids Fight Tobacco
 

DULUTH, MINN - January 23 - Each day, more than 1,000 girls become regular smokers. Roughly one third will die prematurely because of smoking. But tobacco companies still spend billions on ads in magazines read by teens and pre-teens in violation of the agreement settling states' lawsuits against the industry.

The national advocacy group Dads and Daughters (DADs) uses the Internet and grassroots activism to pressure marketers who undermine girls to sell their products, and also to spotlight and strengthen positive messages. Now, fathers across the country are sending messages of support to the SmokeFree Action Network for its groundbreaking work educating young people about the danger of nicotine addiction. In a letter to SAN president Joseph Cherner, DADs wrote:

"As a group of fathers and daughters, we take very seriously the ways in which marketers approach our children. It is particularly disturbing to see how widespread and 'normalized' is the notion that younger and younger girls should be concerned about their weight or looking a particular way to attract attention from boys. More and more, this phenomenon is becoming "normative" for our sons, too.

"Tobacco advertising has long pushed cigarettes as a path to being 'cool,' and no age group is more susceptible to this dangerous message as the highly pursued youth demographic. These are not strangers or statistics. They are our daughters -- and sons. And too often, big tobacco is targeting these potential new customers when they're still just children."

The SmokeFree Action Network has received national attention for innovative counter-advertising created by New York City schoolchildren and placed on taxis and in subway cars. Their Web site (www.smokefreeair.org) facilitates letter writing between smoke-free advocates and key decision-makers through "e-z letters" faxed to lawmakers and others. DADs encourages all parents to use the SAN Web site and voice their support for SAN. E-mail Joe@smokefree.org; write P.O. Box 3316, New York, NY 10008; fax 212-488-8911 or call 212-912-0960.

Dads and Daughters is the national non-profit membership group for fathers and daughters. DADs helps strengthen father-daughter relationships and transform the pervasive messages that value girls more for how they look than who they are. Most recently, DADs convinced Campbell's Soup to pull a TV commercial that marketed Campbell's Soup to young children as a weight control food. For more, visit www.dadsanddaughters.org
