PIRG Urges Senate to Put Patients Before Profits and Pass a Strong Patients' Bill of Rights
Statement of Rachel Weintraub, U.S. PIRG Staff Attorney

 
WASHINGTON - June 21 - The Senate debate on the Patients' Bill of Rights must focus on strong patient protections and enforceable remedies. Without remedies that hold HMOs accountable, patients will not get the quality health care they need. U.S. PIRG urges the Senate to put patients before profits and pass a strong bill that will truly protect all patients from HMO abuses.

Only the Bipartisan Patient Protection Act of 2001 introduced by Senators McCain (AZ), Kennedy (MA), and Edwards (NC) truly provides patients with strong rights and enforceable remedies. This legislation would:

· Include all patients who receive their health care through an HMO, without gaping loopholes that would leave millions of Americans without protections.

· Protect strong state laws by creating a federal floor for state laws, which allows for more meaningful state protections.

· Ensure that patients can hold their HMO accountable when an HMO decision to deny or delay care results in injury or death.

· Ensure that patients have speedy access to a fair and truly independent external review board.

· Ensure that medical professionals rather than HMO bureaucrats make independent external review board decisions.

No other legislation before Congress includes these necessary principles.

The Senate must remember that, above all else, the Patients' Bill of Rights is about patient care. Sick patients don't want to go to court but holding HMOs accountable is the only way to ensure that HMOs put patient care before profit margins. American patients are calling for strong protections and have waited far too long for Congress to act. The Senate now has the opportunity to choose patient care over HMO profits. We urge them to make the right choice.
