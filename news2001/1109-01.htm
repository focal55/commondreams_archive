FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
NOVEMBER 9, 2001
4:54 AM
 CONTACT:  Mobilization for Global Justice
 David Levy, 202-669-5929; Adam Eidinger, 202-744-2671
March and "Shout Out!" Protesting the WTO
Spectacular 70 Foot Smoke-Breathing Dragon Puppet Premiere
 

WASHINGTON - November 9 -

WHO: Mobilization for Global Justice, Wash., DC. Confirmed speakers to include: Patricia Campos, UNITE! trade union; Robert Weissman, Essential Action; David Wasko, Friends of the Earth; Margrete Strand, Global Trade Watch; Jeremy Woodrum, Free Burma Coalition; and others.

WHAT: March and rally to protest the undemocratic and unaccountable World Trade Organization and the role of the U.S. Trade Representative in furthering its aims. The march will feature a GIGANTIC, COLORFUL 70 FT. LONG DRAGON representing the power of thepeople to resist corporate globalization and advocate for global justice.

WHEN: TODAY Friday, November 9, 2001, from 11:00 am until approximately 2:30 pm

WHERE:
MARCH, 11:00 am, from Edward R. Murrow Park (across from World Bank HQ), Pennsylvania Ave. & 19th St., NW, Washington DC

MARCH, 11:00 am till 12:30 pm, through the streets of DC to the State Department in Foggy Bottom through the campus of George Washington University to the doorstep of the US Trade Representative at 17th and Penn. (Route subject to change.)

"SHOUT OUT!" 12:30 pm till 1:30 pm, at the U.S. Trade Representative's Office, 17th and Pennsylvania (one block from the White House)
MARCH, 1:30 pm, return to Edward R. Murrow Park (route TBA)

WHY:
PEOPLE BEFORE PROFIT
FAIR TRADE NOT FREE TRADE
PROTECT LABOR RIGHTS, HUMAN RIGHTS, THE ENVIRONMENT & PUBLIC HEALTH

On November 9 through 13th, the World Trade Organization will be meeting in the country of Qatar to open a new round of agreements aimed ostensibly at reducing trade barriers between countries. The U.S. Trade Representative, Robert Zoellick, is the prime advocate for a form of free trade that will benefit only the power elite and the corporate lobby that plies the hallways of the WTO and Congress every day. It's a lopsided battle at the bargaining table that impoverished countries will lose. And, not surprisingly, human rights activists, labor unionists and environmentalists will also lose because civil society (unlike the multinational corporations) is not even at the bargaining table. If these talks are successful, the rich will get richer, the poor will get poorer and sicker, and the environment will be raped and pillaged at an even faster rate. Two years after the surging crowds of demonstrators against the WTO in Seattle, this meeting will take place in Qatar, a country that does not permit public dissent. Now, not only will all decisions be made behind closed doors, but there will be no crowds banging on those doors to be let in.
