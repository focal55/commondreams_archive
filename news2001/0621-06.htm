America Supports Contraceptive Equity For Women, Bush Does Not
NARAL Poll Shows Support For Contraceptive Insurance Coverage
 
WASHINGTON - June 21 - -- President Bush has moved to block insurance coverage for contraceptives for federal employees even as a new National Abortion and Reproductive Rights Action League (NARAL) poll released today shows Americans overwhelmingly support access to contraceptives and legislation requiring insurance companies to cover them.

"On this issue, President Bush is in the minority of even his own party. He is supported by just thirteen percent of Americans. His position is outside the mainstream of American values," said NARAL President Kate Michelman.

NARAL's poll reveals that:

-- 87 percent of Americans support access to birth control, with 69 percent saying they "strongly" support access.

-- 77 percent said they support laws requiring insurance companies to cover prescription contraceptives.

Despite these numbers, President Bush's FY02 budget proposes deleting an existing provision requiring Federal Employee Health Benefits (FEHB) plans to cover prescription contraceptives if they cover other prescription drugs and devices. The Office of Personnel Management has stated that the contraceptive coverage requirement, originally passed in 1998, has not imposed any additional costs on the FEHB program.

While most health plans cover prescription drugs, many exclude contraceptives. Forty-nine percent (49 percent) of all typical large group plans do not cover any contraceptive methods and only 15 percent cover all five of the most commonly used reversible prescription methods. This inequity leads to higher out-of-pocket health care costs for women. Indeed, women pay 68 percent more for their health care than men, with reproductive health services accounting for much of the difference.

"The American public wants this inequity addressed. They want the health care system to work in ways that help women prevent unintended pregnancy and thus reduce the need for abortion," continued Michelman.

NARAL has launched an aggressive nationwide campaign to address contraceptive insurance inequities:

-- At the federal level, NARAL will work with members of Congress to secure contraceptive insurance coverage for federal employees and pass EPICC, the Equity in Prescription Contraceptive Coverage Act.

-- Eleven NARAL affiliates are involved in contraceptive equity legislative campaigns at the state level (Mass., Mo., Mont., N.M., N.Y., Ore., Pa., Texas, Utah, Wash., Wis.). Republican Texas Governor Rick Perry recently signed that state's bill and Missouri's bill awaits Democratic Governor Bob Holden's signature. Nine other states are also considering contraceptive equity legislation (Ark., Ariz., Fla., Ill., Ind., La., Neb., Okla., S.C.).

-- NARAL will send out over 600 contraceptive equity action kits to legislators in states that have not passed legislation. In addition, NARAL has sent out an alert to over 100,000 Choice Action Network members asking them to contact their members of Congress, state legislators, and their own employers to ask them to back contraceptive equity.

"Americans overwhelmingly support access to contraception. They want to see that insurance companies treat women fairly by providing coverage for contraceptives. The President and anti-choice lawmakers do not. Their anti-choice, anti-contraception position is extreme and out of step with the American public," added Michelman.
