The Empire Strikes Again
Administration Sets November Deadline for Russia to Agree to ABM Treaty Changes
 
WASHINGTON - August 22 - In what has become nearly a weekly experience, the Bush Administration has again ignored international concerns and acted unilaterally on a foreign policy issue. This time, Under Secretary of State for Arms Control, John Bolton, has told the Russians that they have until November to agree to radical changes to the Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty (ABM) or else the U.S. would give its six month notice for pulling out of the treaty. Such a statement, much less unilaterally pulling out of the ABM Treaty, is dangerous to U.S. and world security.

"Bolton's statement reiterates the fact that the ABM Treaty is the Administration's boogeyman, and they are hell-bent on killing it, regardless of the circumstances," stated Chris Madison, the Council for a Livable World Education Fund's National Missile Defense Campaign Director.

What makes Bolton's statement so absurd is that there is no need to rush the demise of the ABM Treaty. Anti-missile technology has not shown itself to work, so there is no system ready to deploy. Moreover, there is so much testing of anti-missile technology left to do that it could be years before the Pentagon really needed to conduct tests that would violate the ABM Treaty.

Philip Coyle, the Pentagon's previous Director of Operational Test and Evaluation, has stated that "the 1972 treaty is not holding back design and development of the technology needed for National Missile Defense (NMD), nor is the treaty slowing the testing of an NMD system. Development of NMD will take a decade or more for technical and budgetary reasons, but not due to impediments caused by the ABM treaty...Until the U.S. government learns whether the technical, budgetary, and operational problems that National Missile Defense presents can be solved, the ABM treaty is the least of President Bush's problems."

It is becoming increasingly apparent that the Bush Administration's goal is the total destruction of all arms control agreements, and they mean to achieve that by dismantling the ABM Treaty, whether or not Russia, China, or our NATO allies like it. However, ultimatums rarely work in diplomatic circles, so, as every other country in the world wants to see the ABM Treaty intact, the diplomatic and strategic fallout could be disastrous.

"The Administration claims they are ‘consulting' with Russia regarding a new strategic framework. But giving the Russians the options of either modifying the ABM Treaty in a way that virtually eviscerates it, or having the U.S. unilaterally abrogate the treaty is no option at all. These are consultations in name only," claimed Luke Warren, the Council's head analyst and media director.
