Statement of Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger on Senator Cochran's Support for Campaign Finance Reform
 
WASHINGTON - January 4 - Today's announcement by Senator Thad Cochran that he will support the bipartisan McCain-Feingold campaign finance reform bill is a significant development and a profound sign that we have turned a corner in the fight for reform. I suspect it will not be the first time we hear from a Senator who wants to join the fight to shut down the soft money system once and for all.

On behalf of the members of Common Cause and reform supporters everywhere, I thank Senator Cochran for lending his support for this effort. Everyone knows that campaign finance reform has powerful opponents, and it takes a measure of courage to stand up in defiance of the Washington culture of money, access, and influence.

As we get closer and closer to gaining the 60 votes necessary to defeat a filibuster of campaign finance reform, reform opponents in the Senate will look to other tactics to try and derail the bill. Unfortunately for them, the public is paying more attention to this issue than ever before, and will see through any attempts to kill the bill by larding it up with poison pills or watering it down with amendments designed to weaken the soft money ban.

And, Senator McCain is right -- the Senate must move on this vital legislation immediately after the inauguration of the new president, and before the Bush legislative agenda is ready to go.

This legislation has the support of the overwhelming majority of the House of Representatives, and a growing bipartisan majority in the United States Senate sufficient to defeat a filibuster. Most important, this bill has the overwhelming support of the American people who desperately want their federal officials to be less dependent on special-interest money, and more accountable to the people they serve.
