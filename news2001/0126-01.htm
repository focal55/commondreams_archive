European Food-Makers Dump Genetically Engineered Animal Feed
U.S. Farm Exports Imperiled by Spreading Rejection of Gene-Altered Animal Feed
 
WASHINGTON - January 26 - Tesco and Asda/Wal-Mart, two of the top food retailers in Great Britain, today announced that all their own-brand meat and dairy products will be produced from animals fed with non-genetically engineered (GE) feed. This announcement underscores the total rejection of GE crops throughout Europe’s food industry. Over 80 percent of U.S. soybean and corn exports to Europe go into animal feed.

"American farmers should know the market realities before they grow genetically engineered crops this season," said Charles Margulis, a Greenpeace Genetic Engineering Specialist. "With the bottom dropping out of the European market for GE feed, there soon will be no place to sell America’s unwanted crops."

Cargill, the largest U.S. grain supplier to Europe, is finding itself in a losing battle as U.S. grain exports have already dropped by $1 billion due to rejection of genetically engineered crops abroad. Other grain dealers like Archer Daniels Midland and Bunge, which have urged farmers to separate GE crops if they grow them, could be better positioned to serve European needs. Last year Bunge told farmers: “Bunge Corporation recognizes that consumer resistance in the near-term to the use of GMO's in certain markets both in the U.S. and abroad may necessitate segregation for specific commodities."

"Since April 1999, virtually the entire European food industry has eliminated GE ingredients,” added Margulis, "but Brazil’s been cashing in while U.S. farmers struggle. Seeing this trend repeating with animal feed is terrible news for U.S. farmers."

Both Tesco and Asda expect to sell non-GE pork, poultry, eggs and fish by this fall, with other products to follow. All three of Britain’s top supermarkets have now announced their intention to eliminate GE crops from animal feed, including Sainsbury markets, which made its commitment last year. Other European food companies, such as the Belgian supermarket Carrefour and Wiesenhof, Germany’s biggest chicken producer, have announced their intention to sell only animals fed with non-GE feed. McDonald’s recently announced that its chicken products sold across Europe will be from animals fed with non-GE feed.

"Europe will label genetically engineered animal feed, but Americans still don’t have labels on GE food," said Charles Margulis, a Greenpeace Genetic Engineering Specialist. "While Europeans protect their animals from these genetic experiments, the genetic engineers are force feeding these foods to American children."
