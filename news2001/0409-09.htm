Computer Investigation Reveals Government Standards Leave Millions Of Pregnant Women And Their Fetuses At Risk for Mercury Exposure
 
WASHINGTON - April 9 - A new EWG computer investigation reveals that current, one-size-fits-all government recommendations leave millions of pregnant women and their fetuses at risk from exposure to mercury in fish. The report, issued in conjunction with the U.S. Public Interest Research Group, uses data similar to the recent Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) report that found higher levels of mercury contamination in people than previously reported. That information was combined with a one-of-a-kind EWG database on fish contamination that contains 53,000 records of mercury test results in fish from seven different government sources.

In addition to releasing an expanded list of fish that pregnant women should either completely avoid or eat sparingly, EWG will provide a list of fish that pregnant women can safely eat. EWG will also recommend steps that the FDA and EPA can take to prevent mercury contamination in fish and ways to improve health assessments to better protect consumers.
