Faith-Based Organizations Representing Millions of Americans Join AIDS, Health Groups to Call for Debt Cancellation to Stop AIDS
 
WASHINGTON - July 16 - Thirty major agencies involved in the fight against global AIDS today appealed to President Bush to use the upcoming G7 Summit to mandate deeper and broader debt relief for impoverished nations. The agencies sent a letter to Bush outlining their concerns.

"This letter is yet more proof that even though the year 2000 is past the debt cancellation movement is going strong and making new allies," stated David Bryden, spokesperson for the Global AIDS Alliance. "We hope this sends a clear message to President Bush that discussing AIDS at Genoa without seriously addressing debt as well will be rejected by a wide range of AIDS advocacy groups."

The signatories included a wide range of groups including national religious organizations, African American groups, and AIDS service organitions working in Africa. The African Services Committee, the National Association of People Living with AIDS and the American Public Health Association, and Rainbow PUSH Coalition were among the signers. Groups providing AIDS services in Africa , many of them faith-based, including from Nigeria, Mali, Uganda and Zambia, also signed the appeal. "We are concerned that your Administration may not yet be fully taking into account the tremendous potential of deeper and broader debt relief and we are concerned that the U.S. delegation opposed the call for deeper debt relief at the U.N. Special Assembly on AIDS," the letter states.

The letter calls for U.S. support for full cancellation of that portion of the debt owed by impoverished countries to the IMF and the World Bank. "Already, some of the funds released by debt cancellation are being used to fight HIV/AIDS -- for example in Uganda, Tanzania and Cameroon. A program of deeper cancellation would effectively build upon these successes." The letter also calls for an expansion of debt relief to include Nigeria, Bangladesh and Haiti. .
