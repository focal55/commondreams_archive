Congress Disappoints on Family Farms and Clean Water
 
WASHINGTON - October 4 - The Sierra Club today expressed frustration with the Members of the House of Representatives who voted against helping farmers safeguard clean water, protect wetlands, and prevent suburban sprawl. Leading the effort to protect the environment and hold corporate factory farms responsible for their pollution were Reps. Sherwood Boehlert (R-NY), Ron Kind (D-WI), Wayne Gilchrest (R-MD) and John Dingell (D-MI), who introduced the proposal. The amendment narrowly failed by a vote of 200 to 226.

"We are disappointed that the House passed up an opportunity to help farmers safeguard our wetlands, clean air and clean water. Instead, the House handed tax payers the bill for cleaning up the mess made by giant, corporate-owned farms," said Carl Pope, executive director of the Sierra Club. "However, we are grateful to those Representatives who stood up to corporate interests to protect the health and wallets of all Americans."

The plan, introduced as an amendment to a 10-year Farm Bill, would have increased funding for the successful Conservation Reserve Program, which pays farmers to leave highly erosion-prone stream banks and other sensitive lands alone, protecting 45 million acres. Additionally, the amendment would have increased funding for the Wetlands Reserve Program, allowing farmers to restore at least 250,000 acres of threatened wetlands per year. It also would have increased funding to protect farmland from suburban sprawl.

"While we are disappointed that the House turned its back on family farms and clean water, we are hopeful that the Senate will stand up and protect our wetlands, clean water and open space," continued Pope.

America's drinking water, rivers and lakes are at risk from factory farms. These Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations (CAFOs) confine thousands of animals in one facility and produce staggering amounts of animal waste in the process (2.7 trillion pounds per year). Too often, this waste leaks into our rivers and streams, fouling our air, contaminating our drinking water and spreading disease. According to the Environmental Protection Agency, hog, chicken and cattle waste has polluted 35,000 miles of rivers in 22 states and contaminated groundwater in 17 states.
