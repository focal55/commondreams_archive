NOW to Release Second Annual Primetime Television Report at National Conference in Philadelphia
 
WASHINGTON - June 27 - "This weekend NOW is releasing its second Watch Out, Listen Up! report on primetime programming," said NOW President Patricia Ireland. "NOW's feminist analysts watched hours of TV—much of it degrading, violent and bearing little resemblance to the lives of real women. But several shows did stand out for their ability to portray women and girls as complex, well-rounded people." Ireland will be at the National NOW Conference in Philadelphia, PA., June 29-July 1, where one of the many issues on the agenda is media reform and activism.

The purpose of NOW's Watch Out, Listen Up! campaign is to work toward a positive and broadly inclusive portrayal of women in the media. NOW evaluated programming based on gender composition and diversity, violent content, sexual exploitation and social responsibility. In addition to the four major networks, NOW added UPN and The WB to this year's report.

What: Release of Watch Out, Listen Up! TV Analysis Report

When: Friday, June 29, 1:00 pm
"On the Verge: Women and Media" workshop

Where: Wyndham Franklin Plaza Hotel
Philadelphia, PA
and online at www.now.org/issues/media/watchout2

Last year's report inspired lots of positive response from TV viewers asking what they could do next. "Many women and men write to and call NOW to share their outrage about what passes for entertainment on television," Ireland said. "People are looking for a way to make the networks listen up and respond to their complaints—they want to be part of something bigger. NOW's media activism campaign can make that happen."
