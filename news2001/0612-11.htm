Religious Leaders Unite to Demand Minimum Wage Increase, $1.50 Increase Is Good First Step
 
WASHINGTON - June 12 - News Advisory:

While business receives special treatment from the Bush White House, 11 million minimum wage workers continue to be ignored. On June 13, religious leaders will gather near the Capitol steps to demand an increase in the minimum wage.

On June 13, the National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support will officially launch a national campaign to build support for the minimum wage bill sponsored by Sen. Ted Kennedy (D-Mass.) with a press conference near the Capitol steps. Senate action is expected this summer on minimum wage legislation and the House will follow.

To support this action, 20 religious leaders have signed a letter to President Bush and Members of Congress, including Bishop Joseph Fiorenza, president of the National Conference of Catholic Bishops, Rabbi David Saperstein, director of the Religious Action Center of Reform Judaism, and the Reverend Dr. Robert W. Edgar, general secretary of the National Council of the Churches of Christ in the USA. An ACORN community activist, a minimum wage worker, and Richard Trumka, secretary-treasurer of the AFL-CIO will join the religious leaders at the press conference.
