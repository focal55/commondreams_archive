WASHINGTON - June 18 - News Advisory:

Reproductive Choice: What's Faith Got to Do with It?
Briefing - 2:30 p.m., Wednesday, June 20th

Does the Religious Right's anti-choice dogma represent the majority of Americans who consider themselves deeply religious? The answer may surprise you! 92 percent of Protestant and Jewish clergy polled recently said that every woman should be able to decide when to have children according to her conscience and religious beliefs, and 80 percent of those polled support a woman's right to a safe and legal abortion.

On the eve of a House Judiciary subcommittee hearing on the Born Alive Infants Protection Act, the bipartisan Pro-Choice Caucus invites House members, staff, and the news media to a remarkable Capitol Hill briefing, "Reproductive Choice: What's Faith Got to Do with It?"

Prominent Catholic, Protestant and Jewish clergy and laity will discuss why they are pro-choice, not in spite of their faith, but because of it.

To arrange an interview with Pro-Choice Caucus Chairs and clergy, contact Marjorie Signer of the Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice at 202-628-7700 ext. 208.

WHO:
Bipartisan Pro-Choice Caucus; The Reverend Carlton W. Veazey, President and CEO of the Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice and pastor of Fellowship Baptist Church, Washington, DC: Rabbi Jonathan A. Biatch, Associate Rabbi, Beth El Hebrew Congregation, Alexandria, VA, and member of the Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice Clergy for Choice Network; The Reverend Mark Bigelow, Pastor of the Congregational Church of Huntington, NY, United Church of Christ, and member of Planned Parenthood's Clergy Advisory Board; Frances Kissling, President of Catholics for a Free Choice

WHAT: Briefing on the pro-choice position from a faith-based values-based perspective

WHEN: 2:30 p.m., Wednesday, June 20th

WHERE: HC-6, U.S. Capitol
