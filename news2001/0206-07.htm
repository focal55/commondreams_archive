NWLC: Bush Proposal To Eliminate Estate Tax Won't Benefit Vast Majority Of Women And Families
 
WASHINGTON - February 6 - President Bush's proposal to eliminate the estate tax would benefit only the top 5 percent of tax filers and do nothing for the overwhelming majority of women and their families who do not stand to gain from inherited wealth, the National Women's Law Center said today. Moreover, the size of the proposed tax cut would deplete funds for programs that women and their families depend on.

"To bill this aspect of the plan as 'helping families' is somewhat misleading. Women and their families are disproportionately in the middle and lower income brackets that won't be helped at all by this change," said Nancy Duff Campbell, NWLC co-president. "Over one-quarter of the money in the proposed tax cut comes from the elimination of the estate tax, but all of the benefit would go to a small handful of wealthy Americans."

"It's not the estate tax that is keeping the vast majority of American families from preserving their assets. It's the staggering costs of health care, including long term care and the other costs associated with catastrophic illnesses, especially as people age, that is depleting family assets -- and these are the types of issues we should be addressing instead of a tax cut," said Campbell.

Campbell added, "The size of the proposed tax cut poses not only a threat to money for health care, but also to funds for Social Security, Medicare and other safety net programs and programs that are critical to women and their families such as education and child care." Women have a greater stake in these federal programs because they tend to earn less during their lifetimes, live longer, and are more likely to bear the burden of uncompensated family care-taking responsibilities.

Campbell has almost 30 years' experience in tax law and policy, especially as it pertains to women and their families. She serves on the board of the Community Tax Law Report and chaired the national Coalition on Women and Taxes during the 1986 tax reform debates.

The National Women's Law Center is a non-partisan, non-profit organization that has been working since 1972 to advance and protect women's legal rights. NWLC focuses on major policy areas of importance to women and their families including economic security, education, employment and health, with special attention given to the concerns of low-income women.
