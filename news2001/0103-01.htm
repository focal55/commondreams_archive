Funding Exchange Report Challenges Donors To Tackle Causes of Problems, Not Just Symptoms
 
NEW YORK CITY - January 3 - Americans will give about $200 billion to charity this year. Unfortunately, says the Funding Exchange, only a small share of this generosity from individuals, corporations and foundations goes to organizations addressing the root causes of the problems we face.

Founded in 1979 by wealthy donors with a new philanthropy of "Change, not Charity," the Funding Exchange (FEX) is a network of 15 community funds across the country-from Appalachia to California, New England to Hawaii-with a national office in New York City.

"The Funding Exchange and its member funds have provided lead funding for virtually every contemporary movement for progressive social change," says Executive Director Ellen Gurzinsky. FEX grantees:

  • Fought against domestic violence when it wasn't popular.
  • Helped launch the movement for environmental justice.
  • Began the living wage campaigns that became a national movement.
  • Propelled the movement for gay and lesbian civil rights.
  • Lead education and youth projects on the cutting edge of social change.

This month, W.W. Norton is releasing the paperback version of the FEX network book, Robin Hood Was Right: A Guide to Giving Your Money for Social Change, coauthored by Chuck Collins, Pam Rogers and Joan P. Garner, with a preface by Alfre Woodard. It's a unique how-to guide for giving, whether you are living paycheck to paycheck or a multimillionaire.

"Wait! Don't mail those checks yet...[Robin Hood Was Right] will help you donate your money with thought and purpose."-Arizona Republic

As highlighted in the new FEX report, "Funding Tomorrow's Social Change Today," grantees of the Liberty Hill Foundation, a member fund in Los Angeles, raised thousands of workers from the ranks of the working poor through the Living Wage Ordinance. The FEX Paul Robeson Fund supported the Oscar winning documentaries, "Deadly Deception" and "Breathing Lessons."

The current FEX docket features an array of exciting projects, including organizations building strong coalitions among environmentalists, labor, ranchers and farmers in North Dakota and Montana. The democratic values of social change are reflected in the FEX process of partnership philanthropy. In the words of FEX Cofounder George Pillsbury, "The Funding Exchange is a catalyst-redefining philanthropy from endowment to empowerment and from decisions made solely by donors to partnerships between funders and those seeking funds."
