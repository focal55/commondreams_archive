Sanders Forges Coalition Calling For Immediate Vote to Repeal PNTR with China
Letter to Hastert joins calls from conservatives, proving there is bipartisan support for repealing PNTR
 
WASHINGTON - April 23 - Congressman Bernard Sanders (I-VT) today released a letter signed by 15 Members of Congress calling on the House Leadership to immediately schedule a vote to revoke Permanent Normal Trade Relations with China in response to the Chinese government's detention of 24 American soldiers. The letter, which is signed by Sanders and Democrats, joins calls from conservative Republicans to repeal PNTR following the return of the American service men and women from Hainan Island.

Sanders said, "While it is imperative that we retain a friendly and positive erelationship with China, it is clear that PNTR is not working from an economic point of view, a political point of view, a human rights point of view, or an environmental point of view. The idea that we would continue to open up our markets to China and sustain an $84 billion annual trade deficit at the same time they have held American servicemen and women prisoner and continue to hold an American airplane is incomprehensible."

The letter details the declining environmental and human rights conditions in China since PNTR was signed. In terms of the environment, China is the home of five of the world's ten most polluted cities with 80 percent of Chinese rivers having no fish because of pollution. On human rights, China continues to hold 6 to 8 million political, religious, ethnic and labor activists in forced labor camps - 300,000 of which have had no trial at all. At the same time, PNTR has inflated the U.S. trade deficit with China to $84 billion a year, while the Chinese military grows in strength.

The letter is attached. It is signed by Rep. Sanders, Peter DeFazio, Marcy Kaptur, Sherrod Brown, Ronnie Shows, Frank Pallone, Cynthia McKinney, Jerrold Nadler, Peter Visclosky, Ted Strickland, William Pascrell, James Traficant, Gene Taylor, Pete Stark, and Barbara Lee.

-30- Dear Mr. Speaker:

We are writing to urge you to schedule a vote as soon as possible to revoke Permanent Normal Trade Relations (PNTR) with China. While we believe that the United States should have a positive and friendly relationship with China, and we do not want to see another cold war, we believe that PNTR with China is not accomplishing that important goal.

As you know, in the debate on this issue last year many of us expressed concern that granting PNTR to China would negatively impact the U.S. economy and cost us hundreds of thousands of decent paying jobs. We also felt that such issues as human rights and environmental protection in China, as well as U.S. national security issues, would be set back by granting PNTR and doing away with the annual Congressional review of our trading status with China.

One year after the PNTR vote, it appears that the concerns that many of us had in opposing the agreement were justified:

1. Our trade deficit with China is the largest in the world and has increased from $57 billion in 1998 to $83.8 billion in 2000. This huge trade deficit with China is a major factor in our record breaking $449.5 billion trade deficit in goods. According to some economists, every $1 billion in our U.S. trade deficit equals the loss of 14,000 jobs. This means that almost 60 percent of the 638,000 U.S. manufacturing jobs that we have lost since June 1998 are the result of our growing trade deficit with China. In other words, PNTR for China has been a disaster for American workers.

2. Religious groups from throughout the world, including the Catholic Church, continue to decry China's human rights record. Since passage of PNTR, China's human rights abuses have grown even worse. Presently there are 6 million to 8 million political, religious, ethnic and labor activists in Chinese forced labor camps. About 300,000 of them are held without trial, as was a 5-year-old American child in February of 2001. His mother, a U.S. resident, is still being held in captivity in China today.

3. China's horrendous environmental record continues to threaten the well-being of the entire planet. Today, five of the world's ten most polluted cities are in China and an estimated two million people die each year there from air and water pollution. Water pollution is so severe that 80 percent of China's rivers have no fish remaining in them.

4. PNTR with China has also not benefited U.S. national security interests. As China's economy has expanded through increased trade with the U.S., so has its military. China continues its belligerent stance towards Taiwan and is steadily expanding its arsenal of missiles, aircraft, submarines and destroyers. For example, in recent years China has deployed more than 300 short-range ballistic missiles with the capability of blanketing the island of Taiwan. China has also bought over 4,000 warplanes, four Russian Kilo-class submarines, and two Russian-made Sovremenny-class destroyers that are a major worry for both Taiwan and the United States. Chinese workers have also been detected building a fiber-optic communications system linking Iraq's air defenses.

Despite differing views in Congress on PNTR, clearly a major event has recently occurred that is of enormous concern to all of us: the illegal detention of 24 American servicemen and women who were forced to make an emergency landing after a Chinese fighter pilot rammed their plane over international waters. Through the courage and skill of the American crew, they survived that event only to be held hostage for 11 days by our so-called "friendly" trading partner in gross violation of international law. China, a country that has been granted free access to our markets, still illegally holds the plane it damaged and is rifling through it to steal our technology. On top of all that, China demands an apology for a crisis that it precipitated.

In conclusion, while we want the United States to have a friendly and constructive relationship with China, PNTR is obviously not accomplishing that end. We need to develop a relationship with China that truly advances the goals of U.S. economic and national security, as well as promoting democracy, freedom and environmental sanity in China. Recent events show that giving China a blank check for trade has not worked. Therefore, we ask that you schedule a vote to revoke PNTR with China as soon as possible.
