"Safe" Act Unsafe For The Budget
Energy Bill Takes Big Bite Out of Already Shrinking Surplus
 

WASHINGTON - August 31 - The President’s recent assertion that the dwindling budget surplus will act as a "a fiscal straitjacket for Congress" stands in stark contrast to the budget-busting $38 billion in tax breaks and subsidies for to the nation’s most polluting--and most profitable--corporations included in the Securing America’s Future Energy (SAFE) Act, passed on August 2nd by the House, according to a new analysis by the U.S. Public Interest Research Group.

"Not only is the energy legislation passed by the House dirty and dangerous, it will chip away at an already shrinking budget surplus," said U.S. PIRG Research Director Alison Cassady. “These handouts to corporate polluters may come at the expense of priorities like education, Social Security and Medicare,” she continued.

According to the White House estimates released last week, only $38 billion of the projected surplus is earmarked for non-Social Security and Medicare programs. Coincidentally, this projection is almost identical to handouts for polluters in the SAFE Act. The $38 billion in tax breaks and subsidies to polluters amount to about $292 per taxpayer--virtually negating the $300 tax refund checks mailed out over the last month.

“Instead of having surplus funds to spend on priority programs, we will be left with the side effects of a shortsighted and dirty energy policymore drilling, more spilling, more air pollution, more global warming, and more radioactive waste,” added U.S. PIRG Legislative Director Anna Aurilio. ”These budget-busting handouts line the pockets of polluters and the nation’s wealthiest CEOs at taxpayers’ expense,” she concluded.

U.S. PIRG calls on the Senate to reject the fundamentally flawed House energy legislation and support proactive policies for cleaner, more affordable energy solutions that benefit consumers rather than polluters. Specifically, U.S. PIRG urges the Senate to:

  • Protect the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge and other sensitive areas from oil and gas drilling;
  • Increase miles-per-gallon standards for SUVs and light trucks;
  • Eliminate taxpayer subsidies for polluters;
  • Require significant production from renewable energy sources; and
  • Clean up dirty power plants.
U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for state-based Public Interest Research Groups. The State PIRGs are independent, non-profit, non-partisan public interest advocacy organizations. The full text of this report is available at www.newenergyfuture.com.
