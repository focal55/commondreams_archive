FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
FEBRUARY 27, 2001
7:12 PM
 CONTACT:  Congresswoman Cynthia McKinney
 Jocco Baccus (202) 225-1605
Email: Jocco.Baccus@mail.house.gov
State of the Union Alternative Presentation
 
WASHINGTON - February 27 - * George W. Bush has the lowest job approval rating given to a newly elected President since Dwight D. Eisenhower. Even Richard Nixon scored higher; I wonder why?

* Could it be because he's keeping his promise to govern on the platform he campaigned on?

* That means tax cuts that only his Cabinet seem to want and drilling in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge which, veritably, nobody seems to want.

* If George W. Bush thinks that, under the din of Congressional investigations into the Rich pardon, a spy scandal, a submarine scandal, and bombing in Baghdad, that we won't notice the move on Alaska, he and the Republicans are dead wrong.

* The Progressive Caucus is watching and so are the American people.

* George W. Bush has said that he doesn't want to look back; he wants to look forward; and that his speech tonight is his effort to look forward.

* Well, if I had an environmental record like his, I'd want to look forward, too.

* And based on Texas and the Bush Cabinet, here's what we have to look forward to:

-Increased toxic emissions (Texas led the nation in the release of cancer-causing chemicals, carbon dioxide, and mercury into the air);

-Increased plunder abroad by US oil companies with their tax subsidies and human rights abuses in tow;

-And the specter of franken food becoming a staple in our diet without our knowledge.

* And by the way, over 30,000 Americans died last year due to exposure to air pollution.

* In response to a question about California's power crisis, George W. Bush's initial reaction was to rollback clean air requirements for power companies.

* Presiding over our Energy Department is Spencer Abraham who, as a Senator, wanted to dismantle it.

* Christie Todd Whitman doubts that global warming is a serious problem.

* Gale Norton, Secretary of the Interior, allows mining company polluters to escape cleanup requirements and liability because she believes that companies have a constitutional right to pollute.

* Ann Veneman served on the board of Monsanto who gave us genetically modified foods.

* And the New Attorney General has a history of blocking enforcement of environmental laws.

* And while the Bush Administration readies its chainsaw budget that rips our social safety net to shreds, it has produced energy legislation that transfers a tidy sum--about $24 Billion--to oil and energy companies.

* And George W. Bush, himself, stated that he wanted to double the amount of logging in our National Forests.

* From taxpayer subsidized logging to exclusive drilling rights in endangered wilderness, it seems that the oil and timber barons now have an open invitation to feed at the public trough.

* Senator Murkowski's bill is just the beginning of a wrong-way environmental package, an entire way of thinking, that puts our planet at risk.

* Unfortunately, we're all expected to pay the price. This Administration has chosen environmental recklessness over real money for education and help for the homeless.

* "Looking Forward" as this Administration wants us to do, I envision more pollutants in our environment, with tax breaks to put them there.

* Except for one thing: the melting snows of Kilimanjaro are the clarion call to people everywhere to pay attention to this Administration.

You can bet that we all will be listening intently tonight to Bush's environmental message.
