National Parties Raise Record $98.8 Million in Soft Money During the First Six Months of 2001-2002 Election Cycle
Republican National Committee Raises Record $33.5 Million
 
WASHINGTON - September 4 - The Democratic and Republican national party committees raised a record $98,822,529 in soft money during the first six months of the 2001-2002 election cycle, according to a Common Cause analysis released today -- almost three times the $34.3 million raised in the first six months of the 1997-1998 cycle, the last non-presidential election cycle, and nearly twice the $54.5 million raised during the first six months of 1999-2000 election cycle.

Republican party committees shattered all previous fundraising records by raising an unprecedented $65.6 million in soft money during the first half of 2001, almost double the amount the Democratic national party committees raised during the same period and more than twice the amount Republicans raised during the first six months of the 1999- 2000 election cycle.

The Democratic national party committees also broke previous party records for the first six months of an election cycle by raising $33.3 million in soft money from January through June 2001, surpassing their record of $23.8 million raised during the first six months of the previous election cycle by $9.4 million.

"Both parties are raising soft money like there's no tomorrow," Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger said. "But if the American people have their way, there will be no tomorrow for this discredited and dishonorable soft money system."

The full study, which plots the growth of soft money since 1997 and breaks down soft money by party committee, is available at:
