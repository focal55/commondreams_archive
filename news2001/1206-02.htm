Secret EPA Analysis Shows Bush Plan Means More Pollution
RNC Chair Racicot to Continue Dirty-Air Lobbying
 
WASHINGTON - December 6 - A secret U.S. Environmental Protection Agency analysis shows that the current Clean Air Act -- if enforced strictly -- generally would mean cleaner air than a national "cap and trade" emissions --trading scheme drafted by the Bush Administration's EPA.

News of the analysis comes amid reports that incoming Republican National Committee Chairman Mark Racicot will continue lobbying for a dirty-power company coalition, led by Southern Company, that seeks to weaken enforcement of the Clean Air Act, specifically its "New Source Review" provisions.

The Bush administration is expected in the near future to endorse new loopholes and other weakening changes to New Source Review, which is designed to prevent pollution increases from older power plants and factories that undergo expansions and major modifications.

The secret EPA analysis, presented in a meeting with power company executives from the Edison Electric Institute, indicates that weakening the Clean Air Act will lead to increased pollution -- even if the administration follows up with a new "cap-and-trade" cleanup plan that would allow power companies to buy and sell emission credits.

The analysis shows that dangerous sulfur dioxide emissions from electric power plants would be lower in every part of the country under "business as usual" (enforcement of the current Clean Air Act) than under a draft EPA plan to eviscerate much of current law and replace it with a cap-and-trade plan.

Future coal burning also would be less under current law, suggesting carbon dioxide emissions would be less as well, according to the analysis. The analysis also shows that smog-forming nitrogen oxides emissions would be lower under current law in most areas with smog problems (the Northeast, the Midwest, the Southeast and Texas.) In addition, mercury emissions from coal-fired power plants would be reduced more quickly under current law.

The EPA analysis is available on the Clean Air Trust's Web site at www.cleanairtrust.org.

Additional comments from grassroots organizations are available from the Texas-based Sustainable Energy and Economic Development Coalition at 512-479-7744.
