Gallup: Majority of Americans Favor Allowing Gays to Serve in Armed Forces
 
WASHINGTON - June 5 - An overwhelming majority of Americans - 72% - favor the right of lesbians, gays and bisexuals to serve in the United States Armed Forces, according to a poll released yesterday by the Gallup Organization. Gallup's findings come just
"The American people overwhelmingly support gays in the military."
days after the Pentagon announced it discharged a record 1,231 men and women in uniform on the basis of sexual orientation during the past year. The Gallup poll is the latest in a number of public opinion polls showing Americans favor, and value, the service of lesbian, gay and bisexual service members. "The American people overwhelmingly support gays in the military," said C. Dixon Osburn, Executive Director of Servicemembers Legal Defense Network (SLDN). "Congress should do the same and repeal 'Don't Ask, Don't Tell, Don't Pursue, Don't Harass.'"

When asked, "Do you think homosexuals should or should not be hired for the armed forces," responses since 1992 have indicated an upward swing in favor of allowing lesbians, gays and bisexuals to serve:

2001
Should be hired - 72%
Should not be hired - 23%

1999
Should be hired - 70%
Should not be hired - 26%

1996
Should be hired - 65%
Should not be hired - 29%

1992
Should be hired - 57%
Should not be hired - 37%

The complete results of the Gallup poll are available by clicking on this link.:
