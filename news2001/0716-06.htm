Sierra Club Blows the Whistle on the Bush Energy Plan
In Events Across the Country, Activists Push for Quicker, Cleaner, Cheaper and Safer Solutions
 
WASHINGTON - July 16 - Sierra Club Blows the Whistle on the Bush Energy Plan In Events Across the Country, Activists Push for Quicker, Cleaner, Cheaper and Safer Solutions

In response to President Bush's latest efforts to promote an energy plan that makes the wrong choices, Sierra Club activists are fanning out in cities and towns across America today to voice opposition to the Administration's plan and to advocate for energy efficiency, energy conservation, and environmentally responsible production.

"Today the Bush Administration is trying to breathe life back into their energy initiative, but this is a 'dead plan walking.' Americans have rejected the President's energy plan because it won't clean up our air, won't prevent future energy shortages and won't save us money," said Carl Pope, Executive Director of the Sierra Club. "Poll after poll shows that Americans disapprove of the Bush Administration's energy policies. Our members on the ground are telling the Administration we should be pursuing energy efficiency, renewable energy, and cleaner and more responsible fuels."

Sierra Club activists will respond to Administration's events today in the following cities:

Philadelphia, PA--While Vice President Cheney gives a closed-door address to the National Association of Counties, Sierra Club activists will hold a press conference at 2 PM outside the meeting to stress how the Bush energy plan would exacerbate air pollution problems in Pennsylvania. Volunteers will also distribute leaflets and collect petition signatures in downtown Philadelphia that afternoon. Contact Nancy Rauch, 215-668-5377

Pittsburgh, PA--While Vice President Cheney presides over an invitation-only "town hall meeting" Sierra Club volunteers and coalition partners will protest at the free speech area at Alleghany Community College Boise Campus in Monroeville. Sierra Club representatives will also hold a press conference at 11 AM in downtown Pittsburgh focusing on air pollution problems in Western Pennsylvania and unveiling TV ads which will run on Pittsburgh news programs on Monday. Contact Peter Wray, 412-244-9907

Sioux Falls, SD--Sierra Club volunteers will attend a town hall meeting hosted by Interior Secretary Gayle Norton to ask her tough questions on the Administration's energy policy. Club activists and environmental allies will hold a press conference immediately after the town hall meeting to criticize the Administration's policies of drilling for oil in pristine places, such as the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge. Contact Tracie Weber, 605-366-2912

Chicago, IL--Sierra Club representatives will hold a rally nearby the Argonne National Laboratory to coincide with Energy Secretary Spence Abraham's visit to Chicago. Club activists will criticize the Bush Administration's proposals to drill on the shores of the Great Lakes, the world's largest bodies of fresh water. Contact Jennifer Johnson, 312-251-1680

Houston, TX--Sierra Club activists and environmental allies will rally and hold a press conference outside a town hall meeting hosted by Reps Delay, Culberson, and Brady. Activists will stress that the Bush energy plan will only exacerbate Houston's air pollution problems. Contact Tony Fuller, 713-528-5959

Monroe, NC--Sierra Club activists will demonstrate outside a closed door "town hall meeting" hosted by Commerce Secretary Don Evans, and Representatives Myrick and Hayes. Contact Peter Baker at 828-242-2023.

Oklahoma City, OK--Sierra Club Conservation Organizer Jeanine Hale will be on hand to field comments from the media in Oklahoma City, as Rep. J.C. Watts tours an energy facility in Oklahoma City. Contact Jeanine Hale, 918-227-7399
