Statement of Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger On The Use of the Naval Observatory for GOP Fundraising
 
WASHINGTON - May 21 - The revelation that top GOP soft money donors are being treated to a private dinner at the official residence of the Vice President is something that makes much of the Republican party's justified criticism of the Clinton-era excesses ring hollow, and it's a sign that something is fundamentally broken with our political system.

Because both parties are complicit in this soft money system, neither party can play a credible role in keeping the other party honest. A gentlemen's agreement of sorts, between the parties, is enforcing a code of silence when it comes to these events - because no one in power wants to rule out anything when it comes to fundraising.

But with this event at the Naval Observatory, it's as if a wave of selective amnesia has set in, wiping away the memories of the scandals involving the sale of the Lincoln Bedroom and the White House coffee klatches.

The slippery excuses about this not being a fundraiser recall another Vice President's glib denials about a "donor maintenance" event at a Buddhist temple. There's no controlling legal authority preventing this kind of event at the Naval Observatory tonight, but the Vice President, the rest of the Administration, and both political parties should realize that every revelation like this one will foster even more cynicism about government among citizens.
