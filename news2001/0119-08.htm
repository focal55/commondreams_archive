Choice in the Advent of a Bush Administration -- President-Elect Said To Reinstate Global Gag Rule
 
WASHINGTON - January 19 - The following is a statement by Joan Malin, CEO of Planned Parenthood of New York City:

"On January 22, 1973, the U.S. Supreme Court announced its decision in Roe v. Wade and for the first time recognized the constitutional right to privacy includes a woman's decision whether to terminate her pregnancy.

"Sadly, as we commemorate the 28th anniversary of Roe, we once again find ourselves preparing for new obstacles to a woman's self-determination -- including the nomination of staunchly anti-choice John Ashcroft for Attorney General and threats to international and domestic family planning programs. This Saturday Planned Parenthood of New York City (PPNYC) will join thousands of other pro-choice Americans in Washington, D.C. to protest the Ashcroft nomination and to send a message to the Bush Administration that women have come too far and fought too hard to have our legal rights taken away.

"Today, twenty-eight years after the Roe decision, abortion is 10 times safer than childbirth. Legal abortion is associated with decreases in maternal and infant mortality. The right to make informed, personal decisions about their reproductive lives has also enabled women to pursue educational and employment opportunities unthinkable to women only a generation ago. Safe, legal abortion is an essential part of women's reproductive health care, yet today, American women face ever-broadening encroachments upon their right to choose.

"This year's anniversary of Roe is both a time to celebrate and a time to prepare for future hurdles. The year 2000 saw many hard-fought victories for the reproductive rights movement, from the long-awaited approval of mifepristone to the Supreme Court decision in Stenberg v. Carhart -- a decision that reaffirmed a woman's right to choose. In the 2000 elections, a clear majority of American voters supported a pro-choice presidential agenda and voters in New York elected pro-choice Hillary Clinton to the United States Senate.

"The strength of pro-choice America forced anti-choice presidential candidate George W. Bush to hedge on the issue of choice during his campaign, but Mr. Bush has since revealed stronger ties to the anti-choice efforts put forward by political and religious extremists. In fact, it is predicted that Mr. Bush will reinstate the Global Gag Rule as early as Monday, to coincide with the anniversary of Roe. In the past, the Global Gag Rule denied USAID family planning assistance to any organization abroad who uses their own funds to provide abortion services or advocate for abortion rights. The Global Gag Rule could not only prevent women from accessing abortion services and information, but also financially cripple the organizations that are best equipped to provide other critical reproductive health and family planning services to their communities. The Global Gag Rule may further prohibit organizations abroad from lobbying their governments to change laws and policies regarding abortion -- precisely the type of lobbying that is legal in the United States.

"PPNYC is ready and able to advance the cause of reproductive rights and health, regardless of who is in the White House. We will meet these obstacles with immediate action - from grassroots mobilization to aggressive legislative advocacy, in order to defend reproductive freedom for all women, in the United States and abroad."
