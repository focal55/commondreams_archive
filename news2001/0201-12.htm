Narrow Senate Vote Warns Ashcroft Against Weak Enforcement of Environmental Safeguards
 
WASHINGTON - February 1 - The Senate's narrow confirmation of John Ashcroft as Attorney General today is a warming against weakening enforcement of environmental safeguards, the Sierra Club said today. Ashcroft's past opposition to laws protecting our families from pollution generated serious concerns about whether he will vigorously enforce these laws as attorney general. The Sierra Club praised those Senators who voted against Ashcroft's nomination.

"The narrow vote puts John Ashcroft on notice that he should not let corporate polluters off the hook as America's top environmental cop," said Carl Pope, executive director of the Sierra Club. "America needs an attorney general who will aggressively protect our families from polluters who poison the air we breathe and the water we drink. In his hearing, Ashcroft pledged to uphold the laws safeguarding our families from pollution, and we'll make sure he doesn't duck that commitment. We applaud those Senators who voted against Ashcroft to protect America's environment for our families and for our futures.

"Those Senators who voted for Mr. Ashcroft are now obligated to ensure that he aggressively enforce our environmental laws to provide clean air and water for all Americans," added Pope.

While in the Senate, Ashcroft voted against additional funding for environmental programs including the Clean Water Action Plan and toxic waste cleanups at Superfund sites. He also voted in favor of bills to roll back clean water protections, to prevent the EPA from enforcing arsenic standards for drinking water, and to allow mining companies to dump cyanide and other mining waste on large areas of public lands next to mining sites. As attorney general, it will be his duty to enforce these protections.

During his confirmation hearings, Senator Ashcroft was asked specifically whether he would "aggressively defend and uphold" President Clinton's plan to protect 60 million acres of wild, roadless areas in our National Forests from destruction. Senator Ashcroft stated "I will, regardless of whether or not I supported something as a senator, defend the rule. And if it is a rule with the force and effect of law, I will defend those cases." The Sierra Club will hold him accountable to his promise to defend the protection of these pristine 60 million acres.

The Sierra Club worked to block Ashcroft's appointment by mobilizing its more than 630,000 members and by working in coalition with environmental, civil rights, and other organizations to educate the public about Ashcroft's bleak environmental record.
