Senate Help for Airline Industry Crucial to Minnesota Jobs
Wellstone Will Press for Return-to-Work Package Next
 

WASHINGTON - September 21 - Moving swiftly to address the economic crisis caused by terrorist attacks in a sector vital to the American economy and U.S. jobs, the Senate today passed a sweeping aid package for the airline industry. The aid package for the nation’s airlines includes $5 billion in immediate cash payments for losses since the attacks, $10 billion in loan guarantees to help airlines finance continuing operations in the coming months, and limited exemptions from liability in the September 11 attacks. The move by Congress was urgently required to keep some airlines from imminent bankruptcy, threatening the economy and jobs across the United States and in Minnesota. Senator Paul Wellstone (D-MN) promised to push next week for an additional $3.75 billion aid package for direct assistance to workers hit by the massive layoffs industry-wide, including job placement and retraining programs, health care coverage, as well as strong improvements in airline safety.

“This package today is urgently needed, and I am proud that the Senate acted swiftly to get this economic assistance out to the airline industry. We must next move forward to make flying safer. Airline safety has to be there with the money, because without the safety, people are not going to fly, and we’ll never make this industry financially viable again. People are quite frightened to fly now. We will come back quickly with major safety improvement legislation very soon, because it is a crucial issue,” Wellstone said. “There were other important provisions which we included in this bill, including a guarantee of essential air service to small communities. In my state of Minnesota, we don’t want our small airports left out of air service.”

“Because of these terrorist attacks, in addition to all of the serious issues before us, people have lost their jobs. Congress must provide some help to them. I wanted a $3.75 billion package dealing with all the people that have been laid off to be included in our aid package today. When you look at this economy, there are an awful lot of people who are in a world of economic pain. While it was essential that we get help to the companies today, I also believe that what should have been in this package was help for the employees – extending unemployment benefits, making sure people have access to job training, dislocated worker assistance, and especially to health care coverage. When people lose their jobs, the next thing they lose is their access to health care, because it then becomes too expensive. COBRA is too expensive, and I wish we had something better. But for many employees, this is critically important to their families. These are going to be very hard times for airline employees, and I am very disappointed we did not get this help for employees into this package. Getting help to employees and their families will be our first priority next week – it has to be part of any relief package,” said Wellstone.

Northwest Airlines, Minnesota’s largest employer, could see up to $500 million in direct grants, and will be able to access the $10 billion loan guaranties. Today the company announced 10,000 layoffs nationwide. Northwest CEO Richard Anderson reported that nearly half of these layoffs will be experienced by workers in Minnesota.
