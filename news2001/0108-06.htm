Statement of Physicians for Social Responsibility Executive Director and CEO Robert K. Musil, Ph.D. on the nomination of Gale Norton as Secretary of the Interior
 
WASHINGTON - January 8 - As Bruce Babbitt knows, the job of Secretary of the Interior is a lonely one. The single personage responsible for a third of our nation’s land and the office concerns itself with everything from mining and ranching to endangered species. The Interior Department must be the voice for that most valuable American resource, the land itself. While Babbitt was a noted environmentalist, George Bush’s pick for this post, Gale Norton, has demonstrated time and again that she feels more comfortable in the role of advocate for timber, mining, petroleum and other industries.

Norton has demonstrated her coziness with moneyed business interests throughout her career in government. She first gained prominence working for James Watt at the Mountain States Legal Foundation, an advocacy group whose major donors include a dozen oil companies including Exxon, Amoco, and Chevron. The Mountain States Legal Foundation has taken legal action against environmental protection, seeking to preserve protection only for business and industry, its biggest benefactors.

Continuing her well-documented rise through the anti-environmental ranks, Norton joined then-Interior Secretary Watt in President Reagan’s Interior Department. The department was nearly dismantled during Watt’s tenure. Her creative form of environmental degradation continued through her tenure as Attorney General of Colorado and on into the birth of the Coalition of Republican Environmental Activists (CREA).

CREA is as transparent on the environment as Norton herself. A number of prominent Republicans who actually are environmentalists declined to join the group at its inception because its members were obviously wolves in green clothing. Those refusing the extreme views of Norton and CREA included former Kansas governor John Hayden and Rep. Sherwood Boehlert (R-NY), the leader of moderate House Republicans on the environment.

But particularly disturbing is Norton’s lack of concern for children and their health. Norton is registered to lobby Congress and the Colorado State Legislator on behalf of NL Industries of Houston, Texas. NL Industries was once known as National Lead Company, a defendant in cases involving scores of Superfund sites and a dozen cases about children’s exposure to lead paint. Senators should be mindful not only of Norton’s anti-environmentalism but especially of her views opposing children’s health. They must reject her nomination as Secretary of the Interior. The land, water and health of our children have too few advocates already. The mining, timber and petroleum industries already have more than we need.

Robert K. Musil, Ph.D. is Executive Director and CEO of Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR), a national membership organization in Washington, DC. It shared the 1985 Nobel Prize for Peace with International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War and supports sensible gun laws to reduce firearms injuries and deaths.
