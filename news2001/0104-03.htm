Black Leaders Confirm January Action Agenda
 
WASHINGTON - January 4 - The Black Leadership Forum, Inc. (BLF), announces that BLF and the extended black leadership family will focus their near term attention on several policy priorities of great importance to the African American community, notably:

-- Voting rights and attendant litigation;
-- Management of Year-2000 U.S. census data;
-- Appropriate personnel to guarantee fair administration of justice and public education policies; and
-- Equitable decisions regarding economic initiatives such as interest rates and tax cuts.

Oversight of transition activities of in-coming President George W. Bush's administration and evaluations of nominees to cabinet positions which have serious implications for maintenance of civil rights gains and the African American quality of life, are also central elements of BLF's agenda.

An immediate priority of BLF and its extended family will be a convocation of leadership in Tallahassee, Fla., on Jan. 20, 2001 to continue the redress of voting rights' abuses and to celebrate black voter participation during the November 2000 election.

Leading a Jan. 2 press briefing on policy priorities and other initiatives during the coming month, BLF's chairman Dr. Joseph E. Lowery and Rev. Jesse L. Jackson, Sr., president, Rainbow/PUSH Coalition, announced that the Tallahassee celebration will be a joint venture of national leaders including the AFL/CIO, other national labor organizations and BLF-member organizations and their affiliates.

"Regarding our OEConvocation on the Democratic Process in Tallahassee, we shall assemble because our immediate focus is on Florida where the integrity of the vote is in question. Our assembly, however, will challenge the entire nation to honor voting rights -- in all elections in this country -- which are guaranteed by Article II and the 15th, 22nd and 26th amendments of the U.S. Constitution, concerning race, sex and age, as Federally protected rights of all of the people," stated Lowery.

Leaders also expressing support for the BLF Priority Agenda and the Tallahassee event include Dr. Dorothy I. Height, President Emerita, National Council of Negro Women; Congressman John Conyers (D-MI); Bill Lucy, President, Coalition of Black Trade Unionists; Melanie Campbell, Executive Director, Operation BIG VOTE; Kweisi Mfume, president and CEO, NAACP; John Sweeney, president, AFL/CIO; leadership of the National Urban League; Dr. Yvonne Scruggs-Leftwich, BLF's Executive Director and others.
