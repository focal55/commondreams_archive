Leading Environmentalists Urge Senate to Oppose Norton
 
WASHINGTON - January 15 - The following was released today by Friends of the Earth:

January 11, 2001

The Honorable Jeff Bingaman
Chairman, Senate Committee on Energy and Natural Resources
703 Hart Senate Office Building
Washington, DC 20510

Dear Senator Bingaman:

On behalf of the thousands of members of Friends of the Earth, I would like to formally express this organization's opposition to the nomination of Gale Norton as Secretary of the United States Department of Interior. Ms. Norton's background and stances on the key issues of environmental stewardship signal that her policy initiatives would be a giant step away from recent progress and a downturn from a balanced approach towards natural resource conservation.

The Department of the Interior is our nation's principal conservation agency with jurisdiction over roughly 450 million acres of Federal lands and about 3 billion acres of the Outer Continental Shelf. Its responsibilities include stewardship of 80 million acres of national parks, monuments and historical sites, over 264 million acres of public lands home to over 3,000 species of wildlife, and the 92 million acre National Wildlife Refuge System, which includes 509 national wildlife refuges and 37 wetland management districts. The appointment of Gale Norton as principal manager of these priceless natural treasures would be a direct affront on the spirit of environmental protection.

Ms. Norton's anti-environmental background includes:

-- Attorney General of Colorado, where she was a strong advocate of a state law that allows companies to conduct voluntary audits to determine their compliance with environmental requirements. This "self-audit" law also gives businesses immunity from litigation if they report and correct violations. This and similar laws have been opposed by the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA).

-- A Senior Attorney for the Mountain States Legal Foundation (MSLF), a non-profit law-firm dedicated to undoing public land protections and weakening environmental laws. During her tenure, MSLF worked on cases opposing Clean Air Act enforcement in Colorado and supporting drilling and mining in Wilderness Areas, among others. Other MSLF alumni include the controversial former Secretary of Interior, James Watt, whose sympathy towards private interests and irreverence for environmental laws earned him a reputation as an anti-environmental crusader.

-- National Chair of the Coalition for Republican Environmental Advocates (CREA), a front group posing as a pro-environment "grassroots organization." In fact, CREA was a Political Action Committee whose steering committee included registered lobbyists for the petroleum, mining, auto-making, firearms and alcoholic beverage interests. CREA was funded by Coors Brewing Company, the American Forest Paper Association, the Chemical Manufacturers Association, Amoco and similar corporations.

-- A member of the Board of Advisors for the DC-based Defenders of Property Rights, a legal foundation created to "counterbalance the governmental threat to private property as a result of a broad range of regulations." Defenders of Property Rights has aggressively advocated for "takings" clauses both in Congress and the courts.

-- Assistant Solicitor in the Department of Interior within the Reagan Administration, where she worked to persuade Congress to open the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge to oil drilling.

-- Current member of the Washington Legal Foundation's (WLF) Legal Advisory Board. WLF is a foundation whose stated mission is "to defend and promote the principles of free enterprise and individual rights," and advocates for the easing of "regulatory burdens" on industry, and limiting the legal liability of corporate wrongdoers. WLF's Environmental Law Project is involved in a variety of "property rights" lawsuits seeking to weaken environmental protections.

Gale Norton's recent and past affiliations show a consistent pattern of efforts to undermine and roll back the very environmental laws and protections she will be sworn to uphold. In light of this significant conflict of interests, Friends of the Earth urges you to oppose her nomination as Secretary of the United States Department of Interior.  

Sincerely,

Brent Blackwelder
President
Friends of the Earth
