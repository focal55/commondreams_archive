Sierra Club Blasts Murkowski Energy Bill as Irresponsible
 
WASHINGTON - February 26 - The Sierra Club called on Congress to reject the energy bill Senator Frank Murkowski (R-Alaska) introduced today, stating the legislation fails to solve America's energy problems. Instead of setting America on the path of a sustainable, clean and affordable energy future, the Sierra Club called the bill a polluter give-away.

"Senator Murkowski's bill is a give-away for fossil fuel producers," said Ann Mesnikoff, the Sierra Club Washington Representative. "All it leaves for renewable and clean energy are table scraps."

"Murkowski isn't introducing an energy bill, he's introducing an energy industry bill," said Melinda Pierce, Sierra Club Senior Washington Representative. "He wants more mines, more dams, and more drilling. Trying to drill our way out of energy problems creates more global warming, more air pollution and devastates our last wildlands. Given the nation's energy needs and the public's desire for environmental protection, this bill is irresponsible."

Showing that Senator Murkowski's plan is out of step with most Americans, the Sierra Club cited an Associated Press poll released February 2 that found 53 percent of Americans oppose oil drilling in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge, while only 33 percent support such a move.

The Murkowski bill masks itself as a response to both California's energy shortage and the nation's long-term energy needs. In reality, the bill increases dependence on fossil fuels, worsens air quality, exacerbates global warming and destroys national treasures such as those found in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge and western lands--while failing to solve our nation's energy problems.

Among the provisions contained in the bill that threaten public health and degrade our natural resources are those that: * Destroy the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge. According to an U.S. Geological Survey estimate the Arctic Refuge would only provide enough oil for 6-months' worth of American consumption and would take at least 8 years to come online. Developing oil in this unique wild area teeming with wildlife is too high a cost to pay when our energy needs can be fulfilled in other ways * Increase global warming pollution by focusing on the development of oil and coal that produce the most global warming gases * Allow automakers to sell more gas guzzlers by giving them important fuel economy credits for vehicles which could run on alternative fuel but never do * Restrict the U.S. Bureau of Land Management's (BLM) and Forest Service's authority to protect fish and wildlife, water quality, and other environmental values from oil and gas drilling projects * Increase subsidies to coal, oil, gas and nuclear industries * Exempt companies using so-called clean coal technologies from complying with certain Clean Air Act requirements.

The Sierra Club calls for energy solutions that are clean, green and long-lasting: increased efficiency, the development of renewable energy, and the replacement of older power plants with more efficient, less polluting power plants.

Forty percent of U.S. oil consumption comes from cars and light trucks.

Raising fuel economy standards for cars and light trucks would save more oil than we would get from the arctic, off-shore California, and the Persian Gulf imports combined.

"The sensible solution is to make cars, SUVs, and light trucks drive further on a gallon of gas," concluded Mesnikoff. "Boosting fuel economy will save more oil than we could ever drill in the Arctic Refuge, and we can start saving sooner. For energy security, and big savings at the gas pump, we need fuel efficient cars and SUVs. The Murkowski bill runs away from true efficiency measures and hides in a pool of oil industry contributions."

Murkowski's claim that his bill assists California by developing oil is inaccurate. California currently receives less than 1% of its electricity from oil. Developing oil in the Arctic Refuge will take 8-10 years and will do nothing to alleviate the current California energy situation.
