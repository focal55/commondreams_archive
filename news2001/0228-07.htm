Sierra Club Joins in Legal Actions Against Corporate Hog Operations
 
KANSAS CITY, MO - February 28 - The Sierra Club today joined with the Waterkeeper Alliance to announce that it is a "full partner" with the organizations bringing litigation against major pork producing corporations. The lawsuits aim to stop these factories from polluting America's waterways and fouling them with animal waste.

Robert F. Kennedy, Jr., Jan Schlichtmann, and Charles Speer announced the filing of multiple lawsuits across the United States against Smithfield operations on behalf of organizations such as the Sierra Club and other individual plaintiffs. Smithfield, a Virginia company, operates massive hog production operations in several states including North Carolina, Utah, Oklahoma and Missouri. It is the world's largest pork producer.

"These companies have fouled the air and water of rural America, and run family farmers out of the hog-raising business," stated Ken Midkiff, Clean Water Campaign Coordinator for the Sierra Club. "Since the government is not protecting our waterways from these polluters, we are forced to step in."

"Part of the problem is the revolving door between industry and regulatory agencies," continued Midkiff. "When enforcers turn into apologists, justice will not be served." Many of the people who are supposed to enforce the laws protecting our air and water against these factories are now working for the same corporations they were supposed to be regulating. In the state of Missouri, the former Director of the Department of Natural Resources is now the attorney for facilities acquired by Smithfield, and two former Assistant Attorneys General are acting as legal counsel for Premium Standard Farms.

The Sierra Club has been in the forefront of opposition to these large-scale animal factories. They have joined with rural communities and family farmers across the nation to rein in the intrusive facilities and stop their polluting practices. The Club has documented throughout the country the hundreds of thousands of tons of animal waste that have illegally entered our air and water from these massive facilities.

The lawsuits filed today include multiple pollution suits, as well as common law tort litigation and federal racketeering allegations under the country's organized crime statutes.

"These corporations have created what amounts to pollution factories, pumping out millions of tons of excrement which ends up in our rivers, streams and estuaries," Midkiff concluded. "We stand today arm in arm with rural communities, impacted family farmers and this team of attorneys with a common goal: Keep animal waste out of our water -- stop animal factory pollution."
