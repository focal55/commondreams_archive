Fight for Embattled Pacifica Radio Network Returns to Houston
 
HOUSTON - February 28 - Dozens of free speech activists will descend upon Houston this weekend to face off against the board of directors of the Pacifica Foundation, which runs the only independent radio network in the United States. Activists plan teach-ins and protests during the board meeting, which is the first since the board authorized a hostile take-over of WBAI-FM in New York City.

"Quite simply, the Pacifica board has been hijacked by a small clique that has more in common with corporate vultures than with working-class America. That clique has illegally changed the Foundation's by-laws, and during the past two years it has methodically sought to squash dissent throughout the network. This group does not respect free speech. It does not respect labor or civil rights. It does not even practice due process for its own managers. And it is now seeking to radically alter Pacifica's by-laws to pave the way for the selling of one or more stations," said Juan Gonzalez, an award-winning Pacifica reporter who resigned on-air and started a corporate campaign to oust the Pacifica leadership. Gonzalez will be in Houston this weekend as will fired journalists from WBAI.

The Pacifica Foundation is a nonprofit organization that controls the licenses of five radio stations: WPFW in Washington, DC, WBAI in NY, KPFA in Berkeley, KPFT in Houston, and KPFK in Los Angeles. Over 85% of its revenues come from listeners.

Pacifica's leaders have come under fire in recent months for staging a hostile take-over of WBAI radio in New York City in December 2000, firing the station1s general manager and program director, and banning several long-time station staff from entering the station. The board authorized similar actions at Berkeley1s KPFA in July 1999, when it used armed guards to close down KPFA for several weeks. At its board meeting this weekend, the board may consider a proposal to change its bylaws to allow a small group of board members to sell off KPFA or WBAI.

In response to Pacifica's actions, listeners, station board members, and several dissenting national board members have filed lawsuits against the Foundation. Pacifica's freelance reporters are on strike, demanding an end to censorship and a return to the network's progressive values. And listeners throughout the United States have conducted a relentless campaign of protest against the organization's board members, including numerous acts of non-violent civil disobedience and a boycott of Pacifica1s recent fund drive.
