John Lennon Tribute Concert Benefit to Promote a Non-Violent World Rescheduled for October 2
 
WASHINGTON - September 19 - A John Lennon tribute concert to benefit the Brady Center to Prevent Gun Violence and the Million Mom March Foundation, originally scheduled for September 20, has been rescheduled for October 2 due to the horrific terrorist attacks of September 11.

The event, which will take place at Radio City Music Hall in New York City, is now dedicated to the victims of this overwhelming tragedy, and to generating support for a non-violent world. A portion of the proceeds from the concert will be donated to the disaster relief efforts. The concert will be hosted by Kevin Spacey and will feature performances by Alanis Morissette, Cyndi Lauper, Dave Matthews, The Isley Brothers, Lou Reed, Marc Anthony, Moby, Nelly Furtado, Shelby Lynne, Stone Temple Pilots, Yolanda Adams, and many others.

"In the wake of the tragic events last week in New York and Washington, D.C., it has been brought home, for many of us very personally, that every life is important," said Michael D. Barnes, President of the Brady Center. "Not one life should be lost to senseless acts of violence. Now more than ever, we must redouble our efforts to prevent gun violence in America. Our work to prevent deaths in America must continue.

"Like the rest of the nation, our hearts and thoughts are with the victims of this unspeakable crime and with the families they have left behind. Therefore, in addition to the general concert proceeds that will be donated to the disaster relief efforts, the Brady Center will contribute additional funds from every ticket we sell for this benefit concert to support the victims and their families."

The concert, entitled COME TOGETHER: A Night For John Lennon's Words & Music, Dedicated to New York & Its People, is hosted by Turner Network Television (TNT), in association with Yoko Ono Lennon and Ken Ehrlich Productions. The concert will take place at Radio City Music Hall at 8 p.m. on October 2, and will be broadcast live on TNT.

In addition to the Brady Center to Prevent Gun Violence united with the Million Mom March, the concert will benefit relief organizations including the American Red Cross, September 11th Fund, International Association of Fire Fighters, New York Fraternal Order of Police WTC Fund, The National Organization for Victim Assistance, and The Twin Towers Fund, and the Violence Policy Center.

A limited number of tickets for the benefit concert are still available from The Brady Center to Prevent Gun Violence. For additional information, please contact Kim Taylor at 202-218-4646, or go to www.bradycampaign.org/events.
