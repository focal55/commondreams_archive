Dramatic Growth of Orphaned Children Offers New Challenge to AIDS Crisis
FXB Association to Unveil Scope of Crisis, Announces Innovative Programs
Global Advocate for Orphans Albina du Boisrouvray to Visit Washington DC
Premier New Film, Discuss New Initiatives
 
WASHINGTON - May 23 - While protests mount over the lack of money allocated to medicines for AIDS treatment, the dramatic growth of children orphaned by AIDS threatens to become a global economic and security issue. Albina du Boisrouvray, President and founder of the François-Xavier Bagnoud Association and Foundation, will brief media on the scope of the AIDS orphan crisis and announce new initiatives to bring the issue before international bodies, on Wednesday, May 30, 11:00 AM. She will also premier a powerful new video on Nkosi Johnson, the South African youth who has become a voice of AIDS orphans

Du Boisrouvray, a leading expert on programs for children with AIDS and passionate advocate for children's rights, will reveal the scope of this"hidden problem" which has been outside the AIDS discourse that revolves around prevention and treatment. She will outline FXB Association projects that are already working in the field and announce new initiatives for global action on the AIDS orphan crisis.

“We must reel these children back into society, into caring communities and loving families to enable them to gain access to support, education, HIV prevention, and health care so that these children can become the productive citizens needed to lead their society into the future.” said du Boisrouvray.

FXB Association will also present NKOSI: A VOICE OF AFRICA'S AIDS ORPHANS, a film tribute to the life and work of the world's youngest AIDS advocate, Nkosi Johnson. The video, produced by Globalvision, features Nkosi's groundbreaking speech before thousands in Durban, South Africa.

12-year-old Nkosi's potent advocacy for the tens of millions of other AIDS-affected children in Southern Africa less fortunate than he, has put a human face on the tragedy and brought attention to this under-examined aspect of the AIDS crisis in Africa. It also features comments by Albina du Boisrouvray and Nelson Mandela.

The screening and briefing will be held at the Institute for Policy Studies, 733 15th Street NW, Washington, DC. For more information about the François-Xavier Bagnoud Association and Foundation see www.fxb.org. Its work on orphans appears at www.orphans.fxb.org.

The Countess Albina du Boisrouvray, who recently was awarded France's Legion of Honor for her innovative programs relating to children with AIDS, will be available for interviews by contacting Karen Conner, 202-667-2342 or 202-253-9784 (cell).
