Democrats.com Calls on Congress to Reject Florida's Electoral Votes
 
NEW YORK - January 4 - Democrats.com, the largest independent community of Democratic voters and activists, today called on Congress to reject Florida's 25 electoral votes. Congress will meet in a joint session at 1 p.m. Saturday to select the next President by counting the Electoral votes. Under the Constitution, Congress alone has the power to reject invalid Electors.

Democrats.com is organizing rallies at the district offices of Senators and Representatives on Friday at 12 noon, including both Democrats (Barbara Boxer, Chuck Schumer, Dick Durbin, Zell Miller, Carrie Meek, and Robert Wexler) and Republicans (Rick Santorum). Democrats.com is also sponsoring a rally outside Congress on Saturday starting at noon. "We call upon all Americans who are outraged by the election to call and visit the offices of their representatives and tell them that Florida's Electors were stolen, not won, and should therefore be rejected by Congress on Saturday," said Democrats.com co-founder Bob Fertik.

"Our investigations and those of independent news organizations have revealed significant evidence of voter intimidation, deliberate fraud, and a conspiracy to suppress voter turnout by illegal methods," said Democrats.com co-founder David Lytel. Under constitutional procedures established more than 100 years ago, the electoral votes of any state may be challenged by any member of the House of Representatives in conjunction with any member of the Senate. The challenge requires that each house debate for two hours and decide by majority vote.

The more than 15,000 members and subscribers of Democrats.com contributed more than $30,000 to enable the organization to hire lawyers and investigators to uncover evidence of official misconduct in Florida's presidential election. The complete catalogue of crimes against democracy in Florida is presented at http://www.trustthepeople.com

"With all due respect to Vice President Gore, we believe that it is the duty of the American people assembled in Congress to determine how much election fraud we as a nation are prepared to accept," said Fertik. "The continued tabulation of votes in Florida -- blocked for so long by Republicans -- is making it clear beyond a doubt that Al Gore won Florida by several thousand votes. The evidence shows that Republicans went to extraordinary lengths to disenfranchise Democrats in general and African American Democrats in particular," he added. "History is watching, and Congress must take a stand for the sake of our Democracy," he concluded.
