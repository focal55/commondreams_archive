2,000 Lbs. of Petitions, One Message:
Senate Should Vote 'No' on Ashcroft
 
WASHINGTON - January 23 - As the Senate debates the fate of the highly charged John Ashcroft nomination, the Senators' constituents are weighing in. On Capitol Hill today, boxloads of petitions urging the Senate not to confirm Ashcroft as U.S. Attorney General are being delivered to every Senator. The petitions, literally a ton of them unveiled at a Capitol Hill news conference, were signed online by more than 130,000 Americans at People For the American Way's dedicated website, OpposeAshcroft.com, in just the first ten days of its operation.

The petitions delivered today were signed online between Jan. 10 and 19 in an unprecedented outpouring of response to the controversial nomination. PFAW's website has been flooded with visitors who come to get the troubling facts about John Ashcroft's 6-year record as a U.S. Senator and his 16 years as Missouri's Governor and Attorney General before that. Even though PFAW did not do any advertising to promote the site, traffic has grown through word of "mouse" over the internet, with the numbers of petition signers swelling to well over 2,000 per hour by the end of last week.

"John Ashcroft's record shows that he has, time and again, set himself against fundamental American freedoms, fairness, and equal opportunity. He has distorted the facts about those whose disagree with him on the issues, as well as the facts about his own record," said Ralph G. Neas, president of People For the American Way. "What these petitions are saying today is: John Ashcroft is the wrong man to entrust with our fundamental freedoms."

At the news conference, Neas also announced that PFAW is preparing to supplement its internet campaign with a print advertising campaign to be unveiled later this week.

Petitioners from every state have responded with alarm not only to Ashcroft's extreme record on abortion rights, school desegregation, civil rights, gun control, and other key issues, but also to his attempts to mislead the Senate about his record and actions when he testified before the Judiciary Committee last week.

Neas noted at today's news conference that other organizations that oppose Ashcroft have also had similar responses from their constituents. "By the most conservative estimates," he said, "over half a million Americans have already contacted their Senators -- by petition, letter, postcard, e-mail, or phone -- urging a "no" vote. And these are just the ones we know about."

"We've never seen anything like this outpouring of concern," Neas continued. "We have a very activist-oriented website and high traffic year-'round, but in just twelve days our website has received over a million hits -- nearly matching our volume for all twelve months of last year. There is a lot of unhappiness about this nomination and Senators should pay attention to this unprecedented public reaction."

Even as the first batch of OpposeAshcroft.com petitions are delivered today, tens of thousands more Americans a day are clicking on the website to send their Senators an anti-Ashcroft message. PFAW has promised the petitioners that it will continue to deliver newly signed petitions as they come in.

PFAW's OpposeAshcroft.com website contains the organization's research reports detailing Ashcroft's troubling record as a public official along with its latest report, reviewing his efforts to mischaracterize or explain away that record in testimony before the Senate Judiciary Committee, "Words vs. Deeds: John Ashcroft's Attempt to Recast His Own Public Record and Statements."
