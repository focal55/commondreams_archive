Religious and Civil Rights Leaders Examine Treatment and Role of Muslims in Politics
 
WASHINGTON - February 8 - Karamah: Muslim Women Lawyers for Human Rights and The Interfaith Alliance Foundation (TIAF) will bring together religious leaders and civil rights advocates for a roundtable discussion on Capitol Hill to examine the increasing visibility and role of Muslims in the American political process. Religious leaders and civil rights advocates will also provide analysis of the civil liberties concerns of the diverse Muslim community. The event will be held Monday, Feb. 12 in Room 2105 of the Rayburn House Office Building from 10 a.m. until noon.

Cited as one of the fastest growing religious communities in the nation, there are an estimated six million Muslims drawn from various ethnic and racial communities living within the United States. Increasingly, Muslims are cited by politicians and elected leaders as contributing members of the religious landscape. Despite this recognition, however, the actual treatment of Muslims during the most recent election cycle continues to raise serious questions about the impact of negative stereotypes on the substantive participation of Muslims in American politics.

When: Monday, Feb. 12 at 10 a.m. Continental breakfast provided

Where: Capitol Hill -- 2105 Rayburn House Office Building

Who:
-- Sharifa Alkhateeb, president, North American Council of Muslim Women
-- Asim Ghafoor, managing director, AG Consulting Group
-- Rabbi Jack Moline, Agudas Achim Congregation of Northern Virginia, board member of The Interfaith Alliance
-- Laura Murphy, director, ACLU Washington Office

The Interfaith Alliance Foundation (TIAF) is a non-partisan, educational institution dedicated to promoting the positive role of religion as a healing and constructive force in American life. TIAF provides training, education, research and other support to a growing grassroots network of clergy and people of faith who are working to promote social justice in their communities. For more information about TIA, visit www.interfaithalliance.org.

Since its inception in 1993, KARAMAH: MUSLIM WOMEN LAWYERS FOR HUMAN RIGHTS has dedicated itself to advancing the voice of the Muslim community on matters of human rights, tolerance and respect. Karamah is founded upon the idea that education, dialogue and action can counter the destructive effects of ignorance, silence and prejudice. Through education and advocacy, Karamah seeks to support Muslim communities in America and abroad in pursuit of justice.
