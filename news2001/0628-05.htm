Sierra Club Extols Congress For Protecting the Great Lakes From Damaging Oil Drilling
Congress Stands Firm Against President Bush's Destructive Energy Policy
 
WASHINGTON - June 28 - The Sierra Club today applauded the House of Representatives for voting to protect the Great Lakes from destructive oil and gas drilling. The House approved an amendment, introduced by Reps. David Bonior (D-MI), Marcy Kaptur (D-OH), Steven LaTourette (R-OH) and Bart Stupak (D-MI), to the Energy and Water Appropriations Bill (H.R. 2311) that would prevent drilling for oil or gas beneath the Great Lakes. This amendment passed by a vote of 265 to 157.

"Americans don't want oil drills looming on our beaches or oil spills ruining our Great Lakes," said Carl Pope, executive director of the Sierra Club. "By approving the Bonior amendment, the House has rightly protected another fragile coastline that President Bush has supported opening up to destructive development. Instead of President Bush's drill, dig and destroy approach to finding fossil fuel, Americans want a balanced approach that gives us quicker, cleaner, cheaper and safer solutions, like energy-efficient technologies, renewable power like solar and wind, and responsible production."

"The approaching Independence Day holiday is a perfect reminder that millions of Americans rely on the Great Lakes for drinking water and recreation. Drilling in these delicate areas would be a slap in the face to Americans who want to protect our Great Lakes' beaches and fragile shores. We can meet our energy needs without ruining our favorite beaches and coastlines," continued Pope. "We applaud those Representatives who stood up for all Americans and blocked efforts to destroy our Great Lakes and coastlines. Americans owe a debt of gratitude to Reps. Bonior, Kaptur, LaTourette and Stupak for leading this battle to protect our Great Lakes for our families and for our future."

The vote today to prevent oil drilling in the Great Lakes is the latest rejection of President Bush's irresponsible energy plan. Last week, the House struck down efforts to allow drilling for oil and mining for coal in our National Monuments and off the coast of Florida. The House also voted to force mining companies to pay to clean up their own messes.
