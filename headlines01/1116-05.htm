Published on Friday evening, November 16, 2001 by Reuters
U.S. Bombs Strike Mosque as Ramadan Starts
 
WASHINGTON - U.S. Air Force warplanes damaged a mosque in eastern Afghanistan as the Muslim holy month of Ramadan began on Friday when a malfunctioning bomb missed its target, the U.S. military said.

The errant 500-pound bomb was one of three dropped on a building complex in Khowst, close to the eastern border with Pakistan, said the Tampa, Florida-based Central Command, which is coordinating the U.S. campaign.

Two of the bombs struck a ``known facility'' of al Qaeda, the guerrilla network of Osama bin Laden, suspected mastermind of the Sept. 11 attacks on the United States, a statement said.

``The third bomb suffered a guidance malfunction and missed the facility,'' it said. ``The resulting explosion damaged a mosque.''

Friday marked the advent of Ramadan in Afghanistan, a time that mosques were likely to be crowded.

The Central Command said it did not immediately know the amount of damage caused by the bombing, which it said took place about 10 a.m. EST Friday -- early evening in Afghanistan.

``We are unaware of any injuries as a result of the errant bomb,'' it added.
