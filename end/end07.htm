Expect Democracy

What a great, grand treat to come to the screen and be greeted by dozens and hundreds - come on, let's make it thousands! - of messages from sensible, thinking citizens from all over the nation. We're here and we ain't going away!

The election, for me, was not a disappointment because I never expected much from the voting 45%-of-the-electorate Republocrats. If (as Jim Hightower suggests) half those voting for either of the two corporate candidates were actually voting against, or in fear of, the other, the Bush-boy's mandate is about 11 or 12% of the voting-eligible population. What's that, maybe 6 or 7% of the whole nation? Indeed watching those two scoundrels and their truthspeak talking robots was better than a carny show. What lovely 'though hideous irony to watch these hypocrite Republicans become the horror envisioned by Orwell.

And the Congress - best damned hucksterism since the impeachment posturing. They no longer need a clergyman opening sessions. It's far too late for that. They need a patent medicine barker. Maybe a compromise: Upjohn hires Pat Robertson who tag-teams with Jerry Falwell. Thurmond in one corner, dozing off, chasing small animals in his dreams, drooling on himself and DeLay on the opposite end, snorting rat poison or whatever it was that turned him into whatever he is. James Carvell (or however his name is spelled) referees in seriously baggy, sequined tights borrowed from Gov. Jesse Ventura, Rev. Jesse Jackson makes and plays the odds with his eyes on his own prize, and Greenspan fixes the ticket prices. And so on and so on and so on. You fill in the blanks.

As for the press - well, it is the press and not much can be expected. It's like the whole kit and caboodle got dropped on it's head in its infancy.

The real tragedy of this comedy was the disgrace of the Supreme Court. What shame and what a shame. Enough said.

Meanwhile, my friends, we continue. I agree, don't mourn, organize. Don't be bitter, let's get better. The issues are now in the open. Four years ago ( I accidentally hit the "t" key and wrote "four tears ago." That would be appropriate as well wouldn't it? But as we've said, let's not mourn, let's organize) ... four years ago the populace did not know the word, much less the danger of, corporatization. Now, everyone is aware of it. Four years hence, they'll understand "deep democracy." And four years after that we'll all take back control of our lives, our environment, our government, our cities and our souls. Freedom's not complicated. We just have to demand it with every act, every thought, every breath. Nothing less will do.

Thanks and kudos to CommonDreams. I hope you can keep this comment feature going permanently or on specific issues as they arise.

Let's keep the dialogues going. Start your own topic. Visit the Alliance for Democracy - Minnesota website ( www.afd-minnesota.org ) and join the discussion boards. It's free, it's fast, it's uncensored (except for a couple of specific words), and it's open to all worldwide. Let's continue these exchanges, here at CommonDreams and on the boards.

In solidarity and Alliance
