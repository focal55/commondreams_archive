Marc Shuler

As usual, the Democrats have sold out the very people who need and depend on them the most. Quiet and stability are considered higher values than Democracy and Freedom.

That's what this post election fight was about. Whether or people not people have the right to come out and vote without facing intimidation and a system rigged to keep them from voting. And then whether or not those people have the right to have their votes counted as equal. It seems without doubt that voters in minority or poor neighborhoods faced far greater barriers to voting than voters in white and wealthier neighborhoods. And it seems without doubt that voters in white and wealthier neighborhoods had their votes counted at a much higher rate than those from minority and poor neighborhoods.

Thus the question in the days after this election was less about who had won the close vote. It was more about who had the right to vote and have their votes counted. And as usual, even though the voters who were denied equal protection under the laws and regulation of the system were predominately Democratic voters, the Democratic Party leadership failed to raise the fight to make sure these voters had the right to vote and the right to have their votes counted.

Given the lack of equal protection under the Florida voting system, it was an irony worthy of a Greek tragedy that the Supreme Court sited concerns over equal protection as the reason for the delaying and canceling the recounting of votes in Florida. Particularly since the Democratic Party had failed to raise the equal protection issues of Democratic voters as an issue in their challenges and protests to the election result.

The Democratic Party viewed the question of who won or who lost as their primary concern. Questions about fairness or democracy were germane only to the extent they aided their quest for that narrow goal. So its no surprise at all that Al Gore now calls for unity, stability and patriotism now that he has failed in his quest for that narrow goal. In doing so he once again abandons the fight for fairness and democracy for the American citizens who were intimidated, discriminated against and had their votes stolen. Al Gore's promise that he'll "Fight for You!" lasted only as long as it was useful to his quest for victory.

Unfortunately this has become standard procedure for the Democratic Party.

Politicized efficiency won this election! And to that victor belongs the spoils.

If anything, this election has been a mirror that reveals the degree to which the system and its players are ill equipped to deal with the living forces and energies of an evolving democracy. That same mirror reveals to us the degree of work each of us needs to do to build democracy.

Some 25 years ago, while a UCLA undergraduate I found myself reading the words of the poet-sage Jalal-Ud-Dim Rumi. His words of life philosophy appeared on the opening page of Justice William O. Douglas' autobiography Go East, Young Man. In Rumi's words... "All of your anxiety is because of your desire for harmony. Seek disharmony; then you will gain peace." I had just come upon the book by chance while seated at the university's research library. I loved the quote every bit as much as I did the humane spirit of Douglas. I reacted to the quote by laughing out loud. My laugh was so loud that heads turned as it shattered the silence that dominated the library halls. It's 25 years later. I'm not laughing!

No doubt, the current majority members of the "anxious" United States Supreme Court didn't get the point that living democracy's earned rite of passage is often disharmonious. We live in a strange time. This election has truly shown us to ourselves. We claim democracy, yet we shun the dissent that democracy requires for the evolution of justice. We promise democracy, yet we hear high priests within our "legal and political culture" make claims that counting votes is "irreparably damaging" to the efforts of some of those who seek election based on votes. Wow!

I always thought it was dictatorships that shook in their boots when they had to face the consequences of open processes, truth, and democratized vote counting. I still suspect this is so!

We had better shake ourselves of this "anxiety" over democracy's often messy processes. Results without regards to the means is what has always fueled unquestioning dictatorships in the ugly chapters of the human story.

Don't fear democracy! Be aware of those who fear democracy!

In community...
