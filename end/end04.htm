I am not an American citizen so I wasn't allowed to vote (I am employed in the IT industry on an H1B visa). I followed the elections quite closely and even though I couldn't care less I was quite shocked at what I saw. I guess I don't have to convince anybody here that the elections did not comply to what most Western countries think a democracy should look like. But being bitter about it doesn't help, either. I remember in 1994 most Germans were hoping the federal elections would bring a change in power. They didn't and it was widely believed the following four years would be wasted. In a sense, they were. But people started working on making sure that the year 1998 would bring the change everybody had hoped for and it did. I guess America has to learn a lesson or two from that (even though it likes to lecture those naive foreigners about how to run things). If people want a change in 2004, they have to start working on it RIGHT NOW. All the talk about "Healing the division" and all that crap doesn't lead anywhere! People have to stand up and speak their minds! And people like Nader and the Green Party have to start preparing for the next power struggle. Being a nation might be important for Americans but developing a sane culture of dissent and political struggle is way more important. What we've seen so far is NO sane culture of dissent and struggle. It's insane infighting by two political parties. And moving everybody to the middle doesn't help either. Two political parties with basically the same positions on most issues amount to nothing else but a pseudo one-party system. People have to start speaking up now. Positions people don't dare to stand for any longer (unions, environmental standards, wages, racism, poverty, etc. etc. etc.) have to be addressed! Four years is a good time to make these topics so common that in four years, more people will vote their conscience. I fear as a result of the absurd spectacle which just finished more people will stop being involved and less people will go and vote. That is *NOT* the right choice and everybody has to go out and tell people why it is important to get involved to make a change happen.

This election was stolen, but not just at the ballot box. It was stolen from US (the People) long ago. Selection process for candidates? Rigged. Debate policy? Media access? Rigged to exclude the people's voice. Election process? Rigged to exclude a real choice.

The entire process is rigged to ensure that nobody without access to hundreds of millions of dollars in dirty money could ever have a chance. "We the people" were doomed to lose this election, no matter who won.

I am disgusted and outraged by the blatant attempts to disenfranchise large numbers of mostly poor and Black voters in Florida, and I believe there should be serious hearings on a state and national level to investigate the numerous allegations of suspicious and possibly criminal irregularities.

I offer no apology for my vote, which went for the only two candidates I could in good conscience vote for and support: Ralph Nader and Winona LaDuke.

True, there are some important differences between Mr. Bush and Mr. Gore, and it's scary to me that millions of Americans would place the fate of their homeland in the hands of a selfserving, semiliterate son of a CIA Director.

Perhaps we shouldn't be too harsh on the younger Bush; at least the public knows the names of all the people he's helped to kill, and where the bodies are buried. Not so with Daddy Bush.

Gore couldn't win my vote without addressing the need for radical reforms in the political and electoral process. As a Democrat Party politician, he surely wouldn't support the establishment of an independent progressive political movement that will:

*support deep and significant reforms in the electoral process, including public financing of elections, no more corporate money (legalized bribery), free or low cost media access, same-day voter registration, instant runoff voting, a binding "none-of-the-above" choice on ballots, new & more accurate vote tabulating technology, and the implementation of voting methods that allow for proportional representation

*support a plan for universal, cradle to grave, single payer health care with an emphasis on prevention; put an end to the destructive effect of corporate profits in medicine; more public interest funding and control over medical research and drug/treatment development; end "market medicine" monopoly;

*support a living wage for all workers and a repeal of the Taft-Hartley Act; support passage of a workers' rights act;

*support a plan to provide universal access to higher education; opportunities for all who can make the effort, regardless of financial status;

*support a comprehensive affirmitive action plan that works towards FULL EMPLOYMENT at a LIVING WAGE and UNIVERSAL ACCESS TO HIGHER EDUCATION instead of pitting workers and minorities against each other;

*support a new foreign policy towards "Native Nations" within US borders, one based in cooperation instead of colonialism; a policy which respects the sovereignty of Native peoples and their right to govern themselves; and one which returns some of the vast lands stolen at the point of a gun in blatant violation of sacred treaties.

*support an end the failed "war on drugs" and the institution of a new policy of treatment and rehab on demand instead of incarceration for nonviolent offenders;

*support a massive demilitarization of U.S. foreign policy, beginning with closing the School of the Americas and an end to "Plan Colombia."

*call for an end to the death penalty

*call for an end to the genocidal sanctions against Iraq

*call for an end to the embargo against Cuba and the normailzation of relations with our neighbor to the south

*call for an end to spending on so-called "star wars" anti-missile systems and for the placement of weapons in space

*support total equal rights for gay & lesbian citizens, including the right to legal civil unions and marriage

*support strict enforcement of environmental laws and anti-trust laws, and fund 100,000 new investigators and prosecutors to fight high-level corporate crime

The list could go on and on, but my point is this:

Neither Bush nor Gore could support ANY of these positions.

Gore actually argued in the debates that he would spend MORE on the Pentagon than Bush! That may appeal to the weapons merchants and the contractors and corporations who profit from expanding the war machine, but it creates a moral dilemma for me. As much as I despise what Dubya and his daddy stand for, I can't accept the "lesser of two evils." We can do better.

Honest liberals and progressives in the Democratic Party or any other party should brace themselves for a tough battle ahead. And PLEASE try to remember that it wasn't Ralph Nader and Winona LaDuke, or those who voted for them, who 'spoiled' this election. And the Green Party ain't going away...

Well, it's settled and in a perverse way we are glad to be finally rid of the courtroom dramas, appeals, counter appeals and pregnant chads. It's the fact that Democrats moved their party so far to the right, they became almost indistinguishable from the Republicans and thus the voters were evenly split. Instead of hating and discrediting Nader, they should be thankful that he raised issues which truly need to be addressed if we are to remain a democracy. At one point we thought the way to solve this post-election hangover would be to toss out both Bush and Gore and make Ralph Nader president! We are looking at a wasted 4 years and gearing up towards 2004 for some semblance of progressive candidates. Finally, what this fiasco ought to do is to standardize ballots throughout the country and we are not opposed to doing it with federal dollars. It is absolutely necessary.
