I am a proud Nader voter. And while I despise George W. and everything he stands for, I believe his narrow and illegitimate election may be the best outcome--given the options--in the long run. Bush will be a weak and ineffective executive--not only because he lost the popular vote, and squeaked by in Florida through the efforts of the Republican partisans on the supreme court. But he will also be forced to deal with an evenly divided Senate.

On the other side of the coin, so long as Al Gore is not resurrected in 2004, the Dems can thank their lucky stars that they narrowly averted a Gore administration. We have suffered through eight years of Clinton/Gore's conservative take-over of the party. Think of the damage done during those eight years--NAFTA, the destruction of the welfare system, the Crime Bill, the Immigration Bill, a broadening of the "drug war" and soaring incarceration rates, to name a few items.

But this conservative coup can be written off as a blip. I believe that Clinton was elected twice largely due to his skills as a communicator--much as Reagan was. Clinton was, if nothing else, a very good politician. Had Gore been elected, however, many would have come away with the message that the only way for the Democrats to win the White House is to swing to the right--if you can't beat 'em join 'em."Triangulation worked for Clinton and it worked for Gore," they'd say.

And what's more, Gore would be the presumptive nominee in 2004, thus guaranteeing eight years of conservative rule. That, I believe, would spell the end of the Democratic party as a progressive force.

Now, the Democrats are faced with a stark choice. Will the party turn again to the conservatives in the DLC or will the progressive forces within the party win this internecine war? Only time will tell. But at least there is now some reason for hope. The 2004 election begins today. Who will it be?
