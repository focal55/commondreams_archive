President Clinton Just Tore To Shreds The Last Remnant Of The Peace Dividend
by Matthew Rothschild  
 

President Clinton just tore to shreds of the last remnant of the peace dividend. In his latest budget, he proposes increasing Pentagon spending by $13.5 billion to a total of $291 billion. When you throw in another $14 billion from the Department of Energy for nuclear weapons, we're spending more than $300 billion on so-called defense.

And for what?

We spend more on our military than the next twelve biggest militaries combined.

We don't need $300 billion to defend the citizens or the territory of the United States. We could do that for far, far less.

Peace activist Randy Forsberg calculates that we could do that for about one-tenth the amount. She'd add about $50 billion for international peacekeeping efforts. So, by her numbers, the Pentagon budget should be $80 billion.

The rightwing libertarian group, the Cato Institute, calls for a Pentagon budget of $175 billion.

The Center for Defense Information puts it at $220 billion.

So why did Clinton come in with his $300 billion figure?

Because the Pentagon insists on training missions, exercises, or permanently stationing troops in more than 100 countries.

Because the Pentagon insists on being prepared to fight two wars simultaneously in separate parts of the globe.

Because the Pentagon insists on maintaining thousands of nuclear warheads and the capacity to launch them from land or sea.

Because the Pentagon insists on a developing missile defense system, which will make us less--not more--secure, since it will antagonize Russia and China, the only significant threats on the horizon.

Oh, there's one other reason: Because the arms companies have Washington wired.

And don't look for the leading Presidential candidates to throw the circuit-breaker.

McCain and Bush want an even fatter Pentagon budget, while Gore and Bradley are content to leave it waddling around at its current girth.

If we had real choices and real debates in this country, then maybe we'd be able to enjoy a peace dividend.

But the Democrats, under Clinton's tutelage, have erased just about every distinction between the two major parties on "defense" issues.

Looks like we'll all be paying for that for some time to come.
