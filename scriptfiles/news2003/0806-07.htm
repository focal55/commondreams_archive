Bush's August Trips Focus on Environment - As President Travels 'America the Beautiful,' Americans Ask That He Protect It
   WASHINGTON - August 6 - Environmental issues will be the focus of a large portion of the August trips that President Bush takes from his Crawford, Texas ranch. To date, the President has not made protecting the environment a top priority, but his travel schedule for the next three weeks could reflect a growing political concern. As Republican pollster Frank Luntz noted earlier this year: "The environment is probably the single issue on which Republicans in general -- and President Bush in particular -- are most vulnerable."

In the few times that President Bush has made an environmental address, his speech has been marked by lofty rhetoric and promises. Whether it's "Healthy Forests," "Clear Skies," or promising to protect Yellowstone National Park, the Administration has made sweeping promises. Now Americans are calling for the President's actions to match his words.

Unfortunately, the Bush Administration has a consistent record of eroding environmental safeguards, hindering public participation, and handing over large portions of our National Forests to private, commercial interests.

The President's Schedule: As of today, these are the scheduled environmental events.

August 11: The President will visit Arizona and the area affected by the Aspen wildfire near Tucson. Later that day, the President also will visit Colorado.

August 15: The President will visit the Santa Monica Mountains National Recreation Area to speak about conservation issues.

August 21: The President will travel to Portland, OR to discuss forest issues.

August 22: President Bush will be in Seattle, WA to speak about preserving salmon and their habitat.
