Richmond Rally to Denounce New VA Tobacco Company 'Licensed to Kill, Inc'; 200 Teens to Participate
   WASHINGTON - April 21 - News Advisory:

On March 19, Licensed to Kill, Inc, a new tobacco company with the explicit purpose of "manufacturing and marketing tobacco in a way that each year kills over 400,000 Americans and 4.5 million other persons worldwide" was incorporated in the Commonwealth of Virginia. On Wednesday, April 23rd, tobacco control advocates, including 200 teens, will gather the Virginia State Capitol to denounce the company, call for greater controls on corporations that kill, and stand in solidarity with people around the world who are resisting the tobacco industry's global expansion.

WHAT: Rally to denounce License to Kill, Inc

WHO: Speakers from Across the Country
-- Over 200 teen tobacco control advocates from across the U.S.
-- Licensed to Kill, Inc Corporate Executives, including:
-- Rich Fromdeth, CEO and President; Noam Morrels, Senior VP of Global Expansion; Corrie Prutspin, Senior VP of Corporate Communications;
-- Anna White, Coordinator, Global Partnerships for Tobacco Control/Essential Action
-- Robert Hinkley, lawyer and incorporator of Licensed to Kill, Inc
-- Anne Morrow Donley, President, Virginia GASP
-- Edward Sweda Jr, Senior Attorney, Tobacco Products Liability Project
-- Stacey Folsom, National Organizer, Infact

WHEN: 1 p.m., Wednesday, April 23rd

WHERE: Virginia State Capitol, Capitol Square (near Bell Tower), 9th Street and Bank Street, Richmond

NOTE: Rally will feature excellent visuals and photo opportunities!
