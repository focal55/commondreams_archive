Free Press Launches Congressional Call-In Campaign on Media Ownership
  

NORTHAMPTON, MA - July 8 - National media reform group Free Press announced the launch of a Congressional Call-in Campaign today urging members of both chambers to support a rollback of media ownership rule changes passed by the FCC on June 2.

"Over one million people commented to the FCC, and they spoke with one voice: do not loosen media ownership rules," said Josh Silver, Free Press Managing Director. "This campaign is designed to deliver that message to Congress loud and clear."

The campaign allows citizens from across the country to enter their zipcode on the website and receive talking points and phone numbers for their elected officials. Free Press urges everyone to participate by visiting www.mediareform.net/stopthefcc.

"For far too long, the FCC has only thought of the American public as consumers, and not citizens," said Free Press co-founder Robert McChesney. "The time is now for Congress to pick up the FCC's slack, listen to the people and vote for media diversity. Only with a media system that supports diverse media ownership can we have a functioning democracy."

The Congressional Call-In Campaign focuses on several bills dealing with the FCC rule changes:

* Senate Bill 1046, the "Preservation of Localism, Program Diversity, and Competition in Television Broadcast Act of 2003." This bill would preserve the rule that restricts one company to owning television stations that reach no more than 35 percent of U.S. TV households as well as the rule preventing one company from owning a broadcast station and a newspaper in the same market or a radio station and TV station in the same market. S 1046 would also require the FCC to hold at least five geographically diverse formal public hearings the next time an ownership rule change comes up for consideration.

S 1046 was passed out of the Senate Commerce Committee on June 19 (read the Free Press release); the campaign will put public pressure on Senators, particularly Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist, to have the bill brought for a full vote before the Senate.

* HR 2462, sponsored by Rep. Bernie Sanders (I-VT), rolls back all of the June 2nd rules, and HR 2052, sponsored by Reps. Richard Burr (R-NC) and John D. Dingell (D-MI), reverses the national audience cap rule from 45% of households back down to 35%. Call-in campaign participants are encouraged to phone Rep. Billy Tauzin (R-LA) in addition to their own Representatives, due to his stated opposition to media reform and key role as chair of the House Energy and Commerce Committee.
