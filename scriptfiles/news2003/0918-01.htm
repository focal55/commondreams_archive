Leading Constitutional Law Group Condemns Anti-Terrorism List
   NEW YORK - September 18 - Today, the leading constitutional and human rights law group, The Center for Constitutional Rights (CCR), condemned the use of a far-reaching anti-terrorism list ordered by President Bush, charging that the list unfairly casts suspicion on all those named.

“This list harks back to the days of J. Edgar Hoover when security threats were enough to completely strip Americans of their constitutional rights of free association,” said Michael Ratner, president, CCR. “It has no place in a country that values freedom of speech and due process.”

CCR lawyers say the list, which combines dozens of existing security lists including the much debated “no fly” list, will unfairly target persons who oppose government policies, especially its foreign policy in the Middle East.

When questioned about the guidelines for establishing the “no fly” list, officials from the Transportation Security Administration (TSA) acknowledged to CCR lawyers that there were no specific standard guidelines guiding agencies decisions to designate individuals. TSA officials also admitted that no mechanism had been developed for people to challenge the lists and seek to have their names removed.

“If secret government lists like these ---lists which target peace activists and others who seek only to speak their minds--- are allowed to exist, America as a land of liberty will no longer exist,” said Deputy Legal Director of CCR Barbara Olshansky.

Olshansky added that the list, which will be available to private employers, will be used against potential employees who will be perceived as a terror threat.
