Searching for Judges with Justice
Arkansans Stand Against Ultra-Conservative Judicial Nominees
   WASHINGTON - November 12 - News Advisory:

Prominent women and other leaders from Arkansas will speak out against President Bush's ultra-conservative judicial nominees tomorrow, explaining the futility of the 30-hour debate scheduled by Republicans in the U.S. Senate. Ninety-eight percent of Bush's judicial nominees have been confirmed, and only the ones whose public records and right-wing ideology put them far from the mainstream of American legal thought are subject to a filibuster. As this collection of environmental and women leaders clearly reveal, Republican attempts to portray opposition to the most extreme candidates as anti-woman - or anti-Christian, or racist -- are bogus, according to People for the American Way.

Who:

-- Hadley Glover, Arkansas coordinator, People For the American Way

-- Sarah Keith, president, Sierra Club of Arkansas

-- Lorraine Hatcher, attorney at law, Little Rock

-- Glen Hooks, regional conservation coordinator, Sierra Club

When: Thursday, Nov. 13, 11 a.m.

Where: Old Supreme Court Room, State Capitol Building, Little Rock, Ark.
