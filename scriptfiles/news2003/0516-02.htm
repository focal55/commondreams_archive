Planned Parenthood Statement on Senate Vote on Global AIDS Bill
   WASHINGTON - May 16 - The Following is a statement of Planned Parenthood Federation of America Vice President for Public Policy Susanne Martinez on Senate passage of global AIDS bill:

Congressional Republican leaders have shown once again that they are more interested in pandering to anti-family planning extremists than providing real solutions to a real crisis. They rebuffed efforts to ensure that women and men in the countries hardest hit by AIDS have access to honest, medically accurate information, and comprehensive prevention programs.

Early this morning the Senate passed a global AIDS bill, a piece of legislation that shows a new level of commitment to an international health crisis. The resources authorized in this legislation will provide treatment for millions of women and children with HIV/AIDS. Where it drastically falls short is in helping to prevent many of those women from getting the disease in the first place.

Instead, U.S. taxpayer dollars be able to go to organizations that not only spread false and misleading information about the effectiveness of condoms in preventing AIDS, but also deny young people any information at all.

Congressional leaders have squandered the opportunity, to pass legislation of lasting importance in the battle to end the AIDS pandemic, and women will pay the price.

Planned Parenthood Federation of America is the nation's largest and most trusted voluntary reproductive health care organization. Planned Parenthood affiliates serve nearly five million women, men and teenagers every year.
