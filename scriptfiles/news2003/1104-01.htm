Campaign Finance Reform Community To Announce National Campaign To Fix Presidential Public Finance System

WHEN:

WHERE:

Kellogg Room at the Wyndham Boston Hotel, 89 Broad Street, Boston, MA

Background:

Six national campaign finance reform organizations (Common Cause, Democracy 21, League of Women Voters, Public Campaign, Public Citizen, and U.S. Public Interest Research Group) will announce the launching of a project to fix the presidential public financing system. The groups formed the Presidential Public Financing Reform Project to promote major improvements to the presidential campaign finance system by reducing the role of special interest money in presidential elections, increasing the importance of public financing in the primaries, and making enactment of reforms a priority for the presidential candidates and for Congress.
