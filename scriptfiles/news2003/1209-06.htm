Military Families, Veterans Delegation Report Back from Fact Finding Mission in Iraq
   WASHINGTON - December 9 - A delegation of U.S. military family members and veterans, including members of Military Families Speak Out (MFSO) and Veterans for Peace (VFP), traveled to Iraq on a fact finding mission from Dec. 1-8 to see first-hand the reality being faced by U.S. troops and Iraqis. On Dec. 10 (International Human Rights Day), the delegation will hold a Press Conference at the National Press Club and will present a first hand account of their experience in Iraq.

The delegates left the United States on Saturday, Nov. 29, and arrived in Iraq on Dec. 1. During their stay, they met with representatives of the Iraqi Governing Council, human rights organizations, and women9s organizations. They visited hospitals, schools, and U.S. military installations. The delegation also had a meeting with Coalition Provisional Authority Administrator Paul Bremer.

WHO: Military Families and Veterans Delegation to Iraq (See List Below)

WHAT: A Press Conference on Military Families Delegation to Iraq

WHEN: Wednesday, Dec. 10, at 9 a.m. EST

WHERE: The National Press Club, Murrow Room, 529 14th St., NW, Washington, D.C.

Speakers will include:

-- Medea Benjamin is the co-founder of Global Exchange, a San Francisco-based human rights organization, and co-organized the delegation to Iraq. She will facilitate the Press Conference.

-- Fernando Suarez del Solar, of Escondido, Calif., is the father of Marine Lance Cpl. Jesus Suarez, one of the first U.S. servicemen killed in Iraq on March 27, 2003.

-- Anabelle Valencia, of Tucson, Ariz., is a military mom and school teacher with a son and daughter in Iraq.

-- Sean Dougherty is a Vietnam Veteran whose 22-year-old daughter serves in Iraq.

-- Michael McPhearson is a Gulf War Veteran who lives in Newark, N.J., and has a son in the military.

-- John Grant is a Vietnam veteran from Philadelphia, Pa.

-- Mike Lopercio, is a businessman from Tempe, Ariz., whose son is stationed in Iraq.

-- Billy James Kelly is a Vietnam Veteran and a member of Veterans for Peace.

Upon their return, members of the delegation have requested meetings with U.S. and U.N. policymakers, including President Bush, National Security Advisor Condoleeza Rice, U.N. Secretary- General Kofi Annan, and U.S. Ambassador to the U.N. John Negroponte.

The trip is being organized by Global Exchange and the International Occupation Watch Center in conjunction with Military Families Speak Out and Veterans for Peace. Global Exchange is a human rights group based in San Francisco that sponsors educational trips to such countries as Afghanistan, Vietnam, and Israel/Palestine. The International Occupation Watch Center was established in July by international peace groups to monitor and report on the occupation of Iraq. Military Families Speak Out ( http://www.mfso.org ) is an organization of over 1,000 families across the country speaking out against the war in Iraq, who have family members in the Military. MFSO and Veterans for Peace ( http://www.veteransforpeace.org ) are founding organizations of the Bring them Home Now campaign ( http://www.bringthemhomenow.org ).
