Statement by AFL-CIO President John Sweeney on Obey-Miller Amendment to Block Bush Administration’s Proposed Cuts to Overtime Pay
   WASHINGTON - July 11 - We commend Congressmen David Obey (D-WI) and George Miller (D-CA) for taking swift action to try to block the Bush administration’s attack on overtime pay for millions of American workers. The Obey-Miller amendment to the Labor, HHS and Education appropriations bill would have denied funds to implement the proposed regulations to reclassify workers as exempt from overtime pay. We regret that the House narrowly defeated this amendment.

Many Democrats and Republicans have expressed outrage over the administration’s attempt to deny workers overtime pay to help support their families. By threatening to veto the spending bill because of the amendment to protect workers’ overtime pay, the administration continued to show a callous disregard for the millions of workers who depend on that extra income to make ends meet.

We call on the administration to stop its assault on working families and maintain the 40 hour work week. Congress will have a chance to revisit this issue and we urge workers to tell members to protect their overtime rights.
