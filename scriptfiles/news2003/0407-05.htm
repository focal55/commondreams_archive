Hypocrisy of George Bush & Tony Blair Knows No End
   NORTHERN IRELAND - April 7 -The leader of Northern Ireland's trade union movement has accused George Bush and Tony Blair of staggering hypocrisy over Northern Ireland's 'War Summit'. Politicians claim to talk peace while plotting the destruction of Baghdad and the occupation of Iraq.

Peter Bunting, Assistant General Secretary of the Irish Congress of Trade Unions (ICTU) said today:

"Everyone in Northern Ireland wants lasting peace and we appreciate past and present-day encouragement from the United States in reaching that goal. But Tony Blair and George Bush know no shame in holding a 'War Summit' in a city racked by conflict and struggling for peace.

To have President Bush and Tony Blair talk peace to Northern Ireland politicians while plotting the destruction of Baghdad and the occupation of Iraq is the ultimate in hypocrisy. We want peace, not just for the people of Northern Ireland, but also for the people of Iraq. Unfortunately President Bush has another fate for them in mind - death and destruction from air and land.

Our own experience in Northern Ireland shows that peace can only be achieved through dialogue and not bullets, bombs, or pre emptive strikes. We would question if this Belfast visit is in support of peace or more likely because of the wide spread opposition to the war throughout the UK that this summit could not be held in London or any other city in Great Britain."

The Northern Ireland Committee of the ICTU is co-ordinating the Stop the War Coalition and has announced plans for what it expects to be Northern Ireland's biggest ever anti-war rally following George Bush's announcement that he and Tony Blair are holding a 'War Summit' in Belfast. Organisers of the two massive Belfast peace march and rally, now say that they expect thousands to demonstrate across Northern Ireland following Bush's arrival in Belfast.

The Stop the War Coalition (NI) is asking people to assemble at 6.00pm on Monday at Hillsobrough Castle for a massive demonstration so the people of Belfast and Northern Ireland can join the millions around the world in showing their opposition to the war and this latest conspiracy of hypocrisy . Busses will be leaving from Belfast, L'Derry, and Dublin.

On Tuesday 8 April the Stop the War Coalition (NI) is organizing lunchtime 'die ins' in city and town centres across Northern Ireland at 1.00pm in Belfast the location will be at Belfast City Hall. We are also asking everyone opposed to the invasion and occupation of Iraq to wear black on Tuesday as a mark of respect to all the victims (civilian and soldiers) of this immoral and illegal war.
