House Amendments to Global AIDS Bill Threaten Lives, Says SIECUS
   WASHINGTON - May 1 - Today the U.S. House of Representatives overwhelmingly passed the Global AIDS bill, HR 1298, by a vote of 375-41. The bill provides for $15 billion over five years to fight HIV/AIDS in Africa and elsewhere. Unfortunately, the House tragically passed two amendments to the bill that will severely weaken its effectiveness and cost countless lives, according to the Sexuality Information and Education Council of the U.S. (SIECUS).

An amendment offered by Rep. Joe Pitts (R-Pa.) specifically earmarks one-third of the bill's overall prevention dollars for unproven abstinence-only-until-marriage programs. A second amendment offered by Rep. Chris Smith (R-N.J.) allows faith-based organizations who receive funds under the bill to be exempt from discussing disease prevention methods -- such as condoms -- if they feel it is in conflict with their religious teachings.

"HIV/AIDS is a global crisis that requires bold leadership that grounds prevention and treatment initiatives in sound public health policy," said Tamara Kreinin, president and CEO of the Sexuality Information and Education Council of the U.S. (SIECUS). "These amendments directly contradict what science says works best to prevent HIV/AIDS -- a broad comprehensive approach that addresses multiple factors, including the use of condoms," Kreinin continued.

"The U.S. is exporting policies born of conservative ideology not public health. In so doing, we are censoring critical reproductive health information from the world's most vulnerable populations," Kreinin said.
