No Mother's Day Gift From the Bush Administration on Children's Environmental Health, Says Children's Environmental Health Network
  WASHINGTON - May 6 - News Advisory:

WHO: -- Lynn R. Goldman, MD, MPH; chair, Children's Environmental Health Network; professor, Environmental Health Sciences, The Johns Hopkins University Bloomberg School of Public Health -- Albert W. Morris Jr., M.D.; environmental health chair, National Medical Association

WHAT: "Are Children Left Behind?: Children's Environmental Health Under the Bush Administration"

As a candidate, George W. Bush issued a strong statement detailing how he would safeguard children's health. The Children's Environmental Health Network will issue its second report on how the Bush administration is adhering to his promises. The report also identifies emerging high priority Federal issues in protecting children's environmental health.

WHEN: May 8 at 10 a.m.

WHERE: Zenger Room, National Press Club 13th Floor 529 14th Street, NW Washington, D.C. 20045

WHY: As Mother's Day approaches, how comfortable can parents be that Federal policies are providing a healthy environment, free from toxicants, for their children? The Children's Environmental Health Network is comparing Candidate George Bush's promises with the reality of how well his administration is protecting our children from environmental dangers such as air pollutants, unsafe pesticide and chemical residues, and heavy metals. This report succeeds the Network's July 2001 report, "Bush Administration Six Month Check-Up: A Follow Up Of Campaign Promises" and builds on Bush statements in 2000.

CEHN is a non-profit, non-partisan organization of pediatric experts, researchers, health care practitioners, and community leaders dedicated to protecting the fetus and the child from environmental hazards and promoting a healthy environment.
