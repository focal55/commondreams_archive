Common Cause praises Supreme Court decision in FEC v. Beaumont
   WASHINGTON - June 16 -Common Cause is pleased by the Supreme Court’s decision today upholding the federal ban on contributions by all corporations, even ideological non-profit corporations, at issue in this case, Federal Election Commission v. Beaumont.

“The Court correctly recognized the corrupting political influence of corporate money in the political system and the long-standing consensus that such money should be kept out of American politics,” said Chellie Pingree, president of Common Cause.

Today’s ruling upholds a nearly century-old campaign finance law and bodes well for a landmark decision on the constitutionality of the Bipartisan Campaign Reform Act, a case that will be heard before the Supreme Court in September.

Today’s ruling is the third in a series of cases where the Supreme Court has upheld campaign finance laws and reversed lower court decisions. In 2000, the Supreme Court reversed a decision made by a federal appeals court on Nixon v. Shrink Missouri Government PAC, and in 2001 the Supreme Court reversed a decision made by another federal appeals court in the case of FEC v. Colorado Republican Federal Campaign Committee.
