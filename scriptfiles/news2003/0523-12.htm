IFAW Whale Protection TV Commercial Wins Top Awards and Touches Hearts of Millions
   YARMOUTHPORT, Mass. - May 23 - Threatened by commercial whaling, the world's whales are bidding us all 'goodbye' in a new award-winning public service announcement television spot that is touching the hearts and minds of millions of viewers around the world.

The 45-second TV spot was produced in Hamburg, Germany by the acclaimed advertising firm of Springer & Jacoby, as part of a comprehensive ad campaign the company has developed pro-bono for the International Fund for Animal Welfare.

In it, majestic whales swim underwater singing their haunting songs. Their songs transform from their natural calls to the international music hit "Time to Say Goodbye" by famed Italian composer and singer Andrea Boccelli. As the song climaxes, the whales wave farewell with their flukes as they swim off into the deep waters and the audience is told the whales are bidding the world farewell because "Japan and Norway are whaling again."

The spot is being broadcast throughout Germany on the eve of the annual meeting of the International Whaling Commission (IWC), to be held in Berlin, Germany in June. It will then be aired internationally to generate public support for the protection of whales and the end of commercial whaling worldwide. It may also be viewed at http://www.ifaw.org.

This week, the powerful TV spot won a silver award for sound at the prestigious CLIO advertising competition in the U.S. It also won three awards in the 2003 competition of the Art Directors Club of Germany held earlier this year.

"IFAW is delighted to be collaborating with the talented team at Springer & Jacoby on this campaign, and we are very proud this TV ad is being so well received worldwide," said IFAW Director of Public Affairs, Patrick Ramage. "Surprisingly, many people still do not know that commercial whale hunting takes place. We hope that this captivating and creative TV ad will inform people and encourage them to join IFAW's campaign to end whaling once and for all."

Springer & Jacoby is one of the world's leading international advertising agencies, and is best known for its Mercedez-Benz advertising campaigns. The agency has branch offices in London, Paris, Barcelona and Milan, is a member of the US-group "Partnership/Interpublic," and closely cooperates with Bozell in New York.
