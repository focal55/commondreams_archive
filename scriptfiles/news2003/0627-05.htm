Supreme Court Upholds Nuremberg Files Verdict Re: American Coalition of Life Activists (ACLA) v. Planned Parenthood of Columbia/Willamette
  

WASHINGTON - June 27 - Statement of NARAL Pro-Choice America President Kate Michelman.

NARAL Pro-Choice America applauds the U.S. Supreme Court’s refusal to hear the Nuremberg Files case, letting stand a 9th Circuit Court of Appeals’ decision against proponents of anti-choice violence. The Supreme Court’s refusal to hear the case sends a clear message that threats and intimidation of persons providing constitutionally protected reproductive health services will not be tolerated. Anti-choice extremists cannot justify their repeated harassment, threats, and intimidation of abortion providers as free speech protected by the First Amendment.

Today’s decision by the Supreme Court is a resounding victory for the right to choose, which is imperiled by overarching threats of violence that are often acted upon by anti-choice extremists.

Anti-choice extremists must take responsibility for the damaging effect of their harsh rhetoric. When physicians who provide abortions are referred to as “evil”, “murderers”, and “butchers”, a culture of intolerance and hatred is sure to follow. Threats of violence against abortion providers are very real. Indeed, Drs. Britton, Gunn, and Slepian were all targeted prior to being murdered by anti-choice extremists.

Health care professionals who risk their lives everyday to ensure women’s access to complete reproductive health care deserve the right to live and work without the threat of violence.
