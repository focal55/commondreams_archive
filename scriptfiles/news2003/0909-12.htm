Struggling Families Need Realistic Welfare Bill
  WASHINGTON - September 9 - The Senate Finance Committee is expected to markup a welfare reauthorization proposal later this week. Stephanie Robinson, Esq. National Director for Public Policy for the Center for Community Change commented on the type of bill that Senators should produce:

“As Senators prepare to markup the welfare reauthorization bill, they should pay close attention to the economy in which the program will operate. Struggling families cannot afford a TANF bill totally disconnected from the economic realities of job losses and stubborn unemployment.

“Given the recent record job loss data, Senators must not raise work requirements for welfare recipients. The jobs just aren’t there. Raising work requirements won’t help the economy and certainly won’t help low-income families.

“There is still hope. Last year, acting in the spirit of bipartisanship, the Senate made real progress toward a welfare reauthorization bill that would help people escape poverty. The Senate should revisit those proposals, instead of taking two steps backwards towards the President’s original punitive welfare bill."
