Kucinich on Physicians' Health Reform Initiative
  WASHINGTON - August 12 - Today, a reform proposal for single-payer National Health Insurance -- endorsed by thousands of physicians, including two former Surgeon Generals and hundreds of medical school deans and professors -- is being released. The proposal will appear in the August 13 Journal of the American Medical Association.

Only one candidate for President, Dennis Kucinich, is cosponsor of a National Health Insurance bill in Congress and has made that issue a centerpiece of his campaign. Congressman Kucinich issued this statement today:

"This is a historic day with thousands of doctors backing healthcare reform that addresses the key obstacle to universal coverage: private health insurance. Doctors have seen this obstacle up close. Private insurers add little to healthcare except waste, paperwork, marketing and advertising costs, profits, high executive salaries and sales commissions. Insurance firms don't heal or treat the sick, but they soak up huge amounts of resources that should go to treatment. Our country is already paying for national healthcare; we're just not getting it.

"Other Democratic presidential candidates propose tinkering with a flawed, bureaucratic system dominated by private insurance. But incremental changes haven't gotten our country anywhere close to universal coverage. As thousands of physicians are telling us, National Health Insurance is the cost-effective solution."
