Press Advisory: Thursday Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals Hears Wild vs. Hatchery Salmon Arguments in Portland
   WASHINGTON - May 7 - Press Advisory for Thursday May 8, 2003

Federal Court of Appeals Hears Oral Argument over Endangered Species Act Protections for Wild vs. Hatchery Salmon.

Contact: Earthjustice attorney, Patti Goldman, office 206-343-7340 ex 32 or cellphone, 206-390-4853 John McManus, Earthjustice, 510-550-6707, cell 650-218-8650

Who: Earthjustice attorney Patti Goldman

What: Presents oral argument to the 9th Circuit Court of Appeals

Where: Mark O. Hatfield Courthouse, 1000 Southwest Third Avenue, Portland, Oregon, Courtroom 16

When: Thursday, May 8, 2003; Court session begins at 9:00 a.m.; this argument will likely begin around 9:30 a.m.

Why: Arguments will be presented to overturn a federal court ruling that struck down federal ESA protection for Oregon coastal coho salmon.

How: Federal district court Judge Michael Hogan ruled in September 2001 that the federal agency charged with protecting Oregon coastal salmon from extinction threats erred by not counting hatchery bred and raised coho along with naturally reproducing wild stocks in assessing the status of the wild fish. This ruling represented a radical departure from mainstream interpretation of the federal Endangered Species Act, which requires federal officials to protect and restore wild, naturally reproducing, listed species and the natural habitat they need to survive. Judge Hogan’s ruling was stayed by the Ninth Circuit in early 2002 pending resolution of the hearing being held Thursday. Other challenges to the ESA status of other salmon species around the country will likely be affected by the outcome of this case.
