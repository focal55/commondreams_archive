Tom Gerety, Departing Amherst College President, to Lead Brennan Center for Justice
  NEW YORK - May 1 - The Board of Directors of the Brennan Center for Justice at NYU School of Law today announced the appointment of Tom Gerety as executive director. Mr. Gerety will complete a nine-year tenure as president of Amherst College at the end of June.

"This is urgent work: When faced with external threats, the American republic has always had to struggle to maintain a democracy that is open and energetic, reflecting the diverse voices and interests of our nation," said Mr. Gerety. "The Brennan Center for Justice fights to uphold America's ideals of equality, liberty, and generosity to all. It's a great time to join in this good work."

The Brennan Center is a new type of public interest institution, incorporating elements of a public interest law firm, a think tank, and an advocacy organization. Founded in 1995, the Center brings together thinkers and advocates in pursuit of a vision of inclusive and effective democracy. Growing from a staff of two to a staff of 40 in less than eight years, the Center engages in three programs: Criminal Justice, Democracy, and Poverty. Notable successes include: a 2001 Supreme Court decision striking down a law that barred legal aid lawyers from challenging welfare laws; and the Center's involvement in the enactment and legal defense of the McCain-Feingold law, the first comprehensive federal campaign finance reform in a generation.

The Center's current initiatives include: -- Expanding access to good jobs for working families by supporting initiatives such as living wage laws. -- Removing voting bans to enable ex-offenders to participate in the democratic process. -- Ensuring that community voices are heard in policymaking affecting the criminal justice system. -- Supporting campaign finance reform to control the influence of money on politics. -- Strengthening civil legal aid to increase access to justice for low-income individuals and families.

Said William J. Brennan, III, chair of the Brennan Center Board, "With his experience as a legal scholar, a social justice advocate, and president of a premier liberal arts college, Tom Gerety possesses all the talents needed to advance the Center's mission. The entire Brennan Center community - the Brennan family, my father's former clerks, the NYU faculty, and our Board - could not be more pleased with his appointment."

As Amherst president, Mr. Gerety improved the diversity of the college's student body so that now approximately 35 percent of the first-year class is composed of students of color. In the past year, he organized private colleges in a "friend of the court" brief supporting the affirmative action policies of the University of Michigan. Mr. Gerety strengthened Amherst's financial resources, nearly tripling the endowment to almost a billion dollars. He continued to teach as a professor of philosophy throughout his presidency, including a first-year seminar called "Inner City America" in which he asked students to work at social service agencies.

Said Mr. Gerety, "We're in this fight with lots of others. We need strong allies and friends. So it's essential to work closely with others who care about these issues - about civil rights, about the poor and their advocates, about the promise of democracy. I look forward to working with the Center's great staff, with the Board, and with the NYU faculty at the Law School and throughout the University. Particularly important colleagues are the many experienced and devoted people around the country - in advocacy groups, service agencies, and foundations - who stand shoulder to shoulder with us in all that we do."

Mr. Gerety has received a university-wide academic appointment; he will be the Brennan Center for Justice Professor at NYU. Said Richard L. Revesz, Dean of NYU Law School, "Tom Gerety will be a spectacular leader for the Brennan Center. He will be able to capture the synergies available to the Center from its relationship with NYU School of Law, bringing together the best academics, policy analysts, and litigators to address our nation's pressing social problems."

From 1989 to 1994, Mr. Gerety was president of Trinity College, a liberal arts college located in Hartford, Connecticut. While there, he turned the college decisively towards its inner city environment and developed the strategic plans for magnet schools and other city/suburban collaborations that Trinity maintains today. From 1986 to 1989, Mr. Gerety was Dean of the College of Law at the University of Cincinnati. Earlier, he was a law professor at the University of Pittsburgh, and a visiting professor of constitutional law and jurisprudence at Stanford Law School. Mr. Gerety has a J.D. from Yale Law School. He also holds doctorate and masters degrees in philosophy from Yale.

Since 1989, Mr. Gerety has served on the Board of the International Rescue Committee, and last year he joined the Board of Save the Children.

Mr. Gerety succeeds E. Joshua Rosenkranz, who was one of the Brennan Center's founders in 1995 and is responsible for building the Center to its present form. The Center's mission is to develop and implement an innovative, nonpartisan agenda of scholarship, public education, and legal action that promotes equality and human dignity, while safeguarding fundamental freedoms.
