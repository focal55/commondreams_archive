Four ELF Resisters to be Sentenced in Federal Court;
One Finishes Prison Term
   MADISON, WI - April 8 - Four anti-war activists and opponents of the Navy's ELF submarine transmitter system will be re-sentenced in Federal District Court in Madison, Wisc. (120 N Henry St.), on Friday, April 25, 2003 at 1:30 p.m.

U.S. Magistrate Stephen Crocker will sentence Cory Bartholomew of Black Earth, WI, Jane Hosking of Luck, WI, John LaForge of Luck, WI and Jeff Leys of Milwaukee, for their failure/refusal to complete court-imposed community service work. The four face a possible maximum of 6 months in prison and or a $5000.00 fine.

On Nov. 19, 2002 the Magistrate imposed 85 hours of community service work. The four were part of a group of five convicted Sept. 10, 2002 of trespassing during a Mothers' Day 2002 demonstration at the ELF site. The fifth activist, attorney John Bachman of Eau Claire, WI, has finished most of the 85 hours which the court ordered completed by April 25.

In a related event, John Heid, of Luck, WI, was released Monday, April 7, 2003, from federal prison in eastern Pennsylvania, after serving seven months. Heid was sentenced by Mag. Crocker to one month for refusing to pay a $500.00 fine imposed for an October 2001 trespass conviction at the ELF site. As part of the same stint, Heid also served 6-months for a trespass conviction that resulted from a protest at the U.S. Army's School of the Americas in Georgia.

The ELF transmitter sends one-way messages to nuclear-powered Fast-Attack and nuclear-armed Trident submarines around the world. At least five of the U.S. Navy's Fast Attack submarines have launched Tomahawk Cruise missiles into Iraq in the current war.

Opponents of the ELF system have organized more than 50 demonstrations at the transmitter site since the end of the Cold War.
