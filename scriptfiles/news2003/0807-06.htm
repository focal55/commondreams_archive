Kucinich Challenges Rep. Gephardt on Trade
   WASHINGTON - August 7 - Campaigns should be about clarifying issues and drawing distinctions so voters can make informed decisions among the candidates. I have made my position clear throughout the campaign and at Tuesday's AFL-CIO forum in Chicago that I believe NAFTA and the World Trade Organization are fatally flawed instruments which drive workers rights, wages, human rights and environmental protections downward across the globe. I have pledged that a Kucinich Administration would withdraw from NAFTA and the WTO, and replace them with bilateral pacts that raise up standards.

I have great respect for Rep. Richard Gephardt. He has said he would not withdraw from NAFTA or the WTO. In today's Des Moines Register, his spokesperson referred to Rep. Gephardt as a key negotiator of the WTO: "He thought it would be a powerful force in raising labor standards throughout the world."

Given what we know about the negative impacts of NAFTA and the WTO, I encourage Rep. Gephardt to explain to voters and union members specifically how he proposes to build a global trade system that protects workers and the environment -- and why he thought the WTO was an improvement over NAFTA, which he opposed.

The cause of the Democratic primary process must be the lifting up of workers rights, human rights and the environment. If Democratic candidates will not commit to withdrawing from the fundamentally flawed NAFTA and WTO agreements, then who will protect workers and the environment?
