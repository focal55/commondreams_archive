MoveOn.org Voter Fund Kicks Off $2M Ad Campaign in 5 Swing States
   WASHINGTON - December 3 - MoveOn.Org Voter Fund takes its public education program to 17 cities in five battleground states this week, with a major television ad campaign that asks voters to consider whether through mistakes and poor leadership, the President has squandered opportunities to meet pressing domestic needs.

The cost of the campaign is approximately $2 million, and is the first in a series of ad campaigns planned under the potential $15 million fundraising budget earmarked for public education campaigns in so-called swing states. Other ads will appear regularly through March 2004.

“Our members feel strongly that the President’s failed leadership is costing Americans not only in misspent dollars but in opportunities lost – opportunities to aggressively address national problems,” said Wes Boyd, who with his wife, Joan Blades, founded MoveOn.org and the MoveOn.org Voters Fund. “We believe that we will find lots of others in those target states who agree with us.”

Meanwhile, MoveOn.org members around the country will stage more than 2,000 house parties on Sunday (Dec. 7), showing the important new film “UNCOVERED: THE Whole Truth About the Iraq War.” Produced by award-winning filmmaker Robert Greenwald, the film details the lies, misstatements and exaggerations that led to the “pre-emptive” invasion and occupation of Iraq with its continuing casualties involving American troops and its other global consequences. For details on specific locales of the house parties, please go to www.moveonvoterfund.org

The “MisLeader” 30-second issue ad is part of a continuing series placed by the Voter Fund. It notes that misjudgments and poor leadership by George W. Bush that resulted in the invasion and occupation of Iraq has diverted $87 billion from critical programs at home such as improving public schools, fixing the domestic power grid and expanding child health coverage.

The ad will appear in five states where voters were closely divided in the 2000 election between Bush and his Democratic opponent, Al Gore. They will run in the following markets:

FLORIDA -- Fort Myers, Orlando and St. Petersburg/Tampa.

Missouri – Kansas City, St. Joseph and St. Louis.

Nevada – Las Vegas and Reno.

Ohio –Cleveland, Columbus, Dayton and Toledo.

West Virginia – Beckley, Bluefield, Clarksburg, Parkersburg and Wheeling. (The ad previously ran in Charleston and Huntington).

The MoveOn.org Voter Fund is a Section 527 political committee affiliated with MoveOn.org, an online organizing group with nearly two million members across the United States. The Voter Fund is committed to a continuing campaign to reach the American people and tell them the truth about the presidency of George W. Bush.
