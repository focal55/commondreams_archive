Summer Vacation 2003 -- Last, Best Chance to See America's Wild Places
   WASHINGTON - June 30 - The quintessential summer holiday is upon us, and Americans across the country are packing their bags for July 4th trips and for summer vacations in the weeks to come. Millions of people will head to the great outdoors to enjoy their National parks, monuments, forests and other public lands.

But many of these places are in grave danger from a host of Bush Administration proposals that threaten to carve roads, punch oil wells, or otherwise impair these lands that belong to all Americans. Whether through administrative rulemaking, policy changes or lawsuit settlements, these policies seek to maxmize mining and drilling, allow unfettered access for roadbuilding and destructive off-road vehicle use, and ultimately extinguish any interim protection for hundreds of millions of acres of public lands across the United States.

The Sierra Club has documented 20 places across the country where we could see the on-the-ground impacts of Bush Administration policies in the very near future, places that might not look the same next summer or where we are already starting to see the damage. These are the very places where American families vacation each year, and the summer of 2003 could be the last, best chance to see these spectacular wild places!
