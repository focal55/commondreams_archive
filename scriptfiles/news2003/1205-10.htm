Over 50 US Human Rights Groups to Launch Network to Address “Alarming State of Human Rights in the US”
   WASHINGTON - December 5 - US-based rights groups will announce at a press conference on 10 December 2003 in Washington, DC that they are “joining forces” in a US Human Rights Network to address what they see as the “alarming rate of human rights violations in the US” -- saying that “as the US indulges an increasingly unilateralist bent in both domestic and foreign policy, the cost to rights at home and abroad is mounting.”

The groups, which cover such diverse issues as criminal justice, civil rights, immigration and asylum concerns, and economic rights, will launch the US Human Rights Network amid growing concern domestically and internationally that the US sees itself as exempt from international human rights laws and standards.

“The demonstrations that we are currently seeing against the US around the world are a reaction to the perception that the US – and particularly the Bush administration – thinks it is above international law – laws that the rest of the world are required to abide by,” said Cathy Albisi, of the US Human Rights Network.

Speaking at the press conference will be Laila Al-Arian, daughter of Florida Professor Sami Al-Arian -- who has been imprisoned on “terror” charges since 20 February 2003 with no evidence having been produced against him. Ms Al-Arian will speak about her father’s case and conditions of his imprisonment.

Who: Ajamu Baraka and Cathy Albisi of the US Human Rights Network; Laila Al-Arian, daughter of Florida Professor Dr. Sami Al-Arian

When: 10 am, Wednesday, 10 December 2003

Where: Zenger Room, 13th Floor, National Press Club, 529 14th Street, NW, Washington DC, 20045
