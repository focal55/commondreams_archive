Arizona Kicks Off National Seat Belt Crackdown with TV Ads, Awards to Law Enforcement; Enforcement Message Surrounds Young Drivers
   WASHINGTON - May 13 - News Advisory:

-- Arizona Kicks Off National Seat Belt Crackdown with TV Ads, Awards to Law Enforcement Agencies

-- Aim is to Surround Young Drivers With a Strong Safety Belt Enforcement Message

This Memorial Day in Arizona and across the country, teenagers and young adults will be surrounded with the strong message, "Buckle Up, Arizona-No Excuses; If you won't buckle up to save your life, then buckle up to save yourself a ticket." More than 100 police and sheriffs' departments across Arizona will be participating in the Buckle Up, No Excuses education and enforcement campaign.

Arizona is part of a nationwide seat belt crackdown, deploying more than 12,000 law enforcement agencies from all 50 states, D.C., and Puerto Rico. The campaign is supported by nearly $10 million in targeted national advertising, including $200,000 in Arizona alone. The advertising to support enforcement is based on a proven public health model to increase belt use called "high visibility enforcement."

The Arizona State Capitol will host the kickoff for the two-week Buckle Up, No Excuses enforcement wave, which runs from May 19 through June 1. At the kickoff, the Governor's Office of Highway Safety will also honor law enforcement agencies and officers from across the state for their efforts during the 2001-2002 seat belt enforcement campaigns

In 2001, 5,341 teens, ages 16-20, died and thousands more were injured in traffic crashes nationwide. Fatality rates for teens are twice that of older drivers and the risk of crashes for teens is four times that of older drivers.
