Bono To Discuss U.S. and Global Commitment to HIV/AIDS and Africa
   WASHINGTON - December 2 - The Henry J. Kaiser Family Foundation will host DATA (Debt, AIDS, Trade, Africa) founder Bono as the first participant in the new series, Kaiser Conversations on Health in Washington, DC this Wednesday, December 3.

Bono plans to discuss the record U.S. and global commitment to combating HIV/AIDS, the doubling of U.S. assistance to Africa in the new U.S. budget, and the $2 billion increase in overall assistance to poor countries in 2004. He is expected to call on the U.S. to quickly deliver the promised funding. He will provide insight on his travels including last weekend's concert for Nelson Mandela and first-hand experiences on how the epidemic is affecting people in Africa and around the world.

As founder of DATA, Bono has been a leader in raising awareness of the HIV/AIDS crisis in Africa and the increase of support in the United States and abroad to address this issue. More than 40 million people are estimated to be living with HIV/AIDS around the world and almost 14,000 people are newly infected each day.
