DAV Donates 211 Ford Vans to Veterans Hospitals
  WASHINGTON - May 5 - The Disabled American Veterans (DAV) will donate 59 new Ford Econoline and 152 Windstar vans, valued at nearly $4.9 million, to the U.S. Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) following a "Drive Away" ceremony at Ford Motor Company World Headquarters in Dearborn, Mich., May 6.

The event will celebrate Ford's 100th anniversary and send DAV volunteer drivers on their way to deliver the new vans to VA medical centers in 42 states. The vans join a nationwide fleet previously donated by the DAV and already at work transporting sick and disabled veterans to and from VA medical facilities for much-needed treatment.

"Ford Motor Company and its many employees are honored to be part of a continuing partnership with the DAV and their many outstanding volunteers," said John M. Rintamaki, Vice President, Ford Motor Company. "We take great pride in knowing our craftsmanship is at work when the DAV puts this fleet of specialized Ford vans on the road to help sick and disabled veterans."

Ford Motor Company's partnership with DAV began in 1922 when Henry Ford organized a cross-country caravan of 50 Model T Fords to transport DAV members to the organization's second national convention in San Francisco. Since 1996 Ford Motor Company has donated 79 vans to the DAV.

"The 211 Ford vans we launch on May 6 join the DAV Transportation Network, which is a shining example of a charitable institution stepping in to meet a substantial community need," said Edward R. Heath Sr., National Commander of DAV. "The respect and appreciation I have for our DAV and Auxiliary members who give so freely of themselves caring for veterans and their families in communities across the country is unmatched."

DAV chapters and state departments are actively involved in volunteer transportation programs designed to provide rides for veterans who have no way to get to VA hospitals and clinics for necessary medical attention. VA travel benefits were severely curtailed in 1987, and have not yet been fully restored. The DAV Transportation Network is essential to veterans nationwide. Under the Transportation Network, Hospital Service Coordinators have been placed in 172 VA medical facilities to run programs locally.

The DAV has donated 1,405 vans, worth more than $29 million to VA medical centers nationwide since the program began in 1987. Last year, DAV volunteers drove more than 27 million miles, providing more than 750,000 rides for veterans. In the past 16 years DAV drivers have logged more than 282 million miles providing over 7.4 million rides to veterans.

The nearly 1.3 million-member Disabled American Veterans, a non-profit organization founded in 1920 and chartered by the U.S. Congress in 1932, represents this nation's disabled veterans. It is dedicated to a single purpose: building better lives for our nation's disabled veterans and their families. For more information, visit the organization's Web site http://www.dav.org.
