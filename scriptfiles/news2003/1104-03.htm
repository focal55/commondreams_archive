Al Gore To Denounce Bush/Ashcroft Assaults On Civil Liberties
   WASHINGTON - November 3 - Former Vice President Al Gore will deliver a major address at historic DAR Constitution Hall at 2 pm on Sunday, Nov. 9, under the auspices of MoveOn.org and the American Constitution Society for Law and Policy.

In this, his third major speech on the Administration’s response to terrorism, Mr. Gore will describe the White House assault on civil liberties as un-American.

He will charge that the Bush/Ashcroft attack on the Constitution is a smokescreen that obscures the Administration’s failure to meaningfully protect national security, and will assert that these efforts have weakened, rather than strengthened, America.

Attendance is open only to members of the two sponsoring organizations, and to journalists. Credentialed journalists may, alternatively, listen to the speech by phoning in. Members of the media planning to attend the event or join the listen-in conference call must RSVP by email to Jessica@fenton.com.

In August, Mr. Gore delivered a speech at New York University sponsored by MoveOn that opened a space for other leaders to speak out against the Bush Administration’s deliberate use of false impressions to mislead the nation on war, taxes, the economy and the environment. That speech did nothing less than shift the terms of the national debate.

What: Major address by Al Gore

When: Sunday, Nov. 9 at 2 pm

Where: DAR Constitution Hall, 1776 D Street, NW

MoveOn.org is the nation’s largest and fastest-growing online advocacy group with over 1.7 million members nationwide. The American Constitution Society is a national organization of law students, academics, judges, lawyers and policymakers that is leading a rapidly growing movement to counter today’s conservative vision of American law and public policy.
