Kucinich Labor Day Proposal: Employ the Jobless to Rebuild America's Infrastructure
   WASHINGTON - September 1 - Presidential candidate Dennis Kucinich has released a proposal, timed for this Labor Day, to address the country's high unemployment and decaying infrastructure through federal loans to state and local governments to be made by a Federal Bank for Infrastructure Modernization. This proposal would create jobs for two million Americans in a wide array of fields, including such enterprises as rebuilding schools, designing roads, refurbishing environmental projects, and manufacturing steel for water systems.

"The current president's policies have cost us millions of jobs, and his persistence in failed policies recollects Herbert Hoover. I intend to put Americans back to work, and to work for the common good of the country, on a scale not seen since Franklin Roosevelt," said Kucinich. The Congressman is participating in Labor Day parades and picnics from Cleveland, Ohio (Saturday), to Clinton, Iowa (Sunday), to Des Moines, Iowa (Monday).
