Energy Conference Report Imminent
Public Interest Advocates, Local Officials Report on Consumer and Public Health Impacts of Energy Bill Conference
   WASHINGTON - November 10 - The House and Senate Energy Conference is nearing the conclusion of negotiations on the energy bill conference report. In anticipation of their report, energy experts from leading public interest groups will be joined by consumer and public health experts for a briefing on the environmental, fiscal and public health impacts of the bill's provisions Monday November 10, 2003 @ noon.

Each speaker will give a brief two-minute overview and open the floor to questions from journalists.

WHO: Adam Goldberg, Policy Analyst, Consumers Union
Paul Granger, Superintendent Plainview, NY Water District
(Plainview is threatened by a significant MTBE spill 400 feet from a high capacity water supply well.)
John Johnson, Office of Government Relations, The Episcopal Church
Marchant Wentworth, Legislative Representative for Clean Energy, Union of Concerned Scientists

Other experts will be on hand to answer questions, including:
Dave Albersworth, Bureau of Land Management Program Director, The Wilderness Society
