Texas County Becomes First to Call for a Moratorium on Executions; Movement Building Steam Across Texas and Across the South
  

WASHINGTON - April 29 - The Travis County Commissioners Court Tuesday became the first major elected body in the state of Texas to call for a moratorium on executions. Three of the five commissioners voted for a moratorium, with one commissioner abstaining and one voting against. Travis County includes most of Austin, the Texas capital.

The vote comes as unprecedented media scrutiny has focused on Texas' death penalty system, which has accounted for 302 of the 848 executions conducted in the United States in the past quarter century. Eleven daily newspapers in Texas have called for a moratorium on executions, including the conservative Dallas Morning News and every major metropolitan newspaper in the state. Even smaller newspapers in Victoria, Bryan-College Station, Abilene, Corpus Christi and Wichita Falls have joined the call.

"Texas' death penalty system is broken in almost every way imaginable," said Steven W. Hawkins, executive director of the National Coalition to Abolish the Death Penalty. "Stories abound about sleeping lawyers, ineffective lawyers, racial bias, cases of innocence, faulty and fraudulent lab technicians and forensic scientists and people on death row with severe mental retardation and mental illness. Today's vote was the right thing to do."

Hawkins commended Texas moratorium proponents, including the Texas Moratorium Network and Stand Down Texas, for their leadership in achieving passage of the resolution. Thus far, more than 80 elected bodies across the United States have passed moratorium resolutions, ranging from city councils in large cities such as New York City, Los Angeles and Atlanta, to smaller cities such as Macon, Georgia and Nashville, Tennessee.

In Texas, legislation calling for a moratorium is pending in the Texas Legislature. During the last session of the Texas Legislature, moratorium resolutions were passed by both House and Senate committees.
