More than 40 Groups Call for President Bush to Reverse Three Decisions on Children's Environmental Health, Says CEHN
  WASHINGTON - May 7 - News Advisory:

WHO: -- Lynn R. Goldman, MD, MPH; Chair, Children's Environmental Health Network -- Albert W. Morris, Jr., M.D.; Chair, Environmental Health and Bioterrorism Taskforce, National Medical Association

WHAT: "Are Children Left Behind?: Children's Environmental Health Under the Bush Administration" -- Coalition Letter and Report Release

The Children's Environmental Health Network and over 40 partner groups -- medical, civil rights, faith, public health, and environmental -- call on President Bush to reverse 3 recent decisions: amending the "Executive Order on Children's Environmental Health and Safety" to exempt OMB and its decisions, withdrawing $1 million from 2003 funds for the National Children's Study, and supporting broad Department of Defense exemptions from environmental health regulations.

These decisions are part of a broader context of Administration actions, detailed in CEHN's new report. As a candidate, George W. Bush issued a strong statement detailing how he would safeguard children's health. CEHN will issue its second report on how the Bush administration is adhering to his promises, with an overview of the most important positive and negative actions taken. The report also identifies emerging high priority Federal issues in protecting children's environmental health. Embargoed copies are available upon request.

WHEN: May 8 at 10 a.m.

WHERE: Zenger Room, National Press Club 13th Floor 529 14th Street, NW Washington, D.C. 20045

WHY: As Mother's Day approaches, how comfortable can parents be that Federal policies are providing a healthy environment, free from toxicants, for their children? The Children's Environmental Health Network compares Candidate George Bush's promises with the reality of how well his administration is protecting our children from environmental dangers such as air pollutants, unsafe pesticide and chemical residues, and heavy metals. This report succeeds the Network's July 2001 report, Bush administration "Six Month Check-Up: A Follow Up Of Campaign Promises" and builds on Bush statements from 2000.

CEHN is a non-profit, non-partisan organization of pediatric experts, researchers, health care practitioners, and community leaders dedicated to protecting the fetus and the child from environmental hazards and promoting a healthy environment.
