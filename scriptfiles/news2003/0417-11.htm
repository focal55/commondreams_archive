Coast to Coast: Presidential Candidates Hit New York, California for Fundraising
   WASHINGTON - April 17 - The Democratic presidential contenders each raised large chunks of money in their home states during the first quarter of this year, and relied on traditional money centers including New York City, Los Angeles and Washington, D.C., for much of the rest, according to a study of itemized contributions in reports filed this week.

Sen. John Kerry (Mass.) raised more from New York City donors than did any other candidate. His take in the Big Apple from January through March was nearly $779,000. The only region where Kerry raised more was Boston, where he collected $1.4 million. Sen. John Edwards (N.C.) collected the second-highest total from New York--$567,000, good enough for second among the most lucrative metro areas for him. Sen. Joe Lieberman (Conn.) raised $466,000 in New York, more than anywhere else in the country; former Vermont Gov. Howard Dean took in $238,000 from there; and Rep. Dick Gephardt (Mo.) raised $216,000.

Former Sen. Carol Moseley Braun (Ill.) raised $3,000 in New York, which ranked third on her list of metro areas for fundraising. New York was not among the top five metro areas for Sen. Bob Graham (Fla.) or Rep. Dennis Kucinich (Ohio). As of this afternoon, a report for the Rev. Al Sharpton was not available from the FEC.

Los Angeles donors gave the most money ($637,000) to Edwards. He was the only candidate to raise more in L.A. than anywhere else in the country. Kerry took in $560,000, putting Los Angeles third on his metro-area list behind Boston and New York. Gephardt collected $388,000 from Los Angeles donors; the only place he raised more was in his home town of St. Louis ($769,000). Dean raised $232,000 in the City of Angels, slightly less than his total in New York. Lieberman raised $221,000, putting Los Angeles fourth on his metro-area list.

Washington, D.C., ranked among the most lucrative metro areas for Kerry ($377,000), Lieberman ($323,000), Gephardt ($314,000) and Dean ($168,000). Other top metro areas for presidential fundraising included Chicago, which made the top five list for Gephardt ($242,000) and Moseley Braun ($51,000), and San Francisco, where Kerry raised $445,000 and Dean collected $91,000.

Also on Edwards' list was Raleigh ($380,000) - the only metro area in North Carolina among his top five - as well as Dallas and Houston. Lieberman took in $276,000 and $149,000 from the Connecticut areas of Stamford and Hartford, respectively.

Kucinich posted the most eclectic list of top metro areas - San Francisco, Louisville (Ky.), Cleveland, Santa Cruz (Calif.) and Oakland. Graham, who formed a presidential committee in late February and has been recovering from heart surgery, raised virtually all of his money (89 percent) in his home state of Florida. His top five areas for fundraising were all in the Sunshine State.

Edwards appeared to take the most advantage of the new McCain-Feingold law's increased contribution limit, which is now $2,000 per individual to a candidate. He raised $5.2 million in contributions of $2,000 or more, compared to $4.2 million for Kerry, $2.2 million for Gephardt and $1.8 million for Lieberman. Graham raised $844,000 from contributors who maxed out to his campaign, Dean took in $644,000, Moseley Braun raised $42,000 and Kucinich collected $32,000.
