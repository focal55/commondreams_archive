Save the Children to Release New Report May 6 on World's Most Dangerous War Zones for Women and Children
  April 23 - News Advisory:

-- Save the Children to Release New Report May 6 on World's Most Dangerous War Zones for Women and Children -- Key Members of Congress to Call for New Legislation to Protect Women and Children Caught in War's Crossfire

As Mother's Day approaches, Save the Children officials and supporters - including actress Sally Field - will join members of Congress at a press conference at noon on Tuesday, May 6th on Capitol Hill to discuss key findings of a new report identifying the world's most dangerous war zones for women and children. Sen. Joseph Biden (D-Del.) and other Congressional leaders also will introduce new legislation to set aside $45 million in funds to protect women and children during armed conflicts. Following the press conference, Sally Field and other Save the Children supporters will visit members of Congress to build support for the legislation.

Please Note: Embargoed copies of Save the Children's new report, State of the World's Mothers 2003, which contains its first-ever Conflict Protection Scorecard identifying the world's worst war zones for women and children, are available for review prior to release on May 6. Experts who worked on the report are available for interviews. Materials are embargoed until Tuesday, May 6 at 12:01 a.m. EST (5:01 a.m. GMT) on the morning of May 6 prior to the press conference. The report also contains Save the Children's annual Mothers' Index that ranks the well-being of mothers in 117 countries.

For advance copies of the Save the Children report, contact Mike Kiernan at 202-261-4686 (mkiernan(At)dc.savechildren.org) or Colleen Barton at 203-434-2255 (cbarton(At)savechildren.org). For advance information on the legislation, contact Chip Unruh in Sen. Biden's office at 202-224-0132.

Press Conference Information

WHO: Confirmed participants include Sen. Biden (D-Del.); actress Sally Field; commentator Cokie Roberts; Kim Phuc, the subject of the famous 1972 photograph of her, at age 9, running from a burning Vietnam village; Lesley Jane Seymour, editor-in-chief of Marie Claire magazine; Charles MacCormack, CEO and president of Save the Children; and Beryl Levinger, research director, State of the World's Mothers 2003.
