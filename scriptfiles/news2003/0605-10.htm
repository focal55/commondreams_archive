New House and Senate Legislation Would Codify Protection for Undisturbed National Forests;
Statement by William Meadows, President, The Wilderness Society
   WASHINGTON - June 5 - We applaud the courage and foresight of Senators John Warner (R-VA) and Maria Cantwell (D-WA) and Representatives Sherwood Boehlert (R-NY) and Jay Inslee (D-WA), who today introduced the National Forest Roadless Area Conservation Act in both houses of Congress. These bills would essentially codify the popular Roadless Area Conservation Rule, which protects 58.5 million acres of undisturbed roadless areas in National Forests from road construction and most logging.

Since 1998, the American people have made it crystal clear that they support protecting the wildest places in our National Forests. Time and time again the public has expressed its unwavering support for strong protection of these magnificent wild lands.

When the Roadless Rule was first proposed, more than 2.3 million comments were received from the American people in favor of protection for our national forest roadless areas -- nearly 10 times greater than the level of response to any other rulemaking in federal rulemaking history. And when the Bush Administration proposed changing the rule last year, more than 700,000 people submitted comments; more than 95 percent of them asked the Administration to leave the rule as it stands.

At the core, America's wild and untouched natural places are essential to our national identity and our common heritage. Safeguarding our last remaining roadless forests is a basic expression of America's commitment to protecting the homeland in its most fundamental sense.
