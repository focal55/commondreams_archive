Press Briefing Conference Call: What's at Stake in the FTAA Ministerial in Miami
  

WASHINGTON - November 10 -

WHAT: Phone Conference Call Press Briefing: Leaders and experts from labor, farm, environmental, and consumer advocacy organizations will brief the media on the issues and concerns with the proposed Free Trade Area of the Americas (FTAA). Participants are part of a coalition -- Citizens Trade Committee (CTC) -- who will also comment on the outlook for the negotiations at the upcoming FTAA Ministerial in Miami Nov. 20- 21. A pre-ministerial meeting just concluded in Washington, D.C.

WHEN: Wednesday, Nov. 12, 9:30 a.m. Eastern

DIAL-IN: 1-877-292-2287 (Cite: 'FTAA-Citizens Trade Campaign)

WHO: Confirmed speakers include:

-- Leo Gerard, international president of the United Steelworkers of America. Web: http://www.uswa.org

-- George Naylor, president of the National Family Farm Coalition. Web: http://www.nffc.net

-- Lori Wallach, executive director of Public Citizen's Global Trade Watch. Web: http://www.citizen.org/trade/

-- David Waskow, international policy analyst & trade policy coordinator for Friends of the Earth. Web: http://www.foe.org

For background information: Citizens Trade Campaign, http://www.citizenstrade.org

The Citizens Trade Campaign (CTC) is a national coalition whose members include the National Family Farm Coalition; United Methodist Church General Board of Church and Society; Public Citizen; International Brotherhood of Teamsters; UNITE!; Friends of the Earth; the United Steelworkers of America; United Students Against Sweatshops; Communications Workers of America; Western Organization of Resource Councils; American Lands Alliance; Institute for Agriculture and Trade Policy; Alliance for Sustainable Jobs and the Environment; and Defenders of Wildlife, as well as regional, state, and city-based coalitions, organizations, and individual activists throughout the United States.
