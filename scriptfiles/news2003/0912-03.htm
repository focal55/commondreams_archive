Democracy Rising Rally Sept. 18 in DC
Ralph Nader and Dennis Kucinich to rally Citizens
  WASHINGTON - September 12 - On Thursday, September 18, Ralph Nader's "People Have The Power" tour will bring to Washington a powerful combination of community activism and entertainment. Community leaders and performers working for progressive social change will join Nader, Democratic presidential candidate Dennis Kucinich, and entertainer Patti Smith to activate audience members' involvement with local campaigns. This Washington rally will focus on issues vital to D.C. citizens, including DC Statehood, increased funding for libraries and affordable housing, restoration of D.C. General Hospital, universal health care, and opposing the use of vouchers in D.C. public schools.

Featured Guests Include:

  • Patti Smith and Oliver Ray
  • DC hip hop artists Head-Roc featuring Noyeek the Grizzly Bear
  • DC Statehood Now! Ralph Nader / Stand Up for Democracy / DC Statehood Green Party
  • DC Library Renaissance Project organizer, Increased Funding for Libraries,
  • Elizabeth Davis, DC Public School Teacher, No to Vouchers & Schools Over Stadiums,
  • DC public school teacher -Vanessa Dixon, DC Healthcare Coalition Universal Health Care and Restoration of DC General Hospital
  • Dorothy Scott, Affordable Housing, WISH, Washington Inner City Self Help
  • Reverend Graylan Hagler, Senior Minister, Plymouth Congregational United Church of Christ & National President, Ministers for Racial, Social, and Economic Justice.

Washington, D.C., is the sixteenth stop of Nader's nationwide tour that began in 2001 in Portland, Oregon. The tour has since packed venuesfrom San Francisco to Buffalo and New York City to Tampa. They are sponsored by Nader's most recent not-for-profit organization, Democracy Rising, which seeks to unify people "in the cause of freeing American politics from the grip of corporate interests."

"The purpose of these events is to bring people together in cities across America, which is especially critical now," said Nader, America's leading consumer activist. "Progressive people and organizations in Washington and beyond must work together. Civil liberties, our nation's security, and the nation's major social priorities are at risk in the Executive Branch and Congress. All major peace and social justice successes in our history have started with a mobilization of the citizenry. We must further this tradition and strive to ensure that the values of democracy, law enforcement, respect for human rights and the protection of all innocent people prevail here both at home and around the world."

The Washington, D.C., rally will be held at Metropolitan African Methodist Episcopal Church at 1518 M St., NW. The official program begins at 7:00 p.m. (doors, live music, and community group tabling at 6:00 p.m.). An array of community organizations will have information tables to distribute literature and solicit signatures for a variety of progressive causes. General admission tickets are $10 in advance and $15 at the door. Children 12 and under get in free.

For information on the issues and the rally at Metropolitan AME, call 202-319-2422 or check out www.DemocracyRising.org
