Protesters to Dump One Ton of Coal on U.S. Capitol Lawn to Protest National Energy Bill on May 15
  WASHINGTON - May 14 - A coalition of leaders representing health, taxpayer, student, faith, and environmental groups will dump one ton of coal on the U.S. Capitol lawn Thursday, May 15, to protest the multi-billion dollar subsidies proposed for the fossil fuel industry under the national energy bill now before Congress.

The event will feature speakers and a press conference. An official permit has been obtained for this event.

Speakers will include representatives from Friends of the Earth, U.S. Public Interest Research Group, Physicians for Social Responsibility (Baltimore), Maryland Interfaith Climate Alliance, Sierra Student Coalition, and Progressive Maryland.

Where and When: Thursday, May 15, at 10 a.m., Upper Senate Park (corner of New Jersey Ave. and Constitution Ave., west of the Russell Senate Office Building). Rain location for press conference: Cannon House Office Building Room 441.

For more information call the Chesapeake Climate Action Network at 301-920-1633 (office) or 240-460-5838 (cell).

The Chesapeake Climate Action Network is a non-profit, grassroots organization dedicated to fighting global warming through clean energy in the Virginia/Maryland/D.C. region.
