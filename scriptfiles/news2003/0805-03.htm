Hundreds of Superfund Cleanups Could Be Slowed Down
  

WASHINGTON - August 5 - Lack of Funding for Toxic Waste Cleanup Program Hits Home.

What: U.S. PIRG will be release a new report, "Cleanup Slowdown: How Under-Funding the Superfund Program Harms Communities Across America." The report documents toxic waste sites in 48 states that may be subject to a delayed cleanup or less stringent EPA oversight of cleanup activities conducted by polluters, leaving people and communities at risk of chemical exposure and disease.

When: August 7, 2003 at 11 A.M.

Who: Julie Wolk, U.S. PIRG Environmental Health Advocate, report author Lois Gibbs, Center for Health, Environment and Justice Executive Director, former President of the Love Canal Homeowners Association

Where: Capitol Reflecting Pool (west side)

Visuals: Activists in toxic waste barrel costumes, kids and activists holding signs, graph illustrating slowing rate of Superfund cleanups.

Rain Location: Senate Russell Office Building room 385
