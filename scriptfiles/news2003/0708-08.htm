What is the Real Story Behind the Natural Gas 'Crisis'?; Groups to Hold Teleconference, July 9
   WASHINGTON - July 8 - The United States is veering toward a dangerous path with regard to energy policy, groups say. A centerpiece of the plan is to increase drilling for oil and natural gas on public lands, even in areas where economically recoverable oil and gas are not likely to be found and where the natural values of the lands stand to be dramatically compromised.

As Federal Reserve Chairman Alan Greenspan prepares to testify before the Senate Energy Committee on Thursday, this teleconference will provide valuable insight into the facts -- and the myths -- behind the natural gas "crisis" that has the potential to dramatically impact our public lands. Experts will describe the Rockies' true natural gas potential, possible energy options and alternatives, and how we can we can "do it right" and take a more sensible approach to finding much-needed energy solutions.
