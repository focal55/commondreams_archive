Kucinich To Join Nader At Rally For Democracy And D.C. Statehood
   WASHINGTON - September 15 - WHAT: A mass rally organized by Democracy Rising and featuring presidential candidate and Congressman Dennis Kucinich, citizen activist Ralph Nader, Reverend Graylan Hagler of the Plymouth Congregational United Church and of Ministers for Racial, Social & Economic Justice, Vanessa Dixon of the DC Healthcare Coalition, Dorothy Scott of Washington Innercity Self Help (WISH), DC public school teacher Elizabeth Davis, and leaders from the DC Healthcare Coalition and the DC Library Renaissance Project.

WHEN: Thursday, September 18; doors open at 6 p.m. and event begins at 7 p.m.

WHERE: The Metropolitan African Methodist Episcopal Church, 1518 M Street NW, Washington, D.C.

WHY: To promote civic engagement and expanded democracy, further the campaign to win statehood for the District, and address the growing needs in D.C. for affordable housing, health care facilities, and well maintained libraries.

All nine Democratic presidential candidates were invited to this event, and only Congressman Kucinich, a supporter of D.C. statehood, has accepted.

Dennis Kucinich supports election reform, corporate reform, universal healthcare, public education pre-K through college, and affirmative action. He will be meeting with community leaders to discuss their shared goals for residents of the District of Columbia.
