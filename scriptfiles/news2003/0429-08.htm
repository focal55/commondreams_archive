Interfaith Peace-Builders Delegation Arrives in the Middle East
  WASHINGTON - April 29 - Eleven U.S. citizens departed on Sunday, April 27, for two weeks in Jordan,and Israel/Palestine as part of Fellowship of Reconciliation's Interfaith Peace-Builders delegation. The delegation crossed the Allenby Bridge from Jordan into Isarel and Palestine Monday morning. The delegation is led by Gretchen Merryman from the FOR Interfaith Peace-Builders staff, and Leah Harris, Vice-President of Jews for Peace in Palestine and Israel.

During their stay in the region, delegates are scheduled to meet with peace and human rights activists and organizations, humanitarian assistance workers, community and religious leaders, refugees, settlers, educators, and government representatives from across the political spectrum.

The purpose of their visit is to gain deeper insight into the issues surrounding the Palestinian-Israeli conflict, to examine the effects of United States foreign policy in the region, and express support for Israelis, Palestinians, and others who are working for a nonviolent, just, and sustainable peace.

The delegation includes a retired lobbyist, psychologist, retired educator,realtor, clinic director, a university president, and two members of the Bruderhof religious order. They hail from New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Washington, DC, Virginia, Ohio, and Alaska.

Upon their return to the United States on May 10, delegates are committed to sharing their experiences with the public and their political representatives. If you wish to hear directly from the participants when they return, please contact us. We can provide you with information about their speaking engagements or help you arrange a visit from a participant.

The delegation will be sending back regular reports during their trip, which we will forward to our email network and post on the FOR website at www.forusa.org. If you are not part of our email network and wish to receive delegation reports and updates on the work of FOR's Interfaith Peace-Builders Program, please email us at middleeast@forusa.org or phone us at 202-244-0821.

The next FOR Interfaith Peace-Builders delegation is scheduled for August 1-15, 2003. Further information and an application for that delegation is available on the FOR website at www.forusa.org.
