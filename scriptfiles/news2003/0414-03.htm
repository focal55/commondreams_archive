Chemical Safety Board to Hold New York City Hearing April 16
   NEW YORK - April 14 - The U.S. Chemical Safety and Hazard Investigation Board (CSB) will hold a field hearing in New York City on April 16 on the subject of the April 26, 2002 explosion in Manhattan, which injured 31 people and caused major property damage.

This investigation concerns an explosion during waste mixing operations at Kaltech Industries Group, an architectural sign manufacturing company on West 19th Street in Chelsea. The CSB staff will present preliminary findings on the causes of this incident, to be followed by several panels of witnesses who will discuss the potential role of fire codes in preventing chemical incidents of this kind. NYCOSH Executive Director Joel Shufro will be one of the witnesses. A public comment period will be provided. CSB will use the testimony and comments in developing its safety recommendations from this case.

"Operators like Kaltech represent a major threat to workers and to the community," said NYCOSH Board Chair William F. Henning. "Kaltech demonstrates the need for new regulations to protect workers from chemicals that are not highly hazardous in their pure form, but which can react explosively when they are mixed with other chemical. It also show the need for improved training for workers and better enforcement of existing regulations."

The meeting will occur on Wednesday, April 16, at the Fashion Institute of Technology, Building A, 8th floor, 8th Avenue at 27th Street, New York City, from 9 a.m. to 1 p.m.
