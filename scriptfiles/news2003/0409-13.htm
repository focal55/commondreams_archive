NARAL Pro-Choice America Unveils www.StopOwen.org
Calls On Pro-Choice America to Take Action to Stop Owen Re-Nomination; Announces that Owen Senate Vote Will Be Scored First Time in 20 Years – Appellate Judge Vote Scored By Group
   WASHINGTON - April 9 - NARAL Pro-Choice America turned up the heat today in the fight to stop President Bush’s re-nomination of controversial judicial activist Priscilla Owen. The nation’s premier abortion rights advocacy group launched a new website today – www.StopOwen.org aimed at defeating the re-nomination of Priscilla Owen to the Fifth Circuit Court of Appeals and mobilizing its powerful network of grassroots supporters.

“Pro-Choice America will not stand idly by while President Bush pushes controversial candidates who want to take away our constitutional rights and freedoms,” said NARAL Pro-Choice America President Kate Michelman. “We are working closely with our allies to ensure that Priscilla Owen is not going to make it through the Senate after being rejected last year. We have stopped Owen before and we’ll do it again."

“Americans are mobilizing against the roll back of their reproductive rights and www.StopOwen.org gives them the tools they need to make their voices heard,” added Michelman.

The website, www.StopOwen.org, provides information on Owen’s staunchly anti-choice record and urges pro-choice Americans to call their Senators demanding a “no” vote or, if necessary, a filibuster. Visitors can also send letters and sign a petition – all with the goal of stopping Owen’s re-nomination.

Michelman also announced that NARAL Pro-Choice America will score Senators votes on the motion to proceed to Priscilla Owen in the 2003 Congressional Record on Choice. Owen will be the first lower court nominee to be scored in nearly 20 years by the nation’s leading pro-choice advocacy group.

“Bush and the anti-choice leadership are defying historical precedent and the Senate’s traditional advise and consent role by pushing through a nominee who has been rejected by the Senate Judiciary Committee. NARAL Pro-Choice America will make history and score this vote on an appellate candidate for the first time in nearly twenty years,” said Michelman. “Senators must remain united in opposing conservative judicial activists like Priscilla Owen."
