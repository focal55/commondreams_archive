Immigrant's Rights Leaders Protest 'Terrorizing' Policies of DC-based Policy Group, Take Over Office
  WASHINGTON - June 2 - Hundreds of grassroots and immigrant rights' leaders representing National People's Action (NPA) are descending on the offices of the Federation for American Immigration Reform (FAIR), a Washington D.C.-based policy group, at 1666 Connecticut Ave., suite 400, today (June 2), NPA said.

NPA leaders around the country are protesting the current anti-immigrant policy agenda of FAIR which takes advantage of the atrocities of Sep. 11, 2001 by pushing an anti-immigrant agenda that has little to do with promoting national security yet speaks volumes about the organization's racist policies.

FAIR currently opposes the Student Adjustment and DREAM Act, bi-partisan legislation that would grant access to in-state tuition for children of immigrants whose families have been in the country at least five years and paid taxes for three. According to NPA, the organization further charges the U.S.-housed Mexican Embassy and its Matricula Consular ID cards as a threat to national security, along with any state that does not fully restrict access to drivers' licenses to full citizens.

FAIR attacks the Department of Justice and the former INS, now housed within the Department of Homeland Security, with failing to force local law enforcement to perform the functions of federal immigration enforcement, a practice a growing number of advocates view as a form of racial profiling and which many local police departments argue discourages the reporting of crime and undermines their ability to effectively serve and protect.

"Community organizations, secondary and post-secondary education advocates, organized labor, the business community, and many local police departments are all coming forth in support of access to citizenship, education and banking for undocumented immigrants and against further racial profiling and targeting of hard-working, honest Americans. We need to move forward for our country to grow stronger and not resort to the views of groups like FAIR that put us right back in the middle of the 20th Century," said Emira Palacios, co-chairperson of National People's Action.

FAIR receives funding from the Pioneer Fund, which funded the mid-20th centuries' widely racist "Bell-Curve" theory, which sought to establish the "biological inferiority of African-Americans." Ignoring its own proximity to this unfortunate past, FAIR goes on to argue on its Web site (http://www.fairus.org/html/04128611.htm) that immigrants are responsible for economic adversities faced by U.S. blacks.

National People's Action is a coalition of hundreds of community organizations from across the country that work on issues effecting their communities such as predatory lending, community reinvestment, neighborhood safety, education, youth development and immigration.
