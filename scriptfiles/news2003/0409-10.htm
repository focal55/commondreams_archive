Unemployed Workers to House GOP: Unemployed Not 'Lazy'
100 Unemployed Workers to Attend Thursday Hearing and Demand Work
  WASHINGTON - April 9 - News Advisory:

What: Tomorrow the Subcommittee on Human Resources of the House Ways & Means Committee will hold a hearing of the "impacts of unemployment compensation on returns to work", suggesting that unemployed people themselves rather than the nation's recession are to blame for their situation.

The Subcommittee will be joined by over 100 unemployed workers from New York, Pennsylvania and Washington, D.C. who will tell the committee to blame the economy, not the unemployed or the unemployment system on their failure to find work. One unemployed worker, Joe Bergman of New York, will testify to the committee on behalf of the group.

The workers will ask members of the committee to join them and spend a day in the life of an unemployed worker searching for work in this economy: going on interviews, applying for jobs, cold-calling prospects etc.

The workers are members of the New York Unemployment Project, Philadelphia Unemployment Project and Community Voice Heard.

When: Thursday, April 10, 10 a.m.

Where: Rayburn House Office Building, B-318, Washington, D.C.
