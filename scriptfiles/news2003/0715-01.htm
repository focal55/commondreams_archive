Kucinich Campaign Fundraising: Big Jump Upward
   WASHINGTON - July 15 - The Kucinich campaign has just filed its quarterly financial figures with the FEC, showing the campaign on a steep curve upward in fundraising, poised for expansion and new hiring, with money in the bank. It represents the biggest percentage jump in fundraising of any presidential campaign.

The campaign started in February and raised $180,000 in that quarter. In the campaign's first full quarter, ending June 30, fundraising climbed to more than $1.5 million ($1,539,000). With quarterly expenditures of $527,000, the campaign ended the quarter with over $1 million cash on hand ($1,064,000).

"Our goal was to raise a million dollars in our first full quarter," said Congressman Kucinich. "We far exceeded our expectations. A grassroots campaign like ours, with thousands of volunteers, doesn't need to match the other campaigns in fundraising -- we will invest our cash on hand in expanded field operations."

Kucinich continued: "With the power of the Internet to organize volunteers and raise money -- and thanks in part to our strong campaign in the MoveOn Primary -- we are showing that a campaign based on a progressive issues agenda can resonate with voters and gain momentum...despite those in the media who want to write us out of the race."

Of the $1.54 million raised in the quarter, roughly half was raised in the last two weeks, reflecting the campaign's growing momentum and a topnotch Internet operation that will keep raising money in small donations from Kucinich's growing number of passionate supporters. More than a million dollars or 2/3 of the total raised this quarter were from credit card donations through the Internet; the average donation for the quarter was $77.

The Kucinich campaign has gained momentum thanks to a growing list of endorsements from well known activists and artists -- including Willie Nelson, who will be doing benefit concerts to raise money for the campaign.
