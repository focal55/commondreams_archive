Obey Calls for Rumsfeld, Wolfowitz to "Return to the Private Sector" in Letter to President
   WASHINGTON - September 5 - Wisconsin 7th District Congressman Dave Obey today sent a letter to the President outlining five recommendations to improve U.S. reconstruction efforts in Iraq. The full text of the letter is attached.

Obey's five recommendations include:

allowing the Secretary and Deputy Secretary of Defense to return to the private sector due to the gross mismanagement of post-war Iraq;

fully restoring to the Department of State its traditional responsibilities for developing and implementing foreign policy;

clarifying that the uniformed leaders of the military are to be accorded their traditional role in the formulation of military policy;

establishing government-wide standards for the collection and vetting of intelligence;

strengthening the National Security Council so it can fulfill, as it has in the past, its critical role of balancing the Departments and Agencies that contribute to national security policy and ensuring one does not become too dominant.

As Ranking Member on the House Appropriations Committee, Obey's responsibilities include oversight of U.S. government expenditures on post-war reconstruction efforts in Iraq and of the cost of occupation activities by U.S. military forces.
