The Crimes of the Company - A Roundtable Discussion on the 8th Anniversary of the Hanging of Nigerian Activist, Ken Saro-Wiwa
   WASHINGTON - November 7- In the wake of the Secretary of State Colin Powell’s Corporate Excellence award to Chevron in Nigeria, lawyers and activists from Nigeria and the United States will gather on Monday November 10th at the National Press Club to discuss the current situation in the Niger Delta and ongoing US and Nigerian efforts to hold Shell, Chevron, and other corporations accountable for their actions.

Ken Wiwa, Oronto Douglas, and Jennie Green of the Center for Constitutional Rights will discuss the allegations of Shell’s involvement in the hanging of Ken Saro-Wiwa, and of Chevron’s ongoing human rights and environmental abuses in Nigeria.

The occasion marks the 8th anniversary of the hanging of Nigerian author and Ogoni rights activist Ken Saro-Wiwa. In his final statement to the military tribunal whose conviction on him was universally condemned, Ken Saro-Wiwa said, "I and my colleagues are not the only ones on trial. Shell is here on trial... The company has, indeed, ducked this particular trial, but its day will surely come... for there is no doubt in my mind that the ecological war that the company has waged in the Delta will be called to question sooner than later, and the crimes of that war will be duly punished. The crime of the company's dirty war against the Ogoni people will also be punished."
