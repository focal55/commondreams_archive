Granny D, Tom Hayden & Arun Gandhi Endorse Kucinich
   WASHINGTON - July 9 - The Kucinich for President Campaign is announcing endorsements from 3 leading activists: Granny D (Doris Haddock), Tom Hayden, and Arun Gandhi, the grandson of Mahatma Gandhi.

The insurgent Kucinich campaign has been gaining steam financially and in endorsements and grassroots volunteers. (On Monday, Kucinich was endorsed by prominent feminists who vowed to recruit others to the campaign; last week, Willie Nelson endorsed with a pledge to perform benefit concerts.)

GRANNY D, now in her 90s, is the legendary activist from New Hampshire, the first primary state. In 1999, she wore out four pairs of shoes and was hospitalized in Arizona for dehydration and pneumonia, but she was unstoppable as she marched for political reform on a 14-months trek from California to Washington D.C. Her full statement is here.

It reads in part:

"I am proud to endorse Dennis Kucinich for President. When I walked 3,200 miles across the United States and inquired of people why they were not voting, they said: 'Find us someone who understands us, who cares about us.' I'm talking about people living in trailers; people living in small towns with boarded-up windows on Main Street; people coming out of farmhouses in Iowa, squeezed out of their livelihood by stinking agri-hog farms; people whose well-paid jobs have been shipped overseas.

I promised to look for someone who would understand them, that had a vision of the future for them, that believed in Peace, Love, Justice. Well, here he is: Dennis Kucinich. I believe he is the candidate who can bring new voters and disgruntled voters into the political process. To those people I met on my walk across America: 'Now keep your promise to me and come out and vote for him!'"

TOM HAYDEN is another renowned activist endorsing Kucinich, who he refers to as "the candidate with the best stands and the best history." An author, civil rights activist, leader of the anti-Vietnam War movement and defendant in the "Chicago 8" trial, Hayden went on to become an effective California State Assemblyman and Senator for 7-terms. He is a leading figure in today's peace movement and the movement for global justice and against sweatshops.

ARUN GANDHI heads the M.K. Gandhi Institute of Nonviolence in Tennessee, where he carries on the work of his grandfather, Mahatma Gandhi. Arun Gandhi is a U.S. citizen who endorsed as an individual, not as a representative of any group: "I am happy to support Dennis Kucinich because I believe he is our only hope. Never before has it been so important that the nation rally around one candidate as today. This nation has been hijacked by right-wing politicians who are leading us on to certain disaster. Dennis Kucinich, with his progressive policies and love of peace, is the only candidate who can bring us back from the brink."

Monday's feminist statement and Willie Nelson's endorsement are on homepage at http://www.kucinich.us
