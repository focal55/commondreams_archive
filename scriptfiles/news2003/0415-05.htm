Clean Air Trust Welcomes EPA Plan to Clean Up Diesel Heavy Equipment, Fuel
  WASHINGTON - April 15 -The nonprofit Clean Air Trust released the following statement today by Clean Air Trust Executive Director Frank O'Donnell regarding today's EPA proposal to clean up diesel heavy equipment and fuel:

"We welcome the EPA proposal to set new clean-air standards for diesel heavy equipment and related diesel fuel.

"As we have noted for the past two years, diesel heavy equipment is a huge source of smog and soot pollution. EPA has told us it projects this rule will prevent more than 9,600 premature deaths each year once the standards are fully phased in.

"It is high time EPA moved to clean up this huge and deadly source of pollution.

"EPA Administrator Christie Whitman and her professional staff, led by Margo Oge, deserve credit for taking this issue on.

"We have not had a chance to examine the proposal in detail, but we have been assured by key EPA leaders that it generally incorporates targets sought by environmentalists and state air quality regulators. We have also been assured this plan will not undermine standards that have already been set for big diesel trucks and buses. We look forward to studying this proposal and working with EPA to complete a strong final rule within the next year."
