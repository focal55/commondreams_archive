Bill Gates, Sr, National Farmers Union Urge House To Oppose Repeal Of Estate Tax
   WASHINGTON - June 16 - On Capitol Hill tomorrow, Bill Gates, Sr. will join a national farmers group to urge the House of Representatives not to pass H.R. 8. This bill would permanently extend the current one-year repeal of the federal estate tax in 2010. The bill is expected to come to a vote Wednesday or Thursday of this week.

WHEN: Tuesday June 17, 2003 at 12:30 p.m.

WHERE: Cannon Terrace, Cannon House Office Building

WHO: ** William H. Gates, Sr., chairman of the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, board member of the United Way of America, and author of "Wealth and Our Commonwealth: Why America Should Tax Accumulated Fortunes"

** Tom Buis, Vice President of Government Relations for the National Farmers Union, which represents over 300,000 farmers and ranchers

** Seth Goldman, co-founder of Honest Tea, is a successful business owner interested in leaving his business to his children and believes that his estate should be taxed.

Americans for a Fair Estate Tax is a broad-based non-partisan coalition of nonprofit groups advocating for estate tax reform that would ensure that family farms and small businesses are not unfairly taxed while keeping 98 percent of taxpayers exempt.

The Fair Taxes For All Coalition is a massive coalition of organizations including People For the American Way, National Women's Law Center, AFSCME, the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights, USAction, United for a Fair Economy, and the Campaign for America¹s Future. More than 300 national, state and local groups and coalitions ­ together representing millions of Americans ­ have pledged to defeat Bush Administration tax proposals that would take resources from health care, education, Social Security, and homeland security in order to give massive tax breaks to the very wealthiest Americans.
