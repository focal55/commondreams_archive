Kucinich: Nike v. Kasky Benchmark Case on Corporate Accountability
   WASHINGTON - April 23 - Today, the United States Supreme Court will hear oral arguments in the case of Nike v. Kasky. The case could reshape Constitutional definitions of commercial and political speech, giving corporations unprecedented freedom to make false and misleading statements in advertisements, newspaper editorials, and other venues.

Today, Congressman Dennis J. Kucinich (D-OH), who filed a "friend of the court" brief on behalf of Kasky, issued the following statement.

"I'm pleased that Nike has decided to become concerned about the liberties provided for in the United States Constitution. Unfortunately, their interest in the First Amendment came about right about the same time their own audit revealed factual misstatements to consumers.

"With this case, Nike seeks to extend the right of all corporations to flout state consumer protection laws and head for cover under the First Amendment. For consumer protection, for corporate accountability, and for the integrity of the Constitution, it is absolutely critical that the Supreme Court clarifies that there is no Constitutional right of corporations to distort the truth."

On April 4, 2003, Kucinich, along with Reps. Corrine Brown (D-FL), Bob Filner (D-CA) and Bernie Sanders (I-VT), filed a friend of the court brief on the behalf of Kasky with the court.
