Kucinich for President Campaign in Iowa Announces "Rank and File Labor for Kucinich" Steering Committee
   Des Moines-The Kucinich for President campaign today announced the formation of a new group called "Iowa Rank and File Labor for Kucinich." The group will be headed by a Steering Committee currently comprised of 32 members from 15 different labor unions throughout Iowa.

Members of the Steering Committee are Kucinich supporters who have already been reaching out to other union members in their workplaces, union meetings and communities to encourage them to caucus for Kucinich.

Through the Steering Committee, these members will now begin to coordinate their efforts to organize union support for Kucinich across the state leading up to the Iowa caucuses.

Members of the 32-person Steering Committee include Bruce Clark of the American Postal Workers Union (Dubuque), Midge Slater of the Communication Workers of America (Des Moines), Tom Seymour of the United Auto Workers (Bettendorf), Steve Siegel of the American Federation of State, County, and Municipal Employees (Ottumwa), Sylvia Popelka of the Iowa State Education Association (Cedar Rapids), Mike Marvin of the Transport Workers Union (Council Bluffs), and Wilbur Wilson of the United Steelworkers of America (Des Moines).

Rep. Kucinich, a member of the IATSE union, has the highest lifetime AFL-CIO COPE voting record of any candidate in the Presidential race (98%) and is the only candidate in the race who has pledged to cancel NAFTA and the WTO upon entering office. Rep. Kucinich routinely draws very enthusiastic responses from union audiences and has gathered a strong following of rank-and-file union members from across Iowa.
