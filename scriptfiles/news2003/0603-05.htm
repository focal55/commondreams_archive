New Study Warns That Bush's Proposal for Medicare Privatization Will Lead to Restrictions in Seniors' Choice of Doctors;
Surveys in Maine, Oregon, Utah, Vermont and West Virginia Show Private Insurers Featured in Bush Plan Exclude Most Physicians Currently Treating Medicare Patients
   WASHINGTON - June 3 - MEDIA ADVISORY

CONTACT: Ben Bergman (202)-454-5112, or Ben Peck (202) 454-5155, or Shannon Little (202) 588-7742

WHAT: Press conference to release new report showing that most Medicare doctors in the states of Maine, Oregon, Utah, Vermont and West Virginia do not participate with the private managed care plans that are the centerpiece of the Medicare "reform" proposal supported by the Bush administration and Senate Majority Leader Bill Frist. The Bush administration and Sen. Frist are urging Congress to vote this month to remodel the Medicare program so that it more closely resembles the Federal Employees Health Benefits Program (FEHBP). In that program, private managed care plans compete to offer coverage to federal employees, retirees and their dependents.

The report looks at the number of doctors participating in Medicare and how many of them participate in private plans sponsored by the FEHBP in 20 counties - four from each of the five surveyed states. The report finds that beneficiaries who leave Medicare and enroll in private plans will find that most of the doctors that accepted Medicare, including their longtime health care providers, do not participate in the private plans. The counties studied are: Franklin, Waldo, Oxford and Penobscot in Maine; Harney, Wasco, Polk and Marion in Oregon; Wayne, Emery, Sanpete and Weber in Utah; Lamoille, Caledonia, Windham and Rutland in Vermont; Pendleton, Taylor, Jackson and Harrison in West Virginia.

WHO: Sen. John D. Rockefeller IV (D-W.V.) Sen. Edward M. Kennedy (D-Mass.) Sen. Debbie A. Stabenow (D-Mich.) Frank Clemente, director, Public Citizen's Congress Watch Marilyn Moon, senior fellow, Urban Institute and former Medicare trustee
