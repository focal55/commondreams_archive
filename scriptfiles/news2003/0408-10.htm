Amnesty International to Hold Candlelight Vigil One Year After Death of Charleston Resident Chad Boggess
  CHARLESTON, WV - April 8 - News Advisory:

-- Amnesty International to Hold Candlelight Vigil One Year After Death of Charleston Resident Chad Boggess

-- Family Members and Activists Call for Justice, Launch Charleston Chapter of Amnesty International

On Friday, April 11, the one-year anniversary of the death of Charleston resident Chad Boggess, who was attacked and beaten by one or more prison officials, representatives from Amnesty International will stand with members of the Boggess family and call for justice at a candlelight vigil. Boggess' aunt, Tina Davis, and Shawn Gaylord of Amnesty International, will speak. Spearheaded by Davis, Amnesty International will launch a Charleston chapter the previous day, Thursday, April 10.

Boggess died in a Kentucky hospital on April 11, 2002 after he was severely beaten at the Boyd County Detention Center in Ashland, Ky., where he suffered a heart attack and slipped into a coma from which he never awoke. Amnesty International quickly called for an investigation into the circumstances of his death, and an FBI investigation has resulted in one indictment. Deputy jailer Robert Price pled guilty to aggravated assault and will be sentenced in May of 2003, and the local prosecutor has indicated that he may seek additional indictments.

WHO: Tina Davis of the Boggess family, Shawn Gaylord of Amnesty International, and other family and Amnesty representatives

WHAT: Candlelight Vigil for Chad Boggess Launch of Charleston Amnesty International Chapter

WHEN: Vigil: Friday, April 11 at 7 p.m. Launch: Thursday, April 10 at 6:30 p.m.

WHERE: Vigil: Haddad Park on the Boulevard, Charleston, WV Launch: 3511 Venable Ave, Kanawha City, Charleston, WV
