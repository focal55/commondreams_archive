CWA Warns Delay Equals Death; Hyde-Lantos Global AIDS Bill Must Pass Immediately with No Amendments
  WASHINGTON - May 15 - Concerned Women for America (CWA) President Sandy Rios called on the Senate to pass the Hyde/Lantos global AIDS bill today without amendment, adding any delay would bring more death to Africa.

"Men, women and children are dying in Africa; this is no time for political games" said Rios. "The House bill is already a compromise bill; it passed with overwhelming support. To delay the lifesaving assistance this bill provides would be to fiddle while Rome burns."

Since the House passed the Hyde/Lantos bill, an estimated 112,000 people have died from AIDS; an estimated 182,000 have become infected; and an estimated 26,600 babies have been born with the virus.

"Senator Majority Leader Bill Frist will bring the Hyde/Lantos bill to the floor today, May 15 -- and we expect the Senate to pass it today," said Michael Schwartz, CWA's vice president for government relations. "Any senator who tries to offer an amendment to this bill is choosing to create a huge delay -- not only in the Senate's deliberation today, but in a future conference committee. In the battle against AIDS, every delay equals death."

CWA is thankful for President Bush's call to this mission and for the House's swift efforts to produce a bi-partisan Global AIDS bill that will save lives.

"We know that some traditional AIDS activists and their allies in the Senate will want the bill killed because it contains the Pitts amendment, which reserves just one-third of prevention funds for abstinence programs," said Schwartz. "But abstinence was a key part of AIDS prevention in the only country in the world to lower HIV/AIDS rates, Uganda. These people need to face the reality of what works, and care more about the dying men, women and children of Africa than their failing pet projects."
