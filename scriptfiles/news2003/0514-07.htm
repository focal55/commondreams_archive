Million Mom March Joins Forces with Meetup.com, Continue Aggressive Grassroots Growth, Prepare for Future Mobilizations
  WASHINGTON - May 14 - The Million Mom March organization and Meetup Inc. today announced a new partnership to harness the growing power of technology to allow the Million Mom March to continue building an already strong grassroots "army" of volunteers concerned about the terrible toll that gun violence takes in America.

The partnership will allow the "Moms" to use Meetup.com to generate monthly face-to-face get-togethers with others who want to work to reduce gun violence in America. Through http://millionmoms.meetup.com, citizens across America will be able to self-organize and meet in local places every month.

"The Moms are on the march. This organization is getting bigger every day -- and we expect this technology will expand our organization significantly in the months ahead," said Mary Leigh Blek, president of the Million Mom March. "I think of Meetup.com as being the modern equivalent of a town hall bulletin board. We're a chapter-based organization that can benefit tremendously from these new tools."

"What's more American than groups of Americans gathering and mobilizing around an issue that they care deeply about? The first Saturday of every month is now Million Mom Meetup Day in America," said Scott Heiferman, CEO of Meetup, Inc. "Meetup's mission is to create local group gatherings and help stimulate local community, and we look forward to hearing stories from -- and seeing photos of -- Mom's across America getting involved with a cause that they believe in."

The first "Million Mom Meetup Day" is Saturday, June 7 at 1 p.m. local time across America. Meetups will commence in upwards of 490 U.S. cities and small towns and will continue on the first Saturday of each month thereafter.

Meetup, Inc. creates global Meetup Days for most any topic. Meetups are real world, face-to-face get-togethers that take place in 561 cities in 34 countries at local cafis, restaurants, bookstores, and other local establishments. Meetup's mission is to create local group gatherings and help stimulate local community. Since launching in June 2003, Meetup has become a grassroots phenomenon, with over 300,000 people signed up for monthly Meetups on more than 1,100 topics and interests. Meetup is based in New York City and is privately held. Online at http://meetup.com.

Founded in 1999, the Million Mom March is a national grassroots, chapter-based organization dedicated to preventing gun death and injury and supporting victims and survivors of gun violence. The Million Mom March focuses on education and advocacy through grassroots activity across the country and seeking responsible limits on gun access and use. As the nation's largest national, non-partisan, grassroots organization leading the fight to prevent gun violence, the Brady Campaign, Brady Center and the Million Mom March are dedicated to creating an America free from gun violence, where all Americans are safe at home, at school, at work and in the communities. Online at http://www.millionmommarch.org.
