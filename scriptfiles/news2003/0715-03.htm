Anti Family Planning Lawmakers Jeopardize Health and Welfare of Poorest Women
  

WASHINGTON - July 15 - Washington, DC – “Today anti-choice politicians in the House turned their backs on the world’s poorest women by allowing the Bush Administration to eliminate UNFPA funds that provide health care and voluntary family planning services to women in developing countries.”

“Family-planning opponents in the House sacrificed the health and well-being of the world’s poorest women for the sake of the President’s domestic political agenda. Lawmakers shamelessly gutted the Crowley amendment authorizing $50 million in funding to the UNFPA.”

“UNFPA money provides basic health care and family planning services, yet anti-family-planning lawmakers continue to mislead and mischaracterize the program in their drive to deny services to millions of women worldwide.”
