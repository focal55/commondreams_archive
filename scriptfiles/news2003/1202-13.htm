DOD Delays Boeing Tanker Lease; Inspector General to Investigate
   WASHINGTON - December 2 - The Pentagon today said it will delay the execution of the lease and purchase of 100 Boeing tanker aircraft until the Inspector General investigates possible “improper conduct” on the part of a top Air Force official and Boeing’s former chief financial officer.

In a letter to Senate Armed Services Chairman John Warner, Deputy Secretary of Defense Paul Wolfowitz said he took the action to postpone the lease and purchase contract in light of recent actions by Boeing to fire Boeing CFO Mike Sears and Boeing Vice President Darleen Druyun, a former Air Force chief acquisition official, for discussing Druyun’s employment with Boeing before she left the Pentagon.

“Finally the Pentagon is taking this unethical behavior seriously,” said POGO Senior Defense Investigator Eric Miller. “From the beginning this tanker lease has been suspect. The more closely this corporate bailout is investigated, the worse it looks for the taxpayers. We look forward to a full public disclosure of the details of this questionable contract negotiation. We think a complete investigation is warranted.”
