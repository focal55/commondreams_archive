TomPaine.com Challenges Washington's 'Twisted Allegiance' to Tax Cuts
  WASHINGTON - April 9 -The Internet Journal TomPaine.com today ridiculed House Majority Leader Tom DeLay's statement that "Nothing is more important in the face of war than cutting taxes." The Opinion Ad continued, "Crazy? Yes. But that's Tom DeLay. He's demonstrating the twisted allegiance that now dominates Washington - tax cuts for the wealthy before all other needs."

The ad - the latest in the journal's ongoing series - points out that it's not just Tom DeLay who has a bizarre view of what's important today: "You can see it in President Bush's agenda, and in the budget just passed by the House: huge tax cuts for millionaires, corporations and investors, funded by deep cuts in programs for needy Americans - Medicaid, food stamps, school lunches and, even in the midst of war, benefit cuts for veterans. It's obscene."

DeLay's comment, TomPaine.com notes, is in keeping with other proponents of the extreme tax-cut ideology. For example, Grover Norquist of Americans for Tax Reform has said that he wants to shrink government until "we can drown it in the bathtub." Dan Mitchell of the Heritage Foundation has compared tax evaders to civil rights leader Rosa Parks while lobbying to preserve tax havens used by wealthy Americans to avoid paying their fair share. Stephen Moore of the Club for Growth has said, railing against Social Security, "One of the biggest myths in politics today is this idea that grandparents care about their grandkids."

TomPaine.com charges that "If they didn't push such an agenda, Moore, Mitchell, Norquist and their ilk wouldn't get financial support from Microsoft, GM, Ford, Dow, GTE, American Express and other leading compabies. They'd be out on the fringe instead of at the center of power ... And Tom DeLay, a former exterminator, might still be stomping roaches in Texas. "
