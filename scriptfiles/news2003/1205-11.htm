Mother of One of More Than 400 Women Murdered in Juarez, Mexico to Visit Capitol Hill, Speak at Vigil, Receive Human Rights Award
   WASHINGTON - December 5 - Norma Andrade, whose daughter is one of the more than 370 young women who have been murdered or are missing in Ciudad Juarez, Chihuahua State, Mexico, will be visiting the D.C. area on Tuesday, Dec. 9 and Wednesday, Dec. 10, which is International Human Rights Day. The D.C. Women's Human Rights Action Team of Amnesty International USA is sponsoring her visit, which will include meetings with Congressional leaders, being given a human rights award, and speaking at a vigil in DuPont Circle Park.

On Tuesday, Dec. 9, Ms. Andrade will speak at a luncheon in Room B-369, Rayburn House Office Building on Capital Hill sponsored by the Congressional Women's, Hispanic, and Human Rights Caucuses to raise awareness about her daughter, Lilia Alejandra Garcia Andrade, who was found murdered on February 21, 2001. Lilia is one of more than 370 young women who have been murdered or are missing in Ciudad Juarez, Mexico since early 1993. Representative Solis and a bipartisan coalition has introduced legislation calling on the Mexican and US governments to do more to put an end to these gruesome crimes, which frequently involve the rape and mutilation of the girls and women. She will also meet with officials from the US Department of State. MEDIA AVAILABILITY at 2:30 p.m. following the luncheon.

On Wednesday, Dec. 10, International Human Rights Day and the 55th anniversary of the adoption of the Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Ms. Andrade will meet with representatives from US Senator Mark Dayton's (D-Minn.) office. That night, she will be the guest of honor at an event sponsored by the D.C. Women's Human Rights Action Team of Amnesty International in Dupont Circle Park. At the event Amnesty International USA will also announce its new, national action calling on its more than 300,000 activist members to cosponsor H. R. 466 on the murders of young women in Ciudad Juarez and Chihuahua, Mexico. The event will begin at 5:30 PM, open to the public, and is designed to give a voice to all of the murdered and missing girls and women in Ciudad Juarez. Sociologist, and Coordinating Committee Member, D.C. Women's Human Rights Action Team, Janel Kasper-Wolfe, and the DC Poets Against the War will also be participating in this event. MEDIA AVAILABILITY at 9 a.m. at Amnesty International USA, 600 Pennsylvania Ave, SE, 5th Floor. Call-in number will be distributed prior to the availability.

For more information on the murders in Ciudad Juarez, see the Amnesty International Report, Intolerable Killings, at http://www.amnestyusa.org/women/juarez/download_report.html
