NARAL Pro-Choice America Statement on Judicial Nominees
   WASHINGTON - November 12 - The following is a statement by Kate Michelman, president, NARAL Pro-Choice America:

President Bush's anti-choice allies in the Senate have missed the point: the reason he can't get his judicial nominees confirmed is not a shortage of Senate debate, but an excess of anti-choice, anti-privacy activism.

They'll be staying up all night tonight, but many Americans stay up late every night worrying about the President's tireless efforts to take away our rights. If George Bush would simply nominate judges who respect basic liberties, everyone could get a good night's sleep.

Bill Frist, Trent Lott and Rick Santorum can talk all they want to, but nothing they can say will make these far-right activist judges acceptable to Americans who believe in the right to privacy and right to choose.

In particular this week, the President's friends believe that by showcasing three female nominees they will make us forget the range of nominees - including the three in question - who would work to limit the rights of all women if confirmed. In other words, they think we're fools.

These nominees have long histories of putting their anti-privacy, anti-choice ideology ahead of fair interpretation of the law. That puts them out of the mainstream of American legal thought, but right in the mainstream of President Bush's judicial nominations.
