Americans United Blasts Plan To Ram D.C. Vouchers Through Congress
   WASHINGTON - November 17 - Americans United for Separation of Church and State today criticized a plan by voucher proponents in Congress to circumvent debate on a controversial school voucher proposal by attaching it to an omnibus federal spending bill that cannot be amended.

In a last-ditch effort to send the school voucher program for the District of Columbia to a supportive president, Republican leaders will include the scheme in an omnibus spending bill that is expected to be approved by Congress before it adjourns for Thanksgiving, according The Washington Post.

“Voucher supporters in the Senate know they can’t pass this bill on an up-and-down vote, so they are resorting to strong-arm tactics to get their way,” said the Rev. Barry W. Lynn, executive director of Americans United. “The voucher measure drains desperately needed dollars from the public schools and transfers them to religious and other private schools. It’s bad policy for public education, and it would violate Americans’ right to support only the religious institutions of their free choice.”

In September, the U.S. House of Representatives passed by a one-vote margin the D.C. school voucher bill that would allocate $10 million in federal funds to pay for tuition for about 1,300 students to attend religious and other private schools. A $13-million voucher bill has stalled in the Senate.

Americans United and other members of the National Coalition for Public Education have urged lawmakers to oppose the voucher bills. The coalition has also asked members of Congress not to attach the proposals to other legislation that cannot be amended or debated.

Lynn urged Americans to voice alarm over the tactics congressional lawmakers are using to jam the federal voucher proposal into law.

“This is no way to approve legislation,” Lynn said. “The voucher proposal deserves to be studied and debated. Its supporters are afraid of such scrutiny and therefore are resorting to harsh measures to win the day for vouchers. ”
