Global Warming-Related Risk: The Next Big Hurdle for Electricity Companies
  

WASHINGTON - November 11 -

WHAT:
A discussion for U.S. press of the new report Power Switch: Impacts of Climate Policy on the Power Sector-commissioned from international financial analysts Innovest Strategic Value Advisors-that will be released at the London Stock Exchange on the same day.

WHO:
Dr. Martin Whittaker, lead author and managing director, Innovest Strategic Value Advisors; Tony White, head of research, Climate Change Capital and former head of Pan-European Utility Research, Schroder Salomon Smith Barney; Rebecca Eaton, manager, Private Sector Initiatives, World Wildlife Fund; and Mark Kenber, economist, World Wildlife Fund moderator: Katherine Silverthorne, director, U.S. Climate Change Program, World Wildlife Fund

WHEN:
9:30 a.m. ET on Wednesday, Nov. 12, 2003

WHERE:
Via phone conference 1-800-247-5110 (USA)
Code: 35017

DETAILS:
The new report analyzes the financial risks and opportunities that 14 major international electric utilities-including three U.S. utilities-face from existing and expected global and national climate policies, and illustrates how their responses to this risk could impact their bottom line and, in turn, shareholder value.
