Jim Hightower, i.e. America Radio Network Join Forces to Pack Progressive Radio Punch
   April 7 - Jim Hightower, America's #1 Populist, has joined forces with the i.e. America Radio Network to bring his hard-hitting "Common Sense Commentaries" to the nation's airwaves.

Hightower's "Common Sense Commentaries" are America's longest running progressive radio program, having recently celebrated their 10th anniversary. The two-minute "pops of populism" air each weekday on more than 100 affiliates worldwide.

On i.e. America, the nation's leading progressive radio network, the programs will air each weekday at 2:33 p.m. EST during The Peter Werbe Show.

"I'm happier than a flea at a dog show to be on the air with my friends at i.e. America," cracks Hightower in his trademark Texas twang. "It's high time we progressives put our heads together to link up the snuff-dippers with the bean-sprout eaters. It's the only way we'll be able to crack the overwhelming right-wing slant of talk radio today and help salvage what's left of our American democracy."

Hightower's "Common Sense Commentaries" serve up a Texas barbecue second to none, zesty food for thought based on kitchen table topics -- from doctor bills and the deficit to toxic dumps and the recession. Hightower will make you chuckle, get you riled up, then send you on your way to raise hell.

"We're proud to air Jim's features on the i.e. America Radio Network," says Suzanne Gougherty, i.e. America General Manager. "His performance and ability to sum up a progressive viewpoint is totally in line with our talk show hosts. We also plan to air the commentaries during the network local breaks and weekends making them more accessible for station usage."

According to Werbe, whose lively, issue-oriented show airs daily from 12-3 p.m. EST, Hightower sets the standard for progressive talk hosts. "Hightower's one of the few people in radio who speaks the truth, regardless of the consequences," Werbe says. "I've admired him for over a decade because of his principled stand on the issues, and I'm excited that he's part of our show."
