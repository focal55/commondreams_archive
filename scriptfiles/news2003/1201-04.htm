11 AM Religious Leaders Action/Press Luncheon for WORLD AIDS DAY
   WASHINGTON - December 1 - Vigil and action highlighting the moral imperative to stop global HIV/AIDS and cancel the debt to be held at U.S. Treasury on World AIDS Day

What: Religious vigil and action at U.S. Treasury building with Catholic, Protestant, and Jewish religious leaders, to be followed by press luncheon

Who: Ms. Brenda Girton-Mitchell, Director, Washington Office, National Council of Churches

Rabbi David Saperstein, Director, Religious Action Center of Reform Judaism

Pastor Wallace Smith, Progressive National Baptist Church

Rev. Seamus Finn, OMI, Vice Chair, Interfaith Center on Corporate Responsibility

Ms. Sarrae Crane, (invited) United Synagogues of Conservative Judaism

United Methodist Church Bishop

Jim Wallis, (invited) Editor, Sojourners magazine

Representatives of Jubilee USA, American Jewish World Service, Global Justice, and Africa Action

When/Where: December 1, 2003, World AIDS Day

11am - 12 noon - Religious vigil and action, sidewalk outside U.S. Treasury Department, 15th and "F" Sts. NW 12 pm - 1 pm - Press luncheon and briefing, Old Ebbitt Grill, Cabinet Room (downstairs), 675 15th St., NW

On World AIDS Day, interfaith leaders will hold a vigil at the U.S. Treasury building and press conference at the Old Ebbitt Grill to send a prophetic, moral call to President Bush and Treasury Secretary John Snow for greater leadership on global AIDS and debt. In letters addressed to both Snow and Bush leaders call for an increase in funding to at least $5.4 billion for global AIDS, TB and malaria in the President's '05 budget request along with full debt cancellation for impoverished nations in order free up resources to fight the HIV/AIDS pandemic. Leaders will specifically urge the Bush administration to live up to the promises made in the President's State of the Union address around global AIDS.
