Over-the-Counter Access to Emergency Contraception Will Reduce Numbers of Unintended Pregnancies and Abortions
   NEW YORK - April 21 - Following is a statement of Vanessa E. Cullins, MD, MPH, MBA, vice president for medical affairs, Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

"Emergency contraception (EC) is safe and effective and should be made available over the counter. Every woman at risk for unintended pregnancy should have EC in her medicine cabinet. Studies show that EC can prevent unintended pregnancy up to five days after unprotected intercourse, thereby reducing the need for abortion. We hope that after a thorough review of the data, the U.S. Food and Drug Administration will change the status of emergency contraception from prescription to over-the-counter.

"Increasing access to EC is a Planned Parenthood priority. Planned Parenthood works in communities, on campuses and in city, state and federal legislatures to advocate for policies that support that goal. Our multi-year public awareness campaign reached millions of women. In Congress, Planned Parenthood supports the Emergency Contraception Education Act and looks forward to the introduction and passage of the Compassionate Care for Sexual Assault Survivors Act."
