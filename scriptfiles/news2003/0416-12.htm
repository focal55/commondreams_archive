League of Conservation Voters Statement on Labor Environment Alliance
  WASHINGTON - April 16 - Following is a statement by League of Conservation Voters (LCV) political director Betsy Loyless on the Labor Environment Alliance:

"The Labor Environment Alliance announced today by the Teamsters and The Council of Republicans for Environmental Advocacy has little to do with strengthening labor and nothing to do with protecting the environment. Studies show that more permanent, high-wage labor jobs would be created by protecting our environment and conserving our natural resources than by destroying them. In fact, mainstream labor groups have worked closely with the environmental community for decades to find commonsense ways to protect our environment and strengthen our economy at the same time. The Labor Environment Alliance is merely an attempt by the Bush administration to scare voters into believing that harmful environmental policies are necessary to America's economic security when the exact opposite is true."

For more information on the success of the existing partnership between labor and the environmental community, or information on the economic benefits of protecting the environment, please contact the LCV Press Office.

The nonprofit League of Conservation Voters (LCV) is the political voice of the national environmental and conservation community. LCV is the only national organization working full-time to hold Congress and the president accountable for their environmental actions.
