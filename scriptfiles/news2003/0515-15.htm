NET's Wathen: Dept. of Defense Environmental Exemptions Vote was Worst Overreach on Environment Since 1994 Gingrich Congress
   WASHINGTON - May 15 - Following is a statement of Tom Wathen, executive vice president and general counsel, National Environmental Trust, on the Department of Defense Environmental Exemptions vote last night:

"Last night, the House Armed Services Committee passed a Department of Defense authorization bill giving the military and all other federal agencies sweeping new exemptions from the nation's bedrock wildlife conservation laws. This sneak attack by House Republicans would make even Newt Gingrich blush.

"This assault on the Endangered Species Act and the Marine Mammals Protection Act is far more extreme than anyone expected. It gives all federal agencies an escape clause from protecting endangered species, and lowers the bar for military and even commercial ships for protecting whales and dolphins. The Republican bill even exempts a military golf course.

"Expect this debate to be hugely controversial when it hits the Floor next week."
