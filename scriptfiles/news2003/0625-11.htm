Lambda Legal to Hold Press Conferences Thursday on Major U.S. Supreme Court Gay Rights Ruling
   NEW YORK - June 25 - - Lambda Legal to Hold Press Conferences Thursday on Major U.S. Supreme Court Gay Rights Ruling

-- Lead counsel on the case to speak on national phone-in press conference and live press conferences in Washington, D.C., and Houston following expected ruling

What: Press conferences on Thursday's expected U.S. Supreme Court decision in Lawrence v. Texas.

When: Thursday June 26

Phone-in: 12 noon Eastern

Washington, D.C.: 1 p.m. Eastern

Houston: 1:30 p.m. Central

---

National Phone-In Press Conference; 12 Noon (Eastern)

WHAT: Phone-in press conference on landmark U.S. Supreme Court ruling, its impact on 13 states with sodomy laws and its broad impact on gay civil rights nationwide.

WHO: Ruth Harlow, lead attorney in the case and legal director at Lambda Legal, which represents John Lawrence and Tyron Garner, who were arrested and convicted for violating the Texas law; Patricia Logue, attorney on the case and Senior Counsel at Lambda Legal.

ACCESS: Call Susannah Lupert at 212-809-8585, ext. 281, or e-mail SLupert@lambdalegal.org for access info.

---

Washington, D.C., Press Conference; 1:30 p.m. (Eastern)

WHAT: Press conference on ruling and its broad impact.

WHO: Kevin Cathcart, executive director at Lambda Legal, which represents John Lawrence and Tyron Garner, plaintiffs in the case who were arrested and convicted for violating the Texas law for engaging in consensual sex at home; Ruth Harlow, lead attorney in the case, legal director at Lambda Legal; Elizabeth Birch, executive director at the Human Rights Campaign, which filed an amicus brief in the case.

WHERE: The Mayflower Hotel, Colonial Room; 1127 Connecticut Ave., NW, Washington, D.C.

---

Houston Press Conference; 1:30 p.m. (Central)

WHAT: Press conference on U.S. Supreme Court ruling, addressing its impact on Texas and the 12 other states with sodomy laws and its much broader impact on gay civil rights nationwide.

WHO: Lee Taft, Texas-based South-Central Regional Director at Lambda Legal, which represents John Lawrence and Tyron Garner, who were arrested and convicted for violating the Texas law; Mitchell Katine, local attorney who has worked on the case; and Clarence Burton Bagby, President, Houston GLBT Community Center.

WHERE: Houston GLBT Community Center; 3400 Montrose Blvd., Suite 207
