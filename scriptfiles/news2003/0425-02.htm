Mock Millionaires Protest Bush Tax Cuts at Correspondent Dinner; Photo Opportunity and Press Availability
  WASHINGTON - April 25 - News Advisory:

-- Tuxedo-ed activists, labor leaders rally to oppose massive tax breaks for millionaires at White House Correspondents Dinner

WHAT: Donning their finest to illustrate just who benefits from Bush's massive tax breaks for the wealthy, members of the Fair Taxes for All Coalition and activists will protest the Administration's inequitable and reckless tax plan.

WHO: Fair Taxes for All Coalition

WHERE: In front of the Washington, D.C. Hilton on the corner of Connecticut Avenue and T Street, NW

WHEN: Saturday, April 26
5:30 p.m. (start time)
The protest will continue before, during and after the White House Correspondents dinner.

The Fair Taxes For All Coalition is a massive coalition of coalitions co-chaired by People For the American Way, National Women's Law Center, AFSCME, the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights, USAction, and the Campaign for America's Future. More than 300 national, state and local groups and coalitions -- together representing millions of Americans -- have pledged to defeat Bush Administration tax proposals that would take resources from health care, education, Social Security, and homeland security in order to give massive tax breaks to the very wealthiest Americans.
