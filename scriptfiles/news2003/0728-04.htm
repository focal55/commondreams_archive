House Rebuffs Bush Administration EPA Cuts
   WASHINGTON - July 28 - The Sierra Club today praised the House for rebuffing a Bush Administration proposal to cut the Environmental Protection Agency's enforcement staff. The House agreed to an amendment introduced by Rep. Inslee (D-WA) to restore enforcement funding improving the ability of the program to hold polluters accountable.

"The House rightly rejected the Bush Administration's proposal to take the environmental cop off the beat," said Nat Mund of Sierra Club's Environmental Quality Program. "This vote will help protect our families' clean air and safe drinking water."

The appropriations bill for VA, HUD, and Independent Agencies, which includes EPA funding, originally slashed enforcement by at least 40 positions as compared to last year's appropriation. Today, the House agreed to restore funding to 2003 levels.

The Bush Administration incorrectly attempted to label this cut an increase in funding because last year's request was for a cut of 200 positions.

Rep. Inslee introduced an amendment to restore funding for EPA enforcement to last year's levels. The amendment passed on a voice vote.

Although states have a principal role in enforcing some federal environmental laws, federal enforcement capability is essential to protecting communities from pollution. The threat of federal enforcement is a vital safety net for communities.

"Americans want tough enforcement of the laws protecting our air and water, but the Bush Administration proposed letting polluters get away red-handed," Mund said. "Strong enforcement of environmental laws is the reason our air and water are cleaner than they were 30 years ago. That kind of progress can only continue with a strong enforcement program.
