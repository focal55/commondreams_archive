Published on Tuesday, May 15, 2001 by Agence France Presse
Nicaraguan Court Eyes Billion-Dollar Damages Figure in Suit Against US Firms
 
MANAGUA, May 14 (AFP) -

A Nicaraguan court has estimated at one billion dollars the damages sought by banana workers against six US companies for using chemicals that cause cancer and other ailments, court sources said Monday.

Representatives of Standard Fruit, Dole, Del Monte, Dow Chemical, Shell Oil Company and Occidental Chemical Corporation have been invited to meet here on June 8 with 3,600 plaintiffs to discuss their compensation demands.

The plaintiffs claim the US companies used insecticides, particularly Nemagon, between 1968 and 1983, which has led to infertility, cancer and other health problems among workers who have come into contact with the chemicals.

The US companies have been notified of the court's determination through the US Embassy here.
