Published on Wednesday, August 29, 2001 in the Guardian of London
France Attacks America's High-Handed Unilateralism
by Jon Henley in Paris
 
The French foreign minister, Hubert Védrine, accused the United States of self-interest yesterday and said that the Bush administration was hindering the international community's efforts to solve the problems of globalization.

In the latest Gallic attack on what is politely referred to in France as "American economic and cultural hegemony", Mr Védrine told French ambassadors in Paris that France would pursue its efforts towards "a humane and controlled globalization., even if the new high-handed American unilateralism doesn't help matters".

Mr Védrine said France had learned from the recent wave of anti-globalisation protests and backed "reasonable demands as far as globalization. is concerned, because we too do not want rampant unrestrained globalization.".

The outburst was by no means Mr Védrine's first attack on US policy. He has previously criticized President Bush's decision to abandon the Kyoto accord to combat global warming and the American president's determination to pursue a US missile defense scheme.

Earlier this year he was also scathing about America's reluctance to commit itself fully to an international campaign against money-laundering. He said that such market-driven attitudes "prevent the emergence of a global consensus to combat the destructive effects of globalization.".
