BOGOTA, Colombia –– Sen. Joseph Lieberman said Thursday that two days in Colombia has only reinforced his support for the U.S.-backed drug crop spraying program.

"I emerged with a strong conviction that it's the right thing, that we're protecting our national security," he said during a meeting with the international press here.

Lieberman, D-Conn., met with Colombian senators, business leaders, U.N. officials, and facilitators in the peace negotiations. He planned to meet with Colombian President Andres Pastrana Friday.

The United States provides $1.3 billion in mostly military aid to Pastrana's Plan Colombia, a drug eradication program based primarily on spraying drug crops with herbicides.

Lieberman said he had always supported the U.S. involvement in Plan Colombia, but his brief trip here convinced him that next year's aid to Colombia, part of a package of aid to the countries of the Andean region being discussed in the Senate and the House, should not be cut drastically. Both sides of Congress have cut what the president requested.

"I hope we can restore at least some of that because the cause merits it," he said.
