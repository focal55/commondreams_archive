Kerry Statement on New Robert Wood Johnson Foundation Report
 

WASHINGTON - May 13 - Democratic Presidential candidate John Kerry released the following statement today in response to a new report focusing on the uninsured and emergency room care by the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation:

“The study released by the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation today confirms what millions of Americans across this country already know – skyrocketing health care costs are straining families’ budgets, overwhelming our emergency rooms and jeopardizing Americans’ health.

“Our health care system is broken. I’ve spent the last four days on the campaign trail talking to families, small businesses owners and veterans about our health care crisis. Everywhere I’ve been, I’ve heard the same stories of families struggling to keep up with rising costs. Americans shouldn’t have to rely on emergency rooms for basic health care services because they can’t afford a regular doctor. This country needs a President who will address the impending health care crisis – instead of turning his back as premiums go through the roof. George Bush has offered no real solution to the rising health care costs – all while he takes millions of dollars from insurance and drug companies. As president, I will build a stronger America by addressing rising health care costs and ensuring access to affordable, quality and reliable coverage for all Americans.”
