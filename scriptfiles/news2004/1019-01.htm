Adelphi University Conference Draws Nobel Laureate Joseph E. Stiglitz, Leading Scholars to Assess Global Welfare State
 

GARDEN CITY, NY - October 19 - Nobel laureate in economics Joseph E. Stiglitz and Joakim Palme, director of Sweden's Institute for Futures Studies, will be the keynote presenters at Adelphi University School of Social Work's conference, "Social Policy as if People Matter: A Cross- National Dialogue," on November 11-12.

Dr. Stiglitz and Dr. Palme will join more than 70 leading experts from across the globe for a timely, post-election examination of the current conditions and future directions of the welfare state in industrialized nations.

"This world-class conference reflects the multi-national scholarship and achievements of Adelphi University School of Social Work faculty and is in keeping with Adelphi's commitment to internationalism," said Andrew W. Safyer, dean of Adelphi University School of Social Work. "We are pleased to partner with the Consulate General of Sweden to bring together distinguished researchers and practitioners from five continents to initiate a much-needed dialogue on such topics as welfare, immigration, and labor policies; aging populations; and gender equity."

The conference follows up on a prominent cross-national study edited by conference co-chair, Adelphi University Professor Gertrude Schaffner Goldberg, and Marguerite G. Rosenthal, Diminishing Welfare: A Cross-National Study of Social Provision (Auburn House, 2002), which examines social and economic policies and the future of the welfare state in nine industrialized nations. Distinguished authors representing all nine countries in the study will convene for the first time at the conference to take a critical look at conclusions drawn at the time of their writing and offer updates to their respective chapters.

The conference will kick off on Thursday, November 11 with a keynote address by Dr. Stiglitz on "Full Employment and Social Well-Being in a Global Economy." Awarded the 2001 Nobel Prize in economics, Dr. Stiglitz is recognized around the globe as a leading economic educator and theorist.

Other November 11 conference highlights include:

-- A discussion by an international and interdisciplinary panel of scholars on social trends and social well-being in nine industrialized nations: Canada, France, Germany, Hungary, Italy, Japan, Sweden, United Kingdom, and the United States

-- Six panel presentations on populations with special needs, including the elderly, immigrants, women, and families and youth

Dr. Palme will open the conference on Friday, November 12 with a keynote presentation on "The Future of the Social Democratic Model." Director of the Institute for Futures Studies since 2002, he is also a professor at the Swedish Institute for Social Research, Stockholm University.

Other November 12 conference highlights include:

-- A roundtable discussion on the past and future of welfare reform led by political science, social work, economics, and legal experts from the U.S. and Canada

-- Eleven panel presentations on challenges to social well- being and their solutions, covering such topics as the future of the welfare state, unemployment and underemployment, health care policies, affordable housing, and overcoming social and economic disadvantage

Conference participants are also invited to take part in a post-conference meeting to discuss establishing an international network to promote decent work for all.

The conference will be held on Thursday, November 11, from 8 a.m. to 6 p.m., and Friday, November 12, from 8 a.m. to 4:30 p.m., at Adelphi University's Ruth S. Harley University Center, 1 South Avenue, Garden City, New York. Continuing education credits from the National Association of Social Workers are available to eligible participants.
