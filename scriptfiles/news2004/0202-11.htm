Bush, Kerry Raise The Biggest Bundles of the 2004 Presidential Campaign
 

WASHINGTON - February 2 - Employees of the law firm Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom contributed $99,800 to John Kerry last year, making them the most generous group of contributors to a Democratic presidential candidate in the 2004 campaign, according to a preliminary study of fourth-quarter campaign finance filings by the nonpartisan Center for Responsive Politics.

President Bush has received by far the biggest "bundle" from a single organization in the 2004 election cycle. Merrill Lynch employees and their immediate families contributed more than $432,000 to Bush's re-election campaign last year. The company's CEO, Stan O'Neal, is listed by the Bush campaign as a "Ranger" who has independently raised at least $200,000 for the candidate.

Employees of the University of California contributed the second-largest sum to a Democratic presidential candidate last year, with $91,300 to Howard Dean's campaign. Goldman Sachs employees were John Edwards' top contributors and the third largest to a Democratic presidential candidate, with $86,350 in contributions. Wesley Clark raised $73,500 from employees of Plastech Engineered Products, his top contributor. Joe Lieberman raised close to $72,000 from his top contributor, the law firm Irell and Manella.

Employees of Radio One, the broadcaster that caters to African-Americans, contributed $15,200 to Al Sharpton, making them his No. 1 contributor. The law firm of Borders & Borders and the United Steelworkers Union are the top contributors to Dennis Kucinich with $6,000 apiece.

Of all the year-end reports filed Saturday, the most intriguing may have been that of Clark, the latest entrant into the pool of major presidential contenders. Clark added more than $10 million to his campaign coffers in the fourth quarter of last year, nearly three times the $3.5 million he raised by the end of the third quarter.

Clark has raised a total of $13.7 million in his presidential campaign account. By the end of last year, he had a little over $3.4 million left in the bank. Plastech Engineered Products, his top contributor, is based in Dearborn, Mich., and manufactures molded plastic equipment, mostly for the auto industry. Plastech CEO Julie Brown gave to a number of presidential candidates last year, including Clark, Dean, Lieberman and Bush.

Bear Stearns, one of the nation's top securities and investment firms, was Clark's second-largest contributor last year, with $59,000 in employee contributions. Lehman Brothers, another investment firm, was third with $55,500; Harvard University employees were fourth with $31,000 and the law firm Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom was fifth with $25,900.

Clark raised one-fifth of his total itemized contributions, or $2.1 million, in California. New York was his second most generous state with $2 million in contributions. Third on his list was Florida, with $586,600 in contributions, and fourth was his home state of Arkansas, with $583,700 in contributions.

The metro areas most generous to Clark last year were New York City ($1.8 million), followed by Los Angeles ($1.1 million), Washington, D.C. ($803,600), Boston ($418,000) and Little Rock, Ark. ($374,000).

Breakdowns by geography and top contributors have been updated for all the major presidential candidates. Additional updates, including those for candidates who have dropped out of the race, will follow. Keep up with the latest figures on the Center's presidential candidate profiles.
