Leading Women to Charge Bush with Failing Wisconsin's Moms by Failing to Act on Mercury Poisoning of State's Women and Children
Bipartisan group includes TV actress, leading Republican
Conference call for political and environmental reporters, Tuesday, Oct. 5
 

WASHINGTON - October 4 -

WHAT: Conference call with leaders of national tour by prominent women from all walks of life who are critical of George W. Bush's environmental record.

They will release a new report, Mercury: The Hidden Poisoning of Wisconsin's Women and Children, and fault President Bush for proposing that coal-burning electric plants be allowed to put 5 times as much mercury into the environment as the Clean Air Act would otherwise allow.

They will also charge that Bush has abandoned the "polluter pays" principle and is letting the Superfund go bankrupt, leaving Wisconsin's taxpayers stuck with the bill for toxic waste cleanups.

WHO:

-- Melissa Fitzgerald, TV actress, The West Wing, who like many women in Hollywood is increasingly concerned with her mercury exposure from eating fish.

-- Lynn Goldman, MD, MPH, professor at Johns Hopkins University School of Public Health in Baltimore, Md., pediatrician, and chair of the Children's Environmental Health Network. Former asst. administrator, Office of Prevention, Pesticides and Toxic Substances, U.S. EPA.

-- Lois Marie Gibbs, "the mom who took on Love Canal," which led to the creation of the Superfund for toxic waste cleanups, who is now executive director of the Center for Health, Environment, and Justice.

-- Martha Marks, president and founder in 1995 of REP America, the national grassroots organization of Republicans for Environmental Protection.

-- and other leading women and conservationists.

WHEN: 11 a.m. to noon Central, Tuesday, Oct 5
