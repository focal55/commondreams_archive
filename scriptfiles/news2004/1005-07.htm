New CIA Evidence Outs Cheney's Deception Tying Al-Qaeda to Saddam
Will Veep Debate Tonight Reveal Further Cheney Misleads?
 
WASHINGTON - October 5 - A new CIA report shows that Vice President Cheney was dead wrong about repeated claims that Saddam Hussein harbored Abu Musab al Zarqawi -- a suspected associate of al-Qaeda -- and that al-Qaeda had a working relationship with Saddam Hussein. Cheney has used these claims to justify the invasion of Iraq.

Today, Knight Ridder reported that according to the intelligence agency’s assessment, "there is no conclusive evidence that the regime harbored terrorist Abu Musab al Zarqawi." Furthermore, the wire service quotes a senior U.S. intelligence official who says ‘‘the evidence is that Saddam never gave Zarqawi anything.''

In the vice presidential debate tonight, Vice President Cheney will attempt to showcase his experience and judgment in foreign policy-making. But Cheney and the Bush administration have repeatedly been far off the mark. For example, on June 21, 2004 Cheney said "Mr. Zarqawi, who is in Baghdad today, is an al Qaeda associate who took refuge in Baghdad, found sanctuary and safe harbor there before we ever launched into Iraq." Similarly, President Bush said on June 15, 2004 "Zarqawi's the best evidence of a [Hussein] connection to al Qaeda affiliates and al Qaeda."

As the new CIA report reveals, there is simply no evidence to support these claims.

Ironically, it was Cheney who requested the CIA assessment.
