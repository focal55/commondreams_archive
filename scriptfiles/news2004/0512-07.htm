Broad Coalition of National Organizations Denounces FDA Decision to Deny Women Access to Emergency Birth Control
 

WASHINGTON - May 12 - Forty-two women's, health, and religious organizations (list below) today joined Members of Congress and a long list of medical and public health organizations and scientists in denouncing the Food and Drug Administration's denial of over-the-counter status for emergency contraception.

Speaking at a press conference convened by Congresswoman Louise M. Slaughter (D-N.Y.) and Congressman Christopher Shays (R-Conn.), representatives of the organizations called for quick and decisive action to make emergency contraception as widely and easily available as possible. Many of the 27 organizations- including the American Civil Liberties Union, NARAL Pro-Choice America, the Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice, the National Family Planning and Reproductive Health Association, the National Partnership for Women and Families, and Population Connection-called for an inquiry into the FDA process.

The FDA's denial came after the FDA's own scientific advisory panel overwhelmingly recommended that emergency contraception-a higher dose of ordinary birth control pills-be made available to women over-the counter.

Many of the organizations echoed Congresswoman Slaughter's comment that "The FDA claims it wants to protect young women's health, but it is clear they are concerned instead about partisan politics."

Half of all pregnancies in the United States are unintended. Medical experts agree that over-the-counter access to emergency contraception could dramatically reduce rates of unintended pregnancy and, therefore, the need for abortion. However, time is of the essence because emergency contraception must be taken within 72 hours after unprotected sex or contraceptive failure; taken without that time period, emergency contraception can reduce the average risk of pregnancy by up to 89 percent. Emergency contraception prevents a pregnancy; it has no effect on an established pregnancy and does not cause an abortion.
