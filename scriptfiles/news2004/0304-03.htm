Farmworkers Slam Taco Bell For Ignoring Workers' Rights
 

OAKLAND, CA - March 4 - Food First and Bay Area Support Committee Join the Coalition of Immokalee Workers for Huge Rally. Speakers Include Eric Schlosser, Author of Fast Food Nation and Anuradha Mittal of Food First. Performances by Tom Morello of Rage Against the Machine, and Hip-Hop Artists Lila Downs and Boots Riley.

Who

Coalition of Immokalee Workers (CIW), Food First/Institute for Food and Development Policy, and the CIW Bay Area Support Committee.

What

Friday, March 5, from 12:00 noon to 6 pm, farmworkers, members of labor unions, student groups, activists, and religious organizations of the 2004 Taco Bell Truth Tour, will arrive at Taco Bell headquarters to demand that Taco Bell pressure its tomato producers to pay their workers a living wage.

Farmworkers in East Coast tomato fields are forced to work in conditions ranging from sweatshops to slavery. The Department of Justice (DOJ) has prosecuted five multi-state farm slavery cases involving over 1,000 workers in the past five years. The CIW assisted the DOJ in the investigation and prosecution in four of these cases. The story of the CIW has been covered in The New Yorker and National Geographic, and on PBS.

The Taco Bell Truth Tour, organized by the CIW, began in Louisville, Kentucky on February 27, where farmworkers, labor unions, religious organizations, and student groups gathered at the corporate offices of Yum! Brands, the corporate owners of Taco Bell and the largest restaurant company in the world, to demand better pay and working conditions for farmworkers.

Where

Friday, March 5, 12:00 noon to 6pm, at Taco Bell Headquarters at 17901 Von Karman in Irvine, CA.

Why

The CIW has organized a national boycott against Taco Bell since 2000. After several years of marches, truth tours and hunger strikes, the multi-billion dollar corporation is still unwilling to meet the demands of the farmworkers who are asking just a penny more for a bucket of tomatoes.
