Return from Sudan: 'The World's Worst Humanitarian Crisis'
 

WASHINGTON -- November 16 -- Food for the Hungry President Ben Homan and Special Assistant Pete Howard have returned from Sudan where they visited with refugees and internally displaced people.

They are available for media interviews by calling Tamara Dutch, 602-284-7433 (cell).

RELEVANCE TO THE WORLD:

It's genocide. Unanimously, the U.S. House of Representatives and U.S. Senate have officially named the extreme killings against the blacks by the Arab militia, known as the "Janjaweed," genocide. The ethnic cleansings have also been coined, the world's worst humanitarian crisis."

RELEVANCE TO YOU:

Local connection. Two local staff have just returned from Sudan. One of the world's most influential relief and development organizations, Food for the Hungry, is located right in Arizona - 1224 E. Washington St.

Since the situation began in 2003, the conflict has affected more than 2.2 million people in the Darfur and Eastern Chad regions. Of those, 1.6 million have been torn from their homes in the Darfur region alone, labeling them as internally-displaced persons (IDPs). Another 70,000 people have died and USAID figures report there are 200,000 Sudanese refugees now living in Eastern Chad.

HOW WE'RE HELPING:

Through a partnership called the Darfur Relief Collaboration (DRC), headed by World Relief, the group recently received a grant from the Canadian Foodgrains Bank (CFGB) for nearly $500,000 to begin work in three remote villages in West Darfur that are straining to host thousands of internally displaced families (IDPs).

Relief services are initially being provided to 35,000 people in the following three West Darfur villages: Um Tagouk, Azirni and Sanidadiall within 100 kilometers from El Geneina. We are implementing health, water and sanitation programs immediately with long-term programs to follow.
