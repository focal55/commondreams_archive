Pennsylvania AIDS Activist Disrupt President Bush at Small, Private Battleground State Campaign Appearance
 

CHALFONT, PA - September 9 - In three waves of interruptions, seven AIDS activists disrupted President Bush's campaign appearance at the small venue of Byers Choice factory in the suburbs of Southeastern Pennsylvania. The AIDS activists held signs reading: "BUSH: GLOBAL AIDS LIAR" and "$30 BILLION FOR AIDS NOW!" while chanting "Bush lies, people die, $30 billion for AIDS now."


A protestor shouts slogans, interrupting US President George W. Bush as he made remarks on the economy at Byer's Choice in Colmar, Pennsylvania, September 9, 2004. Bush is spending the day campaigning in Pennsylvania. REUTERS/Jason Reed
An activist shouted a question to President Bush: "The U.S. fair share of global AIDS contributions is $30 billion by 2008. Why do you refuse to pay America's fair share?"

The activists were escorted from the event, and charged with disorderly conduct. The protest comes one week after several high-profile protests and arrests during the Republican National Convention criticizing President Bush's record on AIDS, including a disruption of White House Chief of Staff Andrew Card's speech on the Main Stage of Madison Square Garden.

"American voters have to hold Bush accountable for his compassion deficit on AIDS," said Jose DeMarco of ACT UP Philadelphia.

Bush's controversial bilateral AIDS plan, called "PEPFAR" (the President's Emergency Plan for AIDS Relief), has come under criticism by activists and public health experts. According to critics, PEPFAR underfunds key programs like the Global Fund to Fight AIDS, Tuberculosis and Malaria, and it funds HIV prevention programs that are based in ideology, not scientific evidence.

In addition, the White House has refused to support 100% debt cancellation for at least 50 of the world's poorest countries, although billions of dollars in resources made available through debt cancellation could be invested in life saving HIV treatment and prevention.

The finance ministers of the Group of 7 industrialized countries (G7), including John Snow, US Treasury Secretary, meet October 1 to consider a proposal for 100% debt cancellation. "8,500 people are dying with HIV every day.

"The stakes couldn't be higher," said Rowda Moallin of ACT UP Philadelphia. "President Bush won't cancel poor countries' debts, he won't spend the money needed to fight AIDS, and he won't support science based HIV prevention programs. If that's President Bush's version of compassion, I would hate to see indifference."
