NBA Player Leads Student Movement to Get Money Out of Politics: Brings 150 Students to Albany for National Summit
 

WASHINGTON - February 11 - WHO: NBA player and Colgate University graduate Adonal Foyle, founder and president of Democracy Matters-a national non-profit non-partisan organization that gives students a voice in issues of democracy. Plus...150 students from across the country actively involved in the movement to get money out of politics.

WHAT: National Student Summit featuring a presentation by Adonal Foyle plus two days of workshops and lectures by prominent activists.

WHERE: State University of New York, Albany

WHEN: Friday, February 13th through Sunday, February 15th

Democracy Matters and its founder-Golden State Warrior star player and Colgate University graduate Adonal Foyle-have been gaining national attention for helping students understand the connections between money in politics and issues of national interest. Democracy Matters' National Student Summit brings together student leaders from across the country to discuss the problem of money in politics, explore democratic theory, and plan national strategies for 2004.

Summit attendees will include students from many of Democracy Matters' 50 chapters nationwide, including: American University, Barnard College, Bloomsburg University, Boston University, Brown University, Colgate University, Cornell University, Columbia University, Duke University, Elon University, Emerson College, Johnson C. Smith University, Loyola University/New Orleans, Mount Holyoke College, State University of New York/Oneonta, Oregon State, Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute (RPI), Saint Anselm College, Saint Could State University, Saint Lawrence University, Stockton University, University of Akron, University of Connecticut, University of Iowa, University of Massachusetts, University of Minnesota/Morris, University of New Orleans, University of Puget Sound, Ulster County Community College, University of Southern California, Vassar College, Wayne State College, Wesleyan University, West Liberty State College, and Yale University.
