Military Families Call on Bush Administration:
Start Telling the Truth, Stop Hiding the Toll, Bring an End to Iraq War
 
WASHINGTON - March 11 - On March 14 and 15, as the first anniversary of the Iraq war approaches, military families, including those who have lost loved ones in Iraq, veterans, clergy and peace activists will gather at Dover Air Force Base in Delaware to begin a Memorial Procession for Mourning and Truth to pull back the veil, honor and mourn the dead and acknowledge the wounded -- both U.S. military personnel and the tens of thousands of Iraqi casualties. The memory of these individuals will then be brought to the White House, along with the plea: start telling the truth, stop hiding the toll, and bring an end to this war. The event is organized by Military Families Speak Out, Veterans for Peace, Iraq Pledge of Resistance and other organizations.

WHO:
Military families, veterans, and peace activists

WHAT:
Memorial Procession from Dover, Delaware, to Washington D.C.

WHEN:
-- Sunday, March 14: Dover, Delaware

-- Monday, March 15: Washington, D.C.

WHERE:
Sunday, March 14:

-- 12:30 p.m. Gather at Camden Friends Meetinghouse in Camden, Delaware

-- 1:15 p.m. Casualty Families Available for Interview at Dover Air Force Base

-- 2:30 p.m. Ceremony at the gates of Dover Air Force Base

Monday, March 15:

-- 9 a.m. Gathering and observances at Walter Reed Army Hospital in Washington, DC; current residence of many U.S. service people wounded in Iraq, followed by memorial procession to White House

-- 2:30 p.m. North side of White House
