Campaign Finance Panel Discussion at the NPC Oct. 14
 

WASHINGTON - October 13 - 527 group ads, such as those aired by MoveOn.org and Swift Boat Veterans for Truth, have influenced the current presidential election and brought into question the campaign finance laws that regulate them. The panel will discuss the future of these campaign finance regulations, including: the original intention of campaign finance laws pertaining specifically to 527s, campaign contribution limits, the public financing of campaigns, and more.

What: Deregulation of Campaign Finance: What Can Be Done? What Should be Done?

Who:

-- Craig M. Engle, Arent Fox

-- Dr. Craig Holman, Legislative representative, Public Citizen

-- Michael J. Malbin, executive director, Campaign Finance Institute

-- Stuart Taylor, National Journal

-- Allison Hayward, Federal Election Commission

When: Thursday, Oct. 14, 12 noon to 2 p.m.

Where: National Press Club, 529 14th Street, N.W., Washington, D.C.

The Federalist Society for Law and Public Policy Studies is a group of conservatives and libertarians interested in the current state of the legal order. It is founded on the principles that the state exists to preserve freedom, that the separation of government powers is central to our Constitution, and that it is emphatically the province and duty of the judiciary to say what the law is, not what is should be. The Society seeks to promote an awareness of these principles and to further their application through its activities.
