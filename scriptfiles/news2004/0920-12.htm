War Resisters League Reception Commemorates 50th Anniversary Peace Calendar and Celebrates Contributors
 

NEW YORK - September 20 - The War Resisters League (WRL), is hosting a reception to mark the publication of Let Us Go Forward Together, its 2005 calendar. The reception will be held on Tuesday, September 28, 2004, at 6: 30 p.m., at New York University’s Tamiment Library, 70 Washington Square South, 10th Floor, New York City. The public is welcome.

The reception will be hosted by Grace Paley, prize-winning poet and short story writer and long-time pacifist activist; and David McReynolds, staff member emeritus of WRL and current Green Party New York State Senate candidate. More than a dozen contributors to the 2005 calendar will also be present to read their entries.

WRL, an 81-year-old secular pacifist organization, is commemorating the fiftieth anniversary of its popular Peace Calendar with a special retrospective edition. The 2005 calendar, Let Us Go Forward Together, contains artwork and text from all the previous calendars. It includes full color reproductions of the 49 previous covers; quotations from historical and current nonviolent activists describing peace campaigns, presenting the philosophy of nonviolence, and offering first person accounts of courageous nonviolent actions; photographs, drawings and cartoons. Historian Howard Zinn, author of many books and plays and professor emeritus of Boston University, provided the preface. End notes, consisting of comments and song lyrics, were contributed by singer, songwriter and political activist, Ani Di Franco.

The reception will honor the many hundreds of individuals who contributed to the 50 calendars and, in particular, the 200 individuals represented in the 2005 edition.

Ruth Benn, editor of Let Us Go Forward Together, commented on its contents: “The 60 pages presented in this calendar, culled from the thousands of pages of material printed in the earlier editions, demonstrate the value and vibrancy of the nonviolent movement in the U.S. and, indeed, around the world. As war and terrorism seem to be escalating steadily, it is particularly important to keep words of peace and hope at hand as we go about our daily routine.”

The peace calendar is 5 ½ x 8 ½ in size, and has 128 pages. It has color covers backed with mini-covers of all 50 calendars. The cover price is $12.95. For more info see WRL’s website: www.warresisters.org.
