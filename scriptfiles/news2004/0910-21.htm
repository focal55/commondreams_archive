Punkvoter.com to Kick off Second Rock Against Bush Tour in Portland
with Anti-Flag, Rep. Jim Mcdermott, and Ex-Fox News Members
 

PORTLAND, Oregon -  September 10 - In an effort to educate members of the punk community to take action, question what they read, and as punk icon Jello Biafra states, "become the media," Punkvoter.com has arranged a special screening and discussion of Robert Greenwald's movie "Outfoxed" to kick off their Rock Against Bush Tour. "This tour is about motivating Punkvoters to learn more, take action, and make their voices heard," stated Anti-Flag's lead singer Justin Sane.

Members of the Rock Against Bush Tour: Anti-Flag, Midtown, Epoxies, Strike Anywhere; Seattle Rep. Jim McDermott (star of Fahrenheit 9/11); Clara Frenk (ex Fox employee that is featured in the movie Outfoxed): Oregon political activist Jefferson Smith

When: Friday, Sept. 17, 2004

3 p.m. Press Conference 11 p.m. movie screening/discussion -- Clinton Street Theater

Where: 2522 Clinton Street, Portland, Ore.

Why: To enrage and engage punk community to take action and speak out

Full List of Rock Against Bush Kick off Events:

-- Friday Sept. 17 -- 3 p.m. Press Conference

-- Friday Sept. 17 - 11 p.m. Special Showing of Robert Greenwald's Movie "Outfoxed" -- With a discussion lead by ex-Fox News Employee Clara Frenk after.

-- Saturday Sept. 18 -- 5:30 p.m. Rally with Oregon AFL-CIO

-- Saturday Sept. 18 - 8 p.m. Rock Against Bush Concert at the Roseland 8NW, 6th Ave., Portland, Ore.

Punkvoter.com will be working with many local organizations and holding educational events, bringing in guest speakers, and showing special movie screenings throughout this tour to educate and inform our members and motivating them to become engaged. Punkvoter.com is voter education organization that is made up of over 200 of the nation's largest punk rock bands, 30 independent record labels, and thousands of punk voters!

Punkvoter.com has also been involved in working with Fat Wreck Chords on the Rock Against Bush CD/DVD Vol. 2 compilation that was released on Aug. 10. This compilation has already sold over 70,000 copies, maintained a strong presence for the last month on the Billboard's Top 200 and climbed to the top of the College Music Charts.
