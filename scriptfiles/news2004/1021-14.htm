Campaign Continues: Young Americans Warned That Bush Policy Is Leading To Post-Election Military Draft
 

FLORIDA -- October 21 -- Campaign Continues: Young Americans Warned That Bush Policy Is Leading To Post-Election Military Draft

-- Cher & Rosie O'Donnell to Speak in Miami to Thousands for MoveOnStudentAction.org "Feel a Draft?" Campaign

-- Celebrities to Discuss Devastating Toll of Bush's Iraq Policy on College-Aged Voters

Cher will address an estimated crowd of 2,000 young Americans, college students and undecided voters in Florida this Friday night at Miami's trendy Crobar in South Beach as a part of the MoveOnStudentAction.org nationwide "Feel a Draft?" Campaign.

Saturday night, Rosie O'Donnell will speak to an audience of hundreds, on behalf of MoveOn Student Action, at a concert in Boynton Beach, only minutes from Florida Atlantic University and sponsored by Americans Coming Together (ACT) and Emily's list.

The audience will view "The Ground Truth: The Human Cost of War," a film that looks at the long-term and often invisible effects the war in Iraq is having on thousands of nameless, undecorated Americans. The additional screening will be of "Going Upriver," which highlights John Kerry's experiences during the Vietnam War and the leadership of the veterans and students during the anti-war movement. The "Going Upriver" screening is part of 964 all over the country. Local reporters can find out if there is one their area and contact the organizers by going to http://www.moveonstudentaction.org/events.

MoveOn Student Action launched it's "Feel a Draft" campaign on Monday with an open letter to President Bush that ran as an ad called "Will You Call On Us To Die?" in the New York Times. More than 65,000 young voters demanding a specific plan to avoid the need for a draft as well as an exit strategy to end the war in Iraq signed the letter. Additional print ads ran in 155 campus papers in battleground states this week.

College-age men and women have suffered the vast majority of the 1,000 deaths and 7,000 injuries sustained in Iraq and would constitute the bulk of potential draftees. The mean age for soldiers killed in Iraq is 26.3, with significant numbers of soldiers killed in their 30s, according to military sociologists at the United States Military Academy and authors of a study titled "Is Iraq A Class War?"
