Fixing the Problem, or Putting in the Fix? Get the Facts on EPA's Upcoming Designations of Counties With Dangerously Dirty Air
 

WASHINGTON -- December 8 -- News Advisory:

  • Press Breakfast Briefing Thursday, Dec. 9, 2004 --

What: In the next two weeks, EPA is expected to designate counties that violate or contribute to violations of the National Ambient Air Quality Standard for fine particulate matter ("PM 2.5") or soot. States must develop plans to clean up these counties. In April, EPA announced a companion list of counties that violated the national standard for ozone smog.

However, a new analysis by a coalition of clean air and public Health advocates finds that EPA has dramatically undercounted the number of counties that are experiencing or contributing to dangerously dirty air. Furthermore, EPA is under tremendous pressure from states to leave even more counties out of the air pollution clean up equation. This coalition will hold a press breakfast briefing to discuss the results of its analysis, including:

  • What counties are really experiencing dirty air or making the air pollution problem worse for their neighbors, but may be given a pass by EPA?
  • Where are the coal-burning power plants that EPA will likely let off the hook by failing to include those plants in nonattainment areas?
  • What are the health implications of fine particle pollution?
