Media Briefing Explores Global Issues Facing Children, Child Advocates from Four Continents to be Recognized in New York Nov. 16
 
NEW YORK, NY -- November 5 -- News Advisory:

WHAT: Media briefing explores global issues facing children. Attendees will meet amazing individuals entrenched in:

-- Child displacement in war-torn zones around the globe

-- Re-integration of ex-child soldiers into families

-- The AIDS pandemic in Africa

-- Child labor issues in Africa

-- Healthcare services in Haiti

-- Poverty and socioeconomic conditions in India

Learn what these remarkable people are doing to address very serious global issues facing children, and hear their first-hand stories about their work.

WHERE: Danny Kaye Theatre at UNICEF House, 3 United Nations Plaza, New York, N.Y. 10017

WHEN: Tuesday, Nov. 16, 10 a.m.

Honoree Information:

Cardinal Health Children's Award Honorees:

-- Gopa Kothari, M.D., Mumbai, India, is a pediatrician caring for poor and disadvantaged families. In the past 22 years, Kothari has helped prevent 68,000 children from blindness through Vitamin A treatment and immunization.

-- Jeremiah J. Lowney, DDS, Connecticut, USA, founded the Haitian Health Foundation in 1986 to improve life for Haitians. He and his foundation have vaccinated hundreds of thousands of children, performed minor surgeries, and provided many food and education opportunities.

-- Rona Martin, Causeway, Zimbabwe, has been a senior social worker with Island Hospice and Bereavement Service for over 20 years. She cares for orphaned and dying children. Last year alone, Rona personally worked with 115 children, while her program has benefited thousands.

Kellogg's Child Development Award Honorees:

-- Philista Prudence Minjal Onyango, PhD., Nairobi Province, Kenya, is the Regional Director of ANPPCAN, where she works to stop child labor in Central Africa, East Africa, North Africa, Indian Ocean Islands, Southern Africa, and West Africa through 19 ANPPCAN Chapters.

-- Marie de la Soudiere, New York, USA became the Director of the Program for Children Affected by Armed Conflict for the International Rescue Committee. She has directly impacted close to 100,000 children worldwide.

Founder's Awards: World of Children will announce the winner of its Founder's Award for youth helping youth on Nov. 16.

About World of Children:

World of Children, Inc. -- http://www.WorldofChildren.org -- is a nonprofit organization whose mission is to honor, illuminate, and inspire action on behalf of children by raising global awareness of the plight of children. Annual awards include the $100,000 Kellogg's Child Development Award, the $100,000 Cardinal Health Children's Care Award, as well as a $15,000 Founder's Award, which honors youth serving other youth
