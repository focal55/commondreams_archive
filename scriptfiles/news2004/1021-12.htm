Peace Action PAC Announces Congressional Endorsements
 

SILVER SPRING MD -- October 21 -- Peace Action PAC, the political action committee of the nation’s largest peace group, today announced its endorsements for the 2004 U.S. House and U.S. Senate races.* The peace vote is considered integral to this years election because it represents an energized, growing and highly motivated sector of the electorate. The endorsement list contains 93 House candidates and five Senate candidates. Peace Action PAC bases its endorsements on the voting records of Congressional members, answers to candidate questionnaires and public statements on war and peace issues. Most endorsees had 100% ratings on the Peace Action Education Fund (PAEF) 2003 Voting Record with the exception of Cynthia McKinney—a former representative who lost her seat in 2002.

“We are pleased that so many congressional representatives had the integrity and bravery to consistently vote their consciences. In a hostile political environment, these congress people voted for accountability, the rule of law and diplomacy and against the Bush agenda of developing new nuclear weapons, war and corporate greed. They are a credit to the Americans that they represent,” said Kevin Martin, Executive Director of Peace Action.

The Peace Action Education Fund’s 2003 Congressional Voting Record shows very clear lines have been drawn in the voting patterns of the Congress due to the polarizing effects of Bush’s war and partisan leadership. There are no Republicans who received either a 100% rating or an endorsement from Peace Action PAC.

“Because of the Bush administration’s single-minded, pro-war policies and complete intolerance of points of view other than its own, there were no Republicans who voted pro-peace, pro-rule of law or pro-diplomacy in a consistent manner. It’s a perversion of the ideals of American representative democracy that Republican Party affiliation now prohibits, in the main, Republicans in Congress from both voting their conscience and representing or supporting anything but the views of the administration.

“Mainstream Americans who are represented by members of congress who consistently follow the far-right dictates of the Republican leadership in the House, Senate and White House are being grossly misrepresented,” continued Mr. Martin.

“We encourage Americans of all political persuasions and parties to support the candidates endorsed by the Peace Action PAC. They have demonstrated independence, integrity and a commitment to American democratic values that are nearly extinct in Bush’s Washington,” concluded Martin.

*Peace Action PAC has not endorsed a candidate for President, but it has “dis-endorsed” or advocated the defeat of President Bush. Please see http://www.peace-action.org/pub/releases/rel032904.html for more information.
