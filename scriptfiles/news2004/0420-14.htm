Earth Day Rally To Call Upon The Bush Administration To Strengthen, Not Weaken Environmental Protections
 

WASHINGTON - April 20 -

WHAT: Before President Bush’s arrival in Wells, Maine on Thursday, citizens and environmental groups will gather by the hundreds to highlight the real impact of the Bush administration’s environmental record: more mercury, smog and soot.

Environment Maine will also be distributing their report, Maine’s Environment at Risk, covering 8 environmental issues where the Bush administration has promoted policies that threaten Maine’s environment and health and factsheet, Maine’s Waterways at Risk, discussing specific Bush administration policies and actions that could harm our waterways.

WHO: Matthew Davis, Advocate, Environment Maine

Maureen Drouin, NE Regional Representative, Sierra Club

Kate Moffat, Kennebunk resident, teacher and Sierra Club volunteer

Andy Burt, Environmental Justice Coordinator, Maine Council of Churches

Amy Thompson, Portland Organizer, Maine Peoples’ Alliance

Maggie Drummond, Maine Organizer, Toxics Action Center

WHEN: Thursday, April 22nd, 2004, 8:00am

WHERE: Parking lot adjacent to Wells Town Offices, on Route 109/Sanford Road

DIRECTIONS: Take exit 2 off of I-95 for Route 109/Route 9, take a left onto Route 109/Sanford Road towards Wells, the town offices are on the left at 208 Sanford Road.

MORE INFO: The report, Maine’s Environment At Risk, will be released and posted at www.environmentmaine.org on April 21st. The factsheet, Maine’s Waterway’s At Risk, can be downloaded from http://www.environmentmaine.org/pdfs/MainesWaterwaysatRisk.pdf
