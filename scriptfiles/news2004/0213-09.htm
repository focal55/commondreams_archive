Conservation Groups Announce Major Action to Protect Western Arctic; Teleconference on Bush Oil Designs in Arctic
 

WASHINGTON - February 13 - Conservation Groups Announce Major Action to Protect Western Arctic; Teleconference will discuss action to bring balance to Bush oil designs in Arctic

WHO:

-- Deirdre McDonnell, Earthjustice

-- Eleanor Huffines, The Wilderness Society

-- Stan Senner, National Audubon Society

-- Kelly Hill Scanlon, Northern Alaska Environmental Center

WHEN: Tuesday, Feb.17, 1:30 p.m. Eastern, 9:30 a.m. Alaskan

WHERE: Call in number: 866-453-5550 PIN 469 5606

WHAT: Speakers from national and regional conservation groups announce major action to bring balance to Bush administration plans for massive oil development in the northwest planning section of the National Petroleum Reserve Alaska, in the western arctic.

WHY: On Jan. 22 the Bush administration signed off on plans to open almost nine million acres of pristine arctic tundra, lakes, rivers and coastline in the western arctic to oil and gas development. The administration ignored requests to protect key wildlife areas and is proceeding without adequate study of likely wildlife harm. The region is home to polar bears, peregrine falcons, caribou, wolves, grizzly bears, migratory birds and many other species.

The American people have voiced their desire to protect the key wildlife and wilderness values of the reserve in nearly 100,000 comments submitted to the Interior Department. Government experts have voiced their concern for resident wildlife, urging that key special areas be shielded from development to protect wildlife and subsistence hunting and fishing. Still the Bush administration approved an unbalanced plan that opens the entire study area to oil and gas leasing.
