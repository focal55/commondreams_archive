16 Sudanese Women Leaders/Peace Advocates Address Issues at Briefing
 

Women government and non-governmental leaders from across Sudan will share their experiences and vision for a peaceful Sudan in a panel discussion sponsored by Women Waging Peace and hosted by the Center for Strategic and International Studies and the Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars.

These Sudanese leaders will brief the media on the current crisis in Darfur, the peace process in Sudan and their meetings this week with UN, State Department, White House, the World Bank, NGO representatives and Congressional leaders. The media are encouraged to attend.

WHO: Sudanese Women Leaders and Activists From Across Sudan, Rick Barton from the Center for Strategic and International Studies and Carla Koppell, Women Waging Peace

WHAT: Panel Discussion/Media Briefing on Promoting Peace in Sudan and the Critical Role of Women in the Peacemaking Process

WHEN: Thursday, Oct. 14, 9:30 a.m. to 11 a.m.

WHERE: Center for Strategic and International Studies, 1800 K Street, NW, Washington, DC, B1 Conference Room

For one-on-one interviews and additional background, please contact Kirk Monroe at 202-265-0175.

About Women Waging Peace : An operating program of Hunt Alternatives Fund, Women Waging Peace advocates for full participation of women in formal and informal peace processes around the world. More than 400 women peace builders in the "Waging" network, all demonstrated leaders with varied backgrounds, perspectives, and skills, bring a vast array of expertise to the peacemaking process. They have met with over 3,000 policy shapers to collaborate on fresh, workable solutions to long-standing conflicts. Waging's Policy Commission is researching and documenting women's activities in conflict prevention, negotiations and post-conflict reconstruction in some 15 conflicts; these studies provide models to encourage policymakers to include women and gender perspectives in their program designs and budgets.
