NARAL Pro-Choice America Urges Mainstream Religious Leaders to Stand Up to Bush Campaign Smear Tactics

 

WASHINGTON - September 29 - Responding to the latest in a series of outrageous attempts by the Republican Party to use religion to mislead the American public, NARAL Pro-Choice America encouraged religious leaders to speak out against divisive scare tactics such as ads claiming that John Kerry would ban the Bible or promote widespread abortion.

NARAL Pro-Choice America Board member Reverend Katherine Ragsdale said, "The majority of Americans of all faiths support a woman's right to choose. But the real issue here is the way that George Bush and his allies are trying to turn Americans against each other and use faith as a way to divide us, instead of bringing people together. I hope that my clerical colleagues will join me in speaking out against these completely inappropriate attacks."

Recently, the Republican National Committee admitted to having sent pamphlets to voters in Arkansas and West Virginia insinuating that John Kerry would ban the Bible if elected.

This was followed by the launch of Web sites titled "KerryWrongforCatholics.com" and "KerryWrongforMormons.com" that featured an outrageous misquoting of a speech Kerry gave in the U.S. Senate.

Ten years ago, Kerry made a strong statement to Congress condemning the bombing of a reproductive health clinic. The RNC recently launched a Web site to attack John Kerry for standing up for women, lifting pieces of his statement to make it appear as though Kerry was "promoting" abortion.

NARAL Pro-Choice America called on its allies in the religious community to emphasize Kerry's solid pro-choice record and his commitment to reducing unintended pregnancies and the need for abortion through proactive reproductive health policy that respects women and safeguards their health.
