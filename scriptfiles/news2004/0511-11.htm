Leaders from Broad-Based Coalition Challenge Extremism of “Federal Marriage Amendment” Backers
 

WASHINGTON - May 11 - In advance of a Thursday hearing in the House Subcommittee on the Constitution, representatives of a broad coalition of religious, labor, civil rights, women’s rights, and other public interest organizations will challenge the extremism of a constitutional amendment that would require states to discriminate against same-sex couples and their families.

Witnesses scheduled to testify in favor of the amendment at the 10:00 a.m. hearing include defeated Supreme Court nominee Robert Bork, Religious Right legal activist Jay Sekulow, and the original sponsor of the amendment, Rep. Marilyn Musgrave of Colorado. Rep. Barney Frank of Massachusetts will be testifying in opposition to the amendment.

What: Coalition leaders challenge FMA and its backers

When: Thursday, May 13, 2004 9:00 a.m.

Where: 2226 Rayburn House Office Building
