MoveOn.org Voter Fund Will Run Ads in 17 Battleground States - Spots Focus on Overtime Pay, Outsourcing Jobs, Budget Deficit
 

WASHINGTON - March 2 - MoveOn.org Voter Fund will begin airing TV ads in 67 media markets in 17 battleground states on Thursday, March 4.

In most states, the MOVF ad will focus on jobs issues including overtime pay and outsourcing, and the economic insecurity felt by many working families.

In other states, MOVF will run its “Child's Pay” ad (winner of the Bush-in-30-Seconds ad contest in January) which highlights the Bush budget deficit, a renewed item of concern after the controversy generated last week by Federal Reserve Chairman Alan Greenspan’s Congressional testimony in which he suggested a need to cut back vital services and Social Security benefits in order to pay off the growing federal debt.

The MoveOn.org Voter Fund ad program is an effort to preserve a level playing field in the national issue debate this year by providing the public with the truth about Bush Administration policies. Last fall, MoveOn.org Voter Fund announced its plan to raise

$10 million in small contributions from its members in order to put ads on the air over a six month period from October through March. MOVF exceeded its fundraising goal, with contributions averaging $60, and is concluding this round of its program with a substantial TV ad buy.
