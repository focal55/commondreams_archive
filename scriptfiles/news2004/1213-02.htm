National Celebration for 40th Anniversary of GLBT Civil Rights Movement Includes 28 National GLBT Executive Directors
 

PHILADELPHIA, PA -- December 13 -- The National Celebration for the 40th Anniversary of the GLBT Civil Rights Movement will be held in Philadelphia at Independence Hall and Liberty Bell on Sunday, May 1 during Equality Forum 2005. Equality Forum 2005 will be held from Monday, April 25 to Sunday, May 1.

"We are pleased that twenty-eight (28) national and international GLBT Executive Directors will participate in the 40th Anniversary of the GLBT civil rights movement. The week of programs, parties and special events concludes with a National Celebration on Independence Mall," stated Malcolm Lazin, executive director, Equality Forum. "The participation by these Executive Directors speaks to both commemorating our history and being a united movement for this National Celebration."

"Our movement began when Frank Kameny, Barbara Gittings and 38 other Gay Pioneers were the first to openly demonstrate for equality at Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell on July 4, 1965. They are our Rosa Parks. They refused the closet and demanded equal rights," stated Sally Susman, national co-chair, 40th Anniversary Celebration.

"We take no credit for having the 40th Anniversary Celebration at Independence Hall and the Liberty Bell. This was the iconic site chosen by the Gay Pioneers. It is thrilling 40 years later that the Executive Directors of so many national and international organizations will be part of the festivities," stated Jonathan Capehart, national co-chair, 40th Anniversary Celebration.

"Equality Forum 2005 will be the largest gathering of national and international GLBT Executive Directors. We are delighted that these leaders are playing an active role in this National Celebration," stated Joe Farrell, chair, board of directors, Equality Forum.

There is no registration fee and all substantive programs are free including the National Celebration.

For a list of the 28 Executive Directors or other information about the National Celebration, visit http://www.equalityforum.com or contact Michael Pomante at 215-732-3378 ext. 15 or Michael@equalityforum.com.
