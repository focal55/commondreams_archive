Letter To President Bush: Go To The Middle East and Apologize In Person
 

WASHINGTON - May 5 -

George W. Bush, President of the United States

1600 Pennsylvania Avenue, N.W.

Washington, D. C. 20500

Dear Mr. President:

When Harry S Truman was President, a sign on his desk read, "The Buck Stops Here."

Today, there is deep sense of outrage over the apparently rampant abuse of prisoners held by Americans in Iraq and Afghanistan. Once the abuse and possible murders became public, you expressed your own disgust and investigations have been launched.

That is good but it is not enough. American abuse of prisoners should be soundly condemned. As President, you need to reaffirm the United States prominence and leadership among the family of nations by making your condemnation of these specific atrocities public. Moreover, because the "buck" stops with you, a personal apology to Iraqis, Afghans, the Arab world, our coalition partners and the American people is called for. True leadership demands you rise with humility to express our nation's collective outrage, disappointment, and deep sorrow for the morally abject behavior of those who abused in the name of America.

Last Thanksgiving, you traveled to Iraq to deliver your personal gratitude to those who serve our nation. Now, a second trip to deliver a sincere and abject apology should be uppermost on your agenda.

Sincerely,

Amy Isaacs, National Director

Americans for Democratic Action
