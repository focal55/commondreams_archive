PGI Recommends Progressives For Commerce Secretary
 

CAMBRIDGE, MA - June 16 - The Progressive Government Institute (PGI), a non-partisan educational organization dedicated to ensuring transparency and accountability in the Executive branch of the federal government, today released the names of four individuals who it believes are well qualified to serve as Secretary of Commerce

"When the voters head to the polls this November they have a right to know what kind of leadership to expect," said PGI Founder Dal LaMagna. "One of the most important measures of that leadership is the kind of people our President surrounds him or herself with, starting with the individuals named to serve in the Cabinet. PGI argues that presidential candidates should name Cabinet picks before the election, so that those decisions can be vetted by the electorate."

"Senator Kerry is spending this week highlighting his differences with the Bush Administration on economic policy. The PGI believes that Senator Kerry can further define the differences between his vision for the nation's economy by sharing with voters the names of individuals he would consider well qualified to serve in his Administration as Secretary of Commerce," said PGI Executive Director Ted Carr. "The PGI has carefully vetted the resumes of several individuals who we feel would promote progressive economic policy if they were to be named to the post."

The PGI recommends the following individuals for Secretary of Commerce: *Jeff Gates: Former counsel to the US Senate Committee on Finance and President of the Shared Capitalism Institute.

*Jerry Mander: President of the International Forum on Globalization and author of numerous books and articles on economic policy.

*Jon Cavanaugh: Director of the Institute for Policy Studies and former economist for the U.N. Conference on Trade and Development.

* Laura D'Andrea Tyson: Dean of the London School of Economics and former member of the White House Council of Economic Advisers.

The Progressive Government Institute will be releasing progressive alternatives for all of the Cabinet positions in the next several weeks. To date, PGI has announced selections for Secretary of Defense, EPA Administrator and Director of the CIA. For detailed biographies of these individuals, please visit our web site, www.progressivegovernment.org
