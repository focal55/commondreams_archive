US Cities to Celebrate Carfree Day
Oakland Joins International CarFree Week Celebration International Mobility Week September 19-25, 2004
 

BERKELEY, CA - September 16 - In a historic move, Oakland is sponsoring its first CarFree Day on September 23, joining cities and towns throughout the world in celebrating International Mobility Week and World CarFree Day. This is good news for residents of the Bay Area, where traffic congestion is the second-worst in the nation.

"We are excited to join the international movement to promote mobility that is not dependent on the private automobile," said Gus Yates, president of CarFree City USA.

"For increasing numbers of Americans, the so-called 'love affair' with the automobile has become a nightmare of sprawl, traffic congestion, chauffeuring kids, obesity, asthma, loss of community, global warming, and oil wars. The celebrations happening around the world this week will show people the path to a better lifestyle."

International Car Free Day began in 1997 with an event held in La Rochelle, France entitled En Ville San Ma Voiture ("In Town Without My Car"). The annual event, which typically features street festivals and cafes, public art and children's play areas, soon spread across Europe and throughout the world. The 2003 event was celebrated by 112 million citizens in 1,500 towns and cities from 40 countries around the world.

CarFree City, USA's goal is to provide Americans with an alternative: carfree cities. Creating or redeveloping neighborhoods and towns designed for people, not cars, yields a host of personal, community, and global benefits. It doesn't require futuristic technologies, just common sense and a willingness to try something new. CarFree City USA is affiliated with the World CarFree Network.
