FEC Opinion Scheduled for Feb. 18 Action Could Muzzle Advocates
 

WASHINGTON - February 17 - A number of leading public interest groups today released a legal analysis demonstrating the ways in which a pending Federal Election Commission (FEC) advisory opinion could severely restrict the ability of nonprofit organizations to communicate with the public about important policy issues. The analysis rebuts recent statements by the organization Democracy 21 and other groups, proving that these organizations are “mistaken about the threat posed by the FEC’s pending action.”

On February 18, the Federal Election Commission will consider a request for an advisory opinion submitted by a pro-Republican group, Americans for a Better Country (ABC). A draft opinion (AO 2003-37) prepared by the FEC’s General Counsel was taken off of the agenda on February 5, after it generated a storm of protest from nonprofit organizations across the country. The groups’ primary concern is that the advisory opinion will prevent them from publicly criticizing, or commenting favorably on, the positions and actions of any candidate for federal office, including current officeholders who are also candidates such as President Bush.

The analysis released today responds to recent statements from the groups Democracy 21, the Campaign Legal Center and the Center for Responsive Politics that have tried to support the approach taken in the draft FEC opinion. These three groups contend that the public interest organizations criticizing the draft FEC opinion are wrong about its risks for nonprofit organizations. However the analysis released today points out that the reform groups offer no legal argument to support their assertions and points out that other campaign reform groups share concerns that the FEC draft opinion goes too far. Furthermore, the analysis questions the procedural basis for this FEC action.

The full analysis released today is available at http://www.pfaw.org/pfaw/general/default.aspx?oid=13920.
