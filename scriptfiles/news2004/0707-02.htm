9/11 Whistleblower Challenges Govt Secrecy
 

WASHINGTON - July 7 - WHAT: Statements by FBI whistleblower Sibel Edmonds and Attorney Mark Zaid: --U.S. District Court's decision dismissing Sibel Edmonds' case based on state secrets privilege --Announcement of appeal of court decision --Improper reclassification of evidence by FBI/Department of Justice --Announcement of new lawsuit seeking completion of Department of Justice Inspector General

WHEN: Thursday, July 8, 2004, 1:00 P.M.

WHERE: National Press Club, Lisagor Room, 529 14th Street, NW, Washington, DC 20045628

WHO: Sibel Edmonds & Mark Zaid

CONTACT: Mark Zaid, Krieger & Zaid, PLLC Oversight, (202) 454-2809 direct

BACKGROUND:

On Tuesday, July 6, the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia (Judge Reggie Walton) dismissed FBI Whistleblower Sibel Edmonds' lawsuit challenging the government's excessive secrecy over her case. For over two years Attorney General John Ashcroft has invoked the rarely used State Secrets Privilege in order to prevent from becoming public Ms. Edmond's allegations regarding the FBI's conduct before and after 9/11. He has most recently used it to quash a subpoena request for her deposition from attorneys representing 9/11 family members in litigation against terrorists and their financiers. In May 2004, Ashcroft reclassified information provided by the FBI to Congress that corroborated Ms. Edmonds' allegations. The nonprofit Project On Government Oversight has separately sued Attorney General Ashcroft and the Department of Justice (DOJ), asking the court to find the DOJ's reclassification of information unlawful and unconstitutional.

"The decision today represents another example of the Executive Branch's abusive nature of using secrecy as a weapon against Whistleblowers," said Mark S. Zaid, Edmonds' attorney. "It is quite disappointingly evident that accountability is no longer a word in our Government's dictionary," added Zaid. An immediate appeal is planned.
