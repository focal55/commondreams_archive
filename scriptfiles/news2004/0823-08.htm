Howard Dean, Natalie Merchant Join Star-Studded Gala Sparking 10 Weeks of Grassroots Activism to Defeat Bush
 

WASHINGTON - August 23 - MoveOn PAC will host a star-studded, sold out, gala Tuesday, August 24 called "10 Weeks: Don't Get Mad, Get Even!" to kick-off 10 Weeks of grassroots activism, highlighted by a new TV ad campaign featuring the work of notable American film directors and actors including Matt Damon, Scarlett Johanssen, Kevin Bacon, Woody Harrelson, Al Franken and Rebecca Romijn, Rob Reiner and Richard Linklater. The new ads are part of MoveOn PAC's multi-million TV ad program, between now and the November election.

During each of the next ten weeks, Moveon PAC will announce a new grassroots initiative in its campaign to elect John Kerry. The first activity will be launched with an ad called Register and Vote, written and directed by award-winning hip-hop icon Benny Boom. It will inaugurate Moveon PAC's effort to counter Republican voter suppression efforts like those underway in Florida now.

The 10 Weeks campaign embodies Moveon PAC's continuing effort to combine grassroots activism with celebrity participation in supporting John Kerry's campaign for President.

Entertainment at the event will include musical performances by The Roots, Moby, Natalie Merchant and Perry Farrell, appearances by Howard Dean, Al Franken, MoveOn PAC Executive Director Eli Pariser, Kevin Bacon, Richard Linklater and many others. Janeane Garofalo will host.
