Back From Iraq: An Eyewitness Account - American Activist to Speak of Recent Experiences in Iraq
 

NEW YORK - January 18 - When: MLK Day, Monday, January 19, 2004 at 4:30 PM

Who: Efia Nwangaza, in an event sponsored by Not In Our Name

Where: West Park Church, 165 W 86th St. at Amsterdam Ave

What: An account of a week spent among Iraqis under the occupation, with video and photo documentation.

On January 5, 2004 Efia wrote: "I'm in Baghdad....The US is running amok. All the ills with the police in the US are magnified by 1,000 here. They're shooting unarmed civilians and not even apologizing, much less paying compensation.....Today…we learned that up to 18,000 people have been disappeared by the US military, 'Coalition Provisional Authority (CPA).' Most are treated the same as folk held at Guantanamo Bay, Cuba."

Bio: Efia Nwangaza is a life-long human rights activist. As a youth she participated in the Atlanta Project of SNCC, SNCC’s first effort at urban organizing, and drafted the philosophical underpinnings of its Anti-VietnamWar and Black Power positions. She is the founder/coordinator of the Afrikan-American Institute for Policy Studies & Planning and Malcolm X Grassroots Movement for Self-Determination, complementary volunteer, grassroots, community-based think tank and advocacy organizations in Greenville, SC. She serves on the national steering committee of the October 22nd Coalition Against Police Brutality and Amnesty International-USA’s National Program to Abolish the Death Penalty. She is a member of the national Interim Steering Committee of the Not in Our Name Project and represents NION on the Steering Committee of United for Peace and Justice. She is a people’s lawyer in Greenville, SC.
