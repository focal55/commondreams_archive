Experts Respond to "Anti-Christian" Assertions Made by Pat Robertson
 

WASHINGTON - February 2 - While promoting his new book The Ten Offenses: Reclaim the Blessings of God's Eternal Truths, Pat Robertson has asserted that People For the American Way Foundation, and other organizations working to defend the separation of church and state, are "attempting to take away the religious freedom of Christians." Robertson's definition of "religious freedom" is allowing government officials to use the power of their office and taxpayer dollars to promote their own brand of religion. These comments are an abomination and a clear reflection of continuing attempts by right-wing politicians, televangelists, and activists to use government to promote and endorse religion, and to cast aside real religious freedoms outlined in the Constitution.

PFAWF is a strong defender of the fundamental right to worship as one chooses, and strives to maintain a powerful national voice on church-state issues. The following is a list of available experts to discuss Robertson's remarks.

Contact for the Media:

Ralph G. Neas, President of People For the American Way Foundation

Neas is the former executive director of the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights. In 1995, Senator Edward Kennedy called Neas the "101st Senator for Civil Rights." Neas has been an advocate for religious freedom throughout his career, and a champion for First Amendment rights.

Elliot M. Mincberg,Vice President, General Counsel & Legal Director, People For the American Way Foundation

Mincberg oversees litigation and other legal actions taken by PFAWF in the areas of religious liberty, public education, church-state separation, and other constitutional law issues. Mincberg has served as co-counsel in a number of important First Amendment, religious liberty, and education cases, and has played an important role in drafting the Federal Guidelines on Religious Exercise in the Federal Workplace in 1997.

Judith E. Schaeffer, Deputy Director of Legal/Litigation, People For the American Way Foundation

Schaeffer is a former partner at Dickstein, Shapiro & Morin, a Washington, D.C. law firm, where she specialized in complex federal litigation and performed extensive pro bono work for individuals and public interest organizations. Schaeffer specializes in First Amendment and civil rights cases.

Peter Montgomery, Communications Director, People For the American Way Foundation

Montgomery is a ten-year veteran of PFAWF's campaigns to defend civil rights, speak out against intolerance, and maintain the separation of church and state that protect religious freedom in America. As part of PFAWF's efforts to serve as a watchdog on the excesses of the religious right, Montgomery has a long acquaintance with Robertson's public statements and positions.
