Whistleblowers: Halliburton Routinely Overcharged
 

WASHINGTON - February 12 - Today Reps. Waxman and Dingell wrote a letter detailing Halliburton's repeated and routine overcharging of the U.S. taxpayers under its LOGCAP contract, currently worth more than $3.7 billion. Based on information from two former Halliburton employees who worked for Halliburton's LOGCAP procurement office in Kuwait, the letter discusses improper practices including:

Telling employees price doesn't matter;

Wasteful spending;

Avoiding competition among vendors;

Inviting unjustifiably high quotes; and

Relying on an inadequate list of preferred vendors.

The members ask the Defense Contract Audit Agency to investigate these allegations and initiate action to recover any repayments to Halliburton.

The letter is available at: www.house.gov/reform/min
