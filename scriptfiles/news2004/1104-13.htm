Join FAIR at These Upcoming Public Events in the New York/New Jersey Area This November
 

NEW YORK, NY -- November 4 --
November 11: CAN U.S. DEMOCRACY SURVIVE MONOPOLY MEDIA?
A discussion on the balance of reporting and revenue
with Ben Bagdikian, Janine Jackson (FAIR) and Robert McChesney

Thursday, November 11 at 7:00 PM
Reidy Friendship Hall, All Souls Unitarian Church
1157 Lexington Ave at 80th St
Suggested donation $10

The coverage of news is a for-profit business, and a handful of multi-national companies have a monopoly on this business. Yet we as readers, consumers and viewers of the news want and expect coverage of domestic and world issues that is driven only by the desire to uncover truth. The panel will discuss the dilemma faced as newspapers, magazines and television shows turn news into a commodity.

November 16:

The Record of the Paper:
A Discussion on the New York Times and Foreign Policy
Moderated by Janine Jackson (FAIR)
with Howard Friel, Laura Flanders, Mahmood Mamdani and Michael Massing

Tuesday, November 16 at 7pm
The Judson Memorial Church
55 Washington Square South, New York City
Free & open to the public

Something has gone terribly awry on West 43rd Street. This panel will examine the New York Times and its consistent failure to acknowledge international law in its coverage of US foreign policy, as well as the role media could play in shaping a just and democratic society.

Co-sponsored by Verso Books; call 212 807 9680 for more information.
