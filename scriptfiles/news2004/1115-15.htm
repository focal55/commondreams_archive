Half-Million from Darfur Relief Collaboration; Food for the Hungry Joins World Relief to Provide Help for Sudan; Six Relief Agencies Team Up
 

EL GENEINA, SUDAN -- November 15 -- Six North American-based humanitarian organizations have joined together to provide relief to families in Sudan's war-torn region of Darfur. The partnership, called the Darfur Relief Collaboration (DRC), includes: World Relief, World Concern, Northwest Medical Teams, Food for the Hungry, MAP International and Christian Reformed World Relief Committee. The group recently received a grant from the Canadian Foodgrains Bank (CFGB) for nearly half-a-million dollars to begin work immediately in three remote villages in West Darfur that are straining to host thousands of internally displaced families.

Statistics:

As many as 70,000 people have been killed and 2.2 million displaced in Darfur since the fighting broke out in 2003. Because of continuing violence and fear, crops are not being planted and cultivated, disease rates are skyrocketing and starvation is quickly becoming a reality in the region. The United Nations has described Darfur's conflict as "the world's worst humanitarian crisis."

Genocide:

"When the U.S. Congress unanimously declares the situation 'genocide,' by a vote of 422 to 0, there's no question it's genocide," asserted Ben Homan, president of Food for the Hungry.

Wells, latrines for immediate help:

The DRC, working through their lead agency, World Relief, has established bases of operation in Khartoum and El Geneina and is working in the villages of Um Tagouk, Sanidadi and Azirni. Their immediate goal is to prevent immense disease outbreaks by providing medical care, rehabilitating water systems, digging new wells and building 1000 pit latrines which will serve 35,000 people. They will also provide long-term help.

World Relief has worked with local churches around the world to create sustainable solutions that help the poor and suffering. For more information visit http://www.worldrelief.org
