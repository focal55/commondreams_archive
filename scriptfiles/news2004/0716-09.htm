Anti-Poverty Advocates Launch mobile "Bushville" in Prelude to RNC protests
 

WASHINGTON - July 16 - The Poor People's Economic Human Rights Campaign will initiate a month long campaign linked to the Republican National Convention by launching their mobile “Bushville” tent city to protest the economic policies of the current Administration. “Bushville” is designed to highlight poverty and economic injustice in America.

The Campaign is preparing for a series of activities including impromptu demonstrations, food distributions, human rights monitoring and documentation, voter education and registration. This is all in preparation for their "March for Our Lives" at Republican National Convention at 4pm on August 30th in front of the United Nations.

WHAT: News Conference for Launch of the Mobile "Bushville"

WHEN: Monday, July 19th, 12 pm

WHERE: 420 Grand Street, Jersey City, New Jersey

VISUALS: Mobile Billboards depicting poverty and victims of economic hardship.

Follow the "Bushville" tent city online at www.marchforourlives.org.
