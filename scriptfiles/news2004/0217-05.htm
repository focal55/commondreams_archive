National Poor People's Rights Organization to Announce Plans for March on Opening Day of Republican National Convention
 

NEW YORK - February 17 - The Poor People’s Economic Human Rights Campaign (PPEHRC) a national organization representing numerous poor people’s groups from around the country, will hold a press conference in New York on Thursday, February 19, 2004 to announce their plans to protest at the Republican National Convention in August. The activities will include a March entitled “March for Our Lives” and will coincide with the opening day activities of the Republican National Convention. In addition the PPEHRC will erect a tent city they will call “Bushville” to draw attention to the plight of the poor and homeless.

The Poor People’s Economic Human Rights Campaign garnered attention at the 2000 RNC where10, 000 people participated in their protest. PPEHRC has indicated they will march regardless of whether they are granted a permit. Cheri Honkala, the group’s National Spokesperson stated, “I will march because both the Republicans and Democrats have ignored the plight of poor and I will march to highlight the war here at home: An economic war in which the casualties are massive unemployment, increasing homelessness, and inadequate or non-existent healthcare.”
