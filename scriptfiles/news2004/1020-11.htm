Pat Robertson Reports Major Bush Miscalculation on Eve of War
President Now Insists He’s Not Surprised at Number of Troops Killed & Wounded, But Told Christian Coalition Founder There Wouldn’t Be Any
 

WASHINGTON -- October 20-- Despite words of caution from the founder of the Christian Coalition prior to the war in Iraq, President Bush insisted that America would not suffer war related causalities. Now, with more than 1,100 U.S. troops dead and another 8,000 troops wounded,[1][1] Bush claims he expected this level of violence all along.

On Feb. 8, NBC’s Meet The Press Host Tim Russert asked President Bush: "Are you surprised by the level and intensity of resistance?" Bush replied, "No. I'm not."[2][2] But according to Christian Coalition founder Pat Robertson, there is no way that could be true.

Appearing yesterday on CNN, Robertson said that just before the war he "warned [Bush] about this war...I was trying to say, 'Mr. President, you had better prepare the American people for casualties.'"[3][3] Bush then told Robertson, "Oh, no, we're not going to have any casualties." [4][4]
