WeCount.org Launches Survey to Help John Kerry Build Unified Front to Defeat Bush
 

WASHINGTON - May 25 - WeCount.org and Media-Screen Market Research announced today the launch of the "Tell Kerry What You Think!" survey giving voters an opportunity to voice whether Sen. John Kerry represents their views on key issues, and if not, what he could do differently to gain their support. An inspiring example of digital democracy, "Tell Kerry What You Think!" is an independent, non-partisan effort giving ordinary Americans a chance to influence the political agenda.

Kerry's positions on issues including the War in Iraq, the USA PATRIOT Act, healthcare, environment, education, globalization and the U.S. economy are compared with those of survey participants. Also addressed in the survey is Kerry's need to build formal partnerships with other political leaders and constituents to attract a broad spectrum of voters in order to defeat President George W. Bush in November. Would voters be more enthused by John Kerry if his administration included leaders like Sen. John Edwards, Howard Dean, Ralph Nader, Wesley Clark, Rep. Dennis Kucinich or Republican Sen. John McCain? This question and many others will be answered when the survey is completed in coming weeks.

"Tell Kerry What You Think!" marks WeCount.org's second phase in it's "Coalition for America 2004" project which began with an online petition urging Democrats, Greens, moderate Republicans and independents to form a coalition to defeat Bush in November.

The final survey results will be immediately presented to John Kerry's campaign and other political leaders and organizations. WeCount.org is actively seeking partners to expand the survey's footprint to include a large sample of swing state respondents. For more information or to participate in the "Tell Kerry What You Think!" survey, please visit www.WeCount.org.

About WeCount.org

WeCount.org is a not-for-profit political action committee (Sec. 527) founded in 2003 to promote policy changes in national government by establishing a continuing forum for coalition building, research, advocacy and voter mobilization. We support policies that will improve environmental protection, foreign relations without the use of war, public education, access to healthcare and fiscal responsibility in both government and the marketplace. WeCount.org is not affiliated with any political party. Our goal is to empower the voices of ordinary Americans in the political process throughout the United States.
