Artists, Activists, Celebrities, Politicos Converge at NYC Hotspot Crobar for Massive Convention Week Kick-off
 

WASHINGTON - August 19 - After they rub elbows with each other at the City's "official" Media Party at the Time Warner Mall on August 28th, journalists hungering for the real story in town will get to meet the hundreds of leading artists, activists, politicos and celebrities who have come together in New York City to respond to the Republican National Convention.

"This is going to be an incredible week in New York City history and the City deserves a real party to kick it off," said Chris Wangro, co-founder and co-executive producer of The Imagine Festival of Arts, Issues & Ideas. "Besides giving journalists an opportunity to meet the people who have been working so hard to create a peaceful and constructive response to the Convention, we're going to give them a full on taste of real, live, uncensored New York nightlife."

Already one of the hottest tickets in town, The Imagine Festival's Unofficial, Uncensored Welcome Party will take place immediately after the City's event at Chelsea hotspot Crobar New York, 530 West 28th street (between 10th & 11th Avenues) on Saturday, August 28th from 10pm to 1am.

"Contrary to conventional wisdom, New Yorkers are not leaving town or staying shut in their apartments as some have suggested," Wangro continued. "This party is a chance for New York's creative community to send a powerful message to the nation and the world that we are concerned, charged up, and ready to play a vital role in the country's future."

Wangro added that celebrities, performers and special guests appearing at the party will be announced in the coming days.

From August 28-September 2, The Imagine Festival of Arts, Issues & Ideas will present almost 200 performances, events, and exhibits aimed at exploring the critical issues facing the nation. Artists on board include marquee names from every corner of the arts world, including Margaret Cho, Cynthia Nixon, Richard Gere, Tony Kushner, Yoko Ono, John Sayles, Marisa Tomei, Moby, Chuck Close, The Welfare Poets, Lou Reed, Mark Anthony Thompson, Julie Atlas Muz, Gloria Steinem, Phillip Glass, E.L. Doctorow, Lewis Black, Tammy Faye Starlite, Joan Osborne and hundreds more.

The festival, one of the largest in New York City history, is the brainchild of a wildly diverse coalition of New York City artists committed to using their talent "to inspire, empower, and create change."
