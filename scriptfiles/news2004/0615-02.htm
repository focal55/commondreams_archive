82 Million People, Many in the Middle Class, Were Uninsured At Some Point Over The Past Two Years
Most Were Uninsured For At Least Nine Months
 

WASHINGTON - June 15 - On Wednesday morning, June 16, Families USA, together with several governors, will release a new report, based on Census Bureau data, showing that 81.8 million Americans - one out of three people under 65 years of age - were uninsured at some point during the two-year period 2002-2003. The overwhelming majorities were uninsured for lengthy periods of time.

The report provides; state-by-state numbers and percentages of the uninsured; the lengths of time that people were uninsured; and demographics - based on income, work status, race and ethnicity, gender, and age - of the people who were uninsured over the past two years.
