Brady Campaign: Maryland Sen. John Giannetti Says He Supports Tough Gun Laws, But Record Says Otherwise
 

ANNAPOLIS, Maryland - February 9 - The following was released today by the Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence regarding Maryland Sen. John Giannetti and gun laws:

Sen. John Giannetti, the Democrat who killed Maryland's assault weapons ban two years ago, called the Brady Campaign after a Jan. 19 press release to say his actions on the assault weapons legislation had been distorted out of context, and that he was a strong supporter of the Brady Campaign’s agenda.

The truth is that Giannetti has embraced the NRA's agenda with an enthusiasm that even his colleagues find embarrassing. For example:

-- He voted last year to repeal Maryland's landmark ballistic identification system, which uses cutting edge technology to help police trace shell casings found at crime scenes to the guns -- and the criminals -- who fired them. Shortly after Giannetti voted for a bill to repeal the system, Prince George's County prosecutors used the system to identify and convict a murder suspect, and the bill was scuttled.

-- Giannetti voted against legislation that would have set minimum security standards for gun dealers. Burglaries at gun stores are a major source of firearms for criminals, and prosecutors testified that dozens of stolen firearms taken from Maryland gun stores are used to commit violent crimes every year, yet some gun dealers fail to keep weapons adequately secured.

-- He voted against requiring gun owners to report the theft or loss of a firearm promptly, despite testimony that these reports help police track down guns that have fallen into the hands of criminals. In some cases, police find that owners falsely claim that their guns were lost or stolen when confronted with evidence that the firearms were used in crimes.

The Brady Campaign named Sen. Giannetti as one of its principal national targets for the 2006 election season in a letter to hundreds of thousands of supporters late last year.

The letter said "We will also punish those state legislators who stand in the way of our legislative priorities. In Maryland's 21st Senate District, Senator John Giannetti (D), killed the state assault weapons ban. Senator Giannetti must pay the price for allowing high-powered killing machines on the streets of Maryland. We will target his race and replace him with someone who will make sensible gun laws a priority."
