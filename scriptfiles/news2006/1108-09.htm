Massachusetts Chapter of Americans United Urges Massachusetts Legislature to Block Marriage Amendment
 

WORCESTER, Massachusetts - November 8 - The Massachusetts Chapter of Americans United for Separation of Church and State has urged members of the Massachusetts Legislature to block a proposed amendment to the Constitution that would define legal marriage as being only between a man and a woman.

The legislature is scheduled to vote on Thursday on whether to add H-653 to the state Constitution. Americans United now asks the public to contact their state senator and state representative and ask them to vote against H-653. This amendment also prevents the recognition of civil unions or any other legal equivalent of marriage for same-sex couples.

Americans United said the measure would write discrimination into the Constitution and would favor the marriage doctrines and rituals of some religious groups over others. Further, there is no need for such an amendment because nothing in the law at either the federal or state level allows any court or legislature to require any religious institution or person to perform any type of marriage ceremony. Given that houses of worship are already protected from governmental interference in decisions of who to marry, constitutional definitions of marriage are therefore wholly unnecessary.

"The proposed amendment," said Ronal C. Madnick, president of the Massachusetts Chapter of Americans United, "is a wrong- headed, mean-spirited and an ignoble campaign to write discrimination into the Massachusetts Constitution."
