Voters to Bush: Get out of Iraq
Members, Phone Calls, and Contributions Make Difference in Close Races
5 out of 9 Red Handed TV Ad Targets Defeated
 

WASHINGTON - November 8 - Tonight we saw a historic voter uprising, prompted by the disaster in Iraq. Voters have rejected the Bush administration, and given Democrats a mandate to lead the nation out of this quagmire. This election should put the Bush administration on notice that we need a timetable for a responsible exit.

We congratulate Speaker Pelosi for leading the charge. Tonight’s wins in Ohio, Kentucky, and New Hampshire prove that candidates do not need to sacrifice their principles to win – that voters rally around bold progressive leaders.

In races around the country, MoveOn members made an unprecedented effort to get out the facts and get out the vote. A few of the critical numbers:

  • $27 million in hard dollars raised – with $25 million focused on the House. Average donation: $45.

  • Over 7 million person-to-person calls to drop-off voters in 50 swing districts, plus the major Senate races, through our Call for Change program. More volunteers than in 2004 – a whopping 181,000. In most cases, the number of calls into the winning districts far outstripped the winning margin.
  • $4 million on “red-handed” ads to target nine second and third-tier races. Of the nine we’ve targeted, we’ve contributed to victories in five. Our ads exposed the special-interest money candidates took from oil companies, drug companies, and war profiteers early in these races.

In the months ahead, we will focus on turning this mandate for change into action on the issues our members – and the nation – care about most: a responsible exit from Iraq, health care coverage, clean energy, and a fair democratic system.

Our members have worked for six years to create this change. We’re glad this day has finally come.
