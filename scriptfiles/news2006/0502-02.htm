PFLAG Brings Message of Equality Home to Capitol Hill; Families Lobby for Fairness and Honor Out Legislators Frank, Baldwin, and Kolbe
 

WASHINGTON - May 2 - For the fourth year, Parents, Families and Friends of Lesbians and Gays (PFLAG) members will take to Capitol Hill with family photos in hand as part of their Bringing the Message Home campaign to meet with legislators to lobby them for support on legislation that promotes equality for their gay, lesbian, bisexual and transgender (GLBT) loved ones and to fight the Federal Marriage Amendment.

They will also use the opportunity to honor three members of Congress -- Rep. Barney Frank (D-Mass.), Rep. Tammy Baldwin (D- Wis.) and retiring Rep. Jim Kolbe (R-Ariz.) - for their courage to be out and open legislators.

"This event is a time for elected officials - many of whom are facing a vote on June 5 on the discriminatory Federal Marriage Amendment - to see the faces of their constituents and see in a very real way that we are about family values, too," said Jody M. Huckaby, PFLAG's executive director. "One of our core family values is simple, basic equality for all of our family members, and we want our legislators to understand what it means. That's what this day is about."

PFLAG members will be using the fourth edition of PFLAG's Bringing the Message Home campaign toolkit, a guide to some of the organization's key issues in real-world terms. Legislation included in the 2006 edition includes bills related to marriage equality, hate crimes, the military, discrimination in the workplace, safe schools, and immigration.

Collectively, PFLAGers will make more than 50 visits during the day on May 4. Meanwhile, PFLAG members nationwide are scheduling in-district lobby visits as part of the campaign that spans from Mother's Day to Father's Day, adding hundreds of additional meetings with legislators to the list.

Following the Lobby Day event, PFLAG is hosting a Capitol Hill reception to honor three openly gay and lesbian members of congress for their courage. They include Rep. Barney Frank (D- MA), Rep. Tammy Baldwin (D-WI) and Jim Kolbe (R-AR).

"These legislators have set a tremendous example for all Americans through their basic honesty and personal integrity and they serve as powerful role models, particularly for our GLBT young people. By virtue of being out, they send a message to everyone that GLBT people are everywhere, come from every kind of city and region, represent different political parties, and take on different issues, but all united for equality."
