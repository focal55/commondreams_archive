CREW Questions Either Party's Commitment to Real Ethics and Lobbying Reform; Oversight and Enforcement Mechanisms Absent from Proposals
 

WASHINGTON - January 18 - Today, Citizens for Responsibility and Ethics in Washington (CREW) questioned the effectiveness of the myriad lobbying reform proposals being rolled out on Capitol Hill this week.

Melanie Sloan, CREW's Executive Director, asked today "why doesn't a single one of these proposals include any oversight or enforcement mechanisms? Without oversight or enforcement rule changes are virtually meaningless." Sloan noted that many of the activities engaged in by former lobbyist Jack Abramoff and the Members of Congress he lobbied are already prohibited. For example, both House and Senate rules already prohibit Members from accepting meals totaling over $100 in the course of a year, and from accepting travel for recreational purposes.

Despite these prohibitions and clear evidence of violations of these rules, neither the Senate nor the House Ethics Committee has instigated a single investigation into any Member's relationship with Abramoff.

Sloan also criticized both parties for requiring lobbyists, but not Members of Congress or congressional staff to disclose their activities. Democrats require lobbyists to certify that they have not violated any rules, but fail to require similar certifications from Members and staff.

Finally Sloan noted that neither party has included any provisions reforming the congressional ethics process despite the fact that both Houses' ethics committees have remained completely silent in the face of the ever-escalating ethics crisis. "CREW believes that any meaningful reform must include ethics reform, including changing House rules to allow outsiders to file ethics complaints," said Sloan.

Other pieces missing from both parties' proposals are limitations on the use of appropriations earmarks, restrictions on the employment of Members' spouses and children, and restrictions on the use of corporate jets. Further, the Democrats have failed to include an important provision stripping pensions away from Members of Congress who are convicted of crimes related to their official actions.
