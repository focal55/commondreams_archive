Amnesty International Honors Expansion of Congressional Human Rights Caucus to U.S. Senate Reception to Include Remarks from Deputy Secretary of State Robert Zoellick, Senators Brownback, Harkin and Representatives Wolfe and Lantos
 

WASHINGTON - February 14 - Amnesty International USA has organized a reception celebrating the expansion of the Congressional Human Rights Caucus (CHRC) to the United States Senate on Wednesday, February 15, at 5:30 p.m. in room 345 in the Russell Office Building.

The reception, cosponsored by a coalition of leading human rights groups, will include opening remarks by Deputy Secretary of State Robert Zoellick, CHRC co-founder Representative Tom Lantos (D-CA) and CHRC co-chair Representative Frank Wolf (R-VA), followed by statements from Senate co-chairs Sam Brownback (R-KS) and Tom Harkin (D-IA). Eric Olson, Amnesty International USA Acting Director of Government Relations, will emcee the event.

The 240 bipartisan House Members of the CHRC have been vital players in the struggle to uphold and promote human rights worldwide. Amnesty International is confident that with the support and leadership of Senators Brownback and Harkin, the bicameral caucus will enhance its important role as one of the largest and most active caucuses in the U.S. Congress.

The reception is open to the public and press. Cosponsors include: The Carter Center, Freedom House, Human Rights First, Human Rights Watch, International Justice Mission, International League for Human Rights, Jacob Blaustein Institute for the Advancement of Human Rights, Minnesota Advocates for Human Rights, Physicians for Human Rights, Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Center for Human Rights and the Open Society Institute.

WHO: Deputy Secretary of State Robert Zoellick; Representatives Tom Lantos (D-CA) and Frank Wolf (R-VA); Senators Tom Harkin (D-IA) and Sam Brownback (R-KS); Eric Olson, Acting Government Relations Director for Amnesty International USA

WHAT: Reception to Celebrate the Expansion of the Congressional Human Rights Caucus to the U.S. Senate

WHEN: Wednesday, February 15, 2006, 5:30 p.m. to 7:30 p.m.
