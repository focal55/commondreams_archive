100 'Caught Red-Handed' Press Events Nationwide Today at Noon; GOP Reps to Get Hammered at Home for Financial Ties to Big RX Companies
 

WASHINGTON - May 11 -

News Advisory:

-- $60 Million in Drug Company Contributions to GOP Buys Plan that Sells Out Seniors

-- Members Will Urge Cancellation of May 15th Sign-Up Deadline Outside of Reps' Offices

On Thursday, May 11, MoveOn.org Political Action members in over one hundred cities nationwide will hold "Caught Red-Handed" mock check deliveries outside the offices of eighty-one GOP Representatives. At the events, members will expose what they believe is the Representatives' record of putting drug company profits ahead of seniors in the districts.

MoveOn members, who will express their dissatisfaction with the GOP passed Medicare prescription drug plan, share the following views:

-- Big drug companies spent hundreds of millions of dollars in campaign contributions and lobbying to get the GOP controlled Congress to pass the Medicare drug program in 2003.

-- To corral seniors into the program, President Bush and Congressional Republicans are going to slap as many as 14 million un-enrolled seniors with heavy, permanent penalties if they fail to sign up by the May 15 deadline -- amounting to a "senior tax" on local seniors.

"This Republican Medicare drug program does more for the drug companies than it does for seniors," said Ben Brandzel, Advocacy Director for MoveOn Political Action. "And now, Republicans in Congress are charging seniors with a massive lifetime penalty if they don't go along with this corrupt program by May 15th. Congress should stand up to big drug companies and protect seniors," Brandzel concluded.

On Thursday at 12 p.m., MoveOn members across the country will deliver oversized mock checks to their Members' district offices showing how much money they accepted from big drug companies.
