Families USA Annual Health Care Advocacy Conference Jan. 25-27 Will Bring Together Key Members of Congress and Hundreds of Consumer Health Advocates
 

WASHINGTON - January 3 - Families USA is hosting its annual Health Action conference at the Mayflower Hotel (1127 Connecticut Avenue NW, Washington, DC) on January 25-27, 2007. The three-day conference will bring together hundreds of grassroots health care leaders from over 40 states, as well as key members of congress and special guests. The following are among the confirmed guest:

> Senator Barack Obama (IL)

> Uwe Reinhardt, Professor of Economics & Public Affairs, Princeton University

> Bruce Vladek, Professor of Health Policy & Geriatrics, Mount Sinai School of Medicine, New York City

> The Rev. Dr. Bob Edgar, National Council of Churches

> Senator-elect Sherrod Brown

Participants will attend workshops and plenary sessions where they will share information, innovative strategies, and tactics to improve the affordability and quality of health care in America. Some of the issues and topics that will be discussed include: State coverage initiatives, health disparities, Medicare Part D, uninsured children and the SCHIP program, surviving medical debt, the federal budget fight, NIH and Drug Research Funding, Views from Capitol Hill, and many more.

This year we will also feature our first ever "radio row," featuring noted radio personalities, broadcasting live from the conference.

For the conference agenda and other information on Health Action 2007 visit our Web site at www.familiesusa.org.
