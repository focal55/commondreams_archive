Aids Drugs for Africa: No Backtracking on "Ambitious" Treatment Targets Call from ActionAid
 
LONDON - August 17 - At the World Aids Conference today, members of African civil society groups called for renewed commitment to universal access to Aids treatment by 2010.

Ugandan delegate, Leonard Okello of ActionAid said: “Universal access should mean just that, 100 per cent access to HIV testing and Aids care and treatment for all who need them.

“But we are now seeing African governments beginning to backtrack on ambition. They are setting targets to meet the money available, but not the need. That’s not good enough.”

ActionAid is a member of the newly formed African Civil Society Coalition on HIV and Aids, comprising nearly 40 groups from across Africa, which is calling for:

  • African governments, donor countries and international agencies to make meeting the 2010 target for universal access a top priority

  • Clear national structures and processes in place to ensure momentum is maintained at governmental level.

  • Real access for civil society to influence the decision making processes of international (including IMF and World Bank), regional (Africa Union) and national (governmental) institutions.
