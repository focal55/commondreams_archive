Suit Challenges New Law Requiring over 50 Million People in Medicaid to Document Citizenship
Millions of Citizens Who Can’t Produce Passports or Birth Certificates Could Lose Health Coverage Starting July 1
Families USA Will Host Audio Briefing on Wednesday, June 28 at12:00 Noon (EST)
 

WASHINGTON - June 28 - Starting July 1, 2006 citizens applying for or receiving Medicaid coverage will be required to provide passports, birth certificates, or other special documentation proving their status as U.S. citizens. This is likely to cause millions of U.S. citizens—especially those in nursing homes, people with mental or physical disabilities, and disaster victims—to lose their Medicaid coverage.

A lawsuit to be filed today, Wednesday, June 28, in Federal District Court, will challenge the constitutionality of this new law and will seek to stop its implementation. To explain what this law will mean for current and future Medicaid beneficiaries, and to describe the lawsuit, Families USA is hosting an audio conference call today, at 12:00 noon (EST).
