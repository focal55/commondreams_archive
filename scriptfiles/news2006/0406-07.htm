CBPP Briefing: Helping States Meet the New, Tougher Federal Work Requirements in Their TANF Programs
 

WASHINGTON - April 6 - WHAT: Center on Budget and Policy Priorities Conference Call: Helping States Meet the New, Tougher Federal Work Requirements in Their TANF Programs

WHEN: Tuesday, April 11 at 12 p.m. (EDT)

Register: http://www.cbpp.org/confcall.htm

WHO: Center staff:

-- LaDonna A. Pavetti, senior fellow, Mathematica Policy Research

-- Sharon Parrott, director, Welfare Reform and Income Support, CBPP

In the wake of enactment in February of the largest changes in federal welfare policy in a decade, the Center on Budget and Policy Priorities will hold a conference call briefing on Tuesday, April 11 at 12 p.m. (ET) to discuss an important new study that offers lessons to states as they seek to meet the stricter work requirements that the new law mandates in their Temporary Assistance to Needy Families (TANF) programs.

During the call, LaDonna A. Pavetti, co-author of the study and a nationally recognized welfare expert, will discuss the demonstration program in Minnesota that the new study examines and its implications for other states.

The Minnesota program focuses on identifying long-term TANF recipients' barriers to employment and developing ways to address them. In response to the sharply increased work requirements that soon will take effect, some states may place TANF recipients in "one-size-fits-all" work programs. As the evaluation of the Minnesota program shows, however, many long-term TANF recipients do not succeed in such programs because of the formidable challenges they face, such as mental health problems and low cognitive functioning.

This issue is of immediate concern to states. In June, the Department of Health and Human Services will issue regulations governing the new TANF work requirements. The National Governors Association recently recommended that states be given flexibility to design welfare-to-work programs for the kinds of families targeted by the Minnesota program. After opening remarks, the panelists will take questions.
