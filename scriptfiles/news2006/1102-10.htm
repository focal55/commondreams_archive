MoveOn 'Liquid' Phone Bank Expands from 30 to 57 Races; Micro- Targeting Fuels GOTV Mobilization Nationwide
 

WASHINGTON - November 2 - As the political landscape shifts and more Republican races become competitive, more than 80,000 MoveOn.org Political Action members are making 5 million phone calls to Democratic "drop-off" and Independent voters. The members are mobilizing voters in the nation's most competitive House and Senate races in order to increase Democratic performance at the polls on Election Day.

Call for Change is a "liquid" program. In the past week, several races including MO-Sen, VA-Sen, NC-08, PA-04 and NY-25 were added to MoveOn's list of races where MoveOn members make GOTV calls. "On Oct. 19 we saw a poll that showed Tim Walz was closing the gap. Within a few days, calls were pouring into Minnesota's first district. That's what makes our program 'liquid,'" said Eli Pariser, executive director of MoveOn.org Political Action.

Over 2.6 million calls have already been made -- including over 138,000 Wednesday night alone. While many MoveOn members call from the comfort of their own homes, the organization has opened 39 campaign offices throughout the country. At each of these offices, members who live in the area will make thousands of phone calls through Election Day aimed at defeating the Republican Congress.

To see the "Call for Change" program, visit: http://pol.moveon.org/nov06/practicegotv/call.html Log-In: journalists@elipariser.com / Password: journalist

MoveOn's one-of-a-kind "Call for Change" technology allows regular people to log onto http://www.callforchange.org and use their home computers and phones to call targeted voters. MoveOn utilizes consumer data and other "micro-targeting" methods to ensure that millions of voter mobilization calls go to Democrats and like-minded Independents who are infrequent voters.

"I'm so very grateful to Call for Change for affording me the opportunity to serve in the democratic process," said Susan Eppley User, a MoveOn member in Decatur, Ga. "As a single working mom of two girls under the age of six, I have very little time or money to dedicate to my civic duty. The calling was easy, it didn't take much time," User concluded.

The organization projects over 80,000 members will make 5 million phone calls this election cycle-including over 802,050 calls on Nov. 7. Members will host nearly 4000 "Call for Change" house parties this weekend and on Election Day.

The offices are located in 25 states, including Arizona, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Florida, Georgia, Illinois, Indiana, Iowa, Maryland, Massachusetts, Minnesota, Missouri, Montana, New Mexico, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Texas, Virginia, Washington State, Washington, D.C. and Wisconsin. These offices will target vulnerable races including Rep. Nancy Johnson (CT-5), Rep. Deborah Pryce (OH-15), Rep. Thelma Drake (VA-02) and Rep. Chris Shays (CT-4)

-- Media are invited to cover activities at these offices -- or this weekend's house parties. For exact locations, please call Trevor Fitzgibbon at 202-246-5303 or Laura Gross and Alex Howe at 202-822-5200.

The office locations were selected because thousands of MoveOn members live in the selected areas and because the local Republican incumbents are politically vulnerable due to consistently voting for President Bush's failed agenda.

"It's time to end one-party Republican rule in Washington -- they've messed everything up," Pariser added. "MoveOn has opened 39 offices in 25 states because we need to do everything we can to hold the Bush Administration accountable and move America in a new direction."

Each office will implement the Call for Change phone program, using cutting-edge targeting methods. Members will reach out to Democrats and like-minded Independents who are infrequent voters -- such as new registrants or those who voted in past presidential elections but not congressional elections. Each voter will get up to three phone calls: one in October, another the weekend before the election, and a third on Election Day -- totaling 5 million calls nationally by Election Day.

The program was tested in a special election in April and a recent study reviewed by the nation's foremost scholar of voter turnout shows these targeted calls boosted voter turnout more than any volunteer phone bank ever studied.

More information is available at http://www.CallforChange.org.
