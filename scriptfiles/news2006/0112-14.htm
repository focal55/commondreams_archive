State of the World 2006: China, India, the U.S., Europe, and Japan by the Numbers
 

WASHINGTON - January 12 - These slides offer a glimpse of the rising ecological impact of two populous developing nations, China and India, while illustrating that the per-capita resource consumption and pollution of countries in the industrialized world—including the United States, the European Union and Japan—is much higher. If by 2030, China and India alone were to achieve a per-capita footprint equivalent to that of Japan today, together they would require a full planet earth to meet their needs.

Learn more in the State of the World 2006 report.
