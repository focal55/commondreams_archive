Federal Lawsuit Seeks End to Warrantless Electronic Surveillance of Domestic Phone Calls and Emails
Center for Constitutional Rights Believes Privileged Attorney-Client Communications Were Intercepted by NSA without Warrants
 

WASHINGTON - January 17 -

WHAT: News conference to announce and provide details of a federal lawsuit challenging the NSA’s surveillance of persons within the United States without judicial approval or statutory authorization.

WHEN: Tuesday, January 17th at 10:30 A.M.

WHERE: The offices of the Center for Constitutional Rights, 6th Floor Conference Room, 666 Broadway, New York, NY

WHO: CCR Attorney/Plaintiffs who have engaged in innumerable telephone calls and e-mails with people outside the United States, including their clients, their clients’ families and friends, potential witnesses, and others.
