Voting Begins at 9 a.m. Monday for Best Idea Since Sliced Bread; National 'Bread Box' of Ideas Becomes Ballot Box of the Nation
 

WASHINGTON - January 6 - Participatory democracy has reached new heights. Americans are invited to vote for the public policy ideas that they believe have the best chance of strengthening our economy and improving the lives of working people. The top ideas will get support from the nation's fastest growing union, which will promote them with lawmakers and the public. And it's all happening online.

On SinceSlicedBread.com, people can review 21 policy ideas chosen by a diverse panel of judges from more than 22,000 submitted by everyday working people. Beginning Monday at 9 a.m., visitors to the site can vote for the three they think are the best ideas "since sliced bread."

Sponsored by the Service Employees International Union (SEIU), SinceSlicedBread.com received ideas from people in every single state. They addressed improving public schools, simplifying to tax code, making health care more accessible and affordable, and more. The winning ideas will be announced on Feb. 1 and will receive SEIU's support. The creator of the winning idea also will receive $100,000, and the creators of the two runners up will receive $50,000 each.

"We asked Americans for their ideas on how to make this country a better place to live and work," said SEIU President Andy Stern. "The response was overwhelming. We received more than 22,000 suggestions from people in every state and congressional district. Now we're asking Americans to choose the ideas that would most improve their lives from the 21 finalists."

A panel of leading economists, thinkers, businesspeople, labor leaders, activists and Wall Street investors selected the top 21 ideas to be voted on. The panel includes such notable names as Bill Bradley, Ed Rollins and President and Founder of Teach for America, Wendy Kopp.
