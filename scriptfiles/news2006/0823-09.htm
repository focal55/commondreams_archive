Lawyers' Committee for Civil Rights and the Mississippi Center for Justice Hold Housing Workshop for Those Forced to Move
 

WASHINGTON - August 23 - The Lawyers' Committee for Civil Rights Under Law ("Lawyers' Committee") and the Mississippi Center for Justice ("MCJ") will hold a free housing workshop on Saturday, August 26 from 2 to 4 p.m. at the Isaiah Fredericks Community Center in North Gulfport. The workshop is open to the public.

The goal of this workshop is to provide legal assistance and support to the over 400 families that live in Charles Warner Homes in Pascagoula and the L.C. Jones Homes and W.M. Ladnier Homes in Gulfport. Earlier this month, the Mississippi Regional Housing Authority ("MRHA") announced plans to sell or transfer these public housing complexes. If approved, this plan would displace hundreds of residents and force them into a rental market that is already stretched thin. The legal workshop will provide individual counseling for the residents of these properties.

WHO: Lawyers' Committee for Civil Rights and Mississippi Center for Justice

WHAT: Free Public Housing Workshop - Open to the Public

WHEN: Saturday, August 26, 2 p.m. - 4 p.m.

WHERE: Isaiah Fredericks Community Center in North Gulfport

3312 Martin Luther King Jr. Blvd., Gulfport, MS

Brief remarks will be made by Barbara Arnwine, Executive Director of the Lawyers' Committee, Ella Holmes-Hines, City Council Member, City of Gulfport, and Reilly Morse, Attorney with the Mississippi Center for Justice.

In the wake of Hurricanes Katrina and Rita, the Lawyers' Committee and MCJ have coordinated more than 25 community legal workshops for thousands of hurricane survivors. Through these workshops, we have directly reached more than 1,000 families in core disaster areas.

The Lawyers' Committee is a nonpartisan, nonprofit civil rights legal organization, formed in 1963 at the request of President John F. Kennedy to provide legal services to address racial discrimination.

For more information on the Lawyers' Committee, visit http://www.lawyerscommittee.org
