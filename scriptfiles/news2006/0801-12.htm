When Is A Raise Not A Raise?
 

WASHINGTON - August 1 - Answer: When it’s a minimum wage increase passed by the House, you are an employee who receives tips for your work, and you live in Alaska, California, Minnesota, Montana, Nevada, Oregon, or Washington.

H.R. 5970, passed by the House on July 29, includes a raise in the federal minimum wage for most kinds of employment. But buried in its language is another provision which, by overturning minimum state wage provisions for employees who receive tips from customers, will cut their pay by as much as $5.50 per hour.

Analysis by Ross Eisenbrey, the Economic Policy Institute’s vice president and policy director, explains how and to whom this could happen.
