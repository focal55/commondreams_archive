---

Under the Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty of 1968, the five nuclear powers - the United States, Britain, France, China and the Soviet Union -- committed themselves not to use their nuclear weapons to threaten or blackmail the non-nuclear states, and to reduce and eventually dismantle their nuclear arsenals. For their part, the 177 non-nuclear powers that signed it committed themselves not to acquire nuclear weapons. But the nuclear powers have ignored their commitment in Article Six to phase out their nuclear arsenals. The United States now deploys 5,966 operational strategic nuclear weapons, with some 2,400 on hair- trigger alert ready for launching. Russia has a comparable number. And the Bush Administration is now planning to restore a large-scale capacity to manufacture nuclear weapons -- in order to replace, it says, older bombs that it claims are no longer safe or reliable.
