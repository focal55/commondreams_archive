Record High Trade Deficit Driven by Oil Prices
 

WASHINGTON - February 10 - The trade deficit in goods and services reached a record $726 billion in 2005, according to today’s release of data from the U.S. Department of Commerce.  Analysis by Economic Policy Institute economist Robert Scott shows that not only higher oil prices, but a substantial jump in manufactured trade deficit, occurred in 2005.  In today’s Trade Picture, Scott also looks at the role Chinese imports, the value of the dollar, and advanced technology products play in this deficit, as well as what this record deficit means to the whole economy.
