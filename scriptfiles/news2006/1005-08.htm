Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence: Transcript of Letter to President Bush from Brady President Paul Helmke
 
WASHINGTON - October 5 - Paul Helmke, president of the Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence, sent the following letter today to President George W. Bush.:

President George W. Bush

The White House

1600 Pennsylvania Ave. NW

Washington, DC

Dear Mr. President:

I was pleased to hear that you have asked your staff to convene a White House Conference on school gun violence. I want to congratulate you for taking this action and offer my strong support.

As the mayor of Fort Wayne, Ind. for three terms, I saw firsthand the terrible toll gun violence takes on our communities. As mayor, I tried everything I could to fight this scourge. I worked with survivors, faith leaders, neighborhood activists and law enforcement on a variety of programs. We didn't find all the answers, but working with all the groups, violence did begin to decline in our community.

A few months ago, I was approached by members of the board of directors of the Brady Center and Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence about leading that organization into the future. I was deeply honored to have the Board ask me to take over the organization as president, which I did in July. They knew they were getting a different kind of perspective by selecting a republican former mayor from Indiana. Although being in Washington, D.C. is a big change from Fort Wayne, I truly believed that working to make our schools and communities safer from gun violence was a critical task that I should not shy away from.

It is in my capacity as president of the Brady Center and Campaign that I write to you today. I am asking you to approach next week's conference in a way that will defy the pundits and brush aside narrow and partisan political pressures. You have a unique opportunity to help start an honest effort to reduce gun violence in our schools and elsewhere. It is my fondest wish that our country can start down the path towards resolving a divisive argument that has lasted for many years and take steps that will keep our children safer.

I know you want your efforts in this policy area to produce results. I know that you will face strong political forces urging you to sweep some potential solutions under a rug. I urge you to resist those forces and help America have an honest conversation about gun violence. We all understand that politics is the art of the possible and that it is very hard for political leaders to tackle intractable problems and make progress. But we need to have a legitimate national conversation about the root causes and costs of gun violence and a genuine, multi-dimensional discussion of the policy options available.

I stand ready to assist. Events like the terrible ones of recent days are bound to occur again and again in the future unless we take concrete actions to make our communities safer. Please allow me to help you in this formidable challenge.

Sincerely,

Paul Helmke, president, The Brady Center Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence, Washington, D.C.
