CBPP Conference Call Briefing Feb. 3: Health Savings Accounts - Examining the President's Proposals
 

WASHINGTON - February 2 -

WHAT: Center on Budget and Policy Priorities Conference Call: Health Savings Accounts -- Examining the President's Proposals

WHEN: Friday, Feb. 3 at 11 a.m. (EDT)

Register: http://www.cbpp.org/confcall.htm

WHO: Center staff:

-- Edwin Park, Senior Health Policy Analyst

-- Jason Furman, Senior Fellow

-- Robert Greenstein, Executive Director

DETAILS:

The Center on Budget and Policy Priorities will hold a conference call briefing on Friday, February 3 at 11 a.m. (ET) to discuss the implications of the President's proposals to expand health savings accounts, in light of the additional details relating to these proposals that the White House has now released. The White House has issued a fact sheet on these proposals, and Allan Hubbard, director of the White House's National Economic Council, held a press conference on these proposals on February 1.

The new information shows that the HSA tax breaks the President is proposing are considerably larger than had earlier been understood. The additional detail about how the proposals would work has implications for whether employers would be induced to drop coverage, the size and cost of the tax preferences that would be created or expanded, and the attractiveness of HSAs as tax shelters.

Considerable confusion about these proposals already is arising, such as with regard to an Administration claim that its proposals would make all out-of-pocket health care costs deductible for HSA users.

After opening remarks, the panelists will take questions.
