Wages in the Doldrums
 

WASHINGTON - January 12 - The picture painted by the latest data shows that for most American workers (except the very highest-paid ones), the blues aren't over yet. Given the moderate pace of labor demand, employment grew only 1.5% in 2005, less than half as fast as the average for this point in past recoveries. Coupled with acceleration in inflation, fed largely by rising energy prices, that spells considerable pain for workers' pocketbooks, whose wage growth failed to keep pace with rising prices.

Today's Economic Policy Institute snapshot, by senior economist Jared Bernstein, shows what's been happening to both nominal and inflation-adjusted hourly wages from 2000 to 2005.
