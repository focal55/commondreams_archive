America's Gifts for Valentine's Day Commonly Include Toxic Pesticides and Child Slavery;
Largest Organic Consumer Group says, 'Unchain Your Love; Buy Organic/Fair Trade Valentine's Day Chocolate and Flowers in 2006
 

WASHINGTON - January 17 - A month from now millions of Americans will give flowers and chocolate on Valentine's Day to show their love for moms, dads, kids and spouses. Yet millions of people don't know about the large quantities of toxic pesticides used in conventional flowers and of the horrific conditions children as young as nine experience while harvesting cacao on plantations used in popular chocolate confections. The Organic Consumers Association (OCA), with over 850,000 members in its national network, is urging Americans to boycott traditional chocolate and flowers this Valentine's Day in favor of gifts that are organic and Fair Trade.

To draw attention to the hidden dangers of non-organic and non Fair Trade chocolate and flowers, OCA is releasing a new Flash animation February 6, entitled Slammed. Slammed is designed to reach a mainstream audience of consumers who are heretofore unaware of these issues. OCA's Flash animation is expected to reach over one million people via the internet. On February 3 there will be a SNEAK PREVIEW for MEMBERS OF THE MEDIA of Slammed in advance of its public release on February 6.

"In the name of greater profits transnational corporations are cutting corners by polluting the environment as well as exploiting children and workers to supply the huge demand for flowers and chocolate on Valentine's Day," says Ronnie Cummins, National Director and co-founder of the Organic Consumers Association. "With 40 percent of the world's chocolate coming from Côte d'Ivoire where the International Labor Organization (ILO) and the U.S. Department of State have reported for three years there is pervasive child slavery, it is simply immoral for companies such as Nestle, Hershey's, M&M/MARS, Kraft and others to continue to do business there or through their global commodity importers such Archer Midland Daniels, Cadbury and Cargill. Fortunately Americans can choose from a wide variety of organic and Fair Trade options on February 14, Valentine's Day, that are competitively priced and ethical."

Meanwhile organizations such as the International Labor Rights Fund and the Pesticide Action Network have found that commercial flowers, produced in countries such as Colombia, are the most toxic and heavily sprayed agricultural crops on Earth and that workers in the flower industry, often young women, are forced to work 18 hour days for poverty wages during peak flower buying times such as Valentine's Day.

Millions of Americans will receive emails and letters over the next month providing them with a list of retailers and distributors of Fair Trade and organic chocolate and flowers. The list can also be found online at http://www.organicconusmers.org.

"OCA doesn't want to be a "Debbie Downer" but most Americans have no idea what goes into these Valentine's Day gifts. It's up to the media to give people the facts they need to make informed decisions in order pressure the transnational companies that are part of this global network of environmental pollution, labor exploitation, and child slavery," said Cummins.
