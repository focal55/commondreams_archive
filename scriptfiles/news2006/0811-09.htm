Interfaith Vigil for Darfur in Crawford, TX Calls President Bush to Action
Local Religious Leaders and Activists Gather in Prayer and Solidarity; Africa Action Reiterates the Need for UN Peacekeepers in Darfur
 

WASHINGTON - August 11 - Last night, Africa Action joined with local religious leaders and activists in Crawford, TX, to pressure President Bush to take new action to stop the continuing genocide in Darfur. This interfaith vigil, held at the Crawford Peace House, gathered dozens of people in prayer and discussion about the need for increased U.S. leadership to mobilize international action on this crisis.

Speakers at the vigil included Rev. Robert Flowers, campus minister for the Wesley Foundation at Baylor University, and Rev. Edward Fana Vincent, Social Action Coordinator at the Shrine of the Black Madonna in Houston, TX.

Ann-Louise Colgan, acting Co-Executive Director of Africa Action, said today, "This vigil is only one example of many gatherings around the country calling for greater U.S. commitment to Darfur. President Bush must now challenge the Sudanese government's opposition to a UN peacekeeping force for Darfur, and pave the way for such a force to be authorized and deployed. This is the immediate next step required, and the U.S. must take every step necessary to achieve this goal."

This week, an op-ed by Ann-Louise Colgan entitled "Empty Promises for Darfur" was published at TomPaine.com, detailing the need for a UN peacekeeping operation and appropriate steps for the U.S. to overcome the current deadlock on this crisis. The full text is available here: http://africaaction.org/newsroom/index.php?op=read&documentid=2031&type=15&issues=1024

Ann-Louise Colgan went on to say, "Almost two years ago, the U.S. took an important step and accurately applied the genocide label to the killing in Darfur. In the years since, Africa Action has made the simple demand that the U.S. follow through on its own statements and take seriously the responsibility to act that such a declaration requires."

Africa Action, following the success of the interfaith vigil in Crawford, will press on with its Darfur escalation strategy, available here: http://www.africaaction.org/campaign_new/docs/PumpingUpthePowertoProtect2.pdf.

The organization and its activists continue to mobilize towards a major rally in front of the White House on September 9th, recalling the date two years ago when the U.S. first declared the crisis in Darfur to be genocide and urging new action to stop the violence and protect the people of Darfur.
