Congressmembers' Call for EPA to Respond to the EPA Inspector General's Recommendations in the August 2003 IG Report on EPA's Response to Ground Zero
Statement of Suzanne Mattei, Sierra Club NYC Executive
 

WASHINGTON - August 22 - I’m proud, as a Sierra Club representative, to stand with these members of Congress. Real leaders pursue the truth. Real leaders put public health and safety first. Real leaders still believe that honesty and  integrity are American values.

We need accountability in government, especially on issues that  affect human health and safety. At Ground Zero, we have seen lies  compounded by more lies. And those lies have had a devastatingly harmful  impact on the health of thousands of people. We need an end to lies.

And Congressman Nadler is right. We should be very vigorous in our scrutiny of the process to select a new Inspector General for the federal  Environmental Protection Agency.

Will that new appointment ensure that our next Inspector General is an independent  investigator who will speak the truth even if it sometimes  makes important people uncomfortable?

Or will that new appointment ensure that high quality, high integrity reports like this one [the August 2003 EPA Inspector General Report)  never, ever happen again?

The Sierra Club thanks these members of Congress for their leadership on this important issue.
