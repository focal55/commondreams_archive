Free Email Service Conserves Rainforest Land and Jaguar Habitat
 

PORTLAND, Maine - February 27 - PlanetSave.com announces free email accounts (your name@planet-save.com) that can help conserve rainforest land and jaguar habitat.

How it Works
 “Green” sponsors pay for unobtrusive messaging above the users inbox. The messaging is often from green not-for-profits asking you to sign petitions to help global environmental causes. When a user is done reading, composing, or sending their email they are sent to the sponsors webpage. The sponsor money goes to Friends of the Calakmul to conserve land and to PlanetSave.com to upkeep the site and pay for the service.

Finally you can use a free email service that helps the environment and the planet.

Currently PlanetSave.com has 20,000 users and is growing fast.

Want to Sign Up?
 Go to http://www.planet-save.com to sign up and you will be on your way to helping the earth by sending emails.

PlanetSave.com is working with Friends of Calakmul to protect part of the 6 million-acre Selva Maya forest, the largest tract of rainforest in the Americas north of Amazonia, by helping to secure the buffer zone of the Calakmul Biosphere Reserve in the Yucatan Peninsula, southern Mexico.
