Defining The Issues Series
 Mexican Elections: Another Florida?
 

WASHINGTON - July 17 -

When: Wednesday, July 19, 2006
12:00pm-1:00pm
Where: Institute For Policy Studies, Conference Room
1112 16th St. NW, Suite 600
(16th and L Streets, near Farragut North Metro)

Mexico's July 2nd presidential elections ended in a virtual tie, with pro-poor candidate Andres Manuel Lopez Obrador coming in only a half a percentage point behind pro-business candidate Felipe Calderon. Join Mexico specialist Jonathan Fox of the University of California, and Manuel Perez Rocha of the Mexican Action Network on Free Trade, for a discussion of what happens next in this razor thin electoral battle. Was there fraud or was it a clean election? With hundreds of thousands of Mexicans taking to the streets, what will happen over the next six weeks before the Mexican electoral tribunal names a winner? Will all the ballots be recounted? And, what is at stake for Mexico and the United States?

Panelists Jonathan Fox , University of California, Santa Cruz
Manuel Perez Rocha, Mexican Action Network on Free Trade
Host: John Cavanagh, Director, Institute for Policy Studies
