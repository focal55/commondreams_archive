Military Families and Iraq War Vets Honor Fallen US Troops and Iraqi Civilian War Victims on Veterans Day on the National Mall
Families Say: Honor the Fallen, and Support Our Troops by Bringing Them Home Now
 

WASHINGTON - November 10 - Military Families Speak Out (MFSO), a national organization of people opposed to the war in Iraq who have relatives or loved ones in the military will honor the over 2,800 American service men and women, and tens of thousands of Iraqi children, women, and men who have died as a result of the war in Iraq this Saturday November 11, 2006 for Veterans Day. Fifty military families from across the country, including Gold Star families whose loved ones were killed in Iraq, military families with loved ones currently serving in Iraq and those with loved ones who have served and/or are due to deploy, will display an exhibit of over 2,800 flags and photographs on the National Mall. Earlier this week, over thirty military families from California, Colorado, Missouri, New Jersey, South Carolina and other states gathered in Washington, D.C. to deliver petitions to the Pentagon and Congress calling for an end to troop extensions, stop-loss orders, involuntary re-calls, multiple deployments, and other aspects of the “back door draft,” and for all troops to be brought home now.

Saturday November 11, 2006: Veterans Day Memorial

WHO:
Military families, Gold Star families, and Iraq War veterans

WHAT:
Memorial to honor American and Iraqi casualties of the War in Iraq

WHERE:
National Mall (between 14th St. and 15th St. at Constitution Ave., near the Washington Monument

WHEN:
Saturday, November 11, 2005 from 10:00am – 5:00pm

VISUALS:
Military and Gold Star families with photos of their loved ones, posters, will pay tribute to victims of the Iraq War with over 2,800 flags, and photographs on the National Mall

Families will be available on site for interviews.
