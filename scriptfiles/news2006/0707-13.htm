Job Growth Slows Again, Indicating Trend
 

WASHINGTON - July 7 - June’s 108,000 new jobs mark the third month in a row that job growth is slower than analysts expected.  In today’s Jobs Picture, EPI senior economist Jared Bernstein sees job growth being affected by the same forces cooling the overall economy and expects this to persist.  Wages have accelerated and finally exceed inflation.  Bernstein notes, however, that wage growth still lags far behind productivity growth and poses no inflation threat.
