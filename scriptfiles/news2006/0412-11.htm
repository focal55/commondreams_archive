Don't Blame Health Insurance
 

WASHINGTON - April 12 - We’ve heard from many sources, including the President’s Council of Economic Advisors, that rising health care costs are what's behind sagging wages. Trouble is, that explanation doesn’t pass the numbers test for low-wage workers. 

In today’s Economic Policy Institute Snapshot, economists Elise Gould and Sylvia Allegretto show that health care costs can’t fully explain what's happened to the wages of most workers in the lowest-paid 20% of the workforce, since three-fourths of them aren’t covered by employer-provided health insurance anyway.
