Flawed Formula Could Mean Fewer Section 8 Housing Vouchers In 2007; New CBPP Report Details Impacts on 2,400 Housing Authorities
 

WASHINGTON - March 7 -

WHAT: Conference Call Briefing

WHEN: Monday, March 13 at 2 p.m. (ET)

Register: http://www.cbpp.org/confcall.htm

WHO:

Barbara Sard, director of Housing Policy, CBPP

Michael Kelly, executive director, District of Columbia Housing Authority

Douglas Rice, Housing Policy Analyst, CBPP

The Center on Budget and Policy Priorities will hold a conference call briefing on Monday, March 13 at 2 p.m. (ET) to discuss a new Center report on the funding level and disbursement formula for the Section 8 housing voucher program contained in President Bush's proposed budget for Fiscal Year 2007. Section 8 is the largest subsidized housing program.

On March 14, a House Appropriations subcommittee is scheduled to hold a hearing on the budget for the Department of Housing and Urban Development.

The new Center report includes state-by-state and locality-by- locality estimates of the projected effects of the budget proposal on the areas served by the 2,400 state and local housing agencies that administer the program. The report finds that many state and local agencies would have to cut back their programs and serve fewer households in 2007 than in 2006.

After opening remarks, the presenters will take questions.
