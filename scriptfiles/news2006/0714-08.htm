Congressional Anti-War Leaders to Speak at Assembly of Nation’s Largest Peace Group in Detroit
 

WASHINGTON - July 14 - In the midst of dramatically increasing violence in Iraq and the Middle East, as well as rising tensions with Iran and North Korea, Reps. Lynn Woolsey (D-CA) and John Conyers (D-MI) will join peace movement icon Tom Hayden in speaking to the 18th National Congress of Peace Action, to be held this weekend, July 14-16, at Wayne State University in Detroit, Michigan. All three are available for media interviews.

Rep. Woolsey is a founder of the “Out of Iraq” caucus in the U.S. House of Representatives, the first member to offer legislation on troop withdrawal, and a strong advocate for non-proliferation. She will be giving a keynote speech at 1:30pm on Saturday, July 15 in Manoogian Hall (Warren and John C. Lodge Freeway).

Rep. Conyers is a founder of the Congressional Black Caucus, a supporter of legislation to end the war in Iraq, and as ranking member of the House Judiciary Committee has been a leader in demanding accountability from the White House on the pre-war intelligence and justification for the Iraq war. He will be speaking and receiving a Lifetime Achievement Award immediately after Rep. Woolsey’s speech on Saturday.

Tom Hayden was a founder of the Students for a Democratic Society, one of the seminal organizations of the “New Left” in the 1960s. He went on to become a California state legislator, and most recently a noted speaker, author, and analyst of U.S. foreign policy. He will be giving a keynote speech at 7:30pm on Friday, July 14 in General Lectures Hall (Warren and Anthony Wayne Drive).

Media are invited to attend these sessions. To schedule interviews at other times, please call the Peace Action contacts at the numbers above.
