Military Families in New Jersey Hold Press Conference, Call on State Sen. Tom Kean, Jr. to Answer Their Questions about the Iraq War
Press Conference will be 3 p.m. Monday, Oct. 9, National Guard Armory Teaneck Road and Liberty Road, Teaneck, N.J.
 

WASHINGTON - October 9 -

On Monday, Oct. 9, Military Families from New Jersey will hold a press conference to highlight the questions that they have asked State Sen. Tom Kean, Jr. about the war in Iraq and about our troops -- questions that he has, to date, refused to answer.

The press conference will be at 3 p.m. Monday, Oct. 9, at the National Guard Armory, Teaneck Road and Liberty Road, Teaneck, N.J.

On Aug. 14, four members of Military Families Speak Out - Bergen County Chapter met with Kean to ask him specific questions about the war in Iraq and U.S. troops who were sent to fight there. Three of these families have loved ones currently deployed to Iraq, one has a son who will soon deploy. Kean answered some of the groups' questions, and agreed to answer the rest and put all of his answers in writing. On Aug. 28, his staff informed the group that Kean would not supply them with written answers to their questions.

The questions that Kean failed to answer involve whether or not he would serve in the military, the logic behind troops remaining in Iraq, a timetable for withdrawal of troops from Iraq and care for returning veterans.

At a press briefing on Oct. 6 in Manhattan, Jo Ann Sohl, a military mother and member of Military Families Speak Out - Bergen County, again asked Kean to answer the group's questions. A member of Kean's staff rudely intervened, preventing her from communicating with the State Senator, who then left the room without responding.

"The answers to our questions concern the life or death of our loved ones as well as all our troops and the people of Iraq," stated Sohl, whose son and only child was injured in Iraq and most recently had his tour of duty there extended. "More than 2,744 U.S. troops have been killed -- 30 service men and women in the first eight days of October," Sohl continued. "Tens of thousands of Iraq children, women and men have died as a result of this war. Over 20,687 U.S. troops have suffered serious injuries. Each one of these people is someone's child, someone's loved one."

"The questions we are asking are critical to the troops and their families who bear the human cost of the war, but they also affect every citizen of this state," said Paula Rogovin, whose son will soon deploy to Iraq. "With each passing day the human and financial costs of this war escalate. The stakes are too high (and too precious) to allow those who are seeking to represent us to be unwilling or unable to provide answers to these critical questions."

Military Families Speak Out members from New Jersey are also asking Kean to reconsider his response to one of the questions he did answer on Aug. 14. Kean was asked, on behalf of MFSO member John Fenton from Little Ferry, whether or not he has been to Bethesda Naval Hospital or other military hospitals to visit wounded troops. Fenton's son Matthew, a Marine, died in Bethesda on May 6 of wounds he received in Iraq. Kean responded that candidates for office are not allowed to visit troops in these hospitals. "Officials in these hospitals have said that this is absolutely not the case," said Sohl. "We think Mr. Kean needs to acknowledge that he can indeed visit wounded troops -- not campaign there, mind you -- but has, in fact, chosen not to."
