Published on Tuesday, May 14, 2002 in the San Francisco Chronicle
E-mail on Energy Shocks Capitol Hill
Member of Bush administration task force asked that California be avoided in report
by Richard Simon
 
WASHINGTON - An e-mail from a high-ranking staff member of the Bush administration's energy task force said officials were "desperately trying to avoid California" in a report dealing with the energy crisis last year, Rep. Henry Waxman, D-Los Angeles, said Monday.

The e-mail, obtained under the Freedom of Information Act by watchdog and environmental groups and also provided to Waxman, was largely redacted, and neither administration officials nor the congressman could say specifically what the task force staffer was discussing.

But Waxman seized on the missive to turn the heat up on the White House to release all documents relating to the task force's discussions about California's energy crisis.

Waxman said the e-mail, along with the recent release of documents showing Enron sought to manipulate the California energy market, "underscores the need for the administration to provide a complete accounting of its understanding of and approach to the energy crisis."

The e-mail was sent May 4, 2001, by Karen Knutson, deputy director of the energy task force, to Jacob Moss of the Environmental Protection Agency. It raised concerns about putting into the report a paragraph "emphasizing environmentally difficult issues about expediting permitting to deal with a crisis."

"We are desperately trying to avoid California in this report as much as possible," the e-mail continues. "Can you make the same point without going into California . . ."

Knutson could not be reached for comment, and administration officials could not immediately say what it was about California that she sought to keep out of the report. But they defended their actions on California's energy crisis.

"Congressman Waxman has confused the Bush administration with the lack of action taken by the Clinton administration," Energy Department spokeswoman Jeanne Lopatto said. She said the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission, on Bush's watch, had imposed electricity price controls and begun an investigation into whether Enron and other power sellers sought to use their market power to drive up prices.

The task force report released last year actually features California prominently, using the state's problems to bolster its arguments for building more power plants.

Waxman said the e-mail raised questions about whether the widespread deletion of information from thousands of pages of documents turned over by federal agencies was the result of the administration's claims that disclosure would chill frank and open discussion or was intended to avoid embarrassment.

Waxman's letter was the latest salvo in a yearlong effort by congressional Democrats to force the administration to make public details of private meetings held with lobbyists during drafting of the energy plan.

The battle was fired up last week with the release of internal Enron memos that detailed an array of Enron price-manipulation strategies. Two Senate committees plan hearings on the memos Wednesday.
