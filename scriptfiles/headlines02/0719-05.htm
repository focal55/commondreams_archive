Published on Friday, July 19, 2002 by Reuters
Thousands of Iranians Rally Against Bush Comments
by Parisa Hafezi
 

TEHRAN - Tens of thousands of Iranians chanting "Death to America, death to George Bush" took to the streets on Friday in an official demonstration of defiance against what they view as U.S. interference in Iran's affairs.

Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei called the rally to show popular opposition to President Bush's statement last week voicing support for reform in Iran.

Crowds around Tehran University -- the main venue for Friday prayers in the Iranian capital -- burned an effigy of Bush with a sign around its neck saying: "I made a mistake."

"From the start, we said we want America to change its arrogant behavior against Iran and accept that Iran is a free, Muslim, independent country," influential former President Akbar Hashemi Rafsanjani told worshipers who lined the streets around the university campus.

"We tell the Americans to put aside their arrogant behavior, then this nation could start dialogue with you and talk to you," said Rafsanjani, a top advisor to Khamenei.

Soldiers, civil servants and hordes of women in black chadors joined the demonstration, but there were also a number of pro-reform students present.

"We don't want the United States' support, we want reforms," said student Ahmad Ali Aminiha.

"SLAP IN THE MOUTH" FOR BUSH

The U.S. administration traditionally shies away from commenting on Iran's internal politics for fear of further strengthening powerful conservatives in their campaign to undermine reformists allied to President Mohammad Khatami.

Rafsanjani pointed to the tens of thousands at Friday's rally compared to the roughly 4,000 disaffected youths who clashed with security forces on the third anniversary of violent student unrest on July 9.

Hard-liners appointed to strong state posts by Khamenei have blocked Khatami's attempts to reform the Islamic Republic at every step, leaving many analysts wondering whether the mild- mannered president is willing or able to challenge them.

Khamenei said on Wednesday that Khatami had given Bush a "slap in the mouth" by condemning his statement on reform.

Bush has branded Iran part of an "axis of evil," enraging Iranian conservatives already wary of the U.S. troop presence in Afghanistan to the east and now eyeing nervously a possible U.S. attack on Iraq to the west.
