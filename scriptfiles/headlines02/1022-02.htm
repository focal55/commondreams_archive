Published on Tuesday, October 22, 2002 by the Associated Press
Peace Activist Challenging Kerry
Forsberg To Run For Senate Seat
 

BOSTON -- A peace activist is challenging U.S. Sen. John Kerry to protest Kerry's vote in favor of using military force in Iraq. The activist calls her write-in candidacy a "referendum on commitment to peace in Massachusetts."

Randall Forsberg, a Democrat who has been active in the nuclear freeze movement for more than 20 years, said unilateral U.S. action on Iraq would weaken the United Nations and hurt international arms control efforts. (http://www.rforsberg.com/)

She said Kerry, a Vietnam War veteran, voted in favor of President Bush's Iraq war resolution in order to buttress his all- but-announced bid for the presidency in 2004.

"It looks like the best way to run for president is to favor going to war, instead of preventing it," Forsberg said.

A Kerry spokesman did not immediately return a call seeking comment.

Forsberg, 59, has been director of the Institute for Defense and Disarmament Studies, a non-profit think tank in Cambridge, since 1990.

She alerted Kerry of her intentions on Friday and registered her write-in candidacy with the Secretary of State's office on Monday.

An author and expert on disarmament, Forsberg advised former U.S. Sen. Alan Cranston of California, a nuclear arms control activist, during his unsuccessful 1984 presidential run.

Kerry faces Libertarian Michael Cloud on the Nov. 5 ballot. His only Republican opponent, Anthony Kandel, failed to collect enough signatures to qualify.

Kerry voted two weeks ago in favor of a congressional resolution authorizing President Bush to use force to disarm Iraq. He said at the time, "I believe that a deadly arsenal of weapons of mass destruction in (Saddam's) hands is a real and grave threat to our security."
