Published on Monday, September 23, 2002 in the Islamic Republic News Agency
Palestinians Observe General Strike in Solidarity with Arafat
 

Al-Khalil, Sept 23, IRNA -- Palestinians throughout the West Bank, Gaza Strip and East al-Qods on Monday observed a general strike to express solidarity with the beleaguered Palestinian Authority leader Yasser Arafat.

Israeli occupation tanks have been laying siege on Arafat and 250 of his aides and guards inside his virtually completely destroyed headquarters in Ramallah.

Schools, colleges and shops in the West Bank and Gaza remained closed as more demonstrations were organized in several Palestinian population centers in defiance of the Israeli curfew on Palestinian movement.

In some areas like in al Khalil, Israeli troops opened fire on peaceful demonstrators, causing some casualties.

The strike comes in the aftermath of the massive and largely spontaneous demonstrations which took place in several Palestinian towns Saturday and Sunday after Israeli troops threatened to kill the Palestinian leader and his aides.

Israeli bulldozers demolished up to 90% of PA headquarters in Ramallah, generating widespread anger among ordinary Palestinians who viewed the destruction and the accompanying threats to kill Arafat as an insult to Palestinian national dignity.

The demonstrations, in which all Palestinian political groups took part, amounted to a moral booster to the Palestinian leader who has been isolated by Israel and shunned by the United States.

Meanwhile, Israeli occupation forces allowed PA official Sa'eb Ureikat access to Arafat's encircled office.

Earlier, Ureikat said Arafat would never agree to surrender a single Palestinian to Israel.

Ureikat's remarks were made in reaction to an offer by Qatari Foreign Minister Hamad Ibn Jasem to host some 50 Palestinians holed up with Arafat whom the Zionist regime says are wanted for taking part in the resistance against Israeli occupation and apartheid.

The UN Security council is expected to discuss the standoff in Ramallah, with the US threatening to veto any draft resolution calling for the immediate lifting of the Zionist siege on the Palestinian leader.
