From Crawford, Texas, to Washington, D.C.: Three-Bus Tour Leaves Camp Casey Aug. 31;
Gold Star and Military Families, Iraq War and other Vets Travel Country, Converge at Anti-War Rally in D.C. Sept. 24

 
WASHINGTON - August 30 -

DETAILS:

On Aug. 31, the last day of the nearly month long vigil outside of President Bush's Ranch, the Bring Them Home Now Tour will launch three buses from Crawford, TX each carrying military and Gold Star families, veterans of the Iraq War and veterans of previous wars. These vehicles will travel different routes across the country, converging in Washington, D.C., on Sept. 21, to join the mass mobilization against the Iraq War that will occur Sept. 24-26.

WHO:

Cindy Sheehan, Military Families, Gold Star Families, Iraq War and other Veterans

WHAT:

Launch of Bring Them Home Now tour to carry message and spirit of Camp Casey from Crawford, Texas, to cities and towns across the country

WHERE:

Camp Casey I on Prairie Chapel Road, Crawford, Texas

WHEN:

Aug. 31, 10:30 a.m. press conference; 11:30 a.m. departure of vehicles

The three bus tour, covering forty-two cities in 26-states will launch from the first Camp Casey site, outside George Bush's ranch in Crawford, Texas, following a press conference on Wednesday, Aug. 31. The press conference will feature families and veterans who will be traveling the three different routes. After launching the tour, Cindy Sheehan will ride on the bus heading to Austin and Houston and will speak at events in those cities.

After departing from Crawford, one vehicle will head north through Oklahoma City to stops in Wichita, Kansas City, Des Moines, Minneapolis, Madison, Milwaukee, Chicago, Toledo, Detroit, Buffalo, Rochester, Syracuse, Albany, Amherst, Boston, New Haven and New York City. The southern route will stop in Austin, Houston, New Orleans, Mobile, Tallahassee, Montgomery, Atlanta, Savannah, Columbia, Fayetteville, Raleigh-Durham, and Richmond, while the central tour will visit Dallas, Little Rock, Memphis, St. Louis, Indianapolis, Cincinnati, Columbus, Cleveland, Pittsburgh, Harrisburg, Philadelphia, Trenton, and Baltimore.

All will arrive in Washington, D.C. on Sept. 21.

The Bring Them Home Now Tour is sponsored by Gold Star Families for Peace, Iraq Veterans Against the War, Military Families Speak Out, and Veterans For Peace.
