Dover Trial Triggers National Action by Scientists & Clergy
New Online Campaign Launched Against Religious Right
Report Thursday Will Highlight Top Ten “Islands of Ignorance” Where Teaching Evolution Is Threatened
 
WASHINGTON - September 26 - The Campaign to Defend the Constitution (www.defconamerica.org) will release a letter on Thursday to all fifty governors signed by Nobel laureates, other leading scientists and scores of clergy, calling on the states to ensure that science classes teach evolution and base curricula on established science, not ideology.

The Campaign will also release a report highlighting the top ten “Islands of Ignorance” around the country where science education is under attack.

These announcements are the first actions of the newly formed Campaign to Defend the Constitution (DefCon), an online grassroots movement to combat the threat posed by the religious right to American democracy, public education and scientific leadership. The Campaign is led by prominent national scientists, legal scholars and clergy (*list below).

The launch of DefCon coincides with the start of the trial in Dover, PA over a school board mandate to teach intelligent design as an alternative to evolution.
