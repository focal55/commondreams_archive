Cindy Sheehan Arrives in DC Tomorrow: Calls for "Money for Hurricane Relief, Not for War"

 

WASHINGTON - September 20 - Cindy Sheehan and Bring Them Home Now Bus Tour Arrive at U.S. Capitol as Momentum Builds for Sept. 24 Anti-War Rally

Gold Star and Military Families, Iraq War and other Vets Visited 51 cities in 28 states after their vigil in Crawford, Texas

Sheehan Back From Delivering Aid to Gulf Coast Joins Majority of Americans in Calling for ‘Money for Hurricane Relief – Not for War’

Wednesday, September 21, 2005

WHEN: 12 Noon

WHERE: West Lawn of the U.S. Capitol between 3rd Street and the Capitol Steps..

WHAT: Military families, Gold Star families and veterans will arrive on buses at the U.S. Capitol and hold a pressconference

WHEN: 1:30 PM

WHERE: White House

WHAT: Military families, Gold Star families and veterans will attempt to deliver to President Bush a copy of the four questions they want to ask him about what is the “noble cause” their family members are fighting and dying for

On September 21, Cindy Sheehan and the Bring Them Home Now Bus Tour will arrive in Washington, DC to bring their call for an end to the Iraq war directly to the doorstep of members of Congress.

The 51-city, 28-stop tour has been traveling the country for the last month to highlight the voices of Gold Star families, who show the devastating human cost of the war in Iraq; the voices of military families, for whom each and every day of this war brings the potential for the most devastating of consequences; and the voices of veterans, who can share the ground truth about war and the impact on those who were sent off to fight it.

Their message: "Bring the troops home now, take care of them when they return, and to never again send our loved ones to war based on lies."

In recent events, Sheehan and the other tour members have been calling attention to the relationship between Hurricane Katrina and the Iraq war.

Sheehan has also joined the national volunteer effort to aid victims of Hurricane Katrina. Last week, she was in Covington, Louisiana, where she witnessed first hand the devastation in New Orleans as she helped deliver food and water to one of the poorest and hardest hit neighborhoods. Veterans who’d participated in her vigil in Crawford, Texas have been in Covington since September 2, operating a relief operation out of “Camp Casey III.”

“My efforts for these last weeks since we left Camp Casey in Crawford have been about standing up against the disastrous policies of the Bush Administration and about helping people who are suffering because of those policies,“ Sheehan said.

“That includes the US troops who are risking their lives every day in Iraq, and now it also includes the good citizens of Louisiana, Mississippi, and Alabama. We need to take the money that’s being spent on war and destruction in Iraq and put it toward hurricane relief and reconstruction in New Orleans.”

According to Monday's CNN/USA Today/Gallup poll these sentiments are shared by a majority of Americans. 54 % said they would cut spending for the war in Iraq to pay for hurricane relief, and 59 % said launching the war was a mistake. That figure is the highest recorded in a CNN/USA Today/Gallup poll. Only 39% said the invasion of Iraq was the right thing to have done.

In addition to Cindy Sheehan, the following veterans and families who also lost loved ones in Iraq are available for interviews, among others:

Al Zappala of Philadelphia, PA, whose son Sgt. Sherwood Baker was the first Pennsylvania National Guardsman to die in combat since World War II. Tammara Rosenleaf of Belton, TX. Her husband is stationed at Ft Hood, TX and will deploy to Iraq this fall. Elliott Adams of Sharon Springs, NY enlisted in the Army during the Vietnam War. He served in Vietnam, Korea, Japan, and Alaska as an infantry paratrooper.

These military families and veterans, among others, have been traveling with the three-bus Bring Them Home Now bus tour launched from Camp Casey in Crawford, Texas at the end of August. They will remain in Washington, DC thru Saturday, September 24, to participate in the large anti-war march and rally.
