AIUSA Chairman to Discuss Anti-Muslim Bigotry, Increasing Anti- American Sentiment in the Islamic World, at CAIR Conference
 

WASHINGTON -- May 10 -- News Advisory: Amnesty International Chairman to Discuss Anti-Muslim Bigotry, Increasing Anti-American Sentiment in the Islamic World, at Council on American-Islamic Relations Conference

DETAILS:

Joe "Chip" Pitts, Amnesty International USA (AIUSA) Board Chair, will address the Council on American-Islamic Relations (CAIR) 2005 Annual Conference: "Islamophobia and Anti-Americanism - Causes and Remedies." The conference will explore the root causes behind the twin phenomena of Islamophobia and anti-Americanism as well as positive and practical solutions.

"There's no question that Muslims in the United States and around the world have borne the brunt of the latest war-on-terror measures," said Pitts. "Although the Patriot Act had some soft language at one point saying that Muslims shouldn't have their rights violated, the truth is that Muslims have been US society's latest scapegoats in the current environment of fear."

Pitts formerly served as Treasurer of the AIUSA Board, and he is currently a volunteer leader with the Bill of Rights Defense Committee and the corporate social responsibility movement. He is a Lecturer at Stanford Law School, and he also serves on Advisory Boards of the Center for Social Entrepreneurship and Accountability (University of Texas at Dallas), the ACLU of Dallas, the United Nations Association of Dallas and the London- based Business & Human Rights Resource Centre. He is a member of the Council on Foreign Relations in New York and the Pacific Council on Foreign Policy in San Francisco. He resides in Dallas.

WHEN:

Pitts will speak Saturday, May 14, at 1:10 p.m. as the keynote speaker for the conference luncheon and also on Sunday, May 15, on a panel titled "Impact of Islamophobia and Anti-Americanism on Civil Rights and Policy-Making," 10:25 a.m. until 12:20 p.m.

WHERE:

Sheraton Premiere at Tysons Corner, 8661 Leesburg Pike, Vienna, Va. (703-448-1234)
