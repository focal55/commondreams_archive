Congressman Jesse Jackson, Jr. to Join Keynote Panel At Progressive Counter-Inaugural Summit
 

WASHINGTON -- January 21 -- Tim Carpenter, executive director of Progressive Democrats of America, announced today that U.S. Representative Jesse L. Jackson, Jr. (D-IL) will speak at the PDA Counter-Inaugural Summit tomorrow, Saturday, January 22 at the U.D.C. Law School Auditorium.

Congressman Jackson will be the lead speaker during the morning session beginning at 9:15 a.m. He will first address the several hundred attendees on the topic “Building A More Perfect Union,” then he will participate in a blue ribbon panel discussion on the “Challenges and Opportunities for Progessive Democrats in 2005.”

Joining Congressman Jackson on the dais will be James Zogby, founder of the Arab-American Institute, Kim Gandy, president of the National Organization for Women (N.O.W.), Medea Benjamin, of Global Exchange and CODE PINK, and Hillary Shelton of the D.C. chapter of the N.A.A.C.P. The discussion will be moderated by PDA Policy Advisor, Steve Cobble who will also field questions from the audience.

“We are extremely honored to have Congressman Jackson’s participate in the Summit,” said Carpenter. “His appearance affirms PDA’s growing strength and influence.”
