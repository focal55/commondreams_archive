Children's Defense Fund to Release New Data on Damaging Impact of Tax Refund Loans on Low-Income D.C. Taxpayers
 

WASHINGTON -- February 11 -- News Advisory: Millions of low-income American workers, including thousands in the nation's capital, paid millions in unnecessary fees and excessive interest in 2003 to quickly collect their tax refunds.

On Saturday, Feb. 12, the Children's Defense Fund will join with D.C. Congresswoman Eleanor Holmes Norton at the Annual Norton Tax Preparation and Information Fair in a news conference on the need for low-income workers to use free income tax preparation services to avoid paying too much to commercial tax preparation firms. CDF will release its report on the impact of this problem on District of Columbia taxpayers.

WHAT: Release of CDF Report on impact of Refund Anticipation Loans on low-income D.C. taxpayers at the Annual Norton Tax Preparation and Information Fair

WHEN: 11 a.m., Saturday, Feb. 12

WHERE: D.C. Convention Center, Room 156. Use Convention Center/Mount Vernon Square station on Metro's Green and Yellow lines.

WHO: D.C. Congresswoman Eleanor Holmes Norton, Deborah Cutler- Ortiz, CDF Director of Family Income, and a taxpayer who was charged excessive fees for tax refund loan.

The Children's Defense Fund's Leave No Child Behind(r) mission is to ensure every child a Healthy Start, a Head Start, a Fair Start, a Safe Start and a Moral Start in life and successful passage to adulthood with the help of caring families and communities
