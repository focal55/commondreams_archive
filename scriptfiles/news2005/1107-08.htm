Oxfam International Can Facilitate Trips for Reporters to Countries Struck by South Asian Tsunami;
As One-Year Anniversary Approaches, Oxfam Invites Reporters to View Progress at Key Recovery Programs
 

BOSTON - November 7 - Almost one year after a tsunami struck Southeast Asia, claiming more than 224,000 lives and displacing more than 1.6 million people, international aid organization Oxfam International is offering journalists an opportunity to view the progress being made in some of the hardest-hit areas where the group is working.

Much of Oxfam's work is now focused on the long-term recovery of devastated communities in Sri Lanka, Indonesia, and southern India. Following are some of the areas of focus Oxfam has developed over the past year, which journalists are invited to visit:

-- Cash-for-Work Recovery Programs: Oxfam is helping people rebuild communities by developing cash-for-work programs that will restore livelihoods like fishing, agriculture, and salt production.

-- Shelter: Oxfam continues to build shelters in the hardest-hit areas. In Sri Lanka alone, more than 3,700 shelters have been built.

-- Marginalized Communities: Oxfam is committed to delivering aid to the most vulnerable populations affected by the tsunami, such as women or low-caste manual laborers, and has created financing tools and other recovery programs to support the infrastructure for long-term growth.

Reporters should contact any of the Oxfam press officers listed above if they are interested in touring Oxfam development programs in India, Sri Lanka, India, or Aceh, Indonesia.
