Media Briefing: How the U.S. Pressured Guatemala to Adopt a Law Limiting Public Health Protections and Ratify CAFTA
 

WASHINGTON -- March 15 -- News Advisory:

WHAT: Leaders of the Association of Guatemalan Pharmaceutical Industries will discuss U.S. pressure on Guatemala to ratify the Central America Free Trade Agreement (CAFTA), as well as the role of multinational pharmaceutical corporations in the debate.

The Guatemalan Congress voted on Thursday to ratify CAFTA, as hundreds of protestors took to the streets in Guatemala City. U.S. pressure also led to the repeal of an important public health law that had allowed greater competition from generic drug companies as a method of reducing drug costs in a sustainable way. Health groups, supported by international agencies such as the Pan-American Health Organization and UNICEF, had worked to pass the law, which the USTR objected to as inconsistent with CAFTA. The U.S. government pushed for the repeal of the law, installing provisions that unduly extend the monopoly period enjoyed by international pharmaceutical companies and potentially hindering Guatemalans' access to important life-saving drugs at accessible prices.

WHEN: Wednesday, March 16, 10 to 11 a.m.

WHERE: National Press Club, Lisagor Room, Washington, D.C.

WHO:

  • Juan Carlos Ucross, member, Association of Guatemalan Pharmaceutical Industries
  • Leonel Rodas, director, Manufacturing Association of Pharmaceutical Products
  • Moderated by Ellen Shaffer, director of the Center for Policy Analysis on Trade and Health and Stephanie Weinberg, trade policy advisor, Oxfam America
