Coalition of Spinal Cord Injury Groups Plan Wheelchair “March” on the Capitol April 12
“Spring Into Action” Rally to Push for Passage of Christopher Reeve Paralysis Act Dana Reeve, Marc Buoniconti to Speak
 

WASHINGTON -- April 5 -- In the spirit of carrying on the legacy of Christopher Reeve, wheelchair activists and loved ones will “Spring Into Action” in the nation’s capital on Tuesday, April 12.

Under the banner of CureParalysisNow.org, the rally was organized by grassroots volunteers from the CareCure Community, an online forum for spinal cord injury victims and their families, of over 9,000 members. The rally is a “first” for the largely invisible disabled population. Armed with statistics on the costs of their care versus the cost of a cure, the participants from multiple organizations plan to rally in a showing of solidarity.

Following the event the participants will meet with their legislators to urge support for the Christopher Reeve Paralysis Act, as well as funding for the first clinical trial network for spinal cord injuries. The legislation calls on the National Institutes of Health and other federal agencies to place a higher priority on spinal cord injury research, rehabilitation and quality of life programs for people living with paralysis.

What: “Spring Into Action” Rally of wheelchair activists

When: Tuesday, April 12th at 10:00am

Where: West Side of the Capitol Building

Featured speakers include:

  • Dana Reeve – Chairman of the Christopher Reeve Paralysis Foundation;
  • Marc Buoniconti – Ambassador of The Miami Project to Cure Paralysis;
  • Justin Richardson – 24-year-old who was forced to travel across the globe just a few days after his spinal cord injury to receive a life changing treatment;
  • Rep. James R. Langevin – Spinal cord injured US Representative from Rhode Island;
  • Dr. Wise Young – pre-eminent neuroscientist in the field of spinal cord injury of the W. M. Keck Center for Collaborative Neuroscience, Rutgers University;
  • Professirx – Spinal cord injured artist performing musical tribute, “Forever Superman”;
