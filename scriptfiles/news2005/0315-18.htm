National Freedom of Information Day Conference; 'Congress and the Courts: Confronting Secrecy' March 16 in Arlington, Va.
 

WASHINGTON -- March 15 -- News Advisory:

WHAT: The 2005 National Freedom of Information Day Conference will bring together access advocates, government officials, lawyers, librarians, journalists, educators and others to discuss the latest issues and developments in freedom of information.

WHO:

  • U.S. Sen. John Cornyn, R-Texas, a forceful advocate for open government since his days as Texas Attorney General (keynote address)
  • Floyd Abrams, First Amendment attorney
  • Lee Levine, First Amendment attorney

WHEN: Wednesday, March 16, 8 a.m. - 3:30 p.m.

  • 8:45 a.m. -- Keynote address "FOI and the Consent of the Governed" by Sen. John Cornyn
  • 9:30 a.m. -- Presentation, "An Access Challenge: Electronic Court Records"
  • 10:30 a.m. -- Panel discussion, "Access and Courts"
  • 1 p.m. -- James Madison Award presentation
  • 1:30 p.m. -- Panel discussion, "Stirring in Congress: Strengthening Access"

WHERE: Rooftop Conference Center, Freedom Forum

1101 Wilson Blvd., Arlington, Va.

BACKGROUND: The seventh annual 2005 National Freedom of Information Day Conference will feature panel discussions and updates on FOI issues and developments during the past year in the courts, in the administration and in Congress.

The FOI Day Conference comes midway through 'Sunshine Week,' an initiative by news media and other organizations to create a public dialogue on the value of open government, which began on March 13. On March 17, Missouri University's journalism school has organized a program on the press and confidential sources at the National Press Club in Washington, D.C.

Among reports and surveys to be launched at this year's conference: a First Amendment Center First Report on government secrecy; FOI Updates on federal legislation, recent court decisions and electronic access to court records; the Society of Professional Journalists' new CD-ROM, "FOI Audit Toolkit"; and a special report on state FOI laws by the Marion Brechner Citizen Access Project.

The annual event has been held at the Freedom Forum - a nonpartisan foundation dedicated to free press, free speech and free spirit for all people - since 1999. Details about the conference - including a list of panel participants - are available at http://www.firstamendmentcenter.org.
