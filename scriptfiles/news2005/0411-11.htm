Global Week of Action for Trade Justice, April 10-16; Hundreds Throughout Boston Take Action to Make Trade Fair, Alleviate Poverty
 

WASHINGTON -- April 11 -- News Advisory:

WHAT: Hundreds of Bostonians will participate in a wide variety of local events –- from Fair Trade cooking demonstrations to a presentation on Corporations and Democracy led by Noam Chomsky –- during the Global Week of Action for Trade Justice (April 10-16). Students, faith groups and community members will be taking action and raising awareness on trade policies that perpetuate the poverty millions in the developing world face daily.

WHY:

  • 1.2 billion –- approximately one-fifth of the world’s population –- live on less than $1 a day.
  • More than 842 million people in the world suffer from hunger and malnutrition.
  • In the US alone, 35 million people experience daily hunger.

These events have been organized in conjunction with the Global Week of Action for Trade Justice, taking place April 10- 16, 2005. Millions of people in over 70 countries will participate in events throughout the week. In the United States, Oxfam America supporters have organized more than 250 events in 43 states. Together, we are creating a movement in support of a leveled playing field for farmers, Fair Trade coffee and other products, and against unfair trade agreements.

The events listed are just a few of the events taking place this week. For additional details contact Helen DaSilva at 617-331-2984.
