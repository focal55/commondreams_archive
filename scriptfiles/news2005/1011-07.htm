Former UN Weapons Inspector Scott Ritter to Speak at National Press Club, Oct. 12

 

WASHINGTON - Scott Ritter, Former UN Weapons Inspector and author of the newly released Iraq Confidential, will be available for media Q & A on Wednesday, October 12 in Washington at a 9 a.m. breakfast briefing at the National Press Club, 529 14th Street NW, 13th floor, co-sponsored by Nation Books and the Government Accountability Project.

One-on-one interviews, schedule permitting, can be arranged for Tuesday afternoon, October 11 and Thursday, October 13 (morning only).

Ritter is available to discuss his book, which reveals how the CIA manipulated the work of UN departments to achieve the U.S.'s foreign policy agenda in the Middle East, and current concerns including:

-- His views on an exit strategy from Iraq

-- The state of current intelligence on Iran

-- The awarding of the Nobel Peace Prize on Friday to the International Atomic Energy Agency (IAEA) and its chief, Mohamed ElBaradei. ElBaradei led a team of weapons inspectors in Iraq and questioned whether Iraq had nuclear weapons during the run up to the US invasion there. The Bush Administration recently opposed ElBaradei's third terms as chief of the IAEA, on the grounds he would be too soft on Iran.
