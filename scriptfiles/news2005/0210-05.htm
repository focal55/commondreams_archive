Drum Major Institute Announces Fellows Program
Leading Progressive Think Tank Establishes Program to Counter Conservative Right on Public Policy Issues
 
NEW YORK, NY -- February 10 -- Today, The Drum Major Institute for Public Policy (DMI), the leading public policy group, launches a Fellows program that will challenge the traditional model for the development of social and economic policy.

The organization’s Fellows program will seek to insert New York City’s grassroots leaders into the broader public policy debate. Calling on the lived experiences of progressives working on the ground of failed public policy to provide new thinking, DMI's first-rate Fellows program will take on the institutes of the right, with an innovative model they believe will more adequately address problems faced by communities and lead to better solutions for them.

“The time is ripe to take on extreme right wing groups like the Manhattan Institute whose views on public policy issue too often go unchallenged,” said DMI Executive Director Andrea Batista-Schlesinger. “Our fellows are unique in that they are both activists and intellectuals who combine grassroots experience with intellectual rigor. Unlike most public policy pundits when it comes to the issues they address, they will be able to speak from the experience of having been there.”

The fellows range from the founder of Brooklyn's first black-run credit union, to a pioneering advocate for environmental justice and sustainable development in the South Bronx, to the organizer of a grassroots student activist and leadership training organization at Hunter College in Manhattan. These Fellows not only span the physical city, but a range of pressing issues its residents care about. Combining their talent and experience with rigorous research support provided by DMI staff, communications training, and aggressive dissemination and placement of their work, DMI aims to change the face of discussion about public policy in this city.

The fellows are:

Majora Carter

Environmental Justice

Majora Carter is the founder and executive director of Sustainable South Bronx, a community organization dedicated to the implementation of sustainable development projects for the South Bronx that are informed by the needs of the community and the values of environmental justice. As a DMI Fellow, Majora focuses on environmental justice issues.

Andrew Friedman

Language Access and Immigrant Rights

Andrew Friedman is the founding co-director of Make the Road by Walking, a not-for-profit, membership-led organization which organizes Bushwick, Brooklyn community residents to work collectively to improve neighborhood life, to demand accountability from neighborhood institutions and to create a community that is controlled by everyday people living within the community. As a DMI Fellow, Andrew focuses on language access and immigrant rights.

Mark Winston Griffith

Community Development and Economic Opportunity

Mark Winston Griffith is the founding director of the Central Brooklyn Partnership (The Partnership), a nonprofit organization that builds community-owned financial cooperatives, conducts research and advocacy on behalf of community reinvestment, develops youth leadership and promotes financial literacy among local residents. As a DMI Fellow, Mark focuses on community development and economic opportunity.

Maureen Lane

Welfare Policy and Higher Education

Maureen Lane is the co-director of Welfare Rights Initiative (WRI), a grassroots student activist and leadership training organization located at Hunter College which seeks to mobilize, empower and support women directly affected by welfare policy. As a DMI Fellow, Maureen focuses on welfare policy and higher education.

Kate Kyung Ji Rhee

Criminal Justice

Kate Kyung Ji Rhee is the director of the Prison Moratorium Project, a multi-racial group of young activists, community members and formerly incarcerated people in New York City that works locally and nationally to stop prison expansion and mass incarceration, and re-invest resources into communities most impacted by criminal justice policies through educational programs, alternatives-to-incarceration initiatives, housing and sustainable economic development. As a DMI Fellow, Kyung Ji focuses on criminal justice issues.

The Drum Major Institute for Public Policy is a non-partisan, non-profit organization dedicated to challenging the tired orthodoxies of both the right and the left. The goal: progressive public policy for social and economic fairness. DMI’s approach is unwavering: We do not issue reports to see our name in print or hold forums for the sake of mere talk. We seek to change policy by conducting research into overlooked, but important social and economic issues, by leveraging our strategic relationships to engage policymakers and opinion leaders in our work, and by offering platforms to amplify the ideas of those who are working for social and economic fairness.
