Beijing +10 Review: U.S. Taking Steps Backward on Women's Rights
 

WASHINGTON -- March 3 -- Moving Ideas just released "Beijing +10: U.S. Record on Women Here & Abroad" (http://www.movingideas.org/content/en/report_content/beijing10.htm), a report detailing the U.S. record on promoting the rights of women here and abroad. Moving Ideas issued this report in conjunction with the 10-year review of the 1995 Beijing Platform for Action (BPFA) on Women's Rights taking place in New York City this week. Also this week, Moving Ideas' new blog, Ideopolis (http://www.movingideas.org/minblog/), is featuring CodePink reports directly from the Beijing +10 review and Global Week of Action events.

The "U.S. Record on Women Here and Abroad" report details the U.S. record on women in the areas of poverty, health, violence against women, and power and decision-making. The report also includes links to resources from policy experts as well as ways to get involved with the Global Week of Action for Women's Rights Worldwide. "While we've made strides for women's rights, our country's record is far from perfect," says Moving Ideas Director Melanie Alston-Akers. "As a world leader, it is important that the U.S. takes the lead in the fight for women's rights worldwide. Unfortunately, that is presently not the case."

Demonstrating this point, the U.S. representatives to the Beijing +10 review seem unable to focus on anything else other than trying to convince the world that the BPFA didn't establish a right to abortion. That the BPFA does not in fact include language saying countries should work to ensure women have access to the full range of healthcare options.
