 National Chair of Veterans for Peace Impeachment Committee to Speal at D.C. Rally
 

Mike Ferner, member of Veterans For Peace and chair of the VFP Impeachment Committee, is scheduled to speak at a rally in Washington D.C.’s Lafayette Park, directly across from the White House, Thursday, June 16, 5:00pm.

The rally has been called by the AfterDowningStreet.org coalition in support of Congressman John Conyers, ranking Democrat on the House Judiciary Committee, who plans to deliver a letter to the White House addressed to President Bush, signed by over 500,000 Americans and at least 94 Congress Members. The letter asks the President to respond to questions raised by the “Downing Street Minutes,” recently-released British documents describing plans to invade Iraq based on manipulated intelligence information.

Earlier on Thursday, from 2:30 to 4:30 pm, in Room HC-9 of the U.S. Capitol, Rep. Conyers and other Congress Members will hold a hearing on the Downing Street Minutes and related evidence. The hearings will be broadcast on C-SPAN http://www.c-span.org/ and Pacifica Radio http://pacifica.org/programs/20050616_Downing_Street_Memo_Hearings_Special_John_Conyers.html

Speakers at the 5:00 rally include: Members of Congress John Conyers, Barbara Lee, Lynn Woolsey and Maurice Hinchey (schedules permitting following the hearings); Cindy Sheehan, Co-Founder of Gold Star Families for Peace; Reg Keys, Member of Military Families Against the War, challenger to Tony Blair in last election (flying in from UK); John Bonifaz, Co-Founder of AfterDowningStreet.org; Tom Hayden, Co-Founder of Progressive Democrats of America; Medea Benjamin, Co-Founder Code Pink/Global Exchange; Stephen Cleghorn, Member of Military Families Speak Out; Rev. Lennox Yearwood, President and CEO of HipHop Caucus; Ray McGovern, former CIA analyst; William Rivers Pitt, Reporter for Progressive Democrats of America; Leslie Cagan, National Coordinator of United for Peace and Justice; Kevin Zeese, Director of Democracy Rising; and Terry O'Neill, Vice President of the National Organization for Women.
