EWG Shows Reforms Would Alter Subsidy Map
Redrawn Analysis Bases Aid on Economic Contribution; Map Would Change Dramatically if U.S. Distributed Subsidies According to Value
 

WASHINGTON - December 13 - Environmental Working Group (EWG) President Ken Cook will offer a new analysis of U.S. subsidy data at an event sponsored by the U.S.-based German Marshall Fund (GMF) at the World Trade Organization (WTO) talks in Hong Kong tomorrow.

The current subsidy program lets producers of four crops take 78 percent of the money, leaving two-thirds of America’s farmers with no aid. A new formula for apportioning aid would mean a fairer deal for the vast majority of America’s farmers, their communities, taxpayers and the environment.
