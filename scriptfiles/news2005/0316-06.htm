Military Families, Iraq Veterans Come to Fayetteville on 2nd Anniversary of Iraq War to Say: 'Support the Troops; Bring Them Home NOW'
 

WASHINGTON -- March 16 -- News Advisory: Military Families Speak Out will join with Iraq Veterans Against the War, Gold Star Families for Peace, Veterans for Peace, Vietnam Veterans Against the War and thousands of others from North Carolina and around the country in Fayetteville, North Carolina to mark the 2nd anniversary of the U.S. invasion of Iraq. They will call for an end to the U.S. military occupation of Iraq, for bringing the troops home now, for troops to be taken care of when they get home, and for an end to the policies that allowed this war. Fayetteville is home to Ft. Bragg and the Army's 82nd Airborne Division, and is adjacent to Pope Air Force Base.

Military families, veterans and others will gather on Saturday, March 19, beginning at 10 a.m. at the County Health Center, 227 Fountainhead Lane, and march to Rowan Street Park in Fayetteville at 12 p.m. A speaking program will last from 1 p.m. to 4 p.m. at Rowan Street Park that will include representatives of Military Families Speak Out, Iraq Veterans Against the War, Gold Star Families for Peace and others. Military Families Speak Out members with loved ones currently in Iraq, those whose loved ones have returned from Iraq, and those whose loved ones died as a result of the war in Iraq will come from as far away as Hawaii, Washington state, Oregon and California and as close as Ft. Bragg and Camp Lejeune, N.C.

Members of Military Families Speak Out will also participate in a press conference on Friday, March 18 at 1 p.m. at the Quaker House, 223 Hillside Ave., Fayetteville, N.C. Among those speaking at the press conference will be MFSO member and Gold Star Families for Peace co-founder Lila Lipscomb (featured in Fahrenheit 911) whose son Sgt. Michael F. Pedersen was killed in Iraq on April 2, 2003; MFSO member Kara Hollingsworth from Ft. Bragg, N.C. whose husband is serving on his second deployment to Iraq; MFSO co-founders Nancy Lessin and Charley Richardson whose son served in the U.S. Marine Corps in Iraq in spring, 2003; and Michael Hoffman, co-founder and national coordinator of Iraq Veterans Against the War, who served in the U.S. Marine Corps during the invasion of Iraq.
