Scientific Study Reveals Elevated Mercury Across Northeast; Songbirds, Hotspots Impacted; Teleconference March 8
 

WASHINGTON -- March 7 -- News Advisory:

What: Findings from new research and new report, “Mercury Connections: The Extent and Effect of Mercury Pollution in Northeastern North America,” include:

  • Mercury inputs are higher in some areas than previously reported by EPA.
  • First account of mercury in forest songbirds, and high levels found in mink & otter.
  • New map of nine biological hotspots published.

This research is contained in 21 papers in the peer-reviewed journal "Ecotoxicology" which is available and will be published in a special double issue on April 1, 2005.
