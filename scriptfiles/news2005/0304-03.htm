Educators to Stop the War Conference; Saturday, March 5, 2005, 08:30am – 5:00pm
 
NEW YORK, NY -- March 4 --

Hunter High School

Manhattan

Over 500 educators, students, and labor leaders from all over the east coast will meet at Hunter High School in Manhattan for a day-long conference on Saturday, March 5, 2005 to plan ways to build anti-war activity in teacher unions, public schools and colleges in coming months.

Speakers will include Iraq veterans and military families, Mike Hoffman (co-founder of Iraq Veterans Against the War), Nancy Lessin and Charlie Richardson (Military Families Speak Out) and well-known anti-war activists Medea Benjamin (Global Exchange, Code Pink, Occupation Watch), Fred Mason (President of the Maryland-DC State Federation of Labor and national co-convener of US Labor Against the War), and Rahul Mahajan, author of Full Spectrum Dominance: US Power in Iraq and Beyond. These speakers will be available at the 8:30 a.m. press conference (see below).

As the war and occupation drag on, more and more students and educators have begun to question its validity, its continuous drain on public resources and the targeted recruitment of America’s children by the military. This conference is an example of the critical importance of intellectual inquiry in a free society. Throughout the day, educators and students will participate in over forty workshops designed to:

  • Assess the moral implications of the war, empire, and how educators can play a role in the growing movement to fight back
  • Build anti-war activity in our unions, schools, colleges, and universities
  • Explore pedagogy and intellectual and cultural materials to bring the war and the anti-war movement into classrooms from kindergarten to graduate school
  • Resist military recruitment in our schools, colleges, and universities
  • Organize against any move to bring back the draft
