State of the Union: Social Security/Health Care
 

WASHINGTON -- February 2 -- The following analysts will be available Wednesday night and Thursday to comment on policy proposals in the State of the Union address:

DIANA ZUCKERMAN, dz@center4policy.org, www.center4policy.org
Zuckerman is president of the National Research Center for Women & Families. She wrote the article "Social Security and Women."

BILL SPRIGGS, billsprigg@aol.com, www.dollarsandsense.org/1104spriggs.html, www.prospect.org/web/page.ww?section=root&name=
ViewPrint&articleId=9035
Spriggs wrote the recent articles "African Americans and Social Security: Why the Privatization Advocates Are Wrong" (in Dollars and Sense magazine) and "Another Mistaken Racial Stereotype: Contrary to the right's claims, Social Security is a good deal for blacks" (in the American Prospect). He is a senior fellow at the Economic Policy Institute.

Dr. JOHN GEYMAN, jgeyman@u.washington.edu, www.pnhp.org
President of Physicians for a National Health Program, Geyman is author of the books The Corporate Transformation of Health Care: Can the Public Interest Still Be Served? and the just-released Falling Through the Safety Net: Americans Without Health Insurance.

Dr. QUENTIN YOUNG, pnhp@aol.com
National coordinator of Physicians for a National Health Program, Young has chaired the Department of Medicine at Chicago's Cook County Hospital. The co-founders of the group completed a study published today in Health Affairs, the premier health policy journal, which finds that one-half of U.S. bankruptcies are related to health expense and illness. The group argues that medical savings accounts that Bush backs would exacerbate the problem.
