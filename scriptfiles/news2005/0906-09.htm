Gas Price Hikes Hit Hardest In Metro Areas Short on Transit

 
WASHINGTON - September 6 - How hard will record gas prices hit drivers’ wallets?  It depends on where they live, according to a new online calculator developed by researchers at Environmental Working Group (EWG) that draws on the latest federal data.
 
The EWG analysis calculates how much more the average driver in the 50 largest metro areas will spend on gasoline based on different levels of price increase, the average miles driven each year in that city, and the average gas mileage of cars and light trucks.
 
“Communities that have underinvested in mass-transit give commuters few options except to drive longer distances, so they end up spending much more on gas than people whose communities have convenient bus or rail systems,” said Dusty Horwitt, EWG analyst. “By investing in transportation alternatives, communities can help buffer the price increases from Hurricane Katrina and other events, such as instability in the Middle East, that may keep prices high in the months and years ahead.”
