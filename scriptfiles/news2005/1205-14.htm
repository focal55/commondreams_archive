Nationwide Call-In To Congress: “Bring The Troops Home”
U.S. Occupation Fuels Insurgency, Spreads Terrorism
First Nationally Coordinated Effort to End The War in Iraq
 

WASHINGTON - December 5 - Win Without War and United for Peace and Justice, the two largest antiwar coalitions in the country, have organized an unprecedented, nationwide call-in to members of Congress on Tuesday to demand an end to the U.S. occupation of Iraq.

According to today’s TIME Magazine poll, 60% of Americans disapprove of President Bush’s handling of the war in Iraq. Most recent polls show that a majority of Americans believe the President misled the country about the rationale for the war and believe the war was a mistake.

The two coalitions correctly predicted that a pre-emptive strike against Iraq followed by a lengthy U.S. occupation would result in a quagmire and provide a recruiting ground for al Qaeda and other terrorists.

Former Congressman and National Director of Win Without War, Tom Andrews (D-ME) and United For Peace and Justice National Coordinator Leslie Cagan will be available for comment.

Member groups of United for Peace and Justice and Win Without War are participating in the Congressional call-in day. These groups include MoveOn.org, TrueMajority.org, Physicians for Social Responisibility, The United Methodist General Board of Church and Society, AfterDowningStreetMemo.org, Peace Action, WAND, and Progressive Democrats of America, among others.

WHAT: National Call-in to Washington, DC offices of Members of Congress

WHO: Win Without War’s Tom Andrews, former Congressman (D-ME) and National Director
United for Peace and Justice’s Leslie Cagan, National Coordinator
