Hands Off NPR and PBS: Ask CPB Chairman to Stop Playing Politics with Public Broadcasting
 

WASHINGTON -- May 19 --   Common Cause has watched with growing concern as the Corporation for Public Broadcasting (CPB) and its chairman, Kenneth Tomlinson, play

politics with public broadcasting.

 

We expect the CPB and its chairman to defend critical, fact-based reporting and respect the editorial independence of public broadcasting.  But Mr.

Tomlinson's actions demonstrate a lack of respect or even understanding of public broadcasting's mission in a democracy. 

 

The latest reports say Tomlinson is toying with the integrity of National Public Radio (NPR) by ordering the CPB staff to redirect money for NPR away from national newscasts and public affairs programming towards music programs. Tomlinson is also considering a plan to monitor Middle East coverage on NPR programs for evidence of bias, an issue particularly sensitive for the Administration.

 

We are committed to fighting back, and we need your help. Common Cause is collecting petition signatures asking Tomlinson and the CPB board of directors to change course immediately and:

 

  • Stop efforts to influence programming decisions at NPR and Public Broadcasting System (PBS).
  • Eliminate the two ombudsman positions recently created to evaluate and critique public broadcasting programs for bias.  This is an inappropriate role for the CPB and is contrary to its mission of serving as a "heat shield" to protect public broadcasting from political influence.
  • Support the appointment of board members to the CPB who have demonstrated expertise and commitment to public broadcasting as opposed to the current system, which favors the appointment of partisans.
  • Publicly assure journalists working for public broadcasting that they can conduct fact based investigative reporting critical of government without fear of reprisals.
