Assessing Katrina's Impact on Jobs

 
WASHINGTON - The first effects of Katrina are reported in today’s release from the Bureau of Labor Statistics, which shows that payrolls contracted by 35,000 and the unemployment rate rose to 5.1 percent. EPI’s Jobs Picture, by senior economist Jared Bernstein of the Economic Policy Institute, calculates that about 229,000 jobs were lost due to Katrina. Other possible losses attributable to the storm include a drop in the share of African-Americans in the labor force, which fell by 0.4 percentage points, and job losses in tourist-related industries like restaurants (-54,000) and recreation facilities, including casinos (-19,000). Solid job growth in the rest of the country partially offset the job losses from Katrina. Hurricane Rita struck after the period considered for the BLS September report.
