State Publications on Budget Impact Now Available
 

WASHINGTON -- February 10 -- The National Priorities Project, a non-partisan research group, has just released its state-by-state publications, showing the impact of the President's budget on each state along with a general budget overview. The publications can be found at: www.nationalpriorities.org/budgetpublication.

"The President's Budget: Impact on the States" provides state numbers on the President's proposed cuts to community and economic development; low-income home energy assistance; and the Clean Water State Revolving Fund. It also shows the amount of under-funding in each state for the No Child Left Behind Act. It juxtaposes these cuts and under-funding with the cost to each state's taxpayers of the proposed increase in military spending and what each state's residents pay for the Iraq War thus far.

The publication provides a breakdown of proposed discretionary spending for fiscal year 2006, showing the rising dominance of military spending over shrinking domestic discretionary spending. It presents an overview of the national impact the budget will have in every area and among every population in the country.

"There's something wrong with our national priorities when Pentagon spending threatens the health of our communities," said Greg Speeter, executive director, National Priorities Project.
