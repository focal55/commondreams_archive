Malteser International Prepares Medium-Term and Long-Term Assistance for Katrina
 
COLOGNE - September 7 - Ingo Radtke, Secretary General of Malteser International arrived in the United States yesterday:

" In Houston, which is about eight hours car drive from New Orleans, are currently 25,000 people accommodated in the Astrodome. An emergency reception camp for several thousand people has been erected, another one is in preparation.”

"It is assumed that the rehabilitation and reconstruction phase will last several months, states Radtke, Furthermore. “More than 25 percent of the people in the affected areas lived below the poverty line already before the disaster. They lost all their belongings in the water. Despite the starting aid programs and perceptible solidarity, some people are in danger not to receive any social service at all. We want to help especially those people at risk”, emphasizes Radtke.

Programs, which are designated to assist the most vulnerable and socially disadvantaged to regain a basis of existence, are developed in close cooperation with the United States Malteser Associations and experts of the Catholic Church in the diocese of New Orleans. Associations and experts of the Catholic Church in the diocese of New Orleans.

Today, two more Malteser International disaster relief experts will arrive in the United States. “We are prepared to stay for several weeks – we will stay as long as our assistance is needed,“ says Radtke.
