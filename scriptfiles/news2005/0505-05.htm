Progressives to Hold Major Political Conference; Largest Progressive Gathering Since Election Aims to 'Take Back America'
 

WASHINGTON -- May 5 -- News Advisory: Campaign for America's Future co-directors Robert Borosage and Roger Hickey today announced that the organization's 2005 "Take Back America" conference will bring together the largest group of progressive leaders since last year's elections to discuss the ideas and infrastructure needed to forge a political majority. The conference will feature major presentations and training workshops for grassroots activists at the Washington Hilton from Wednesday, June 1 to Friday, June 3.

Participants include Democratic National Committee chair Howard Dean, former presidential candidate Sen. John Edwards, Los Angeles mayoral front-runner Antonio Villaraigosa, Rev. Jim Wallis, Democratic pollsters Stanley Greenberg and Celinda Lake, EMILY's List president Ellen R. Malcolm, NOW president Kim Gandy, strategist Donna Brazile and journalist Bill Moyers.

The Campaign for America's Future is fighting for an Apollo- like project for jobs and energy independence. The group has also brought public attention to House Majority Leader Tom DeLay's abuse of power and is fighting President Bush's plan to privatize Social Security.
