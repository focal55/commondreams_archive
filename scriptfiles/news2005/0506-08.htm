Gay Democrats Disagree with Sen. Kerry on Platform Position
 

WASHINGTON -- May 6 -- Today, the National Stonewall Democrats (NSD) strongly disagreed with the position of Senator John Kerry (D-Mass.) regarding a proposed marriage equality plank in the platform of the Massachusetts Democratic Party. Yesterday, speaking to reporters after a healthcare event in Baton Rouge, Kerry told reporters that he opposed a plank supporting marriage equality for same-sex couples in his home state.

"The majority of people in Massachusetts have embraced reality with acceptance," said Eric Stern, NSD executive director. "We respectfully and strongly disagree with Senator Kerry and urge him to follow the example of the state's senior Senator by supporting the enforcement of state law. Senator Kerry has been a champion for gay and lesbian families in the United States Senate for decades. That is why we are so deeply disappointed by his remarks."

Next week, the Massachusetts Democratic Party is expected to approve a party platform plank supporting civil marriage for same-sex couples. The proposed platform is the result of more than two-dozen meetings of the party's platform committee conducted across the state. Massachusetts would join the Democratic parties of Colorado and Iowa in adopting such a plank. The Massachusetts Democratic Party has already voiced support for marriage equality through a resolution adopted more than a year ago. Since then, the Democratic Parties of California, New York State, Washington State and numerous county parties have issued similar resolutions.

"I'm opposed to it being in a platform. I think it's a mistake," Kerry told reporters. "I think it's the wrong thing, and I'm not sure it reflects the broad view of the Democratic Party in our state. I'm opposed to gay marriage. I support partnerships and civil unions."

Following Senator Kerry's remarks, a spokesperson for Senator Edward Kennedy (D-Mass.) told reporters that the Senator supported the platform plank along with current state law. "Senator Kennedy has demonstrated leadership by supporting marriage equality in principle and standing with the majority of Massachusetts residents," said Stern.

Senator Kerry's remarks were also rebuked by the Philip Johnston, Chair of the Massachusetts Democratic Party. "I have great affection and respect for John, but I disagree on this issue," Johnston told the Boston Globe. "It is important that the state Democratic Party support civil rights. We need to take a stand. Most residents of the state view this as a fundamental civil right for gay and lesbian citizens."

"We not only disagree with Senator Kerry in principle, but his statement is inaccurate in that the proposed platform plank does reflect the broad view of Democrats in Massachusetts," said Stern.

A Boston Globe poll conducted in March found that nearly 60 percent of people in Massachusetts now support marriage equality for same-sex couples. The poll found that 71 percent of Democrats supported marriage equality, along with 53 percent of independents and 35 percent of the state's Republican minority.
