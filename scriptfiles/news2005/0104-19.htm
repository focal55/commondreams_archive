Telephone News Conference Wednesday, 10:30 am EST: New TV, Print Ads Demand Gonzales Repudiate Torture
 

WASHINGTON -- January 5 -- News Advisory:

Tens of Thousands of Americans Will Urge Gonzalez and U.S. Senators To Sign "Declaration Against Torture"

Nation's Largest Antiwar and Human Rights Groups to Unveil Grassroots "Campaign To Stop Torture"

On the eve of the U.S. Senate confirmation hearing for Attorney General nominee Alberto Gonzales, leaders of a coalition of antiwar and human rights groups, led by Win Without War, and including MoveOn.org, Amnesty International, True Majority, and FaithfulAmerica.org, will hold a telephone news conference to unveil a multi-pronged, national campaign to demand that Gonzales and the Senate publicly repudiate the use of torture.

Gonzales, who has come under criticism for the legal memos that authorized torture tactics at Abu Ghraib Prison in Iraq and at Guantanamo Bay, Cuba will be the focus of a new New York Times ad by to run on Thursday.

The national campaign will be spearheaded by a "Declaration Against Torture" petition and will culminate with nationwide calls to every U.S. Senator.

A 30-second television ad by MoveOn.org will also highlight Gonzales's role as the legal mastermind of torture tactics. The ad begins running Thursday in New York and Washington, D.C. on CNN.
