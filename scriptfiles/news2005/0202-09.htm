MoveOn Members Turn Out to Challenge President on His Tour to Sell Social Security Benefit Cuts
 

WASHINGTON -- February 2 -- News Advisory: Large TV Buy in Montana Calls Bush Plan 'Working Retirement' for Seniors

View Ad at: http://cdn.moveon.org/content/video/SS_ad.wmv

As President Bush tours the country promoting his plan to privatize Social Security, members of MoveOn.org, a nation-wide three-million-member grassroots organization, will challenge him at each stop over his plan to cut to Social Security benefits.

The MoveOn.org effort is part of a national campaign coordinated by the Campaign for America's Future, including groups such as: U.S. Action, ACORN, AFL-CIO, Alliance for Retired Americans, National Organization for Women, Coalition of Citizens with Disabilities, NAACP, League of United Latin American Citizens, and others.

Starting today, MoveOn.org will air a television ad called "Working Retirement" in Great Falls, Montana, where the President is expected to stump on Thursday. The ad also airs this week in the districts of three Members of Congress -- Allen Boyd (D-Fla.), Chris Chocola (R-Ind.), and Jim Gerlach (R-Pa.) -- who may support the President's plan to privatize Social Security. MoveOn.org is running a full-page advertisement in today's New York Times.

"We can't countenance Bush's plan to cut Social Security benefits by up to 46 percent when small adjustments in the system would put it in the black indefinitely," said Eli Pariser, MoveOn.org Executive Director. "We will make sure that Members of Congress who consider supporting a plan that reduces benefits for future retirees know that their constituents will hear from us -- and they will hear from their constituents."

To view the ad, visit: http://cdn.moveon.org/content/video/SS_ad.wmv. For a script or a copy of the TV or print ads, contact Jessica Smith at: Jessica@fenton.com or 202-822-5200.
