Rwandan Archbishop Summoned by Genocide Court
 
KIGALA, RWANDA - July 20 - The Roman Catholic Archbishop of Kigali Thaddée Ntihinyurwa will on Thursday appear before a community court investigating genocide crimes.

The court will decide whether accusations brought against the archbishop are reasonable ground for a genocide trial.

Ntihinyurwa, 63, is accused, among other allegations, of attending meetings planning the genocide in the south west province of Cyangugu. He was the bishop of Cyangugu at the time.

Ntihinyurwa, a Hutu, has previously denied the allegations in the media saying he had instead tried to save ethnic Tutsis.

He will appear before a Gacaca court in the Nyamasheke district. He could not be reached for comment.

Gacaca courts were set up three years ago to speed up genocide trials. Most of the courts are still in a pre-trial phase of investigation.

Ntihinyurwa will be the most senior church leader to appear before the courts as a potential suspect.
