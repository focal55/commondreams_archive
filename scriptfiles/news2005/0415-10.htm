Stopping the Spread of Nuclear Arms Is Topic of American Friends Service Committee Tour
 

WASHINGTON -- April 15 -- Judge Christopher Weeramantry, former vice president of the International Court of Justice in The Hague (World Court), former Justice Supreme Court of Sri Lanka will speak at public meetings in Connecticut (April 16); Boston, Cambridge and Medford, Massachusetts (April 18 -23); New York City (April 26 – May 2), and Washington, D.C (April 13 - 15).

The need for modern law to come to terms with the social realities of the world today has never been more urgent. Socially-oriented systems that take into consideration not only the interests of the larger society but also of the generations to come are gradually coming into focus. Much of this could be attributed to the work of one man, Judge Christopher Weeramantry, a judge and former vice president of the International Court of Justice in The Hague, also known as the World Court.

Judge Weeramantry has covered an extraordinary range of issues during his remarkable career. Many of his judgments introduced a new dimension to international law, such as the World Court's advisory opinion on the Illegality of Nuclear Arms, where he declared that the use of nuclear weapons is illegal under any circumstances whatsoever.

Weeramantry is also widely respected for having introduced a multi-cultural approach based on the wisdom of the ancients and of various Third World nations.
