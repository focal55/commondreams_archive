Pro-Choice Americans to Secretary Rice: You Can't Have it Both Ways on International Family Planning
 

WASHINGTON -- March 14 -- NARAL Pro-Choice America, the nation’s leading advocate for personal privacy and a woman’s right to choose, today called on Secretary of State Condoleezza Rice to square Administration policy with her self-described "mildly pro-choice" viewpoint by lifting the global "gag rule" on family-planning programs and releasing the $34 million Bush has blocked from the United Nations Family-Planning programs each of the last three years. Together, these two actions could prevent unintended pregnancies and reduce the need for abortion around the world.

In weekend interviews, Rice declared that, "we have to try and bring people to have respect for it and make this as rare a circumstance as possible," while still endorsing Bush's indefensible policies.

NARAL Pro-Choice America President Nancy Keenan called Rice’s position on international family planning a contradiction: "I don't see how you can claim to be for reducing abortion while enforcing policies that make abortion more necessary. As long as these policies stay in place the label `mildly pro-choice' will be as big a deception as 'compassionate conservative.' The Bush Administration’s actions defy common sense—and Secretary Rice should use her leadership to change them."
