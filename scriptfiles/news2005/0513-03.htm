Common Cause in St. Louis at National Media Reform Conference
 

WASHINGTON -- May 13 -- Common Cause President Chellie Pingree and a half dozen Common Cause national staff members are off to St. Louis this weekend to participate in the 2005 National Conference for Media Reform.

 More than 2,000 people -- media reformers, activists and celebrities -- are expected May 13-15 in the city to learn about the media, strategize ways to improve media, network and organize. Scheduled speakers include Al Franken, musician Patti Smith, Linda Foley of The Newspaper Guild, Seattle Times Publisher Frank Blethen, and New York Daily News Columnist Juan Gonzales.

 For more than two years, Common Cause has been fighting to oppose media consolidation, and working for other media reforms, such as an independent public broadcasting and public interest obligations for broadcasters. We believe our democracy suffers from a lack of diverse voices and sources of news and information.

 While in St. Louis, Chellie Pingree will moderate a panel discussion on public broadcasting, with a look at its goals, how it's structured, funded and governed. The panel will address formulating local activism to work for quality public broadcasting. Celia Wexler, Common Cause's vice president for advocacy, will also moderate a panel on holding the media accountable through policy and activism.  Check out Common Blog for updates from our staffers in St. Louis.

 The conference is run by Free Press, a national nonpartisan media reform group. The event is sold out, but streaming audio and video will be available online at www.freepress.net/conference, and Saturday's keynote session will be broadcast on Pacifica radio affiliates at 8 p.m. CDT.
