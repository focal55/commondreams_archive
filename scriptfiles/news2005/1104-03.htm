Civil Liberties at Stake in Alito Nomination Fight
 
WASHINGTON - November 4 - President Bush has nominated 3rd Circuit

Court Judge Samuel Alito to replace Sandra Day O'Connor on the U.S. Supreme Court. Advocates have raised concerns about Judge Alito's record on the spectrum of civil rights issues, and a number of groups have already announced their opposition to his nomination. Alito's record is said to be in line with conservative Justice Scalia, so much so that his nickname is "Scalito."

To help activists stay informed and connected to the progressive community in this fight, Moving Ideas just released "Progressives' Guide to the Samuel Alito Nomination" , our one-stop guide to information on Alito and ways activists can get involved. This guide is constantly updated with the latest actions and reports.

"Our civil rights and liberties are at stake in this fight," says Moving Ideas Director Melanie Alston-Akers. "The public must get involved in urging the Senate to thoroughly vet this nominee, and to ensure that he will protect our rights."

This activist guide includes the latest reports on Alito from the progressive community, actions to contact Senators, and ways to get communities involved.
