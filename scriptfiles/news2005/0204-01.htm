Congressional, Business and Community Leaders Join Forces to Highlight Failures of the Bush Budget
Wealthy Americans pledge to refuse President's tax breaks
 

On Feb. 7, at 9:30 a.m. at the National Press Club, congressional, business and community leaders will highlight how the new Bush budget, released that day, will place the American dream further out of reach of most Americans and create a polarized society of haves and have-nots. A plan of action to address the growing wealth divide will also be announced.

REP. PETE STARK, senior Democrat on the Ways and Means Health Subcommittee, who will speak at the press conference, said, "President Bush's budget is waging an all out attack on sensible programs that ensure retirement security and make health care affordable for America's working families. Republicans say they want to privatize Social Security to create an 'ownership society,' when what they're really telling working Americans is, you're on your own."

THE HONORABLE WALTER FAUNTROY, former Congressman, founding member of the Congressional Black Caucus, and Southern Christian Leadership Conference staffer under Martin Luther King, will also speak. He commented, "Many of us struggled mightily to open the doors of economic opportunity to all Americans, regardless of the color of their skin. A budget that ensures that the rich get richer and the poor poorer will set our nation back."

Responsible Wealth, a national network of over 800 business people, investors and affluent Americans who are concerned about the deepening wealth divide in America and who advocate widespread prosperity, will kick off a protest against millionaires' tax cuts. Elizabeth A. Letzler, a wealthy former banker and business Ph.D., will launch the campaign along with her 15-year-old daughter Stephanie, by presenting the Responsible Tax Pledge and signing it. The pledge calls on affluent Americans to refuse the President's tax breaks that primarily benefit the 275,000 millionaire taxpayers in the U.S., and on Congress to reject more tax cuts for wealthy Americans.
