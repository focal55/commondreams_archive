Presidential Election 2008: Political and Reform Implications of a Shrinking Battleground
Report Shows How Fewest States in Modern History Will Matter;
Impact on Turnout Could Last for Generations
 

WASHINGTON - July 27 - 1980 Presidential Campaign Veterans Hendrik Hertzberg and John B. Anderson to Lead FairVote’s Launch of National Campaign for Direct Election of the President


WHAT:

A press conference to launch FairVote’s campaign for direct election of the president and to release an incisive new report that provides powerful evidence that the 2008 presidential election will have the fewest battleground states in modern history – and how this shrinking of presidential battlegrounds will have implications on voter turnout that could last generations. The report details which states will be battlegrounds in a close election – and the growing number of states that won’t.

Speakers include FairVote’s executive director Rob Richie, 1980 independent presidential candidate John B. Anderson and Hendrik Hertzberg, New Yorker editor and chief White House speechwriter for Jimmy Carter in 1980. They will explain FairVote’s initial reform strategies to revitalize the campaign for direct election that nearly succeeded in the late 1960’s.
