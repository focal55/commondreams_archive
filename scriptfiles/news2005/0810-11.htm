Kucinich: Highway Bill Signed Into Law Today Will Create Jobs In Ohio
Kucinich Secures $38 Million In Bill For Area’s Transportation Needs
 
WASHINGTON - August 10 - Today, with the President’s signature on the Highway Bill, Northeast Ohio will receive over $38 million in federal funds secured by Congressman Dennis J. Kucinich for the area’s transportation needs.

The Highway Bill, officially known as the Transportation Equity Act: A Legacy For Users (TEA LU), recently passed the House and Senate, and was signed into law today by President Bush. The funds secured by Kucinich will improve congestion problems, reduce train noise and congestion from train crossings, provide safe pathways for bicyclists across the region and create new high paying jobs.

“This is a jobs bill,” stated Kucinich. “Northeast Ohio desperately needs these funds. I am proud to have worked for over two years with my colleagues to secure this substantial federal investment in our community. Now, it is time to get to work improving our roads, building bike paths, and improving the quality of life of those living near train tracks. Today is a good day. Today, we are creating new high paying jobs in Ohio.”

Kucinich, working with Congressman LaTourette, Congresswoman Tubbs Jones and Ohio’s two Senators, assisted in securing funds for the following projects:

Cleveland Towpath Trail, Cleveland: $6,440,000
Upgrade Circuitry at Sheldon Road Grade Separation, Berea: $112,000
Construct Grade Separation at Front Street, Berea: $4,400,000
Construct Grade Separation at Eastland Road, Berea: $600,000
Upgrade the I-480 and Tiedman Road Interchange, Brooklyn: $1,600,000
Construct Bike and Walking Path, Fairview Park: $280,000
Reconfigure I-480 and Transportation Blvd. Interchange, Garfield Heights: $800,000
Intermodal Bikeway, Independence: $2,000,000
Widening Pleasant Valley/Bagley Road, Parma and Middleburg Heights:$1,200,000
Construct Crocker/Stearns Connection, North Olmsted and Westlake:$1,380,000
Construct Grade Separation at Stearns Road, Olmsted Township:$3,600,000
Construct Grade Separation at Columbia Road, Olmsted Falls:$448,000

Construction of an Intermodal Facility at University Circle, Cleveland:$7,056,000
