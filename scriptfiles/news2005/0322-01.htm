Veterans to Call on Congress for Freedom from Dangerous Oil Addiction
Red, White, Blue and Green Coalition Necessary to Keep America Safe, Vets Say
 

WASHINGTON -- March 22 --

WHO: Charles Sheehan-Miles, Executive Director, Veterans for Common Sense

Jamie Crown, Philadelphia Field Organizer, PennEnvironment

WHAT: As the Senate prepares to consider a Bush administration energy bill that would increase America’s reliance on foreign oil by 85 percent, veterans from Pennsylvania will join with environmental advocates and security experts to call on Senator Specter to champion a secure energy future by reducing America's reliance on oil.

"Energy is a critical factor in our nation's security: that's why it's time we pulled together all Americans to call for a secure and energy-independent America", said Charles Sheehan-Miles, Executive Director for Veterans for Common Sense, explaining his decision to stand with environmental advocates to call on Senator Specter to secure freedom from a dangerous reliance on foreign oil.

WHEN: Thursday, March 24, 2005 11am

WHERE: 6th and Arch, outside the Federal Building offices on 6th Street
