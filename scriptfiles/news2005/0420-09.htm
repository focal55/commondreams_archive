Statement of Democracy 21 President Fred Wertheimer on Pence-Wynn Charade 'Reform' Legislation
 

WASHINGTON -- April 20 -- The following is a statement of Democracy 21 President Fred Wertheimer on Pence-Wynn Charade 'Reform' Legislation:

The House Administration Committee is holding hearings today on campaign finance proposals.

Legislation introduced by Reps. Christopher Shays (R-Conn.) and Marty Meehan (D-Mass.), entitled the "527 Reform Act of 2005," would close the huge soft money loophole for 527 groups that resulted in hundreds of millions of dollars in unregulated, unlimited contributions being spent to influence the 2004 federal elections.

Legislation introduced by Reps. Mike Pence (R-Ind.) and Albert Wynn (D-Md.), entitled the "527 Fairness Act of 2005," is a charade and a farce.

The Pence-Wynn bill contains no provisions dealing with 527 groups.

Instead, the legislation repeals the aggregate limit on the total contributions an individual can make to political parties and to federal candidates in a two-year election cycle and would take us back to a corrupt system of financing federal elections.

The aggregate contribution limits that the bill would repeal were enacted in 1974, in response to the Watergate scandals.

Under current law, for example, an individual can give a total of $61,400 to all political party committees in a two-year election cycle. Under the Pence-Wynn bill, an individual would be able to give a total of more than $1 million to the committees of a single political party in a two-year election cycle. And, a President, Senator or Representative, or a federal candidate would be able to solicit this amount -- more than a million dollars -- for their party from a single donor. Currently federal candidates and officeholders can solicit an individual donor for no more than $61,400 in contributions to a party in a two-year cycle.

Under current law, an individual can contribute a total of $40,000 to all federal candidates in a two-year cycle.

Under the Pence-Wynn bill, an individual could contribute a total of $2 million or more to all federal candidates in a two- year cycle. The limit per federal candidate is $4,200 for a primary and general election.

And, a President, Senator or Representative, or a federal candidate, would be able to solicit this amount -- $2 million or more -- from a single donor. Currently federal candidates and officeholders can solicit an individual donor for no more than $40,000 in contributions to federal candidates in a two-year cycle.

The Pence-Wynn bill represents an extraordinary step backwards to a corrupt system where federal officeholders could once again solicit huge sums of money for federal elections from wealthy individuals and Washington lobbyists seeking to influence government decisions.

The naming of the Pence-Wynn bill as the "527 Fairness Act of 2005" mocks all citizens who care about protecting the integrity of our political system and preventing the use of huge sums of money to corrupt our democracy.
