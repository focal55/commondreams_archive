Published on Wednesday, September 14, 2005 by The Daily Telegraph / Australia
Activist 'Baffled' at Deportation
by Mark Phillips
 
US peace activist Scott Parkin was strongly opposed to political violence and is "baffled" at the reasons for his deportation from Australia, he said today.

Mr Parkin, who has had his visa cancelled on the grounds he poses a national security risk, is expected to be flown out of Melbourne for Los Angeles tomorrow morning.

He will not oppose his removal from Australia, but he has vowed to continue to fight the cancellation of his visa to visit Australia.

The 36-year-old teacher from Houston, Texas, was detained by the Australian Federal Police in Melbourne on Saturday after an "adverse security assessment".

Mr Parkin, who had been in Australia since early June on a six-month visa, has been held in solitary confinement in a Melbourne custody centre since then.

The Federal Government refused to explain why his six-month tourist visa had been cancelled, other than to say it was based on matters relating to "politically motivated violence".

Mr Parkin has participated in rallies against the Forbes chief executives' conference in Sydney, a protest outside the Sydney headquarters of US energy multinational Halliburton and conducted workshops on non-violent protest and trends in grassroots activism.

Justice Minister Chris Ellison said yesterday the visa had been revoked because of Mr Parkin's behaviour while in Australia.

The Opposition has decided not to oppose the deportation after receiving a briefing on the case.

In a short written statement issued tonight through Greenpeace Australia, the Texan disowned political violence.

"I am strongly opposed to any violence and do not believe that violence delivers any political gain, and in fact detracts from positive political engagement," he said.

"I find this entire experience incomprehensible and am still baffled as to why my visa has been cancelled and I have been detained under these circumstances.

"To this date the only information that I have received is that I have been assessed as `a direct or indirect risk to Australian national security'."

Mr Parkin, a member of the Houston Global Awareness Collective which aims to end the US-led occupation of Iraq, said he was overwhelmed by the support he had received during his detention.

He was visited at the custody centre today by three members of his legal team, including prominent human rights lawyer Julian Burnside, QC.

Greenpeace political adviser Helen Oakey, who was also allowed to visit him, said he was in high spirits but keen to go home.

"Greenpeace will continue to support Scott's style of peaceful protest and to fund legal challenges to overturn this gross decision," she said.

Mr Parkin is expected to be flown out of Melbourne under escort on a Qantas flight direct to Los Angeles mid-morning tomorrow. He faces a bill of close to $20,000 for his detention and deportation in Australia.

A Greenpeace spokesman said supporters would attempt to embarrass Mr Howard in New York, where he is due to address the United Nations.
