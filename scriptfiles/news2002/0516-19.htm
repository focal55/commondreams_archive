Brady Campaign: 'American Taliban' John Walker Lindh Seeks to Have Firearms Charges Dropped, Using Ashcroft's Second Amendment Reversal
   WASHINGTON - May 16 - Michael D. Barnes, President of the Brady Center to Prevent Gun Violence, today issued the following statement regarding reports that lawyers for John Walker Lindh, the California man accused of fighting with the Taliban, are seeking to dismiss a firearms charge against him based on the Bush Administration's reversal of the United State's long-standing position on the Second Amendment:

"It was only a matter of time before the Bush Administration's new position on the Second Amendment would be used in an attempt to prevent terrorists and criminals from being prosecuted on firearms charges, but this is incredible," said Barnes. "It is extremely disturbing that one of the first cases to use Ashcroft's new position would be an individual who was fighting for the fanatics who took so many innocent lives on September 11, 2001. What an ironic development in the war on terrorism."

"This is just the tip of the iceberg. The National Rifle Association (NRA) has already stated that it would use a changed U.S. position to challenge the constitutionality of common-sense gun laws. Look at how hard the NRA has tried to defend the ability of Timothy Joe Emerson to own guns, a man who had threatened his wife and child with a pistol, owned an arsenal of weapons, and had a domestic violence restraining order against him.

"Sadly, thanks to Mr. Ashcroft and the Bush Administration, we can expect to see more challenges to life-saving gun laws and more attempts by criminals to overturn firearms convictions."
