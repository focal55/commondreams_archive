'Real' Judicial Crisis Is Ideological
Bush's Controversial Nominees Threaten Constitutional Rights
   WASHINGTON - May 9 - NARAL released a report today detailing what's at stake in the current fight over the future of the federal judiciary. NARAL President Kate Michelman also called the judicial crisis the President recently decried "ideological," not vacancy-related, and said it is largely due to the controversial nature of the President's nominees.

"The Administration has concocted a phony crisis over the pace of judicial confirmations as a smokescreen for their attempt to ram far right, anti-choice nominees through the Senate. It's not about the numbers; it's about their ideology.

"The Constitution does not provide a rubber-stamped express ticket to the federal bench. The President's nominees need to rise or fall based on their qualifications and fitness. Our report shows clearly that the President is nominating judges based on their extreme conservative activist views -- in fulfilling their advice and consent role, the Senate must take those views into consideration.

"Despite the President's bluster and demagoguery, the Senate should not be railroaded into confirming judges who at the first opportunity will roll back constitutional rights."
