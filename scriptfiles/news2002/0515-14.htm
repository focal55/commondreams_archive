May 17 Press Conference:
CSPI To Release Findings On Pizza
CSPI Study To Reveal What One Of America's Favorite Foods Really Delivers
   WASHINGTON - May 15 - News Advisory:

The non-profit Center for Science in the Public Interest (CSPI) will release the findings of its study of pizza at a Washington, D.C. press conference on Friday, May 17 at 12 noon. CSPI, which spotlighted movie theater popcorn, Chinese food, and other restaurant meals in previous years, publishes its pizza findings in the June issue of its Nutrition Action Healthletter, and in a new book, Restaurant Confidential (Workman, 2002). This advisory is only for media planning purposes. Substantive information within is embargoed until 12 noon Eastern on Friday, May 17.

WHAT:
CSPI's latest study of restaurant food examines pizza. CSPI looks at calorie, fat, saturated fat, and sodium content of one of America's favorite foods, and makes recommendations for consumers to make more healthful selections. CSPI to serve one of the few pizzas given its "Best Bite" rating.
