House Appropriations Committe Completes Mark-Up of FY02 Supplemental Appropriations Bill
  WASHINGTON - May 16 - The House Appropriations Committee completed its mark-up of the fiscal 2002 Supplemental Appropriations Bill. The bill was adopted by voice vote.

The bill is expected to be considered by the full House the week of May 20.

Highlights of bill:

== Total in bill - $29.8 billion ($1.4 billion above the President's request according to the committee's press release)

-The committee's total is actually $2.4 billion above the request, but the release claims the increase was "adjusted for Pell Grants" ($1 billion was added for Pell Grants).

=====================

Foreign Policy Amendments

=====================

-Withdrawn: Sabo (D-MN) amendment to bar cancellation of the Crusader Mobile Howitzer until the Army completed an Analysis of Alternatives study. A similar Sabo amendment was adopted by voice vote as part of the report language.

-Adopted 32-30: Tiarht (R-KS) amendment to remove a provision allowing the release of funds for UN family planning programs if an investigation certifies China is cooperating in stopping forced abortions.

-Adopted 38-18: Delay (R-TX) amendment barring cooperation with the International Criminal Court and authorizing use of force to free any U.S. troops held by the court.

=======================================

Defense: $15.77 billion ($1.77 billion above request)

=======================================

-$7.2 billion for overseas operations

-$4.3 billion for Guard and Reserve personnel

-$500 million for weapons procurement: "smart bombs", unmanned aerial vehicles and special forces equipment

-$1.6 billion for classified programs

-$420 million for foreign aid for "coalition support"

-$790 million added for personnel costs

-$621 million added for training, spare parts and equipment maintenance.

-$100 million added to destroy U.S. chemical weapons stockpiles.

-$93 million added to replace three MH-47 helicopters

================================================

Homeland Security: $5.8 billion ($522 million above the request)

================================================

== $4 billion for Transportation Security Administration ($400 million below the request)

-$850 million for airport renovations to allow installation of bomb detecting equipment

-$630 million for bomb detecting and baggage screening equipment

-$100 million to reimburse airlines for cockpit door barriers

-$75 million for port security

== $210 for Coast Guard ($33 million above the request)

-$8 million added to provide "a smoother transition for reservists"

-$12 million added to study port vulnerability

-$12 million added for patrol boats

== $378 million for Corps of Engineers and Department of Energy ($352 million above the request)

-$128 million added for security enhancements at Corps of Engineers facilities.

-$29 million added for security at Dept. of Energy labs

-$18 million added to improve security regarding nuclear weapons and nuclear materials transportation

-$88 million added for security at the nuclear weapons facilities.

-$83 million added for security at Dept. of Energy environmental cleanup sites

==$194 million for Department of Justice ($143 million above the request)

-$112 million for FBI

-$75 million for the Immigration and Naturalization Service

==$63 million for Department of Treasury ($63 million above the request)

-$16 million for the Federal Law Enforcement Training Center

-$38.9 million for the Secret Service

===========================================

Foreign Aid: $1.35 billion ($225 million above the request)

===========================================

-$200 million added for international AIDS programs

-$200 million added in aid to Israel

-$370 million in aid to Afghanistan ($120 million above the request)

-$110 million in aid for former Soviet republics

-Retains the provision allowing the President to allow Colombian counter-narcotics aid to be used against terrorists

-Removes the provision authorizing the Department of Defense to support foreign nations and indigenous forces by providing defense services and training. The authority is shifted to the State Department.

-$373 million for Foreign Military Financing

======================================

Aid to New York: $5.5 billion (matches the request)

======================================

-$2.8 billion for FEMA disaster relief efforts

-$2.0 billion to rebuild mass transit and roads

-$750 million for Dept. of Housing & Urban Development Community Block Grants
