NAACP President to Speak Out on Welfare at Capitol Hill Rally; Surprise Action, Race To The Bottom Part of Busy Day For Activists
   WASHINGTON - May 20 - News Advisory:

Several events tomorrow will highlight the need to make poverty reduction the central focus of welfare reform reauthorization in the Senate. The day starts with a rally and press conference followed by a surprise action at a high-profile location and closes with the "Race To The Bottom" with Mobilization for Global Justice.

WHO:
-- Kweisi Mfume, president, NAACP
-- Deepak Bhargava, director, NCJIS
-- Low-Income Welfare Activists

WHAT:
Rally and Press Conference

WHERE:
United States Capitol
The Fountain in Upper Senate Park
Constitution Avenue, between New Jersey and Delaware Avenues

WHEN:
Tuesday, May 21
1 p.m.

Also tomorrow:

1 K Race To The Bottom:
The National Campaign will join the Mobilization for Global Justice in hosting a 1K "Race to the Bottom" to draw attention to the links between poverty in the U.S. and abroad. A rally and speakout will begin at 4:30 p.m. and the race will start at 6 p.m. at WEF Meeting at U.S. Chamber of Commerce (northwest corner of Lafayette Park, 16th and H St, NW) and finish at World Bank/IMF Headquarters (Murrow Park, 18th and H St, NW)

Surprise Action
Low-income activists will be involved in an action at a high profile Washington, D.C., location. Details available early afternoon.
