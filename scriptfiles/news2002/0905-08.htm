New Yorkers Say No To War Mourning
New Yorkers commemorate 9.11.01 tragedy with mourning, reflection, hope for peace
   NEW YORK - September 5 - After September 11, 2001, New York’s Union Square became a center of hope, of grief, of protest and of mourning. On September 11, 2002, New Yorkers Say No To War will return to Union Square with these same emotions, to honor those who died in last year’s terrorist attacks and in all subsequent U.S. military actions.

At 8:46 AM—exactly one year after the first jet struck the World Trade Center—mourners will lay silently on the ground in Union Square Park wearing tee-shirts proclaiming their message: Say No To War. Participants will rise and disperse at 10:29 AM, the anniversary of the fall of the second tower.

“With our Mourning, we are creating a space for New Yorkers to come together in quiet reflection to mourn our losses over the past year and to contemplate our political obligations going forward,” said Jennifer L. Pozner, a member of the anti-war group’s planning committee. “New Yorkers Say No To War mourns the violent loss of our friends, our families and our collective sense of safety in New York City and across our country. We protest retaliatory U.S. military actions that have killed hundreds of innocent Afghan civilians in our names. We protest the brutality and arrogance of the Bush administration's ‘war without end,’ and the massive loss of life that could occur in Iraq and elsewhere throughout the world. We protest the racist and anti-democratic profiling of people of color and the increasing suspension of American civil liberties in the name of patriotism,” Pozner said.

New Yorkers Say No to War, which formed immediately after September 11, 2001, is a diverse group of individuals meeting to explore alternatives to war in the face of grief, fear and anger following the events of September 11. NYSNTW meetings feature presentations by experts and artists on topics both local and global, and provide opportunities to share resources, insights, creative energies and hope through art and political action. NYSNTW seeks urgent, non-violent and constructive solutions that promote peace, equality and justice around the world.
