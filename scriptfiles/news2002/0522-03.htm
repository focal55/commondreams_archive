Gun Violence Prevention Advocate Wins Pennsylvania Primary
Gun Safety A Prominent Issue in the Race
  

WASHINGTON - May 22 - Today, the Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence united with the Million Mom March (MMM) hailed the Pennsylvania primary victory of Ed Rendell, Democratic candidate for Governor and a long-time advocate of sensible gun laws. The Brady Campaign Voter Education Fund and the MMM endorsed Rendell, and were a key presence in the primary. Gun violence prevention issues figured prominently in Rendell's race against Bob Casey.

"I congratulate Mayor Rendell on his victory in this highly competitive primary," said Michael D. Barnes, president of the Brady Campaign. "As we work to create an America free from gun violence, Ed Rendell is a leader who is capable of making this dream a reality for the residents of Pennsylvania. We look forward to working with him to keep guns out of the hands of criminals and children.

"This race proves yet again that Americans want common-sense gun laws and they will support elected officials who pursue them. Ed Rendell is to be commended for having the 'courage of his convictions,' even in the face of an aggressive negative advertising campaign launched by the National Rifle Association. Ed never wavered in his support for the sensible gun laws that would help to make Pennsylvania's neighborhoods safe from gun violence."

The Brady Campaign, the Million Mom March and other gun violence prevention groups endorsed Ed Rendell early in his campaign and were active throughout Tuesday's primary election. Sarah Brady campaigned for Mayor Rendell, including recording a voice message on Rendell's strong record on gun violence prevention that reached more than 10,000 Pennsylvania homes. Ed Rendell's victory comes in spite of the National Rifle Association's well-funded attempts to defeat him through an attack-advertising blitz and a significant "get-out-the-vote" campaign.

As the Mayor of Philadelphia, Rendell aggressively sought passage of a one-gun-a-month bill for the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania, recognizing that such laws thwart gun trafficking and drastically reduce the number of guns that fall into the black market each year. Similar initiatives have been extremely successful in Virginia and other states.

"Ed Rendell grasps that there are tangible steps that can -- and should -- be taken now to reduce gun violence and increase firearm safety," continued Barnes. "He supports the development of personalized smart guns and standards for trigger locks, two measures that would go a long way toward reducing tragic gun deaths -- especially those terrible incidents where children are killed after finding a firearm that wasn't stored properly."

Pennsylvania is fertile ground for advancing common-sense gun safety measures. In January, a Pennsylvania court preserved the right of State Police to maintain a 70-year-old handgun sales database used by the department to help solve crimes. This decision confirms that gun sale records are a critical tool for law enforcement officials. Such records provide a starting point for investigators when a handgun is used in a crime, and they can help to disarm felons who may have bought guns legally in the past, but have since committed a crime and are no longer allowed to possess firearms.
