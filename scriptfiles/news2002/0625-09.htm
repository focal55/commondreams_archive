Common Cause Calls on Pitt to Recuse, Permanently: SEC Should Also Require Companies Disclose Contacts with the Agency
   WASHINGTON - June 25 - News Advisory:

On Wednesday, June 26, Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger will hold a press conference in front of the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) marking SEC Chairman Harvey Pitt's first year in office. Harshbarger will call on the SEC Chairman to recuse himself from enforcement actions against his former clients for the remainder of his time in office.

"Chairman Pitt is highly qualified, but his meetings with a former law client and executives of companies being investigated by his agency show he still has an uphill battle in ensuring average investors that he now works for the American people. That is why we have called on him to take this extraordinary step. In addition, his inappropriate meetings point to a need for greater disclosure of contacts between the SEC and publicly traded corporations. The FCC requires any individuals or groups with certain business before its agency to file a report and a summary of what was discussed within 24 hours of its occurrence -- the SEC should do the same," Harshbarger said.

Common Cause is releasing a letter to Pitt calling for him to recuse himself from any votes on matters that directly affect his former clients for the five-year duration of his term; disclose a list of SEC matters on which Pitt has refrained from voting; and release a list of all the individuals and groups with whom he has met since taking office on August 2.

Common Cause has also written Sens. Paul Sarbanes (D-Md.) and Phil Gramm (R-Texas) of the Senate Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs Committee to ask that they act swiftly to convene confirmation hearings to fill the vacancies on the commission.

WHO: Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger

WHEN: Wednesday, June 26 10 a.m.

WHERE: Securities and Exchange Commission -- 450 Fifth Street, NW Washington, D.C.

Background:
The SEC has refused Freedom of Information Act requests for information. The agency should make information available to the public on its Web site.
