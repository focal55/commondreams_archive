Bush Administration Tries to Escape the Heat on Air Conditioners
Statement of Katherine Morrison, Staff Attorney
   WASHINGTON - May 22 - By announcing today that air conditioner manufacturers can meet lower efficiency requirements, the Bush Administration is once again letting industry off the hook for their actions while consumers and the environment foot the bill.

The quickest, cheapest, cleanest way to reduce America's dependence on polluting energy sources is to dramatically increase energy efficiency. Increasing energy efficiency and renewable sources of energy raises the efficiency of the economy as a whole, making the U.S. more competitive around the globe.

The Department of Energy (DOE) under the Clinton Administration set a standard calling for a 30 percent increase in efficiency for central air conditioners and heat pumps. In April 2001, however, the DOE announced its intention to roll back the standard to a 20 percent improvement.

Air conditioning accounts for as much as 70 percent of electricity used during hot summer days. As temperatures soar, so do electric bills and power plant emissions. Using existing technology to make air conditioners use less energy will not only save consumers $1 billion annually but will help reduce the threat of rolling blackouts by decreasing demand for electricity.

American deserves a safe, clean, affordable energy future. Given all the benefits of pursuing a clean energy path, there is no reason to darken our skies with pollution by continuing to rely on outmoded energy thinking.
