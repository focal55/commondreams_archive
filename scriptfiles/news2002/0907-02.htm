'God is on My Side' Declares 'No on 14' Campaign Volunteer
   MIAMI - September 7 - The National Gay and Lesbian Task Force (NGLTF), the oldest advocacy organization for the gay, lesbian, bisexual and transgender (GLBT) community in the United States, today celebrated a sea change in reaction to gay civil rights activism by organized communities of faith.

"We are here in Miami where 25 years ago Anita Bryant fell to her knees and weepingly prayed at the steps of a local church and launched the "Save our Children" campaign-a direct and libelous assault on the civil rights of the gay and lesbian community, said Lorri L. Jean, NGLTF Executive Director. "By doing so in Miami, she set the stage for the formation of organizations like the Moral Majority, Christian Coalition and many other conservative institutions that have for decades used the name of God to forward a campaign of hate and bigotry on behalf of communities of faith nationwide."

On September 10, voters in Miami will again go to the polls to decide on the very issue that Anita Bryant led in 1977: whether or not protections from discrimination based on sexual orientation should be removed from the county's human rights ordinance.

"Once again, religious fanatics are trying to force their brand of bigotry on everyone. In 1977 it was Anita Bryant. Today, it is the Christian Coalition and the Family Research Council leading the homophobic charge," continued Jean. "Thankfully, that is where the similarity ends."

For example, on August 11, more than 175 local people of faith organized and went door to door in precincts throughout Miami-Dade County, talking to people from a religious perspective about why discrimination is wrong and urging them to oppose discrimination by voting no on Question 14 on Tuesday's ballot.

In fact, the current pastor of a church that, years ago, Anita Bryant called home, led the opening prayer at the canvass orientation. "Religious leaders and people of faith can no longer stand idly by and let the religious extremists speak for all of us, especially when most of us oppose discrimination," said the Rev. Dr. Annette Jones, now pastor of St. John's First United Methodist Church in Miami Beach.

On Monday night, Sept. 9, Jones and the Interfaith Coalition for Human Rights, along with NGLTF, Save Dade, and People For the American Way, will co-sponsor a people of faith candlelight vigil. It will begin at 7 PM at Trinity Cathedral, 464 NE 16th St., Miami.

This is all great news to Rodney McKenzie, a campaign volunteer who traveled from New York City to help defeat Tuesday's ballot measure. "When I was 16, I came out to my pastor and he told me I was going to hell," McKenzie remembered. "It's a relief to see that religious leaders are coming around to what I always knew-God is on our side."
