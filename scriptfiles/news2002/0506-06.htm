NGLTF to Honor Six Feet-Under Star, Nationally Acclaimed Comedienne with Leadership Awards at Washington Ceremony
   WASHINGTON - May 6 - News Advisory:

On May 14, the National Gay and Lesbian Task Force (NGLTF) will honor actor Mathew St. Patrick, who has received national acclaim for his portrayal of openly gay police officer "Keith Charles" on the HBO series Six Feet Under, for his role as an outstanding ally to the nation's gay, lesbian, bisexual and transgendered (GLBT) communities.

St. Patrick will be on hand to accept the 2002 NGLTF Leadership Award, established in 1989 to honor and recognize GLBT allies in the straight community and individuals in the GLBT community who have made unparalleled contributions to advancement of freedom and equality for the nation's GLBT community.

Award-winning actress and comedienne Margaret Cho, as well as DC Black Pride, the organizers of the nation's first and by all accounts largest Black Lesbian & Gay Pride Day, will also attend to accept awards for their contributions to the advancement of the nation's GLBT community.

EVENT DETAILS:

For biographical information on the honorees, contact Jubi Headley or visit the Web site at www.ngltf.org.

Founded in 1973, the National Gay and Lesbian Task Force works to eliminate prejudice, violence and injustice against gay, lesbian, bisexual and transgender people at the local, state and national level. As part of a broader social justice movement for freedom, justice and equality, NGLTF is creating a world that respects and celebrates the diversity of human expression and identity where all people may fully participate in society.
