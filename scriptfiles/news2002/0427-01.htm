Amnesty Int'l to Hold Vigils at Palestinian, Israeli U.N. Missions
Activists Call On Arafat, Sharon: No More Violence, No More Victims
   WASHINGTON - April 27 - News Advisory:

On Sunday, Amnesty International will hold simultaneous vigils at the Palestinian and Israeli Mission to the United Nations in New York. The vigils will honor and mourn the victims on both sides to the Israeli/Palestinian conflict. Amnesty International members in dozens of cities across the United States and throughout the world are holding similar actions this weekend.

"The majority of the casualties on both sides of this conflict have been civilian, and Amnesty's vigil honors and mourns all the victims of violence," said Marty Rosenbluth, Amnesty International USA's country specialist for Israel, the Occupied Territories and the Palestinian Authority. "All violations of human rights need to stop, whether committed in the name of security or in the name of liberty."

When: 6-7 p.m., Sunday, April 28

What: Vigil for victims on both sides of the Israeli/Palestinian conflict

Where: -- Palestinian Mission to the United Nations, 115 East 65th Street -- Israeli Mission to the United Nations, 800 2nd Avenue

Note to photo editors: the vigil will feature colorful banners and placards

Amnesty International's Secretary General, Irene Khan, will visit Israel and the Occupied Territories beginning April 27. She will meet both Palestinian and Israeli victims and their families, human rights defenders and non-governmental organizations.

"It is time to stop playing politics with people's lives and suffering," said Irene Khan before her departure for Israel. "The international community must act now to end the human rights violations against both Palestinian and Israeli civilians. Any solution for this crisis must have human rights at its heart."
