AFSC Medicine for Palestinians in the West Bank
   PHILADELPHIA - May 3 - The American Friends Service Committee (AFSC) Emergency and Material Assistance Program has shipped 85 pounds of essential medicines for immediate relief for Palestinians in the West Bank. The supplies, valued at approximately $31,000, will be administered by AFSC field staff based in Amman, Jordan.

This medicine was donated by Interchurch Medical Assistance (IMA) in New Windsor, Maryland and will be consigned to the Union of Palestinian Medical Relief Committees (UPMRC) in Ramallah in the West Bank. Follow-up shipments are expected.

In addition two 40-foot containers full of quilts will be sent to the region for distribution to Palestinians affected by the recent violence. In addition, these supplies may be earmarked assist refugees displaced into Jordan by any potential US conflict in Iraq.

AFSC is a faith-based organization working for peace and justice in the world with headquarters in Philadelphia and regional offices in Atlanta, Baltimore, Chicago, Des Moines, New York, San Francisco, Seattle, Pasadena, California; and Cambridge, Massachusetts. The AFSC Emergency and Material Assistance Program (EMAP), now in its 81st year of operation, provides material assistance to those in need in this country and abroad. Materials include clothing, medical supplies, seeds, Friend-Ship Kits, and other requested items. The program receives volunteer support to collect, sort, and pack donations.

Operating from a belief in the dignity and worth of all people, AFSC has been active in the Middle East since the 1940s, when it was asked to receive and provide assistance to Palestinian refugees in Gaza. In the 1950s, AFSC continued to work with internally-displaced Palestinians in Israel and operated a youth center in Acre to foster dialogue between Jewish and Palestinian youth in Israel. AFSC supports similar programs in Israel, as well as working with Palestinian youth in the West Bank and Gaza. AFSC will continue to work closely with existing Palestinian and Israeli partner organizations to assure that assistance will reach those most in need.

Recently, responding to the escalating crisis in the Middle East, AFSC launched a Palestine-Israel Crisis Fund to provide humanitarian aid to Palestinians affected by the recent military incursions into the West Bank and Gaza Strip, and to support longer-term reconstruction and peace building efforts in the region. $90,000 was initially earmarked to be used for immediate medical aid, food, bedding and housing reconstruction.

Donations to the Palestine-Israel Crisis Fund should be directed to AFSC, marked "Palestine-Israel Crisis Fund" and mailed to AFSC/Development, 1501 Cherry Street, Philadelphia, PA 19102. To contribute via Visa or Mastercard, call 1-888-588-2372.

For information regarding in-kind material donations, please contact Michael Poulshock at (215) 241-7041. For more information about AFSC, please visit the web site at: www.afsc.org.

The American Friends Service Committee is a Quaker organization that includes people of various faiths who are committed to social justice, peace and humanitarian service. Its work is based on the belief in the worth of every person and faith in the power of love to overcome violence and injustice.
