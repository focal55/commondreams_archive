Common Cause Calls for Disclosure of Expenses and Attendees at Hawaiian Aviation Conference
   WASHINGTON - January 11 - Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger today called on the American Association of Airport Executives to make full disclosure of the costs of its recent conference in Hawaii and the names of all legislators and staff in attendance. The text of the letter follows:

January 11, 2002

Charles Barclay
President
American Association of Airport Executives
601 Madison Street, Suite 400
Alexandria, VA 22314

Dear Mr. Barclay:

Now that the four-day Aviation Issues Conference at the Hapuna Beach Prince Hotel in Kona, Hawaii has concluded, we believe you owe the public full disclosure about how much each of your sponsoring organizations - which included several major airlines and their trade group, the Air Transport Association -- paid to host the conference, and names of the legislators and their staffs who attended.

The public ought to have this information because your organization makes clear that this event is intended to give various segments of the airline and related industries exceptional access to key federal and congressional policymakers. The conference theme this year was "The New World of Aviation Security," and your own registration form called it "a rare opportunity . to meet with aviation leaders from both the Executive and Legislative branches of the U.S. government AND with elected officials ... in an informal debate format, over a multi-day meeting . These officials influence your day-to-day operations."

We are making our request on this, the four-month anniversary of the terrible events of September 11. Those events made vividly clear what is at stake when Congress and federal regulators make and implement public policy concerning aviation security. Because the stakes are so high, we think it is vitally important for you to disclose the all the information we are requesting, including the total cost of the conference, and the names and titles of all federal and legislative employees who attended and whether they paid their own way or were guests of your sponsoring organizations.

We look forward to your response to our request.

Sincerely,
Scott Harshbarger
