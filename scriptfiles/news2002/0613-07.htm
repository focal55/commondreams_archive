D.C. Medical Marijuana Signature Drive Begins
National Zogby Poll: Congress Should Let District Voters Decide
   WASHINGTON - June 13 - Buoyed by results from a new national Zogby poll showing that voters overwhelmingly want Congress to let District voters enact protection for medical marijuana patients without interference, the Marijuana Policy Project today began gathering signatures for the Medical Marijuana Initiative of 2002. 17,000 valid signatures must be turned in by July 8.

"This is the initiative that wouldn't die," said MPP Executive Director Robert Kampia. "Four years ago District voters supported protecting medical marijuana patients by a margin of 69 percent to 31 percent, but drug war ideologues led by Congressman Bob Barr have fought relentlessly to stop us from exercising our democratic rights. First they tried to keep the votes from being counted, and they lost. Then they blocked the measure from taking effect, but that wasn't enough: They even tried to deny District residents the right to vote on the issue ever again -- and they lost again. This is the beginning of the final battle: We will win because protecting patients is the right thing to do and because the public is overwhelmingly behind us."

Kampia -- joined at today's signature drive kickoff by Candida Fraze, plaintiff in MPP's successful lawsuit to overturn the Barr Amendment (which prohibited D.C. residents from voting on medical marijuana), and D.C. Councilmember Jim Graham -- noted that the political atmosphere has changed dramatically since Congress first quashed the District's medical marijuana law. In a new national Zogby poll released today, 62.5 percent of registered voters said that Congress should allow the medical marijuana initiative to take effect without interference, while only 24.1 percent said Congress should block the measure.

"This initiative will pass by a huge margin, just like it did in 1998," Kampia said. "The message from the Zogby poll is clear: Congress should respect democracy and let District voters decide. What is remarkable about these results is how broad the support is: Voters in every region, every age group, every religious group, every income bracket, and every political affiliation -- including Republicans and conservatives -- agree. Members of Congress who ignore such overwhelming sentiment are risking trouble at the polls."

Zogby International conducted interviews of 1,012 registered voters chosen at random nationwide. All calls were made from Zogby International headquarters in Utica, New York, from Friday, June 7, to Sunday, June 9, 2002. The survey has a margin of sampling error of ±3.2%. Margins of error are higher among subgroups. Slight weights were applied to region, age, party, race, religion, and gender to more accurately reflect the voting population. Full results are available at http://www.mpp.org/Zogby.html .

The Marijuana Policy Project works to minimize the harm associated with marijuana -- both the consumption of marijuana and the laws that are intended to prohibit such use. MPP believes that the greatest harm associated with marijuana is imprisonment. To this end, MPP focuses on removing criminal penalties for marijuana use, with a particular emphasis on making marijuana medically available to seriously ill people who have the approval of their doctors. For more information please visit http://www.mpp.org .
