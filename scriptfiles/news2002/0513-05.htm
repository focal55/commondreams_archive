U.S.-Russian Agreement Important Step Forward
   WASHINGTON - May 13 - The nuclear reductions agreement announced by President George W. Bush today is an important step forward," said John Isaacs, president of Council for a Livable World.

"However, there are many details that will have to be worked out that could greatly enhance -- or diminish -- U.S. security," continued Isaacs.

President Bush announced that the U.S. and Russia will sign an agreement in Russia during the May 23 - 26 summit meeting to reduce each country's active strategic nuclear weapons force to between 1,700 - 2,200.

These are significant reductions from the present levels of about 6,000 each of the most deadly weapons ever devised.

However, the two sides have not determined what will happen to the retired nuclear weapons. The U.S. is proposing storing, instead of eliminating the weapons.

"If the United States and Russia leave thousands of nuclear weapons in storage, U.S. security could ultimately be diminished," noted Isaacs. "The security of nuclear weapons and nuclear facilities in Russia is inadequate. These weapons would be highly vulnerable to theft or illicit sale."

"It would be much better if the two countries agree to destroy the weapons once they are taken out of the active inventory," concluded Isaacs.
