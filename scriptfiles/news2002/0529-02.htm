Groups Ask, When Will EPA Tackle Our Nation's Smog Problem?
   WASHINGTON - May 29 - News Advisory:

Nearly five years ago, the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency set new national health standards for ozone, or smog. But, thanks in part to lawsuits by big polluters, those standards are still not being enforced. And public health is suffering as a result.

Now the polluters have lost in court. But when is the EPA going to get moving and put those standards into effect?

Please join us at a luncheon briefing on Thursday, May 30, as a coalition of health and environmental groups explains our perspective on this issue and our next steps, as we spell out what EPA must do to protect our health.

Who: Representatives of health and environmental groups, including:
