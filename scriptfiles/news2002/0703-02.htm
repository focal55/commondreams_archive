Ralph Nader, Advocates to Discuss Needed Reforms to Address the Corporate Crime Wave
   WASHINGTON - July 3 - News Advisory:

WHAT:
Citizen Works press conference with consumer advocate Ralph Nader and other corporate reform specialists to outline an agenda for real reforms that will protect workers, taxpayers, shareholders, pensions, and restore confidence in our economy. Nader and other experts will announce concrete steps that lawmakers and government agencies can take to end the corporate crisis.

WHEN:
Friday, July 5
10 a.m.

WHERE:
National Press Club, Murrow Room
529 14th St. NW, 13th Floor

WHO:
-- Ralph Nader, consumer advocate, founder, Citizen Works
-- Travis Plunkett, legislative director, Consumer Federation of America
-- Tom Devine, legislative director, Government Accountability Project and others...

WHY:
With the crisis in corporate America reaching an ever more fevered pitch and a President and a Congress both marinated in corporate money, trusted advocates for consumers, workers, and shareholders will set standards for Congress and President Bush's upcoming address on corporate responsibility.
