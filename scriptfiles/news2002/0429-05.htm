College Loan Corporation Calls on White House, Congress to Protect Affordable Education for Students
   WASHINGTON - April 29 - In response to breaking news reports on possible changes in the federal student loan program, the College Loan Corporation (CLC), a leading student loan provider, called today on the White House and Congress to protect affordable education for American students by safeguarding fixed interest rates on federal student loans.

"The consolidation of fixed rate student loans has made higher education a reality for millions of students in this country," stated Mark Brenner, General Counsel for CLC. "The system works for students and their families by making education more accessible to more students. We need to protect the consolidated loan program that offers low fixed interest rates. Without this protection, high interest rates could price thousands of students out of their dream of obtaining a higher education."

Brenner cited figures from the Congressional Research Service stating that the federal loan program helped over 680,000 students consolidate $16.8 billion of loans in fiscal year 2001. He noted that abolishing fixed interest rates on federal student loans could cost the typical borrower over $2,800 more to pay for their education. The poorest students who depend on federal Pell Grants to finance higher education could pay over $3,100 more.

"Both the Administration and the 107th Congress have worked hard to make quality education affordable. We urge all members of Congress and the Bush Administration to seriously consider the impact that this change would have on the ability of all students to obtain a higher education," Brenner stated.

The College Loan Corporation is a leading national student loan company offering Federal Family Education Program Loans (Stafford, Plus and Consolidation loans) to students in all 50 states and the District of Columbia. In the past two years alone, CLC has helped tens of thousands of students borrow to finance their education at more than 1,500 colleges and universities.
