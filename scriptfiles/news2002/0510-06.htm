As Women Defend Liberties Abroad, Lawmakers Block Their Abortion Access
   WASHINGTON - May 10 - "Once again, lawmakers have needlessly jeopardized the health, lives and dignity of American servicewomen and dependents stationed overseas by blocking their access to safe abortion services," stated NARAL President Kate Michelman. By a vote of 215 to 202, the House defeated an amendment to the Department of Defense authorization bill offered by Rep. Loretta Sanchez (D-Calif.) that would have repealed the current ban on privately funded abortions at overseas military facilities.

"Women willing to sacrifice their lives for this country should not have to forego basic healthcare services overseas," stated Michelman. "Anti-choice lawmakers are denying women the very liberties they are committed to defending."

"American military women deserve the same options available to women here at home. They are serving abroad to protect and defend the rights they themselves are continuously denied as servicewomen."

Background: The Sanchez amendment would lift the current-law ban on privately funded abortions in overseas military hospitals. The amendment would not require the Department of Defense to pay for abortions, nor would it affect current policies that permit military personnel who object to abortion not to participate in the procedure.
