Senate Panel Holds Hearing on Historic Women's Rights Treaty
Statement of NARAL President Kate Michelman
   WASHINGTON - June 13 - "Thanks to the leadership of Senators Barbara Boxer and Joe Biden, today the Senate Foreign Relations Committee highlighted the importance of ratifying the long-overdue Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women (CEDAW). The first treaty of its kind, CEDAW explicitly promotes human rights for women and opposes any form of gender discrimination. NARAL believes the United States should join the 165 countries that have already ratified the 22 year-old international treaty.

CEDAW seeks simply to ensure that women have equal access and opportunities in all aspects of public and political life. The treaty is designed to discourage discrimination based on political affiliations, cultural heritage and socio-economic status while encouraging equality in education, employment, health care and property rights.

Twenty years after its adoption by the United Nations, the Bush Administration — which frequently states its support for international women's rights — now has an opportunity to demonstrate in concrete terms its values.

NARAL supports CEDAW and urges its adoption."
