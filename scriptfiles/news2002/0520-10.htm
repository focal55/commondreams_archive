Activists Link Fight Against Global and Domestic Poverty
"1K Race to the Bottom" to Coincide With World Economic Forum Meeting and Votes on U.S. Welfare Reform

Attn: Photo Editors - Spirited Street Theater
  WASHINGTON - May 20 - Domestic and global anti-poverty activists will join forces on May 21, 2002 for a major rally at the headquarters of the U.S. Chamber of Commerce to expose the links between poverty at home and abroad.

The "1K Race to the Bottom" will take place as Congress votes on changes to the 1996 welfare reform law, which has resulted in thousands of families being kicked off the rolls due to punitive time limits and sanctions. It will take place outside of an exclusive meeting of the World Economic Forum, at which global business executives will discuss policies that impact poor people throughout the world.

The event is co-sponsored by the Mobilization for Global Justice (MGJ) and the National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support (NCJIS). National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support is a coalition of grassroots anti-poverty organizations in 40 states organizing to turn welfare into a meaningful anti-poverty program. It convened a rally of more than 2,000 low-income activists in Washington, DC in March. The Mobilization for Global Justice is a DC-based coalition of activists devoted to global economic justice and environmental sustainability. It sponsors protests and educational events that have drawn as many as 30,000 concerned citizens to Washington, DC.
