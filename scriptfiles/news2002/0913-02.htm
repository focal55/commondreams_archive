Rahall: State Department Says Iraq Trip in "National Interest"
   DAMASCUS, SYRIA - September 13 - Preparing to depart Damascus on his trip to Baghdad, U.S. Rep. Nick Rahall (D-WV) said the U.S. Department of State has approved his travel under the National Interest category.

In a telephone interview today, Rahall said:

"I was happy to see the President call for support of UN resolutions. The UN cannot become a relic of the past. It must be more active, and it must strive harder to help resolve the current stalemate between Iraq and the US. But the President, unfortunately, continues to ratchet up the war rhetoric.

"I think we need to calm down this rhetoric, pursue peaceful means. We don't need the rhetoric of the people who put words into the president's speech - bellicose rhetoric written by the same people who were there twelve years ago during the Gulf War when Bush the first was President. I did support Bush the first using force to drive Iraq from Kuwait. But this is vastly different today.

"We may have won the battle in the Persian Gulf, but we didn't win the war. We have not won the war against the real enemy - Al Qaeda. Bush has built a great coalition to fight our true enemy, Al Qaeda, but an attack on Iraq will not help maintain that support. We must fight the true terrorists, especially where the evidence is clearly there. These partners in our coalition will find themselves in an uneasy position if we force them to support military action in Iraq."
