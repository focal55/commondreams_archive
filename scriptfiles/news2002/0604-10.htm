Press Conference Wed June 5 for National Forest Roadless Area Conservation Act
   WASHINGTON - June 4 - Wednesday morning Sierra Club representatives will join Representatives Jay Inslee (D-WA) and Sherwood Boehlert (R-NY) and other environmental groups at a press conference announcing the National Forest Roadless Area Conservation Act. This bipartisan legislation would protect nearly 60 million acres of pristine National Forest lands from most logging, mining, drilling and other destructive activities.

The proposed legislation codifies the Roadless Area Conservation Rule, one of the most sweeping land conservation measures in a generation. The rule was approved following years of scientific study and more than 600 million public meetings across the country. To date the Forest Service has received more than 2.2 million comments favoring roadless protection. This outpouring of public response is almost 10 times greater than that of any other rule in history. The Sierra Club is now calling on other members of Congress to join the more than 170 Representatives who are cosponsoring this important bill.

WHAT: Press conference to announce the introduction of the National Forest Roadless Area Conservation Act in the House of Representatives.

WHO: U.S. Representatives Jay Inslee (D-WA), Sherwood Boehlert (R-NY), and others members of Congress, and representatives of environmental groups.
