Young People's Reproductive Health at Risk: House Panel Authorizes More "Abstinence-Only" Funding
   WASHINGTON - April 24 - ""Today anti-choice lawmakers in the House voted to censor information that would allow young people to make intelligent and informed decisions about their reproductive health," said NARAL President Kate Michelman. The vote came as members of the House Energy and Commerce Committee defeated every reasonable attempt to improve restrictive "abstinence-only" education. Instead, the bill that was passed provides another quarter of a billion dollars to unproven programs that censor discussion of contraception, thereby failing to help young people protect themselves against unintended pregnancies, HIV and STDs.

"When it comes to responsible sex education, President Bush and anti-choice lawmakers continue to defy medical and public opinion. Over 125 national health organizations and the American public strongly support providing responsible sex education to American youth," stated NARAL President Kate Michelman.

"Over the past few years, ideologically driven groups, not scientific or public health organizations, have pushed for abstinence-only education," said Michelman. "This is a potentially deadly mistake. Approximately one-quarter of all new STDs occur in teens, almost half of all new cases of HIV occur in people under 25, and three-fourths of all teenage pregnancies are unintended. The most effective way to reduce these numbers is by providing comprehensive, medically accurate information to young teens," stated Michelman.

"Contrary to anti-choice rhetoric, responsible sex education does not promote or encourage young people to have sex." Michelman continued, "It does just the opposite. Teens receiving comprehensive sex education wait longer to have sex, have it less frequently, and increase their use of contraceptives."
