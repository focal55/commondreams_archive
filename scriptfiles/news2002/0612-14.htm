House Tells Conferees:
Pay for Tax Breaks to Energy Companies
Friends of the Earth Calls for $27B Cut in Polluter Tax Breaks
   WASHINGTON - June 12 - Today, the House of Representatives accepted a "motion to instruct" offered by Reps. Markey (D-Mass.) and Stenholm (D-Texas) that would pay for the tax provisions in the House energy bill -- H.R.4. Although non-binding, the motion instructs House conferees to find ways to offset the more than $36 billion in tax credits in the energy bill -- of which $27 billion directly benefits the most dangerous and polluting industries, according to Friends of the Earth.

"It's laudable that the House is finally discussing fiscal discipline in the energy debate," said Friends of the Earth President Brent Blackwelder. "The House can demonstrate their commitment by eliminating the more than $27 billion in anti-environment tax breaks that go to the oil, gas, coal and nuclear industries."
