ABM Treaty's Demise a 'Threat to Global Security'
Physicians Group Deplores the End to a Cornerstone of International Arms Control
  WASHINGTON - June 13 - Physicians for Social Responsibility deplores the demise today of a main cornerstone of international arms control that has brokered security between the U.S. and Russia for over thirty years.

"The ABM Treaty has been, and continues to be, essential to the prevention of nuclear war and the elimination of nuclear weapons in the United States, Russia, and around the world," said Robert K. Musil, Ph.D., M.P.H., executive director and CEO of Physicians for Social Responsibility. "The end of this treaty marks a historic turning point away from proven arms control regimes that have successfully ensured American security for decades towards a future which will be destabilizing and vastly less secure."

The ABM Treaty expires today, six months after President Bush announced his intentions to withdraw from the treaty. Bush's announcement came without Congressional approval and several members of the House of Representatives have filed a lawsuit to prevent the President from withdrawing from the treaty.

The ABM Treaty, which was intended to stop the United States and the Soviet Union -- and now Russia -- from deploying national missile defenses, is considered an obstacle to the Bush administration's multi-layered missile defense efforts. Preliminary construction on a missile defense installation at Fort Greely, in Alaska, is set to begin June 14, 2002.

"The ultimate irony is that the Bush administration decision to withdraw from this treaty is prompted by a dream to pursue a National Missile Defense system to make America more secure. Yet development of such a system will only further exacerbate the destabilization caused by the end of the ABM Treaty, worsen relations with Russia and China, and make America less secure. It is a Catch-22 of the worst kind."

Physicians for Social Responsibility represents more than 22,000 physicians and health professionals who are committed to a sustainable environment, nuclear disarmament and an end to gun violence. http://www.psr.org.
