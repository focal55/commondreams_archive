Critics of World Bank-Financed "White Elephant" Project to Demonstrate Today
 

WASHINGTON - June 17 -

WHO: Critics of Bujagali Dam, Uganda

WHAT: Demonstration Outside World Bank and Release of Letters from Ugandan and U.S. NGOs Calling for Delay in Board Vote

WHEN: Monday, June 17, 5:30 PM

WHERE: The World Bank, 18th and H Streets NW, Washington DC

BACKGROUND

As the World Bank prepares to consider further financing of the controversial Bujagali Hydropower Project in Uganda, a recently leaked report of the World Bank's Inspection Panel confirms that the project could become another World Bank "white elephant." NGOs are calling for a delay in the World Bank Board vote until: (1) the Inspection Panel report is made public; (2) the Power Purchase Agreement between the Government of Uganda and the project sponsor, the AES Corporation of Arlington, Virginia, is publicly released; and (3) concerns about the economic viability and environmental and social impacts of the project are addressed.

On Monday, June 17, the World Bank's Executive Board is scheduled to meet with the Inspection Panel to discuss its report on Bujagali. The Inspection Panel report raises serious concerns that the project may not be economically viable. Under the still-secret Power Purchase Agreement, the Government of Uganda will guarantee the dollar returns of AES, which could leave Uganda mired even deeper in foreign debt. The Panel report calls for the public release of the Power Purchase Agreement to allow an informed decision by the people who will bear the costs and risks of the project.

On Tuesday, June 18, the Board of the Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (MIGA)-- the little-known division of the World Bank Group that provides political risk insurance to corporations that invest in developing countries--will consider a last-minute request to insure the project. While the project already has secured financing from the World Bank Group's International Development Association, and its private sector lending arm, the International Finance Corporation, concerns have arisen over AES's ability to finance the project because its stock value has fallen precipitously in the wake of the Enron debacle. Support from export credit agencies and other investors hinges on MIGA's decision.
