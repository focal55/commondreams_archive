U.S. Church Leaders Return From Middle East
Call For Bold Steps To Achieve A Just And Lasting Peace
   WASHINGTON - April 30 - A delegation of 13 U.S. church leaders returned from the Middle East and, in a far-ranging statement, called for bold steps to be taken by Israel and the Palestinian Authority in order to end violence in the region and to achieve peace with justice.

Entitled "By My Spirit," a reference to a biblical passage from Zechariah that the delegation quoted in the document, the statement expressed sympathy for all "Israelis and Palestinians who have lost family members and friends to the senseless violence over the past months."

The delegation of church leaders was assembled by the National Council of the Churches of Christ in the U.S.A. and its General Secretary, Rev. Dr. Robert W. Edgar. The leaders traveled in the region from April 16 to 27 at the invitation of the Middle East Council of Churches. Before arriving in Palestine and Israel, the delegation had stopped in Turkey, Lebanon, Syria, and Jordan to speak with church and political leaders. Among others, the delegation met with Prime Minister Rafiq Hariri in Lebanon, President Bashara al Assad in Syria, and King Abdullah II in Jordan. Most meetings, and indeed, the statement issued by the group, focused on the conflict between Israel and Palestinians.

Of the violence currently taking place in the region, the delegation said in part, "We condemn equally and unequivocally both the suicide bombings and Palestinian violence against Israeli society and the violence of the Israeli occupation of Palestinian territories. All are counterproductive to achieving peace with justice. ...Both societies are caught in a cycle of violence and revenge."

The group showed particular concern for the continuing standoff at the Church of the Nativity in Bethlehem. Some members of the delegation had traveled to Jenin with a convoy of food and medical aid; the group urged that the UN investigation of events be allowed to proceed unimpeded. The delegation also demonstrated a particular concern for the plight and future of Arab Christians in the Middle East, noting that "(t)he Arab Christian population has declined precipitously in recent decades."

However, it was on the attainment of a just and lasting peace to the Israeli-Palestinian conflict that the church delegates expressed the most opinion: "Our delegation leaves the Middle East convinced that an enduring peace can be achieved if the Israeli occupation of Palestinian territories ends and if the establishment of a viable Palestinian state alongside a secure State of Israel follows soon." The group was equally strong in assessing the need for Israel to have its peace and security guaranteed: "It (Israel) is entitled to full recognition of its legitimacy within the international community, including by the Arab states. ...Palestinians cannot expect to achieve the dignity, rights, and respect they have sought for so long without ceasing acts of violence against the civilian population of Israel."

The delegation identified eight specific components for a just resolution to the conflict. Included in these were "the establishment of an international peacekeeping force, agreed upon by Israel and the Palestinian Authority, to oversee the Israeli withdrawal from the West Bank and Gaza and maintain order until a peace agreement can be fully implemented."

The full text of the statement, as well as the list of delegates/signers can be found at www.ncccusa.org.
