Peaceful Demonstration: Communities Respond to Catholic Church Abuse Scandal
   NEW YORK - April 26 - A diverse coalition of organizations, individuals and leaders from the lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender and allied community will conduct a peaceful demonstration and press conference to express their concern and dismay over the response to the growing Catholic Church sex abuse scandal. These events offer an opportunity to address a variety of issues, including the scapegoating of gay priests, the church hierarchy¹s continued lack of compassion for abuse victims and the ongoing refusal of Cardinal Edward Egan (New York) to admit his role in the cover-up of abuse. The events will also respond to recent anti-gay comments made by Monsignore Eugene Clark (rector of St. Patrick¹s Cathedral in New York), Archbishop Wilton Gregory (head of the U.S. conference of Bishops), Archbishop Theodore E. McCarrick (Washington, DC) and Cardinal Adam Maida (Detroit).

WHEN:
Sunday, April 28, 2002
9:30am (Demonstration)
11:00am (Press Conference - Immediately following the Cardinal's 10:15 Mass)

WHERE:
Across from St. Patrick's Cathedral (5th Avenue & 50th/51st Streets, New York, NY)

WHO:
Elected officials, clergy members, abuse survivors, gay, lesbian, bisexual and transgender community members, our allies, families, and friends.

The demonstration and press conference are being organized by a wide variety of lesbian, gay, bisexual and transgender individuals and organizations. Our collective anger and dismay is directed at the church hierarchy, not members of the Catholic faith.

For more information regarding the Catholic Church abuse scandal and the GLBT community response, please see www.glaad.org.
