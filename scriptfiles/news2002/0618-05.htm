'People Pedaling Peace' Bike Ride Honors Victims of Gun Violence
Press Conference to Take Place at Finish Line on Capitol Hill
  

WASHINGTON - June 18 - News Advisory:

More than 25 dedicated cyclists will make the 190-mile trek from Hampton, Va., to Washington, D.C., June 22-25 to honor and remember victims of gun violence.

A press conference and rally will take place at the finish line on Tuesday, June 25 at 10 a.m. at Upper Senate Park in the shadow of the U.S. Capitol. The conference and rally will feature remarks from Sandra McSweeney, founding member of "People Pedaling Peace"; Mary Leigh Blek, Million Mom March director; Elisha Encinias, Columbine survivor; Michael Barnes, president of The Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence united with the Million Mom March; Nan Aron, president of Alliance For Justice; and other riders participating in the event.

Sandra and Mike McSweeney formed the "People Pedaling Peace" bike ride last year after their 14-year-old daughter, Stephanie, was shot and killed as she walked out of a Hampton, Va., roller rink in March of 2001. The Million Mom March united with Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence is proudly partnered with "People Pedaling Peace" to rally against gun violence.
