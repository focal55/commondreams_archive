PFAW Available for Comment on Today's Hearing On Lawsuit Against Justice Department's Unprecedented Secrecy On Detainees
   WASHINGTON - May 29 - A hearing was held this morning in a case brought by more than a dozen civil liberties, human rights and public access organizations challenging the Justice Department's refusal to release meaningful information about hundreds of people who have been detained since September 11.

Judge Gladys Kessler of the federal district court in Washington heard cross-motions for summary judgment from the government and the public interest organizations. The case was filed on December 5, 2001. Groups providing counsel in the case include People For the American Way Foundation, Center for National Security Studies, the ACLU and Electronic Privacy Information Center.

People for the American Way representatives are available for comment.
