Common Cause Call For Full And Open Debate On War With Iraq
  

WASHINGTON - September 11 - Common Cause is among the first major national advocacy groups to call on the President and Congress to engage in full and open debate before committing military forces to Iraq.

"The War Powers Act, the Constitution and citizens' right to know demand a thorough debate before Americans are asked to send their sons and daughters to war," Scott Harshbarger, president and CEO of Common Cause, said in a letter Wednesday to President Bush and congressional leaders.

Common Cause is not seeking the revelation of information that could jeopardize national security. We ask only that the Bush administration provide federal lawmakers with information they need to make an informed decision as to whether war with Iraq is in the nation's best interest.

"The lives of American soldiers should not be put at risk without the advice and consent of Congress," the letter said.

Last week, Common Cause collected over 7,000 names on a petition calling for Congressional approval and input before troops are committed to war against Baghdad.

Common Cause is a citizen watchdog group representing more than 200,000 members.
