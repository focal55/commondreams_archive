National Environmental Trust Statement on the Bush Administration Plan to Roll Back New Source Review
   WASHINGTON - June 13 - The following was released today by the National Environmental Trust:

As you may have seen in some preliminary reports last night and this morning, the Administration is moving today at 11 a.m. with its rollback of New Source Review. We have just been briefed by Jeffery Holmstead, EPA assistant administrator for air and radiation, on their proposal. The following is our response:

STATEMENT OF PHIL CLAPP, President, National Environmental Trust:

"This is the end of the Clinton Administration's long attempt to force dirty coal-fired power plants to clean up. It will undermine all the pending lawsuits EPA and states like New York have brought to make big utilities comply with the Clean Air Act.

"What is really amazing is that the Administration has made this decision without calculating how much more air pollution it will mean, how many more asthma attacks, or how many more deaths. They simply have no numbers.

"This administration is beginning to smell of corruption. What else can you call it when the White House is willing to sacrifice the health of millions of people to pay back big utility campaign contributions, looks the other way while Enron bilks Californians of billions of dollars, and dumps its campaign promise to do something about global warming when the coal industry weighs in?"
