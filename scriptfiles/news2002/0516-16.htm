NARAL: Nuremberg Files Constitutes A True Threat
   WASHINGTON - May 16 - The following is a statement of NARAL President Kate Michelman on Planned Parenthood of the Columbia/Willamette v. American Coalition of Life Activists (ACLA):

"NARAL applauds today's decision by the 9th Circuit Court of Appeals recognizing that the Nuremberg Files website and the two "WANTED" posters targeting specific doctors constitute true threats. This ruling sends a clear message to those who use threats and violence against physicians who perform abortions that such behavior is illegal, and deplorable. It is a victory for physicians around the country and for a woman's right to choose."

"As the Court held: 'There is substantial evidence that these posters were prepared and disseminated to intimidate physicians from providing reproductive health services. Thus, ACLA was appropriately found liable for a true threat to intimidate under FACE' (Freedom of Access to Clinic Entrances Act)."

"Anti-choice extremists must take responsibility for the damaging effect of their harsh rhetoric. When physicians who provide abortions are referred to as "evil", "murderers", and "butchers", a culture of intolerance and hatred is sure to follow. These threats of violence are very real. Indeed, Drs. Britton, Gunn, and Slepian were all targeted prior to being murdered by anti-choice extremists."

"Today's decision demonstrates in stark terms the importance of the Schumer/Hatch Amendment to the pending bankruptcy bill. Six of the Nuremberg files defendants, hours before their asset depositions, filed for bankruptcy as part of a litigation strategy designed to avoid paying their fines."

"Health care professionals who risk their lives everyday to ensure women's access to complete reproductive health care deserve the right to live and work without the threat of violence."
