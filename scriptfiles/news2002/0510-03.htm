House Panel Rejects President's Position On Family Planning, Votes To Release Frozen UNFPA Funds
  WASHINGTON - May 10 - Last night, the House Appropriations Committee sent a clear message to the President that lawmakers will not sacrifice the health and well being of millions of women worldwide for the sake of the President's anti-choice agenda. The Committee passed an amendment releasing $34 million in U.N. family planning funds, frozen by the President at the behest of anti-choice lawmakers. The bipartisan amendment was authored by Reps. Lowey (D-N.Y.) and Kolbe (R-Ariz.).

"The loss of $34 million this past year has had a devastating impact on developing countries. The funds help prevent over 2 million unintended pregnancies, nearly 800,000 abortions, 47,000 maternal deaths, almost 600,000 cases of serious maternal illness and more than 77,000 infant and child deaths annually," said NARAL President Kate Michelman. "In bowing to anti-choice lawmakers, the President has needlessly jeopardized the lives of millions of women around the world."

"The priorities of the UNFPA are clear: to increase access to reproductive health, promote safe pregnancy and delivery, reduce maternal mortality and assist refugees with disease prevention, treatment, and emergency aid," said Michelman. "But for years, the UNFPA has been intentionally mischaracterized by anti-family planning lawmakers. The program does not fund abortions; it provides voluntary reproductive health care and family planning services to those in dire circumstances."
