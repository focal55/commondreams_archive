Planned Parenthood Urges Senate to Remedy Health Care Inequity for Women, Pass Contraceptive Coverage Bill
Planned Parenthood Stands Up for Women in the Courts, in the States, on Campus and in Congress
  

WASHINGTON - June 18 - The following is a statement by Gloria Feldt, president, Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

The contraceptive coverage bill (Equity in Prescription Insurance and Contraceptive Coverage) is a potent remedy to a longstanding health care inequity. When it comes to health insurance, men get a better deal. An overwhelming majority of Americans recognize that access to safe, effective contraception improves women's health, reduces unintended pregnancy and lowers the need for abortion. The public wants this legislation and it's time Congress stepped up to the plate.

Health care without contraception is a like a car without seat belts. It took an act of Congress to get automakers to install seat belts in cars and now another act is needed to get insurance companies to cover contraception.

Planned Parenthood works in the courts, in the states, on campus and in Congress to ensure all women have access to the safest and most effective contraceptive methods. Our momentous victories in Erickson v. Bartell Drug Co. and in 20 state legislatures nationwide send an important message to employers and insurers: Denying contraceptive coverage is sex discrimination. Planned Parenthood vows to end this injustice.

Planned Parenthood Federation of America is the nation's largest and most trusted voluntary reproductive health care organization. Planned Parenthood affiliates serve nearly five million men, women and teenagers every year. For more information on PPFA and our efforts to ensure access to contraception, visit http://wwwplannedparenthood.org and http://covermypills.com
