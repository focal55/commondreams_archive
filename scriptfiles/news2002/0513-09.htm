Activists to Link fight Against Global and Domestic Poverty
"1K Race to the Bottom" to Coincide With World Economic Forum Meeting and Domestic Welfare Legislation
  

WASHINGTON - May 13 -

WHAT: Press conference

WHO: National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support
Mobilization for Global Justice

WHERE: National Press Club, 529 14th St., NW, Washington, DC

WHEN: May 15, 2002, 12 noon

Domestic and global anti-poverty activists will hold a press conference to to announce a new alliance and jointly sponsored event, the "1K Race to the Bottom" to be held on Tuesday, May, 21st during the World Economic Forum (WEF) meeting and deliberations on Capitol Hill regarding domestic welfare programs.

The "1K Race to the Bottom" will take place as the Senate votes on changes to the 1996 welfare reform law, which has resulted in thousands of families being kicked off the rolls due to punitive time limits and sanctions. It will also coincide with an exclusive meeting of the WEF, at which global business executives will discuss policies that impact poor people throughout the world. Preceding the 1K Race to the Bottom will be a major rally at the headquarters of the U.S. Chamber of Commerce (where the WEF forum will take place) to expose the links between poverty at home and abroad.

The event is co-sponsored by the Mobilization for Global Justice (MGJ) and the National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support (NCJIS). National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support is a coalition of grassroots anti-poverty organizations in 40 states organizing to turn TANF into a meaningful anti-poverty program. It convened a rally of more than 2,000 low-income activists in Washington, DC in March. The Mobilization for Global Justice is a DC-based coalition of activists devoted to global economic justice and environmental sustainability. It sponsors protests and educational events which have drawn as many as 30,000 concerned citizens to Washington, DC.

For more information, please visit our websites at <www.globalizethis.org> and <www.nationalcampaign.org>.
