New England Religious Leaders Demand Climate Action
Converge on Six State Capitals to Sing, Pray for End to Global Warming
   BOSTON - June 17 - Calling global warming "a crime against Creation," religious leaders of many faiths converged on their state capitals last week to ask the six New England governors to implement the Climate Change Action Plan they approved last August. The plan calls for regional reduction of greenhouse emissions to 1990 levels by 2010 and long-term reductions of 75-85 percent, but it depends entirely upon implementation by individual states.

At the Massachusetts State House June 11, over a hundred Christian, Jewish, Muslim, and other worshipers asked Gov. Jane Swift to reduce Massachusetts' greenhouse emissions through conservation, efficiency, and alternative fuels. "Like Noah, we must build an ark," said Rev. Fred Small of Religious Witness for the Earth, which convened the service. "The sky is dark, the storm has come, and still we pretend it's just a passing shower. Let's stop pretending and start building."

Author and environmentalist Bill McKibben, a United Methodist Sunday school teacher, told fifty people gathered at the Maine capitol that global warming will devastate New England, where the climate will feel like Georgia's and the maple sugar industry will be destroyed.

Over 1000 New Englanders, including 330 clergy, have signed an Interfaith Call for Climate Action asking their governors to meet or exceed the goals of the regional Climate Change Action Plan. Each state is expected to release its own plan by August, when the governors will reconvene in Quebec City with the premiers of the eastern Canadian provinces, who also approved the regional plan.

Outside the State House in Hartford, CT, Rev. Susan Veronica Rak of the Unitarian Universalist Church of Greater Bridgeport rang a Tibetan singing bowl and read from the Psalms. In Concord, NH, 11-year-old Emma WestRasmus held listeners rapt as she sang "Turn the World Around." In Providence, RI, Rev. Amy Freedman of Channing Memorial Church led a prayer circle around the United Nations flag. And in Montpelier, VT, an interfaith delegation delivering the Call for Climate Action serenaded Gov. Howard Dean's staff in his office lobby.
