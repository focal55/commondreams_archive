Palestinian Women Deliver Food to Those Trapped in Church of the Nativity; Israeli Soldiers Hurl Grenades
  BETHLEHEM, PALESTINE - May 9 - At approximately 4:30 p.m. (ME time) Wednesday, a group of Palestinian women did what the Israeli government and military have refused to do, and what the international community has failed to do—delivered food to those trapped in the Church of the Nativity. IDF troops immediately hurled grenades and opened fire at the door through which the food was delivered.

Those in the church—Palestinians, international activists (from the U.K., the U.S., Ireland and Sweden) and a journalist—had gone three days with only hot water, pasta pieces and boiled weeds as meals. What the Palestinian women brought is enough food for four days.

On 2 May, 10 international activists, bearing packages of food, darted through Manger Square and entered the Church of the Nativity, stunning Israeli Defense Force troops. 13 others were caught and detained by troops in their attempt to enter the church; several of those activists are now in illegal detention and on hunger strike.
