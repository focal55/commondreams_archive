Demonstration Against Plan Colombia in Columbus, OH Leads to Arrests of Oberlin Students, Others
  

COLUMBUS, OHIO - May 2 - On Wednesday morning, May 1, ten Colombia solidarity activists from Ohio appeared in Franklin County Municipal Court before Judge James Green. After a night in the county jail, all were arraigned on charges of having criminally trespassed and resisted arrest in Sen. Mike DeWine's Columbus office on Tuesday afternoon. All pled not guilty, and their release is pending the posting of bonds.

The ten represent civic groups throughout Ohio that have tried for two years to meet directly with DeWine -- always unsuccessfully. On Tuesday they insisted, as constituents, on a definite date for a meeting with the senator -- and they refused to leave his office without the promise of a timely appointment.

Sen. DeWine helped to craft "Plan Colombia" for the Clinton Administration, which provided $1.3 billion in mostly military aid to Colombia. Currently he supports the Bush Administration's efforts to further increase military aid, as well as efforts to chemically eradicate coca crops.

The protesters maintain that evidence on the ground in Colombia proves that US policies are killing and displacing rural civilian populations, many of whom are living over vast and coveted oil reserves. As newly arraigned John Ewers stated before entering DeWine's office on Tuesday, "Sen. DeWine cares a lot about keeping the children of Ohio healthy. We insist that he care as much about the children of Colombia."

Plan Colombia was originally supposed to provide money to fight the "drug war" -- to stop Colombian farmers from growing coca plants -- by providing military aid to a government that has ties to human-rights abusing paramilitary groups. To the Bush administration, Colombia now falls under the "counterterrorism" banner. Currently there is a bill pending in congress that would provide another $98 million not specifically targeted at stopping the drug war; the money is instead to be used for a number of counterinsurgency tactics including the guarding of an Oxidental Oil pipeline that is frequently broken by a guerilla group because it goes straight through the land of the indigenous U'Wa people.
