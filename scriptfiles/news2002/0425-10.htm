Senate Once Again Rejects Oil Savings
Overall, Energy Bill Fails America
   WASHINGTON - April 25 - The Sierra Club today expressed disappointment with Senators who turned their backs on an opportunity to save one million barrels of oil a day. By a vote of 57 to 42, Senators rejected an amendment offered by Sens. Tom Carper(D-DE) and Arlen Specter (R-PA), to require the Department of Transportation to reduce oil consumption from our cars and light trucks by one million barrels of oil per day by 2015.

"The Senate failed to give Americans what they've asked for -- a responsible energy plan that reduces our dependence on oil," said Carl Pope, Executive Director of the Sierra Club. "The Carper-Specter amendment was a reasonable, realistic and responsible goal that could have put us on the right path to reducing our dependence on oil. The Senate threw away a chance to reduce global warming pollution while saving Americans money at the gas pump."

The Carper-Specter amendment would have:

  • Saved more oil than the U.S. currently imports from Iraq and Kuwait combined,
  • Helped curb global warming by keeping some 45 million metric tons of carbon equivalent emissions out of the atmosphere every year,
  • Improved our air quality, and protected our public health, by keeping millions of tons of cancer-causing toxic and smog-forming emissions out of the air we breathe,
  • Saved consumers billions at the pump, keeping dollars at home rather than sending them overseas to pay for oil. These dollars, invested in the economy, would help improve wages and create jobs.

Though this amendment was not as effective as specifically increasing fuel economy standards, it could have been an important step in the right direction to protect America's national energy security and our environment.

Americans want to cut our oil dependence and increase our use of clean, renewable energy. While the Senate energy bill began as a promising plan, polluters plundered the bill. Now the Senate bill utterly fails to meet America's energy needs, and instead sends America backward. The abysmal Senate bill:

  • saves virtually no oil;
  • hardly increases renewable energy;
  • eliminates safeguards that protect consumers from Enron-like manipulations; and
  • gives billions of dollars in subsidies to dirty coal and oil, and dangerous nuclear power.

Next stop for this bill is a conference with the House energy bill that shares the shortcomings of the Senate bill and compounds them by opening the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge to oil drilling. The House bill is missing even the modest energy efficiency and renewable provisions of the Senate bill, and adds about $20 billion in tax breaks for dirty energy industries. Both the House and Senate bills fail the test of a sound energy policy.

"This bill is a failure because it doesn't significantly reduce oil dependence or pollution," said Pope. "Congress must create a different plan which protects our energy future, while protecting our land, air and water at the same time."
