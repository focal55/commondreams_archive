Common Cause Calls On DNC Chair To Stop Fighting Reform Announces 'Whose Side Are You On, Terry?' Web site
   WASHINGTON - June 13 - News Advisory:

WHAT:
Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger will call on DNC Chair Terry McAuliffe to stop his lawyers from trying to weaken the McCain-Feingold law (the Bipartisan Campaign Reform Act of 2002) in the courts and at the Federal Election Commission.

WHEN:
Friday, June 14, 10 a.m.

WHERE:
Outside the Democratic National Committee 430 S. Capitol St. SE

"Mr. McAuliffe and other party leaders cannot take public credit for enacting important reforms supported by the American people on the one hand, while sending their lawyers out to undermine the law," Harshbarger said. "It undercuts the success of brave legislator s, Democrats and their leadership, as well as Republicans, who voted to end the corrupt system of unlimited contributions."

Harshbarger will release a letter to McAuliffe, announce the "Whose Side Are You On, Terry?" Web site and a grassroots effort to encourage citizens to contact the DNC.

Lawyers for the Democratic National Committee and other Democratic committees are actively working to undermine McCain-Feingold in the courts and at the Federal Election Commission. The FEC will vote next week on regulations intended to implement the soft money ban.
