Ralph Nader Calls on Members of Congress to Post Voting Records on Their Personal Web Sites
  

WASHINGTON - January 14 - The following is a statement of Ralph Nader calling on members of Congress to make their voting records more easily accessible to the public by posting them on their personal Web sites:

It is the public responsibility of Members of Congress to provide this information to the people they serve in an easy-to-use format. In addition to a comprehensive database, Members should post their voting records on their personal Web sites in an easily searchable format.

Most offices cite THOMAS as a source for this information. Thomas is a source for roll call votes, and using it to compile a voting record is both time-consuming and confusing.

As of now, only two Members of Congress -- Reps. Frank Wolf (R-Va.) and Christopher Shays (R-Conn.) -- have posted their voting records for their constituents.

With today's technology, an easily searchable database of votes is a simple task to accomplish. By taking the small amount of time necessary to make the database of congressional voting records more easily searchable, Congress will be taking a necessary step toward improving the way in which they serves their constituents. In turn, voters will have the information they need to evaluate their elected officials.
