Center for Constitutional Rights to Sponsor Forum on State of Civil Liberties
   NEW YORK - September 4 - One year after the events of 9-11, The Center for Constitutional Rights is issuing a report and sponsoring a forum on the State of Civil Liberties in America. The report is focused on the Bill of Rights and basic tenets of democracy and how they are being threatened in today's socio-political climate. Topics include governmental secrecy, the US Patriot Act, the assault on an independent judiciary, the unlawful and indefinite detention of thousands of Muslim men (and the denial of consular notification); the cooptation of local law enforcement into FBI interrogation of these individuals, and the assault on attorney/client privilege. The format include presentations by CCR on each of these areas and responses/perspectives by advocates community members and activists. The daylong event will begin with a press conference followed by a forum for more detailed discussion of the issues.
