Sanders Votes To Subpoena WorldCom Executives
Congressman says that corporate fraud must end
   WASHINGTON - June 27 - Rep. Bernie Sanders (I-VT), a senior member of the House Financial Services Committee, voted today to subpoena former WorldCom CEO Bernard J. Ebbers and other WorldCom executives requiring them to appear at an upcoming hearing on the financial mismanagement and corporate abuse of the former telecom giant. The stock price has plummeted and they have announced a lay off of 17,000 workers.

The Committee's hearing will be held Monday, July 8. Subpoenas will be issued to: Bernard J. Ebbers, former CEO of WorldCom; John W. Sidgmore, former CFO of WorldCom; Scott Sullivan, CEO of WorldCom; and Jack Grubman, a telecommunications analyst at Salomon Smith Barney

Sanders said, "I am getting very tired of well paid CEOs ripping off their employees, their investors and taxpayers of this country. As a senior member of the House Financial Services Committee, I look forward to meeting with the top executives of WorldCom so that we can understand what happened there, and do everything in our power to prevent this type of corporate behavior from continuing."

The hearing continues the Committee's examination of accounting problems in the telecommunications sector, and their effects on employees, retirees, and investors. WorldCom revealed that it had overstated profits and understated expenses by $3.8 billion, which allowed the firm to artificially meet earnings projections over the last five quarters.

Sanders continued, "Congress needs to protect the American people from companies and auditors who are willing, for their own gain, to deny important financial information to the public. Investors, workers and taxpayers are entitled to have a truthful account of the financial condition of a company. It is not acceptable that CEOs and auditors continue to cook the books and mislead the public about the true financial condition of the companies they work for."
