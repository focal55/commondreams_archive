Unprecedented Wave Of Grassroots Activism On Welfare Reform
More Than 15,000 People To Be Involved In Events
   WASHINGTON - May 6 - News Advisory:

In a wave of grassroots mobilization not seen in a generation, thousands of anti-poverty activists will participate in a nationwide series of coordinated events around welfare reform reauthorization. Over the next month, candlelight vigils, rallies, speeches, lobbying days and conferences will call attention to the opportunity Congress has in welfare reform reauthorization to reduce poverty in America.

"We're sending the message that the extreme welfare proposals put forth by President Bush and passed by the House will not pass quietly in the United States Senate," said Deepak Bhargava, director of the National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support and chief organizer of the Make TANF Work! campaign. "Tens of thousands of activists will create a massive swell of grassroots activity that Senators will find impossible to ignore. These activists are the people impacted by TANF reauthorization and they refuse to sit idly by while so-called moderate Democrats push unfair, unworkable welfare plans on the Hill."

This Mothers Day, May 12, grassroots organizations led by low-income people will hand-deliver Mothers' Day cards to their Senators' homes to demand action on behalf of millions of poor mothers in America. On May 20, National Interfaith Day of Action, religious leaders from many different faiths will lead simultaneous candlelight vigils of more than 100 people each in 20 major cities to demand their Senators heed the moral imperative to end poverty in America.

The National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support (NCJIS), a project of the Center for Community Change, is a coalition of more than 1000 grassroots anti-poverty groups that has been organizing since 2000 to transform TANF into a meaningful anti-poverty program. Under the banner Make TANF Work!, the National Campaign is spearheading a national effort to fashion a progressive policy and political strategy on welfare reform.

A calendar of grassroots events is below.

---
Grassroots Mobilize Around Welfare Reform Reauthorization

May 9 -- 5,000 Rally in Illinois
Springfield, Ill.
The Grassroots Collaborative, an Illinois coalition of immigrant, labor, anti-hunger and poor people's organizations will host a rally with more than 5,000 people to focus on TANF reauthorization and state budget issues.

May 12 -- Senators Get Mothers' Day Cards
Grassroots organizations of low-income people around the country will hand-deliver a Mothers' Day card to their Senators' homes to demand that senators reflect on the effects of their decisions on millions of poor mothers in America.

May 20 -- Religious Leaders Rally In 20 Cities
National Interfaith Day of Action
Religious leaders from many different faiths will lead simultaneous candlelight vigils in 20 major cities to call on their Senators to heed the moral imperative to end poverty in America.

Springfield, Mass.
More than 500 low-income leaders will attend a convention and rally co-convened by the Anti-Displacement Project and the Massachusetts Chapter of the National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support. Sens. Kennedy and Kerry have been invited to attend.

Sacramento, Calif.
Grassroots groups in California will be teaming up turn out nearly 1,000 low-income parents to make their voices heard on TANF reauthorization and the state budget crisis.

Washington, D.C.
More than 1,000 leaders from the faith community will voice their support for poverty reduction at Call to Renewal's national mobilization around TANF, "Pentecost 2002: Speaking the Truth About Poverty" on May 20-22.

May 23 -- 1K Race To The Bottom
Washington, D.C.
The National Campaign will join the Mobilization for Global Justice -- one of the groups responsible for major anti-globalization protests in Seattle, Quebec and Washington, D.C. -- to host a 1K Race to the Bottom to draw attention to the links between poverty in the U.S. and abroad.

June 1-3 -- NPA Descends on D.C.
National People's Action, a national network of grassroots organizations, will be bringing thousands to Washington, D.C. for their annual conference. One of the primary focuses of this year's event will be TANF reauthorization.

June 15 -- AFL-CIO VP Chavez Speaks At Rally
Los Angeles, Calif.
The Los Angeles Chapter of the National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support will be joining with Linda Chavez-Thompson, vice-president of the AFL-CIO, to hold a 1,000-person rally on TANF.

June 15-18 -- RESULTS Anti-Poverty Conference
Washington, D.C.
RESULTS, a D.C.-based grassroots anti-poverty organization, will be hosting its international conference in Washington, D.C. RESULTS volunteers from around the world will gather to focus on TANF and other anti-poverty issues at home and abroad.

June 25 -- Bread For The World Lobby Day
Washington, D.C.
Bread for the World will host its annual lobby day, which will bring hundreds of faith-based activists to D.C. to generate support for their "Working from Poverty to Promise" TANF reauthorization campaign.

Wisconsin, Georgia & Iowa
Major grassroots mobilizations are also in the works in these three states in the months of May, June and July. Details have yet to be announced.
