Rendell Victory A Victory For The Right To Choose, Says NARAL
  

WASHINGTON - May 22 - "Ed Rendell's victory in the Democratic primary clearly demonstrates that the pro-choice message is a winning message," said NARAL President Kate Michelman. "Democratic voters rallied around Rendell and his pledge to protect and defend the right to choose, highlighting once again the saliency of this issue and its ability to move voters.

"Pro-choice voters will now have a champion of choice on the ballot in November -- and the opportunity to return the state's highest office to pro-choice hands," Michelman continued. "Rendell's victory was a crucial step toward ensuring that the right to choose remains an option for Pennsylvania's women."

"We need Ed Rendell in the governor's office to counter Pennsylvania's fervently anti-choice state legislature," said NARAL-PA Executive Director Leslie Anastasio. "Rendell's success was a tremendous victory for Pennsylvania's women, but we must ensure he wins in November if we are to protect the right to choose for future generations."

NARAL-PAC and Pennsylvania's Campaign for Choice contacted 82,000 pro-choice voters using mail and phones. During the last week of the campaign they organized grassroots outreach through rallies, literature drops, and door-to-door, Get-Out-The-Vote (GOTV), canvassing. An electoral TV ad was also aired during the final seven days on targeted cable stations in Philadelphia and Pittsburgh.
