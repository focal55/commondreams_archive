Activists to End 40 Day Fast at UN
Protesting Sanctions and Warfare Against Iraq
Participants to break fast with embargoed Iraqi dates
   NEW YORK - September 9 - oices in the Wilderness, a Chicago based campaign to end the economic sanctions against Iraq, today announced that members of a forty day fast at the UN Headquarters in NY City will break their fast, as planned, on September 10, at 11:00 a.m. with symbolic servings of embargoed Iraqi dates. The fast was undertaken as a nonviolent call for UN members and officials to break ranks with policies that have used the UN to wage economic warfare against vulnerable people in Iraq.

“In our 48 delegations to Iraq, we have seen mass suffering and death imposed on the Iraqi people by our government. As Americans of conscience, we must bear some responsibility for the infant mortality and chronic malnutrition created by U.S.-led economic sanctions. To bear witness to this suffering, we’ve shared some of the burden of economic sanctions by going without food for 40 days,” said Cey Mooney of Memphis, TN.

Members of the fast are concerned about the Bush Administration’s threats to wage a massive war against Iraq. While fasting, they have been helping to prepare for two projects that will follow the fast: initiation of the Iraq Peace Team and coordination of the Mirror of Truth Tour. Two members of the fast, Henry Williamson, (Charleston, SC), and Cynthia Banas, (Verona, NY), will prepare to join a team planning to take up residence in Iraq, before and during a massive US assault, should such an attack occur. Banas and Williamson will depart for Iraq within the next several weeks.

Cey Mooney, (Memphis, TN), and Mary Ann Muller, (New York, NY) co-coordinators of the Mirror of Truth tour, will prepare for the tour’s September 26 departure from NYC. The tour will visit sites in the US where weapons of mass destruction are developed, stored, sold and prepared for use, calling on US people to bear accountability for weapon proliferation in the US and to create nonviolent alternatives to war.

Paul Frazier, (Syracuse, NY) and Kathy Kelly (Chicago, IL), will return to their homes to continue organizing local nonviolent resistance to US warmaking against Iraq. “Cracks are beginning to open up as we see divergences, both internationally and within the Republican party regarding US buildup toward a new war,” said Kelly, who has been to Iraq sixteen times since the Voices in the Wilderness campaign began in 1996. “It’s another sign that the US government is not immovable. We shouldn’t all throw up our hands and feel that all we can do is complain. These divergences are results of pressures on the administration. Voices in the Wilderness members urge participation in the nationwide Pledge of Resistance campaign which is organizing grassroots groups to form affinity groups dedicated to education, outreach and nonviolent resistance to escalation of US warfare against Iraq.

Members of the fast will continue the last week of their vigil across from the UN headquarters at First Avenue and 45th Street from 8:00 a.m. – 1:00 p.m each weekday through September 10th.
