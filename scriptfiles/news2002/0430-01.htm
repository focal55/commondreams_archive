Sharpton's National Action Network Demonstrates Today; Protest Against Mediacom in Middletown, N.Y., 10 AM
   NEW YORK - April 30 - News Advisory:

Rev. Al Sharpton and Rev. Horace Sheffield III of the National Action Network will lead two demonstrations in New York today.

The first demonstration will take place in Middletown, N.Y., at 10 a.m. this morning (Tuesday, April 30) outside of the headquarters of Mediacom Communications Corporation, 100 Crystal Run Road, Middletown, N.Y. The second demonstration will occur this afternoon at 2 p.m. at the Sky Forum conference outside of the Waldorf Astoria hotel in New York City.

Both demonstrations are part of a National Action Network national campaign to have the nation's TV satellite and cable broadcasting industry provide more positive programming for African Americans.

Sheffield, the president of the Michigan Chapter of the National Action Network (NAN), says that NAN attempts to opened dialogue with Mediacom were rebuffed and NAN was forced to conduct a direct action protest against Mediacom.

Sheffield commented on the reasons NAN has targeted Mediacom: "We have traveled the nation protesting and negotiating to open the door of positive programming for African Americans in the satellite TV and cable broadcasting industry. Cable giants such as Comcast Cable, Charter Communications and AT & T have set the tone for corporate responsibility by agreeing to carry positive programming such as The Word Network. Mediacom CEO Rocco Commisso has not returned calls nor communicated in a respectful manner. That is why NAN has commenced direct action against Mediacom," said Sheffield.

Previous NAN demonstrations in Washington, D.C., St. Louis and Denver have attracted hundreds that marched in protest with some demonstrators being jailed for civil disobedience. The demonstrations were protests against the lack of positive programming for African Americans and the exclusion of The Word a religious network that broadcasts to millions of African Americans daily.
