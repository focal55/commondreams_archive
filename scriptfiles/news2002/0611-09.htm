U.S. Not a Rights-Free Zone, Says Amnesty International on Transfer of 'Dirty Bomb' Suspect to U.S. Military Custody
   WASHINGTON - June 11 - Amnesty International today urged the U.S. to uphold key international law principles relating to fair trials and detention following the apprehension and transfer to military custody of Abdullah al Muhajir.

"There should be no rights-free zones in the United States," said William F. Schulz, Executive Director of Amnesty International USA. "No matter how the US labels these suspects, they are guaranteed rights, whether under the Geneva Conventions or the broader safeguards of long-recognized principles of international law."

As the human rights organization has done previously in correspondence with U.S. Attorney General John Ashcroft, Amnesty International raised key areas of concern, including a seeming failure of U.S. officials to respect the presumption of innocence in public statements; the apparent limited access to counsel and the courts; and the pick-and-choose approach to the so-called "laws of war."

"U.S. anti-terrorism efforts should be sound enough to stand up to basic legal scrutiny," Vienna Colucci, international justice specialist at Amnesty International USA. "It should go without saying that suspects are informed of the charges against them and have their day in court, with counsel."

Amnesty International has called the events of September 11 crimes against humanity and has urged that the perpetrators be found and brought to justice in accordance with international law.

For a copy of the memo to Attorney General Ashcroft outlining legal concerns stemming from the war on terrorism, visit http://www.amnestyusa.org/usacrisis.
