Capitol Hill Open Discussion On Iraq and the History of Insurgency, Terrorism and Guerrilla War
Wednesday September 19, 2007
 

WASHINGTON - September 18 -

Purpose: The Congressional Progressive Caucus (CPC), co-chaired by U.S. Representatives Lynn Woolsey and Barbara Lee, and the Out of Iraq Caucus, chaired by U.S. Representative Maxine Waters, will co-host a Capitol Hill Open Discussion to review the Bush administration’s policy in Iraq, assess current status and future prospects of the insurgency, and address the need to proceed in a new policy direction to achieve redeployment of U.S. troops before President Bush leaves office.

Speaker confirmed: Dr. William Polk, distinguished senior statesman, Middle East expert and author of multiple books including the just published Violent Politics - A History of Insurgency, Terrorism, & Guerilla War from the American Revolution to Iraq.

Audience: Members of Congress and staff, leading diplomatic, military, and intelligence experts, media (including C-SPAN for nationwide broadcast), general public

Format: U.S. Representatives Woolsey, Lee, and Waters to function as facilitators. Dr. Polk to give opening remarks to be followed by discussion and questions from Members of Congress.

Date: Wednesday, September 19th

Time: 9:30 A.M. to approximately 12 Noon

Location: Cannon Caucus Room
