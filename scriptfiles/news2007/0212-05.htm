League of Women Voters Turns 87
February 14th is Always a Sweet Occasion for the League
 

WASHINGTON - February 12 - The League of Women Voters celebrates its 87th birthday on February 14. As the League honors the dedicated work of its many volunteer members over the years, and the accomplishments that have resulted, it is also looking to the future. In 2007, League members will continue their commitment to improving communities across the country. Nationally, the League continues its work of strengthening voter participation and education, as well as promoting meaningful public policy change in the areas of civil liberties, representative government, and social policy.   

“For nearly nine decades, Leagues members have engaged citizens in major public policy debates all over this country and encouraged elected leaders to support sound policies that benefit all citizens. They’ve registered and educated millions of voters. They’ve dedicated countless volunteer hours to improving their communities—and in doing so have made an incredible national and global impact,” said League President Mary G. Wilson.

“Looking back, I could not be more proud of the League’s legacy of providing quality, trustworthy and balanced resources to citizens and lawmakers alike. The League of Women Voters truly has left its footprint on American history, and I believe our democracy is stronger because of it.”

“Looking forward, I recognize that there is much work to be done. Today and in the future, the League remains committed to fulfilling its vital voter education role,” said Wilson. “Our members also look forward to affecting change by working on pressing issues such campaign finance reform, civil liberties, DC voting rights, election administration, ethics and lobbying reform, global climate change, healthcare reform and more.”
