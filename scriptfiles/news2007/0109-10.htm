MoveOn Applauds Senator Kennedy's Bill to Oppose Iraq Escalation
Group Will Press Members of Congress From Both Parties to Support Measure
 

WASHINGTON - January 9 - MoveOn.org Political Action announced its enthusiastic support for a bill introduced today by Senator Edward Kennedy (D-MA) "to prohibit the use of funds for an escalation of United States Forces in Iraq above the numbers existing of January 9, 2007."

The organization plans to put the full force of its members' energy and fundraising capability behind ensuring that Democrats and Republicans in both the House and Senate vote to pass the measure.

"Senator Kennedy is providing the leadership on Iraq that the American people asked for in November. His resolution is entirely consistent with the message Majority Leader Harry Reid and Speaker Nancy Pelosi sent to the president last week. An escalation in Iraq is the wrong way home -- it will make things worse, not better," said Tom Matzzie, MoveOn's Washington Director.

"In the coming weeks and months, MoveOn members will press Congress on both sides of the aisle to force President Bush not to escalate and to start a responsible exit. Members will work all across the country and in Washington, on the Internet and through advertising. Senator Kennedy's legislation is the best hope yet for getting America out of the Iraq quagmire. Every Senator -- especially those who would be president -- should support it," Matzzie added.
