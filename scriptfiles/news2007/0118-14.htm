League Statement on Late Night Blockage of Senate Ethics Package
League President Challenges Senate to Right Last Night’s Wrong
 

WASHINGTON - January 18 - League of Women Voters President Mary G. Wilson today condemned last night’s blockage of comprehensive ethics reforms by 46 Republican senators. The reform package includes vital reforms matching the rule changes passed earlier this month in the House that limit the influence of lobbyists and require increased transparency on the part of corporate contributors.

“This latest ploy won’t work,” said Wilson.  “The American voters were very clear last November – clean up Washington or we will vote you out of office.  Voters expect their elected representatives to solve this mess.  They don’t want late night shenanigans that kill ethics reform.”

“The American people are fed up with Congressional scandals. They voted to change the status quo—and last night’s actions under the Capitol dome show just how low the integrity and credibility of Congress and its members has sunk. “

Senate leaders plan to bring the ethics package to another vote later today. Last night’s blockage occurred when lawmakers refused to let it pass without a vote on an unrelated measure that would essentially grant the President line-item-veto power.

Last month, the League worked closely with lawmakers and other groups to support the passage of sweeping ethics rules changes in the House of Representatives. In recent weeks, the League has consistently called upon the Senate to pass equally strong measures.

“Senators have a choice to make,” Wilson concluded. “They can either face the challenge of cleaning up Congress head-on, or they can continue to waste the precious time and resources of the American people with worthless measures that do nothing to curb the rampant greed and corruption that has infected Congress. League members and voters across the country urge the honest, ethical members of the Senate to show their colleagues the path to restoring integrity to the United States Congress.”
