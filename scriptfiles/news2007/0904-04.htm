As District Files for High Court Review of Gun Ruling, Brady Center Charges Appeals Court with Obscuring Second Amendment Issue
 

WASHINGTON - September 4 - As the District of Columbia today filed its petition asking the United States Supreme Court to review a Court of Appeals ruling striking down the District's strict handgun law, the Brady Center released the latest installment in its continuing on-line critique of the ruling.

The Center's "rolling critique," Second Amendment Fantasy: The D.C. Circuit's Opinion in the Parker Case, is a comprehensive discussion, by Brady Center lawyers, of the landmark 2-1 Second Amendment ruling in Parker v. District of Columbia, handed down on March 9 of this year, in which the U.S. Court of Appeals for the District of Columbia Circuit became the first federal appeals court in history to strike down a gun law as a violation of the Second Amendment. The Brady on-line critique, in its entirety, is available at www.gunlawsuits.org.

The new installment, entitled Parker and "The People": How the Parker Court Obscured the Real Issue in the Second Amendment Debate, addresses in detail the D.C. Circuit's argument that the Framers' inclusion of the phrase "the people" in the Second Amendment itself establishes that the right "to keep and bear Arms" extends to private purposes such as hunting and self-defense and is not confined to service in a "well regulated Militia."

The Brady Center discussion exposes this argument as an effort by the Parker majority to obscure the real issue in the Second Amendment debate: What is the nature and scope of the right granted "the people" in the Second Amendment? As argued throughout Second Amendment Fantasy, there is only one answer to that question that accounts for all the words of the Second Amendment and accurately reflects its history: the Amendment guarantees to the people the right to keep and bear arms only in a well regulated militia.

The Supreme Court is expected to decide whether to take review of the Court of Appeals ruling in October. Now that the case is before the Supreme Court, it is captioned District of Columbia v. Heller.

Previous installments of Second Amendment Fantasy included Mangling Miller (about the Parker court's disregard for binding Supreme Court precedent), Decision by Eraser (showing how the court's opinion effectively "erased" the first thirteen words of the Second Amendment), and Militia Madness (on how the court distorted the nature of the "well regulated Militia" referenced in the Amendment's text).
