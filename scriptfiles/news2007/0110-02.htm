AIUSA and CCR Urge President Bush to Close Guantánamo Bay On 5th Anniversary of First Detainee Arrivals
Press Conference at Supreme Court Highlighting the Lack of Basic Rights
 

WASHINGTON - January 10 - On the 5th anniversary of the arrival of the first detainees at the U.S.-controlled detention facility at Guantánamo Bay, Cuba, Amnesty International USA (AIUSA) and the Center for Constitutional Rights (CCR) are urging President Bush and his administration to close down the Guantánamo prison at a press conference on Thursday, January 11, at 10:30 AM in front of the U.S. Supreme Court.

Speakers include AIUSA Executive Director Larry Cox, CCR Staff Attorney Gitanjali Gutierrez, and Tom Wilner of Shearman & Sterling LLP. Michael Ratner, CCR President, and Jumana Musa, AIUSA Advocacy Director for Domestic Human Rights and International Justice will co-emcee the program. In addition, there will be Amnesty International members dressed in Guantánamo-style orange jumpsuits, 50 habeas corpus attorneys who represent Guantánamo detainees and other activists in attendance.

Details:
