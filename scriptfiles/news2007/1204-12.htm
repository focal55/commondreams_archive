Ahead of Major High Court Case, Justice at Stake Report Highlights Role of Courts in Wartime
 

WASHINGTON, DC - December 4 -This Wednesday, for the third time in four years, the Supreme Court of the United States will hear arguments on habeas corpus rights of Guantanamo detainees in two separate cases (Boumediene v. Bush and Al Odah v. U.S.). Beyond issues of detention and civil liberties, these cases represent the latest chapter in a struggle over the role of courts in a constitutional system of checks and balances.

This latest review of the government’s attempts to remove court scrutiny of government conduct comes in response to the Military Commissions Act of 2006 (MCA). Despite the fact that the courts have historically been deferential to the government during war-time, the MCA strips federal courts of jurisdiction and throws off the balance of power that the framers of the Constitution put in place.

For more about the role of the courts in the struggle against terrorism, download Justice at Stake’s report “Courting Danger” available online at http://www.justiceatstake.org
