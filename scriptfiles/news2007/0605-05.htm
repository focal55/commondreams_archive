Ethics Group Asks Pelosi to Seek Rep. William Jefferson's Resignation From Congress
 

WASHINGTON - JUNE 5 - Ken Boehm, Chairman of the National Legal and Policy Center (NLPC), today made the following statement regarding the indictment of Rep. William Jefferson (D-LA):

"These indictments are no surprise. They are long in coming. There is no more luxury of time for House Speaker Nancy Pelosi. She must seek Jefferson's immediate and unconditional resignation from Congress.

Unfortunately, prosecutors have had to deal with political interference in this criminal probe by Nancy Pelosi and Dennis Hastert. These House leaders should not have criticized the FBI raid on Jefferson's Capitol Hill office, and the administration should not have agreed to seal the records that were seized. The American people side with the prosecutors and FBI agents who are trying to get to the bottom of all the corruption on Capitol Hill.

There is indeed a 'culture of corruption' in the House. Pelosi now has a chance to do something about it." END BOEHM STATEMENT

NLPC filed a 500-page Complaint with the U.S. Attorney for the District of Columbia on February 28, 2006, resulting in an investigation of Rep. Alan Mollohan (D-WV). In the ensuing firestorm, Mollohan resigned as the ranking member of his party on the House Ethics Committee. NLPC's Complaint alleges that Mollohan had 262 omissions and misrepresentations on his financial disclosure forms, and had an unexplained increase in personal wealth from 2000-2004. As a member of the Appropriations Committee, Mollohan earmarked at least $178 million to nonprofit groups he created during the same period. A grand jury has reportedly been convened in the Mollohan case.

NLPC promotes ethics in public life, and sponsors the Government Integrity Project.
