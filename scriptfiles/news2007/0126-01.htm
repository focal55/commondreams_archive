Speakers at Massive Jan. 27th Antiwar Protest Announced
 

WASHINGTON - January 26 - On Saturday, January 27th, people from every corner of the country will gather in massive numbers in Washington, DC, to protest the war in Iraq. Organized by United for Peace and Justice, the rally and march will call on Congress to listen to the voters, not Bush, and use its power to end the war now. The last three national marches organized by UFPJ each attracted between 300,000 and 500,000 people.

The pre-march rally will be held on the National Mall, between 3rd Street and 7th Street. Organizers of the protest today released the list of scheduled speakers. This list is subject to change:

In addition to Saturday's massive protest, United for Peace and Justice is organizing a Grassroots Lobby Day on Monday, January 29th. Over 700 people from 47 states have already made appointments to meet with at least 260 Congressional offices.

United for Peace and Justice's website has received more than 5 million hits thus far in January -- 650,000 on Wednesday alone.

Media professionals planning to cover the march should register for press credentials at http://www.unitedforpeace.org. For advance interviews with United for Peace and Justice spokespeople, please call UFPJ's media coordinator, Hany Khalil, at 718-637-7351.

LOGISTICS TIP SHEET FOR UNITED FOR PEACE AND JUSTICE

ANTI-WAR RALLY AND MARCH ON SATURDAY, JANUARY 27, 2007

PARKING

• Broadcast vehicles should be parked on Jefferson Drive, NW, between 3rd Street and 4th Street. All vehicles must be onsite and parked BETWEEN 6:00AM AND 7:00AM. Broadcast vehicles must be registered with UFPJ by phone before 12 pm Friday.

PRESS CHECK-IN

• Press badges may be picked up after 9:30am. No exceptions. Press check-in will be on 3rd Street between Jefferson and Madison Drives, NW, on the south side of the stage (closer to Jefferson). Your name will be checked against the UFPJ press list. If it is not there, you will be asked to present your press pass.

PLACE AND TIME

• The stage for the event will be located on the National Mall at 3rd St. between Jefferson and Madison Drives with the stage facing west.

• The United for Peace and Justice Rally will begin at 11:00am and last until approximately 1:00pm. A list of speakers and artists can be downloaded at http://www.unitedforpeace.org.

• The march will kick off approximately at 1:00 pm and proceed north on 3rd Street.

TECHNICAL NEEDS

• A mult box will be available for plug in.

• There are two risers in front of the stage for the cameras and photographers. Network cameras will be assigned locations.

• There is a press pit in the front of the stage which is 10 feet in width. Cameras and photographers are welcome there as well.

• Other than these two locations, there is no additional designated space for cameras.

BACKSTAGE

• Interviews with speakers may be conducted in an area between the backstage press area and the backstage speakers area. There is limited space in the backstage area. We have many requests for interviews, so press will not be permitted to linger. We will do our best to meet your interview requests.

• Please note that not all speakers will be available for comment. Other speakers are available for limited comment during specific times. We will try to accommodate all requests to the best of our ability.

• There are NO electrical outlets plug into backstage.

ONSTAGE

• Photographers will only be permitted on stage when escorted by a UFPJ media staff person, capacity permitting. Very few cameras will be granted stage access.

• The crowd is expected to reach its highest numbers between 12:00pm and 1:00pm.

With more than 1,400 member groups under its umbrella, United for Peace and Justice (UFPJ) is the nation's largest grassroots peace and justice coalition. Since its founding in October 2002, UFPJ has spurred hundreds of protests and rallies around the country, including the three largest marches against the Iraq war. http://www.unitedforpeace.org
