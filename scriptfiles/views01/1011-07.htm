Published on Thursday, October 11, 2001
The Mujahid and his Driver
by Isa Daudpota
 
A small car stops to pick up a young lad in white shalwar kameez and white cap who is hitching a ride. The driver, a middle-aged professional, drives daily from the western edge of town to his institute near the Presidency in the centre of Islamabad. "Where do you work?" asks the driver, after it is established that the lad is going to be a passenger for the whole journey.

'Oh, I only study," said the lad. That makes the academic more inquisitive.

Academic : "Where is your school?"

Laddie: "I am in a Madrassa in Golra, but am now going to get a loan from someone so I can pay for my food and lodging at the Madrassa."

A: "How long does it take for you to study and get a job?"

L: "I will study for 10 years and thereafter spend 3 years learning the Quran by heart. Students who put their heart into the Holy book can do it in 6 months. After I get this done I can get a job in a mosque or a seminary ."

A; "In addition to just studying religion, don't you feel like studying a skill that could get you into a profession. Let's say become an electrician or a plumber."

L: "No, that's for people who want to live *here*! I wish to die in the Jehad, and now it is so nearby, so once I pay off my loan, and it won't take more that 2 months, I will be off with the Lashkar (the fighter group) to Aghanistan."

The academic rather shaken by the reply, takes a breather to make sure he is still headed in the right direction!

But again:

A: "What does your dad do?"

L: "May his soul rest in peace, he died in the Jihad last year, as did my eldest brother. My mum now lives with my uncle in Swat? I only have a brother who works in Karachi and he supports me. Since he has not sent me money I have had to take a loan these last two months."

A: "How will you be able to fight, given that you have been studying at the Madrassa?"

L: "We have all be trained by 'Hizbullah' in Kotli, a town in northern Pakistan, for three months. We were shown how to use firearms and other master some military maneuvers. There are Lashkars leaving all the time -- 20,000 jehadis are going from my area and we expect 50,000 to join from Punjab -- and I can join any of these parties or smaller groups. We can cross over into Afghanistan easily as the border is 2000 miles long and cannot be guarded by the Pakistan military." He continues after a pause, "The real tragedy is that our leadership is afraid of the American, but this military man (Musharraf) will die disgraced, more so that Nawaz Sharif. Zia-ul-Haq [the general whose rule led to the rise of militancy in the Madrassas during the anti-Soviet struggle of the 1980s ] was martyred as he was killed by the Americans. We will win as we are on the right and no enemy of Islam can defeat us."

A: "What is your age?" trying to change the subject.

L: "I am 19, and ready to go to the next world."

The car has reached the parking lot where the academic works. After the "thank you, and God bless you," the lad leaves.

Slightly shaken, the academic enters the Institute, the entrance area filled with smartly dressed boys in jeans and girls in colorful dresses. The notices inside are all in English and there's a new one today from Western Michigan University. It informs the Institute's students that the American Embassy will still be open to offer them a visa to go for future studies in Kalamazoo.

I am the academic and this happened today - October 9, 2001

Isa Daudpota teaches in Islamabad, Pakistan
