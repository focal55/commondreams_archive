What do CommonDreams.org RSS/XML feeds do?
They gather information related to your areas of interest - Breaking News and Views - and deposit them all on your desktop. So, in effect, if you don't stop by the site, the headlines pretty much pay your PC a visit. RSS/XML feeds can update headlines on your computer software as they change through sections at CommonDreams.org. Content can fly to your desktop faster than it takes to say 'Peace in the Middle East.'

RSS (really simple syndication) is a format for distributing and aggregating all kinds of content on the Web such as news links, headlines and summaries. It is a form of XML, which means that each piece of data - a headline, introduction or description - is coded separately. In this way, software will know exactly what to do with it.

How do I read these feeds?
Once information about news items is in RSS format, RSS reader software can check for changes and updates. These headlines will appear on your screen with a summary. If you like what you see, you can always click through to the full copy on the CommonDreams.org site.
