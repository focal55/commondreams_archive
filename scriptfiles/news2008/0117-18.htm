"Climate Change Policy and Global Justice: Bridging the Gap"
MADRE Joins Activists to Emphasize Links Between Climate Change & Human Rights
 

NEW YORK - January 17 - The urgency of the climate change crisis increasingly figures in international negotiations. Yet, MADRE stresses that the perspectives of women have been dangerously ignored. A panel discussion event next week will highlight the voices of women creating community initiatives and international climate change policies that promote human rights and social justice.

WHAT: A panel discussion bringing together local and international activists on climate change, women's human rights, and global justice.

WHEN: Thursday, January 24, 2008. 10:30am-12:30pm

WHO: Speakers will include:
Sonia Smallacombe, UN Permanent Forum on Indigenous Issues
Yifat Susskind, MADRE representative to the UN Climate Change Conference
Annette Williams, Sustainable South Bronx
Mary Fridley, Women's Environment and Development Organization (WEDO)

This event is co-sponsored by MADRE, NYU Education Forum, Earth Matters, and the Women's Environmental Development Organization (WEDO).

WHERE: Kimball Hall, New York University. 246 Greene St., New York. Please come with an ID for entry, and RSVP to interadvocacy@madre.org.
