Capitol Hill Town Hall Meeting On The US Economy and The Progressive Promise Legislative Agenda
Progressive Caucus to host January 16th Capitol Hill Town Hall on U.S. Economy with U.S. Rep. Barney Frank and economist Bob Kuttner
 

WASHINGTON - January 7 -

Purpose:

Amidst the ongoing mortgage and credit crises, there are increasing signs that the U.S. economy is slipping toward recession. Oil prices topped the $100-a-barrel mark recently. The manufacturing sector contracted in December and payrolls grew less than expected, while the unemployment rate hit a two-year high of 5%. In response, the Dow Jones Industrial average lost 3.5% in the first three days of trading in 2008, while the Standard and Poor’s 500 index fell 3.86%, and the NASDAQ composite index dropped 5.57%.

The Congressional Progressive Caucus (CPC), co-chaired by U.S. Representatives Barbara Lee and Lynn Woolsey and CPC Economy Task Force Leaders – U.S. Representatives Phil Hare, Marcy Kaptur, and Jan Schakowsky -- will host a Capitol Hill Town Hall Meeting to assess the current state of our national economy and preview an action plan to advance the legislative priorities and prescriptions contained in the CPC’s legislative program in the 110th Congress and beyond, which is called The Progressive Promise. (See attached copy.) This gathering will also be co-hosted by the Campaign for America’s Future, the American Prospect magazine, and cosponsored by other organizations and coalitions.

Speakers confirmed:

+ Robert Kuttner, esteemed economist and author of his newest book entitled The Squandering of America: How the Failure of Our Politics Undermines Our Prosperity. He is also co-founder and co-editor of The American Prospect magazine, as well as a senior fellow of the think tank Demos, and a longtime columnist for Business Week and the Boston Globe

(Introduction by Roger Hickey, Co-Chair of CFA)

+ U.S. Representative Barney Frank, Chairman of the House Financial Services Committee, and designee of House Democratic Leadership to spearhead development of economic legislative package to be considered in 2008 during second session of the 110th Congress

Audience: Members of Congress and staff, interested media, and general public

Format: U.S. Representatives Woolsey and Lee to function as facilitators, when legislative questions posed by CPC Members.

Date: Wednesday, January 16th

Time: 2-4:00 P.M.

Location: Cannon Caucus Room
