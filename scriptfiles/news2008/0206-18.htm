McCain Responsible for Green Stimulus Defeat
Once Again, Senator No-Shows on Crucial Vote and Proves He is No Straight Talker
 

WASHINGTON, DC - February 6 - Friends of the Earth Action President Brent Blackwelder released the following statement in response to Sen. John McCain’s failure to show up to vote this evening on an economic stimulus package that promoted clean energy. The package failed by one vote:

“In December, John McCain pledged to voters, ‘I will clean up the planet.’ But tonight, when his presence was needed most, he didn’t show up. That is not straight talk. It is not being a friend of the environment. And it is not acceptable. If Barack Obama and Hillary Clinton were able to find time to show up—and they found time—McCain could have too. This is the second time in a matter of months that McCain’s failure to vote resulted in the defeat of a clean energy package that would have grown the economy and created jobs.

Friends of the Earth Action (foeaction.org) provides extra muscle to our sister organization, Friends of the Earth, in legislative battles affecting our environment. FoE Action and our affiliated PAC also serve as Friends of the Earth's political arm, making thoughtful political endorsements, providing direct support to candidates, and placing environmentalists in the field on critical campaigns. Friends of the Earth Action's mission is to promote a clean, healthy and just world and to ensure that we have lawmakers who will work to protect the environment.
