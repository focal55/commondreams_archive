Constitution Project Welcomes Lethal Injection Arguments in Baze v. Rees
 

WASHINGTON - January 7 - Today, as the Supreme Court hears oral arguments in Baze v. Rees, the Constitution Project welcomed legal deliberations on the most commonly used method of execution in the United States.

Virginia Sloan, president of the Constitution Project, said, "As a result of the Baze case, the Supreme Court has effectively imposed a nationwide moratorium on capital punishment, and has spurred a much-needed national debate about the death penalty itself."

Baze will be the first case since 1878 in which the Court has examined the constitutionality of a specific method of capital punishment. The case was brought by two Kentucky death row inmates who argue that a state violates the Eighth Amendment's prohibition against cruel and unusual punishment when its execution procedures create a significant and unnecessary risk of inflicting severe pain that could be prevented by the adoption of reasonable safeguards.

The Constitution Project's bipartisan Death Penalty Committee has issued "Mandatory Justice: The Death Penalty Revisited," a report finding that our country's capital punishment systems create a profound risk of convicting and even executing the wrong people. The report contains recommendations for a series of urgently-needed reforms.
