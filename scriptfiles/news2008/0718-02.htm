Fate of Consumer Safety Whistleblower Protections to Be Decided
Conference Meeting Set for Tuesday, July 22, 2008
 
WASHINGTON - July 18 - House and Senate members are scheduled to meet in conference this Tuesday to approve the final version of the Consumer Product Safety Reform Bill (H.R. 4040 and S.2663). It is expected that the future of the whistleblower provisions of this law will be decided at this time.

On July 17 the conference reached compromise on nine major items, but could not reach an agreement on the whistleblower provisions of the bill.

This Legislation is Congress’ response to the massive consumer product recalls, on everything from lead-laden children’s toys to toxic toothpaste, which had angered Americans and prompted calls for reform.

The most important aspect of this legislation is protection for whistleblowers. Other provisions of the bill, such as increasing the Consumer Product Safety Commission budget, establishing a consumer database, and requiring Inspectors General to conduct safety audits, can only be effective if employees have the freedom to tell the truth about defective and dangerous products.

Stephen M. Kohn, the President of the National Whistleblower Center issued the following statement on behalf of the NWC:

"The American Public will not be served by weakened sham reforms. If whistleblowers are stripped of protections, and can be fired and blacklisted from their jobs simply for exposing health risks to consumers, the proposed reforms will not work."
