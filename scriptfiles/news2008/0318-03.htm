Anti-War Efforts Get Creative for Iraq War Anniversary
Cost of War Drives People Into The Streets with Colorful Costumes & Message of Peace
 

WASHINGTON, DC - March 18 -After 5 years of anti-war marches, rallies and lobbying, with nearly 4,000 US military dead, most in Iraq living on 2 hours of electricity a day, and a sinking economy, thousands of peace activists are converging this week on Washington, D.C. Over the next 2 days the women of CODEPINK will lead provocative, creative street actions to restore the Constitution and condemn war profiteers and Congress for buying Bush's war. The anti-war movement is ratcheting it up a notch with a call for massive direct action in Washington D.C. and hundreds of cities across the country.

TUESDAY MARCH 18th, 'Restore the Constitution' Day with CODEPINK

WHEN/WHERE: 11am press conference, National Archives -700 Pennsylvania NW

WHO: Medea Benjamin, CODEPINK and Colonel Ann Wright, a retired United States Army colonel and official of the U.S. State Department, will discuss the importance of Restoring and reinvigorating the Constitution after 5 years of war.

WHEN/WHERE: 12-2pm "Restore the Constitution" parade on Constitution Ave. 6th and Constitution Ave NW (across from the New Newseum)

WHAT: CODEPINK women will hold various government agencies accountable for their involvement in the war. With a visually vibrant parade down Constitution Avenue, CODEPINK will visit the Justice Dept, the IRS and end at the Capitol reflecting pool.

WEDNESDAY MARCH 19th, Wake Up America! 5 Years of War!

GREAT PHOTO OPPS

WHEN/WHERE: 8am blockade of the IRS to demand the government stop funding the war in Iraq with our tax dollars, Constitution Ave and 10th St NW

WHEN/WHERE: 2:30pm Wake Up at the White House- the mobile "Wake Up America" Bed will tour to the offices of war profiteers, media outlets and government agencies, calling attention to the institutions that try to keep America asleep.

VISUALS: Pink pajamas, pink slips and sleeping gear ~ pillows, bedding, alarm clocks, slippers, coffee mugs and our mobile "Wake Up America" bed!

"We are seeing that Americans are willing to go the distance, skip work and even spend precious time away from their families to deliver the message to the war-profiteers, the federal agencies that have destroyed our Constitution and the Congress that we are tired of their support for this war- we want them to work for peace!" says CODEPINK Cofounder Jodie Evans.

There will be more than 640 events throughout the United States to mark the fifth anniversary of the war. For interviews with CODEPINK call Dana Balicki, National Media Coordinator at (202) 422-8624 or dana@codepinkalert.org
