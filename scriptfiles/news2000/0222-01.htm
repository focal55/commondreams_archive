Recently Retired Officials Slam Federal Salmon Recovery Efforts
 

WASHINGTON - February 22 -

News Advisory:

Two recently retired high-ranking federal officials will call for the removal of four dams on Washington's Snake River to save endangered salmon at a press breakfast on Feb. 24 at the offices of American Rivers.

Edouard J. Crateau, a fishery biologist and Snake River project leader with the U.S. Fish & Wildlife Service, and Tom Kovalicky, a former National Forest supervisor in Idaho, will provide an insider's perspective on why past efforts to save the fish have failed and why removing the four dams must be the cornerstone of any salmon recovery plan.

The Clinton-Gore Administration will decide this summer whether to remove the dams.

Gov. Kitzhaber of Oregon recently became the first major public figure to support dam removal. Public hearings are currently being held on this issue across the Northwest. The public comment period closes March 31.
