Brennan Center for Justice Urges Senate to Reject Nomination of Brad Smith to Serve as FEC Commissioner
  NEW YORK - March 8 - The Brennan Center for Justice at NYU School of Law today urged Senators to reject the nomination of Bradley A. Smith to the Federal Election Commission (FEC).

The Senate Rules Committee will begin hearings on the nomination today.

The Brennan Center first raised concerns about Smith when Republicans named Smith their choice to join the FEC in early 1999.

Smith is "a wholly inappropriate choice to serve as a Commissioner to the FEC," Brennan Center President E. Joshua Rosenkranz wrote in a letter to Senators. "While FEC Commissioners must administer and enforce the nation's campaign finance laws, Smith has a long, documented history of vigorously opposing them. A decision to appoint Smith as an FEC Commissioner would be tantamount to voting to repeal the Federal Election Campaign Act. In short, Smith would be a disastrous FEC Commissioner."

The Brennan Center has also issued a report detailing the facts about Smith and his self-described "radical" position on campaign finance laws and their enforcement. Smith has become, the Brennan Center points out, "the single most aggressive advocate for deregulation of campaign finance in the academy today." For example, in a recent Wall Street Journal op-ed, Smith summed up his feelings about the nation's federal campaign laws: "The most sensible reform is a simple one: repeal of the Federal Election Campaign Act."

"Smith's tirades about campaign finance laws have strayed so far from the mainstream that even he had trouble believing the nomination was serious," Rosenkranz wrote in a Roll Call op-ed published on March 6. "The bottom line is that anyone who cares about enforcing the law as it is written - not to mention anyone who cares about meaningful reform - should oppose Smith."

For more information contact the Brennan Center for Justice at 212-998-6736. Copies of the Brennan Center letter to Senators, the Center's report on Smith, and Rosenkranz' recent op-ed on Smith are available online at http://www.brennancenter.org.
