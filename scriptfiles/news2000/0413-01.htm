Record Number of BP-Amoco Shareholders Support Arctic Refuge
13.5% Vote in Favor of Canceling Drilling Plans
   WASHINGTON - April 13 - An unprecedented 13.5% percent of BP-Amoco shareholders voted in favor of a resolution to Save America's Arctic from oil drilling at the company’s annual meeting today in London.

"This vote shows that investors are starting to recognize that the coastal plain of the Arctic Refuge is sacred ground," said Athan Manuel, director of PIRG’s Arctic Wilderness Campaign. "More than thirteen percent of the owners of one of the largest oil companies in the world just called on BP-Amoco to save the Arctic."

The resolution calls on BP Amoco to cancel future drilling plans for the coastal plain of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge, abandon their drilling plans for the offshore oil field at Northstar, and go solar.

The BP Amoco resolution was initiated by Greenpeace and co-filed by Trillium Asset Management and U.S. PIRG.

"We call on BP Amoco to acknowledge the growing shareholder support for protecting the Refuge and cancel their drilling plans," Manuel stated. "We look forward to negotiating with BP Amoco to cancel their drilling plans."
