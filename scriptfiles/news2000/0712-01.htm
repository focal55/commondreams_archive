Rep. Lazio to Join Rep. Shays, Friends of the Earth to Urge Administration Not to Obliterate Clean Water Act
  WASHINGTON - July 12 - Reps. Rick Lazio and Christopher Shays will join Republican House colleagues including Reps. Nancy Johnson, Brian Bilbray, and others to denounce the Clinton-Gore Administration's efforts to overturn the Clean Water Act and allow companies to dump waste directly into clean waters -- streams, estuaries, wetlands -- anywhere in the country.

The Army Corps of Engineers is proposing a rule change to preserve the destructive practice of mountaintop removal coal mining, which will have broad, nationwide implications. Reps. Shays and others will be submitting public comment to the Corps on the proposed rule.

Representatives from environmental groups, including Friends of the Earth and the Earth Justice Legal Defense Fund will present several thousand postcards to be delivered to the White House later that day.

What:
Press conference to release comments on the Administration's proposed rule change to allow waste dumping in clean waterways.

Who:
Reps. Rick Lazio (R-NY), Christopher Shays (R-CT), Nancy Johnson (R-CT), Brian Bilbray (R-CA), and others, as well as representatives from environmental groups including Friends of the Earth and the Earth Justice Legal Defense Fund.

Where:
Area behind House Triangle, outside the Capitol at the foot of the East House steps.

When:
Today (July 12) at 12:45 p.m.
