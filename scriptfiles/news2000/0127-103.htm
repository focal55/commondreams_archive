David Brower & Allies to Engage Candidates in New Hampshire
  MANCHESTER, NH - January 27 -

From Seattle... to Des Moines... to Manchester
Round One: The Battle in Seattle
Round Two: The People's Challenge, Des Moines, Iowa
Round Three: The People's Agenda, Manchester, New Hampshire
 

Following on the heels of the protests against the World Trade Organization (WTO) in Seattle, an alliance of over 100 farm, labor and environmental groups issued a "People's Challenge" to the presidential candidates on January 12 in Des Moines, Iowa.

The groups asked the candidates to articulate their plans on the following issues:

1) What is your plan for the management of our federal public lands?
2) What is your plan for guaranteeing workers the freedom to organize without retaliation from corporations, and to protect workers who strike from being permanently replaced?
3) What is your plan for protecting small family farms and other small businesses in this country?

Despite promises from several of the candidates and their campaigns to response to the "People's Challenge" by January19, none did.

Thursday morning, the groups will send a delegation of leaders, led by David Brower, to engage the candidates on these and other vital issues that are threatening the planet and our democracy. They will then hold a press conference to share with the American people what they have learned about the candidates' plans.

"Many of these same organizations and leaders shut down the WTO in Seattle," said Jake Kreilick, Campaign Coordinator, National Forest Protection Alliance. "We will not be ignored by the presidential candidates. It is time for the candidates to listen to the will of the people, not the corporations."
 

"The American people realize that he who pays the piper calls the tune," said Nancy Snow, Executive Director of New Hampshire Common Cause. "As campaign spending goes up, voter turnut goes down. We're looking for leadership from our candidates to end this cynical and destructive pattern that is threatening our democracy."

"72 percent of New Hampshire voters and 76 percent of New Englanders want our national forests protected from logging, roadbuilding and other destructive practices," said John Demos, NE Representative for the Amerian Lands Alliance and Heritage Forests Campaign. "Where is the leadership from the candidates on these issues?"

Rick Katzenberg, New Hampshire Sierra Club stated, "New Hampshire is not immune from the effects of global warming. 23 out of the past 25 months, the temperature in New Hampshire has been above normal. New Hampshire's citizens have a right to demand that
each of the candidates provide not just rhetoric, but a specific plan to combat this monumental threat."
 

"The bottom line is that none of the candidates are considering what the decisions we make cost the Earth," summarized David Brower, the senior statesman of the American environmental movement.
