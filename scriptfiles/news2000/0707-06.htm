GEICO Pulls Ads From "Dr. Laura"
Insurance Giant Latest Company to Distance Itself from Schlessinger
  WASHINGTON - July 7 - In a statement finalized just late yesterday, the insurance giant GEICO has announced it will no longer permit its advertising to appear during Laura Schlessinger's radio program.

"We have decided we will not continue our advertising on the 'Dr. Laura' radio program," the statement read.

"GEICO is an equal opportunity employer and does not condone discrimination of any type directed toward any minority," the statement continued, directly linking GEICO's decision with the controversy over Schlessinger's anti-gay statements.

The statement, provided to StopDrLaura.com by GEICO's assistant vice president and director of communications, Walter Smith, points to a flood of concerned messages from consumers regarding GEICO ads during the "Dr. Laura" radio show. "Recently, we have received a great deal of feedback -- some positive, mostly negative -- regarding our advertising on the 'Dr. Laura Schlessinger' radio program. Most of the negative feedback has been from the gay and lesbian community, which finds many of Dr. Laura's comments concerning sexual orientation to be highly offensive and prejudicial."

For three weeks, the StopDrLaura.com site had urged viewers to contact GEICO. The site, which has received a flood of over 30 million hits and millions of unique visitors since its March 1 launch, contained contact information for GEICO and other companies connected with Schlessinger's program.

GEICO joins a growing list of major corporations that have distanced themselves from Schlessinger, including Procter & Gamble, United Airlines, American Express, Xerox, Toys R Us, More.com, BoxLot, and Amica Insurance.

"Corporate America has spoken: Schlessinger is bad for business," said Joel Lawson, a co-founder of StopDrLaura.com. "Paramount is the only company in America that hasn't figured this out."

GEICO STATEMENT:
Thursday, July 6, 2000

"Recently, we have received a great deal of feedback -- some positive, mostly negative -- regarding our advertising on the "Dr. Laura Schlessinger" radio program. Most of the negative feedback has been from he gay and lesbian community, which finds many of Dr. Laura's comments concerning sexual orientation to be highly offensive and prejudicial.

"GEICO is and has been an insurer for everyone. To reach all audiences, we advertise in a wide range of media. We do not endorse the views expressed by the personalities on whose shows we advertise, but we do monitor the results of these advertising efforts closely, and we take seriously the feedback we receive from our policyholders and the listening and viewing public. Having analyzed these factors, we have decided we will not continue our advertising on the "Dr. Laura" radio program effective July 1. GEICO is an equal opportunity employer and does not condone discrimination of any type directed toward any minority."
