Sierra Club Launches Its Largest Air, Ground Blitz In Presidential Race, 12 Congressional Contests
Fall Voter Education Campaign to Inform Communities about Candidates' Positions on Environment
   WASHINGTON - September 28 - On Monday, October 2, the Sierra Club will launch a month-long drive to inform Americans where their Presidential and Congressional candidates stand on the environment. With all three branches of government up for grabs and polls showing that Americans want public officials who will protect the environment, the campaign takes to the airwaves, the internet, mailboxes and the door-to-door canvassing to provide people with printed and broadcast voter guides about their candidates' environmental positions.

"Using new technology and old-fashioned shoe leather, the Sierra Club will help millions of Americans learn where their candidates stand on the environment," said Sierra Club Political Director Daniel J. Weiss. "With the Sierra Club's all-out effort, Americans won't have to look far for environmental information about their candidates."

Totaling $3 million, the October push includes $2 million for broadcast voter-guide ads and an additional $1 million for printed voter guides with side-by-side comparisons. The Sierra Club will spread the word in 12 Congressional contests and the Presidential race, with volunteers distributing voter guides door-to-door; internet banner ads and websites; TV, radio and newspaper ads; and U.S. mail. The Sierra Club's October drive caps its $8 million, year-long Environmental Voter Education Campaign.

"People are hungry for the facts about their candidates, and the environment provides a clean, bright, defining line between many candidates," Weiss said. "By turning on their TVs, logging onto the internet, or simply opening their front doors, people can learn where these candidates stand on protecting our clean air, clean water and favorite parks. Before they head to the polls, voters want to know the differences between Vice President Gore and Gov. Bush on clean water, clean air and controlling suburban sprawl."

The voter guides provide information about the issues that matter most to local residents. In Washington State, voters learn about efforts to protect salmon and wild forests. In Michigan, voter guides feature the Great Lakes and clean water safeguards. In northwestern Illinois' 17th Congressional District, spotlighted issues include factory farms and protecting the Mississippi River.

The TV ads comparing Vice President Al Gore and Gov. George W. Bush are tailored to the local issues that matter in Philadelphia, Detroit, St. Louis and Madison, WI, where those ads will air. Localized Congressional voter guide ads will air for the Senate races in Michigan, Montana, Washington and Virginia, and for the House races in: Colorado 06 (Rep. Tom Tancredo vs. Ken Toltz), Connecticut 05 (Rep. Jim Maloney vs. Mark Nielsen), Illinois 17 (Rep. Lane Evans vs. Mark Baker), Kansas 03 (Rep. Dennis Moore vs. Phill Kline), Nevada 01 (Rep. Shelley Berkley vs. Jon Porter), North Carolina 11 (Rep. Charles Taylor vs. Sam Neill), Pennsylvania 10 (Rep. Don Sherwood vs. Pat Casey), and Washington 01 (Rep. Jay Inslee vs. Dan McDonald).

More than 2 million households will receive printed voter guides either in their mailboxes or under their front doors, the latter distributed by an army of Sierra Club volunteers. Banner ads on internet sites Lycos.com and Voter.com will direct surfers to the voter guides at: http://www.sierraclub.org/voter_education/

Ads in Montana began airing last week to take advantage of high viewership during the Olympics.
