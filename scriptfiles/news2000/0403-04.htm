Statement by AFL-CIO President John J. Sweeney in Support of the Student Labor Action Project of Jobs with Justice Day of Action on April 4, 2000
  WASHINGTON - April 3 - On April 4, thousands of activist students around the nation will participate in solidarity actions to support workers who are standing up for a living wage, fair working conditions and a voice on the job.

Their actions are part of a firestorm of support for working people's issues among university students — one which is historically significant and important to today's unions.

Student activism itself is certainly not new. Students have always shown an ability to hold a mirror up to society, and force it to face the truth about its flaws. What is new is that today's students are organizing and mobilizing for workers' rights and on issues of economic justice.

The students have chosen the anniversary of Dr. Martin Luther King's death for their day of action. Dr. King died while supporting Memphis public works employees like L.C. Reed. Reed was part of a sanitation strike that began shortly after two workers were crushed in a malfunctioning dumpster. In a still-segregated South, Reed's and his co-workers' struggle grew out of the same impulses that fueled the civil rights movement's overall drive for economic and racial justice. Workers wanted a fair pay system, equal treatment, and a safe workplace, guaranteed in a union contract.

This week, students will mobilize across the nation to support workers who, like Reed, want to improve their lives by standing together. Students will rally behind another group of sanitation workers in Richmond, Indiana who are fighting the same fight as the Memphis workers, thirty years later. They will back Sedexho Marriott workers who want a voice at work — workers they see every day, serving food in their school cafeteria. They will sleep-out for a living wage for local workers, march against sweatshops, and serve lunch and coffee to university employees to express their gratitude for their service.

Today's campus activism is at the heart of today's union movement. The AFL-CIO strongly endorses this day of action, and applauds the students' efforts. Their energy, enthusiasm and idealism are an inspiration to us all.
