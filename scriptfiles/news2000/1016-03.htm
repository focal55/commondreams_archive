Here We Go Again: Proposed Chevron -Texaco Merger Will Lead to Higher Gas Prices For Consumers, More Political Clout For Big Oil
Statement of Athan Manuel, PIRG’s Arctic Wilderness Campaign
  WASHINGTON - October 16 - The proposed merger of Chevron and Texaco sets up another lose - lose - lose scenario for American consumers, democracy, and the environment.  Consumers will continue to lose from the lack of competition.  Democracy will lose, as these mega-companies will exert more and more influence over the political process. And the environment will lose as both companies  but especially Chevron  have an aggressive anti-environmental agenda. 

We urge the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) to block this proposed $35 billion merger.  It’s time to stop the oil industry merger mania.

One of the consequences of the rash of oil industry mergers has been a lack of competition  and record high gas prices  for consumers. This merger would make a bad situation even worse. 

The new ChevronTexaco would also contribute to an excessive concentration of political power in a small number of huge corporations.  The original authors of the antitrust laws sought to avoid excessive concentrations of political power by the oil industry.  That is one of the reasons Standard Oil was broken up into 34 companies in 1911.

This new oil giant would undoubtedly use its increased political clout to push environmentally dangerous drilling plans in Alaska and Florida. Chevron is actively lobbying Congress to allow oil and gas drilling in the coastal plain of the Arctic Refuge and is trying to drill the first offshore drilling well 30 miles off of Pensacola Beach in Florida. 

We call on the FTC, the Clinton Administration, and Congress to block the proposed Chevron  Texaco merger and reject these environmentally disastrous proposals.
