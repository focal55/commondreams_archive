Greenpeace Calls for Clinton-Gore to Shut Down WTI Incinerator Following Recommendation from Independent Investigation
   WASHINGTON - October 23 - Greenpeace called on the Clinton/Gore administration today to fully implement the recommendations of an independent EPA investigation and shut down the most controversial hazardous waste incinerator in the United States.

In a report released late Friday, the EPA's National Ombudsman urged the agency to stop operations at the WTI incinerator in East Liverpool, Ohio by "halting the feeding of waste to the incinerator for a period of no less than six months," and requiring incinerator re- testing and a new risk assessment. The Ombudsman said his investigation found that WTI's original operating permit was based on faulty test burn data, unreliable air monitoring and a flawed risk assessment. (1)

In its discussions with the administration, the EPA has told Greenpeace it accepts all the Ombudsman's findings but refuses to shut down the incinerator. Meanwhile, Katie McGinty, an environmental advisor to the Gore campaign speaking at a journalism conference in Michigan yesterday, said the Vice President supports all the Ombudsman's recommendations, including the shut down of the incinerator.

"Greenpeace hopes that this time Al Gore can be trusted to use his power to convince the EPA to shut down WTI," said Greenpeace campaigner Rick Hind. "Eight years ago the Vice President made similar promises. The Ombudsman's report now gives him every reason he could possibly want to keep his promise on WTI this time around."

Both immediately before and after the 1992 presidential election Vice President Gore promised a Clinton/Gore administration would block operation of the WTI incinerator, which releases highly toxic substances into the air and is located just 400 yards from an elementary school and 320 feet from the nearest home. (2) Despite Gore's promise and more than 130 violations of its permit, the WTI incinerator continues to operate.

Meanwhile, new results from Greenpeace's testing of soil samples taken from the East Liverpool school playground located near the WTI incinerator reveal dioxin levels six to nine times the national average. (3) These levels are equivalent to, or greater than, those found near a Columbus, Ohio incinerator that the EPA shut down in 1994 after widespread community opposition. The Greenpeace results also show elevated levels of lead and arsenic.

Residents of East Liverpool, who have been fighting the WTI incinerator for over a decade, are increasingly frustrated by the Clinton/Gore administration's failure to make good on its promise and shut down WTI. In an October 18 letter to the Vice President, a delegation of citizens from East Liverpool said they were headed to Washington this week to engage in civil disobedience.
