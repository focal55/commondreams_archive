Independent Expenditure Campaign for Nader Woos Democrats and Independents in 'Non-Swing States'
Unusual Political Ad Campaign is First Ever to Target Non-Swing States

"Citizens for Strategic Voting" Buys Full-Page Ads in San Francisco, Austin, Boston, Boulder, Los Angeles and NY City, Including National NY Times
 

NEW YORK CITY - October 23 - News Advisory:

WHO: Susan Sarandon, Actress; Tim Robbins, Actor; Michael Moore, Filmmaker (to be confirmed); Greg MacArthur, Businessman
WHAT: Conference Call for Reporters
WHEN: 12 Noon E.D.T., Wednesday, October 25, 2000

In an effort to encourage independents, disgruntled Democrats and McCain supporters to vote for presidential candidate Ralph Nader, a group of celebrities is announcing an advertising campaign in states where Vice President Al Gore or Gov. George W. Bush is strongly in the lead.

Citizens for Strategic Voting says voters in all but a small number of swing states can vote Nader without hurting Gore's chances of beating Bush. This is the first ever national political advertising campaign to target voters in non-swing states.

Political analysts believe that Nader could receive the necessary five percent or more of the national popular vote to win matching federal funds in 2004 if people understand that casting ballots for Nader won't help Bush to be elected.

"Why Voting for a Candidate Who Can't Win Is the Smartest Thing You'll Ever Do," the full-page newspaper ad headline reads. "And Don't Worry, a Vote for Nader is Not a Vote for Bush."

Susan Sarandon, Tim Robbins and filmmaker Michael Moore will discuss the ad campaign during a conference call with reporters on October 25, 2000.

The independent expenditure ad campaign was paid by Citizens for Strategic Voting — a group started by businessman Greg MacArthur, grandson of billionaire insurance executive John D. MacArthur (founder of the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation) and son to J. Roderick MacArthur, who created the MacArthur "genius awards". Greg MacArthur is a businessman, documentary film producer and former foreign correspondent for the Associated Press.

"People must understand that the President is chosen by the electoral college and not by the popular vote," said MacArthur. "This means that in all but a few swing states, people can follow their hearts and vote for Nader without fearing that they will help elect Bush."

"Gore and Nader can both win," MacArthur said. "Gore needs the majority of electoral college votes to become President. Nader needs at least five percent of the popular national vote to win matching funds for a potential presidential run in 2004. Both are easily achievable if people in states where either Gore or Bush is strongly ahead vote for Nader."

Citizens for Strategic Voting is running ads in newspapers read by a large percentages of Democratic and Independent voters. The papers include The New York Times (national edition), the Village Voice, the Boston Globe, the Boston Phoenix, the Los Angeles Times, the Los Angeles Weekly, the Austin American Statesman, the Austin Chronicle, the San Francisco Chronicle, the San Francisco Examiner, the San Francisco Bay Guardian and the Boulder Camera.

"It does not matter if Gore or Bush win a state by one vote or by one million votes," said MacArthur. "But a vote for Nader can invigorate democracy by creating an equal playing field for fresh new ideas."

Voters who wish to see the ads and a list of states where it is safe to vote for Nader can visit www.undergroundads.com/Nader.
