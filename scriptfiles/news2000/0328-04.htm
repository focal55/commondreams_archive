Interior Secretary Bruce Babbitt’s Plan for Visitor Management In Yosemite National Park
  

WASHINGTON - March 28 - Statement by Thomas Kiernan, President National Parks Conservation Association:

For many years it has seemed that the beast of modern traffic would kill the beauty of Yosemite National Park. But the new National Park Service plan unveiled today by Interior Secretary Bruce Babbitt promises to free the Yosemite Valley from the strangulation of automobile fumes and to restore the park to full health.

The National Parks Conservation Association has for years urged the National Park Service to abandon an anything-goes policy in regard to motor vehicle use in Yosemite National Park and to take decisive measures to protect the fragile and ineffable beauty of the Yosemite Valley. The plan outlined by Secretary Babbitt ensures that visitors today and tomorrow will find at Yosemite the same qualities that so beguiled visitors more than a century ago.

The National Parks Conservation Association backs Secretary Babbitt, Park Superintendent David A. Mihalic, and the National Park Service for the measures it has proposed at Yosemite National Park to protect park resources as well as the quality of the visitor experience. If the plan is carried out, Yosemite will become a model for parks across the nation.

The National Parks Conservation Association is America's only private, nonprofit advocacy organization dedicated solely to protecting, preserving, and enhancing the National Park System. NPCA was founded in 1919 and today has more than 400,000 members.
