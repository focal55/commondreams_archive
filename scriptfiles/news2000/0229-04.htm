Greenpeace to Host Press Briefing on Gore's and the Administration's Policies on Toxic Chemicals
  

WASHINGTON - February 29 -

News Advisory:

What:
The international environmental group Greenpeace will hold a breakfast briefing to highlight toxic hot spots in the United States and around the world. Experts will offer analysis of the Administration's flip-flop on the WTI toxic waste incinerator in East Liverpool, Ohio. WTI is a hazardous waste incinerator located 1,100 feet from a local elementary school and 320 feet from a residential neighborhood. Vice President Gore called for a halt to the incinerator after the '92 election but reversed himself right after the inauguration. The speakers will address toxic contamination in Lake Charles, La., on Native American Lands, and on U.S. military bases in the Philippines. They will also highlight the White House's apparent "bully" role in pushing European delegations to back off stronger treaty language to ban toxic chemicals.

The briefing comes as the State Department prepares to go to Bonn, Germany on March 20 for weeklong UN negotiations on a global treaty to ban persistent organic pollutants. (POPs) such as dioxin, DDT and PCBs. The administration was recently embarrassed by the leak of a communique to European countries threatening the "collapse" of the U.N. negotiations unless they weakened their position against toxic pollutants. So far, the U.S. position mirrors the chemical industry lobbyists' agenda.
 

When:
Thursday, March 2, 10 a.m.

Where:
The Old Ebbitt Grill, Cabinet Room.
675 15th St., N.W.
Washington, D.C.

Who:
Tri-State Environmental Councilwoman Terri Swearingen will speak about Gore's first broken promise to stop the WTI incinerator in East Liverpool, Ohio; Southern regional Greenpeace campaigner Damu Smith will speak out about the impact of PVC facilities on African American communities in Louisiana and a new report on the high levels of dioxin in the residents of these communities; Lands regional Greenpeace campaigner Jackie Warledo will speak about a new award-winning Greenpeace/Indigenous Environmental Network documentary on the impact of POPs on indigenous peoples; Greenpeace Toxics Campaign Legislative Director Rick Hind will speak about the U.S.
role in the POPs treaty and recent Greenpeace efforts to get the U.S. military to clean up PCB contamination at abandoned U.S. bases in the Phillippines.
