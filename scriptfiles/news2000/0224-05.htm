Statement by Taxpayers for Common Sense Director of Water Resources on U.S. Army Corps of Engineers Program Growth Initiative
  WASHINGTON - February 24 - Following is a prepared statement by Steve Ellis, director of water resources at Taxpayers for Common Sense on the recent revelations about the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers, Program Growth Initiative, a program to grow the agencies budget by 50 percent to $6.2 billion by 2005 as described in The Washington Post (February 24, 2000):

"This initiative is an outrageous grab for the taxpayer's money to benefit empire building at the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. This initiative is basically a plan to increase the agency's budget from $4 billion to $6.2 billion, a 50 percent growth in only five years. It doesn't appear, however, that this plan has any particular benefit for the nation or the taxpayer, only to build the Corps of Engineers empire.

"The Corps has several guidelines and restrictions to ensure the taxpayer receives a good return on their investment. But according to the Washington Post, the Corps' characterizes these rules as 'impediments to growth.'

"The nation is attempting to reduce the national debt. However, the Corps' ambition is to feather its nest with the same tax dollars that could be used to reduce the debt burden on our grandchildren.

"The Corps of Engineers appears to be an agency out of control. Assistant Secretary of the Army Joseph Westphal said in response to the Corps growth initiative, "I don't think (the Corps) should be in the business of searching for work. No way. We've got enough on our plate." We couldn't agree with Secretary Westphal more.

"The taxpayers place a great deal of trust in the Corps of Engineers to objectively analyze projects and only recommend construction of those that have a national benefit. However, an effort to build or grow the Corps of Engineers destroys that trust and all Corps analysis becomes questionable. A thorough analysis of all the Corps' projects should be conducted by an independent panel and any proposed Corps projects should be reviewed with great skepticism."
