Growing Economic Justice Coalition to Bring Washington Protests to New York's Niketown Tomorrow
  NEW YORK - April 24 - News Advisory:

-- Hundreds to Demonstrate on 57th Street as Union Members and Students Release New Report Documenting Nike's Use of Sweatshops Abroad

-- Great Visuals as Oversized Puppets from D.C. Demos to Make NY Debut

WHAT:
Demonstration by labor unions, students and other members of the growing international economic justice coalition outside Nike's flagship store in New York City. Unite, the nation's preeminent textile and garment workers union, will release a report containing new documentation of Nike's excessive use of sweatshop labor in China, Thailand and Indonesia. The demonstration will feature great visuals, as several oversized puppets from the Washington D.C. protests will make their New York City debut.

WHEN: Tuesday, April 25
4:30 p.m.

WHERE: Niketown
57th Street between Madison Avenue and 5th Avenue, Manhattan

BACKGROUND:
Last Fall, demonstrators descended on Seattle to demand that the World Trade Organization (WTO) be made accountable to the people of the world. Recently, union members and activists went to Washington to say that the rules for international trade and for the world's financial institutions must be made to work for working people -- not just for the wealthy elite. On Tuesday, April 25, this growing alliance of workers, students and activists will gather in New York City at Niketown to say clearly and forcefully: "Here in America's business capital, we say 'NO MORE' to a global economic system that rewards billionaires like Nike CEO Phillip Knight at the expense of exploited workers in places like China and Thailand." Sponsors of Tuesday's demonstration include Unite, United Students Against Sweatshops, the National Labor Committee and the People of Faith Network. Press coverage is invited.
