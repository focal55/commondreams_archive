Rep. Hall to Discuss His Findings from Iraq Visit
  WASHINGTON - April 20 - News Advisory

WHAT:
U.S. Rep. Tony Hall, D-Ohio, will discuss his recent trip to Iraq - the first by a Member of Congress to focus on humanitarian conditions - on Monday:

WHEN:
Monday, April 24, 10:30 a.m.

WHERE:
Room 2168, House Rayburn Building
Capitol Hill

Hall was in Iraq April 16-20, and visited hospitals, schools, clinics and water-treatment plants in Baghdad, Basra, Babylon, Samawah and Nasiriyah. He was accompanied by representatives of the Red Crescent, Red Cross, UNICEF, and others and met with other aid workers as well as Iraqi officials involved in humanitarian issues.

"I have been concerned about the humanitarian situation in Iraq for some time, and particularly troubled that the last two United Nations officials charged with running the oil-for-food program resigned. There is ample evidence that the suffering of Iraq's people -- and especially its children -- is real, and this trip let me see the people behind the disturbing statistics," Hall said. "However, the question of whether lifting sanctions would solve the humanitarian problems is less clear."

Hall first got involved in humanitarian work when he served in the Peace Corps 30 years ago. In recent years, he has focused his legislative and other efforts on fighting hunger and the other problems that affect the poor of the United States and other nations.

Text of his statement will be posted at http://www.house.gov/tonyhall on Monday, April 24.
