Greenpeace Serves The Real 'Breakfast Of Champions'
   WASHINGTON - June 9 - News Advisory:

WHAT:
Greenpeace invites the public on board its campaign ship, the Arctic Sunrise for an organic Kellogg's free breakfast, a meal free of genetically engineered foods, on the same day that Kellogg's holds its annual "so called" world's longest breakfast table in Battle Creek, Michigan, a meal chockfull of experimental GMOs.

WHO:
Greenpeace activists and community members of all ages

WHEN:
Saturday, June 10, at 10:30 A.M. (PDT)

WHERE:
The Broadway Pier, San Diego, Calif..

DETAILS:
The Arctic Sunrise, making its first voyage to the United States, marks the launch of Greenpeace's True Food Network, an intensive California based consumer campaign against Kellogg's for its double standard of offering GMO free foods to Europeans, while continuing to sell genetically engineered foods to consumers in the United States.

Greenpeace, the only organization to campaign internationally against genetically engineered food, has been enormously successful in Europe in educating the public about the genetic experiment in their food; in igniting a wholesale consumer rejection of these foods; and in forcing governments to take action against the growing and distribution of genetically engineered crops.

In Germany 250,000 consumers joined the Greenpeace True Food Network (3,000 have already joined the U.S. Network before its official launch).

In the UK, Greenpeace led consumer protests to force genetically engineered seeds into Europe, and has forced EU governments to declare a moratorium on their commercial planting.

While Kellogg's no longer feeds genetically engineered foods to European consumers, they are still dishing out this science experiment to Americans.
