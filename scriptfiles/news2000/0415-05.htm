Police Surround Non-Violent Protesters
Hundreds Arrested
  WASHINGTON - April 15 -Capping a day of police harassment against activists, at least 500 peaceful protesters were arrested this evening at 20th and K St NW. The crowd had just completed a rally for justice in the prison system. As of 8pm, activists were still being arrested.

The activists unexpectedly encountered a police blockade at which time they were surrounded and confined for "at least one hour." During the hour activists made several attempts to negotiate their release. According to multiple eyewitness reports, police refused requests by the protestors to immediately disperse and return home.

After refusing to communicate with protesters, additional police arrived on the scene – some in an armored vehicle – dressed in riot gear. Lt. Jeff Harold of the DC Police addressed the crowd with a bullhorn, informing them that they would be arrested for "parading without a permit". Only press displaying credentials were allowed to leave. Riot police then began to advance on the crowd from both directions.

"It is obvious to us that this is an outrageous attempt by the police to block our first amendment rights and to prevent as many people as possible from making their voices heard tomorrow," declared Richard Becker of the International Action Center in San Francisco, an organizer of the rally.

"Despite police attempts throughout the day to intimidate nonviolent protesters, hundreds of new activists were trained today in church basements around the city," said Ilyse Hogue of the Mobilization for Global Justice. "No matter how many of us are arrested today, we are resolved to carry our message to the streets tomorrow."
