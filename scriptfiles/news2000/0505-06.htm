AFL-CIO President John Sweeney to Address Founding Convention of National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support
March and Rally Highlighted During Chicago Conference on Poverty and Inequality
  WASHINGTON - May 5 - AFL-CIO President John Sweeney will join a thousand grassroots delegates as well as Children's Defense Fund President Marian Wright Edelman, Senator Paul Wellstone, and Representative Luis Gutierrez for the launching of the National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support which will take place in Chicago on Saturday, May 6. The new coalition will shine a spotlight on national efforts to reduce poverty and inequality — working state-by-state and community-by-community to lift wages, provide health care and child care benefits, restore benefits to immigrants and rewrite the federal welfare laws, which comes up for reauthorization in 2002.

"It is shameful that in the midst of the greatest economic boom in the history of our country, poor families and working poor families are being denied the benefits that shield them from despair," President Sweeney will say.

The group will release a comprehensive report on how low-income families in Illinois have been denied federal benefits, and then march to the State of Illinois Building in downtown Chicago to dramatize the failures of the state's KidCare health insurance program.

WHO: AFL-CIO President John Sweeney to join CDF President Marian Wright Edelman, Sen. Paul Wellstone, Rep. Luis Gutierrez and a thousand activists

WHAT: Founding of National Campaign for Jobs and Income Support — Conference, March and Rally, and Release of New Study

WHEN & WHERE: 9:30 to 11:00— Conference begins at the Chicago Hilton, 720 S. Michigan, Ave. — March to the State of Illinois Building
