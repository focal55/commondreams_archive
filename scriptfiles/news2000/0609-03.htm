Scientists to Educate Congress on National Missile Defense
Experts from Across U.S. Want Science to Guide NMD Debate
   WASHINGTON - June 9 - News Advisory:

Frustrated by the politicization of the national missile defense debate, more than 35 scientists and missile experts from 16 states will converge at the U.S. Capitol to tell lawmakers that there is no scientific basis for a deployment decision this year. President Clinton will make the decision after a third intercept test in early July and a Pentagon recommendation in July or August. The scientists will tell the President and Congress that the proposed system won't work and that the deployment decision should be based on sound science and realistic testing.

States represented by scientists in attendance are California, Colorado, Connecticut, Hawaii, Illinois, Massachusetts, Maryland, Michigan, Minnesota, North Carolina, New Jersey, New Mexico, Nevada, New York, Texas and Washington.

WHAT: Scientists Press Conference on National Missile Defense

WHEN: Monday, June 12, 3 p.m.

WHERE: Outside: US Capitol Building, House Triangle
(Rain location: US Capitol Building, Room H-137)

WHO:
-- Rep. Rush Holt (D-N.J.), Rep. Tom Allen (D-Maine) and more than 35 scientists from across the nation.

Speaking on behalf of the scientists:

-- Dr. Michael Jones, physicist at the University of Hawaii

-- Roy Danchick, mathematician and former employee of TRW, a major NMD contractor

-- Dr. Joseph Lach, physicist at Fermi National Accelerator Lab in Chicago

-- Dr. Lisbeth Gronlund, research fellow, MIT Security Studies
Program; senior staff scientist, Union of Concerned Scientists
