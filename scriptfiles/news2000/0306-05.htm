In 1999, Voters Passed 90 Percent of Open Space Ballot Questions
   WASHINGTON - March 6 - As Californians prepare to vote tomorrow on a $2.1 billion measure to fund open space protection and public parks, the Land Trust Alliance has prepared a report that shows that voters elsewhere in the country gave overwhelming approval to such issues in 1999 elections.

The Land Trust Alliance (LTA), a nonprofit organization dedicated to helping the nation's 1,200-plus land trusts protect open spaces, found that 101 ballot questions on open space protection faced voters last year, despite 1999 being an "off-year" for elections, with no Congressional or gubernatorial races scheduled. Ninety percent of these -- 91 referenda -- were passed.  

Open space funding ballots had similar success in 1998 when voters passed 124 of 148 ballot questions, or 84 percent.

Many of these ballot measures were explicit tax increases to fund open space protection. In 1999, referenda committed more than $1.8 billion in public funds for open space preservation and parks.

A year earlier, referenda raised at least $8.2 billion for the same purposes.

"We hope that California voters, who have often been leaders on political and social issues, follow much of the rest of the country and pass propositions on the ballot tomorrow to fund open space and parks, especially the question protecting lands adjacent to rivers, streams, lakes and the coast," noted LTA President Jean Hocker.

The Land Trust Alliance, based in Washington, D.C., promotes voluntary land conservation and strengthens the land trust movement by providing the leadership, information and resources land trusts need to conserve land for the benefit of communities and natural systems.

Headquartered in Washington, D.C., LTA has regional programs in the Northwest (Seattle office), New York (Saratoga Springs office) and the Southwest (Grand Junction, CO). Local and regional land trusts have protected more than 4.7 million acres of open space, working hand-in-hand with landowners interested in voluntary land protection.
