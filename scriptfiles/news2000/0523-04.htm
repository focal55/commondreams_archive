Bush Arms Control Statements Mutually Inconsistent
  

WASHINGTON - May 23 - "Republican presidential candidate George W. Bush today has outlined mutually incompatible goals for his administration," charged Council for a Livable World in a statement released today.

John Isaacs, the Council's president, lauded Bush's advocacy of reductions of nuclear weapons and taking some of these weapons off alert status -- although details of the proposals are lacking.

"However," Isaacs noted, "deploying a national missile defense will surely crush hopes for the positive arms control steps that Bush has proposed."

Russian leaders have made it clear that if the United States proceeds with missile defense deployment, it will stop reducing nuclear weapons, build new missiles with multiple warheads, end cooperation with the United States in safeguarding and eliminating its arsenal of nuclear weapons and materials, and increase military cooperation with China.

"Pursuit of a national missile defense that has not been demonstrated to work and that will cost from $60 - $120 billion is a policy of ideological delusion," stated Isaacs.

"George W. Bush wants to have it both ways. In a campaign speech he can; in the real world, it will not be possible."
