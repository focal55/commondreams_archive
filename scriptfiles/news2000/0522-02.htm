Coalition Announces Campaign to Increase Access to Women's Reproductive Health Care
  

WASHINGTON - May 22 -

News Advisory:

Coalition Of Women's, Health, Religious & Pro-Choice Organizations To Announce Multi-Year, Grassroots Organizing & Education Campaign To Increase Access To Women's Reproductive Health Care

A five-year relentless legislative assault on the availability of reproductive health care has created an access gap making even some basic medical care inaccessible to many women -- especially low-income women, young women and women of color. The Campaign for Access and Reproductive Equity (C.A.R.E.2000), a new initiative uniting hundreds of national and local organizations from a broad range of disciplines, will launch a counteroffensive on Wednesday, May 24, during a news conference in the Lisagor Room of the National Press Club at 9 a.m.

C.A.R.E.2000, a two-year grassroots, public education project of the National Network of Abortion Funds, will recruit, train and empower women across America to reduce barriers to health care and increase equality of reproductive health care services for all women. C.A.R.E.2000 will address an array of the factors contributing to the inequitable medical care for women of color, young women, and low-income women, including health care access, welfare reform, and sexuality education. The Washington news conference launches the project in the District and Chicago. C.A.R.E.2000 will roll out in three additional states in July.

Among the speakers at the news conference will be three women sharing personal stories about their difficulties accessing reproductive health care. Researchers from the Alan Guttmacher Institute will present new data on the difficulty low-income women face accessing abortion services.
