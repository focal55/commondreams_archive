House Appropriations Turns Its Back On World's Poorest
  WASHINGTON - June 27 - The following was released today by Jubilee 2000/USA:

Today's 32-21 defeat of the Pelosi amendment in the House Appropriations Committee condemns the world's children to poor health and a future without hope. The amendment would have restored funding for poor country debt relief instead of the $69 million that was in the foreign operations bill, an amount far short of the $435 million request to fund the United States share of the Cologne Debt Initiative through 2001.

"Our legislators have once again turned their backs on the 19,000 children that die every day as a result of debt. Where is the compassion in this conservatism? Now, when the United States is expecting an enormous surplus, is the time to take action and let the people in these heavily indebted countries get on with the business of caring for their own people," said Dan Driscoll-Shaw, National Coordinator for Jubilee 2000/USA. "It is appalling that the committee could earmark $1.3 billion for military aid to Colombia and leave the poor behind. The faith community supports debt relief and will remember this vote on election day," Driscoll-Shaw added.

Debt cancellation has the potential to:

-- Save 21 million children from disease and hunger in Africa

-- Preserve the environment. Debt induced poverty leads to greater exploitation of natural resources, contributing to global warming and general environmental degradation

-- Promote growth in the US and abroad by lifting the burden of debt from the economies of impoverished countries

-- Increase global security. Debt creates social unrest and war.

Debt cancellation can make an immediate difference in the lives of those living in the world's poorest countries. In Uganda, resources freed from debt relief combined with other sources have led primary school enrollment to double in just three years.

The campaign is inspired by the scriptural idea of the Jubilee Year: periodically, every 7 x 7 years or 50 years, debts are forgiven and slaves set free, and "liberty is proclaimed throughout the land". Jubilee is a time to apply self-righting mechanisms for restoring balance to society.
