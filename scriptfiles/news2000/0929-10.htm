Ralph Nader And Winona LaDuke To Lead Thousands In Super-Rally To Open The Debates
Phil Donahue, Michael Moore, Howard Zinn to Speak at Highlight Event
   WASHINGTON - September 29 - Ralph Nader will storm Boston with a fourth super rally Sunday, Oct. 1 that is expected to draw thousands of people who will demand Nader's inclusion in the presidential debates.

The event will distributed LIVE on satellite, Sunday, October 1st, from 13:30 to 17:30 Eastern, on GE3, Transponder T18.

The Boston rally is the next in a series of Nader super rallies that have drawn the largest crowds of any presidential campaign events this election year. Last week, more than 12,000 people paid to hear Green Party presidential candidate Nader and vice presidential candidate Winona LaDuke speak at a rally in Minneapolis, Minn., and more than 10,000 showed up to paid events in Seattle, Wash., and Portland, Ore.

Free campaign events with Al Gore and George W. Bush are drawing crowds one third that size.

The rally will take place just two days before the first presidential debate sponsored by the bi-partisan Commission on Presidential Debates is scheduled to take place at U-Mass Boston. The commission has yet to invite Nader to participate in the debates, despite the fact that he is on the ballot in 44 states and polls show that the majority of Americans want to see him in the debates. Nader plans to be in Boston on Oct. 3 on the day of the first debates.

Nader's lawsuit against the corporate financing of the presidential debates has been granted an expedited hearing on Oct. 5 in the First Circuit Court of Appeals.

The rally begins at 2 p.m. at the Fleet Center. Doors open at 12:30 p.m. and tickets are $10 each.
