90-Year-Old Reform Crusader Approaches Washington as Campaign Finance Reform Debate Continues
  WASHINGTON - February 25 - News Advisory:

Doris "Granny D" Haddock is now just 20 miles from the successful completion of her transcontinental walk for campaign finance reform. She started her walk on January 1, 1999! Over the past 14 months this amazing woman has walked more than 3,200 miles through 11 states in an effort to show that Americans care deeply about the need to reclaim our elections and our democracy from the special interests that have it in a stranglehold.

A woman of limited means, Haddock has relied on the kindness and generosity of supporters and strangers alike to sustain her. Hundreds have opened their homes, restaurants, classrooms and town meetings to her, providing her with food, shelter, and even medical attention when necessary. Thousands have come to walk with her and show their collective outrage with the corruption that is so commonplace in today's system. She has also participated in campaign reform events at the invitation of both Bill Bradley and John McCain.

Haddock is walking the remaining miles on the towpath of the historic C&O Canal along the Potomac River. She will arrive in Washington on Tuesday, Feb. 29, when she will walk the final five miles from Arlington National Cemetery to a rally on the East Steps of the Capitol at 12:30 p.m.
