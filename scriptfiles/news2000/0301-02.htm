'Polar Bears' Set Up Protest Camp at BP Amoco's Chicago Headquarters
   CHICAGO - March 1 - The following was released today by Greenpeace:

A group of "polar bears" (Greenpeace activists) have established a protest camp at BP Amoco headquarters to highlight concerns about the company's role in the global warming that is destroying polar bear habitat.

The new camp rose two days after Greenpeace established Ice Camp Sirius on the frozen Arctic Ocean near the site where BP Amoco intends to build its controversial Northstar offshore oil facility. If allowed to proceed, the Northstar project will open a new frontier for oil, the burning of which is the primary cause of global warming.

"By setting up camp at company headquarters, Greenpeace is confronting BP Amoco executives with the impact of their actions," said Greenpeace climate specialist Iain MacGill. "If they don't stop drilling for and burning new sources of oil, global warming will become much worse." Already, global warming is having a profound effect on the polar regions. "Polar bear populations are already under severe stress because their hunting grounds are literally melting away," continued MacGill.

Both the Alaska and Chicago Greenpeace camps are powered by wind and solar energy sources-an overt challenge to BP Amoco to make good on its claim that it will push ahead with solar power. From their camp near the Northstar site, Greenpeace activists will keep close watch on Northstar activities. The facility calls for a dangerous, untested sub-sea pipeline that exposes the fragile Arctic environment to a one-in-four chance of a major oil spill.

"Greenpeace is challenging BP Amoco's claim that it's concerned about the fragile Arctic ecosystem and global warming," added MacGill. "Proof of that concern would be pulling out of Northstar and putting the company on a pathway to solar power."

Greenpeace also will use the two camps as platforms to gather support for a BP Amoco shareholder resolution that calls on the company to pull out of Northstar and reinvest the savings in its solar division. The resolution will be voted on at the company's upcoming annual general meeting April 13.
