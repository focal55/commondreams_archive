Nader Denounces Efforts To Block Low-Power FM Stations
   WASHINGTON - October 26 - Ralph Nader today denounced the big radio interests that are lobbying to block efforts to expand low-power FM stations as community radio outlets.

Nader said the effort led by the National Association Broadcasters would stifle the voices of local organizations, schools, churches and others who do not have access to the commercial stations. Nader said the low power stations could be a tremendous boost to democracy throughout the nation. He noted that the stations are being supported by the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights, the National Education Association, the National Association of Evangelicals, and the National Council of Churches.

Nader said an op-ed by Federal Communications Commission Chairman William Kennard's in the Washington Post of October 23 was "right on the mark" in stating that the efforts to block the low-power stations was not about ideology, but about money.

Nader said that the Speaker of House Dennis Hastert and Senate Majority Leader should take steps immediately to end the closed door maneuvering in the Congressional appropriations process to wipe out the expansion of the low-power stations.

"This is a real test of the Congressional leadership-whether they will side with communities and local groups who need the low power radio stations or whether they will side with the money of the big commercial broadcasters," Nader said.
