Communities Across America to Stand for 'Building Safer and Healthier Communities for All Children'
  WASHINGTON - May 31 - Hundreds of thousands of people across America will unite on June 1, 2000 for the fifth annual Stand For Children Day.

More than 2,000 local events are planned across the nation focus on the theme of "Building Safer and Healthier Communities for All Children." Additionally, more than 200,000 people will participate in Cyber Stand, the online education and action component of Stand For Children Day 2000.

Communities are planning violent toy trade-ins and violence prevention workshops, rallies to encourage decisionmakers to make children and families a priority, forums on children's health needs, playground builds and playspace renovations, events honoring local child care providers, and a variety of other activities. All events are listed on Stand For Children's web site at www.stand.org. A few examples are:

-- In Chicago, Ill., a variety of organizations are working together to organize the Stand For Children Festival of Hope which will be attended by more than 1,000 people. In addition to entertainment and educational activities for children, there will be information on a variety of health and safety issues. Eligible families will be able to sign up for KidCare, Illinois' State Children's Health Insurance Program at the event.

-- In Columbia, S.C., more than 4,000 people will attend "Stand For Children: Putting the Pieces Together," a statewide rally to call for increased services for children and families. Each county's First Steps to School Readiness program is bringing a decorated piece of a quilt that shows what their county is doing for children. The pieces will be pinned together to make an outline of South Carolina.

-- In Los Angeles, Calif., UCLA staff and students are holding a violent toy trade-in for hundreds of children. They have collected used children's shoes to represent the number of children killed by gun violence in California in one year. Participants will learn about ways to end violence in their community, write their legislators about ways to decrease the level of violence, and sign up for California's low-cost health insurance program.

-- In Philadelphia, Pa., more than 4,000 people will participate in the Stand For Children Safety, Health and Literacy Fair. There will be pavilions for health and safety issues with information on issues such as poison control, child abuse prevention, immunization, and Pennsylvania's Children's Health Insurance Program. The School District is bringing students in kindergarten through 2nd grade to the Fair.

"Cyber-Stand For Children" will take place from May 22 through June 9 at www.stand.org. More than 200,000 people will participate and learn more about the issues affecting the safety and health of children and families, send cyber-letters to Members of Congress, share their thoughts about issues affecting children, take the Stand For Children pledge, and find out how to take action for children locally and nationally.

The first Stand For Children Day in 1996 drew more than 300,000 people to the Lincoln Memorial in the largest demonstration of commitment to children in U.S. history. In 1997, 700 events focused on the health issues, and encouraged legislators to pass the Child Health Insurance Program. In 1998, 1,200 events focused attention on issues related to child care, and helped pass the Kerry/Bond amendment. In 1999, the theme was "Ready to Learn, Ready to Succeed," and 1,700 events focused on issues affecting children's ability to achieve in school.

------ Stand For Children is a nonprofit, nonpartisan membership organization organizing a powerful citizen voice to give all children the opportunity to grow up healthy, educated, and safe. Stand For Children has members and Chapters throughout the country which have completed or are undertaking hundreds of initiatives for children.
