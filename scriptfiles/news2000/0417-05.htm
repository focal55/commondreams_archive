Common Cause Calls For Release Of "LaBella Report"
  WASHINGTON - April 17 - In a letter sent today to Attorney General Janet Reno, Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger called for the public release of the so-called "LaBella Report" - a memorandum written by Charles LaBella, the former head of the Justice Department's Campaign Finance Task Force.

The memorandum, written in 1998, reportedly recommended that the Attorney General seek the appointment of an Independent Counsel to investigate charges that the parties and their presidential candidates were engaged in an illegal scheme to raise soft money in the 1996 election.

The Common Cause letter contends that releasing the report will be an important addition to the ongoing debate about the role of soft money in the political process.

"The public interest would best be served by opening to public scrutiny the factual and legal analysis that led to Mr. LaBella's recommendations," Harshbarger wrote.
