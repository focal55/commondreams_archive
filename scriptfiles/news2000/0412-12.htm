Protesters Celebrate Victory, Global South Speaks Out
Rally Tomorrow to Celebrate Starbucks' Fair Trade Deal; Next Target – The Gap
   WASHINGTON - April 12 - Thousands of protesters already gathered here will celebrate their first victory Thursday, days before the massive rally planned for April 16th. Starbucks has agreed to sell Fair Trade Certified coffee, helping to end the virtual enslavement of some coffee producers. Protesters now turn their attention to sweatshop labor contracted by The Gap. In addition, action and protest continues across the capitol.

Fair Trade Victory Rally; On to The Gap:
Demonstrators will turn protest into celebration in their fight for fair trade. Speakers will connect demands for corporate responsibility with opposition to the I.M.F. and the World Bank. Then they will continue the campaign against sweatshops at a local Gap store.
When: 10:30 a.m., Thursday, April 13
Where: Dupont Circle (within two blocks of three Starbucks outlets)
More info: Deborah James, Global Exchange, (510) 290-0573

Carlos Chen, World Bank Massacre Survivor, Speaks Out on Closed-off Street:
Carlos Chen survived a massacre of 400 Guatemalan indigenous people – including his wife and two children – who resisted the World Bank-sponsored Chixoy Dam. Now Chen is in Washington to bear witness against the World Bank on its home turf. He'll speak at a forum on the World Bank at 10:00 a.m. at 1525 Newton St. N.W., then again outside the World Bank, in an area which D.C. Police have declared off-limits.
When: 5:30 p.m. , Thursday, April 13
Where: Park in front of World Bank, 18th St. & H St., N.W.
More info: Dekila Chungyalpa, (202) 783-7400; dekyl@starpower.net

Voices from the Global South Speak Out Against Structual Adjustment:
Activists from Africa, Asia, Latin America and the Caribbean bear witness to the structural havoc wreaked on the Global South by the World Bank's and I.M.F. structural adjustment policies.
When: 7:00 pm, Thursday, April 13
Where: Shiloh Baptist Church, 1500 9th St. (9th and P)
More info: Neil Tangri, (202) 387-8030, cell (301) 602-7653

People's Assembly/Students Emergency Press Conference; Steelworkers Vigil:
STUDENTS strike back with a press conference against George Washington University's shutdown of the University and trampling free speech in the process—from April 14-18. When: 10:00 a.m. at 23rd & I St., N.W. More info: Leslie Byster, (408)420-7050
STEELWORKERS protest proposed change in China's trade status! Union workers and supporters join U.S.W.A. president Gary Becker in a candlelight vigil against Permanent Normal Trade Relations status for China. 7:00 p.m. at Lafayette Park across from the White House.
