House Meets President's Request On Debt Relief
Waters Amendment Passes House of Representatives, Conference with Senate to Follow
   WASHINGTON - July 13 - The House of the Representatives today took a giant step towards relieving the debt burden on the developing world by passing an amendment offered by U.S. Representative Maxine Waters (D-CA-35) to provide a total of $225 million for debt cancellation in FY2001.

"This is a real shot in the arm for the debt relief campaign," said Dan Driscoll-Shaw, National Coordinator for Jubilee 2000 USA. "It shows that lawmakers in the House are truly hearing the call of the poor and are ready to lead efforts to help. It also shows members of Congress are feeling the pressure of Jubilee 2000 supporters, many of them members of religious groups from across the US, who have called and written over the past year. These supporters will keep up the pressure." Earlier this week Jubilee 2000 USA delivered to Congress 500,000 signatures on a petition for definitive debt cancellation.

Earlier this year the Senate vote to appropriate only $75 million for debt cancellation. The two chambers now conference on the final figure.

"It is our great hope that the Senate will follow the House's lead and elevate the final figure. We are losing 19,000 children a day in the poorest indebted countries, that is not only inexcusable, but more importantly, preventable," added Driscoll-Shaw.

Jubilee 2000/USA is part of the international coalition of religious, environmental, human rights and labor organizations, working for the cancellation of the debt of the world's most impoverished countries in this Jubilee Year of 2000.
