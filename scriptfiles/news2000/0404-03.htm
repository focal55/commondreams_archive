Common Cause, Democracy 21 File Complaint In New York Senate Race
Complaint Alleges Campaigns of Hillary Clinton and Rudy Giuliani Engaging in Illegal Soft Money Scheme With Their Parties
   WASHINGTON - April 4 - Common Cause and Democracy 21 today filed a complaint with the Federal Election Commission (FEC) alleging that the U.S. Senate campaigns of Hillary Clinton and Rudolph Giuliani are engaged in ongoing illegal schemes to raise federally illegal soft money and inject it through their political parties into their Senate races. 

The Clinton and Giuliani campaigns have set up so-called "Victory Committees," which are "joint fundraising" committees sponsored by their Senate campaign committees and the Democratic Senatorial Campaign Committee (DSCC) and the National Republican Senatorial Committee (NRSC) respectively. 

The complaint points out that the names of the joint fundraising committees, the New York Senate 2000 Committee and the Giuliani Victory Fund, make clear to donors that their contributions will aid specific Senate candidates, namely Clinton and Giuliani. 

The complaint charges that through these "joint committees" Clinton and Giuliani are directly raising contributions to be spent to support their Senate races in amounts far in excess of the federal legal limits and from sources, such as corporations, that are prohibited by law from giving in connection with federal campaigns.

The funds will then be channeled to state parties to be spent on TV ads and other communications promoting the Clinton and Giuliani candidacies and/or attacking their opponents, according to the complaint. Such expenditures have already occurred in the Clinton campaign. 

Unless these illegal schemes are stopped, the complaint states, they threaten to inject millions of dollars of federally illegal campaign contributions into the New York Senate race.

The problem, furthermore, is not just limited to the New York Senate race and will be widespread in Senate races in 2000 unless action is taken to prevent the practice, according to the complaint. 

"Under this 'joint fundraising' scheme, according to the complaint, "federal candidates are and will be systematically raising soft money directly for their own campaigns, and using their national and state party committees as pass-through conduits to launder the money into their federal campaigns. 

"This scheme institutionalizes the practice of federal candidates making direct solicitations for soft money to be used in their campaigns, and systematizes a direct giving relationship between soft money donors and federal candidates, in a manner that plainly violates the spirit and letter of the federal election laws," the complaint states. 

"What we're doing today has nothing to do with reforming campaign finance laws," Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger said. "What we're doing is calling on the Federal Election Commission to enforce what is already the longstanding law of the land by keeping illegal money from federal elections." 

"In fact," Harshbarger said, "one would have to be completely devoid of common sense to look at the Clinton and Giuliani campaigns and not realize that these laws are being violated."

"Everyone knows precisely what is going on here," Democracy 21 President Fred Wertheimer stated. "The Clinton and Giuliani campaigns are thumbing their noses at the federal campaign finance laws and are treating the voters of New York as if they were fools."

"With these 'joint fundraising' committees and this concerted scheme, the old fig leaf that soft money is being raised and spent by political parties for 'party building' purposes and issue discussion' has been dropped, even as a pretense," according to Wertheimer. 

The complaint asks the FEC to investigate the allegations, declare that the parties named in the complaint have violated federal campaign finance laws, enjoin them from further violating the laws, and impose appropriate sanctions. 

The full text of the complaint is enclosed, as are statements from Wertheimer and Harshbarger, and a letter to Attorney General Janet Reno asking for a Justice department inquiry into the matter.
