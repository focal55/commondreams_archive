Puppet Procession Planned for Dupont
   WASHINGTON - April 14 -Drawing attention to various egregious acts of the International Monetary Fund and World Bank, activists dressed as giant puppets will perform and pantomine several examples of these multi-lateral financial institutions' destructive economic development activity.

The hand-made puppets, 40 feet in height, will present the cases of Guatemala, India and Argentina. They will show how the development paradigm of the IMF and World Bank emphasize profits over people and push for industrial development – regardless of the costs.

In both Guatemala and Argentina hydroelectric dam projects were responsible for the dislocation of thousands of indigenous peoples. In India, the IMF and World Bank was directly responsible for promoting coal-fired power plants. These projects ignored traditional Indian reliance on alternative energy sources such as cow dung. What all of these projects reveal is that the World Bank and IMF do not promote sustainable development as they have claimed, but instead they force a blueprint of industrial growth that is not only harmful to the environment but to cultures as well when people dislocated are propelled into poverty.

The puppets will gather at 5:30 PM at Dupont Circle.

The parade and performance will begin at 6 PM

Do not miss this great visual event!! The puppets will be available for photos and several peoples the South will be present and available to give testimonials of their own negative experiences with IMF and World Bank projects.
