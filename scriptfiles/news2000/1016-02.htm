Sierra Club Contrasts Gore and Bush at Debate
Texas Air Quality Expert Will Address Pollution Issues Before St. Louis Debate
  ST LOUIS - October 16 - Before and during the debate tomorrow between George W. Bush and Al Gore, the Sierra Club will highlight the environmental track records of both Presidential Candidates.

"When it comes to protecting the environment, there is a stark contrast between Al Gore and George W. Bush," said Dr. Neil Carman, director of the Texas Clean Air Project. "People are hungry for the facts about their candidates. Before they head to the polls, voters want to know the positions of Al Gore and George Bush on clean water, clean air and protecting our wilderness areas."

On Tuesday, October 17, 2000, Dr. Neil Carman, an expert on Texas air quality, will join fellow Texans who are fighting toxic pollution at a joint news conference with the Sierra Club and the League of Conservation Voters. The news conference will be at 1 PM at the Holiday Inn Forest Park, 5910 Wilson Avenue. The organizations plan to voice their concerns about Governor Bush's environmental record in Texas.

On Tuesday afternoon, volunteers will distribute detailed voter guides on the Bush and Gore environmental records. People who want to learn more about the candidates' records on the environment should log onto www.sierraclub.org/voter_education.

WHAT: Texans Talk about Bush and Gore's Enviromental Records
