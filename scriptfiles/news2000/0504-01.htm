Building Blocks for Youth Commends Leadership Conference on Civil Rights
  

WASHINGTON - May 4 -

STATEMENT BY MARK SOLER
PRESIDENT, YOUTH LAW CENTER
DIRECTOR, BUILDING BLOCKS FOR YOUTH

The Building Blocks for Youth initiative, a new national initiative to promote a justice system that is both fair and effective, commends the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights for the release of their report, "Justice on Trial." As the report makes abundantly clear, despite the fact that minority communities in America are making progress in accessing educational and economic opportunities, in the criminal justice arena racial inequality is growing. Unless addressed these injustices will continue to threaten the hard-fought civil rights victories of the last fifty years.

As "Justice on Trial" indicates, African-Americans and Latinos are disproportionately targeted by police, unfairly victimized by skewed charging and plea bargaining decisions by prosecutors, given harsher sentences and disproportionately impacted by "get tough" policies enacted by lawmakers. The findings in "Justice on Trial" are consistent with the report released last week by the Building Blocks for Youth initiative examining racial disparities in the juvenile justice system. That report, titled "And Justice for Some," looked at decisions to charge youth in juvenile court, to detain them before trial, to transfer them to adult criminal court, to incarcerate them in juvenile facilities, and to incarcerate them in adult prisons, in each case comparing minority youth and white youth charged with similar offenses. The results are consistent. As a consequence, minorities suffer a "cumulative disadvantage" when it comes to justice in this country. Moreover, recent legislative and policy trends throughout the country make it clear that these disparities will continue for the foreseeable future unless political leaders and public officials take significant action at the federal, state, and local levels.

As these two reports indicate, there is something terribly wrong with our justice system. Justice by race is no justice at all. The reports paint a devastating picture of a system that has totally failed to uphold the American promise of "equal justice for all."

The Building Blocks for Youth initiative proudly joins with the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights in the call for federal, state, and local officials to take significant action now to address this problem. Federal, state and local officials should immediately investigate the disparate racial impact of their laws and policies, particularly those providing for prosecution of juveniles in adult criminal court, and should immediately stop incarcerating young people with adult inmates in jails and prisons. ###

Mark Soler is President of the Youth Law Center and directs the Building Blocks for Youth initiative. The Youth Law Center is a national public interest law firm based in Washington, DC and San Francisco which has worked on behalf of children in juvenile justice and child welfare systems across the country for over twenty years. "And Justice for Some" can be viewed at www.buildingblocksforyouth.org
