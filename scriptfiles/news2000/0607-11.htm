Judge Jackson's Decision Is Pro-Consumer
   WASHINGTON - June 7 - Statement of U.S. PIRG on Microsoft Breakup Order
Edmund Mierzwinski
Consumer Program Director

"We hope that Judge Jackson's decision will mean that consumers will have more choices to buy better software at lower prices. Judge Jackson's lawful order breaking Microsoft in two recognizes first, that Microsoft's pattern of abuses of the anti-monopoly laws has cost consumers, competitors, innovation and the economy billions of dollars and second, that Microsoft's paltry conduct remedy proposals would fail. We welcome strong enforcement of the antitrust laws."

U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for state Public Interest Research Groups. The State PIRGs are non-profit, non-partisan public interest advocacy groups with offices around the country.
