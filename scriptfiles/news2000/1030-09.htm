LCV Launches Radio Ad Campaign Highlighting Real Differences Between Bush, Gore on the Environment
  WASHINGTON - October 30 - The League of Conservation Voters today launched a radio ad campaign speaking directly to the real differences between presidential candidates George W. Bush and Al Gore on the environment.  The ads are running in the key battleground states of New Mexico, Florida, Wisconsin, Oregon, Maine and Washington. 

"Tuesday, November 7 will determine our nation’s next leader and set the course for environmental policy well into the future," said LCV President Deb Callahan.  "With so much at stake for the environment, LCV is hitting the air waves to inform voters of the stark differences between Al Gore and George Bush when it comes to protecting our air, water and land."

The ads focus on Gore’s lifelong commitment to fighting for stronger environmental and public health protections.  They highlight his efforts to strengthen both the Clean Air and Clean Water acts and to speed the clean up of toxic waste sites across the country.  In contrast, the ads criticize Bush for exempting hundreds of old, polluting power plants from clean air laws in Texas and for appointing industry representatives to the Texas Natural Resource Conservation Commission, Texas’ environmental agency.  They also address Bush’s legacy of pollution in Texas.   

According to the League, the ads aim to reach Independent and undecided voters who rank clean air and water among their top concerns.  A poll conducted for LCV in September by Peter D. Hart Research found that 51 percent of Independent voters to whom clean air and water are very important find little difference between the environmental records of Bush and Gore, preferring Bush 34 percent to 30 percent.  After learning about the records of the two candidates, these voters swing significantly away from George W. Bush and towards Al Gore, choosing the vice president 36 percent to 25 percent.

"On Election Day, only Al Gore or George Bush will be elected president," Callahan said.  "Voters of all political beliefs and demographics have to decide if Bush’s toxic record in Texas is something they want for the entire nation.  Only Gore and Joe Lieberman can legitimately carry the environmental banner all the way to the White House."

The League of Conservation Voters is the political voice for the national environmental community.  LCV’s radio ads are airing in Santa Fe and Albuquerque, N.M; Portland, Maine; Tallahassee and Gainsville, Fla.; Madison, Wis.; Eugene, Ore.; and Seattle, Wash.   LCV was the first national conservation group to endorse Gore for president.   For more information about the presidential campaigns, including candidate profiles and comparison charts, visit www.lcv.org/campaigns.
