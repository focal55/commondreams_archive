Wanted: Information On Mysterious 'Clean Air' Ad
  NEW YORK - March 2 - The Sierra Club is looking for answers to a question that is confusing people from coast to coast: "Who are `Republicans for Clean Air' and why are they running ads that contradict George W. Bush's poor environmental record?" Republicans for Clean Air are broadcasting ads that attack Senator John McCain's environmental record, and make misleading claims about Bush's environmental record. A LEXIS-NEXIS search on "Republicans for Clean Air" revealed that they have never appeared in an article. In addition, the group has no website, did not include any documentation with the ads.

"It's time for the Oz behind these ads to step out from behind the curtains and reveal himself," said Carl Pope, Sierra Club Executive Director. "Environmental issues should be openly debated and not subjected to misleading ads from phantom groups lurking in the shadows."

The ad praises Governor George W. Bush's record on clean air, despite the fact that Texas has the worst air quality in the nation, and that Houston became the nation's Smog Capital in 1999. The ad also makes assertions about Texas air pollution that are not supported by the facts.

The ad claims that "Bush clean air laws will reduce air pollution more than a quarter million tons a year." This claim is misleading because Texas law only requires 117,000 tons of pollution reductions per year. The rest of the 250,000 tons of reductions are purely voluntary, and therefore it is unclear whether they will actually occur.

"This ad takes very optimistic speculation and makes it sound like facts," said Ken Kramer, director of the Sierra Club Lone Star (Texas) Chapter. "As someone who breathes the air in Texas every day, I would be interested in learning how Republicans for Clean Air came to their conclusions."

The ad also implies that Governor Bush supports efforts to clean up New York's air. One of New York's biggest environmental efforts was the state's recent lawsuit against Midwestern coal-burning plants, which was eventually joined by the EPA. However, lawsuits which protect New Yorkers from pollution are likely to be opposed by Gov. Bush. He told the New York Times, "the best way to achieve clean air and water was `to not try to sue our way or regulate our way to clean air and clean water'" (11-9-99).
