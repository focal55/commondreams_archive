'Polar Bears' and 'Caribou' to Protest Against BP Amoco's Drilling
  WASHINGTON - March 23 - News Advisory:

WHAT:
Solar powered press conference and protest against BP Amoco's Arctic expansion plans

WHO:
Activists representing Ecopledge Campaign, Greenpeace, Public Interest Research Groups, and the Dirty Jobs Boycott

WHEN and WHERE:
Friday, March 24:

10 a.m:
BP Amoco headquarters, 535 Madison Ave.,
New York, NY

2 p.m:
Times Square, Viacom Buiding, 1515 Broadway
New York, NY

Activists wearing polar bears suits, accompanied by a large inflatable caribou, will mark the 11th anniversary of the Exxon Valdez oil spill with protests in Times Square and in front of BP Amoco's New York City office. Using a solar-powered message board, the activists will lobby the public and members of the city's financial community, gathering support for a BP Amoco shareholders resolution to be voted on in three weeks time at the company's annual general meeting.

The resolution, filed by SANE BP, a group of socially responsible investors organized by Trillium Asset Management US PIRG and Greenpeace, focuses on BP Amoco's plans to drill for oil in two of the most sensitive parts of America's Alaskan Arctic: on the coastal plain of the Arctic National Refuge and offshore in the Northstar oil field. The resolution calls on BP Amoco to cancel all Arctic drilling plans and switch investments towards clean renewable sources of energy like solar power.

At the 10 a.m. protest, students with the Ecopledge.com campaign will deliver resumes, and inform the company to hold them until BP Amoco cancels drilling plans for the Arctic Refuge and Northstar. Students are pledging through the ecopledge.com campaign to not work for BP Amoco until the company cancels their Arctic drilling plans.

Arctic wildlife will be highlighted in Friday's protest because drilling threatens their habitat. The burning of oil is the number one cause of global warming which is already melting permafrost and sea ice. Arctic oil drilling also exposes wildlife habitat to unacceptable risks from oil spills.

Meanwhile on the frozen Arctic Ocean near thee Northstar construction site on the Arctic Ocean, Greenpeace has maintained a renewable energy camp for the past three weeks to protest and monitor the opening of a new oil frontier in the fragile Arctic environment.
