Protesters Nationwide Give Star Wars 'No Stars' Rating
   WASHINGTON - June 6 - From Portland, Ore., to Great Neck, N.Y., peace activists are preparing for Action Days -- a three-day, nationwide protest coordinated by Peace Action and affiliated groups to oppose Star Wars deployment. From June 8-10, participating organizations will hold local actions, meet with editorial boards, sponsor advertisements in local papers and organize call-ins and write-ins to President Bill Clinton and Congressional representatives in opposition to Clinton's proposed $60 billion Star Wars initiative. The overarching goal of Action Days is to promote global security through greater arms reductions.

To date, Peace Action affiliates have planned events in 10 cities across the United States. Oregon PeaceWorks will hold a noon protest Thursday, June 8 in Portland's Courthouse Square. Activists will criticize Sens. Wyden and Smith for their votes on Star Wars and engage the public in anti-Star Wars lobbying efforts. Also on June 8 at noon, Peace Action Delaware Valley will demonstrate in front of Republican Sen. Rick Santorum's Philadelphia office. Great Neck (N.Y.) Peace Action members will write op-eds to local newspapers and hold a peaceful protest outside the office of Democratic House Rep. Gary Ackerman. Similar events will take place in Annapolis, Md., Milwaukee, Wis.; Concord, N.H. and Chicago.

"Debate shouldn't be about which Star Wars system to deploy, but whether there should be one at all," said Illinois Peace Action (IPA) Acting Executive Director Kevin Kinter. In Chicago, hundreds of IPA members will spend June 8-10 distributing fliers, staging guerrilla theater and generating support through a satirical signature ad published in the Chicago Reader (circulation 138,000) for Star Wars Episode 2000: The Nuclear Menace. IPA "critics" label Star Wars "A tremendous failure!" and "Surprisingly expensive!"; donors' names appear at the bottom of the ad, along with a call to President Clinton in support or arms reduction initiatives.

"All of these efforts are to beat back the missile defense hysteria since the Republicans took over Congress in 1994," said Peace Action Research Coordinator Jim Bridgman. Peace Action's anti-Star Wars efforts are part of a three-prong strategy to mobilize activists through direct action, lobbying efforts and emediacy campaigns such as www.DontBlowIt.org, a new Web site developed by the Technology Project which enables citizens to send electronic postcards to President Clinton.

Action Days follows the Peace Voter Fund's recent $100,000-plus anti-Star Wars television and newspaper ad campaign to educate voters about the dangers of building Clinton's expensive anti-missile system. The campaign coincided with the June 4-5 meetings between Clinton and Russian Prime Minister Vladimir Putin. Despite several hours of talks, the two leaders didn't reach an agreement on Star Wars due to Putin's reluctance to amend the 1972 Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty, a cornerstone covenant which would be violated if Star Wars goes forward. Meanwhile, presidential candidates Al Gore and George W. Bush both claim that a new missile system is necessary to protect the United States and its allies from potential missile attacks by "rogue" states such as North Korea and Iran.

Results of a May ABC News poll show that 53 percent of Americans oppose building even a limited missile system. Peace Action and Action Days co-sponsors such as Peace Links and Fourth Freedom Forum hope to build upon existing anti-Star Wars sentiment by promoting public opinion.
