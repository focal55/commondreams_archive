Pediatricians to Release Policies to Reduce Gun Injuries in Children
  

WASHINGTON - April 3 -

 News Advisory:

WHO:
-- Donald Cook, M.D., president, American Academy of Pediatrics; Greeley, Colo.
-- Danielle Laraque, M.D., member, AAP Committee on Injury and Poison Prevention and lead author, AAP policy statement on firearms; New York, N.Y.
-- David Kaplan, M.D., M.P.H., chair, AAP Committee on Adolescence and lead author, AAP policy statement on adolescent suicide; Denver, Colo.
-- Howard Spivak, M.D., member, AAP Committee on Injury and Poison Prevention and former chair, AAP Task Force on Violence; Boston, Mass.
-- Carol Price, mother of a 13-year-old boy who was shot and killed by an 11-year-old friend

WHEN:
Monday, April 3
1 p.m. (ET)

WHERE:
National Press Club, First Amendment Lounge
-- Reporters may phone in for interactive participation at 800-311-9404, code: "AAP news conference."

The American Academy of Pediatrics will release two updated policy statements addressing the dangers of firearms to children and adolescents.

The first policy statement will offer recommendations for reducing gun injury and death in children, particularly from handguns and semi-automatic weapons. The second policy statement will address risk factors for teen suicide and the impact of guns on suicide attempts.

------
The American Academy of Pediatrics is an organization of 55,000 primary care pediatricians, pediatric medical subspecialists, and pediatric surgical specialists dedicated to the health, safety, and well-being of infants, children, adolescents and young adults.
