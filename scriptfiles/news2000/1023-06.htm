Nader To Gore: Time To Shut Down The WTI Incinerator
   CHICO, CA - October 23 - Ralph Nader today called on Vice-President Gore to heed the EPA National Ombudsman’s report and immediately halt operations at the WTI incinerator in East Liverpool, Ohio. “It’s past time to honor your promise,” Nader told Gore, “there are no more excuses. There is a critical mass of information and informed opinion calling for a halt in operations.” Nader campaigned in East Liverpool, Ohio on September 27.

On Friday, the EPA National Ombudsman issued a long-delayed report recommending that the “EPA and Ohio EPA restrict operations at the WTI facility immediately by: (1) Halting the feeding of waste to the incinerator for a period of no less than six months; (2) Make preparations for a retest of the trial burn or a new trial burn in 2001 as a necessary step in the consideration by Ohio EPA of permit renewal for the facility next year; and (3) With new data from the new trial burn, perform a new Addendum to the risk assessment for the WTI facility.”

Vice-President Gore had called for the report in February, while under pressure from citizen activists, and staffers had promised its delivery “before Mother’s Day.” “The delayed release of this report can’t be used as an excuse for inaction,” Nader said today. “The people of East Liverpool have heard the ‘my hands are tied’ excuse already.”

“The EPA should not ignore these recommendations,” Nader stated, “but the vice president has already had ample opportunity to make good on his 1992 promise and shut the incinerator down.”

Trial-burns conducted in 1993 revealed that the incinerator released toxins in quantities greater than the already dangerously high levels allowed by the EPA. Mercury and dioxin levels were far higher than expected, giving the Clinton Administration ample reason to deny approval of full commercial operation to the WTI incinerator. As the September 1994 GAO report that Gore himself requested noted, toxic emission standards were lowered by Gore’s own EPA in order to allow the incinerator to operate. “In each case [where toxic emissions exceeded allowable levels],” the report explains, “EPA responded to the trial burn results by changing the operating conditions under which the incinerator could continue to operate.”

The hazardous waste incinerator in East Liverpool, OH burns 60,000 tons of hazardous waste every year, making it one of the largest incinerators of its type in the world, despite the fact that its permit expired in 1995. It is located in an area with a history of environmental contamination, on a flood plain, and less than 400 yards away from an elementary school, in violation of numerous state and federal environmental regulations. Campaigning for Vice-President in 1992, Al Gore called the incinerator, then in construction “unbelievable,” adding: “the Clinton-Gore administration is going to give you an environmental presidency to deal with these problems. We’ll be on your side for a change.” That statement represents just one more broken promise by the Clinton-Gore Administration. No one is more eloquent on this subject than the parents and residents of East Liverpool, Ohio.
