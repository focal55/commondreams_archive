UNDER THE RADAR: Common Cause Release Expose of 527 "STEALTH PACS"
  WASHINGTON - April 7 - Common Cause today released "Under The Radar: Attack of the Stealth PACs on our Nation's Elections," a report about the so-called "Stealth PACs," organized under Section 527 of the tax code which can hide their donors, activities, and even their very existence from public view.

"These shadowy groups represent a dangerous and emerging trend in a campaign finance system already near a state of collapse," Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger said. "These committees are throwbacks to the off-the-books slushfunds of the Watergate era - and Congress needs to act now to shut down the 527 loophole. Already in this election season, we have seen the effect of these 'Stealth PACs' involved in last-minute anonymous smears. It's a bipartisan problem requiring a bipartisan solution."

The loophole in question allows these groups to claim - for the purposes of tax law - that they are devoted to influencing elections. But, these groups also are claiming - for the purposes of election law - that they are not influencing the outcomes of elections - thereby sparing themselves the disclosure requirements and the limits on the sizes and sources of contributions that other PACs have to abide by.

"Just when you thought that the current campaign finance system couldn't get any worse - it does," Harshbarger said. "And these Stealth PACs are flying under the radar to avoid detection from the public at large."
