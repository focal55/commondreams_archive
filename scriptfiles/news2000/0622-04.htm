"Teddy Roosevelt" to host National Forest Press Conference
Nearly 400,000 Acres of Virginia Forests at Stake to be Saved or Destroyed
  WASHINGTON - June 22 - The late President Teddy Roosevelt will reappear in Washington on Monday to build on his historic efforts to conserve America's forests. Roosevelt -- in the person of television actor Dean Whitworth -- will be in Washington to testify at Monday evening's regional U.S. Forest Service hearing in Arlington, and will hold a press conference on the West side of the U.S. Capitol that morning to discuss his efforts.

Roosevelt will be joined by modern-day conservationists working to strengthen the U.S. Forest Service's proposal to save the fragments of our National Forests that remain wild and unspoiled. If the Forest Service selects a plan that ends logging and other destructive activities in wild National Forest areas larger than 1,000 acres and protects Alaska's Tongass National Forest now rather than delaying action, a total of 60 million acres of pristine forests could be saved for Americans to explore and enjoy.

In Virginia, 394,000 acres of pristine forests could be protected from logging, roadbuilding and other destruction so Washingtonians can continue to enjoy those lands for hunting, fishing, camping, hiking and other activities.
