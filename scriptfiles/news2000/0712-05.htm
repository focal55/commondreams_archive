Wellstone Blasts Merger Talks Between Northwest Airlines and American
Asks Why Company Failed to Inform Minnesotans of Future Plans;
Calls on All Elected Minnesota Officials to Demand Accountability
   WASHINGTON - July 12 - Responding to reports that Northwest Airlines is intensifying negotiations on a merger deal with American Airlines, Senator Paul Wellstone today issued a sharp rebuke to the company, saying Northwest has failed to communicate its plans to Minnesota. Wellstone predicted that the contemplated merger would have negative consequences for the state's economy, hurting workers as well as air travelers to Minnesota. He said he would oppose the merger vigorously.

Last month Wellstone, an outspoken advocate for tough anti-trust action in many sectors of the economy, called on Northwest Airlines CEO John H. Dasburg to inform Minnesotans about the impact of the company's rumored merger plans with American. Northwest never responded.

"We ought to be alarmed to hear that negotiations which had merely been rumored now appear be a fact, and are intensifying. Should these negotiations lead to a merger between the two airlines, I will actively oppose it. A few short years ago, the State of Minnesota helped save Northwest Airlines financially when there were serious concerns about the economic future of the company. Minnesotans are stakeholders in Northwest Airlines, and they deserve a careful explanation of both why a merger may take place and what the impact of this merger would be to our state. Minnesotans, business travelers and vacation travelers, travel agents and teachers, Northwest employees and Minnesota taxpayers have a right to know what Northwest's intentions are. All of Minnesota's elected officials should hold Northwest accountable, and demand that the company make clear its future intentions in Minnesota," Wellstone wrote.

In June, Wellstone pressed the U.S. Department of Justice to block a proposed merger of United Airlines and US Airways. In a letter to Joel Klein, Assistant Attorney General of the Antitrust Division, Wellstone said that a merger by the two airlines would reduce airline competition nationally, resulting in higher prices for air travelers, and would lead to yet another round of industry mergers and consolidation, specifically a possible one between Minnesota-based Northwest Airlines and American.

"I believe Northwest Airlines has an obligation to Minnesotans to explain why the airline feels that it might need to merge with American Airlines. Does Northwest believe a merger with another airline is inevitable if the United Airlines - US Airways merger is approved? If a merger were to take place, what would the new airline look like? Would Minnesota lose its hub airport? Would it lose Northwest's headquarters? Would we lose jobs?" asked Wellstone.

In the early 1990s when Northwest was facing financial difficulties, the State of Minnesota extended a financial aid package worth more than $300 million. Based upon Northwest keeping its operations hub in Minneapolis, the airport currently is in the midst of undertaking tax-payer supported improvements worth more than $2 billion.
