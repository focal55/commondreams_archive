Doctors Hold Wednesday News Conference Exposing EPA's 'Disregard for Children's Health'
   WASHINGTON - June 26 - News Advisory:

What:
News conference calling on the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) to take immediate action to protect children from chemical exposures rather than delaying with endless cruel and inaccurate animal tests.

The Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine (PCRM) will release a report proving that the EPA already has extensive data on many of the chemicals slated for testing in a new program called the "Voluntary Children's Chemical Evaluation Program." PCRM will also unveil its alternative proposal for child health, and a provocative new educational and advertising campaign it will launch the same day.

When:
9-10 a.m.
Wednesday, June 28

Speakers will be available immediately afterwards for interviews.

Where:
The Lisagor Room
National Press Club
529 14th St., N.W.
Washington, D.C.

Speakers:

-- Neal D. Barnard, M.D., clinical researcher and president of PCRM. Dr. Barnard has written and spoken extensively on controversies in health and research.

-- Terri J. Swearingen, R.N., internationally recognized environmental activist featured on "60 Minutes" and in Time Magazine and past winner of a 1997 Goldman Environmental Prize.

------
Founded in 1985, the Physicians Committee for Responsible Medicine is a Washington, D.C.-based nonprofit organization focused on preventive medicine and higher standards in research. Its membership includes 5,000 physicians and 100,000 laypeople.
