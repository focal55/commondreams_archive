Nation's Most Endangered Rivers to be Announced April 10
  

WASHINGTON - March 27 -

News Advisory:

American Rivers will release its fifteenth annual report, America's Most Endangered Rivers of 2000, on Monday, April 10. The report ranks the most endangered rivers in the country facing the most serious and immediate environmental abuses this year.

Thirteen rivers in the following states will be listed this year as the nation's most endangered rivers:

Alabama, Alaska, Arkansas, California, Colorado, Georgia, Florida, Illinois, Iowa, Kansas, Louisiana, Maine, Minnesota, Mississippi, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, New Mexico, North Dakota, South Dakota, Tennessee, Texas, Utah, Washington State, West Virginia and Wisconsin

Press conferences will be held in various locations around the country (details will be available at www.americanrivers.org)
