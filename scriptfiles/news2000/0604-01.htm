Major UN Event on Women Opens June 5
  NEW YORK - June 4 - On Monday, June 5, UN Secretary-General Kofi Annan will join members of the U.S. Congress, other key leaders from the U.S. and abroad, and celebrities for the "Women 2000 Opening Ceremony" sponsored by the Beijing Plus 5 NGO Host Committee.

This 11:30 am to 1:00 pm ceremony across from the United Nations at Dag Hammarskjold Plaza (East 47th Street and First Ave.) will welcome the thousands of women and government officials who will be in New York City June 5-9 for Women 2000, also known as Beijing+5, a UN review assessing advances and progress since the Fourth World Conference on Women held in 1995 in Beijing.

Speakers include: UN Secretary-General Kofi Annan and the heads of lead UN agencies including Carol Bellamy of UNICEF and Nafis Sadik of UNFPA; Her Majesty Queen Noor of Jordan; U.S. Lt. General Claudia Kennedy; New York Congresswoman Carolyn Maloney (D-NY); UN Ambassador Richard Holbrooke; and U.S. Cabinet Secretary Donna Shalala.

Also joining the Opening Ceremony are: Singer and UNICEF Goodwill Ambassador Judy Collins; Indian Film Director Mira Nair; Actor Olympia Dukakis; Supermodel and UNFPA Goodwill Ambassador Waris Dirie; Singer Ghada Ghanem; the NY Center School Children's Choir and more.

Later in the day, First Lady Hillary Rodham Clinton will be on a panel at the United Nations discussing Microcredit from 1:15-3:00 pm in UN Conference Room 4. Also, a press conference featuring U.S. members of Congress discussing women's attitudes towards international affairs will be held at 3:30 pm in the UN Press Conference Room (UN 226).

For a full schedule with speaker times and technical information, call the NGO Media Center in New York City at 646-227-1440. More information is also posted on the journalists' Web site: www.women2000newsroom.org

The Women 2000 NGO Media Center is a centralized source of information for journalists covering the UN General Assembly Session on "Women 2000: Gender Equality, Development and Peace for the 21st Century." The Center will operate daily from 9 am to 5 pm. It is located across from the United Nations on the 2nd Floor of the Church Center (44th Street, between 1st and 2nd Avenues).
