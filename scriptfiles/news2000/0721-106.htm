Prominent Baptist Leader Challenges 'Priests for Life' Ad Questioning Faith of Christians
   WASHINGTON - July 21 - This week, Priests for Life, an organization of Catholic priests, launched an ad campaign calling on lawmakers and voters to restrict abortion. In today's New York Times, Priests for Life ran a full-page ad stating, "To those who would allow abortion and claim to be Christian, we say, 'STOP BEING A SCANDAL TO THE GOSPEL OF JESUS CHRIST'." In response, the Rev. Dr. C. Welton Gaddy, executive director of The Interfaith Alliance, issued the following statement:

"This ad seeks to create a political litmus test for determining Christian identity. To suggest that an individual is not a true Christian because he or she holds a different view on abortion is manipulating religion in a way that is dangerous, divisive and lacks civility. Abortion is a complex and passionate issue that divides Christians as well as other religious communities. While we don't take a position on the issue of abortion, we recognize the necessity of creating a civil environment that fosters open debate where people of faith can have honest differences without having their faith called into question."

Founded in 1994, The Interfaith Alliance (TIA) is a non-partisan clergy-led grassroots organization dedicated to promoting the positive and healing role of religion in the life of the nation and challenging those who manipulate religion to promote intolerance. With more than 100,000 members drawn from over 50 faith traditions, clergy-led local Alliances in 38 states, and a national network of activists in every state, TIA promotes civility, mutual respect, and cooperation in our increasingly-diverse society.
