Nader Campaign Not Affiliated with Tactical Voting Ad
  WASHINGTON - October 25 - Nader 2000 campaign manager Theresa Amato said today that the full-page “tactical voting” advertisement running in newspapers in several states was not generated by the campaign.

The ad urging people to vote for Nader was designed and paid for independently from the campaign by Greg MacArthur, a progressive New York philanthropist. The ads are running in states where either Bush or Gore has a solid lead.

“We are urging voters in every state to cast their votes for Ralph Nader and Winona LaDuke, and for a progressive third party that will be a watchdog over the two corporate parties after November,” Amato said. “In this election, the only wasted vote is the one cast without conviction and for cash-register politics as usual.”

“Our intent in this campaign is to garner the maximum number of votes for Ralph Nader. This is a national 50-state campaign and we will continue to run it in every state for every vote.”

Amato said she is available for interviews on the subject. Reporters can call the press office at 202-265-4000.
