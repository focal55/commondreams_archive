Student Peace Action National Post Office Actions to Protest Embargo on Iraq
   WASHINGTON - April 12 - Despite the economic sanctions that prohibit the flow of goods to Iraq, concerned students are mailing food, medicine and school supplies to the suffering people of Iraq at the National Capitol Post Office, 2 Massachusetts Ave., N.E.

The action begins at noon with a brief press conference, followed by a procession inside the post office to mail humanitarian aid to the Iraqi Red Crescent. Invited speakers include Stephanie Schaudel, student at American University, Amber Waldref, national coordinator of Student Peace Action Network (SPAN) and Kate Reuer of the Fellowship of Reconciliation.

The packages, once rejected by the U.S. Postal Service, will be re-addressed and delivered to several U.S. Representatives, urging them to address the human crisis in Iraq by passing new legislation: H.R. 3825, the Humanitarian Exports Leading to Peace (HELP) Act.

The Student Peace Action Network, a nationwide network of campus groups and young people working for peace and justice, vigorously opposes the sanctions against Iraq because they are both morally wrong and politically ineffective. The Iraqi population has suffered tremendously under the United Nations sanctions regime led by the U.S. government.

"Nearly a decade of massive sanctions on the Iraqi people has created a devastated Iraqi society," said Amber Waldref, national coordinator of the Student Peace Action Network (SPAN). According to UNICEF, the last nine years of bombing and sanctions have claimed more than 1 million Iraqi lives, including 500,000 children. The sanctions have also failed to achieve the illegitimate U.S. goal of removing Saddam Hussein from power.

Parallel post office actions are being organized in 15 states by 25 student groups coast to coast. For more information or a list of these location actions, contact Amber Waldref at SPAN, 202-862-9740, ext. 3051 for more information or www.goSPAN.org.
