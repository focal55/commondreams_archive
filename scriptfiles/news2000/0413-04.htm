Historic Shareholder Vote Against BP Amoco's Arctic Oil Plans
Confrontation continues at Alaskan offshore oil project
  LONDON/ BEAUFORT SEA, AK - April 13 - Greenpeace called on BP Amoco today to act on a historic vote at its annual general meeting calling on the company to cancel its Arctic expansion plans. Specifically, the resolution called on the company to abandon its controversial offshore Northstar oil project in the Beaufort Sea, stop lobbying to open up the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge to oil development and redirect funds into its solar subsidiary BP Solarex.

Today's initial proxy vote, displayed at the BP annual general meeting, showed 9.5 billion shares against the resolution and 1.491 billion shares in favor, or 13.5 per cent of the vote. This was one of the highest votes for an environmental shareholder resolution ever recorded. Votes of the shareholders attending today's meeting have yet to be counted.

"BP Amoco cannot ignore this message from its own investors," said Greenpeace energy policy analyst Iain MacGill. "BP Amoco's board must immediately outline a strategy on how it now intends to make the transition from fossil fuels to renewable energy. Either the company delivers or it faces a reputation meltdown." Despite claims it is concerned about global warming, BP Amoco's oil and gas investments represent 99.9 per cent of its total energy investments.

Meanwhile, at the Northstar construction site on the frozen Arctic Ocean two Alaskans were arrested this morning for entering BP Amoco's "No Trespass Zone" while holding a sign reading: "BP: Global Warming Starts Here." Altogether, eleven activists have been arrested at the site this week.

"Both Alaskans and the company investors are sending a very strong message that BP Amoco must stop fueling global warming and risking the Arctic environment," said Greenpeace campaigner Melanie Duchin from the Greenpeace camp near the Northstar site.

Greenpeace challenged the BP Amoco Board to develop a series of options with different levels of capital investment in oil exploration and renewable energy and let shareholders decide which level they want.

The resolution, filed by Greenpeace, the US Public Interest Research Group and Trillium Asset Management of Boston, was publicly backed by a large number of individual and institutional shareholders in the US and the UK.

"This resolution represents a warning bell for BP Amoco; this issue will not go away. The BP Amoco board can ignore this resolution but they will expose the company later to the risk of stringent regulation and possibly consumer boycotts," said Simon Billenness, senior analyst for Trillium.
