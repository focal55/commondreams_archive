Today Marks Second Annual STOP the HATE: Interfaith Vigils Against Violence
Diverse Coalitions Remember Victims, Join in Fight Against Hate Violence
  

WASHINGTON - October 5 -

As people of faith and goodwill, we have a moral responsibility and civic obligation to take a leading role in doing all that we can to STOP the HATE in our society.

--Rev. Dr. C. Welton Gaddy

(Today interfaith religious communities across the nation organized an array of events for the second annual STOP the HATE: Interfaith Vigils Against Hate Violence campaign sponsored by The Interfaith Alliance Foundation and the Fellowship of Reconciliation. As brutal stories of hate crimes continue to shock communities across our country,religious leaders are working in coalition with civic leaders and activists to raise awareness about the devastating effects of hate in our society.

“Our message is simple,hate is not a religious value, but an evil that distorts personhood among individuals and, within societies erodes justice and breeds violence,” said the Rev. Dr. C. Welton Gaddy, executive director of The Interfaith Alliance Foundation.

“Too often, these acts of violence desensitize our communities, creating a culture of powerlessness and isolation. As religious leaders we have a moral obligation to challenge hatred and educate communities on its destructive powers.”

Below are highlights of events occurring across the nation.

In a Nassau County, Long Island, the Rev. Gaddy will join local religious leaders for an evening rally and program that will remember the local victims of hate crimes. This evening’s event is expected to draw thousands of New Yorkers who will not only remember the victims of violence but share organizing strategies for challenging hate in their community.

On the other side of the country, Sudarshan Kapoor, a retired California State University Professor, has worked tirelessly to organize a series of events at the three major college campuses in Fresno, California. Themed, “Build the Culture of Peace,” events will include vigils,rallies, movies, workshops and celebrations engaging students, faculty and the community.

In Raleigh, North Carolina, The Interfaith Alliance of Wake County will be honoring the life of Roger Cody Jarman, Jr., a local man who was killed in an apparent hate crime.

In the Midwest, The Interfaith Alliance of Greater St. Louis will convene a vigil and a march designed to empower both religious leaders and lay activists in the fight against hate. Prior the vigil,they will host a workshop for clergy and congregational leaders that will train religious leaders in addressing the social, economic and racial issues of their communities. Then, another workshop will follow for youth leaders, campus ministers, and youth that will take a look at white supremacist use of music to recruit followers.

Last week in West Virginia, over 100 diverse students gathered at West Virginia University to participate in a STOP the HATE vigil. The goal of the event was to “bring people together in a common cause. If we can’t bring people together to oppose hatred and violence, we’re never going to get people together,” according to Bob Turner,director of the Baptist Campus Ministry at WVU.

“Uniting behind their shared values and spiritual commitment to creating a just society that respects the human dignity of its members, Muslim, Jewish, Christian, Buddhist and other faith leaders are organizing various events in their communities demonstrating the positive and healing role of religion in our nation,” said the Rev. Gaddy.

For more information about the STOP the HATE campaign, visit www.interfaithalliance.org or call 202-639-6370.

 

Founded in 1996, The Interfaith Alliance Foundation is a non-partisan 501(c)(3) non-profit research and educational organization with supporters from over 50 faith traditions, including Buddhists, Catholics,Hindus, Jews, Muslims, Protestants and Unitarians. A commitment to core values,including compassion, civility, and mutual respect for diversity and human dignity, unite us as people of faith and goodwill.
