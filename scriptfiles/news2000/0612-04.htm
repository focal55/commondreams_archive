Groundbreaking Study Reveals 'Get Tough' Initiatives Fail to Reduce Juvenile Crime; Offers More Effective Approaches
  WASHINGTON - June 12 - News Advisory:

WHAT:
"Less Hype, More Help: Reducing Juvenile Crime, What Works -- and What Doesn't." An extensive report showing that trying youthful offenders in adult courts -- "adult time for adult crime" -- is a counterproductive fad that actually exacerbates juvenile crime. The report unveils alternative approaches that are far more effective in preventing teens from committing crimes-and in protecting the communities in which they live.

WHO:
-- Samuel Halperin, the American Youth Policy Forum
-- Hugh Price, The National Urban League
-- Jack Calhoun, National Crime Prevention Council
-- Michael Petit, Child Welfare League
-- Richard Mendel, Author

WHEN:
Wednesday, June 14, 10 a.m.

WHERE:
National Press Club, 529 14th St., N.W., 13th Floor
Washington, D.C.

Based on nine months of research, data analysis and investigation into dozens of successful programs around the nation, "Less Hype, More Help: Reducing Juvenile Crime, What Works -- and What Doesn't" shows that America has the knowledge to substantially reduce youth crime and violence without a substantial increase in spending. Unfortunately, the research also shows, the reforms that will solve the problem are not being widely used.

EXTENSIVE BACKGROUND ON MODEL LOCAL PROGRAMS IS AVAILABLE.
Cities profiled include Orange County and San Francisco, CA, Boston, MA, Charleston, SC, Milwaukee, WI, Eugene, OR, Salt Lake City, UT, Philadelphia, PA, Everglades and Ft. Lauderdale, FL, Giddings, TX and Seattle, WA.
