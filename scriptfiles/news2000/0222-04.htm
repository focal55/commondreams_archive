Amnesty International Condemns Texas Board of Pardons and Paroles
  WASHINGTON - February 22 - Amnesty International USA (AIUSA) condemns the decision by the Texas Board of Pardons and Paroles to proceed with the execution of Betty Lou Beets despite her history of abuse at the hands of her husband.

In an open letter to Gov. Bush last week, AIUSA's Women's Human Rights Program and national and state domestic violence groups drew attention to the repeated sexual, physical and emotional abuse Beets endured throughout her life. Neither this history nor the abuse by her husband were presented at her trial.

Unless Gov. George W. Bush grants a 30-day stay, Betty Lou Beets will be executed on Thursday, Feb. 24.

"Gov. Bush must ensure that Texas does not commit the final act of violence against this woman. We ask the governor to act on his stated concern for the victims of domestic violence and demonstrate his compassion by staying the sentence of Betty Lou Beets," said Sheila Dauer, director of AIUSA's Women's Human Rights Program.

"This case demonstrates how poorly the legal system protects against unjust executions. Gov. Bush should grant a 30-day stay and consider commuting this sentence to prevent a fatal miscarriage of justice in this case," said Dr. William Schulz, executive director of AIUSA. "We urge Gov. Bush to acknowledge that the application of the death penalty in Texas is flawed and also halt all executions pending a broad review of the death penalty."

Legislatures around the country have recognized the role of domestic and sexual violence as mitigating factors in capital cases. Governors in states such as Maryland, Ohio, Illinois, California and Florida have commutted the sentences of battered women.

More than 3,600 men and women remain on death row across the United States. Four countries -- the United States, China, Saudi Arabia and Iran -- lead the world in use of the death penalty.
