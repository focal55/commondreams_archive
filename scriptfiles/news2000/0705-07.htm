Protest Training to be Held for Citizens Targetting Republican National Convention
   PHILADELPHIA - July 5 - Over 100 people of color and poor citizens from Philadelphia, New York, and Washington D.C. who want to hone their skills in direct action methodologies and polish their techniques for organizing effective non-violent actions through civil disobedience. There will be special emphasis in this training weekend for constituencies who have been traditionally targeted and brutalized by the police and the legal system for their involvement in direct action.
 
 
WHAT:  Three days of hands-on trainings geared toward participation in the events surrounding the 2000 Republican Convention at the end of July. Trainings will empower citizens in the strategies and tactics of nonviolent direct action to reclaim democracy from big business and elected elite. These include:
" Non-violence training
" Media and press strategy
" Legal issues specific to groups of people of color and poor people
" Blockades and Effective Mass Action Tactics
" Political Theater and Art in Action
 
 
WHERE:  The Friends Center, 1501 Cherry Street, Philadelphia, PA. In Center City at the intersection of 15th Street and Cherry Street.
 
 
WHEN:    Friday, July 7 from 5:00pm to 10:00pm
                  Saturday, July 8 from 9:00am to 10:00pm
                  Sunday, July 9 from 9:00am to 6:30pm
 
 
RSVP Required
Limited Camera Access
 
 
Sponsors:  Philadelphia Direct Action Group (P-DAG) and The Ruckus Society. The Ruckus Society has provided training in the skills of non-violent civil disobedience to help environmental and human rights organizations achieve their goals. Ruckus has trained thousands of citizens in the srategies and tactics of nonviolent direct action, most recently seen in Seattle at the WTO Ministerial and in DC and the World Bank/IMF meetings. P-DAG formed earlier this year to organize non-violent direct action demonstrations at the Republican National Convention. The Peoples Action Training will combine the efforts of both groups in order to share skills that participants can use to practice civil disobedience safely and effectively during the convention and beyond.
 
Supporters:  Philadelphia Black Radical Congress, The Brown Collective, Youth Health Empowerment Program, The R2K/D2K Network, ACT UP Philadelphia, Kensington Welfare Rights Union, Training for Change, Spiral Q Puppet Theatre, Continental Direct Action Network and Rainforest Action Network.
