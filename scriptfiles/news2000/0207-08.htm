Environment Shrinking in Federal Budget
   WASHINGTON - February 7 - Increased spending on the environment proposed by President Clinton today will not happen without a stronger commitment from the president and Congress, according to Friends of the Earth.

Today, President Clinton released the fiscal year 2001 budget request and proposed an overall increase of more than 11 percent -- or $4 billion -- for environmental and conservation purposes in fiscal year 2001.

Despite increases proposed by President Clinton in past years, federal spending on environmental and conservation programs has not kept pace with other budget priorities. While overall federal spending has grown 22 percent since 1993, environmental programs have only grown 14 percent. In 1993, environmental spending was 1.81 percent of the overall federal budget at $26.7 billion; in 2000, environmental spending will shrink to 1.69 percent at $30.4 billion.

"New initiatives and proposals are good, but history shows that Congress and the president are not serious about beefing up conservation and environmental protection," said Gawain Kripke, director of economics campaigns for Friends of the Earth.

The president's fiscal year 2001 budget request seeks major new funding for land acquisition, farm conservation, and a variety of new climate and water-related initiatives. However, Congress has quickly rejected similar proposals in recent years.

"Since 1993, the U.S. economy has grown more than 40 percent the federal government has budget surpluses as far as the eye can see. At the same time, drinking water systems serving more than 50 million Americans violate health standards, and air pollution is so bad in several metropolitan areas, it's causing respiratory diseases and increasing asthma in children," said Kripke. "This country should be investing more in public health and the environment; we can afford it and we owe it to our children and future generations."

A fact sheet on the federal budget can be viewed at Friends of the Earth's website at www.foe.org.

Founded in 1969, Friends of the Earth is a national environmental advocacy organization dedicated to protecting the planet from environmental degradation; preserving biological, cultural and ethnic diversity; and empowering citizens to affect the quality of their environment -- and their lives.
