APTA Commuter Rail Conference Begins April 9, 2000
 

WASHINGTON - March 28 -

News Advisory:

The 2000 Commuter Rail Conference of the American Public Transportation Association (APTA) begins Sunday, April 9, at the Hilton Huntington Hotel in Melville, N.Y. The nation's largest and busiest railroad -- MTA Long Island Rail Road -- is hosting this year's conference and will demonstrate how "public transportation delivers" on Long Island and around North America. Conference highlights include:

--
SUNDAY, April 9
General Session, 10-11:30 a.m. -- APTA President William W. Millar, APTA Chair John P. Bartosiewicz, Acting FRA Administrator Mark Lindsey and U.S. Bombardier Transportation President Peter Stangl provide opening remarks.

MTA Long Island Rail Road's Hillside Facility Tour and Technical Sessions, Noon - 6 p.m.

--
MONDAY, April 10
Technical Showcase Breakfast, 7-9 a.m. -- Expert personnel will answer questions as participants inspect the latest innovations in the world of commuter rail equipment and services.

General Forum, 9-11:45 a.m. -- Panelists explore challenges presented by the renaissance in passenger rail. Speakers include Kawasaki Rail Car Executive VP Yoshinori Kanehana, Burlington Northern Chairman and CEO Robert Krebs and NJ Transportation Commissioner James Weinstein.

Luncheon, Noon-1:30 p.m. -- U.S. Rep. Peter King is the invited keynote speaker.

--
TUESDAY, April 11
Workshops, 9 a.m.-5 p.m. -- Crisis Management, Customer Communications, Grade Crossing Safety, Rail Safety Research, Training Safety Personnel and New Starts are the subjects of the day's workshops.

Luncheon, Noon-1:30 p.m. -- Nationally recognized management expert Ron Archer is the featured speaker.

--
WEDNESDAY, April 12
APTA System Safety Seminar, 8:30 a.m.-5 p.m. -- A seminar for safety managers and other partners in safety management, sponsored by APTA's Commuter Rail Safety Management Program.

Complimentary registration is available to working members of the news media who are actively covering conference activities. You may request media conference credentials by calling APTA at 202-898-4116 or e-mail Amy Coggin at acoggin@apta.com or register on site. During the conference, the APTA media center, located in the Eisenhower Room in the Huntington Hilton, will be open Sunday through Tuesday.
