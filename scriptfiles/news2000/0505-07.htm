Nobel Prize Winner Jose Ramos-Horta, Activist E Timorese Priest to be Honored by Tanenbaum Center
Activist Priest, Father Filomeno Jacob from War-torn East Timor to receive Peace Activist Award on May 22 at Park Avenue Synagogue, Joined by James Earl Jones, Felicia Rashad (MC) and Fellow Honorees Ad Mogul Jerry Della Femina and Dr. Eugene Fisher of National Council of Catholic Bishops
   NEW YORK - May 5 - On May 22, The Tanenbaum Center for Interreligious Understanding will recognize Bishop Basilio do Nascimento, an East Timorese Catholic bishop, with its Peace Activist Award for his work in preserving peace amongst his fellow East Timorese, despite a quarter of a century of bloody, civil conflict between East Timor and occupying armed forces from neighboring Indonesia. Fellow countryman and 1996 Nobel laureate Dr. José Ramos-Horta will accompany Father Filomeno Jacob, a priest who led the resistance campaign against the Indonesian militia in East Timor, to the Tanenbaum Center's Sixth Annual Award Ceremony and Memorial Lecture, 6-7:30 PM at the Park Avenue Synagogue (50 East 87th Street, NYC). The annual awards recognize the outstanding contributions of individuals to the cause of interreligious understanding around the world.

The Tanenbaum Center's Peace Activist Award recognizes religious leaders whose lives and freedom are at risk because of their efforts to promote peace and reconciliation in the face of violent conflicts. Through it, the Tanenbaum Center works to assist religiously-motivated individuals to prevent, ameliorate or resolve conflicts around the world, whether they are in Northern Ireland, Bosnia or East Timor. In this respect the Center's objectives are fourfold:

  • To provide a degree of international recognition to individuals who are risking their lives and their freedom in the cause of peace;
  • To create a measurable degree of impact on the ground of the recipients' home countries through case studies which should help not only our activists but others striving for peace to better understand when and how to intervene to resolve conflict;
  • To inspire activists in other hotspots to follow the example of the Peace Activists and to assist these other peacemakers in adapting to use in their own countries the techniques which our Awardees have applied;

  • To provide visibility to the Tanenbaum Center which could make a future channel for assistance and aid from international donors and other NGOs to these hotspots

The Tanenbaum Center plans to identify ten additional Peace Activists each year to help form a global network of religiously-motivated leaders who can consult each other in a time of crisis. A Tanenbaum Center-coordinated website (to be established) will also help develop, maintain and inform this network of activists.

"Bishop do Nascimento, East Timorese parish priests like Father Jacob, and the other individuals recognized by the Tanenbaum Center at this year's Award Ceremony have been instrumental, via their words and their conscientious actions, in healing the wounds of interreligious strife in their communities and countries," said Dr. Georgette Bennett, President of the Tanenbaum Center. "The past year has witnessed religion as a decisive factor in a number of ongoing conflicts-from a slow rebuilding of an entire nation in East Timor to continuing efforts at a lasting peace in Northern Ireland, and as an element in the Presidential elections here in the U.S. The Tanenbaum Center intends to play a vital role in organizing and assisting these peace activists to overcome religious differences and resolve the national struggles they embody."

It is especially appropriate and poignant that this year-in the wake of the Pope's recent visit to the Holy Land and his formal apologies to Jews for persecution by Christians-that Dr. Eugene Fisher is recognized for his efforts with the Rabbi Marc H. Tanenbaum Award for the Advancement of Interreligious Understanding. His writings have helped clarify areas of prejudice and misunderstanding between Catholics and Jews. Dr. Fisher is Director for Catholic-Jewish Relations of the Commission on Ecumenical and Interreligious Affairs of the National Conference of Catholic Bishops, and the only American Consultor to the Vatican Commission for Religious Relations with the Jews (appointed by Pope John Paul II in 1981). William Cardinal Keeler, Archbishop of Balitmore will be presenting Dr. Fisher's award.

Among the other honored guests and award recipients attending this year's ceremonies will be advertising guru Jerry Della Femina, principal of the advertising firm Della Femina/Jeary and Partners and a respected innovator in creative advertising since the 1960s. Mr. Della Femina will be honored with the Tanenbaum Center's Media Bridge Builder Award for his philanthropic work in building bridges of communication between communities. Mr. Della Femina has long used his eminence in advertising for philanthropic purposes and was recently named by Advertising Age as one of the 100 most influential advertising people of the century.

Actor James Earl Jones will take a key role in the ceremony, and Felicia Rashad will serve as Master of Ceremonies. Entertainment will be provided by the Boys Choir of Harlem.

The Tanenbaum Center is a nonsectarian organization whose sole purpose is to apply interreligious understanding as a tool to prevent and resolve intergroup conflicts in the workplace, classroom, and the public arena. Our namesake, Rabbi Marc H. Tanenbaum, was a world-renowned leader in ecumenism, human rights, and social justice. The Center was established shortly after his death in 1992 to carry on his life's work to promote interfaith understanding and take action against the divisive and destructive effects of religious and ethnic hatred.
