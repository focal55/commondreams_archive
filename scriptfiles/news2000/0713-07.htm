Senate Republicans Reject Realistic Testing of National Missile Defense
  WASHINGTON - July 13 - The following is a statement by Tom Z. Collina, Union of Concerned Scientists (UCS) Arms Control Program Director:

By a party-line vote of 52-48, all but three GOP Senators rejected an amendment to the Senate Defense Authorization Bill that would have required realistic testing of the National Missile Defense (NMD) system. This amendment called for testing the NMD system against the types of threats the US Intelligence Community tells us may soon arise -- missiles armed not just with warheads, but with decoys and countermeasures designed to foil our defense. If we do not test the system against such threats, we will never have confidence that this system would work in a real attack.

The amendment, sponsored by Senator Richard Durbin (D-IL), was supported by the Pentagon's chief weapons tester, Dr. Phillip Coyle, Director of Operational Testing and Evaluation. In a letter to Senator Durbin, Coyle stated: "As we move forward, test realism will need to grow with system capability, and it will become more and more important to achieve realistic operational conditions in NMD system tests. This will include realistic countermeasures and engagement conditions."

The Pentagon's Ballistic Missile Defense Organization did not oppose the amendment, and in fact had already called on the Welch Panel of missile experts to review plans for NMD testing against countermeasures, also called for by the Durbin amendment.

How can the Republican Party claim to support NMD if they do not want to conduct the tests that are necessary to show that it will work? What are they afraid of? That tests against realistic targets may show that NMD can not do the job?

The Republican Senators who voted against the Durbin amendment are saying "build it and it will work." By supporting a $60 billion system without adequate testing they appear to be more interested in fleecing Americans than in defending them.
