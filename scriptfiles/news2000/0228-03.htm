Forum On Money In Politics & Campaign 2000: Common Cause, Commonwealth Club To Convene Forum For Campaign Finance Experts, Journalists, Citizens
  

WASHINGTON - February 28 - Common Cause and the Commonwealth Club are cosponsoring a Forum on Money in Politics and Campaign 2000 at 12:30 p.m. on Thursday, March 2, 2000 at the Commonwealth Club, 595 Market Street, San Francisco, Calif. Journalists interested in attending should call the Common Cause Press Office at 202/736-5770 to reserve a spot.

The forum is open to the public, and will be an opportunity for a broad discussion on the role that money plays in politics, especially the 2000 election. The forum will explore issues such as how to make elections more competitive, the impact of big money on the political and legislative process, the role of television and the Internet in elections, public views on campaign reform issues, and ways to improve civic participation. The forum will be moderated by Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger.

Charles Kolb, President of the Committee for Economic Development, a nonpartisan public policy organization representing business and academic leaders, and Cheryl Perrin, executive director of the Campaign Reform Project, another group of business leaders devoted to reform will serve as panelists. Both groups are partners with Common Cause in a series of forums across the country on the role of money in politics. Joining them will be Rebecca Avila, executive director of the Los Angeles City Ethics Commission; television journalist Belva Davis; and novelist Richard North Patterson.
