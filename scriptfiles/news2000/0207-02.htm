Corvallis Group Organizes WTO Teach-In; Webcast to Feature Corvallis WTO Teach-In
  

CORVALLIS, OR - February 6 - A local group has scheduled a teach-in on the World Trade Organization and the "Battle in Seattle" for 7 p.m. Tuesday, Feb. 8 at the Majestic Theater, 115 S.W. 2nd in Corvallis. Composed primarily of local residents who were in Seattle during the WTO Ministerial early in December, Corvallis Action on Globalization has arranged for seven speakers to appear in an event expected to run two and a half to three hours.

The event will center on the issues that brought approximately 50,000 protesters to Seattle to protest the WTO's structure and operations. A broad coalition including human rights activists, environmentalists, labor and others converged in Seattle to participate in marches, rallies and protests in an attempt to call public attention to the WTO.

Scheduled speakers at the teach-in include Wayne Anderson of the United Steelworkers of America; Chuck Willer, Coast Range Association; Manuel Pacheco, professor of philosophy at Oregon State University; journalist and filmmaker Paul VanDevelder; Albert V. Krebs of the Corporate Agribusiness Research Project; Alan Kapuler, research director of Seeds of Change; and Sharon Scott, social worker and member of Benton Pacific Greens.

The speakers will respond to questions from the audience following individual 15-minute presentations related to such issues as labor, agriculture, the environment, democracy, and human rights.

Local sponsors of the event include Benton Pacific Greens, Carpenters Local 1094, the Coast Range Association, the Committees for Correspondence, Students for World Awareness - Crescent Valley H.S., the Corvallis Environmental Center, the Corvallis Page, First Alternative Co-op, Jobs with Justice, the Just Peace Committee of the First Congregational Church, KBOO Radio 100.7 FM, NW Earth Institute, OPEU - OSU Classified Employees, and the United Steelworkers of America.

Admission to the teach-in is $3.00, though no one will be turned away for lack of funds.

Webcast to feature Corvallis WTO teach-in

Audio of Tuesday evening's WTO teach-in at the Majestic Theatre will be available to anyone in the world with Web access via a "webcast", according to Sam Ettaro of Unisun Communications, Corvallis.

Ettaro said that he was attracted to the teach-in, organized by Corvallis Action on Globalization, as a subject for a webcast by "the potential to educate people who may not be up to speed on just what is happening with the WTO and the general 'globalization' of the economy: what it means, where it's taking us, and the direct impact on our everyday lives...

"Also, it's very important to keep the momentum going after the Seattle protest. We need to be sure that we keep this issue in the forefront of people's minds. It's all-encompassing and affects every single aspect of our lives."

Information on the webcast is available on the World Wide Web at www.unisun.org.

Potentially, the webcast can make the teach-in available to tens of thousands of people around the world, Ettaro said. People listening to the event on the web will be able to participate in near real-time by e-mailing questions or comments through the Unisun website as the event is underway.

"Right now, due to monetary constraints we're going to have a limited number of feeds available," Ettaro said. "For now, I'm just telling people: 'The early surfer catches the feed!'

Ettaro founded Unisun three years ago with the intent of basing his company on the internet. He began live webcasts in January when he covered a performance of Kerosene Dream at the Fox and Firkin in Corvallis. A few days later, he webcast a performance by Greg Brown.

"During the Greg Brown show," Ettaro said, "we received responses from Quebec, St. Paul, Colorado, Alaska....All over the place." The teach-in begins at 7 p.m. Tuesday, Feb. 8.
