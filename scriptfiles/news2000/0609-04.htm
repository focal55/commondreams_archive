U.S. Senator Paul Wellstone, Author Jonothan Kozol Go to Bronx, NY School to Address High Stakes Testing and Educational Inequality in America
  

WASHINGTON - June 9 -

Monday, June 12
U.S. Senator Paul Wellstone (D-MN), one of the most progressive voices in Congress, will join Jonathan Kozol, author of Death at an Early Age and Amazing Grace, at a press conference and round table discussion on High Stakes testing and inequality in education. The Principal, teachers and students from Public School 30 in the Bronx, NY which Kozol wrote extensively on in his most recent book, Ordinary Resurrections, will participate in the discussion. The event will be held Monday, June 12 at 10:30 a.m. at Public School 30, 510 East 141st St. Bronx, NY.

Wellstone is a national leader on the issue of high stakes testing and in the fight to increase federal funding for public schools. He introduced S. 2348 in April, which would ensure that states and school districts meet professional standards of educational testing by using multiple measures of student achievement when they assess student progress. As an educator turned Senator, he has fought for decades to increase educational funding for all children.

Kozol has told the story of the millions of poor children in America who are denied fair and equal educational opportunities. He is one of the nation’s most powerful voices in the fight for racial justice and equal opportunity for all of America’s children.
