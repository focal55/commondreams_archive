Headwaters Heroes Come Forward to Post $250,000 Bond
Groups Decry Effort to Block Access to Justice
  EUREKA, CA - July 19 - With help from Headwaters heroes, Sierra Club and Environmental Protection Information Center today took the unprecedented step of posting a substantial bond to maintain a preliminary injunction against logging adjacent to the Headwaters Reserve. The large bond was required by Judge Quentin Kopp as a condition for keeping the injunction in place until trial. "If we continue to be called upon to post large bonds, we will face a huge obstacle to citizen enforcement of public interest environmental laws," said Alex Levinson, Sierra Club Legal Director.

EPIC and Sierra Club had challenged the approval of a 705-acre logging plan adjacent to, and surrounded by, the newly created Headwaters Reserve in northern California. When Judge Kopp issued the preliminary injunction July 10, he noted that the California Department of Forestry had violated the law in prohibiting public comment on major changes to the logging plan. The plan would have severely impacted one of California's last healthy coho salmon streams.

Kopp ruled that "A believer in orchestration might reasonably conclude CDF's actions were intentionally executed to prevent public exposure or comment" and that issuing the injunction was in the public interest.

Plaintiffs were stunned by the requirement to post $250,000 to maintain the injunction. "Requiring significant bonds flies in the face of the intent of these laws, that citizens be able to enforce laws when the government is actively engaged in violating them," said EPIC's Paul Mason. "EPIC and the Sierra Club never would have been able to bring the dozens of successful cases in the public interest if we regularly had to post such bonds."

"It took a Herculean fundraising effort, the high profile of Headwaters Forest, and the extreme generosity of people like Don Henley, Bonnie Raitt and James Garner for us to raise this bond in such a short period of time," said Kathy Bailey of the Sierra Club. Rock musician and environmentalist Henley donated all of the proceeds from his Tuesday concert in Berkeley to help raise more than half of the $250,000 for the bond. "This extreme generosity in the name of justice and protection of the forests is truly inspiring," said Bailey.

"A bonding requirement is a polluter's wildest dream," said Mason. "It bars the courthouse doors to public-interest litigants by requiring them to be wealthy as well as right."
