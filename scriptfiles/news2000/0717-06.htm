Environmental President 2000?
National Environmental Group Asks Voters to Speak Out Online on Candidates and Issues
  

WASHINGTON - July 17 -

WHAT: LIVE INTERNET WEBCAST

WHERE: www.foeaction.org

WHEN: 4-5 P.M. Thursday July 20

Voters nationwide will have a chance to weigh in on Presidential candidates and environmental issues during a live internet town hall meeting Thursday, July 20 from 4-5 p.m. EST hosted by Friends of the Earth Action, a national environmental group.

The meeting, to be webcast as a call-in show live on the internet, will offer information on candidates' records and seek voter feedback on who should get the environmental vote. In a first for national environmental groups, Friends of the Earth Action is using a live internet event to take the pulse of citizens nationwide on their presidential preferences. Live calls and comments will be taken and questions answered by FoE Action president, Dr. Brent Blackwelder. The event will be open to the public online at www.foeaction.org. Participants will be able to email questions and comments into the show at webmaster@foeaction.org, or by calling 1-800-3514894
