ACORN Demands End to Predatory Lending
   PHILADELPHIA - June 25 - Chanting "Predatory Lender - Criminal Offender," over 2,000 members of the community group ACORN will march through Center City, Philadelphia ending with a rally demanding that the Federal Reserve Bank use its authority to stop unscrupulous and abusive mortgage companies. The group will stop along the route to demonstrate at the offices of financial institutions which engage in or fund predatory mortgage lending - the targeting of poor, minority, and elderly homeowners for high cost loans, which are often more than the borrower can afford and lead to the loss of their homes.

ACORN members will depart on buses for the march at 11 a.m. on Monday, June 26 from Temple University's Liacouras Center at Broad and Montgomery in Philadelphia and will assemble at a location to be announced. The March will end with the rally at the Federal Reserve Bank on 7th Street at 12 noon.

"Six years ago, Congress gave the Federal Reserve the power to prohibit unfair and deceptive lending practices on home loans, but they haven't done anything about it," said Maude Hurd, ACORN National President. "For six long years the Fed has just been sitting by, letting these con-artists and loan sharks rip off our neighborhoods."

The rally and protest are part of the organization's "Fighting for Our Future" 30th Anniverary National Convention and culminates a week of actions against discriminatory and predatory lending. In the past week, ACORN filed a landmark lawsuit against Wells Fargo/Norwest Bank charging them with racial steering via the internet. On Sunday, 300 ACORN members marched on the suburban home of Advanta Corp. President William Rosoff and staged a symbolic foreclosure in protest of Advanta's predatory lending. Also on Sunday, over 1,000 ACORN members canvassed North Philadelphia neighborhoods in a grassroots effort to make the community a "predator-free zone."

From an organization founded by welfare mothers in Little Rock, Arkansas, ACORN has grown to become the nation's largest organization of low and moderate income people with over 150,000 members in 34 cities. Organizational accomplishments include:

-- Leading campaigns throughout the country to pass living wage ordinances which require companies that receive public assistance to pay their employees a living wage. ACORN has passed seven such ordinances and has assisted other communities in passing over more than 40 other living wage ordinances.

-- Ground-breaking community reinvestment agreements with some of the nation's largest banks and helping over 30,000 families to realize their dream of becoming a homeowner.

-- The establishment of alternative public schools in lower income areas of Brooklyn, Queens, Jersey City, Chicago, and St. Paul. Improved school facilities and governance in Chicago, New York, San Jose, Little Rock, and Bridgeport.

-- Increased voter registration in lower-income communities by striking down barriers to voter registration in Atlanta, Little Rock, Bridgeport and Pine Bluff, Ark. and the registration of over 500,000 new voters since 1980.

ACORN kicked off its recent campaign against predatory lending last summer and has been fighting on several fronts to stop this problem: holding community meetings and workshops to educate the public; researching and documenting the problem and its impact on communities; exposing the abuses of specific mortgage companies; pressing state and federal regulators to investigate lenders and take appropriate action; counseling individuals who had received predatory loans; and advocating for legislative change at the city, county, state, and federal level.
