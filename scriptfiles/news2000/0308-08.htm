Drilling Not the Answer to US Energy Needs
  

WASHINGTON - March 8 - Statement of Athan Manuel, PIRG Arctic Wilderness Campaign:

The United States will never drill our way to energy independence, no matter how may new areas are opened up for oil and gas exploration and development.

Clearly, more oil drilling is not the answer. Even if oil drilling was allowed in the coastal plain of the Arctic Refuge, off of the Florida Panhandle, or off new parts of California's coast, the U.S. would still import more than half of its oil.

Unfortunately, Senator Murkowski appears to care more about Big Oil than he does about energy policy. If Senator Murkowski really cared about energy independence he wouldn't have voted against raising our nation's corporate auto fuel economy standards. He also wouldn't have sponsored legislation that repealed a ban on the export of Alaskan crude oil in 1995. As a result, millions of barrels of crude are being diverted from California to China and other Asian countries.

What we need is an energy policy that focuses more on energy efficiency and renewables than on ruining some of the last unspoiled areas left in the U.S. We have plenty of oil rigs -- and plenty of pollution from the burning of fossil fuels -- but there is only one Arctic National Wildlife Refuge. It should preserved, not plundered.
