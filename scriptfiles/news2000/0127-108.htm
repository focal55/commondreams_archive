New Hampshire Voters Want Tax Breaks for Polluters Eliminated
  WASHINGTON - January 27 - Most New Hampshire residents want tax breaks for polluting industries eliminated, according to a recent poll by Friends of the Earth. The poll found that 66 percent of respondents favor eliminating corporate welfare for polluters. Only 23 percent of those questioned favored keeping the tax breaks in place. Support for cutting the tax breaks is strong among both Democrats and Republicans.

"This poll shows that Bill Bradley and John McCain are onto something by attacking corporate welfare to polluting industries," said Gawain Kripke, Friends of the Earth's economics campaigns director. "New Hampshire voters strongly favor eliminating unfair tax breaks."

When asked how to use the money saved from the eliminated tax breaks, nearly half of New Hampshire residents favor using the funds to save Social Security and Medicare programs. Alternatively, 27 percent of respondents prefer using the money for additional environmental protection and 22 percent want to use the money to pay down the national debt.

Democratic candidate Bill Bradley and Republican candidate John McCain have proposed eliminating a variety of special tax breaksfor polluting industries as part of their policy proposals.

Friends of the Earth recently reported that the cost of tax breaks to oil & gas and mining industries has been growing in recent years, rising to $20.1 billion over the next five years.

"Tax loopholes reward corporations for polluting the air and water while placing a greater tax burden on the public" said Brian Dunkiel, Friends of the Earth's director of tax policy. "These unnecessary and harmful subsidies should be eliminated."
