Social Security Coalition to Respond to Bush Speech Today
   WASHINGTON - May 15 - News Advisory:

The Sign the Pledge Coalition, organized by The Campaign for America's Future, will hold a press briefing directly following George W. Bush's speech that will explain what they call the inherent flaws in his "principles" for privatizing Social Security.

Speakers will include, Roger Hickey, Coalition Director, and leaders of national women's organizations, the labor movement, civil rights groups, senior citizens and young people, churches and disability rights activists.

The Sign The Pledge Coalition is a powerful organization of citizen groups demanding that all candidates for Federal office explain to the voters where they stand on the questions of the future of Social Security and Medicare. The pledge calls on candidates and elected officials to oppose privatizing Social Security, support measures to lower poverty rates among elderly women and oppose efforts to turn Medicare into a privatized voucher system.

Vice President Al Gore is scheduled to sign the groups' Pledge next week, affirming his commitment to protecting Social Security.
