Statement of Gene Karpinski, Executive Director on the Forest Service’s Draft Plan to Protect the Last Wild Forests
   WASHINGTON - May 9 - The good news is that the proposal on the table today has the elements of a strong forest protection policy that meets the vision of the president and the American public.  But the bad news is that the specific proposal preferred by the Forest Service falls far short of that vision.

Now it’s time for the American public to let their voices be heard again to ensure that the final proposal truly protects our national forests.

The American public made it clear that it wants real protection for pristine wilderness areas in our national forests. That means no more logging, road building or other destructive activities in these areas.

We are very disappointed that the Forest Service draft proposal, while prohibiting road building, allows logging in these forest wilderness areas and fails to protect the Tongass altogether. Leaving the Tongass out of a final forest protection plan would be like leaving the Grand Canyon out of the National Park System. The Forest Service must close these loopholes in the final wild forest protection plan. The final plan must protect all pristine wilderness areas in all national forests, including Alaska’s Tongass, from all destructive activities, including road building and logging.

Let’s hope that the Forest Service listens to the public, reverses its present course, and issues a final plan that closes the loopholes and achieves real protection for the last of our wild forests."
