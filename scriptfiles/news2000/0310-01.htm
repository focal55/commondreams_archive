Diverse Coalition to Explain Why Thousands Will Protest World Bank/IMF Policies in April
Organizers of Spring Protests Expose Other Side of Wolfensohn's Globalization Story
  

WASHINGTON - March 10 -

Who: Mobilization for Global Justice
Kevin Danaher - Global Exchange
Njoki Njoroge Njehu - 50 Years is Enough Network
Dennis Brutus - Jubilee 2000 Afrika

When: 2pm Tuesday, March 14

Where: National Press Building, Zenger Room (13th Floor)

The press conference immediately follows a luncheon speaking engagement by World Bank President James Wolfensohn at the National Press Club.

Members of the Mobilization for Global Justice will explain why thousands of people will converge in Washington, DC April 16 to protest the International Monetary (IMF) and the World Bank. The speakers will reveal the harmful social, economic, and environmental impact of World Bank and IMF programs.

The event marks the one-month countdown to protests timed to coincide with the annual Spring Meetings of the World Bank and IMF. The convergence on Washington is composed of a broad coalition of student, environmental, labor, human rights and development groups that have planned ten days (April 8-17, 2000) of non-violent protests and educational events focused on the World Bank, IMF and WTO.

Many of the groups that helped organize the Seattle WTO protests are participating in the Mobilization.
