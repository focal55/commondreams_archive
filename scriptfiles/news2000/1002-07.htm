Vigil Participants Say the Price is Not Worth It: Economic Sanctions on Iraq Must End
  PHOENIX, AZ - October 2 - In April 1999, the United Nations Humanitarian Panel issued a report based on their assessment of the humanitarian situation in Iraq. The panel concluded that "the gravity of the humanitarian situation of the Iraqi people (was) indisputable and (could not) be overstated."

Today, members of End the Economic Sanctions Against the Iraqi People-Valley Coalition will hold a peace march (5 p.m.) and a vigil/performance piece (7 p.m.) at Trinity Cathedral in Phoenix. Trinity Cathedral is located at 100 W. Roosevelt St., Phoenix.

Religious speakers participating in the candlelinght vigil include: Monsignor Edward Ryle, director of the Arizona Catholic Conference; Rabbi Albert Plotkin, rabbi emeritus of Temple Beth Israel in Scottsdale; Deacon Veronica Ritson of Trinity Cathedral; Rev. Mary Jacobs, Disciples of Christ; Stan Shantz, pastor of Trinity Mennonite Church; Rev. Jo Ed Miller, Presbyterian Church (U.S.A.); Imam Latif Al-Gafaji and Nasjid Al-Rousel. During the solemn ceremony, a bell will ring every 10 minutes in remembrance of an Iraqi who has died as a result of economic sanctions. Candles will be lit as a singer sings a song of peace, hope and healing, followed by a moment of silence.

A performance piece, We Think the Price is Worth It, will be performed by Brophy and Xavier High School Justice Club members and Scottsdale Community College dance students. The performance piece is based on Secretary of State Madeleine Albright's statement to Leslie Stahl during an interview on 60 Minutes. When Leslie Stahl asked her if half a million dead children in Iraq was a price worth paying, Albright replied that it was a difficult choice, but "we think the price is worth it." Members of the End the Economic Sanctions Against the Iraqi People-Valley Coalition disagree.
