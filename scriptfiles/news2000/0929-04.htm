Hundreds of Students Besiege Commission on Presidential Debates
  WASHINGTON - September 29 - The following was released today by the Open Debate Society:

-- The People Will Say in Boston OPEN THE DEBATES!

-- Hundreds of Students Besiege Commission on Presidential Debates

-- 8 Arrested During Early Morning Sit-in; 85,000 Sign Petition for Nader

Eight members of the Open Debate Society were arrested yesterday at the offices of the Commission on Presidential Debates (CPD) and charged with unlawful entry. For the past three weeks picket line speak-outs and non-violent direct actions have erupted at the corporate headquarters of Wagner Communications and the Brewer Consulting Group, Inc., firms located at 1200 New Hampshire Avenue, the address given on the CPD's Web site and literature.

Next Tuesday, October 3, thousands of democracy activists are expected to protest in Boston, site of the first CPD event with George W. Bush and Al Gore. Civil disobedience and disruptions are expected. Citizens will march on Oct. 3 at 5 p.m. from Dudley Square in Boston to the University of Massachusetts, site of debates.

"The debates are the All-Star Game of American politics," says Adam Eidinger, an organizer with the Open Debate Society. "Players don't get to decide who's in the All-Star game, people do." Citizens say numerous surveys of likely voters reveal a great deal of support exists for the Green Party's Ralph Nader (60.9 percent) and Reform candidate Pat Buchanan (58.7 percent) to be allowed to participate in the presidential debates.

Eidinger and students from George Washington University and the University of Maryland delivered 85,000 petitions to the CPD today for Ralph Nader to be admitted. Local police mediated a tense situation when building management refused to allow the activists in, despite an agreement reached the day before to allow the activists into the building at 9 a.m. D.C. Police officer Rayford Larkin carried the petitions to the offices of Wagner and Brewer ending the dispute.

The Open Debate Society occupied the offices of Wagner and Brewer in the early morning hours on Sept 20 and debated employees face to face about the illegitimate nature of the CPD itself. The protests, part of efforts nationwide to open the debates to third party candidates, have focused on corporate sponsors of the debates while others have targeted the regional offices of the Bush and Gore campaigns. Attacking the two candidates' unwillingness to talk about issues like the death penalty, globilization, human rights and defense spending, some protests have resulted in day long occupations and annoying hecklers at public events as was seen yesterday when Gore spoke at the Brookings Institute.

According to Eidinger, "The CPD is a stealthy private corporation controlled by the Republican and Democratic parties and funded by Anheuser-Busch, 3 Com, and U.S. Airways." For more information on the sit-in please visit http://www.sinkers.org or call Adam Eidinger at 202-744-2671.
