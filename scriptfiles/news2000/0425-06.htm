Former UNSCOM Inspector to Speak Out Against Iraq Sanctions Policy
  CONCORD, NH - April 25 - News Advisory

Scott Ritter, former UNSCOM chief weapons inspector, will be in Boston, Nashua and Concord on April 27-28 to speak out against the Iraq sanctions policy before he testifies before Congress on May 3.

Background:

According to Ritter, the sanctions strengthen Saddam Hussein's control over Iraq, "are counterproductive and a stain on the moral character of the United States."

After the 1998 Desert Fox bombings, Ritter blasted the Clinton administration for destroying UNSCOM by fabricating a crisis. He argues that the U.S. now lacks the credibility to insist on sanctions given the fact that intelligence gathered by UNSCOM was used for U.S. military purposes, a complete subversion of the inspections process. He argues that the only way to now deal with Iraq is to work on normalizing relations. The first step would be to trade inspections for sanctions which would stop the preventable deaths of innocent civilians and get inspectors back on the ground to finish the task of confirming disarmament.

New Hampshire Peace Action is sponsoring Scott Ritter and also helped coordinate a Congressional briefing on May 3 when Ritter, Denis Halliday and Hans von Sponeck (both former heads of the "Oil for Food" program who quit in protest of the sanctions) will all be speaking out against the sanctions. The briefing is sponsored by Rep. Kucinich (D-OH), Rep. Conyers (D-MI), Rep. McKinney (D-GA) and Rep. Brown (D-OH) and will be held at 2203 Rayburn, 3 p.m., May 3.

Hans von Sponeck will also be speaking at Harvard University on May 1, 7:30 pm, Emerson Hall, Room 105. Contact Mobilization for Survival at 617-354-0008 FMI.

According to the UN, the Iraq sanctions kill over 90,000 civilians each year, with more than 5,000 children dying each month from disease and lack of proper medications that are banned under the "dual usage" program.
