House Democratic Leader Richard Gephardt, Republican Congressman Amo Houghton, and Religious Leaders Examine Role and Contributions of American Muslims in Public Service
   WASHINGTON - July 12 - Although Muslims are one of the fastest growing faith communities in the United States, stereotypes and misinformation prevent an understanding of how American Muslims are participating in civic life. To further awareness about the role and contributions of American Muslims in the political process, Congressmen Richard Gephardt and Amo Houghton will join The Interfaith Alliance Foundation on Friday, July 14th for "Faith in Public Service: American Muslim Experiences."

This event, the first in a series of forums, will bring together American Muslim leaders, members of Congress, and others for an open and productive discourse that will explore the various roles and achievements of American Muslims in public policy and the interfaith context in which we work.

Founded in 1996, The Interfaith Alliance Foundation is a non-partisan 501(c)(3) non-profit research and educational organization with supporters from over 50 faith traditions, including Buddhists, Catholics, Hindus, Jews, Muslims, Protestants and Unitarians. A commitment to core values, including compassion, civility, and mutual respect for diversity and human dignity, unite us as people of faith and goodwill.
