Common Cause Condemns Congress' Attempt to Stop Justice Department Suit Against Big Tobacco
  WASHINGTON - June 13 - On Thursday, the House is expected to vote on an effort to deny the Department of Justice the funding it needs to complete a lawsuit that has already been started against the major tobacco companies. A vote is expected as part of the HUD/VA Appropriations bill this Thursday in the House.

"As one of the first Attorneys General to bring Big Tobacco to court, I find it appalling that Congress won't let the United States Department of Justice have its day in court against the largest tobacco companies," Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger said. Between 1989 and 1999, the tobacco industry contributed a total of $33 million to the parties and to the candidates. Of that $33 million, $24 million was distributed to the Republicans, and $8.5 million went to the Democrats. In 1999 alone, the industry contributed roughly $2 million to parties. "Unfortunately, this seems to be a situation where campaign cash may drown out common sense," Harshbarger said.

"Time and time again, Big Tobacco gets its way with Congress. The huge infusion of soft money to the parties, and the constant influx of PAC dollars have members of Congress hooked on Big Tobacco cash. The fundamental issue here is that if Big Tobacco prevails, it's the American people who will lose their right to have their day in Court," Harshbarger said.
