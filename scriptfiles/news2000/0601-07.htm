WASHINGTON - June 1 - Media Advisory

for Monday, June 5, 2000

Sierra Club Wetlands and Farming News Advisory

Farmers and Conservation Groups Support Wetlands Reserve Program
Report Shows How Wetland Restoration Can Reduce Flood Damage, Water Pollution, Farmland Loss

WHERE: National Press Club
Lisagore Room
529 14th Street NW
Washington, D.C.

WHO: Farm families from Illinois, Iowa, Minnesota, Wisconsin, North Dakota and South Dakota
Noted hydrologist Donald Hey Sierra Club farm and flood experts

WHAT: The Sierra Club will release a report showing that wetland restoration can help protect family farms, reduce flood damage and water pollution. The report, "Wetland Restoration in Waiting in the Upper Mississippi River Basin," shows that farmers are waiting to restore more than 175,000 acres under the Wetland Reserve Program (WRP) in Upper Mississippi River basin states of Iowa, Illinois, Missouri, Minnesota, North and South Dakota, and Wisconsin -- the sites of the country's worst flood disasters in 1993.

WHEN: 1 p.m. Monday, June 5
