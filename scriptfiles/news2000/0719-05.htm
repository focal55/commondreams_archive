AFL-CIO Urges House To Act On Hate Crimes
  

WASHINGTON - July 19 - The AFL-CIO today urged the U.S. House of Representatives to move immediately to pass the Hate Crimes Prevention Act recently approved by the Senate. "The prevention, swift prosecution and severe punishment of hate crimes is an issue that is of importance to all Americans," said AFL-CIO President John Sweeney, "and we urge the House to move quickly to pass the Hate Crimes bill."

The Hate Crimes Prevention Act would strengthen existing laws covering hate crimes based on race and religion, and would extend the current law to cover sexual orientation, gender, and disability.

President Sweeney also applauded the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights for launching a web-based campaign — United Against Hate — to secure passage of the Hate Crimes bill, and urged "union members and other Americans with access to the internet to learn more about this issue and campaign by visiting www.unitedagainsthate.org."
