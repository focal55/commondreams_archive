Statement Of Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger On The Importance of Reform as an Issue in New Hampshire Primary
   WASHINGTON - February 2 - The voters in New Hampshire yesterday dramatically demonstrated that the issue of special-interest money in politics matters with voters and will be a pivotal issue in campaign 2000.

According to a Washington Post poll, for supporters of Senator John McCain, campaign finance reform was the "most important issue in deciding whom to support." A New York Times poll found that those voting for McCain said that his stand on campaign finance reform mattered more to them than Social Security/Medicare, education, taxes and abortion. More than eight out of 10 voters who supported McCain ranked campaign finance reform as the most important issue. And on the Democratic side, in the close race between Vice President Al Gore and former Senator Bill Bradley, campaign finance reform was a key issue where both candidates competed to show their credentials on the issue.

New Hampshire showed us that if candidates talk about reform, voters will respond. But ultimately, voters bear their share of responsibility for making this happen. Citizens have to demand that the candidates take a stand on reform, and citizens have to realize - despite what entrenched special-interests want them to believe - that their voices do count, and that their votes truly matter.
