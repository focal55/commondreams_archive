Planned Parenthood Applauds FDA Approval of Mifepristone
  WASHINGTON - September 28 - The following is a statement by Gloria Feldt, president, Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

The announcement today by the FDA approving the use and distribution of mifepristone in the United States means that women's personal reproductive decisions have taken another dramatic step forward toward freedom from political intrusion.

We have arrived at the end of a long, frustrating, and often heart-rending wait, and the beginning of a new era. For the last decade, news stories about the impending availability of mifepristone triggered calls from countless women to Planned Parenthood health centers nationwide asking for what they often called "that French abortion pill."

At last, we can tell these women definitively and without equivocation that:

-- Yes, this early option pill will be available to you.

-- Yes, Planned Parenthood centers will begin offering the service very soon -- probably in about a month.

-- And yes, science has prevailed over politics, and we as a society will have the opportunity to get past the intrusions and interference that have impeded women's access to this abortion method all these years.

Reproductive health care is an essential part of basic women's health care. American women will finally have access to the same reproductive health care options that our sisters in Europe have had for all these years and that is simple justice.

Mifepristone will give thousands of American women an expanded choice by giving them a very safe additional option for very early abortions.

Because many health care providers who do not now offer abortions have said they would like to provide mifepristone, more women in more places should soon be able to access this non-surgical early option.

When access is geographically more widespread, women may be afforded a higher degree of privacy, with less fear of harassment, or violence.

Mifepristone thus holds the potential to change the debate about abortion, and in doing so, turn the tide against anti-choice intimidation. For far too long, that kind of intrusion has undermined women's access not only to abortion, but also to the full range of reproductive health care services -- including family planning services that prevent the need for abortion.

Yet even as we celebrate this victory for women and for individual freedom, we must remember that a woman's right to choose is still not secure.

Anti-choice forces will continue to campaign against mifepristone. They will continue to use intimidation, harassment, violence, and political pressure to eliminate women's options.

Planned Parenthood has always been about choices. We will continue to work every day to protect the right of women to make their own responsible choices.

And on this particular day, we applaud the FDA's approval of mifepristone and salute everyone involved for their courage in bringing this important reproductive health advance to American women.
