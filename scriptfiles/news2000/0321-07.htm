Arctic Oil Forces Budget Battle
 Sierra Club and Rep. George Miller call for Responsible Oil Independence
   WASHINGTON - March 21 - As the oil price issue continues to boil, some members of Congress are using the issue to promote their own agendas. A Sierra Club breakfast briefing tomorrow will dig into the core issues, and point out what's posturing and what are workable solutions.

On this past weekend's radio address, President Clinton committed himself to a strong position on lowering oil prices, saying that along with immediate solutions, "we also need to take a longer view -- one that rejects environmentally damaging alternatives, like drilling in the protected and treasured natural habitats of Alaska."

Oil industry allies in the Senate are expected to include $1.5 Billion in Arctic oil revenues in the budget, even though such drilling is not currently allowed. This is meant to force the Administration to come up with offsets if they refuse to drill the Arctic Refuge. In addition Senators Frank Murkowski (R-AK) and Ted Stevens (R-AK) have introduced legislation that would open the Alaska National Wildlife Refuge to oil and gas exploration and development.

At a breakfast briefing on tomorrow, Rep. George Miller (D-CA) will discuss efforts to restore the ban on exporting Alaskan oil to Asia. Sierra Club Alaska and oil experts will offer their analysis of the Senate Budget Committee vote expected Wednesday or Thursday on including non-existent Arctic drilling revenue in the Federal budget. In addition, the briefers will release a report outlining the Club's solutions for short-term relief and long-term oil independence.
