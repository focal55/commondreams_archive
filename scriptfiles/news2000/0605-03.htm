Mental Health Advocates Gear-Up to Mobilize Voters
Project Gives Voice to People with Mental Illnesses
   WASHINGTON - June 5 - News Advisory:

To strengthen the political voice of Americans with mental illnesses, the National Mental Health Association's Voter Empowerment Project on June 7 will train advocates from 19 states to register people with mental illnesses to vote and become a political force in their communities.

NMHA's Voter Empowerment Project ensures that people with mental illnesses are full participants in the electoral process. Advocates and people with mental illnesses learn to conduct targeted voter registration drives by registering people with mental illnesses to vote, survey candidates about their positions on important mental health issues, get the survey results to prospective voters, and then "getting out the vote."

The brainchild of New York consumer leader Ken Steele, the Voter Empowerment Project has registered more than 30,000 people with mental illness to vote in New York City alone.

The National Mental Health Association is the country's oldest and largest nonprofit organization addressing all aspects of mental health and mental illness. With more than 340 affiliates nationwide, NMHA works to improve the mental health of all Americans through advocacy, education, research and service.
