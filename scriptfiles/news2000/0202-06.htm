Greens Electoral Action Resource Services Opens New Center
   CHICAGO - February 2 - News Advisory:

WHO:
Electoral Action Working Group Greens/Green Party, USA and the Chicago Greens/Green Party, USA

WHAT:
Ribbon cutting and opening of the Greens Electoral Action Resource Services (GEARS) Center

WHEN:
Thursday, Feb. 3, at 10 a.m.

WHERE:
202 S. State, Suite 1500

DETAILS:
As the national elections season begins, the Green Party gears up for action.

On Thursday, Feb. 3, the Electoral Action Working Group of the Greens/Green Party, USA and the Chicago Green Party will open for the Greens Electoral Action Resource Service (GEARS) Center.

Located in the Chicago Loop at 202 S. State St., the GEARS Center will be the touchstone of independent and coordinated campaigns of Green Party through the United States. The GEARS Center 15th floor office is staffed to further the work of the Electoral Action Committees objectives of networking the more than 70 current Green Party Officeholders through GONet Green Officeholders Network as well as provide support for the Green Party candidates. GONet is a program of GEARS. The GEARS Center will be the legislative research bureau for Green-elected officials and candidates.

In addition, the GEARS Center will be a resource for achieved Green public policy accomplishments and plans. "In the coming election cycle, GEARS will be vital to spreading the Green Party message of social justice and environmental concerns," said Karen Harris, the GEARS Center office manager. While the main office of Greens/Green Party, USA are located in Lawrence, Mass., the GEARS Center expands the organization capacity and focuses our work," said Harris.

"There are many people that support the ideals of the Greens but have not known how to reach us, or where to sign up. GEARS will be a place that volunteers interested in election work can contact GEARS and get connected with local campaigns in their area or national efforts," said Marc Loveless, chairperson, Greens/Green Party, USA Electoral Action Working Group. "We don't have the problems of the major parties or current media darlings. Our objectives are clear from the grassroots up. From sea to sea and providences beyond we will change American public policy," said Loveless. "We will be an incubator for Green campaigns and programs and then take those programs right where they matter: to the people," said Loveless.

The Green Party has been organized in the United States since the 1980s. The Greens/Green Party, USA is active with International Green Party associations and dialogues throughout the world. Since then, more than 100 people have been elected to public office as Greens. Most serve on local townships, cities, county boards and governing bodies of school educational boards and councils. In 1996, the Green Party candidate for president was consumer activist Ralph Nader.

This year, the Greens will choose from a field of four candidates for the Green Party presidential nomination, including: Jello Biafra (Eric Boucher), lead singer of the rock group Dead Kennedys; Stephen Gasken, activist and author of such publications as "Cannabis Spirituality"; Dr. Joel Koval, college professor and former Green Party New York U.S. Senate candidate; and Ralph Nader, national consumer activist and attorney. In addition, the Greens and the GEARS Center will be encouraging Congressional candidates through the country. Districts and contact information for all national candidates will be available through the GEARS Center, as well as information on other national Green activities.

The GEARS Center is the Greens nationally getting our collective act together.
