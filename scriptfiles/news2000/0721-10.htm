New Poll Shows Women Move from Bush to Gore...On Guns
  WASHINGTON - July 21 - News Advisory:

On Tuesday, July 25 at 9:30 a.m. at the National Press Club, Handgun Control will release the results of a new survey that reveals the influence of the gun issue on the 2000 presidential election. The survey, conducted by Hart Research Associates, sampled more than 800 voters nationwide and oversampled 300 suburban women in the acknowledged battleground states of Pennsylvania, Michigan, Missouri, Ohio, Illinois and New Jersey.

The survey explored public attitudes on specific gun issues and how those issues could play a significant role in the 2000 presidential election. The survey demonstrates that the candidates' positions on the gun issue significantly influences the vote in November, and that the level of public (and specifically suburban female) support for common-sense gun laws remains overwhelming.
