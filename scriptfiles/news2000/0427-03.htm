Jubilee 2000 Leaders to Further Examine Third World Debt Crisis with IMF, World Bank, Others at Harvard Conference
   WASHINGTON - April 27 - News Advisory:

At the World Bank and International Monetary Fund spring meetings last week In Washington, D.C. chief among the issues discussed was the debt crisis that is crippling some of the planet's most impoverished nations. Now the debate moves to Boston, as key figures in the debt dialogue meet at Harvard University.

Date:
Monday, May 1

Time:
8 a.m.-4:30 p.m./Q & A media session 3:15-4:30 p.m.

Place:
Taubman Conference Center, Harvard University

The Center for International Development at Harvard University, in conjunction with Jubilee 2000, the international movement for the cancellation of unpayable debts for the poorest countries in the millennial year, is organizing a conference entitled "HIPC Debt Relief -- Past Movement, Future Prospects". High-profile panels will discuss the implementation and limitations of the Heavily Indebted Poor Countries or HIPC initiative and possible reforms and replacements.

Kofi Annan, secretary general of the United Nations, UNCTAD, all African heads of state, the G77 South leaders, world religious leaders, environmental groups and even the Republican-dominated Meltzer Commission are calling for 100 percent cancellation for the poorest countries. The current HIPC package agreed by the G8 world leaders falls far short of this goal.

Among those scheduled to speak:

-- His Excellency F.G. Mogae, president of Botswana
-- Jeffrey D. Sachs, Harvard University
-- Alan Meltzer, co-author of the recent Meltzer Report
-- Dan Driscoll Shaw and Ann Pettifor, Jubilee 2000
-- Karin Lissakers, U.S. executive director for the IMF
-- Axel von Trotsenburg, director of HIPC at the World Bank
-- Representatives from WHO, UNDP and the U.S. Treasury

On the same day as the conference, Pope John-Paul II along with musicians Lou Reed and the Eurythmics are appearing at the May1st Jubilee celebrations in Rome to make an urgent appeal to the international community for immediate action on debt cancellation The Pope has consistently championed the cause of Jubilee 2000.
