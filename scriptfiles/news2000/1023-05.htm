League of Conservation Voters to Release 2000 National Environmental Scorecard
   WASHINGTON - October 23 - News Advisory:

WHO: League of Conservation Voters, Deb Callahan, president

WHAT: Release of LCV's 2000 National Environmental Scorecard

WHEN: Tuesday, October 24
10 a.m.

WHERE: Omni Los Angeles Hotel
Crocker Room
251 South Olive Street, Los Angeles, CA

Background Information:
Recent polls show that the American public strongly supports environmental protections, especially for clean air and water. A poll by Peter D. Hart Research released this week found that 86 percent of voters say that clean air and water are important concerns, 15 percentage points higher than those who say cutting taxes is important to them and three points higher than those naming the economy.

To hold members of Congress accountable and assist citizens in making environmentally conscious decisions, the League of Conservation Voters (LCV) will release the 2000 National Environmental Scorecard at a 10 a.m. news conference, Tuesday, October 24, at the Omni Los Angeles Hotel. Every member of the U.S. House and Senate will be given a rating for their environmental votes during the first session of the 106th Congress.

Based on the consensus of experts from 25 respected environmental, conservation and sportsmen groups, LCV's National Environmental Scorecard assigns a percentage score to each member of Congress based on the year's key environmental votes, LCV has produced a Scorecard for each Congress since 1970.

LCV's Deb Callahan will present the 2000 Scorecard findings and analysis to reporters and answer Scorecard-related questions at Tuesday's news conference.

The League of Conservation Voters is the political voice for the national environmental and conservation community. LCV is the only national environmental group dedicated full-time to informing citizens and holding members of Congress accountable for their environmental votes. For more information about LCV or the National Environmental Scorecard, visit www.lcv.org.
