Amnesty International Launches Campaign to Stop Torture
  WASHINGTON - October 12 - Amnesty International, the world's largest human rights organization, will launch a global campaign to stop torture on Oct. 18. New research on torture practices around the world and a fast-response Internet action network to protest torture will mark the beginning of the year-long campaign.

Amnesty International's new report, "Torture Worldwide: An Affront to Human Dignity," based on research on 195 countries and territories from 1997 to mid-2000, examines torture and ill-treatment by police, in prisons, and in armed conflict as well as judicial corporal punishment. The report, embargoed for Oct. 18, reveals that torture remains one of the most widespread human rights violations, with officials inflicting torture or ill-treatment in over two-thirds of the world's countries, including the United States.

"Torture is an affront to human dignity that can never be justified and must be stopped in every corner of the world," said William F. Schulz, Executive Director of Amnesty International USA (AIUSA). "Those who try to justify torture are as guilty as those who inflict the beatings, electric shocks, rapes, or mock executions."

AIUSA will galvanize its members to take action via a new Internet-based Fast Action Stops Torture (FAST) network. FAST allows activists to send thousands of electronic messages directly to torturers or their superiors within hours of receiving an e-mail or cell phone alert that someone is at risk of torture. A series of Internet events on launch day, all accessible at www.amnestyusa.org, will seek to raise awareness about torture, encourage participation in the campaign, and raise funds to aid survivors of torture.
