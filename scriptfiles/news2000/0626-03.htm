Decoding of Human Genome Underscores Urgent Need for Federal Action to Prevent Genetic Discrimination
   WASHINGTON - June 26 - The race to decode the human genome was completed today, opening an era of new possibilities for the use of genetics in medicine. Today's announcement promises better health for millions of Americans, but that promise will be kept only if Congress passes comprehensive federal protection from genetic discrimination. Without immediate action, the fears of losing health insurance or a job because of genetic information will only escalate.

Our legal system lags dangerously far behind the genetic revolution. A patchwork of state and limited federal laws leaves Americans vulnerable to genetic discrimination by health insurers and employers. Already, this fear is preventing people from seeking tests and participating in research that could help them lead healthier, more productive lives.

The Coalition for Genetic Fairness calls on Congress to pass comprehensive federal protection from genetic discrimination in health insurance and employment. It's time for our public policy to catch up with medical technology for the sake of Americans' health.

------
The Coalition for Genetic Fairness was founded by the Alpha-1 Association, the Genetic Alliance, Hadassah, the National Partnership for Women & Families and the National Work Rights Institute to urge Congress to pass comprehensive legislation outlawing genetic discrimination by insurers and employers. The Coalition represents thousands of Americans in civil rights, patients and health care organizations working to secure federal protection from this emerging threat.
