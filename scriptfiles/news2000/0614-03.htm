Physicians Group Condemns Congressional Drive to Create New Nuclear Weapons
   WASHINGTON - June 14 - Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR) Tuesday condemned plans by Senate Republicans, led by Sen. Wayne Allard (R-Colo.), to allow the Department of Energy to develop a new line of nuclear weapons. This will permit development of a small, bunker-busting nuclear weapon designed to take nuclear war to the regional level.

Senate supporters of the Allard Amendment see a new regional nuclear weapon, working in tandem with the National Missile Defense as a guarantee of nuclear weapons use with impunity.

"In voting against the Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty last year, and now moving to mandate development of regional nuclear weapons, the Senate is promoting nuclear proliferation and risking the catastrophe of nuclear war," said Robert K. Musil, Ph.D, executive director and CEO of Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR). "This sends a signal to the world that the U.S. places a high value on nuclear weapons, and are not prepared to disarm. Other countries are bound to follow us into this new arms race."

The proposal for new nuclear weapons development is contained in an amendment to the FY2001 Defense Authorization Act, currently under debate in the Senate. The amendment requires a DOD/DOE study of the need for a small, bunker-busting warhead and allows research and development necessary to develop such a new weapon. The weapon would be intended for use in conflicts such as the Gulf War to attack national command facilities, such as Saddam Hussein's bunker under Baghdad. This move makes clear the motivation behind the Republican Leadership in the Senate's drive to defeat the Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty in 1999. That treaty would have prevented testing of such a new warhead, a necessary step in new weapon development.

"The world is moving to prevent proliferation of nuclear weapons, as a step in preventing the ultimate health emergency -- nuclear war. Ending testing and development of new weapons is a necessary part of that vital task," said Kimberly Roberts, security program associate. "Now Sen. Allard wants to move in the opposite direction, lowering the bar for nuclear use, threatening millions of deaths and environmental disaster in a nuclear war, by accident or even by design."
