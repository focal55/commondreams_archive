National Parties Raise Record $160.5 Million In Soft Money Through First 15 Months Of 2000 Election Cycle
Congressional Campaign Committees Triple Their Receipts Over 1995, According to Common Cause Study
  

WASHINGTON - April 27 - The $160.5 million in soft money raised by the national parties in this perios was nearly double the $84.6 million raised during the first 15 months of the 1996 election cycle, the last comparable presidential election period, according to the study.

Republicans raised $84.3 million in soft money, while Democrats raised $76.1 million in the 15 month period.

In the first quarter alone, Democrats slightly outraised Republicans, $26.7 million to $26.5 million.

The full text of the study is available online at: http://www.commoncause.org/soft_money/april00/
