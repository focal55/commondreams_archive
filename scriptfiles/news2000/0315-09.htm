Divestment of ARCO Holdings in Alaska is Good News for Environment, Consumers, Alaska
 

WASHINGTON - March 15 - Statement of Athan Manuel, PIRG Arctic Wilderness Campaign:

The decision of BP Amoco and ARCO to sell ARCO's Alaska holdings to Philips Petroleum is good news for America's Arctic. Allowing BP Amoco to take over ARCO Alaska's holdings would have given one company control of almost 75 percent of the oil from America's largest oil field. Hopefully, the divestiture will ensure a competitive environment on the North Slope.

In our numerous meetings with commissioners and staff we urged the Federal Trade Commission to block the BP Amoco - ARCO merger, or at a minimum to force BP Amoco to divest ARCO's holdings. We are pleased that the FTC initially blocked the merger, and is now negotiating the divestiture.

It's good news that BP Amoco has finally agreed to divest. They should have never tried to monopolize the oil from America's Arctic. Today we repeat our call for them to be a responsible corporate citizen and cancel their drilling plans for the coastal plain of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge.

We also call on Philips Petroleum to recognize the unique wilderness values found in the Arctic Refuge and ask them to pledge not to drill in the coastal plain.
