Sierra Club Calls on Rep. Bliley to End Website Attack
  WASHINGTON - February 17 - The Sierra Club today called on Representative Thomas Bliley (R-VA) to stop his shut-down of vital information from the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. By knocking the EPA's website -- www.epa.gov -- off-line, Bliley is keeping Americans from learning about the environmental dangers they face.

"Congressman Bliley's attack on information doesn't change the fact that every American has the right to know when dangerous chemicals are being released in their own neighborhoods. Congressman Bliley should stop trying to keep Americans in the dark about local environmental and health threats," said Kathryn Hohmann, Director of Sierra Club's Environmental Quality Program.

This isn't the first time that Bliley has attempted to intimidate the EPA and prevent public disclosure. Last year, Bliley tied up important provisions that would have given Americans information about the potential for chemical disasters facing their regions.

"Last year, Congressman Bliley's scare tactic was telling the public that terrorists could obtain information about what chemicals are being stored at industrial facilities. Now, Congressman Bliley is using hackers as an excuse, but his goal is the same: locking us all out of information about pollution that affects our communities and families," Hohmann continued.

"By shutting down the web site at the EPA, Rep. Bliley is keeping the public from learning about pollution prevention programs, clean water partnerships, law enforcement efforts and more. Rep. Bliley's action shuts the public out of knowing the amount of toxic chemicals a company releases into a community and pollution levels in lakes and streams," she added.
