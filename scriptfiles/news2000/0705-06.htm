Coloradans Rally For Debt Cancellation; Religious Leaders to Call for Debt Relief Funding
   DENVER - July 5 - News Advisory:

This Saturday, July 8, Coloradans will gather in the heart of downtown Denver to add their voices to the international chorus calling on elected officials to begin the new millennium by erasing the multi-billion dollar third world debt.

What: Turn Up the Heat on Debt Relief Human Chain and Rally

Where: On the Green behind Currigan Hall
(corner of Speer Blvd. and Stout St.)

When: Saturday, July 8 1-3 p.m.

Participants will form a "Human Chain" along Speer Blvd. to symbolize the debt that now enchains over forty countries, most of them in Africa.

The rally, sponsored by Jubilee 2000 Colorado, will feature statements by religious leaders, including Episcopal Bishop of Colorado Jerry Winterrowd and Methodist Bishop Mary Ann Swenson, as well as representatives from other religious denominations. Right Reverend Simon Chiwanga, a leading Anglican Bishop in Tanzania, will give the key-note address.

Dr. Jeffrey Sachs, Harvard economist, Bono, Archbishop Desmond Tutu and Rev. Billy Graham are among those who have supported the call for debt relief to help address HIV/AIDS, hunger and illiteracy in the developing world. Nonetheless, Congress has yet to come up with the funds to support the current initiatives.

The event is supported by the Episcopal Church Office on Government Relations in conjunction with the General Convention being held in Denver. Episcopalians from around the country are expected to join the Human Chain.

The rally coincides with a Summit of the G7 finance ministers to plan the agenda for the upcoming G7 Summit in Okinawa, Japan. Jubilee 2000 worldwide is calling on the leaders of the G7 to address the debt crisis at this summit.

Development specialists maintain that debt cancellation is in the interest of the United States and would present virtually no financial hardship to industrialized nations.
