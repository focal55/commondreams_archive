Handgun Control, Center to Prevent Handgun Violence Unveil New Ads in Support of Million Mom March
   WASHINGTON - May 1 - Led by television talk-show host Rosie O'Donnell, five Hollywood celebrities have donated their voices for a new ad campaign sponsored by Handgun Control and the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence dramatizing the problem of children and guns and garnering support for the Million Mom March Across America this Mothers Day, May 14.

The new ads, featuring voice-overs by celebrity moms Rosie O'Donnell, Susan Sarandon, Whoopi Goldberg, Gloria Estefan and Sarah Jessica Parker, will premiere on the Rosie O'Donnell show today (Monday, May 1), and will begin airing in at least a dozen markets on cable networks the same day. The ads were produced by Rosie O'Donnell and Laughlin, Marinaccio & Owens Advertising.

The ads use home video footage of six American children who died at the hands of guns and urges parents who are concerned about the high levels of gun violence in our nation to participate in Million Mom March events across the country. The children featured in the ads are:

-- Ryan Williams, Muncie, Ind., age 14, accidentally shot and killed;
-- Zachary Clements, Houston, Texas, age 13, accidentally shot and killed by a friend;
-- Alex Stasenka, Fairfield, Calif., age 14, shot and killed by a friend accidentally;
-- Louis D. Brown, Dorchester, Mass., age 15, murdered on his way to a Christmas party of "Teens Against Gang Violence," a local anti-violence organization he had joined;
-- Zachary Borgman, Battle Creek, Mich., age 2, shot accidentally with his father's handgun;
-- Omar Soto, Deltona, Fla., age 11, shot and killed accidentally by a neighbor.

The Million Mom March Across America is a citizens' grass-roots movement which began after a mother from New Jersey watched on the news about the Granada Hills day care shooting only a few months after the nation's worst school shooting in our history at Columbine High School. The images of terrified children, about the same age as her own, being led in a line by police from the carnage that had just taken place inside sparked her into action. She came up with the idea of a movement of mothers like herself who are concerned for the safety of their children when it comes to guns, and she decided to mobilize these mothers in a nationwide demonstration called the Million Mom March.  

The Million Mom March and Handgun Control share long-term goals: support for gunowner licensing, gun registration, and closing the loopholes that allow children and criminals easy access to guns. Handgun Control is a primary sponsor of the Million Mom March and the new ads will recruit concerned parents across the country into long-term activism for common-sense gun policies.

"We are very grateful to Rosie and to the other celebrities who lent their voices to this important effort," said Sarah Brady, HCI Chair. "Their determination to prevent the unnecessary deaths of children from gun violence and accidents will help us call attention to the Million Mom March in the two weeks leading up to Mother's Day. And those thousands of moms who show up on Mothers Day will help call attention to this cause as never before."

On May 1, Handgun Control will honor Rosie O'Donnell for her leadership and dedication at a special salute in New York City, where we will be joined by Sen. Charles Schumer, Rep. Carolyn McCarthy and Nita Lowy, actress Lauren Bacall, and other special guests.

------
Handgun Control, chaired by Sarah Brady, was founded in 1974 by Dr. Mark Borinsky and N.T. "Pete" Shields, two victims of gun violence. Based in Washington, D.C., HCI works with law enforcement, public health, religious, and community groups across the country to strengthen and protect federal, state and local gun control laws, but does not seek to ban all guns. HCI has more than 400,000 members nationwide, making it the nation's largest citizens' gun control lobbying organization.

Handgun Control's sister organization, the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence, was founded in 1983 and is also chaired by Sarah Brady. It seeks to reduce gun violence through education, legal advocacy, research, and outreach to the entertainment community. The Center's national initiatives include violence prevention programs for youth, education programs on the risks associated with guns, legal representation for gun violence victims, reform of the gun industry, and research on the risks associated with guns and on the efficacy of gun control laws. More information about both HCI and CPHV can be found on the websites at www.handguncontrol.org and www.cphv.org.
