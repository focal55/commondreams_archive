Poll: US Women Could be Major New Constituency Supporting International Issues
  NEW YORK - June 4 - On Monday, June 5, members of Congress will discuss the challenges and opportunities of building a constitutuency for international affairs in the context of a new poll: "Connecting Women in the U.S. and Global Issues."

Reps. Joseph Crowley (D-NY) and Carolyn Maloney (D-NY) will speak at a 3:30 pm EDT press conference in the United Nations Press Conference Room (Room 226), UN Headquarters, East 46th St. and 1st Ave., New York City (NOTE: UN Media Accreditation is required for entry to UN Facilities.)

Also speaking will be Joan Dunlop of the Women's Lens on Global Issues, which commissioned the poll, and Nancy Belden of the polling firm Belden, Russonello & Stewart.

This comprehensive poll on U.S. women's interest in global issues finds a significant potential for mobilizing women on behalf of international aid, multilateral organizations and a "good neighbor" role in the world for the United States. At the poll's release, the members of Congress will comment upon its significance for their efforts to build a U.S. constituency in support of international affairs.

The survey report, "Connecting Women in the U.S. and Global Issues," shows that American women, contrary to their image, recognize that the world has become a highly interconnected place and believe it is essential that the United States take an active role in world affairs because of the impact global events have at home.

Commissioned by Women's Lens on Global Issues, the poll is being released on the eve of the United Nations General Assembly Special Session called "Women 2000: Gender Equality, Development and Peace for the 21st Century." It offers the first known baseline survey of women's interest in foreign affairs and their sometimes ambivalent views of globalization and the U.S. role. It outlines the values that contribute to women's interest in engagement and the types of engagement women welcome most.

More information is posted on the journalists' Web site: www.women2000newsroom.org and the Women 2000 NGO Media Center.
