Clinton Levels Veto Threat at Bankruptcy ‘Reform’
Wellstone Says Delay Gave Opponents Time to Mobilize; Looks Forward to Legislation’s Defeat
   WASHINGTON - June 29 - U.S. Senator Paul Wellstone praised President Clinton’s threat to veto the so-called ‘Bankruptcy Reform Bill,’ issued today from the White House. In his letter to Congress, the President sent a clear message that the bankruptcy legislation which has emerged from the shadow conference fails the test of balance, and will result in undue hardship to middle income working families who find themselves in desperate fiscal circumstances. Wellstone expressed optimism that the President’s threat would greatly strengthen the bill’s opponents in Congress.

“I am delighted that the President has issued a strong veto threat of this terrible legislation today. This is an enormous step forward in our efforts to defeat this bill, and I look forward to working with the President to defeat it. It has taken a long time to get people to focus on the brutal effects this bill will carry in its wake, and it has been a long, hard fight. To have the President’s strong veto threat advances our cause immeasurably,” Wellstone said.

In February, the bill was passed in the Senate by a large bipartisan margin of 83-14, after having passed late in 1999 in the House by a margin of 313-108. In March, Wellstone blocked the House and Senate from going to conference on the bill by use of a procedural prerogative. Since then he has refused to remove his objection to the measure, and led the effort to educate Congress and the public about the harsh impact of the legislation on families and seniors.

“In the time since the Senate passed a flawed, harsh bankruptcy measure, the real story about bankruptcy in America -- as opposed to the skewed version propounded by the big banks and credit card companies -- has slowly come to light. We now know that bankruptcy is nearly always a last resort undertaken primarily by low and middle income families who file in reaction to a financial catastrophe like a job loss or insurmountable medical bills. We now know that the vast majority of these families – over 96% – do not have sufficient income to pay back any of their debts. We now know that bankruptcy is a critical safety net for these families that allows them to turn their lives around, have a fresh start, Wellstone said.
