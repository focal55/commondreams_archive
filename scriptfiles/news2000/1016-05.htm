Illinois Nader Campaign And Green Party Launch Take-Back-Our-Debates Caravan To St. Louis Protesting Ralph Nader's Exclusion From The Debate And Corporate Sponsorship By Anheuser-Busch
   CHICAGO - October 16 - Local supporters of the Green Party presidential ticket will join forces with their colleagues from across Illinois on Tuesday morning to protest the exclusion of Green Party presidential candidate Ralph Nader from the debate to be held in St. Louis at 8 p.m.

In St. Louis, Illinois Nader supporters will join rallies and protests outside of the corporate-sponsored debate, which only includes George Bush and Al Gore.

“People are getting angrier about the arrogance of the major parties. Who do they think they are? First, the Democrats spend months trying to take Nader off the ballot in Illinois; now they conspire together to keep Nader out of the debates.” said Dan Johnson-Weinberger, Illinois coordinator for the Nader campaign. “Ralph Nader has drawn the biggest crowds of any candidate – 10,000 in Chicago last Tuesday, 15,000 in New York last Friday. The corporate-backed parties must not be allowed to silence a candidate favored by millions of progressive voters.”

The statewide caravan of Nader supporters will get its start in Chicago on Tuesday morning at 8 a.m. on Solidarity Drive, near the Adler Planetarium. From there, the caravan will split into two groups, one heading to Bloomington-Normal for a rally there, and the other to Champaign-Urbana and then on to Decatur. Both groups will meet in Springfield, and then together roll down to St. Louis. Groups from Peoria and Carbondale will also join the caravan.

At each city, Green protestors will call attention to the exclusionary tactics of the two-party system and to the need for a grassroots democratic movement to take political control away from corporate sponsors and lobbyists and return power to voters.

The caravan will arrive heading to the Washington University campus in St. Louis, where the Republican and Democratic candidates, George Bush and Al Gore, are scheduled to appear in a televised debate sponsored by Anheuser-Busch Brewing Company.

Nader will be in St. Louis Tuesday along with hundreds of his Illinois supporters.
