Anti-Environmental Riders Rock the House and Senate
   WASHINGTON - July 21 - The Sierra Club condemned anti-environmental votes in both the House and Senate today, which passed appropriations bills loaded with anti-environmental riders. These riders, which are important policy changes attached to must-pass legislation, threaten streams with cyanide-laced mining waste and actually kill an effort to require that federal contractors obey federal law.

In the House, anti-environmentalists blocked a proposal to simply reaffirm that compliance with federal laws, including environmental law, is part of the test for determining whether a contractor can be eligible for federal contracts. The amendment to the Treasury Appropriations bill was introduced by Representative Tom Davis (R-VA).

"It's amazing that anyone would oppose the idea that federal contractors shouldn't break the law," said Ed Hopkins of Sierra Club's Environmental Quality Program. "Who wants known abusers of environmental safety measures to receive our tax money?"

In the Senate, a rider struck a blow to environmental protection despite efforts to defeat it. The rider undermines badly-needed environmental safeguards for the hardrock-mining industry. Existing safeguards haven't kept pace with modern mining practices, which now involve massive open pits and the extensive use of toxic chemicals such as cyanide and sulfuric acid in the open environment.

In a victory for the environment, Senator Baucus was successful in his effort to remove language from the bill that would have transferred public lands from a National Seashore and National Wildlife Refuge in North Carolina for a controversial, costly, and environmentally harmful jetty project in Oregon Inlet.

"Despite the efforts of environmental leaders in the Senate, enemies of the environment chose to ignore public support for environmental protection and voted instead to help polluters and special interests," said Melanie Griffin, Director of Sierra Club's Land Protection Program. "Members of Congress are attacking our nation's democratic process when they use riders to undermine environmental protection and circumvent public debate."
