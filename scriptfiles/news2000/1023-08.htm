National Religious Leaders Warn Clergy of Partisan Election-Strategy Targeting Churches
Clergy Urged To Be Stewards of Information Not Political Pawns;
Asked to Reject Christian Coalition’s 70 Million Deceptive “Voter Guides”
  WASHINGTON - October 23 - National religious leaders of diverse backgrounds will convene a press conference on October 25th at the National Press Club to warn the nation’s clergy of the Christian Coalition’s partisan election-strategy targeting churches with 70 million blatantly partisan pamphlets distributed under the guise of information on the Sunday before the election.

The Interfaith Alliance will release “Dear Clergy” letters sent by local religious leaders to houses of worship in states including Florida, Michigan, Minnesota, Missouri, Ohio, New York and Wisconsin. Letters respectfully urge clergy not to distribute voter guides or partisan literature because of ethical and legal considerations.

Religious leaders will also unveil a non-partisan, faith-based ‘GOTV- Get Out The Vote’ campaign enlisting clergy to encourage informed civic participation.

WHAT:
The Interfaith Alliance Press Conference rejecting the distribution of partisan voter guides in houses of worship and urging people of faith to cast informed votes.

WHERE AND WHEN:
Wednesday, October 25 -- 9:30 am

Founded in 1994, The Interfaith Alliance (TIA) is a non-partisan clergy-led grassroots organization dedicated to promoting the positive and healing role of religion in the life of the nation and challenging those who manipulate religion to promote intolerance. With more than 120,000 members drawn from over 50 faith traditions, clergy-led local Alliances in 38 states, and a national network of activists in every state, TIA promotes civility, mutual respect, and cooperation in our increasingly diverse society.
