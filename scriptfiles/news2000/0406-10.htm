National Day of Solidarity With The People Of Vieques, Puerto Rico
"Not One More Bomb In Vieques, Puerto Rico"
   WASHINGTON - April 6 - On April 19th, a broad coalition that encompasses elected officials, trade unions (AFL-CIO), religious groups, over 30 support committees from 20 major U.S. cities, and thousands of Puerto Ricans nationwide, will march against President Bill Clinton's presidential directive to resume bombing on the island of Vieques, Puerto Rico. This day marks the 1st anniversary of the death of David Sanes Rodriguez, killed as a result of a U.S. Navy F-18 aircraft dropping a 500 pound bomb in Vieques.

Furthermore, the coalition will urge Attorney General Janet Reno not to order the removal or arrests of Puerto Ricans who have established civil disobedience camps on the island and who are acting as human shields against further bombing.

For almost 60 years, the people of Vieques have suffered human rights violations. They have been exposed to toxic hazardous materials, including napalm and depleted uranium, as well as live fire. This has resulted in the deaths of many innocent civilians and continues to jeopardize the health and safety of almost 10,000 residents (Viequenses). As such, we oppose any pending legislation which calls for the continued bombing of Vieques, Puerto Rico.
