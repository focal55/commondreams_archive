Jesse Jackson: Rocker Comments Not Out of Context
Civil Rights Leader Asks Braves, MLB to Join Ban of Confederate Flag
   CHICAGO - February 2 - Today, the Reverend Jesse Jackson, president and founder of the Rainbow/PUSH Coalition, released the following comments upon hearing news that Major League Baseball Commissioner Bud Selig suspension of John Rocker for inflammatory comments published in Sports Illustrated.

Selig banned Rocker, of the Atlanta Braves, from joining the team until May 1 because of disparaging comments against homosexuals, minorities and foreigners. The Rainbow/PUSH Coalition wrote Selig in December after the comments were made public, asking for clarity.

"What Mr. Rocker said was offensive and embarrassing to baseball," Jackson said. "The commissioner appropriated punishment to show intolerance with language that insults players and fans.  While we believe that Mr. Rocker's statements were divisive, we must not separate his statements from the cultural ideology that fuels (such comments). In this regard his statements were not out of context.

"Major League Baseball cannot play under a Confederate flag in Georgia with some expression of indignation and without some affirmation of one America, one flag.  Mr. Rocker's published comments actually reflect an ideology that must now move beyond the legacy of the Cotton Curtain of yesterday and embrace the one America of today. 

"The Braves should support the removal of the Confederate flag-surely it is hypocrisy to punish Mr. Rocker for his comments, yet take no position on having players play under a symbol that represents bigotry, hatred, sedition and racial supremacy.

"He must not be used as a scapegoat for a crisis in our culture that deserves healing," he said. "The time has come to remove the Confederate symbol. We hope Major League Baseball and the Atlanta Braves would join this effort."
