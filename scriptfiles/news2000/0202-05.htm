Senate Backs Effort to Hold Abortion Clinic Violence Perpetrators Accountable
   WASHINGTON - February 2 - In a hard-fought pro-choice victory, the Senate today passed an amendment that ensures that perpetrators of abortion clinic violence cannot escape judgments against them.

The amendment to the Bankruptcy Reform Act (S. 625) offered by Sen. Charles Schumer (D-N.Y.) ensures that criminals who commit acts of violence against abortion clinics, women and doctors cannot avoid paying fines and penalties by declaring bankruptcy. The Schumer Amendment makes clinic violence-related debts "non-dischargeable" under the U.S. Bankruptcy Code.

"This is a major victory for the women of America and the doctors who serve them," said NARAL President Kate Michelman. "Today, the Senate has taken an important step to ensure that those who harass and intimidate women and commit acts of violence against clinics are held accountable for their crimes."

Until the final vote, it was unclear whether the Schumer amendment would pass. The vote count appeared to be so close that the Democratic Leadership called Vice President Gore yesterday to ask him to revise his campaign schedule and journey to Washington today to cast a potentially tie-breaking vote. After the Vice President's arrival and a press conference featuring Gore and the amendment's supporters, including NARAL, Sens. Trent Lott (R-Miss.) and Orrin Hatch (R-Utah) instructed their majority to vote for the Schumer amendment, which subsequently passed by a vote of 80-17.

"Clearly, the anti-choice majority in the Senate, fearful of handing the Vice President and Democrats a key campaign issue, caved in to political pressure and chose to support the same amendment they had been debating against all morning," said Michelman. "Without the Vice President's willingness to stand up for the health, safety, and reproductive rights of American women, this vote would never have been possible."

"Manipulation of the bankruptcy code is now a regular tactic in the wider campaign by anti-choices forces to intimidate and threaten women seeking abortions and the physicians who provide them," Michelman added. "The Bankruptcy Code is intended to give law-abiding Americans a second chance when faced with overwhelming debt. But individuals whose debt is the result of repeated violations of the law are not 'honest debtors' and should not be allowed to use the law for essentially criminal purposes.

"Sen. Schumer is to be applauded for ensuring that the promise of FACE is not undermined and for his leadership and principled stand on this important issue."
