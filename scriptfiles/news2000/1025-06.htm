Environmental Groups Say:
Don't Hold Everglades Hostage
Republican Leadership Holds Everglades Hostage to Questionable Water Projects
   WASHINGTON - October 25 - Environmental groups today urged the Republican leadership not to hold passage of the Everglades restoration plan hostage to an agreement to pass a costly and environmentally destructive water projects bill. The groups include American Rivers, Coast Alliance, Defenders of Wildlife, Environmental Defense, Friends of the Earth, Sierra Club and the National Wildlife Federation.

Senate leaders have told Senator Bob Smith (R-NH) they will not permit approval of the Everglades restoration bill unless he also agrees to support a water projects bill filled with new subsidies and questionable projects Smith opposes.

Last Friday, the House of Representatives passed a $6 billion Water Resources Development Act (WRDA) that contains scores of new dredging, flood control and "infrastructure" projects that have not been subject to environmental and economic review. Despite recent reports of abuses by Army Corps planners, WRDA failed to include significant reforms and instead included new subsidies for deep port dredging and risky beachfront development.

Senators Smith and Max Baucus (D-MT), Chairman and the highest-ranking minority member of the Senate Environment Committee, have rejected questionable projects and provisions during negotiations with House members.

The House and Senate WRDA includes the bipartisan Everglades Restoration Act.

Congressman Bill Young, Chairman of the House Appropriations Committee, has previously stated that he will move the Everglades bill separately if there is no agreement on a broader water projects bill.

This new pressure is an effort to force Senator Smith, who has championed the Everglades bill, to agree to a water projects bill that violates established procedures for approving new projects, conservation groups say.
