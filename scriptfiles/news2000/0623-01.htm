Heed Graham's Last Words: Call for a Moratorium
Statement by the Reverend Jesse L. Jackson, Sr.
  WASHINGTON - June 23 - "I witnessed one of the most heart-wrenching events imaginable last night: state-sanctioned murder. The legal lynching of Gary Graham was one of the most reprehensible things a state can do to one of its citizens. I wept uncontrollably.

"In the few moments he had before he was put to death, Gary Graham not only continued to proclaim his innocence, but he challenged us all to end this senseless, state-sanctioned  slaughter we call capital punishment. He said, 'Push the moratorium for all Black people.'  We must take his challenge and go even further. We must push the moratorium for ALL people.

"We have information proving that across the nation the death penalty system is so judicially bankrupt that it would only be continued by a government that is morally bankrupt. I meet people every day who are outraged by the continued practice of capital punishment. Those who watch polls cannot help but notice that support for the death penalty stands at its lowest level in almost 20 years, and that support drops dramatically when life in prison without the possibility of parole is an option. But leaders must do more than measure public opinion, they must mold it.

"More jails aren't the answer. Bold leadership that imposes a moratorium is the answer. The system is too fundamentally flawed to continue tinkering around its edges with anything less than a moratorium. Congressman Jesse Jackson Jr. is leading the call for a moratorium with his H.R. 4162, The Accuracy In Judicial Administration Act of 2000 (AJA).  The AJA calls for a minimum 7-year national moratorium on all executions until all inmates sitting on death row right now have a chance to explore potentially exculpatory DNA and other evidence.

"But DNA evidence is no silver bullet. DNA does not racial profile, police do. DNA does not abuse confessions out of suspects, police like Commander Jon Burge do. DNA does not preemptively strike minorities from the jury, prosecutors do. DNA does not cross-examine witnesses and challenge circumstantial evidence, competent lawyers do. DNA cannot hire you a legal 'dream team,' defendants with money do. DNA does not test the ballistics of a gun, an adequately-funded crime lab does. DNA does not have political ambition, prosecutors do. DNA does not appoint boards of pardons and paroles, governors do. DNA does not appoint federal judges, presidents do. DNA does not have political aspirations

"Yes, DNA is very important, but it would not have helped Gary Graham. Just
like it cannot help most people sitting on death row right now." 

-"Why does H.R. 4162 call for a seven-year moratorium? The Death Penalty Information Center reported that the average time between a capital conviction and execution is eight years.  The average time that innocent people have spent on death row before proving their innocence is seven years. In 1996, Congress passed the Anti-terrorism and Effective Death Penalty Act (AEDPA), which effectively cut an inmate's rights to appeal a capital conviction to four years. H.R. 4162 is the only bill that completely halts executions nationwide.

"H.R. 4162 also uses the Voting Rights Act's proven mechanism for state compliance with national law, allowing states to re-certify their death penalty systems in a way that remains consistent with constitutional law and federal policy.

"The imposition of death must also be taken out of the hands of people running for public office. That only creates state-sponsored murder that leaves all of us twisting in political winds. Last night, George Bush followed Bill Clinton's example. In 1992, Bill Clinton left the campaign trail to execute mentally retarded Ricky Ray Rector. Yesterday, George Bush left the campaign trail to execute Gary Graham. He has turned his state's death row into an assembly line.  If the Elian Gonzalez leadership model is followed, Al Gore will certainly not be outdone, and the first federal execution in 37 years will go forward on August 5th.

"This madness must be stopped. We must all, on either side of the death penalty issue, have the collective courage to do what must be done. We must have a moratorium on the death penalty, and we must have it now.
