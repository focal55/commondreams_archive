Violence Policy Center Faults President's Call for Federal 'Smart' Gun Subsidy
   WASHINGTON - January 27 - Commenting on President Clinton's call during his State of the Union address for millions of dollars in grants toward development of so-called "smart" guns, the Violence Policy Center tonight issued the following statement:

What President Clinton called for tonight was nothing less than a subsidy for the gun industry, whose products claim more than 30,000 lives every year in this country. "Smart" gun technology cannot decrease gun homicides or suicides, but will increase them as the total number of gun owners increases. (One gun maker has predicted 60 million new gun owners.) Whatever positive effect these proposed "smart" guns could have on unintentional firearm deaths and injuries could be better achieved with existing low-tech devices such as child safety locks, load indicators, and magazine disconnect devices. Above all, the Violence Policy Center asserts that the gun industry needs to be federally regulated for health and safety standards-and definitely not subsidized.
