Statement of Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger on Confirmation Hearing for Bradley Smith, FEC Nominee
   WASHINGTON - March 8 - At a time when the need for an effective Federal Election Commission has never been greater, we should be strengthening it with Commissioners who are fair-minded, and who believe in the laws they are sworn to administer and enforce.

Not even on the current Commission - as weak and ineffective as it is - do any of the current Commissioners share the kind of outright hostility to basic campaign finance laws displayed by Professor Smith. It is no wonder that Smith is the hand- picked choice of Senator Mitch McConnell and Majority Leader Trent Lott - two Senators clinging to the increasingly outmoded idea that we need more money in politics rather than less, and less protection from the corrupting influence of big money rather than more.

The full Senate should reject Bradley Smith to serve on the FEC because of his fundamental disagreement with the law he would be sworn to enforce. We urge every Senator who voted for campaign finance reform in the Senate last year to reject this anti-reform nominee and vote "no" on Bradley Smith.
