90-Year-Old 'Granny D' Scheduled to Complete Cross-Country Trek for Campaign Finance Reform at U.S. Capitol
   WASHINGTON - February 24 - News Advisory:

Legendary activist Doris "Granny D" Haddock is scheduled to complete her 3,000-mile trek across the country for campaign finance reform on Tuesday, Feb. 29, when she arrives at the East Steps of the Capitol at 12:30 p.m. for a press conference with supporters from inside and outside Congress.

Haddock, who began her walk in Los Angeles on Jan. 1 of last year, has celebrated her 89th and 90th birthdays on the road. She has since become a media sensation and folk-hero, profiled in The New York Times, People magazine, National Public Radio, and named one of the most fascinating women in American politics by George magazine.

Granny's Route on February 29, 2000

o 8:30 - 9 a.m. Assembles at Arlington Cemetery Metro Stop; walks across Memorial Bridge
o 11:15 - 11:45 a.m. Walks along Reflecting Pool to 17th Street; north to K Street Farragut Square
o 11:45 a.m. Arrives at Freedom Plaza, 14th Street and Pennsylvania Avenue
o 12 p.m. Leaves Freedom Plaza, walks down Pennsylvania Avenue toward Capitol
o 12:30 p.m. Press Conference on the East Steps of Capitol.

The day's events will finish with a potluck reception at 6 p.m. in St. Mark's church, near the Library of Congress at 3rd & A Streets, S.E., in Washington, D.C. For more information, call the Common Cause press office at 202-736-5770 or visit Granny's Web site at www.grannyd.com.
