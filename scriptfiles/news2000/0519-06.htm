NOW to Release Feminist Primetime Report, Set to Name "Network of Shame"
  WASHINGTON - May 19 - The National Organization for Women (NOW) is targeting one of the four major networks for protests by naming it a "Network of Shame."  As part of NOW's Watch Out, Listen Up! campaign, teams of feminist analysts across the country rated primetime programs on ABC, CBS, FOX, and NBC by documenting incidents of violence, gender composition/stereotypes, sexual exploitation and social responsibility.  NOW President Patricia Ireland will release the results at a demonstration in New York City's Bryant Park on Monday, May 22, 2000 at 12 noon.  NOW will also recognize the network with the highest marks as a "Primetime Leader."
 
WHO: NOW President Patricia Ireland & NOW-NYC Activists

WHAT: Network of Shame Announcement and Protest

WHERE: Bryant Park (42nd Street & 6th Avenue)

WHEN: Monday, May 22, 2000 at 12 Noon


NOW activists will organize protests at affiliate stations of the national "Network of Shame" in cities across the country.  Demonstrations in Los Angeles and Washington, DC already are scheduled for Tuesday, May 30, 2000.
