Sierra Club, NAACP, UAW link for Supreme Court
  WASHINGTON - September 29 - On opening day of the U.S. Supreme Court's 2000-2001 term, prominent Members of Congress and leaders of national grassroots organizations will host a news conference to unveil details of their final push to mobilize voters around this presidential campaign's most urgent issue: the future of the Supreme Court.

With the next President likely to appoint as many as three new justices to the high court, more Americans than ever recognize the Court's importance as an election issue. A right-wing Supreme Court would wreak havoc on many of the rights and liberties that Americans have come to take for granted over the past half century.

When: Monday, October 2, 2000, 10:00 a.m.

Where: Across from the Supreme Court, large Senate grassy area (1st Street between Maryland and East Capitol)

Who: Gloria Steinem, president, Voters For Choice Debbie Sease, Legislative Director, Sierra Club Ralph G. Neas, president, People For the American Way Hilary Shelton, Washington office Director, NAACP Ellie Smeal, president, Feminist Majority Gerald McEntee, president, American Federation of State, County, and Municipal Employees Nat LaCour, executive vice president, American Federation of Teachers Reps.
