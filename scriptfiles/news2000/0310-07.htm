First Round of Drinking Water Right to Know Reports: How Do They Measure Up?
  

WASHINGTON - March 10 - PRESS ADVISORY

WHAT:
The Campaign for Safe and Affordable Drinking Water, a nationwide alliance of environmental, consumer and public health organizations, will release “Measuring Up: Grading the First Round of Drinking Water Right to Know Reports.”

The Safe Drinking Water Act Amendments of 1996 require most drinking water utilities to produce Right to Know Reports (also known as “Consumer Confidence Reports”) to provide information to consumers about the source of their drinking water, any contaminants found in the water and other issues.

“Measuring Up” is a national survey of the first round of these reports and assesses how well the reports followed federal requirements for content and how well they provided straightforward information to consumers about the safety of their drinking water.

**Findings for the District of Columbia Water and Sewer Authority’s report will be discussed following the national summary.
