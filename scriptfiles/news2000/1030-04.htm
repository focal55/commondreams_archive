Friends of the Earth Challenges World Bank to Declare a Moratorium on Fossil Fuel, Mining Projects
   WASHINGTON - October 30 - In a letter to World Bank President James Wolfensohn, Friends of the Earth International Chairman Ricardo Navarro today called on Wolfensohn to declare a moratorium on World Bank support of fossil fuel and mining projects. Navarro's letter follows up on President Wolfensohn's September pledge at the World Bank Annual Meetings in Prague to work with Friends of the Earth (FoE) to take a hard look at the negative impacts of Bank investments in fossil fuel and mining. A FoE position paper, handed to Wolfensohn by Chairman Navarro argues that the World Bank should phase out fossil fuel and mining projects that are destructive to the environment and local communities because they do not meet the World Bank's stated mission of poverty alleviation.

"Mr. Wolfensohn has promised that the World Bank will take a hard look at the impacts of fossil fuel and mining investments and their negative impacts. Friends of the Earth believes that a good faith demonstration of Mr. Wolfensohn's intent to follow through on the rhetoric is an immediate moratorium on fossil fuel and mining investments," said Friends of the Earth International Chairman Ricardo Navarro.

This letter follows up on a declaration signed by more than 200 organizations representing environmental, human rights, religious, and economic justice organizations from 55 countries called on the Bank to stop financing oil, gas, and mining projects. FoE provides numerous suggestions for the completion of this phase-out and shifting priorities towards investments that directly meet the needs of the poor such as renewable energy. Wolfensohn responded favorably to the paper pledging to look at the possibilities of exclusions from World Bank support within these sectors.

"The World Bank's fossil fuel and mining projects have left a trail of environmental devastation, poverty, and severe social disruption in poor countries," said Andrea Durbin, director of Friends of the Earth's (US) International Program. "President Wolfensohn needs to initiate a process of identifying how the phase out will work including no-go zones that protect local communities and the environment and targeted investments in renewable energy."
