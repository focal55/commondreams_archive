Statement Of Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger On House Passage Of 'Stealth PAC' Disclosure
   WASHINGTON - June 28 - The overwhelming bipartisan majority who voted early this morning for shedding light on the secret money of so-called 527 "Stealth PACs" is a victory for a fundamental principle that just about everyone in America should agree on: that we should have full disclosure of precisely who is paying to influence federal elections. It is also a testament to the growing momentum for campaign reforms, and the growing disgust with politics dominated by special interest money.

This vote was a big defeat for House Republican leaders like Majority Whip Tom DeLay, who on the one hand have given lip service to the concept of full disclosure, but on the other, desperately tried to kill this effort.

The Senate has already voted to disclose Stealth PAC activities in principle, with passage of the McCain amendment. The Senate should act quickly to pass the language approved in the House, before the July 4 recess, and deliver, for the first time in years, a campaign finance reform bill to the White House that the President can sign into law.
