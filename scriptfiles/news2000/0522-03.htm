New Report Issues Warning of Hard Right Shift at Supreme Court
 

WASHINGTON - May 22 -

News Advisory:

A new People For the American Way Foundation report, "Courting Disaster," documents the devastating impact that a further rightward shift on the U.S. Supreme Court would have on Americans' fundamental rights and freedoms. The report, which examines decades of Court rulings on civil rights, reproductive rights, privacy, separation of church and state, worker and consumer rights, environmental protection, and other issues, will be issued on Thursday, May 25, at the National Press Club.

"Courting Disaster" reports on PFAWF's six-month-long examination of the concurring and dissenting opinions of the Supreme Court's two most right-wing justices, Antonin Scalia and Clarence Thomas. The 80-page report concludes that a Scalia-Thomas majority on the Court -- a goal that far right leaders and organizations like Pat Robertson and the National Rifle Association are pushing this election year -- would overturn decades of established precedent, sharply curtailing or abolishing rights and freedoms that many Americans take for granted.

The report documents exactly how adding just one or two Justices who share Scalia's and Thomas' far-right legal philosophy to an already conservative Court could tip the balance on the Court for decades to come.
