Arab Americans Demand End to Israeli Bombing and Occupation of Lebanon
  WASHINGTON - February 8 - The American-Arab Anti-Discrimination Committee (ADC), the nation's largest grassroots Arab-American organization, strongly condemns the Israeli bombing of civilian areas in Lebanon last night. The Israeli bomb attacks, which injured numerous civilians, including women and children, and destroyed three major electrical power plants, were in blatant violation of 1996 cease fire agreements brokered by the United States which barred attacks on civilian targets. Israel has declared the agreement "null and void."

The Israeli attacks come in response to a series of successful military actions by Lebanese resistance forces against Israeli occupation troops in Lebanon. These actions were directed solely against military targets and were staged away from civilian areas. Once again, Israel has chosen to respond to actions directed against its occupation troops by attacking civilian targets.

Israel has occupied southern Lebanon since 1978, when the UN Security Council, including the United States, unanimously demanded in Resolution 425 that Israel withdraw its troops from Lebanon "forthwith" and without condition. For 22 years, Israel has ignored its obligations under UNSCR 425 with impunity. ADC affirms that the Lebanese people have an absolute right to resist Israeli military occupation of their country. ADC also notes that Lebanese resistance against Israeli occupation forces is in furtherance of the demands of UNSCR 425.

Israeli attacks on civilian targets are designed to terrorize the Lebanese population and impede the rebuilding of the country. The Lebanese people suffered for years without adequate electricity during and after the civil war, and Israel has repeatedly attacked Lebanon's power system, most recently in June, 1999. It is outrageous that Israel should be attacking key civilian infrastructure sites at a time when Lebanon is trying to rebuild itself. These unjustifiable bombings are also a major blow to the prospects for peace, and cast further serious doubts over the intentions of Israeli Prime Minister Barak.

ADC demands an immediate end to the unjustifiable Israeli attacks on Lebanon. ADC President Hala Maksoud said "we urge the U.S. government take immediate action to put an end to these attacks. Israel must at long last abide by UNSCR 425 and end its brutal occupation of Lebanon. The international community, including the United States, must ensure that the Lebanese are allowed to concentrate on putting their lives back in order, instead of having their civilians terrorized and their painstakingly rebuilt infrastructure destroyed by Israel."
