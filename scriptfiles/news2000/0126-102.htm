Transparency, Democracy and Accountability Needed for Talks in Davos
   WASHINGTON - January 26 - Pointing to the failure of the WTO meetings in Seattle last year, Friends of the Earth and partner groups say environment, labor and human rights issues cannot be sidelined any longer in discussions about the global economy at the World Economic Forum held this week in Davos, Switzerland.

"This gathering is a test of whether or not world leaders heard the message of Seattle -- all international institutions and forums must open up and become democratic, " said Andrea Durbin, Director of International Programs for Friends of the Earth.

Friends of the Earth is calling on the leaders gathered for the World Economic Forum to recognize that globalization must benefit the majority of the people around the world, not just a small minority or corporate interests. Further, the group insists that when world leaders gather in Davos, they must commit themselves to a new agenda to right the wrongs of globalization and deal with concerns raised by Friends of the Earth and other NGOs.

Specifically, Friends of the Earth has called on government leaders in Davos to: 1) commit to an environmental review and assessment of the past 5 years of the WTO; 2) strengthen Multilateral Environmental Agreements; 3) stop the WTO's interference with international and national environmental law; and 4) negotiate a binding code of conduct for corporations.

"Last year, UN Secretary-General Kofi Annan called on corporations to abide by the basic values of human rights, environmental protection and worker rights," said Durbin. "We are here in Davos to hold governments and companies accountable to this pledge. The biggest gap in the global economy are binding standards for good corporate behavior."

There will be a media conference on NGO concerns with Davos called "The Public Eye on Davos," a joint project of the Berne Declaration, Friends of the Earth/U.S. and the Globalization Challenge Initiative, on Friday, Jan. 28, 2 p.m., at Dutch Asthma Clinic, Scalettastrasse 19, Davos (next to the Congress Centre).
