Frito-Lay Applauded by Agriculture and Environmental Groups
Frito-Lay Praised for Responding to Consumers Concern about Genetically Modified Foods
 

WASHINGTON - February 14 - Environmental and agribusiness groups today joined together to praise Frito-Lays decision to discontinue using genetically modified corn in their products. The Sierra Club and the American Corn Growers Association jointly applauded Frito-Lay's recognition of consumers' concern for the health and environmental impacts of genetically modified food.

"Many people have doubts about the safety of genetically modified corn for their health and the environment," said Laurel Hopwood, Chair of the Sierra Club's Biotechnology Task Force. "We applaud Frito Lay for its decision to respond to consumers' concerns."

"Farmers must grow what their customers demand," noted Gary Goldberg, Chief Executive Officer of the American Corn Growers Association. "If we don't, farmers will lose even more than the $200 million in lost corn export sales in 1999. If the customer wants food that has not been genetically modified, then it is the responsibility of the American farmer to provide that product."

Ms. Hopwood also cited the recent studies on Monarch Butterflies and the persistence of toxins produced from genetically modified corn that stay in the soil.

"We're also gravely concerned about how Bt corn and other genetically modified crops impact the environment. No one should give this technology a green light when it kills unintended insects and beneficial microorganisms indiscriminately." Adding her support for organic farming, she adds, "Pollen carrying genetically modified traits is already contaminating crops grown organically."

The Sierra Club supports a moratorium on further use of genetically modified products until extensive, rigorous research is done which addresses the long-term impacts. The Sierra Club also supports proper labeling of genetically modified products.

"The real issue is the consumer's right to exercise caution in selecting food products," added Hopwood. "Without a label alerting the consumer to the presence of genetically-modified corn in Frito-Lay's products, concerned citizens were forced to go elsewhere. Now, with this courageous decision, Frito-Lay has taken the initial steps to reassure the public that their products are free of uncertain health and environmental threats."

The Sierra Club is the nation's largest grassroots environmental organization, with more than 550,000 members.
