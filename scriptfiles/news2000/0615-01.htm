ABM Treaty Reinterpretation: First Shoot The Lawyers
   WASHINGTON - June 15 - Clinton Administration lawyers who are proposing to reinterpret the 1972 Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty in order to permit a deployment decision of national missile defenses should be marched to the banks of the Potomac and dumped in the river, said Council for a Livable World in a statement released today.

According to the June 15 New York Times ("Clinton Lawyers Give a Go-Ahead to Missile Shield"), Administration lawyers are suggesting that the President can authorize construction at an initial anti-missile site in Alaska without violating the ABM Treaty.

If the President chooses this option, he can move forward toward missile defense deployment -- in theory protecting the political interests of Vice President Gore -- while avoiding announcing a withdrawal from the ABM Treaty and crushing arms control.

These legal tricks are reminiscent of the questionable interpretations of Reagan Administration lawyer Abraham Sofaer who claimed that the ABM Treaty permitted Star Wars in the 1980's.

It is also reminiscent of the legal maneuvers that the Russians tried to use to justify building the Krasnoyarsk radar in the 1980's.

John Isaacs, Council for a Livable World's president, condemned the Clinton lawyers for their ignominious behavior: "These lawyers believe that because the President triangulated the Democratic and Republican parties, he can now triangulate Russia and arms control."

"Rather than trying to twist the treaty's meaning," added Isaacs, "the President should defer a decision on national missile defense deployment until the next Administration."

"National missile defense is not ready for prime time, technologically, politically or legally," concluded Isaacs.

The Council is one of the nation's oldest and largest arms control organizations.
