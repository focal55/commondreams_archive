Anti-I.M.F./World Bank Protests Escalate with Rally for Human Rights in China, Economic Justice in U.S.
Labor unites with enviro and human rights activists at "No Blank Check for China" demonstration
   WASHINGTON - April 12 - The AFL-CIO, the Teamsters, and thousands of activists will join forces in the nation's capitol Wednesday to demand that Congress not grant China Normal Trade Relations status next week. The demonstration comes as the temperature rises in Washington, following protests against military aid to Colombia Tuesday, direct street action on Monday, and Sunday's giant Jubilee 2000 rally for debt relief.

Nigerian World Bank Expert Available for Interviews
Oronto Douglas, lawyer to murdered Nobel Laureate and environmental organizer Ken Saro-Wiwa, will be available for interviews. Douglas is one of the world's leading experts on the environmental and social impacts of World Bank-financed fossil fuel exploitation.
When : 10 am – 2 pm
Where : Convergence Space (1328 Florida Ave NW)
More Info : 202-210-7800

"No Blank Check for China"– Mass Rally on the Washington Mall:
Organized labor will flex its muscles on the Washington mall to stop corporate-backed trade legislation on behalf of the Chinese government and big business. Organizers assert that Normal Trade Relations status puts business interest before American workers and human rights in China and Tibet. Speakers include : renowed Chinese dissident Wei Jinsheng, held for 18 years as a political prisoner; John Sweeney, AFL-CIO president; Jimmy Hoffa, Jr., Teamsters president; and the leaders of the United Auto Workers, the Steelworkers, UNITE, AFSCME, and the American Federation of Teachers. Organizers expect thousands and thousands to attend.
When: Noon, Wednesday, April 12
Where: The Mall, West of the Capitol
More info: Genie Bidwell, 202-637

Mr. and Ms. Smith Go to Washington; or Workers Take the Protest to Congress:
Labor's long decline has turned around, and legislators are taking notice. Join union activists as they team up with environmental and human rights activists to lobby for a fair deal with China –which means no Normal Trade Relations status for a nation that lets corporation use its people as slave labor. Tag along with citizen-lobbyists as they make the rounds in the halls of congress.
