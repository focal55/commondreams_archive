Nader Ad Campaign Backer:
Vote For Nader Everywhere, Including California
   NEW YORK - October 26 - Greg MacArthur, the funder behind Citizens for Strategic Voting, clarified today that he pulled ads from the Los Angeles Times, the San Francisco Chronicle, and the San Francisco Examiner because he thought New York voters were a better target for his limited budget than California voters. MacArthur said he still hopes California voters will cast their ballots for Nader.

"Citizens for Strategic Voting is still encouraging Californians to vote for Nader," Greg MacArthur, the businessman paying for the ads said. "We just think we will get more bang for the buck in New York, where many more people can vote for Ralph Nader without fearing that Bush will win."

The ads are being run in an effort to encourage independents, disgruntled Democrats and McCain supporters to vote for presidential candidate Ralph Nader in states where Vice President Al Gore or Gov. George W. Bush is strongly in the lead. Tim Robbins, Susan Sarandon and Greg MacArthur will discuss the advertising campaign on Wednesday, Oct. 25. (Call Underground Advertising for the dial-in number.)

It is the first ever national political advertising campaign to target voters in non-swing states. Political analysts believe that Nader could receive the necessary five percent or more of the national popular vote to win at least $7 million in matching federal funds in 2004 if people understand that casting ballots for Nader won't help Bush be elected.

"Why Voting for a Candidate Who Can't Win Is the Smartest Thing You'll Ever Do," the full-page newspaper ad headline reads. "And Don't Worry, a Vote for Nader is Not a Vote for Bush."

The independent expenditure ad campaign was paid by Citizens for Strategic Voting -- a group started by businessman MacArthur, grandson of billionaire insurance executive John D. MacArthur (founder of the John D. and Catherine T. MacArthur Foundation) and son to J. Roderick MacArthur, who created the MacArthur "genius awards". Greg MacArthur is a businessman, documentary film producer and former foreign correspondent for the Associated Press.

"People must understand that the President is chosen by the electoral college and not by the popular vote," said MacArthur. "This means that in all but the swing states, people can follow their hearts and vote for Nader without fearing that they will help elect Bush."

"Gore and Nader can both win," MacArthur said. "Gore needs the majority of electoral college votes to become President. Nader needs at least five percent of the popular national vote to win matching funds for a potential presidential run in 2004. Both are easily achievable if people in states where either Gore or Bush is strongly ahead vote for Nader."

Citizens for Strategic Voting is running ads in newspapers read by a large percentages of Democratic and Independent voters. The papers include The New York Times (national edition), the Village Voice, the Boston Globe, the Boston Phoenix, the Los Angeles Times, the Los Angeles Weekly, the Austin American Statesman, the Austin Chronicle, the San Francisco Bay Guardian and the Boulder Camera

"It does not matter if Gore or Bush win a state by one vote or by one million votes," said MacArthur. "But a vote for Nader can invigorate democracy by creating an equal playing field for fresh new ideas."

Voters who wish to see the ads can visit www.undergroundads.com/Nader. Citizens for Strategic Voting urges voters to check the state polls right before election day before making a final judgment.
