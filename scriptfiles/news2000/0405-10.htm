International Grassroots Campaign Targets World Bank Financing
As thousands converge in Washington to protest Bank/IMF policies, activists launch World Bank bond boycott
  WASHINGTON - April 5 -

Who:
Dennis Brutus, Jubilee 2000 Afrika
Kevin Danaher, Global Exchange
Vineeta Gupta, Insaaf International, India
Trevor Ngwane, Alternative Information and Development Centre, South Africa
And representatives from institutions signed on to boycott

When: 10:00 AM, Monday, April 10, 2000

Where: National Press Building, Zenger Room (13th Floor)

The World Bank bonds boycott campaign is a new, international campaign using grassroots economic power to weaken the power of the World Bank, which receives 80% of its financing from publicly traded bonds. Before its official launch, the Campaign has already enlisted Trillium Asset Management and the city of Berkeley, California, among other institutions, in pledges not to buy World Bank Bonds.

World Bank policies of "structural adjustment" are responsible for the greater impoverishment of the poor and the destruction of the environment across the globe.

The campaign, composed of organizations from eleven countries thoughout the world, will now reach out to pension funds, labor unions, churches, municipalities, universities and private investors. We aim to make the World Bank the target of a worldwide financial boycott similar to the anti-Apartheid divestment campaign of the 1980's. The campaign is the financial corollary to the mass mobilizations in Seattle and Washington DC against the World Bank, the IMF and the WTO.
