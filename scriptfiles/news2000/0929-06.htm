Planned Parenthood Action Fund Announces Major Campaign to Educate Women Voters
$10 Million in Television and Mail Targets Key States
  WASHINGTON - September 29 - The Planned Parenthood Action Fund (PPAF) today announced a major media and grassroots campaign to educate voters about what is at stake in the 2000 elections. The media component is a $7 million television ad campaign about the presidential candidates' positions on choice to be unveiled Oct. 2. The grassroots component includes voter guide mailings to 2.2 million households.

This effort marks the first time Planned Parenthood has ever engaged in presidential politics. "In our eighty-four year history, no election has been so critical to women's rights and health," said Gloria Feldt, president of Planned Parenthood Action Fund. "Planned Parenthood is a trusted voice on family planning issues and it's time to raise that voice. This election is too important to remain silent."

Planned Parenthood will spend $7 million on an ad campaign to run in seven key battleground states beginning next week: Florida, Michigan, Missouri, Nevada, Oregon, Pennsylvania and Washington. PPAF will also do direct mail and phone calls in eight states: Florida, Michigan, Missouri, Washington, Rhode Island, Montana, New Jersey and Kentucky.

In addition to PPAF's national campaign, state and local action funds in 46 states are involved in more than 700 races nationwide. Planned Parenthood action funds in California alone will spend an additional $1 million to educate voters in that state about federal, state and local races.
