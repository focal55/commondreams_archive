Activists, Experts From Global South Speak Out Against IMF and World Bank Policies
   WASHINGTON - April 13 - Activists from Africa, Asia-Pacific, Latin America, and the Caribbean in Washington to participate in the (April 8 - 17) Mobilization for Global Justice activities will speak to the media about the impact of International Monetary Fund (IMF) and the World Bank policies and practices.  They will explain the reasons they felt compelled to travel to Washington to participate in the historic series of events bringing thousands to protest the institutions' spring meetings.

The speakers will include: Carlos Chen, a Guatemalan who survived a massacre by his own country's armed forces of villagers who opposed a World Bank-financed dam.  Trevor Ngwane, a South African who sits on the Johannesburg municipal council, will describe the pressure exerted by the institutions to privatize services in the newly-liberated South Africa. Maria Teresa Pascual, who is President of the Freedom From Debt Coalition of the Philippines, will describe the work of one of the oldest and largest grassroots organizations devoted to education and advocacy on the global economic policies that are devastating societies throughout the Southern countries.

Most of the speakers come from countries that have endured many years under structural adjustment, the harsh austerity policies imposed by the IMF and World Bank on countries too indebted to get credit or capital from other sources.  Critiques of those policies will be offered both from the speakers' own personal experiences as well as from their work in analyzing the broad impact of structural adjustment.
