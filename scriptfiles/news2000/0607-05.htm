Advisory: Vote Lets Senators Act on High Gas Prices
  WASHINGTON - June 7 - With prices up at the pump, the Senate will have an opportunity next week to take a concrete step to reduce the impact of high gas prices.

The Senate is expected to vote next week on the Clean Car Resolution, which calls for technology-- not political contributions from car companies-- to drive our nation's fuel economy standards. The resolution seeks to strip an anti-environmental rider which freezes the Department of Transportation's ability to even study ways to improve fuel economy.

A bipartisan coalition at a press conference on Thursday will be discussing how to increase support for this resolution which got 40 votes when it came up last September-- more than enough to sustain a Presidential veto.
