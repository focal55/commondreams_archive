Sierra Club To Announce Position On China Trade Deal
Will join AFL-CIO in Grassroots Campaign
   WASHINGTON - April 7 - Sierra Club Executive Director Carl Pope, joined by AFL-CIO President John Sweeney, will announce the Sierra Club's opposition to permanent normal trade relations (Permanent NTR) with China on MONDAY, APRIL 10. At the press conference, Mr. Pope will describe a plan to mobilize grassroots environmental activists to fight permanent NTR.

Successful collaboration during the Seattle Summit of the World Trade Organization between the Sierra Club and the AFL-CIO has led the two organizations to coordinate more closely in the fight to make trade clean, green, and fair.

What:
Sierra Club Press Conference to describe new position on Permanent Normal Trade Relations with China and grassroots activities.

Who:
Carl Pope, Executive Director, Sierra Club (via Satellite)
John Sweeney, President, AFL-CIO

Where:
Murrow Room, National Press Club, Washington, DC

When:
1:00 pm, MONDAY, APRIL 10

The Sierra Club's Responsible Trade Campaign works to clean up America's polluted trade policy by insuring that environmental concerns and impacts are considered when making trade agreements.
