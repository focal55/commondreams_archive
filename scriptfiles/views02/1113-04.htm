Published on Wednesday, November 13, 2002 by the Madison Capital Times
Elections Confirm Shift Toward Devout Plutocracy
by Ed Garvey
 

Was the election just a week ago? Hard to absorb all the news in a few days, let alone adjust to the realities of the day. What are those realities?

The first and most obvious is that our government has never before been taken over by an extremist group of ideologues. Some remember when Eisenhower was president and both houses were Republican. But they forget we had a Supreme Court that was in no way subservient to Republican doxology, and the Republicans in control were more Jim Jeffords than Trent Lott. In fact, in 1952, many regular readers of this paper were Republicans who followed the La Follettes into the Republican Party.

Despite the occasional Joe McCarthy, the 1950s debates were generally more civil. Differences in party affiliation had as much to do with foreign policy as domestic, and our president was a war hero not a warmonger. In fact, we can now look back with warm feelings for Ike, who warned that big money in the defense industry threatened our democracy and who appointed Earl Warren as chief justice. Can you imagine President Bush appointing Earl Warren? It was unclear whether Ike would run for president as a Democrat or a Republican. Today he would not be allowed to speak at a Republican convention.

During the Reagan-Bush administration, candidates for the judiciary were given a take-home exam before they could be vetted in person for appointment. Two of the questions tell the story: "Do you believe Roe vs. Wade was correctly decided and if not, why not?" And, "Do you believe that Brown vs. Board of Education was correctly decided and if not, why not?" You get the drift.

And Reagan was a moderate compared to this crowd. John Ashcroft, a religious and political fanatic, will now offer up the judicial appointments after consulting with Jerry Falwell and Pat Robertson to make sure that the choices are "good Christian men." We are in for some tough times with the judicial branch of government. Can't sugarcoat that reality. (While Wisconsin's system of electing judges does not guarantee a sound judiciary because of the money boys, it is a hell of a lot better than the federal system, where the nuts can take over and make lifetime appointments that threaten the right of women to exercise control over their own bodies, eviscerate what is left of the Americans With Disabilities Act, and do irreparable harm to our environment.) Scary times.

Jim Hightower called to suggest that you know it is a bad night when the best news for progressives is that "the voters of Oklahoma outlawed cockfighting."

So why did it happen? Was it the popular Bush or was it the constant drumbeat of the talk-show hosts, Fox "news" and the other conservative media? All of the above. Was it the excessive money poured into the Republican campaigns and their effort to hold down turnout with negative commercials that would have made Bob Kasten blush? Yes. Was it Terry McAuliffe? C'mon. No one in America other than insiders has ever heard of this guy. It wasn't McAuliffe, bad as he is. He simply represents the big-money boys and the DLC (Democratic Leadership Council or "Democrats for the Leisure Class," as I refer to them).

The DLC was formed by the white Southerners to join with big money to take the Democratic Party away from dangerous lefties like Gaylord Nelson, Bob Kastenmeier, George McGovern and the reach of Bobby Kennedy loyalists. And they succeeded beyond their wildest dreams. They have blurred the distinction between the two main political parties in order to offer a safe haven for the big contributors. They eliminated any reason to get excited about politics. They are the ones who get nervous when Democrats express real emotion and passion for the downtrodden, those without health insurance, and those who are unemployed. They castigated the speakers and audience at the Wellstone celebration of life. They would have us believe that Walter Mondale lost because of the incredible outpouring of emotion for Paul and Sheila Wellstone. Were the speakers responsible for electing the Republican governor as well? Did they defeat Max Cleland and Rod Moen?

But how about our victories in Wisconsin: Jim Doyle/Barbara Lawton, Peg Lautenschlager, and Sondy Pope-Roberts? Oh yes, the Republicans and Wisconsin Manufacturers & Commerce finished the job of taking over the Legislature and setting the agenda for the next two years if not four, but what the hell. Let's celebrate the end of one-party rule. Some of the damage can be corrected, but before the helium was out of the balloons at the Democratic celebration, the Republicans - who brought us 16 years of fiscal and policy mismanagement under Tommy Thompson, Scott McCallum and Scott Jensen, and a deficit that may be the worst in the nation - demanded "no new taxes." No new taxes? How are we going to deal with the Thompson/McCallum gifts? You know, squandering the tobacco money, overcrowded jails, too many prisons, insufficient funding for special education, a university in crisis. How do we find shelter for the homeless and provide health care for all our citizens without at least "shifting" taxes from the middle class to big business?

One marvels at the chutzpah of the right wing. They take government in Wisconsin and Michigan into huge deficits by giving incredible tax breaks to big business, and then wag fingers at the incoming Democrats while shouting "No new taxes or else!" They might as well say, "Enjoy your four years. We will be back."

In an almost humorous moment, the Wisconsin Education Association Council declared "the war on teachers is over." Really? Where will the end of the qualified economic offer begin? Will it be in the Assembly or the Senate? Could we get serious for a moment?

So, as we prepare for a Wellstone memorial here in Wisconsin, it is time to do some soul-searching. Kevin Phillips and Bill Moyers have it right. We are a plutocracy, not a democracy. Can we just acknowledge that reality and follow the advice of Panglos to hoe our own garden? Only if we don't have children, grandchildren or a conscience.

Ed Garvey is a columnist for The Capital Times.
