Published on Tuesday, April 16, 2002 in the Cape Cod Times
The Pacified Ads
by Sean Gonsalves
 
FOR SALE:

FURNITURE: The United States seat at the International Criminal Court.

The first permanent war crimes tribunal will be officially open for business on July 1 now that the Rome Treaty has been signed and ratified by the 60-plus countries needed to establish the court.

Last week, when the final ratifying votes were cast at a United Nations ceremony, U.N. secretary-general Kofi Annan said: "Those who commit war crimes, genocide or other crimes against humanity will no longer be beyond the reach of justice. Humanity will be able to defend itself - responding to the worst of human nature with one of the greatest human achievements: the rule of law."

Even though many nations and non-government organizations have been working for years to establish such a court to fill a gap in international law made apparent by the Nuremberg and Tokyo trials of German and Japanese war criminals, "the United States has been the only vocal opponent of the permanent court, fearing its citizens would be subject to frivolous or politically-motivated prosecutions," the Associated Press reported.

Three weeks ago, "the Bush administration said it was considering 'unsigning' the treaty (initially signed by former President Clinton) to stress that it won't be bound by its provisions - provisions, by the way, disproportionately influenced by American legal minds.

Price: Credibility of claim to being global leaders when it comes to the rule of law, or best offer.

SPORT UTILITY: 2002 GMT (Gigantic Monster Truck): Gas guzzling, air-polluting, four-door Suburban Sprawler, black, w/tinted widows, 2 small, ratty-looking American flags attached to side rear windows, w/an "America: Love it or leave it" bumper sticker. Take over five-year car payment of $660 a month, which is roughly the per capita GDP of Third World countries like Ethiopia and Bolivia.

HELP WANTED

PSYCHIC OPERATOR: Ever wonder how Miss Cleo, the TV psychic, does it? Want to earn up to $1000/week while sitting on your couch, talking on the phone and flipping the TV channel back and forth between Rikki Lake and Judge Judy ?

Become an oracle in less than three weeks under the tutelage of our licensed Tarot card trainers. They will teach you how to divine the future of mostly poor and lonely callers looking for money and relationship advice. You will be taught the ancient astrological art of asking loaded questions and giving vague, catch-all answers with a fake accent.

Good phone manners not necessary but must demonstrate the ability to keep callers on the $4.99-per-minute-line for a minimum of 15 minutes. Must have your own phone line. No resume or references needed. Call 1-900-FOOLISH.

TALK RADIO HOST: Seeking angry right-wing ideologue who thinks logic is a new liberal, socialist, post-modern, federal housing program. Must see a vast liberal conspiracy behind every disturbing fact or complex social phenomena. Must also demonstrate an abiding, hateful obsession with former President Clinton.

Good starting salary, health benefits, 401k, plus cash bonuses for each time you say on the air, "I know this isn't politically correct but...." Send resumes to WMAD, 101 Conniption Blvd., NY, NY

VENEZUELAN PRESIDENT: Good crisis management skills needed in oil-rich nation. Third biggest supplier of oil to the U.S. and the world's fourth-biggest oil exporter. Many citizens, however, living in squalor.

Hugo Chavez was elected on anti-poverty platform, only to be the victim of a coup last week. Chavez replaced by Pedro Carmona, the pro-business authoritarian. When Carmona took power last week, the oil-friendly Bush administration said "now the situation will be one of tranquillity and democracy." The very next day the people of Venezuela mounted a successful nonviolent insurrection. Carmona resigned as pro-Chavez protesters demanded "Democracy, not dictatorship" in the streets of Caracas.

Applicant must be willing to accept high political risks. Considering the Bush doctrine of either you're with us or you're an evil terrorist, if the United States should accept Carmona the rightful ruler, even though the 19-nation Rio Group of Latin American countries voted not to recognize Carmona's government, Venezuela could shoot up on the United States' Top 10 terror list. Unless, of course, the next Venezuelan president can manage to keep the rabble in line so the oil profits can keep flowing.
