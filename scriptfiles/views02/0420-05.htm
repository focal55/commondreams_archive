Published on Saturday, April 20, 2002 in the Madison Capital Times
U.S. Should Aid Jenin
Editorial
 
The destruction in the Jenin refugee camp on the West Bank, in the words of the United Nations envoy to the Middle East, is "shocking and horrifying."

News reports confirm the observations of Terje Roed-Larsen, the U.N. envoy, who says that a sustained assault by the Israeli army has left the community devastated physically and emotionally.

"It looks as if an earthquake has hit the heart of the refugee camp here," Roed-Larsen told CNN. "I've just been witnessing two brothers digging out of the rubble their father and five other family members. I witnessed a family digging out their 12-year-old son from beneath the rubble. ... The scene is absolutely unbelievable." According to the U.N. envoy, the air is thick with the stench of decaying corpses.

News reports in the European press confirm the view that Jenin is a city of destroyed buildings, dead bodies and the walking wounded. Throughout the city, according to Britain's Guardian newspaper, people are digging with their hands to try and find the bodies of lost loved ones.

The government of Israeli Prime Minister Ariel Sharon, which ordered the destruction of Jenin, has been slow to give full access to the camp to aid agencies distributing food and water to the remaining residents. This is an indefensible decision on the part of the Sharon government, which went ahead with the attack on Jenin even after President Bush demanded that Israeli troops withdraw from Palestinian territories "without delay."

Israeli human rights groups and progressive members of the Israeli Knesset have joined the U.N. call for opening Jenin to aid groups. They are right to do so, and their willingness to publicly oppose the reprehensible actions of Sharon should serve as a reminder that a great many Israeli Jews have, even in these difficult times, refused to support their prime minister in his unreasonable excesses.

The United States should side with the Israelis, the Palestinians and the United Nations observers who recognize that the people of Jenin must be helped if further tragedy is to be prevented.

The Bush administration should make an emergency authorization of financial assistance to groups that are prepared immediately to help the people of Jenin, such as the Palestine Red Crescent and the U.N. Relief Agency for Palestine Refugees in the Near East. And the president should then demand that Sharon allow the aid the United States has provided to get to the people of Jenin.
