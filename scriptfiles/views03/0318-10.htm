Published on Tuesday, March 18, 2003 by CommonDreams.org
Kennedy's Torch in Bush's Hand
by Bill C. Davis
 

The Christian unifier has put a cleaver down the middle of the world as he asks for a moment of truth. Ironic that a man who was elevated to the most powerful position in the world through deception would ask for such a moment. The path to this moment of truth has been paved with fabrications, distortions, arrogance and lies. Ironic too that as Cheney and Bush exploit 9/11 in the service of this action no one asks how the investigation into 9/11 is going. If it remains a vaporous reality it can be used any way that is useful. But like the energy policy, 9/11 will not benefit from an investigation that might reveal more than a moment of truth. There were no weapons of mass destruction or Iraqis on that day - there was an emotion - the same emotion that is being enflamed every day Bush is in power.

Bush comes from a tradition where it is not only fine to say anything to achieve your end but also recommended. Willy Horton in 1988, McCain's wife's breast cancer in the South Carolina primary, Noriega, drugs and Panama, Clinton protesting the Vietnam war in Moscow, Gore claims he invented the internet, my brother said I won Florida - say anything, hope it sticks and take whatever benefit you can from the believed lie.

Bush makes sweeping statements about the inherent and irredeemable evil of Saddam Hussein, who ultimately is a symptom and a harvest of the kind of policies that Bush is now implementing.

On Sunday morning Cheney made it clear that Iraq's oil will belong to the Iraqi people - the money their oil generates will be used to rebuild Iraq. Again, no one asked Mr. Halliburton who will be paid that Iraqi money to rebuild what the American taxpayers paid to destroy. No one asked who will make the decisions as to who gets what contract in the noble rebuilding process. Will the Iraqi people make that decision? Will

France or Germany get a contract? The Iraqi oil will produce the money that this government will use to pay the people this government chooses to hire.

The stand up routine in the Azores proved that Bush is not afraid to speak Texan in the middle of the Atlantic. It proved again that Bush is neither passionate nor committed - he's not even driven. He is petulant.

Petulance grows out of the inability to reconcile contradictory realities. Most of the world is naturally riddled with contradictions. Statesmen and leaders have an intuition as to how to navigate those crashing waves. A petulant, impatient leader snaps and belittles - he refuses to talk - he blames everything and everyone but himself for the mess he feels only he can resolve. If only France would cooperate - if only Clinton had taken care of this - if only Saddam would leave the country - if only congress would do what I tell it to do. There have been crises and conditions which have resolved through peaceful means. It has and does happen if visionary men and women lead the way.

We wait now to hear what he has to say - we waited to hear what he had to say in the Azores - we'll wait to see what he'll do and what orders he'll give. It's humiliating for the country to rest its fortunes and conscience on this particularly disagreeable and unevolved human being. He represents many things about this country none of which we should invest in or try to import. And yet he is now America to the world and we citizens are prisoners of that perception.

In his speech, which he has just delivered to the world with the look of pinched consternation, he spoke directly to the Iraqi people. With 250,000 troops poised and 3.000 "precision guided" missiles aimed at Baghdad Bush told the Iraqi people that he has no desire to harm them. I wonder if that sounded like a moment of truth to them.

Pundits say that Bush will be coronated "If the war goes well..." How does a war go well? People die quickly and survivors in terror surrender? Not as many people as expected are killed? Bush has made the world impossible for moderates. Peacemakers are wimps and appeasers; inspectors are enablers; hard hitting journalists are terrorists; protesters don't support the troops; Fox is fair and balanced and artists should keep their mouths shut. And the extremism that hit us on September 11th is being fueled the way the desert sand in southern Iraq is being soaked with oil. Bush is the generation that Kennedy said a torch had been passed to. But a torch meant to light the way is being used to light a fuse.

Bill C. Davis is a playwright. www.billcdavis.com
