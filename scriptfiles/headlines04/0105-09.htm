Published on Monday, January 5, 2004 by Agence France Presse
Majority of Japanese Oppose Bush: Poll
 

TOKYO - Most Japanese people oppose the policies of US President George W. Bush, but pro-US sentiment in the country remains high, according to an opinion poll.

Some 57 percent of respondents said they don't back Bush while 13 percent voiced support for him, the Mainichi Shimbun said in its poll.

The newspaper carried out the survey in mid-December, questioning 2,500 eligible voters across the nation of whom 1,521 responded validly.

The survey showed some 73 percent have a favorable view of the United States, compared with 72 percent in a similar survey carried out by the daily in December 2002.

Some 22 percent said they dislike the United States, up from 20 percent a year earlier.

Seventy-six percent wanted Japan to engage with the international community on diplomacy and security matters, while 13 percent wanted Tokyo to focus on its bilateral alliance with Washington.
