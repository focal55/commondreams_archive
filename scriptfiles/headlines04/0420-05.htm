Published on Tuesday, April 20, 2004 by Agence France Presse
Support For Iraq War Falling
 
LONDON (AFP) - Opposition to Prime Minister Tony Blair's Iraq policy has risen among voters, with almost half -- 48 percent -- now believing that the war was unjustified, according to a poll.

Support for the conflict among Britons fell from 53 percent in January to 41 percent, said the ICM survey for the Guardian newspaper.

After the bloodiest month of fighting in Iraq since last year's invasion by the US-led coalition, more than two-thirds of Britons had little or no confidence in the United States' handling of the deteriorating security situation.

However there was some good news for Blair. The poll, which quizzed 1,002 people April 16-18, put support for his Labour party at 38 percent, five percentage points ahead of the Conservatives.
