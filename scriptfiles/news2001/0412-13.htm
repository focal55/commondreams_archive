Privacy Regulation Implemented But Administration Targets Minors' Privacy Rights
   WASHINGTON - April 12 - While the implementation of patient privacy rules appears to be a victory for women and families, it is deeply disturbing that the Bush administration plans to deny some key privacy protections to young people. We will be watching carefully as the administration proceeds.

Privacy of medical information is an essential component of high quality care. It is especially important to women because they are the largest consumers of health care services and because of their higher use of sensitive services like reproductive health and mental health.

The existing regulation preserves a young person's ability to obtain healthcare confidentially. We are concerned that any change to this carefully crafted approach would undermine existing state and federal laws. Such changes would unnecessarily endanger the lives and health of countless young people who will forego critical medical services if their information is not kept confidential.

We commend the administration for moving forward with this privacy rule, but we will be watching carefully to ensure that patient protections, including protections for young people, are not undermined as the rule is modified.
