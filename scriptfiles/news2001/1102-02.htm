Conflict Diamonds/Terror: Amnesty International Expert Available for Interview
  WASHINGTON - November 2 - News Advisory:

Adotei Akwei, Amnesty International USA's Advocacy Director for Africa, is available to provide expert analysis of the role of conflict diamonds in funding terror, and on the urgent need for the U.S. House of Representatives to pass the Clean Diamond Trade Act (HR 2722).

The U.S. market accounts for over 50 percent of global diamond sales. The legislation, if passed, would require the Customs Service to permit diamond imports only from those countries that have adopted strong and effective controls on the exportation and importation of rough diamonds to ensure that conflict diamonds do not enter the international stream of commerce.

The Senate has already passed the Clean Diamonds Act (S. 1084).
