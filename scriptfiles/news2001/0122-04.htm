New Administration Will Set Anti-Choice Tone By Reinstating Int'l Gag Rule
   WASHINGTON - January 22 - In response to news reports that President George W. Bush will reinstate the international gag rule by executive order today, the National Women's Law Center decried the action and called it a move that will endanger the health of women in developing counties.

"If on his first day in office, President Bush chooses to reinstate the gag rule, through executive order, he would be adopting a harmful policy that will seriously affect women's health. Reinstating the gag rule, along with the nomination of Senator Ashcroft for Attorney General, would set an ominous tone for women in this country and the security of their right to safe and legal abortion and family planning more broadly," said Marcia D. Greenberger, Co-President of NWLC. "The gag rule endangers the health of women in developing counties resulting in more unwanted pregnancies, more maternal deaths and more abortions. It is a dangerous restriction for women worldwide."

As part of its FY 2000 funding for international family planning programs, Congress adopted the international gag rule preventing recipients from using their own funds to provide abortions or advocate for abortion rights before their own governments. However, for FY 2001, former President Clinton negotiated an agreement where Congress lifted the gag rule, but funding to family planning organizations was suspended until Feb. 15, giving the new president the opportunity to reinstate the restriction.

The National Women's Law Center is a non-profit organization that has been working since 1972 to advance and protect women's legal rights. The Center focuses on major policy areas of importance to women and their families including economic security, education, employment and health, with special attention given to the concerns of low-income women.
