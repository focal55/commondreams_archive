Thousands of Students Gathering for a Peaceful, More Sustainable Future
  

WASHINGTON - October 1 -

What: ECOnference2001

Where: Washington, DC

When: October 19-21, 2001

ECOnference2001 will be the largest gathering of environmental student activists in over 10 years! Over two thousand students from Freeport to Seattle, Los Angeles to Dublin will be descending on the nation's capitol for a full weekend of expert panel discussions, speakers (such as Lois Gibbs (toxics activist), Jim Hightower (populist author/radio talk show host), Carl Pope (Exec.Dir. Sierra Club), John Passcantando (Exec.Dir. Greenpeace), and more!), workshops briefing students on environmental issue developments and how to run environmental campaigns, and opportunities to meet with leaders of the major environmental activist organizations from across the country!

It will be an amazing weekend; an opportunity to unify universities and colleges throughout the country in the name of a more sustainable and peaceful future in which we shape the environmental policy of our nation and protect the biosphere against the onslaught of corporate pollution and consumerism!

Check out the web site, www.ECOnference2001.org OR www.ecopledge.com to learn more about the weekend.

Feel free to also give us a call at ECOnference headquarters, where our toll free phone# is 1-866-ECO-7533.

ECOnference is sponsored by over 50 national environmental organizations including Sierra Student Coalition, Greenpeace, the PIRGs, Free the Planet!, ecopledge.com, ZeroPopulationGrowth, Rainforest Action Network, NRDC, The Wilderness Society, Kyoto Now!, SEAC, Campus Greens, and more!!
