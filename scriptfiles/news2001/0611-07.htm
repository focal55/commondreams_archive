Friends of the Earth Comment: Bush Climate Change Speech
  WASHINGTON - June 11 - The following statement was released today by Friends of the Earth:

"The President can't have his new energy plan AND seriously address global climate change. His energy plan is a fossil fuels extravaganza that reads like a veritable recipe for heating up the planet. Today's speech was all talk and no meaningful action. It was a carefully orchestrated smoke-screen of empty rhetoric from which only one thing can be gleaned: Bush knows he has a public relations problem when it comes to his handling of important environmental issues like climate change. Global climate change is real, U.S. pollution is greatly contributing to it, and the public -- both here and abroad -- demand something serious be done to address it. And so the President talks. But where is the substance of his plan?

By failing to take real actions -- by failing to advocate mandatory controls of greenhouse gasses, President Bush sends a clear signal: the polluting industries that benefit most from the Administration's energy plan are still running the show. Going to Europe with this message can only heighten Bush's image there as an arrogant leader who puts the interests of his wealthy, polluting friends above those of the health and safety of the entire planet.

Dr. Brent Blackwelder
President, Friends of the Earth
