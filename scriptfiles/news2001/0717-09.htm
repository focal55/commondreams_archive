Mexican Prisoner's Wife, Groups ask,"Is Mexico Safe for Environmentalists?"
   LOS ANGELES - July 17 - Ubalda Cortés, wife of jailed Mexican environmentalist Rodolfo Montiel will be joined by the environmentalist's lawyer, Ana Hernadez and representatives of the Sierra Club and Amnesty International USA to urge Mexico's President Fox to immediately and unconditionally release jailed environmentalists Rodolfo Montiel and Teodoro Cabrera and to issue a public statement on their behalf. These human rights and environmental advocates will participate in a press conference on the morning of Friday, July 20 in front of the Mexican Consulate in Los Angeles.

As Mexico's court system considers the latest appeal on the case of the two wrongly imprisoned environmental defenders, Montiel and Cabrera have now spent nearly 27 months of their lives in prison. The decision in their case will have profound impacts on the future of human rights and the environment on both sides of the U.S./Mexico border.

The Sierra Club and Amnesty International believe Montiel and Cabrera have been imprisoned for their efforts to protect the forests of Guerrero from destructive logging. A decision on their latest appeal--based on civil rights concerns--is expected on or around July 20.
