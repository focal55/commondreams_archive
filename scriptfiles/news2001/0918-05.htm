NAACP's Mfume Calls Again For American Solidarity
  BALTIMORE - September 18 - Kweisi Mfume, President & CEO, the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP), said today that in the face of attacks from terrorists such as those who attacked the World Trade Center and the Pentagon, all Americans should stand together against terrorism.

Mfume said: "The hijacking of American jetliners culminating in the attacks on the World Trade Center, the Pentagon and the Pennsylvania crash are tragedies of untold proportions and attacks against our way of life without precedent. This massive loss to the family of humankind moves all people of conscience to mourn and pray for the dead and all affected by this tragedy. The hour is upon us to put aside differences and dissent. This tragedy and these acts of evil that occurred on September 11 must not go unpunished. This is a time for all Americans to stand united and defend the ideals of a free and open society where terrorism has no place."

Founded in 1909, the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) is the nation's oldest and largest civil rights organization. Its half-million adult and youth members throughout the United States and the world are the premier advocates for civil rights in their communities, conducting voter mobilization and monitoring equal opportunity in the public and private sectors.
