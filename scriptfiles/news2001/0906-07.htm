H.R. 476 Punishes Teens Seeking Abortion, Says Planned Parenthood
   WASHINGTON - September 6 - The following is a statement by Gloria Feldt, president, Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

Once again, legislators are engaged in a cynical exercise that will hurt the very people they claim to want to protect.

The sponsors of H.R. 476, the so-called "Child Custody Protection Act," pretend that their object is to protect young women. The actual effect of the bill would be to curtail women's access to legal abortion and to legislate how families communicate.

Planned Parenthood encourages young women to involve their parents when they face an unintended pregnancy and are considering an abortion. Most do so, without any law telling them they have to. Yet, not every family is a model family. Minors who are victims of abuse or incest will often choose not to discuss this decision with their parents, regardless of what Congress says.

If this bill were passed, it would obstruct the ability of a young woman to obtain a safe and legal abortion. It would also deter responsible, caring adults from helping a minor in a difficult situation.

This is a cruel attempt by legislators to ignore the reproductive rights of young women. If Congress really wants to reduce the number of unintended pregnancies, they would support comprehensive sex education and access to contraception and family planning for all. They would not impose dangerous barriers to safe abortion.
