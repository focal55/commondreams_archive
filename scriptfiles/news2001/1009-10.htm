Wellstone to Hold Wednesday Hearing on Afghanistan Humanitarian Crisis
  WASHINGTON - October 9 - Senator Paul Wellstone (D-MN) and Senator Barbra Boxer (D-CA) will hold a hearing on Wednesday, October 10 at 2:30 pm to examine the humanitarian crisis facing Afghanistan, and the U.S. and international community’s response to it. Senator Wellstone and the Subcommittee on Near Eastern and South Asian Affairs will hear testimony from administrators of U.S. aid programs in the region as well as representatives of non-profit organizations working in Afghanistan.

Even before the world focused on it as a sanctuary for Osama bin Laden and other terrorists, Afghanistan was on the brink of a humanitarian catastrophe, the site of the greatest crisis in hunger and refugee displacement in the world. After four years of relentless drought, the worst in three decades, and the total failure of the Taliban government in administering the country, four million people have abandoned their homes in search of food in Pakistan, Iran, Tajikistan and elsewhere, while those left behind eat meals of locusts and animal fodder. Five million people inside the country are threatened by famine, according to the United Nations. U.S. airstrikes now pounding terrorist sites in Afganistan may dramatically worsen this situation on the ground.
