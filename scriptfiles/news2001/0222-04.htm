Trust Names Refinery Lobby the
Clean Air 'Villain of the Month'
  WASHINGTON - February 22 - The non-profit Clean Air Trust today awarded its clean air "Villain of the Month" award for February to the National Petrochemical and Refiners Association (NPRA).

The refinery lobby earned this dubious distinction by filing a suit against the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency's landmark plan to clean up dirty diesel trucks and buses and diesel fuel.

The diesel cleanup plan is one of the most important steps to protect public health in EPA's history: it would prevent more than 8,000 premature deaths and more than 360,000 asthma attacks a year, according to EPA's own estimates. The agency calculated the public health benefits of the cleanup would outweigh the costs by a staggering factor of 16 to one.

But rather than agree to modernize its refineries, the NPRA has chosen to spend its money on lawyers. And the refinery lobby has made the outrageous assertion that California's electricity problem is somehow related to the clean diesel plan.

Too expensive to clean up? The refiners picked a bad time to make that argument, given that one of its prominent members, ExxonMobil, made more than $5 billion in profits in the last three months of 2000 alone. That's far more than the diesel cleanup would cost for the entire industry over the next five and a half years.

The cleanup is supported by an unprecedented coalition, including domestic and foreign auto companies, health and environmental groups, state and local clean-air regulators, pollution control equipment makers, the nation's biggest diesel engine maker, the California Trucking Association, and even some oil companies.

Breathers can only hope that the Bush administration vigorously defends the cleanup plan in court and heeds the admonition of a dissident NPRA member, BP Amoco. Just this week, BP reiterated its support for the diesel cleanup. The cleanup technology is available, BP noted, adding that adequate supplies of clean diesel fuel can be made by the mid-2006 deadline.
