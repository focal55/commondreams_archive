Senate Vote on Ashcroft to be Litmus Test on Civil Rights
  WASHINGTON - January 16 - Kweisi Mfume, president & CEO, the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP), said today that information campaigns will be conducted in states of U.S. Senators who vote to confirm the nomination of John Ashcroft for U.S. Attorney General.

"Senators who vote for Ashcroft will not be able to run away from this and assume people will forget," said Mfume. "We are going to fund major information campaigns for the next four years in states where senators voted to confirm. For democratic senators, in particular, this vote comes as close to a litmus test as one can get on the issue of civil rights and equal justice under law from the party's most loyal constituency."

Mfume said the U.S. Senate Judiciary Committee should reject the Ashcroft nomination because he has not proven he will enforce laws with which he disagrees and because of his extremist ideology. "We challenge the U.S. Senate to ask Ashcroft the hard questions that speak to his history of bending the law to conform to his particular ideology," said Mfume. "This is a time for conscious, not congeniality among senators," he said. "The Senate should also press Ashcroft to explain why he opposed efforts to increase voter registration in St. Louis and why he opposed voluntary school desegregation in St. Louis."

Mfume said the Senate would have no choice but to reject Ashcroft after a careful review of his record. Ashcroft received a grade of "F" on each of the last three legislative NAACP report cards having voted to approve only three of 15 legislative issues supported by the NAACP and other civil rights groups.

"The Attorney General of the United States is responsible for enforcing our nation's hate crimes law, but Ashcroft voted in the senate to oppose the Hate Crimes Prevention Act," said Mfume. "Clearly, Ashcroft is a man who will not hesitate to bend the rules to conform to his ideology."

The Ashcroft nomination represents a major test for U.S. Senators who believe in the Constitution, equal opportunity laws, human and voting rights for all citizens, said Mfume. "The policies that Ashcroft has committed his national career towards implementing are so divisive they are not only antithetical to the necessary virtues of an effective U.S. Attorney General, but they are also antithetical to the stated philosophy of President-elect Bush, who said he wants to be a uniter, not a divider."

Ashcroft demonstrated contempt for the history and conditions of African Americans and other Americans of color by making public comments issued in 1998 in Southern Partisan Magazine praising Confederate leaders Robert E. Lee, Stonewall Jackson and Jefferson Davis who fought to preserve the cruel and inhumane institution of slavery.

Ashcroft also opposed the nomination of Bill Lann Lee as Assistant U.S. Attorney General in charge of the Civil Rights Division. He also opposes background checks for gun purchases and supports the death penalty.

Founded in 1909, the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) is the nation's oldest and largest civil rights organization. Its half-million adult and youth members throughout the United States and the world are the premier advocates for civil rights in their communities, conducting voter mobilization and monitoring equal opportunity in the public and private sectors.
