House Affirms Global Gag Rule
Planned Parenthood President Calls On President Bush to Stop Campaign on Women's Lives
   WASHINGTON - May 16 - The following is a statement by Gloria Feldt, President of Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

The Administration and sponsors of the Hyde-Smith amendment used a calculated campaign of misinformation to fool the American public. With this slim victory, they have shown that they would rather turn their back on the women of the world, on human rights and the principles of democracy, to impose the narrow beliefs of a few on the rest of the world.

Supporters of the Global Gag Rule say they support family planning. They don't. Family planning providers around the world need to be able to talk freely with their clients and with their government about important public health issues. The Gag Rule forces providers to withhold information from their clients -- an intrusion that would be considered intolerable in the United States.

Supporters of the gag rule say their policy prevents abortion. It doesn't. Twelve years of this policy under the Reagan and Bush administrations showed no evidence of reducing abortion. Only if people have more access to family planning will there be fewer abortions.

Finally, supporters of the gag rule say that Americans don't want U.S. taxpayer funds to support abortion in other countries. They don't. It has been illegal to use any U.S. funds to provide abortion services, counseling or referrals, or to lobby to change abortion laws, for nearly 30 years.

In fact, the president and the sponsors of this amendment are woefully out of step with Americans, and this discord is a threat to the health of women and our position as a global leader. Americans know that family planning programs prevent abortions, prevent the spread of AIDS, and are vital to women's health and the health of their families. An overwhelming 80 percent of us have said we support international family planning programs.

It is time to stop playing politics with women's lives. The slim margin of victory today proves that many members can see through this cynical maneuver. President George W. Bush still has an opportunity to listen to the majority of Americans who believe that everyone deserves access to family planning. The world is watching.
