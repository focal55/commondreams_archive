President's Global Warming Plans Likely to be Received Coldly by Allies
  WASHINGTON - June 11 - President Bush's statement on global warming isn't likely to make his meetings with European leaders any easier. Coming on the heels of the National Academy of Sciences report, the President's continued harping on scientific uncertainty undercuts his credibility, both at home and abroad.

Research just won't sell any more as a serious response to global warming. It is seen as just another way for the United States to delay real action to cut pollution. A decade later, the President can't expect the Europeans to buy the same vague promises on global warming his father made - promises that produced years of increased pollution.

The President sent the Europeans a clear message, once again, that America is not yet ready to take the lead in fighting global warming. Until he changes that message, he's likely to get a very cold reception from our allies.
