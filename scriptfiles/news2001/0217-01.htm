Wellstone Blasts Bush Assault on Organized Labor
   WASHINGTON - February 17 - Responding to President Bush’s issuance today of fourexecutive orders changing federal labor policy, Wellstone strongly criticized the decision for the harsh impact it will have on American workers.

“President Bush’s executive action today is a direct assault on American workers and their families. It is meant to have a chilling effect on the ability of working men and women to organize and bargain collectively for decent wages and benefits, and basic job security – all central to the well-being of working families,” Wellstone said.

“If this is the opening shot in a long-haul effort to roll back workers rights and protections, President Bush will find stiff resistance in Congress and among working Americans across our country. Following on the heels of his proposal to give big tax breaks to the wealthiest Americans, but not to working families, today’s executive orders reversing good protections of American workers sends a terrible message. This is no way to set the tone for bipartisanship,” Wellstone added.

The Bush executive orders today will:

Require federal contractors to post a notice telling workers they have a right under a 1988 Supreme Court ruling not to pay portions of their dues that sponsor political activities. Unions are a prime source of money and grass-roots organizing for Democrats.

Revoke the ''project labor agreement,'' which requires contractors in many federally financed projects to be unionized. The provision, employed at the discretion of states, is an issue in an expensive proposal to replace the Woodrow Wilson Bridge on heavily traveled Interstate 95 between Virginia and Maryland.

Dissolve the National Partnership Council, a Clinton organization that sought to get government agencies and unions to resolve their differences.

Revoke a Clinton policy of job protection for employees of contractors at federal buildings when the project is awarded to another contractor.
