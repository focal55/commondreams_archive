NOW Calls on Senate to Reject Ashcroft for Attorney General
   WASHINGTON - January 31 - Statement of NOW President Patricia Ireland Why are we making this fight to stop confirmation of John Ashcroft as attorney general of the United States? This fight is about stopping the savaging of everything we've worked for; this is about saving our rights and constitutional protections.

The list of problems with this nomination is long and no secret to anyone. Despite reports to the contrary, Ashcroft did not pledge not to try to reverse Roe v. Wade; but reproductive rights are not our only concern.

Ashcroft is soft on equal rights, civil rights, voting rights, separation of church and state, the rights of free speech and to petition the government. He has abused his power and refused to answer charges of misusing public resources for private political purposes. He has demeaned and slandered others to advance his career.

Some commentators have confidently deemed our campaign a "shot across the bow," or a "you-ain't-seen-nothing-yet" warning of the real fight we will wage over ultra-conservative Supreme Court nominees. Others see this as a continuation of the presidential election, "payback" for Florida. Partisans have even cynically projected that our efforts are nothing more than a money-making machine.

Certainly we want the Bush administration and the senators to understand how determined we are, and how much support we can mobilize, to save the court from nominees who would roll back constitutional protection of our rights.

Yes, the election and the voting-rights fight give us a ready-made network of angry, experienced and organized activists who are committed to stopping Ashcroft. NOW's resources are going to tap into this network to generate constituent contacts with senators, not into dialing-for-dollars to pay for the Stop Ashcroft campaign. But those effects are secondary to our real purpose and are thrown up as a smoke-screen by Ashcroft's supporters.

Ashcroft's record is well-established and clear: his priorities lie with his own political and personal agenda, not with equal justice for all.

Every senator, Republican as well as Democrat, must consider this record and decide whether Ashcroft can be trusted to hold the scales of justice in his hand.
