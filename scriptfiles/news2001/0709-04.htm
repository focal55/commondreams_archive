National Day of Action Against NAHB's Ken Ford
Pacifica Activists in Over 12 Cities Plan July 10 Protest
  

NEW YORK - July 9 -Come July 10, thousands of free-speech activists across the country will picket, phone, fax and e-mail Pacifica Board Chair Ken Ford and his employer the National Association of Home Builders (NAHB) in an effort to pressure his resignation from Pacifica's Board.

Critics say Ford who is a manager at NAHB, supported the 1999 lockout at Pacifica Berkeley station KPFA, the "Christmas Coup" firings and takeover at Pacifica's New York station WBAI last December, and the continuing assaults on the network's flagship, daily news program, Democracy Now!

Ford has recently come under fire by threatening to send listener letters to the F.B.I. A recent e-mail campaign also involved a progressive web site, which allowed listeners across the country to send letters to the NAHB demanding Ford's resignation. The NAHB worked through the web site's internet service provider to have the letter visually taken off the site. Critics say these are flagrant first-amendment violations.

"The threat to the Pacifica Radio Network will be lifted only when the current Board of Directors is reconstituted by removing board members like Ford," said Pacifica Campaign organizer Ursula Ruedenberg.

Edward Herman, Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania recently wrote in a letter to Ford "When presented with a petition signed by hundreds of KPFA (Pacifica Radio) listeners (Ford) tore it up and threw it on the floor without reading it..."

Earlier this year Time magazine reported, "Vice chairman Ken Ford, an engineer with the conservative Housing Trade Association in Washington, says the challenge is to honor the social-justice mission while Pacifica grows. "But we are a corporation. It’s nonprofit, but we have to operate as a business."

The exploding national movement to protect the country's oldest listener-sponsored, non-corporate radio network has, in recent months, launched several successful, nonviolent direct-action campaigns against many in the Pacifica Foundation's leadership.

Critics say a corporate board clique is trying to eviscerate the network's radical heritage. These campaigns have forced the resignations of board president David Acosta, Treasurer Michael Palmer, and board member Karolyn Van Putten. Two of these board members were on the record supporting the sale of one of the Pacifica stations.

Pacifica listeners say Ford lacks progressive credentials. They say the NAHB spends millions on campaign contributions and lobbying to influence public policy for the benefit of homebuilders, and lobbies against any regulation that would protect the environment or the interests of the public if that regulation conflicts with the profits of real estate developers.

The Pacifica Campaign is a grass-roots organization representing listeners and staff alike, fighting to preserve Pacifica's 50-year tradition of progressive, community-based radio.
