Citizens To Clean Up GE Respond To Company Claims
  WASHINGTON - June 13 - We welcome the opportunity to bring the facts on GE's environmental record to light to communities nationwide. GE has stalled long enough. It is time for GE to do the right thing and clean up its PCB mess.

The issues are clear:

-- GE dumped 1.3 million pounds of PCB's in the river and under law they are responsible for cleaning up the mess.

-- The river is not cleaning itself, no matter what GE says, and leaving PCB's in the river has unacceptable health and environmental consequences.

-- The leading governmental agencies that regulate toxic chemicals (EPA, Agency for Toxic Substances and Disease Registry (ADTSDR) of the CDC, and the International Agency for the Research of Cancer) agree that PCB's are a proven animal carcinogen and possible human carcinogen.

-- The vast majority of people in New York (84 percent) want the river cleaned up.

-- Proven technology exists to safely clean the river.

-- GE has already agreed to pay for the clean up of similar problems around the country. It should do the same in New York State.

It is time for President Bush and the EPA to tell GE to stop the foot dragging and start cleaning up the Hudson River. Thirty years of lead time should be enough for Jack Welch and GE.

For more information on the health impacts of PCB's, see http://www.cleanupge.org. Citizens to Cleanup GE is a project of Essential Information.
