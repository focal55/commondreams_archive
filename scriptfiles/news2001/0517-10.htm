Bush Energy Plan is Dirty, Dangerous and Doesn't Deliver for Consumers
Statement of Gene Karpinski, Executive Director
   WASHINGTON - May 17 - President Bush’s energy plan is dirty, dangerous and doesn’t deliver for consumers. It’s a recipe for more drilling, more spilling, more asthma attacks, more nuclear waste, and more global warming. Two things are clear about the plan: it will increase pollution; it won’t be a solution.

The President’s plan offers no relief to consumers who are living with rising prices and rolling blackouts right now. Instead, it rewards the polluters who have created the problems, who are reaping record-setting profits, and who contributed record amounts of campaign money to buy influence and get special favors.

The President’s plan does nothing to ensure energy efficiency -- the cleanest, cheapest, quickest and safest way to save consumers money and reduce pollution. His plan will not result in any significant shift to clean, renewable energy production which could make us less reliant on fluctuating fossil energy markets.

That’s why we are urging the American public to send President Bush a loud and clear message: his energy plan is fundamentally flawed it’s a plan we just can’t afford.

The public education campaign we are launching today is one of the most far-reaching, most intensive and most important campaigns this community has ever been involved in.

Key initial elements of this campaign include:
--A series of television, radio and print ads in two dozen key media markets across the country, including an initial 12 markets in which we will air the TV ad (Denver, CO, Washington, DC, Tallahassee, FL, Tampa, FL, Chicago, IL, Springfield, IL, Helena, MT, Bismarck, ND, Manchester, NH, Albuquerque, NM, Portland, OR, Philadelphia, PA);
--News events like these in more than 30 locations across the country;
--An innovative Internet action and ad campaign;
--Door-to-door public education efforts in at least 35 cities;
--A growing number of environmental, consumer, religious, public health, business, labor and community leaders across the country speaking out against the plan and for a smarter, cleaner energy future.

To learn more about the campaign, go to the website: www.saveourenvironment.org.

We urge members of Congress to reject the Bush plan, and support a smarter, cleaner energy future.

U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for the State Public Interest Research Groups. State PIRGs are non-profit, non-partisan public interest advocacy groups.

The State PIRGs’ policy paper, A New Energy Future, can be found on-line at www.newenergyfuture.com.
