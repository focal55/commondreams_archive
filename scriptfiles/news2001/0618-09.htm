Norton Hires Oil Lobbyist as Arctic Advisor
  WASHINGTON - June 18 - Interior Secretary Gale Norton's appointment of an Alaska oil lobbyist as her special assistant demonstrates again that her main priority is to promote Big Oil's agenda, Defenders of Wildlife said.

"As she visits Alaska this week, Secretary Norton pretends to be objectively examining whether oil development would harm the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge and the wildlife that depends on that magnificent wilderness for survival. But with this appointment, the charade is over. She's willing to surrender America's greatest wildlife sanctuary to the special interests at any cost to the environment," Defenders of Wildlife Vice President Robert Dewey said.

Norton named Camden Toohey, the director of the pro-oil development group Arctic Power, as her special adviser on oil drilling in the Arctic refuge and other Alaska-related issues. Arctic Power, the main voice for oil drilling in the Arctic refuge, receives its money from state tax dollars appropriated by the Alaska Legislature and from the British oil giant BP, among other funders.

Even as Norton was preparing to announce his appointment, Toohey told the Anchorage Daily News that his group is still lobbying hard for drilling in the refuge despite opposition by the Senate's new Democratic majority.

"I feel good about our chances," Toohey said.

Norton named another Big Oil advocate -- Alaska state Sen. Drue Pearce -- as another special assistant on Alaska issues. Pearce is also a staunch supporter of drilling in the refuge.

Dewey said: "If there was any doubt about the Bush administration's direct ties to the oil industry, this appointment ends it. It's sad enough that the state of Alaska is using tax money to fund lobbying for oil rigs in the Arctic refuge. Now, Secretary Norton has put the oil lobbyists on the public payroll, and all Americans will pay their salaries."

Defenders of Wildlife is a leading nonprofit conservation organization recognized as one of the nation's most progressive advocates for wildlife and its habitat. With more than 430,000 members and supporters, Defenders of Wildlife is an effective leader on environmental issues.
