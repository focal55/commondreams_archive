Coalition to Stop Gun Violence to Hold Kennedy-King Awards Dinner
CSGV to Honor Rep. Lowey, Robert Chase of NEA, Sandra Feldman of AFT
  WASHINGTON - October 16 - News Advisory:

The Coalition to Stop Gun Violence will hold its annual Kennedy -- King Awards Dinner, Wednesday, Oct. 17, to honor Rep. Nita Lowey (D-N.Y.), Robert Chase, president of the National Education Association, and Sandra Feldman, president of the American Federation of Teachers. NBC 4 Television News Anchor Pat Lawson Muse will serve as the evening's Master of Ceremonies.

Rep. Nita Lowey has been an outspoken advocate for tough gun restrictions, including closing the gun show loophole, the Brady Law, and the Assault Weapons Ban. Sen. Hillary Clinton (D-N.Y.) will present the Congressional Leadership Award to Rep. Lowey.

Robert Chase and Sandra Feldman are dedicated to educating young people and to ensuring their safety. The NEA and AFT have implemented programs to reduce youth violence in schools and both organizations support tough gun laws to keep young people from getting guns. Former Secretary of Education Richard Riley will present the Robert F. Kennedy -- Martin Luther King award to Robert Chase and Sandra Feldman.

Kennedy-King Awards Dinner

Honorees:
-- Rep. Nita Lowey (D-N.Y.), recipient of the Congressional Leadership Award
-- Bob Chase, president of the National Education Association
-- Sandra Feldman, president of the American Federation of Teachers
-- co-recipients of the Robert F. Kennedy -- Martin Luther King, Jr. Award

Past Recipients of the Congressional Leadership Award include Sen. Charles Schumer (D-N.Y.), Sen. Dianne Feinstein (D-Calif.), Chicago Mayor Richard Daley, and Abner Mikva.

Past Recipients of the Robert F. Kennedy -- Martin Luther King, Jr. Award include the founder of the Million Mom March Donna Dees-Thomases, Actor Gregory Peck, and Abe Pollin, owner of the Washington Wizards.

The Coalition to Stop Gun Violence's mission is to stop gun violence by fostering effective community and national legislative action. Web site: www.csgv.org
