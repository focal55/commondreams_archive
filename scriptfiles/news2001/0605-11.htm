Chicago Symposium to Provide Debate on Food Irradiation
   WASHINGTON - June 5 - MEDIA ADVISORY

WHAT:
Symposium to debate the controversial issues surrounding the use of irradiation to kill bacteria in food and increase shelf life. Foods now approved for irradiation include pork, beef, poultry, fruits, vegetables, eggs and spices. The symposium is one of the first to bring together independent scientists, irradiation companies, regulatory agencies and consumer advocates to discuss this critical public health issue. Participants will talk about irradiation technologies; the regulatory history of irradiation; public policy issues, such as the need for labeling of irradiated food; and whether irradiation can increase the risk for cancer, cause genetic damage, deplete nutrients in food or create environmental hazards.
