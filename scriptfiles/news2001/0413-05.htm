Statement On Bush Administration Rollback Of Air Conditioning Standards
  WASHINGTON - April 13 - The following is a statement by Philip Clapp, president of the National Environmental Trust, on Bush Administration rollback of air conditioning standards:

President Bush has just made California's long-term electricity outlook worse. California will have to build an additional two power plants to make up for what the Administration did today.

The annual energy saved by the air conditioning standard in the long run would have been greater than this summer's projected shortage. According to the Department of Energy, the total savings in peak energy demand would have been 3,672 MW per year if the Clinton standard had not been rolled back. The California Energy Commission reports that the peak shortfall in electricity generation for summer 2001 will be 3,050 MW.
