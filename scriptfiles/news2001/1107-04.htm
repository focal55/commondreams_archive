Pro-Choice Candidates Victorious
Gender Gap And Choice Issue Key To Victory In Governors' Races
  WASHINGTON - November 7 - Virginians turned their back on eight years of anti-choice leadership today, electing pro-choice Mark Warner to the Office of Governor, over anti-choice Mark Earley, largely on the votes of women.

NARAL President Kate Michelman declared, "Today, the women of Virginia decided who decides when it comes to personal medical choices."

"Voters in Virginia today sent a clear message that they want a governor who will protect and defend the freedom to choose," said Michelman. "For eight years, Virginians have watched their governor restrict reproductive rights at virtually every turn. That sad chapter in the state's history came to an end today as voters embraced the pro-choice policies of Mark Warner."

NARAL played a pivotal role in the Virginia race, reaching out to almost 198,000 voters with 272,000 mail pieces and 200,000 phone calls. In all, NARAL delivered approximately 9-10% of the votes Warner needed to win. Warner's victory today, along with Jim McGreevey's triumph in New Jersey, constitutes a pro-choice sweep in the critical races of the day.

"In Virginia and New Jersey, the choice issue fueled the gender gap and propelled pro-choice candidates to victory. For many, especially suburban voters and women, reproductive freedom was a defining issue in both these races.
