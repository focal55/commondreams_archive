City Council Urged to Test the Waters
   SAN JOSE, CA - June 5 - At today's City Council meeting, the Silicon Valley Toxics Coalition will urge the Council to postpone signing a contract with Calpine until appropriate health studies on the use of the recycled water for Calpine's cooling towers are performed. "For more than one year, the Silicon Valley Toxics Coalition (SVTC) has requested the City of San Jose to test the recycled water for harmful health effects", said Ted Smith, SVTC Executive Director. "There is a simple test that we have asked the city to undertake to ensure that the recycled water is safe. To date, the city has declined to do these tests."

Over the last several years, scientists have been increasingly concerned about the impact that low-level exposure to dangerous chemicals can have not only on the reproductive capacity of humans and other animals, but also on their offspring. The state-of-the-art multi-generational fish test advocated by SVTC is designed to determine whether the chemicals in the recycled water could cause harmful effects on generations and populations of species, including humans.

The Metcalf Road Calpine power plant will evaporate up to 3 million gallons of recycled water a day through the cooling towers of the plant. This water will be dispersed into the air and much of it will settle into the percolation ponds that are the main entryway to the groundwater drinking system (aquifers) for much of San Jose.

"We recognize that the energy crisis is driving the decision making on this project, but that should not be used as a rationale to make a snap decision that may have irreversible effects on the health of San Jose residents," said Ted Smith. "Greater research is needed before such a potentially life- altering decision can be made in good conscience."
