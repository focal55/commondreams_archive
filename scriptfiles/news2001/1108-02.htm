Sierra Club Encourages People to Visit "A Special Place" During These Uncertain Times
Ads Remind Us Nature Can Help In the Healing Process
   WASHINGTON - November 8 - The Sierra Club this week is launching a series of newspaper ads nationwide encouraging Americans to explore and enjoy the great outdoors to relieve stress during these uncertain times. The ads guide readers to an internet feature called "A Special Place" where Sierra Club members share their favorite recreation spots to help people locate outdoor treasures. That website is http://www.sierraclub.org/specialplace.

"Since September 11 people have been trying to cope with the uncertainty of terrorism, and Mother Nature can help us heal," said Sierra Club President Jennifer Ferenstein.

The ads, headlined "America is on the mend, and nature can help us heal," were inspired in part by the American Red Cross' advice for coping with terrorism: "Sit under a tree, look at a brook, lake, river or ocean. Remember that you are still free and that there is still beauty in the world." The ads will run in newspapers in 14 states and the District of Columbia twice this week ? Wednesday in the front section and Friday in the "weekend" section.

According to a report released October 22 by the Outdoor Industry Association, Americans view National Parks and mountain resorts as the safest weekend and vacation destinations in the wake of the terrorist attacks. Another survey released this week by outdoor retailer Recreational Equipment, Inc. (REI) found that 93 percent of respondents say spending leisure time outdoors is very or extremely important, and 70 percent plan to spend time doing outdoor activities this holiday season.

"In an effort to return to normalcy, Americans are looking to the outdoors for comfort," Ferenstein said. "They feel safe getting back to nature ? back to hiking, hunting, fishing, and biking."

People can log on to the website to read about special places, share the stories behind their special places, locate places near them to explore, and identify areas that need protection. Some favorites include the urban climbers' sanctuary of Rat Rock in New York City's Central Park, off-the-beaten-path Ruby Lake in southern Colorado's Weminuche Wilderness Area, Mammoth Cave along the Green River in Kentucky, and the rewarding trek to Half Dome Summit in Yosemite.

"Your special place can be as famous as a National Park or as ordinary as a neighborhood pond. But whether it's a childhood favorite or a recent discovery, it's a place that means something to you, and that's all that matters when it comes to healing," Ferenstein said.
