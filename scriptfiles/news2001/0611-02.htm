`Green Group' Letter to President Bush Calls for Mandatory Caps on Greenhouse Emissions
   WASHINGTON - June 11 - In anticipation of an announcement on climate change policy, the nation's leading environmental organizations sent President Bush a letter stating what the proposal would need to contain to be acceptable. (See attached.) The groups laid out clear markers by which to judge the President's proposal.

It must have:

-- A nationwide cap, or series of sectoral caps, on greenhouse gas emissions at levels consistent with the Framework Convention and the Kyoto Protocol; and

-- Emissions caps that are binding, without "safety valve" schemes that would allow businesses to continue polluting at excessive levels.

They stated that the hallmarks of an effective policy are:

-- Measures substantially increasing corporate average fuel economy for automobiles, sport utility vehicles and other light trucks;

-- Measures that clean up all four pollutants emitted by electric power plants; and

-- Measures to ensure widespread deployment and use of clean renewable energy sources.

President Bush is expected to announce a climate change proposal today in preparation for meetings later this week with EU heads of State in Gothenburg, Sweden.

Below is a copy of the letter sent to President George W. Bush:  

June 8, 2001  

BY FACSIMILE TRANSMISSION
The President
George W. Bush
The White House
Washington, DC 20500
Telefax: 202-456-2461

Dear Mr. President:

As your administration completes its climate policy review, we are writing to emphasize the importance of U.S. leadership on this critical global environmental issue, and the importance of your administration's adopting a credible policy for reducing greenhouse gas emissions. We believe that the Kyoto Protocol provides the critical foundation for a sound, long-term strategy for reducing greenhouse gas emissions, and the threat climate change poses to human health and biodiversity.

In upcoming international meetings, including the U.S.-European Summit, the G-8 Economic Summit, and the Conference of the Parties to the U.N. Framework Convention on Climate Change, the U.S. will be called upon to come forward with proposals for addressing climate change. You have stated that you take climate change seriously and that your administration will propose new solutions. We are concerned, however, by reports that you are considering reliance solely on voluntary approaches, which have not prevented emissions from steadily rising in the past and cannot be expected to do so in the future. In addition, we are concerned by the large increases in greenhouse gas emissions projected under your recent energy plan.

In our judgment, a credible and effective climate policy must include a nationwide cap, or series of sectoral caps, on greenhouse gas emissions at levels consistent with the Framework Convention and the Kyoto Protocol. These emissions caps need to be binding, without "safety valve" schemes that would allow businesses to continue polluting at excessive levels.

The hallmarks of an effective policy are measures substantially increasing corporate average fuel economy for automobiles, sport utility vehicles and other light trucks, cleaning up all four pollutants emitted by electric power plants and ensuring widespread deployment and use of clean renewable energy sources.

Numerous studies, including analyses by Department of Energy laboratories in 1997 and 2000, demonstrate that the U.S. can meet ambitious emissions reductions targets and continue strong economic growth. These policies will produce other important environmental and economic benefits as well, including sharply reduced air pollution and energy bill savings worth tens of billions of dollars per year.

Mr. President, time is running out to deal effectively with global warming. As the National Academy of Sciences confirmed in response to your request for additional scientific analysis, the earth's temperature is in fact already rising as a result of pollutants caused by human activity. Global warming is a real and present danger to current and future generations. We urge you to meet this threat with a credible and effective climate policy. A policy that relies on voluntary measures will not meet these tests.

Thank you for your consideration. 

Sincerely,
