Consumers Union Supports Senator Burton's Proposal To Create California Public Power Authority
   SAN FRANCISCO - January 18 - A bill introduced today by California Senate President John Burton today would help the state become more energy self sufficient and protect consumers from price gouging in the future, according to Consumers Union. The consumer organization endorsed SB 6X, which creates the California Consumer Power and Conservation Financing Authority. SB 6X was co-authored by Assemblymember Jackie Goldberg and is also supported by state Treasurer Philip Angelides.

"We believe this is an essential step to ensuring a reliable and reasonably priced supply of energy for California," said Harry Snyder, Senior Advocate for Consumers Union's West Coast Regional Office in San Francisco. "We urge state lawmakers to make the creation of a state power authority a top priority as they look to clean up the mess created by the failure of deregulation."

Under Senator Burton's proposal, the power authority would be empowered to finance the construction of additional generating capacity, energy efficiency and conservation programs, and assist with financing to make necessary retrofits to improve the plants efficiency and environmental performance of older power plants. The authority would consider the feasibility of the state acquiring and operating transmission facilities currently owned by investor-owned and municipal utilities. It would also develop a strategy for making necessary investments to assure a sufficient supply of natural gas. The Authority would underwrite its work through the issuance of bonds and the sale of land and facilities.

"California's future economic success depends on our ability to regain control over the state's energy resources," said Snyder. "This proposal offers a means to ensure adequate power supplies, reduce demand through conservation, and help stabilize rates."

Consumers Union has also urged state lawmakers to require PG&E and Edison to sell the power they generate to homeowners, renters, and small businesses, on a cost plus reasonable rate of return basis subject to rate making by the P.U.C. The group has also urged the state to provide Californians who are on fixed incomes, retirees, and low-income consumers with additional financial assistance to help with increased electric utility bills.

"Requiring PG&E and Edison to sell their in-state power to core customers at a cost-plus reasonable return basis will help stabilize prices for California ratepayers and protect consumers from price gouging by out-of-state suppliers," Snyder said.
