Members of Congress Seek to Overturn Bush Global Gag Rule
Planned Parenthood Supports Global Democracy Promotion Act of 2001
   WASHINGTON - February 15 - Today Senators Barbara Boxer (D-CA), Olympia Snowe (R-ME) and Lincoln Chafee (R-RI) will introduce the Global Democracy Promotion Act of 2001, legislation that would prevent the United States from imposing undemocratic and dangerous restrictions on health providers overseas. Representatives Nita Lowey (D-NY) and Nancy Johnson (R-CT) will introduce matching legislation in the House when it returns from recess. Both pieces of legislation are co-sponsored by more than 40 members of the House and Senate from both parties.

Senator Boxer and Representative Lowey developed the legislation in response to the "global gag rule," imposed by President George W. Bush on Jan. 22, 2001 -- the 28th anniversary of the landmark Roe v. Wade Supreme Court decision. The rule reinstates the Reagan-era Mexico City policy, which denied U.S. funding to any foreign non-governmental organizations that provides abortion services, counseling or referrals, or lobbied to change abortion laws.

"We are supporting the Global Democracy Promotion Act because we believe that providers and advocates understand better than anyone the health needs of a community," said Gloria Feldt, president of the Planned Parenthood Federation of America. "Restrictions like this create a culture of fear among those best equipped to address the health needs of women and their families around the world."

By imposing the "global gag rule," President Bush interferes with the doctor-patient relationship, and the sovereignty of other nations. The rule requires foreign health care providers to withhold critical medical information from their patients, as a condition of receiving U.S. funding.

"In this country, such a rule would be considered an intolerable intrusion into the sacred relationship between health care provider and patient," remarked Feldt.

Beginning Feb. 15, Feldt will visit a number of cutting-edge reproductive health and adolescent health programs in Kenya and Uganda. Members of the media can learn more about the programs and people that will be affected by the global gag rule by following Feldt on her journey. Daily updates will be available on http://www.plannedparenthood.org/international.

Planned Parenthood Federation of America is the world's largest and most trusted voluntary family planning organization. It comprises 127 affiliates that operate 875 health centers nationwide, which serve nearly five million Americans annually.
