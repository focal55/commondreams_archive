NWLC Co-President Marcia D. Greenberger to Testify in Support of Contraceptive Coverage Bill
  

WASHINGTON - September 7 - News Advisory:

On Monday, Sept. 10, National Women's Law Center Co-President Marcia D. Greenberger will testify in support of the Equity in Prescription Insurance and Contraceptive Coverage Act (EPICC) before the Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor and Pensions.

Greenberger's testimony is expected to focus on the importance of contraceptive coverage for women's health and equality. She will discuss the recent federal court decision in Erickson v. Bartell Drug Co. and a December 2000 ruling by the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission that represent significant progress on this issue, and why EPICC is still needed.

Greenberger will also highlight the Center's efforts through its Pill4Us Campaign to encourage women to ask their employers to add coverage of prescription contraceptives to their health plans. The Center has already worked on behalf of several female employees to encourage employers to cover this health care need. The Center Web site is giving employees the tools and information they need to approach employers about contraceptive coverage.

When:
Monday, Sept. 10 at 3 p.m.

Where:
Dirksen Senate Office Building, Room 430
First and Constitution Ave, N.E.

The National Women's Law Center is a non-profit organization that has been working since 1972 to advance and protect women's legal rights. NWLC focuses on major policy areas of importance to women and their families including economic security, education, employment and health, with special attention given to the concerns of low-income women.
