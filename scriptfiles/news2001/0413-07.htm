Oxfam Opposes Free Trade Area of the Americas
Rallies With ACT-UP, AFL
  WASHINGTON - April 13 - Oxfam America joins with ACT UP and other groups campaigning to make AIDS drugs more accessible to poor countries in a rally today opposing a proposed hemispheric trade agreement that will protect patents, not patients.

"At Oxfam, we believe the Free Trade of the Americas would be way too free. It would be free of respect for the right of free people--of everyone in the Americas--to have a say in the rules that affect their lives and their livelihoods, free of protection for people who need more access to medicines we take for granted in the U.S. It's been free of public scrutiny--but that ends now," said Jo Marie Griesgraber, Policy Director, Oxfam America.

The three Oxfam groups based in North America--Oxfam America, Oxfam Quebec, and Oxfam Canada--will all see the President, the U.S. Trade Representative, and fellow protesters in Quebec City during the Summit of the Americas in two weeks' time, she promised.

For more information, please see "Why Oxfam Opposes the Free Trade Area of the Americas," a briefing paper prepared for distribution at the Summit of the Americas, April 17, 2001.
