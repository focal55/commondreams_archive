Democrats.com Protests Bush in New York City on Tuesday, July 10
   NEW YORK - July 9 - Democrats.com, the largest community of Democratic activists on the Internet, will protest George W. Bush's visit to New York City on Tuesday, July 10.

The protest will take place at 1:30 p.m. at Fifth Avenue between 52nd and 53rd St.

Bush will be in New York to present the Congressional Gold Medal to the family of John Cardinal O'Connor at St. Patrick's Cathedral.

Members of Democrats.com will be dressed in black to symbolize mourning, and will not engage in civil disobedience or show disrepect towards the ceremony at St. Patrick's.

According to Democrats.com co-founder Bob Fertik, "Democrats.com will be holding a solemn 'Memorial for Democracy,' because American Democracy died on December 12, 2000 when the Republican Supreme Court ordered a halt to vote counting and declared Bush the winner. Our message to Bush will be subdued and simple: 'Thou Shalt Not Steal,'" Fertik said.

Democrats.com has helped organize hundreds of protests against the George W. Bush and the Stolen Election since last November, in cities and towns across the United States. Democrats.com has also co-sponsored two major protest marches in Washington DC, on Inauguration Day and May 19.

According to Fertik, "After nearly 6 months in office, it is clear that George W. Bush's agenda is wrong for America - and wrong for New York."

"New Yorkers want better education, more affordable health care, and a cleaner environment. George Bush wants to shift taxes from working people to the rich, a missile defense scheme that will never work, and a secret war on Medicare and Social Security. Bush is wrong on the issues, and we will tell him so," Fertik said.

Democrats.com also believes that Bush is an illegitimate President because he did not actually win Florida, but rather stole the election with the help of his brother Jeb and a partisan Republican majority on the U.S. Supreme Court. The Miami Herald's statewide count of uncounted ballots show that Gore actually won Florida by 662 votes, without counting a single disputed chad. The definitive count of nearly 175,000 uncounted ballots by the National Opinion Research Center is expected any day.

Co-sponsors of the New York City protest include Campaign To End The Death Penalty, Citizens For Legitimate Government, DemocracyMarch.org, International Action Center, New York Sierra Club, NOW-New York City, and Refuse and Resist.
