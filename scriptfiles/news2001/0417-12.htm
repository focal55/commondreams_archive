Planned Parenthood of New York City Declares War on Unplanned Pregnancies
Emergency Contraception Public Awareness Campaign Launched in New York City Radio, Subways, Bars and Restaurants to Carry the Message
   NEW YORK - April 17 - Starting April 23, 2001, Planned Parenthood of New York City (PPNYC) will launch its new public awareness campaign Accidents Happen, promoting use of emergency contraception (EC) to prevent unplanned pregnancies in New York City and announcing its availability at PPNYC health centers. The three-pronged campaign will consist of radio and subway advertisements as well as framed posters in over 100 New York City bars and restaurants. To view the Accidents Happen campaign poster and listen to the radio advertisements, visit our website at www.ppnyc.org.

Whether a couple's contraception fails, contraception isn't used, or a woman is a survivor of rape, EC offers women a strong chance of preventing an unplanned pregnancy. EC can be taken to prevent unintended pregnancy within three days of unprotected intercourse. When EC is taken within 24 hours of unprotected sex, chances of a woman becoming pregnant decrease by 95%.

"PPNYC wanted to take this opportunity to inform women about this highly effective and safe option to prevent pregnancy," said Joan Malin, CEO of PPNYC. "Widespread use of EC could prevent half of all unintended pregnancies and half of all abortions. In New York City alone, with increased availability and public awareness, EC could potentially eliminate 100,000 unintended pregnancies and up to 50,000 abortions a year."

Unfortunately, EC remains reproductive health care's best kept secret. A 1999 Kaiser Family Foundation survey found that only 11% of American women are currently aware of EC as an option. A recent PPNYC survey found that 97% of pharmacists polled were either wholly unknowledgeable or provided incorrect information on EC.

"In 2000, PPNYC provided more than 4,500 prescriptions for women for EC, a 70% increase in the number of visits since 1998," continued Malin. "We expect to see an even greater increase as we continue to educate New York City women and health professionals about EC's potential to greatly reduce the rate of unintended pregnancies."

PPNYC's public awareness campaign on EC will begin on April 23 with radio advertisements. Grammy-winning artists Dice Raw and Scratch from The Roots will inform women about EC and its availability at Planned Parenthood health centers on WBLS FM, Hot 97 FM and WKTU FM. Another Grammy winner, Lilith Fair-founder Sarah McLachlan, can be heard on WPLJ FM, and a Spanish-language advertisement will be heard on La Mega. PPNYC will place framed posters advertising EC in restrooms in over 100 restaurants and bars in New York City from late April through June. Coasters promoting EC will also be given to participating restaurants and bars. Subway advertisements will be posted from May 1st through June. Total expenditure for the campaign is approximately $125,000.

At PPNYC, visits for EC cost $55 and include a physical examination and a cycle of birth control pills. Appointments to obtain EC can be made by calling PPNYC's confidential appointment and information hotline at (212) 965-7000 or by visiting www.ppnyc.org. Walk-in appointments at 26 Bleecker Street are also welcomed.

PPNYC's mission is to protect women's reproductive rights and maintain their access to safe, effective and confidential care. PPNYC pursues these goals through our state-of-the-art clinical services, education and professional training, and advocacy. PPNYC provides birth control; emergency contraception; medical and surgical abortion services; pregnancy testing and options counseling; GYN care; colposcopy; STD and HIV testing, counseling and treatment; prenatal care; social service counseling, and help in obtaining Medicaid or Child Health Plus. PPNYC has centers in the Bronx, Brooklyn, and Manhattan. To make an appointment, call PPNYC at 212 965 7000 or visit our website at www.ppnyc.org.
