Nader Calls on Pataki and Giuliani to Pull Plug on Billion-Dollar NYSE Giveaway
   WASHINGTON - February 12 - Calling the New York State and City decision to grant a series of subsidies, now expected to total more than $1 billion, to the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) "one of the most foolish and cruelly ironic urban public policy decisions in recent memory," consumer advocate Ralph Nader in a letter today urged New York Governor George Pataki and New York City Mayor Rudolph Giuliani to pull the plug on the deal.

"Tragi-comedy may play well on Broadway, but it doesn't belong on Wall Street, at least not with City Hall and Albany as the underwriters," Nader wrote.

In December, the city entered into a letter of intent to assist the NYSE in constructing a new trading floor. The arrangement commits the city to acquire a land for the new exchange, and for the city and state to construct a new trading floor for the NYSE, and to grant tax and subsidized energy benefits to the NYSE. In exchange, the taxpayers receive nothing but $10 million annual rent, which will never come close to reimbursing the city and state for their costs.

"The sole purported rationale for the corporate welfare bonanza, of course, is to retain the New York Stock Exchange in New York City," Nader noted. "If one were to credit this rationale, the gift of more than a billion dollars for the purpose of retaining fewer than 5,000 jobs -- while not even ostensibly creating new ones -- would, even by the corrupt standards of job-retention blackmail deals, set a highwater mark for ransom payments among arrangements of this scale."

However, Nader argued, there is no chance that the stock exchange would leave New York City.

"The New Jersey ploy is nothing more than a NYSE ruse to provide a pretext for covering public officials using what Justice Louis Brandeis once called 'other people's money," Nader wrote.

"Surely some residual sense of shame must persist in New York politics," Nader wrote. "In a state with one of the nation's worst child poverty rates, and one of the largest gaps between rich and poor, is a billion dollar subsidy for the emblematic bastion of global capitalism not beyond the pale?"

The full text of Nader's letter appears at http://www.essential.org/nysesubsidydeal.html.
