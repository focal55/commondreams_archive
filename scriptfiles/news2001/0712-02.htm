Steelworkers, Sierra Club, SEIU, Union of Concerned Scientists Join to Battle Global Warming, Create Jobs, Produce Clean Energy
   WASHINGTON - July 12 - The United Steelworkers of America, the Sierra Club, the Service Employees International Union and the Union of Concerned Scientists will join forces on Tuesday, July 17 to fight global warming and create secure jobs. The groups will announce their support for a worker-friendly, clean energy study developed by the Economic Policy Institute and the Center for Sustainable Economy aimed at halting global warming.

The study develops the basic features for helping the environment and boosting the U.S. economy, while also providing a just transition for workers and retirees in affected industries through income replacement, retraining, and support for employees near retirement. Leaders of the labor and environmental organizations will unveil tenants of the clean energy plan and discuss its importance.
