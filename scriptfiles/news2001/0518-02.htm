Stand For Children Launches Cyber Stand For Children: Building Our Voice and Vision for All Children
   WASHINGTON - May 18 - Cyber Stand For Children is the online component of Stand For Children Day 2001, a series of events held around the country to focus attention on the health, education, and safety needs of children. From May 21 to June 10, this action and education campaign will use the Internet to connect people who share a commitment to children. Visitors will be able to log in to www.stand.org to:
  • Take a virtual tour of local Stand For Children Day events throughout the country.
  • Participate in question and answer sessions with author Betty Holcomb, child advocate Marian Wright Edelman, and Stand For Children organizer and Executive Director, Jonah Edelman.
  • Sign the Stand For Children Pledge.
  • Get information about issues affecting children and families.
  • Find out what communities around the country are doing to support children and families.

Email elected officials encouraging them to stand up for legislation that supports children and families.

On or around June 1, thousands of volunteers in all fifty states and the District of Columbia will hold local Stand For Children Day events. As a result of these events, parents, teachers, and community members will be aware of vital programs and services in their communities, and more decisionmakers will know that their constituents are concerned about children and families. Past events have also galvanized many communities to take long-term action on issues such as child care and mental health.

A few of this year’s Stand For Children Day events:

    • Town Hall Meetings in Brevard County, Florida
    • Statewide Children’s Health Insurance Sign Up Day throughout Texas
    • Student-legislator meetings in Sacramento, California
    • Children’s Sabbaths in hundreds of Mississippi churches
    • March and Rally for Children at the State Capitol in Juneau, Alaska
    • Stand For Children Day at the Zoo in Bismarck, North Dakota

For specific local event information, please call (800) 663-4032 x108 or visit www.stand.org.

Stand For Children, the United States’ only nationwide grassroots voice for children, was founded on June 1, 1996 by Jonah Edelman, son of Marian Wright and Peter Edelman, and Eliza Leighton, when more than 300,000 people stood together at the largest demonstration for children in history. Stand For Children’s mission is to build a voice strong enough to give all children the opportunity to grow up healthy, educated, and safe.

Stand For Children leaders are available for interviews: Jonah Edelman, Executive Director; Eliza Leighton, Deputy Director. Local Event Coordinator interviews arranged by calling (800) 663-4032 x108.
