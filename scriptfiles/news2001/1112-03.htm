Poll Shows Voters Support Rethinking Goals of Welfare Policies
Event to Launch Campaign to Impact Welfare Reform Reauthorization
   WASHINGTON - November 12 - News Advisory:

After five years, the legislation known as Welfare Reform is facing its first crucial test as the economy slumps further into recession. The Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) block grant, the product of the 1996 Personal Responsibility and Work Opportunity Reconciliation Act, is set for Congressional reauthorization early next year. As the debate over reauthorization begins, a new poll shows voters favor a welfare system that helps low-income families lift and keep themselves out of poverty.

The poll results will be released on Wednesday, Nov. 14 at the launch of a movement to repair the gaping holes in our social safety net -- the Make TANF Work! campaign. Leaders from the labor movement, faith community, policy experts and grassroots activists will join to launch a fresh initiative to bring the voices of America's struggling families and laid-off workers to Washington.
