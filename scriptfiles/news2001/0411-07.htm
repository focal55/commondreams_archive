Kyoto Protocol - Talks Continue; Proposal on Rules for the Climate Treaty Offers Basis for Negotiations
   WASHINGTON - April 11 - Negotiations on the Kyoto Protocol are alive and well in spite of the recent statements from the Bush Administration. The latest evidence of this comes in the form of the most recent proposal by the President of the negotiations, Minister Pronk from The Netherlands. Minister Pronk has presented a new proposal on rules for the treaty that will be discussed in a closed ministerial meeting in New York on Saturday. WWF is working on a detailed analysis of the proposal, which will be released shortly.

"This proposal demonstrates that the Kyoto Protocol is the basis for international negotiations, despite the Bush Administration attempts to take it off the table. The Bush Administration should either engage constructively on this basis, or get out of the way and let the rest of the world move forward," said Jennifer Morgan, director of WWF's Climate Change Campaign. "The Administration's sentiments are clearly out of touch with the fears and concerns of the American public and the vast majority of the world."

Dutch Environment Minister Jan Pronk will maintain the Presidency of the climate negotiations regarding the rules for implementing the Kyoto Protocol. These negotiations stalled in The Hague last November and will resume in the Bonn this July. Over 150 countries including the United States have signed the protocol, and polls consistently demonstrate that Americans want action to address global warming. However, both the President and EPA Administrator have stated their firm opposition to the Kyoto Protocol and reversed a campaign pledge that would have cut carbon dioxide emissions from power plants.
