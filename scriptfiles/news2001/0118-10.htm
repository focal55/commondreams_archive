Ashcroft Engages In Affirmative Deception
Nominee For Attorney General Mischaracterizes The Law
   WASHINGTON - January 18 - Former Senator, and Attorney General nominee, John Ashcroft "has misconstrued the law surrounding affirmative action programs throughout his Senate career and continues to do so in his current confirmation testimony," said Georgina Verdugo today after reviewing Ashcroft's testimony before the Senate Judiciary committee.

"In both instances when affirmative action was mentioned during the hearing, Mr. Ashcroft got it wrong. He has purposefully mischaracterized the law."

Senator Orrin Hatch asked Mr. Ashcroft, on Tuesday "what sort of affirmative action programs (he) would support?" Senator Ashcroft completely deflected answering the question and referred to "Affirmative Access" in Texas, California and Florida.

"Clearly Senator Ashcroft will not distinguish the very important differences between the programs that have replaced affirmative action in these three states, or how they came about. And by no means are these programs or their outcomes an adequate substitute for legal and constitutional affirmative action," said Verdugo.

"Access and action are two very different things. During the Presidential debates Governor Bush refused to articulate exactly what he meant by "affirmative access" and now, Senator Ashcroft is trying to hide behind the same undefined term."

During Bill Lann Lee's confirmation hearings, Senator Ashcroft confusedly cited Adarand (Adarand Constructors, Inc. v. Pena, 515 US 200 (1995) as the most important case in civil rights. Senator Ashcroft deliberately ignores a 23-year-old Supreme Court ruling that pronounces affirmative action constitutional. Senator Ashcroft challenged Bill Lee's understanding of the law by injecting the idea that quotas were part of affirmative action, failing to recognize that quotas have been illegal since the Supreme Court ruled on Bakke (Regents of the University of California v. Bakke, 438 U.S. 265 (1978). Four years later Senator Ashcroft still refuses to accept the state of the law as it has been for nearly a quarter of a century.

Senator Ashcroft has been a strong and vocal advocate against affirmative action, and his statements have only reinforced the public misunderstanding on this issue. Affirmative action gives qualified women and minorities an opportunity to succeed. Affirmative action means a fair chance for all Americans.

Ms. Verdugo is the Executive Director of Americans For A Fair Chance and has served as the Deputy Assistant Attorney General in the Department of Justice, Office Legislative Affairs.

Americans for a Fair Chance is a non-partisan consortium of six of America's leading civil rights legal organizations: Lawyers' Committee for Civil Rights Under Law, Mexican American Legal Defense and Education Fund, NAACP Legal Defense Fund Inc., National Asian Pacific American Legal Consortium, National Women's Law Center and National Partnership for Women and Families.
