Statement by Jill Lancelot, Legislative Director at Taxpayers for Common Sense on the Bush Administration's Mining Rule
   WASHINGTON - June 14 - Today's mining decision by the Bureau of Land Management is a good first step toward the comprehensive mining reform that taxpayers deserve. The new rules will protect taxpayers from billions of dollars in liability from mine cleanups.

Mining companies will have to financially bond the mines to make sure that taxpayers don't get stuck with the check when it's time to clean up mine sites. Without these bonding requirements, taxpayers would be on the hook for between $1 billion and $20 billion in potential clean up costs for currently operating metals mines.

While the new rule is a good start, it addresses just one small piece of the mining picture. Despite these new protections, mining companies still get taxpayer-owned land for rock bottom prices ranging from $2.50 to $5 an acre and extract precious metals from those lands for free.

American taxpayers should also remember that there are more than 500,000 abandoned mines across the country that will cost $32 billion to $72 billion to clean up. Those costs will not be lessened under the Administration's new mining regulations.

Let's hope today's announcement can be the impetus for the comprehensive reforms to mining regulations that the American public deserves.
