More than 100 Chapters Active in Safe Schools Program
 

WASHINGTON - February 13 - Parents, Families and Friends of Lesbians and Gays (PFLAG) announced today that more than 100 chapters are participating in "From Our House to the School House: A Recipe for Safe Schools," a nationwide program to make schools safe for gay, lesbian, bisexual and transgendered (GLBT) students and others. Through the program, PFLAG chapters work with their local school district to incorporate GLBT-positive images in their resources, programs and curricula.

"We are very proud of the work that PFLAG members around the country are doing in their local school districts,"said Kirsten Kingdon, PFLAG Executive Director. "Our national safe schools program gives local PFLAG chapters the tools to work within their school system to dismantle institutional homophobia and promote a more inclusive environment."

"From Our House to the School House" is a long-term program to make school a safe and welcoming environment for all students, particularly GLBT students, those whose parents are GLBT, and those who are perceived to be GLBT. It was formally launched in October 2000.

"There is so much change that needs to occur before GLBT students can feel safe in their schools," said Kingdon. "The first step is educating school administration, faculty and support staff about dangerous attitudes and biased practices. Then we can move on to implement training programs, non-discrimination policies, and resources that support GLBT youth. We’re looking forward to more chapters to become involved."

Here are just some examples of the work that PFLAG is doing around the country.

In California, 14 PFLAG chapters are working with their local school districts to train administrators, faculty and counselors on student safety issues. Many chapters are helping to create or supporting student Gay-Straight Alliances (GSA’s).

In Virginia, PFLAG Fredericksburg trained 200 teachers in the Stafford School District. Students report "Safe Zone" stickers are showing up in schools as a result of the training, and the program will now serve as a model for two adjoining counties.

In New York, the Long Island chapter met with Lieutenant Governor Mary Donohue in October to discuss the implementation of the Project SAVE (Safe Schools Against Violence in Education), to promote a safer and more effective learning environment within New York State's schools. Two members of the Buffalo/Niagra Area chapter are doing presentations to local high school educators. They are also meeting with the school board to pass a non-discrimination policy, and are currently preparing a presentation for school nurses on GLBT issues.

In Colorado, the Boulder chapter is a leading organization in the Boulder Valley Safe Schools Coalition. In November, the Coalition held a breakfast for more than 80 school administrators, parents, Board of Education members, and community leaders to learn about safe and inclusive schools. They invited school board candidates to participate in their program, "Can We Legislate Safe Schools for GLBT Students," and to talk about how the Colorado Board of Education can implement new programs.

In Arizona, safe schools work has been going on for several years. Last year, the Phoenix chapter gave 59 educational presentations to a total of 854 adults and 786 students. In conjunction with "From Our House to the School House," Phoenix PFLAG members are hoping to double their numbers this year and expand programs around the state.

Students who are GLBT or who are perceived to be such face unique and overwhelming challenges in daily school life:

  • GLBT youth are 2-3 times more likely to attempt suicide than heterosexual youth, and 30% of completed youth suicides annually are committed by GLBT youth.
  • 80% of GLB youth report severe problems due to cognitive, social and emotional isolation.
  • 97% of public school students report regularly hearing homophobic remarks from peers

PFLAG promotes the health and well-being of gay, lesbian, bisexual and transgendered persons, their families and friends through support, education and advocacy. Serving more than 80,000 members and supporters, PFLAG’s 450 affiliates are located in communities across the United States.
