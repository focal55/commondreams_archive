Massive Failures on Conflict Diamonds Filled Terrorists' Coffers For September 11 Attack
   WASHINGTON - November 2 - U.S. Rep. Tony Hall (D-Ohio) today called a report in today's Washington Post, "Al Qaeda Cash Tied to Diamond Trade; Sale of Gems from Sierra Leone Rebels Raised Millions, Sources Say," more evidence that ending the trade in conflict diamonds ought to be a top priority for Congress.

"Reports in today's Washington Post suggest recent years' efforts by US intelligence agencies, the Clinton and Bush Administrations, the Congress and industry watch-dogs to end the trade in conflict diamonds not only have failed -- but have been so impotent that they allowed terrorists to fill the coffers that support their attacks on Americans," Hall said.

"We've known for a long time that shady operators, including an organization the State Department calls a terrorist group -- the Revolutionary United Front -- were earning hundreds of millions of dollars a year from this blood trade," he said. "If this report is accurate, we've also known exactly who was collecting some of that money: to Osama bin Laden."

"Al Qaeda's workings may be news to most Americans, and the link between the money we spend on tokens of love and terror ought to shock consumers and the jewelers who employ tens of thousands of people in communities across our country alike. Conflict diamonds' exploitation by terrorists appears to have been well known to our intelligence agencies, and yet Congress still has not acted on any of the bills introduced over the course of two years. All of them aimed to sever the link that brutal rebels used to fund their operations - and that terrorists have been milking," Hall said.

"I don't fault US policymakers or civil servants for failing to predict how terrorists would use this blood money," he added. "But our collective failure, along with the diamond industry's, has been to treat those who commit acts of terror differently when the victims are innocent Africans. Any terrorist organization -- including the RUF -- ought to have been targeted long before it claims U.S. victims. That didn't happen, and the result is our job is now much harder and the threat to Americans much greater.

"It is a sorry day for me, and for everyone who has known this blood trade is funding terror but has been unable or unwilling to stop it," Hall said. "I urge Congress to act immediately on legislation to right this wrong before more innocent people -- Africans or Americans -- suffer crimes that are funded in part by the Americans who buy the majority of the world's diamonds."

Hall is an original author of the Clean Diamond Trade Act, HR 2722. It is the sixth bill he has sponsored to end the trade in conflict diamonds since late 1999, when he visited Sierra Leone and Guinea with U.S. Rep. Frank R. Wolf (R-VA). The House bill's lead sponsors are Rep. Amo Houghton (R-NY) and Rep. Charles Rangel (D-NY). Sponsors of its Senate companion, S 1084, are Sen. Dick Durbin (D-IL), Sen. Mike DeWine (R-OH), and Sen. Russ Feingold (D-WI). The bills have the support of a coalition of more than 100 humanitarian, human rights and faith groups, and of the diamond and jewelry industries.
