NWLC Calls For Close Senate Scrutiny Of Judicial Nominations Expected This Week
  WASHINGTON - May 8 - In response to news reports that President Bush may announce his first judicial nominations within days, the National Women's Law Center urged the Senate to carry out its equal and independent constitutional responsibility in the confirmation process, by closely scrutinizing all nominees to ensure their commitment to the rights and principles that Americans hold dear.

"Federal judges have sweeping powers to ensure that women continue to advance or to turn the clock back," said Marcia D. Greenberger, NWLC co-president. "In the judicial confirmation proceedings to come, the burden will be on each nominee to demonstrate to the Senate that he or she is worthy of a lifetime appointment to the federal bench, with all the powers that role carries. With fundamental rights at stake, the Senate must resolve any doubts about a judicial nominee in favor of protecting those rights."

Before a judicial nominee is confirmed, the Senate must satisfy itself not only that the nominee meets high standards of character, experience, legal excellence and temperament, but also that he or she embraces:

-- a commitment to core constitutional principles that protect and advance women's legal rights, especially rights guaranteed under the Equal Protection Clause and the right to privacy, and

-- a commitment to equal justice principles reflected in key statutory provisions that protect and advance women's legal rights, such as rights to education and employment secured by federal law.

While much attention has focused on a possible Supreme Court appointment in the coming months, nominations for the lower courts are also critically important. Because most cases do not reach the Supreme Court, those appointed to judgeships in the lower federal courts will determine the fate of American women -- and men -- for decades to come.

The National Women's Law Center is a non-profit organization that has been working since 1972 to advance and protect women's legal rights. NWLC focuses on major policy areas of importance to women and their families including economic security, education, employment and health, with special attention given to the concerns of low-income women.
