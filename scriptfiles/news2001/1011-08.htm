Celebrities, Activists, Ask
"What Will Make Us More Secure?"
New Yorkers Join International Rally for Peace and Security
  NEW YORK - October 11 - On Saturday, October 13, thousands of New Yorkers will join prominent activists and celebrities to mourn New York's loss and urge the government to invest in peace instead of war and high-tech "defenses." The Rev. Al Sharpton and Father Daniel Berrigan, journalist Amy Goodman, physics professor Michio Kaku, Pakistani scholar Zia Mian, and policy analyst Bill Hartung will be among those trying to answer the question, "What will make us more secure?" Singer/songwriters Toshi Reagon and Patti Smith and the renowned Bread and Puppet Theater will perform.

The event is part of an International Day of Protest to Stop the Militarization of Space supported by 266 organizations world-wide including the War Resisters League, the American Friends Service Committee, the Global Resource Action Center on the Environment and the Global Network Against Nuclear Power and Weapons in Space. The rally in Washington Square will be one of 109 taking place that day around the world.

"In New York City, the rally will be an opportunity for thousands of New Yorkers to voice their support for peaceful, just solutions to our nation's security problems," said Kevin Heffel of the American Friends Service Committee. "Recent events show that the use of violence to resolve human conflict only creates more hatred and more killing," added Bruce Gagnon, Coordinator of the Global Network Against Nuclear Power and Weapons in Space. And Carmen Trotta of the War Resisters League noted, "This rally will take place only a week after 10,000 New Yorkers marched for peace. The voice for peace persists."
