Common Cause to Honor John W. Gardner for Lifetime Achievement
   WASHINGTON - June 19 - Common Cause will honor John W. Gardner with its highest award, the Public Service Achievement Award, at a luncheon ceremony at 12:00 on Friday, June 22, 2001 at the Jurys Washington Hotel, 1500 New Hampshire Avenue, NW, on Dupont Circle in Washington, DC.

Since 1981, the Common Cause Public Service Achievement Award has recognized individuals "who by the force of imagination, initiative, and perseverance have made an outstanding contribution to the public interest in the areas of government performance and integrity."

The Award has historically been given both to nationally known figures as well as community activists and local government officials. Past recipients include reform activist Doris "Granny D" Haddock, former DC Inspector General E. Barrett Prettyman, Jr., the late Supreme Court Justice Thurgood Marshall, and Paul Volcker for his work as chairman of the National Commission on Public Service, among many others.

To further honor Gardner, who founded Common Cause in 1970 and chaired the organization until 1977, the award will be renamed the John Gardner Public Service Award.

What: The first annual John Gardner Public Service Award

Who: John W. Gardner, inaugural recipient

When: 12:00 p.m. on Friday, June 22, 2001

Where: Jurys Washington Hotel at Dupont Circle
1500 New Hampshire Avenue, NW, Washington, DC

John Gardner is a former President of the Carnegie Corporation and the Carnegie Foundation for the Advancement of Teaching, and served as Secretary of Health, Education, and Welfare in the Administration of President Lyndon B.Johnson.

Gardner chaired the National Urban Coalition, cofounded Independent Sector, chaired the National Civic League, and is the author of Excellence (1961, 1984), Self-Renewal (1964, 1981), No Easy Victories (1968), The Recovery of Confidence (1970), In Common Cause (1972),On Leadership (1990), and was editor of John F. Kennedy's To Turn the Tide. Gardner was awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom in 1964, and today is Consulting Professor at the Stanford University School of Education.

"John Gardner has done more to shape the landscape of the nonprofit advocacy community than any other individual in America," Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger said."All of us at Common Cause are honored by our longstanding association with John Gardner, and all Americans have been enriched by his service to his country.

"We honor not only the man, but his vision for our democracy and the inspiration he has provided to thousands of Americans who have chosen careers in the public interest, and for the living proof he provides of the most important notion he brought to Common Cause - the notion that every person can make a difference.

"Renaming our award for public service in his name is a modest yet fitting way to express our appreciation not only for his contributions to Common Cause but for his lifetime of service," Harshbarger said.

To attend the luncheon, or for more information, contact the Common Cause Press Office at 202/736-5770.
