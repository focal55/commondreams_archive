National Environmental Trust President Available for On-Air Comment on Withdraw of Arsenic Standard for Drinking Water
   WASHINGTON - March 20 - Philip E. Clapp, president of the National Environmental Trust is available for TV and radio interviews regarding EPA Administrator Whitman's scheduled announcement that she will withdraw the arsenic standard for drinking water today.

Whitman's withdrawal of arsenic standards flies in the face of scientific evidence that arsenic causes cancer and other debilitating diseases, and occurs in unsafe levels in water supplies in many areas in the United States.

To schedule an interview, contact Brandon MacGillis of NET, 202-887-8833 (office) or 202-422-8328 (cell).

The National Environmental Trust is a national, non-profit environmental organization providing public policy expertise and educational outreach on national environmental issues.
