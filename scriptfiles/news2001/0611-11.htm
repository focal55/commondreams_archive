Handgun Control Hosts News Conference to Announce New Name
   WASHINGTON - June 11 - Leaders of Handgun Control (HCI) and the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence (CPHV) will hold a news conference Thursday morning, June 14, to announce the new names of the organizations.

"The new names will more accurately express our mission: to enact and enforce gun laws, to provide public education about gun violence and to reform the gun industry," said Michael D. Barnes, president of both organizations.

The news conference will be the first official unveiling of the organizations' new names, logos and Web sites. At the news conference, Barnes will also release a new poll showing strong support for key gun control issues: closing the gunshow loophole, requiring child safety locks on all new guns and limiting handgun purchases to one per month.

Later that evening, there will be a National Tribute Gala for Jim and Sarah Brady, featuring celebrities, political figures and gun control supporters from across the nation.
