Lawyers' Committee Concerned Attacks On Americans May Undermine Sacred Freedoms
Distressed About Increase of Violent Hate Crimes
   WASHINGTON - September 21 - The Lawyers' Committee for Civil Rights Under Law ("Lawyers' Committee) joins with the millions of Americans who are mourning the loss and devastation to our nation from the violent and horrific attacks on the United States. As we collectively grieve the loss of life we recognize that the hate manifest on our nation knew no color, religion or ethnicity -- it horrified and devastated everyone equally. It is because of this reality that recent widespread incidents of hate violence against racial and ethnic Americans, particularly those of Middle Eastern descent are especially disturbing. Acts of hate violence present a growing danger to American democracy and the freedoms that all in our nation enjoy, the Lawyers' Committee warned today.

The Lawyers' Committee is grateful that such hateful acts have been condemned by our political leaders both in the Administration and Congress. To demonstrate our national solidarity in deploring these violent crimes, the Lawyers' Committee is urging attendance at next Tuesday's "Vigil for Community Healing, Tolerance, and Unity" in Washington, D.C., because of fears that "misdirected fury" will be a very real threat to our citizens and the unity of the nation.

"With the past week's wave of violent hate crimes against innocent Americans who even appear to be Middle Eastern, we are undermining the very freedoms we're now battling to protect," said Barbara R. Arnwine, Lawyers' Committee Executive Director. "Incidents spreading from California to Cleveland are driven by prejudice and stereotyping and instead of showing the unity and strength in which our nation stands, represents a few committed to hate and violence," Arnwine added.

Arnwine emphasized that the hate crimes upsurge has been horrific and senseless, even resulting in many incidents in the past week against those not of Middle Eastern descent, including Native Americans, African Americans as well as Sikhs who are not Muslim, but a totally separate religion. Arnwine added "to make a scapegoat of Sikhs is nonsensical, they are targeted merely because they often wear turbans and beards -- highly visible symbols of Middle Eastern origin."

The site of the Vigil is not by accident, its location at the National Japanese American Memorial to Patriotism reminds us that we are a diverse nation and as such cannot cast blame on patriotic Americans who by accident of birth share only an ancestry with those who are suspected of attacking the United States. The memorial is at the intersection of Louisiana Avenue, D Street, and New Jersey Avenue, NW in Washington, DC. It is being sponsored by the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights and the Community Relations Service of the Justice Department.
