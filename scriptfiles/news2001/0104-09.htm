Reverend Jesse Jackson Concludes The Year 2000 With a Visit To Death Row
   CHICAGO - January 4 - On New Year's Eve, Reverend Jesse Jackson led a delegation to the Ponitac, Illinois Correctional Facility to visit a group of inmates known as the Death Row 10. The Death Row 10, who are all African American men, were convicted and sentenced to death based on confessions rendered under tortuous conditions. Former Chicago Police Commander Jon Burge and his detectives were identified and dismissed for their inhumane and illegal behavior. However, none of the 10 convictions have been overturned.

Rev. Jackson elected to reach-out to the Death Row 10, in an effort to shed light on the grave injustices suffered by these men and their families. "The cases of the Death Row 10 are plagued with racial bias and police prosecutorial and judicial misconduct. Governor Ryan has acted boldly and morally by calling for a moratorium on the death penalty but we are calling for abolishment," remarks Jackson.

During the visit, Rev. Jackson was joined by Deputy Governor, Matthew Bettenhausen, Director of the Illinois Department of Corrections, Donnie Sneider, members of the Rainbow/PUSH Coalition, Campaign to End the Death Penalty and 3 mothers of the Death Row 10.

Reliving their hideous experiences, the men described the electric shock, suffocation, beatings and other brutal forms of physical abuse they were subjected to. Jackson is waging a campaign for the revamping of the criminal justice system and is calling for an end to police brutality.

In concert with Jackson's local efforts, he will travel to Oklahoma City on Thursday to participate in a march for a moratorium on the death penalty in Oklahoma. Eight death row inmates are scheduled for execution in January. While in Oklahoma, Jackson plans to visit Wanda Jean Allen, the first woman to be executed since statehood.

The march is scheduled to begin at 4:30 P.M. at the Mabel Bassett Correctional Center, 3300 North Martin Luther King Ave and journey to the Fairview Baptist Church, 1700 NE 78, where a rally will be held at 6 PM.
