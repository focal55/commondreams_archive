Why:
An independent assessment of the World Commission on Dams (WCD) concludes that as globalization advances, multi-stakeholders processes like the WCD can advance international debates on contentious environment, development, and social justice issues.

While multi-stakeholder processes do not produce legally binding results, they help shape solutions to conflict and provide a voice for marginalized groups. The success of similar future processes, however, depends on their independence, transparency, and inclusiveness.

The WCD, which existed from 1998-2000, was organized to review the development effectiveness of large dams, and assess alternatives to dam building. This assessment of WCD was conducted by researchers from WRI, Lokayan in India, and Lawyers' Environmental Action Team (LEAT) in Tanzania.

The World Resources Institute (http://www.wri.org/wri) is an environmental think tank that goes beyond research to provide practical ways to protect the earth and improve people's lives.
