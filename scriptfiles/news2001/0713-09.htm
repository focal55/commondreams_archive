Star Wars Opposition Grows
Group Calls for Senate Hearings
   WASHINGTON - July 13 - On Wednesday, the Bush Administration announced that it would pursue a multi-layered Star Wars missile defense system and they would withdraw from the Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty, in the words of Defense Sec. Donald Rumsfeld, "within months, not years."

Citing the Administration's announcement that it would push forward with deploying Star Wars regardless of whether it passes Saturday's test, Scott Lynch, communications director of Peace Action said, "When it comes to rewarding his friends in big business, Bush has proven that no one does it better. The truth is Star Wars is huge business, the contractors are huge political contributors, and Cheney, Hadley and Rumsfeld are working the revolving door with the Star Wars industry. Is it any wonder that Lockheed Martin, Boeing, TRW and Raytheon continue to get billions of our tax dollars."

Lynch continued, "Despite the fact that the various Star Wars systems have swallowed over $80 billion and have failed, spectacularly, for over twenty years, Bush now wants to throw even more money at it. Just this week, Bush asked for a 40 percent increase in funding (for a total of $8.3 billion) for this faulty, dangerous boondoggle that could cost taxpayers up to a trillion dollars. The line needs to be drawn on political patronage. The Bush Administration is prepared to risk a nuclear arms race so that their big campaign contributors can continue living off the government handouts."

Peace Action is calling on the Senate Majority Leadership to hold hearings to investigate the Administration's plan to replace the proven arms control treaties. These treaties have reduced the nuclear danger for over 40 years. The administration admits that Star Wars will be neither able to prevent nor able to stop a nuclear attack in the foreseeable future.

Lynch added, "It should be noted that at no point has Bush called for abolition of nukes -- i.e., he does not believe his own rhetoric about moving away from MAD. So let's say twenty-five years from now, we have a multi-layered system that is 95 percent effective against ICBMs in combat situations. The world that has proliferated nukes to the point where virtually every country with a couple of PHDs in physics has the bomb. Terrorists are sneaking nukes into our country the same way that drugs flow into our country. Are we safer? I don't think so."

Lynch concluded by saying "Ultimately, the problem with Star Wars is that it destroys the arms control framework that has been making progress on nuclear threat reduction for over 40 years and replaces it with nothing. The way forward is to continue multilateral efforts towards the reduction and abolition of nuclear weapons, control of fissile materials and control of missile technologies."

As part of Peace Action's Star Wars is a Lemon campaign, chapters around the country will be sending 300,000 Star Wars is a Lemon postcards to the Senate. These cards will ask the Senators to oppose funding for deployment of Star Wars. The Lemon campaign will also be using print and TV ads aimed at legislators.

Peace Action, formerly Sane/Freeze, organizes for the abolition of nuclear weapons, an end to the international arms trade and a redirection of U.S. tax dollars from the Pentagon budget to domestic investment. Peace Action is the nation's largest grassroots peace and disarmament organization with 85,000 members nationwide.
