Prominent Physicians Issue Call To President-Elect:
End National Missile Defense
Advertisement in New York Times Calls Careless Defense Project a “Prescription for Failure”
  WASHINGTON - January 17 - About 400 prominent physicians have added their signatures to an open letter to President-Elect Bush that will appear in the Western edition of the New York Times on Thursday, January 18. The letter calls on the President to stop the proposed National Missile Defense (NMD) program and take America’s nuclear weapons off hair trigger alert.

The letter is sponsored by Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR) and was organized by PSR chapters in Los Angeles, San Francisco, Seattle and Atlanta. The ad was funded by W. Alton Jones Foundation and the Ploughshares Fund and will be duplicated in other publications.

“National Missile Defense will be one of the first national security issues that President-Elect Bush will face,” said Robert K. Musil, Ph.D. PSR’s Executive Director and CEO. “Any decision to move forward with NMD will open new avenues for reckless military spending and dangerous international brinkmanship. America doesn’t need to relive the Cold war. We were lucky to survive it the first time through.”

The ad makes a clear case against continuing the skyrocketing expense of the National Missile Defense program, pointing out that public health initiatives in the US go wanting while the National Missile Defense program has a hefty $4.7 billion price tag for 2001 alone.

"Our members are deeply upset about the election," says Jonathan Parfrey, executive director of PSR’s Los Angeles chapter. "President-Elect Bush does not enjoy a mandate — and especially among our thousands of supporters — to waste our nation's wealth and genius on an unworkable missile defense system."

PSR is a national nonprofit organization representing 22,000 physicians and health professionals nationwide. PSR was founded in 1961 and shared the Nobel Peace Prize in 1985 with the International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War.
