The Wilderness Society Raises Concerns About Bush Administration's 'Drill America First' Energy Policy
Consumers Will Not See Promised Benefits of Drilling While Pristine Wild Lands Will be Irreparably Harmed
  WASHINGTON - May 16 - The Wilderness Society, America's leading organization dedicated to protecting our forests, rivers, deserts, and mountains, said the Bush Administration energy policy announced today would do little to make a difference for consumers at the gas pumps or in their homes, while causing irreparable damage to some of the country's most spectacular landscapes.

A video news feed including extensive b-roll of the threatened
national monument areas and other pristine wild land as well as
footage of nearby oil exploration is being released
Thursday, May 17 at 3 p.m. EDT. It contains:

-- Wilderness Society President William H. Meadows detailing flaws in the Bush energy policy and what it means for Americans.
-- Footage of the damage caused by oil and gas drilling.
-- Footage of three of America's newest national monuments-- Carrizo Plain, Grand Staircase-Escalante, and Upper Missouri Breaks.
-- Footage of the pristine Arctic National Wildlife Refuge, Red Desert, and Rocky Mountain Front.
-- Comments from Irv McMillen, a California resident who lives near the Carrizo Plain.
