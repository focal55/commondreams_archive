Statement of U.S. Senator Paul Wellstone (D-MN) on Bush Administration Retreat on Fair Trade Laws
  WASHINGTON - November 14 - "At today's World Trade Organization talks, the Bush Administration has now put on the table for negotiation our nation's anti-dumping laws as part of a new round of trade talks. This is outrageous, and I hope the President will reconsider and quickly reverse this decision.

"As more than 60 of my colleagues and I pointed out to President Bush last Spring, such laws are a critical element of U.S. trade policy. Many of our country's agricultural and industrial sectors have successfully employed these statutes to address unfair trade practices and problems. And, unfortunately, experience suggests that many other industries, including our steel industry, may need to rely on them in the future. These laws are critical in protecting American workers, industries and communities from unfair foreign trade practices, and providing ways to fight back against harmful import surges.

"Especially as the President is seeking fast track trade authority from Congress, it defies imagination that his Administration is signaling to our trading partners that these laws are now up for negotiation.

"I know many of my Congressional colleagues share my outrage, and will be joining me in urging, in the strongest possible terms, that the President reconsider this retreat on an issue of such deep concern to American workers and industries."
