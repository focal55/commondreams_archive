Nationwide Coalition To Announce Opposition To Bush Tax Cut Proposal
   WASHINGTON - February 28 - A massive and unprecedented coalition of coalitions -- with total membership of more than 500 national organizations -- will hold a news conference on Thursday, March 1 to announce plans to work for the defeat of the unfair and irresponsible Bush tax cut proposal.

The coalitions joining forces in this nationwide effort include the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights, the AFL-CIO, the Invest in America Coalition, the Coalition on Human Needs, and the National Low-Income Housing Coalition. They represent a wide diversity of organizations including civil rights, labor, women's, environmental, disability, religious, gay and lesbian, health care reform, campaign finance reform, good government, older Americans' and children's groups, and more.

The new nationwide coalition has collected scores of organizational endorsements just since its first organizational meeting last Friday. Even as more organizations sign on, work is already underway to develop a nationwide, three-prong campaign of organizing, lobbying, and public information to educate and mobilize the public against the high risks the Bush proposal poses for millions of Americans and for our economic health, as well.
