Oxfam America Announces New Educational Materials About Afghanistan For Teachers And Kids
   BOSTON - October 12 - In response to requests from teachers and youth workers, international humanitarian aid agency Oxfam America is developing a section on its Web site called All About Afghanistan for Kids and Teachers.

Elementary school teachers can use this to introduce students to the crisis in that country and the conditions under which kids and their families are living, as well as background material on the country and its people. It will also include a variety of related educational and fundraising activities appropriate for the classroom. These free materials will be available by the end of the day today and will be continuously updated.

In the meantime, Oxfam currently has materials posted on our web site that can be used as a resource for older audiences, including middle school, high school and university students. These materials include:

-- A fact sheet on the Humanitarian Crisis in Afghanistan
-- Background information on Afghanistan
-- Updates on the current crisis
-- Oxfam's response to the humanitarian crisis
-- A site map of Oxfam's projects in Afghanistan
-- Maps of the area
-- Frequently Asked Questions regarding the humanitarian crisis

Oxfam has supported programs in Afghanistan for 12 years, focusing on food relief, water and sanitation delivery and projects to promote democracy and women's rights. Oxfam is uniquely positioned to deliver aid effectively because our programs rely on Afghan staff, as well as Afghani organizations to deliver the food to communities in need. By not relying on foreign nationals, we enjoy the cooperation of Afghanis who are trusted leaders in their communities. This trust and commitment is crucial in a war-torn and fractured society. In addition, Oxfam professional staff trained in emergency water and sanitation relief systems is considered among the best in the world at meeting the immediate needs of swelling refugee camps.

Oxfam has a 27-year history of educating the public about issues of poverty and hunger through our Fast for a World Harvest campaign. For more information and to download or order any of the above-mentioned free materials, please visit the Web site at www.oxfamamerica.org. Teachers can also receive information at 1 800 77 OXFAM, ext. 559 or info@oxfamamerica.org.
