Rev. Jesse Jackson Joins the Coalition of Conscience in the Announcement of an Inauguration Day Fairness in Democracy Rally
   CHICAGO - January 10 - The Rainbow/PUSH Coalition along with the AFL-CIO, Black Leadership Forum, Congressional Black Caucus, National Association for the Advancement of Colored People, and concerned citizens will unite forces and march to the State Capitol building in Florida on January 20th, 12 Noon.

This consortium of freedom fighters includes labor, civil rights, religious, women, human rights, and educational groups; all whom have pledged to continue to empower the movement for social change.

"As the new administration is sworn in, we will turn our attention to the chicanery that occurred on November 7th and the crowning of a new president that followed. We, as people of faith, will remain rooted in our struggle for equal protection under the law and work to secure our franchise," remarks Jackson.

The rally will embrace a renewed voter registration and voter education campaign. In addition to serving as a peaceful and non-violent celebration of the tremendous African American voter turnout and will call upon the nation to honor voting rights as guaranteed in the Constitution.

Jackson will follow a host of other scheduled speakers such as: Joseph Lowery, Black Leadership Forum, John Sweeney, AFL-CIO, Dennis Rivera, Rainbow/PUSH and Kweisi Mfume, NAACP.

The march will commence at the Tallahassee Civic Center at 11:00 AM, and travel to the State Capital Building for a 12 Noon rally. (Buses should assemble at 9:00 AM at the Civic Center).
