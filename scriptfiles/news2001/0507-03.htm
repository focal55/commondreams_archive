ACORN To Lead Actions Against Household, Announce Drive For Divestment By Cities
   WASHINGTON - May 7 - Household International, parent company of two of the country's biggest predatory lenders, Household Finance and Beneficial, is holding its annual shareholders' meeting on May 8 in the Tampa, Fla., area. As part of ACORN's ongoing campaign to urge Household to change its predatory lending practices, more than 50 ACORN members and victims of Household plan to protest outside the gathering. ACORN will present Household with its "Shark of the Year" award.

Present will be these victims of Household: Margaret Dickens (from St. Louis), Amy Swanson (St. Paul), and Rudy Tachias (Albuquerque). Massachusetts ACORN leader Angie Wilkerson will be speaking inside the shareholders' meeting.

Events are also scheduled for the 8th and 9th in: Albuquerque, Boston, St. Louis, Hempstead (NY), Minneapolis and St. Paul (MN), Little Rock, Hartford, Denver, San Diego, and Los Angeles. ACORN members and allied elected officials will announce a drive to urge cities to divest their retirement and other funds from Household until and unless it puts a stop to its abusive lending.
