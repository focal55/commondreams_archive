Women's Rights Leader Watching Bush Tax Plan for Impact on Women and Families
NWLC Co-President Campbell Available for Comment
   WASHINGTON - February 5 - The National Women's Law Center is analyzing President Bush's tax plan this week to determine how it affects women and their families in particular. Initial analysis shows that both the size and the distribution of the proposed tax cuts present concerns for women.

"The size of this proposed tax cut poses a serious threat to Social Security, Medicare and other safety net programs, including the need to make increased investments in areas critical to women and their families such as education, child care and health care. Women have a greater stake in these federal programs because they tend to earn less during their lifetimes and are more likely to bear the burden of uncompensated family care-taking responsibilities," said NWLC Co-President Nancy Duff Campbell.

Campbell added that NWLC will also be watching closely to see how the distribution of the plan affects different kinds of women, such as single heads of households, women in the paid labor force, and older women.

"We are concerned that the tax rate cuts announced today are not sufficiently tilted toward middle- and low-income families. For example, the rate cut changes provide no benefit to the lowest-income families, so many of whom are headed by women. If there is to be a tax cut, it should help these families most in need, such as by making the child tax credit refundable or increasing the earned income tax credit," said Campbell.

Campbell has almost 30 years' experience in tax law and policy, especially at it pertains to women and their families. She serves on the board of the Community Tax Law Report and chaired the national Coalition on Women and Taxes during the 1986 tax reform debates.

The National Women's Law Center is a non-partisan, non-profit organization that has been working since 1972 to advance and protect women's legal rights. NWLC focuses on major policy areas of importance to women and their families including economic security, education, employment and health, with special attention given to the concerns of low-income women.
