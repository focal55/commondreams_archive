Sierra Club Celebrates Election of Rep. Pelosi as New House Minority Whip
   SAN FRANCISCO - October 10 - The Sierra Club today praised Democrats in the House of Representatives for electing environmental champion Rep. Nancy Pelosi (D-CA) as the House Minority Whip. Rep. Pelosi will succeed Rep. David Bonior (D-MI), who will step down in January, 2002.

Executive Director Carl Pope issued the following statement:

"Rep. Pelosi's election is among the best news for America's environment this century. Rep. Pelosi is a treasured friend of the environment and we look forward to continuing to work with her to protect our most fragile places and defend human rights around the world. We are thrilled that House Democrats selected her as their second in command and we want to extend our warmest congratulations to her. Rep. Pelosi is a worthy successor of Rep. David Bonior, who has always made protecting the environment a priority.

"From the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge in Alaska to the redrock canyons of Utah to all of our national forests, she has been a leader in protecting our pristine places. When Americans gaze at vistas explored by Lewis and Clark, they can thank Rep. Pelosi for helping to keep the landscape unspoiled. As a vocal defender of human rights of environmentalists around the world, she has worked alongside the Sierra Club on behalf of Ken Saro-Wiwa and Alexander Nikitin. To slow the threats our growing population has on our environment, Rep. Pelosi has been a key supporter of family planning initiatives both at home and abroad."
