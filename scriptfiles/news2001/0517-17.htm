Bush/Cheney Focus on Dirty Power Condemned
Statement by John Passacantando, Executive Director of Greenpeace On Bush/Cheney Energy Policy
   WASHINGTON - May 17 - President Bush and Vice President Cheney have just issued an energy plan that, with few exceptions for a thin green veneer, looks like nothing more than a speedy payback to the oil, gas, utilities and mining interests.

Mr. Bush and Mr. Cheney, both hailing from the oil industry, have failed to produce a plan that would protect the environment and American consumers. Bush is blind to the scores of corporations that have recognized the need for US leadership to reduce emissions of global warming pollution, the White House has chosen to serve as a lobby arm of the new OPEC, the cartel of US energy companies.

This plan will worsen global warming, will increase pollution in our cities with its corresponding lung cancer and asthma and increase our reliance on nuclear power -- the most expensive, most dangerous source of energy in the world.

Reliance on these 19th century technologies to solve 21st energy problems is a huge step backward. He’s building on current American fears of short term energy costs to make the case for polluting, outdated technologies.

The last minute mentions of conservation and renewable energy research that have been cynically tied to revenues that must come from the destruction of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge is appalling.

Given that the sheer folly of this plan is readily apparent, the American public will reject it, revealing the true crisis, a crisis of leadership.
