Attorney General Ashcroft will Make Good on NRA Promises, Physicians Warn
42 Senators Oppose Confirmation of Pro-Gun, Anti-Environment Former Colleague
   WASHINGTON - February 1 - Despite opposition from a broad spectrum of American public interest groups, former Senator John Ashcroft was confirmed today as Attorney General of the United States. Among many of his most contentious beliefs is Ashcroft’s devotion to the National Rifle Associate and other pro-gun groups.

“The NRA’s promise of a place high in this administration has been fulfilled,” said Robert K. Musil, Ph. D., Executive Director and CEO of Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR). “John Ashcroft’s relentlessly pro-gun stance as a US Senator, Missouri Governor and Attorney General will bring a dangerous precedent to the Department of Justice.”

Ashcroft’s time as a Senator has seen him vote against all thirteen gun measures considered during his tenure. He also recorded radio spots urging Missouri voters to support a dangerous concealed weapons law, and received a 0% score on his environmental voting record as well.

“Ashcroft’s commitment to deterring environmental protection and thwarting meaningful violence prevention measures is absolute,” said Musil. “We opposed his nomination because we believed he would not be able to enforce laws in which he doesn’t believe. We are committed to closely monitoring his tenure, as the progress we’ve made despite his unflagging opposition in the Senate is too valuable to see go untended.”

Physicians for Social Responsibility is a national non-profit organization committed to protecting environmental health and ending violence and its causes. We represent over 20,000 physicians and health professionals. PSR shared the 1985 Nobel Prize for Peace as the US affiliate of International Physicians for the Prevention of Nuclear War.
