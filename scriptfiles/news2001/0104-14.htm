Consumers Union Blasts California PUC Electricity Rate Hike; State Urged to Enact Reforms to Protect Consumers From Price Gouging
   SAN FRANCISCO - January 4 - California's Public Utilities Commission unanimously approved a plan today that will allow Pacific Gas and Electric (PG&E) and Southern California Edison to raise their electricity rates by 7 percent to 15 percent. The rate hike is an "interim" increase that will be in effect for the next 90 days. The proposal was roundly criticized by community advocates because consumers served by these utilities were supposed to be protected from such rate increases under the state's 1996 electricity deregulation law. Consumer advocates also fear that this will be the first of many rate increases the utilities will seek.

Under the plan approved today, residential consumers will face a nine percent hike, small businesses will pay seven percent more, medium size businesses' rates will go up by twelve percent, and large industrial customer's rates will climb by 15 percent.

"Four years ago, consumers were promised a ten percent rate reduction under the state's deregulation law," said Harry Snyder, Senior Advocate for Consumers Union's West Coast Regional Office in San Francisco. "Today they are being socked with a nine percent rate hike and could face even higher increases in the future. Consumers shouldn't have to foot the bill for a flawed deregulation scheme designed by the utility lobbyists now that it's gone bust."

"Under the 1996 deregulation law, consumers were forced to bail out the utilities to the tune of $20 billion," said Snyder. "There shouldn't be another bailout of PG&E and Edison because the utilities gambled with the market and lost. Bailing out and subsidizing these companies sends the wrong signal to the marketplace that the state will pay for problems the companies may create in the future. Losses should be borne by the utilities' shareholders and creditors not customers."

Consumers Union has called on Governor Gray Davis and state lawmakers to take necessary steps to guarantee an adequate power supply to meet residential needs sufficient to prevent price gouging. Specifically, the group has recommended that the state should:

  • Require that all power generated in the state (by PG&E, SDG&E and So. Cal Edison) is dedicated to meet the needs of homeowners, renters and small businesses at prices based on the actual cost to produce the power allowing a reasonable rate of return, subject to control by the PUC. This will stabilize prices for California ratepayers at lower prices than they now face and protect consumers from price gouging by out-of-state suppliers.
  • Take over the California Independent Systems Operator and the California Power Exchange to obtain state ownership and management of our transmission capacity
  • Provide Californians who are on fixed incomes, retirees and low-income consumers with additional financial assistance to help with increased electric/utility bills and to exchange old outdated inefficient appliances with modern appliances that will conserve energy, keep bills down and free up more electricity to meet the whole state's needs
  • Invest in energy statewide demand reduction and efficiency programs to stabilize prices and maintain an emergency reserve of electricity to prevent blackouts.
  • Encourage cleaner burning new power plants and renewable energy development to meet the growing demand for electricity by providing state matching grants, financial support, and / or loan guarantees to public and private power developers willing to enter into contracts to provide affordable electricity to residential customers.
  • Require utilities to implement a five-year plan to restore the state's energy self-sufficiency and toughen law enforcement to police price gouging by increasing funding for the Attorney General's anti-trust and investigative units.

"Now that it's clear that deregulation has been an unmitigated disaster for California, Governor Davis and the state's lawmakers must work together to stem this crisis," consumers," said Snyder. "We need bold leadership from Sacramento to protect consumers from greedy price gouging by energy suppliers and to chart a long-term plan to restore the state's energy self-sufficiency."
