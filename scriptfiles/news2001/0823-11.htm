Pacifica Campaign Organizes Groundbreaking Bilingual Gathering to Reclaim Community Access to KPFK-Pacifica Radio
Activist and Journalist Juan Gonzalez to Address Meeting
La Participación de la Comunidad en la Radio: KPFK-Pacifica
  LOS ANGELES - August 23 - Progressive activists from Spanish speaking communities from across Southern California will gather to make plans to reclaim KPFK 90.7 Pacifica Radio. KPFK management has dismantled most of the Spanish language programming and almost all public affairs programmers of color have been fired or purged in the last five years. Critics say KPFK management has been unwilling to tap into the resources and needs of the more than three million Latinos in their listening area.

KPFK was founded over 40 years ago with the mission of serving the information and cultural needs of the diverse communities within its signal area; progressive activists built it, sustained it and helped it grow.

WHO: Juan Gonzalez, activist, former Pacifica Radio co-host of Democracy Now!, columnist NY Daily News, author Harvest of Empire: A History of Latinos in America and founder of the Pacifica Campaign, will be the key note speaker. Music by: Son Real.

WHAT: Bilingual conference La Participación de la Comunidad en la Radio: KPFK-Pacifica-Community Access to Radio:KPFK-Pacifica. How KPFK can better serve the different Spanish speaking communities of Southern California and how can these communities contribute to the progressive growth and rebuilding of Pacifica?

WHEN: Saturday, August 25, 2001 10 AM to 4 PM

WHERE: Student Lounge, Loyola Law School 919 S. Albany St. at Olympic Blvd.

COST: $5.00 donation at the door (no one will be turned away for lack of funds).

For information call: 323 878-5680

"Community radio is one of the best ways through which we can reach a mass audience" said Arturo Lemus, a conference organizer. "Our diverse communities in Los Angeles and Southern California have a right to local community radio stations that serve their information and cultural needs. We must reclaim and transform KPFK into a truly democratic and progressive community radio station."

In a Washington Post column yesterday "In Image Battle, Pacifica Takes Aggressive Action," Pacifica, CEO Bessie Wash was quoted as expressing concern about the lack of programming for Los Angeles' Latino communities on Pacifica station KPFK. What Wash didn't say is that the Pacifica regime that installed Wash in her current and previous positions is responsible for that lack of coverage.

In the past five years, KPFK/Pacifica management has:

  • Forbidden news personnel to use Spanish pronunciations of Spanish words
  • Removed the only Latino drive time talk show host in Los Angeles and replaced him with an Anglo
  • Shortened, then cancelled, "Radio Chicana" a bi-lingual program on Latino issues, culture and history
  • Caused the resignation of young Latino programmers by criticizing the use of expressions like "peace and love" because it would "alienate"older listeners
  • Severely reduced all Latino-oriented programming, and Latino-produced information programming in English or Spanish.

Earlier this year, Pacifica Radio co-host Juan Gonzalez dramatically resigned on-air in protest at the anti-democratic policies of the Pacifica national board. "The Pacifica board has been hijacked by a small clique that has more in common with corporate vultures than with working-class Americans," he explained, vowing to lead a nationwide campaign to restore democratic accountability at the network.

The Pacifica Campaign is a grass-roots organization representing listeners and staff alike fighting to preserve Pacifica's 50-year tradition of progressive, community-based radio and is co sponsoring this event with Aztlan Media Collective and Comite Pro-Democracia en Mexico.
