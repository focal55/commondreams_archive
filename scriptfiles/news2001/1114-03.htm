WWF Praises WTO Decision on Fishing Subsidies
   WASHINGTON - November 14 - Reacting to the decision taken today at the fourth ministerial meeting of the World Trade Organization in Doha, Qatar, to open new negotiations on fishing subsidies, Kathryn Fuller, president of World Wildlife Fund-U.S., issued the following statement:

"The decision to launch WTO negotiations aimed at eliminating subsidies that encourage overfishing is an important advance in the fight to promote the health of our planet's oceans. It is a move that promises real benefits not only for our marine ecosystems, but also for people around the world who depend on fish for their livelihoods and their food.

"The majority of the world's most valuable fisheries today are either overfished or fished to the limit. Misdirected government subsidies are among the factors responsible for this crisis. WWF applauds the decision taken by nearly 150 governments to craft new WTO rules to reduce and reform harmful fishing subsidies.

"Today's success is owed in large measure to a handful of governments committed to addressing the problem of harmful fishing subsidies. The leadership of Iceland and the United States deserves special mention at the head of an energetic coalition that also included Australia, Chile, New Zealand, Peru, and the Philippines. The role of the United States was critical, for it alone among the world's major economic powers treated fishing subsidies as a top priority.

"The opening of negotiations on fishing subsidies also marks a significant step towards a better balanced and environmentally sensitive WTO. WTO rules and practices today are still too often biased against the needs of the conservation and sustainable development, and WWF will continue to work towards their substantial reform. But for the first time ever, an element of a major international trade negotiation will now be directed towards helping conserve a vital natural resource.

"While there is much work ahead -- both to achieve sustainable fisheries and to reform the WTO -- WWF views the opening of WTO negotiations on fishing subsidies as an important step in the right direction."
