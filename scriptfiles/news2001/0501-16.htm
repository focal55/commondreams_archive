Activists Protest During Bush’s Speech at National Defense University
Greenpeace Calls on President Bush to Stop National Missile Defense
  WASHINGTON - May 1 - Carrying an inflatable missile, a large banner reading "Stop Star Wars" and sounding an air siren, several activists from Greenpeace and other environmental groups protested outside of National Defense University today, where President Bush was giving a speech promoting a missile defense shield. Although Bush talked of reducing nuclear weapons, he then laid the groundwork for breaking the Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty in order to build a national missile defense system. Calling Bush "The Treaty Breaking Toxic Texan", Greenpeace says that today’s protest is part of its intensive campaign to safeguard the planet against Bush’s attempt to re-ignite a nuclear arms race.

According to a senior administration official, the President spent much of yesterday calling leaders of several major European allies to discuss today’s speech. After world wide criticism over his abandonment of the global warming treaty, Bush spent the day trying to clean up his "treaty breaking" image. According to a senior administration official, the President called leaders of several major European and Asian allies to discuss today’s speech.

In a statement released yesterday, Greenpeace called Bush’s plan to reduce nuclear weapons, "a publicity driven sleight of hand designed to rip up the Anti-Ballistic Missile (ABM) Treaty while pushing the National Missile Defense down the collective throats of the world." The international environmental group further stated, "Although Bush would like the American public to believe that his defense plan is designed to protect the country from so-called rogue countries such as Iran and Iraq, the United States is the true rogue country in trying to re-ignite a nuclear arms race."

While Bush has announced proposed reductions in the number of nuclear weapons, mainly from stock that is outdated and useless, he is proposing an increase in the budget of $5.3 billion for the design, development and production of nuclear weapons. In addition, Bush has proposed a 20% cut for the Department of Energy’s Office of Defense and Nuclear Non-Proliferation, which works to prevent the spread of materials, technology and expertise relating to weapons of mass destruction.

Immediately following Bush’s speech, Greenpeace Executive Director John Passacantando, Greenpeace Nuclear Disarmament Coordinator Gordon Clark, M.I.T. professor, Ted Postol and U.S. Representative Dennis J. Kucinich, (D-OH), held a telephone press conference denouncing President Bush’s missile defense system and its danger to world security and peace.
