Health Care For Women Again Under Attack By Bush Administration As Medicaid Waiver For Family Planning Is Denied
  WASHINGTON - July 13 - Following is a statement by Gloria Feldt, president, Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

"Family planning services, including contraception, are an essential part of basic healthcare for women. Many countries make these services universally available to all who need them. In the United States, individuals with low incomes must often rely on a variety of fragmented programs to help offset the cost."

"These programs have particular importance to low-income women who have just given birth and may not be ready to be pregnant again for some time. Pregnant women who qualify for Medicaid can receive 60 days of family planning services following their delivery. But 60 days just isn't long enough. That's why many states have long sought and received federal waivers that have allowed them to extend this benefit. Waivers have also been granted to allow states to provide family planning benefits to women who did not previously qualify for Medicaid. But that may be changing."

"State health officials in Georgia recently had a request to extend these benefits for up to two years denied, despite the fact that many other states have been granted similar requests. According to The Atlanta Journal-Constitution, a top Medicaid official stated that the Bush administration "would not approve any family planning waivers."

"All pregnancies have a significant impact on a woman's health and well-being, and the consequences of unintended pregnancies can be particularly dire. This is especially true for women who lack financial resources. And yet, despite campaign promises to care for all Americans, President Bush continues to slap women in the face. Furthermore, it is the height of hypocrisy for a president who opposes a woman's right to choose, to also limit women's access to the very thing that prevents unintended pregnancies and makes abortions less necessary. It's time for the attacks on women's health to stop."
