People of Faith Don’t Believe Campaign Finance Reform Would Violate Free Speech
New Poll Explores Connection Between Religion and Political Participation
   WASHINGTON - September 4 - A new Gallup poll, sponsored by The Interfaith AllianceFoundation, finds that people who identified themselves as conservatives with deeply religious values overwhelmingly reject the notion that restricting donations to political campaigns would be a violation of the Constitutional right to freedom of speech. This view is contrary to the position of groups such as the Christian Coalition, which strongly opposes campaign finance reform on those grounds. More than half of the people of faith surveyed reported that campaign finance reform was very important to them.

Results of this survey suggest that people of faith have complex views on political participation and campaign finance reform that are not accurately represented by groups such as the Christian Coalition.

The results of this nationwide poll of people of faith and clergy, as well as feedback from ten focus groups, will be released at a press conference at the National Press Club on September 6 at 9:00 a.m. After a briefing on the poll data by The Gallup Organization, national experts on campaign finance reform and religion will discuss the findings and their implications on the Congressional debate. Questions in the survey included:

--Do you think changes to the campaign finance laws would encourage members of Congress to be more or less ethical and honest?

--Do you think changes in the campaign finance laws would make the political election system work better than it does now, or work worse than it does now?

--Have you ever contributed any money to a political party or to some other political interest group?
