Bush Administration to Undermine Forest Rule
  WASHINGTON - May 4 - Statement of Tiernan Sittenfeld, U.S. PIRG Conservation Advocate

"By announcing that it will modify the forests rule, the Bush Administration is caving to the special interests yet again rather than protecting our national forests. The Administration has said it plans to allow a rule to protect our national forest roadless areas to go forward, but that it will give local officials power to modify the plan on a case by case basis.

Today’s announcement is expected to publicly give lip service to protecting our national forests while quietly reopening them to chainsaws and bulldozers. Local control by the timber industries for the last hundred years has resulted in more than 380,000 miles of roads in our national forests, a roads maintenance backlog of $8.6 billion, and millions of acres of devastated national forests.

The rulemaking process took more than two years to complete, during which time local people spoke at 600 public meetings held on each national forest across the country. A record-breaking 1.6 million people submitted comments on the plan, more than 95% of which were strongly in support of a strong protection plan. In addition, a new national poll shows that 67% of voters favor the forest protection policy.

This policy is already balanced. The new roadless rule is a balanced plan that protects 31% of our national forests while leaving more than half of them open to logging, mining, and drilling. It allows access for recreational vehicles and allows existing oil and gas leases to continue. The rule already allows for many decisions to be made at the local level, including maintaining existing roads, building roads to fight catastrophic wildfires, and cutting dense underbrush and small fire-prone trees to reduce the risk of fire.

Members of Congress, religious organizations, scientists, economists, environmental groups, and the American people have all urged President Bush to protect our national forests by implementing the roadless rule. We urge the President to reverse his present course and fully and immediately implement this historic conservation rule as it is written. The American public will remember his decision for generations to come."

U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for the State Public Interest Research Groups. State PIRGs are non-profit, non-partisan public interest advocacy groups.
