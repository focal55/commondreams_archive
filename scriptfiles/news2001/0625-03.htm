NWLC Co-President Marcia D. Greenberger To Testify About Senate's Role, Standards For Selecting Judges
   WASHINGTON - June 25 - News Advisory:

Marcia D. Greenberger, NWLC Co-President, will present testimony about the role of judicial philosophy in the judicial confirmation process before the Senate Judiciary Committee's Administrative Oversight and the Courts Subcommittee on June 26 at 10 a.m. Greenberger will testify on a panel that includes Harvard law professor Laurence Tribe and University of Chicago law professor Cass R. Sunstein.

Greenberger's testimony is expected to focus on the history and Constitutional basis for the Senate's active and independent role in giving its "advice and consent" to the nomination of federal judges, a role that is co-equal with the President's; the standards that Senators should apply in considering judicial nominees; and the rights and liberties that are at stake.

"Dating back to George Washington's time, a judicial nominee's judicial philosophy has been an important area of inquiry at Senate confirmation hearings," Greenberger will tell the Committee. "Federal judges are appointed with lifetime tenure and the scrutiny they receive during the confirmation process is the only real form of accountability that our system provides for them."

For women -- and in fact, all Americans -- the federal courts over the last 30 years have allowed important advances in employment, education, health, safety and welfare through their interpretations of the equal protection and privacy guarantees of the Constitution and federal statutes. Yet, many of these guarantees now hang in the balance on a closely divided Supreme Court. Greenberger will urge Senators to satisfy themselves that each judicial nominee not only meets high standards of character, experience, legal excellence and temperament, but also that he or she demonstrates a commitment to the core principles that protect and advance women's legal rights and other key legal protections for American citizens.

The National Women's Law Center is a non-partisan, non-profit organization that has been working since 1972 to advance and protect women's legal rights. NWLC focuses on major policy areas of importance to women and their families including economic security, education, employment and health, with special attention given to the concerns of low-income women.
