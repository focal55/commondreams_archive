Non-partisan Groups Announce Coordinated Opposition to Individual Accounts Carved out of Social Security
   WASHINGTON - February 7 - The National Council of Women's Organizations (NCWO), in partnership with seven of its national member organizations, will convene a Summit on Women and Social Security on Feb. 8 to launch a coordinated opposition to Social Security privatization. More than three hundred leaders of grassroots organizations representing all fifty states will participate in the two days of meetings, training workshops, and Congressional office visits. The attendees will deliver a unified message to Capitol Hill: Individual Accounts carved-out of Social Security will hurt women and families. The conference has been called because national women's groups are concerned that privatization would necessarily jeopardize the guarantee of lifetime, inflation-adjusted benefits that Social Security now provides.

Speakers will include:

-- Rep. Robert Matsui (D-CA), Ranking Member, Subcommittee on Social Security of the Ways & Means Committee, U.S. House of Representatives
-- Beth Kobliner, Financial Journalist and Commentator
-- Heidi Hartmann, Ph.D. Chair, Women & Social Security Task Force, and President, Institute for Women's Policy Research
-- Patricia Ireland, President, National Organization for Women
-- Dean Baker, Ph.D., Co-Director, Center for Economic and Policy Research
-- Marilyn Moon, Ph.D., Senior Fellow, Urban Institute
-- Martha Burk, Ph.D., Chair of the National Council of Women's Organizations, and President of the Center for Advancement of Public Policy
-- Joan Entmacher, Vice President, National Women's Law Center
-- Kilolo Kijakazi, Senior Policy Analyst, Center on Budget and Policy Priorities
-- Deborah Briceland-Betts, Executive Director, OWL
-- Cindy Hounsell, Executive Director, Women's Institute for a Secure Retirement

"Most workers will face cuts in guaranteed benefits that are not made up by income from an individual account," according to Heidi Hartmann, Ph.D., President of the Institute for Women's Policy Research and Chair of the NCWO Women and Social Security Project. "If privatizers keep their promises to current retirees-younger workers will get less when they retire."

"The majority of the more than 100 organizations in NCWO are extremely concerned about Social Security privatization," added Martha Burk, Ph.D., Chair of NCWO. "The Women & Social Security Summit will mark an important milestone in the effort to preserve the guaranteed benefits of Social Security that millions of Americans-women in particular-depend on."

The event will be cosponsored by Business and Professional Women/USA, the American Association of University Women, OWL, the voice of midlife and older women, the National Council of Negro Women, the Women's Institute for a Secure Retirement, the National Organization for Women, and the Feminist Majority. The Summit Advisory Committee includes the Institute for Women's Policy Research, the Center for Advancement of Public Policy, the National Women's Law Center, the National Committee on Pay Equity, and the YWCA of the USA. The goal is to engage citizen activists in the debate and to build support for addressing gender inequities in the current system.

The National Council of Women's Organization's (NCWO) is a non-partisan, non-profit coalition of more than 115 women's organizations across the nation collectively representing over six million women. For years, NCWO has convened the leadership of major women's organizations dedicated to focusing on national issues and public policy agendas affecting women. In 1998, NCWO formed the Women & Social Security Project and launched a national education campaign to highlight Social Security's importance to women.
