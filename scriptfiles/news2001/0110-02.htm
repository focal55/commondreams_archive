George Bush Declares War on the Environment, Nominates Extreme Anti-Environmentalist as Interior Secretary
  WASHINGTON - January 10 - News Advisory:

WHERE:
National Press Club, Holeman Lounge, Washington, D.C.

The nomination of Gale Norton to be Secretary of Interior signals that President-elect Bush is not interested in a balanced approach to environment and natural resources issues.

Norton spent four years as a staffer at the Mountain States Legal Foundation, whose alumni include Reagan's infamous Secretary of Interior James Watt and Ann Gorsuch, Reagan's EPA Administrator.

The agenda of the Mountain States Legal Foundation has included aggressive litigation against environmental protections, a "takings" agenda to pay polluters to obey the law, an effort to dismantle the Endangered Species Act, and a campaign to deny the seriousness of air pollution and the existence of global warming.

The foundation was a sponsor of the first "wise use" conference in 1988. The "wise use" movement has laid out an extensive anti-environmental agenda, including opening all wilderness and national parks to mineral and energy production under wise use technologies.

"The nomination of Gale Norton amounts to a declaration of war on the environment," said Dr. Brent Blackwelder, President of Friends of the Earth.

The Mountain States Legal Foundation received donations from over 175 corporations in 1977, its first year of operation. Contributors to the organization have included Exxon, Amoco, Phillips 66, Marathon Oil, Ford Motor Company, and Chevron. "It's no wonder that Norton supports drilling in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge," Blackwelder said.

The Secretary of Interior is responsible for management of the majority of the federally owned lands in the United States, having jurisdiction over about 70 percent of the 740 million acres of these lands (roughly one third of the total land area of our nation). "At a time when we need stewardship and restoration of our public lands after a century of abuse, Norton is looking toward a new century of continued plunder and exploitation," Blackwelder said.

Norton has been associated with a number of extreme "property rights" groups. She was a member of the legal Advisory Council for Defenders of Property Rights and of the Farm Credit Bank Property Rights Task Force.

Norton has been national chairman of the Coalition for Republican Environmental Advocates whose steering committee includes registered lobbyists for the oil, auto, mining, and alcoholic beverage industries. Honorary board members of this Coalition include some members of Congress with the worst environmental records such as Pombo (R-Cal), Don Young (R-Alaska) and Sen. Larry Craig (R-Id). While this Coalition is trying to pass itself off as a pro-environment grassroots group, it has been funded by Coors Brewing Company, the American Forest Paper Association, the Chemical Manufacturers Association, ARCO, Amoco, and the Ford Company. "Norton's chairmanship of this Coalition suggests readiness to participate in a gross deception of the public," Blackwelder said.

Norton's pattern of operation is clear: she associates with the hard-core anti-environmental organizations. She is willing to use all sorts of code words to sound reasonable and even pro-environment, but behind the scenes, she is against regulations to protect the environment and is eager to move forward with an extensive program of natural resource exploitation.
