NWLC Calls Bush Tax Plan a Threat to Needs of Women and Families, Offers Alternatives for 'Real Relief'
   WASHINGTON - February 8 - Women and families may be harmed by President Bush's tax cut if it passes in Congress, according to the National Women's Law Center. The size of the tax cut plan poses a serious threat to Social Security and Medicare and other safety net programs and important services that women and their families depend on. And, in contrast to the administration's rhetoric, the distribution of the plan is not targeted to help middle- and low-income families, but is unfairly tilted in favor of high-income tax filers.

"The President's plan uses up a sizable portion of the revenues needed for programs in areas that are critical to women and their families, such as Social Security, Medicare, education, child care and health care. If the projected surpluses do not materialize, it could mean cuts in these programs as well," said Nancy Duff Campbell, NWLC co-president. Women have a greater stake in these federal programs because they tend to earn less during their lifetimes, live longer, and are more likely to bear the burden of uncompensated family care-taking responsibilities.

Campbell explained that the plan is unfairly tilted to provide the greatest benefit to the highest-income filers. For example, the plan to eliminate the estate tax would benefit primarily the top five percent of tax filers and not the overwhelming majority of women and their families who are not taxed on any inheritance. At the same time, the plan provides nothing to the lowest-income families, who are most in need of help in meeting their sizeable federal payroll tax burden.

Real tax relief should be modest in size, should be contingent on surpluses materializing, and should focus most of its benefits on helping middle- and low-income families. Specifically, women and their families need:

-- Modest rate reductions that target the greatest tax savings to middle- and low-income families.

-- An increased and refundable child tax credit that helps families with children, especially the lowest-income families, meet the substantial expenses of raising a child.

-- An expanded and refundable child and dependent care tax credit that helps middle- and low-income working families meet one of the largest expenses in their family budgets - child care.

-- Marriage penalty relief that is targeted to the two-income couples who currently pay the penalty.

-- Modifications to the earned income tax credit that help the lowest-income working families whose basic living expenses consume a disproportionate share of their budgets and whose marriage penalties are among the highest.

Campbell has over 30 years' experience in tax law and policy, especially as it pertains to women and their families. She serves on the board of the Community Tax Law Report and chaired the national Coalition on Women and Taxes during the 1986 tax reform debates.

The National Women's Law Center is a non-partisan, non-profit organization that has been working since 1972 to advance and protect women's legal rights. NWLC focuses on major policy areas of importance to women and their families including economic security, education, employment and health, with special attention given to the concerns of low-income women.
