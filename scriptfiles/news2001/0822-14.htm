Study Details Political Giving from Cuban-Americans
   WASHINGTON - August 22 - With the debate over the future direction of the Cuba lobby in America receiving national attention, a report by the non-partisan Center for Responsive Politics details, for the first time, more than two decades of political giving from the Cuban-American community.

"The Cuban Connection" details Cuban-American money in U.S. elections from 1979 to 2000. It is available on the Center's Web site at http://www.opensecrets.org/pubs/cubareport/index.asp.

The report finds that although campaign contributions from Cuban Americans increased ten-fold between the 1979-80 and 1995-96 election cycles, donations to federal candidates and party committees over the past two elections have shown signs of decline. Overall, according to the report, Cuban Americans have made at least $8.8 million in individual, PAC, and soft money contributions since 1979.

The report examines political fund-raising and contributions by the Cuban American National Foundation (CANF) and its political action committee, the Free Cuba PAC. Since the 1981-82 election cycle, the Free Cuba PAC has taken in $1.7 million in contributions and doled out more than $1.3 million to candidates and party committees, 56 percent of which has gone to the Democrats.

Other areas covered by the report include

*major donors, and their families, within the Cuban-American community;
*elected officials who have received the most money from Cuban Americans;
*Cuba-related legislation and vote correlations in Congress;
*political giving by Cuban Americans as compared to that of other ethnic groups.
