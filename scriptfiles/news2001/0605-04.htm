President George W. Bush to be Sued by Center for Reproductive Law and Policy: Global Gag Rule Censors Free Speech of Americans
   NEW YORK - June 5 - News Advisory:

Tomorrow, Wednesday June 6 at 11 a.m., the Center for Reproductive Law and Policy (CRLP) is holding a press conference to announce that it has filed a lawsuit against President Bush and the Global Gag Rule.

When:
Wednesday, June 6 at 11 a.m.

Where:
The New York City Bar Association
Stimson Room
42 West 44th Street (between 5 & 6 Avenues)
New York City

CRLP will sue President Bush for censoring the speech of Americans under the Global Gag Rule. "President Bush took away my right to speak because I support a position with which he disagrees: that access to safe and legal abortion is a human right of women worldwide. Yet groups that oppose abortion rights are not censored. The Global Gag Rule violates fairness, freedom, and democracy," says Janet Benshoof, president of CRLP.

Speakers will include human rights advocates and experts:

-- Janet Benshoof, president and founder of CRLP

-- Dr. Nafis Sadik, former executive director of the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA), and member of CRLP's Board of Directors

-- Aryeh Neier, president of the Open Society Institute, human rights expert and scholar

-- Melissa Upreti, CRLP staff attorney for South Asia

A continental breakfast will be available.
