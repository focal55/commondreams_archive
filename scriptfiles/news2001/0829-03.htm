Administration's Fiscal Missteps Have Real Consequences For Today's Seniors Says NCPSSM
  

WASHINGTON - August 29 - "This week's announcement that the federal government will have to dip into the Social Security trust fund is a tragedy for seniors who desperately need prescription drug coverage," according to Max Richtman, executive vice president of the National Committee to Preserve Social Security and Medicare. "The loss of tax revenue due to the President's tax cuts and the slowing economy will lead to new federal debt and $600 billion in additional interest payments over the next 10 years -- enough to pay for a generous prescription drug benefit under Medicare," he added.

"Now, it looks like the federal government will have to pay bondholders instead of providing seniors with the help they need on prescription drugs. That is a case of misplaced priorities," he said.

The administration also announced this week that it would ignore the $300 billion set aside by Republicans and Democrats in Congress for prescription drug coverage and limit itself to a benefit that costs no more than $190 billion over 10 years. "Seniors would be smart to use their tax refund to pay for prescription drugs -- it may be the only assistance they get to meet their prescription drug needs for some time," he said.

"The $600 billion could fund a prescription drug program with copayments and deductibles at a level that is more affordable for all seniors," Richtman said. "Alternatively, some of those funds could be used to fund a more modest drug benefit, while at the same time, leaving money to address some of the other unmet health care needs of senior citizens in this country. The quality of life for millions is diminished because they can't afford to get their glasses repaired, get their dentures refitted or get a new battery for their hearing aids. None of these costs are covered by Medicare," he said.

With millions of members and supporters across America, the National Committee is devoted to the retirement security of all citizens. The National Committee is a non-profit, non-partisan, grassroots advocacy and education organization independent of Congress or any government agency.
