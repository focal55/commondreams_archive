Rally and Protest Wednesday, Feb. 28 in San Francisco Law Offices of Epstein, Becker and Green
To Mark the 2nd Anniversary of Illegal Pacifica Bylaws Changes - And to Protest Newly Proposed Pacifica Bylaws Changes
   SAN FRANCISCO - February 27 - The Coalition for a democratic Pacifica in conjunction with Media Alliance and the KPFA Steering Committee call for a mass Rally and Picket outside the law offices of the firm of Epstein, Becker and Green in San Francisco, Wednesday, February 28, from 11:30 am - 1 PM at 2 Embarcadero Center.

Listeners to KPFA FM, 94.1 who have been waging a battle against the corporate takeover the the country's oldest listener-supported radio network will rally to protest proposed illegal bylaws changes by John Murdock, a member of the Pacifica National Board of directors - and a member of the law firm of Epstein Becker & Green, the law firm retained by the Pacifica Foundation to defend itself against multiple lawsuits by stakeholders in Pacifica, the nation's oldest community supported radio network. The proposed bylaws changes would effectively and undemocratically hand over power to a tiny group within the Pacifica Board - changes in apparent violation of state and federal law and of the principals the Foundation was formed to uphold, namely progressive, non-commercial, listener-supported free speech radio - changes which the listeners of KPFA and Pacifica have vowed to fight.

The allegations in the lawsuits against Pacifica include that the directors of the Foundation sit on its board of directors illegally. Plaintiffs in 3 major lawsuits have filed those actions based on the Foundation's efforts to liquidate the radio broadcasting assets of the Pacifica Foundation.

Epstein, Becker & Green is one of the primary union-busting law firms in the United States, and Epstein Becker & Green member (attorney John Murdock) is a defendant in those lawsuits by virtue of his seat on the board of directors of the Pacifica Foundation.

Epstein Becker & Green has also threatened to file lawsuits against Free Speech advocates on the Internet who are critical of the role the firm plays in Pacifica, critics who espouse support for the very principals upon which Epstein Becker & Green's client Pacifica bases its operating principles.

The rally will picket the law offices of Epstein, and will feature statements written by Danny Glover, Noam Chomsky, Alice Walker, labor unions and other prominent progressives who defend the traditional role of Pacifica and who decry the attempted corporatization of the 51 year old radio network.
