Taxpayer Group Blasts Administration On Ethanol Decision
  WASHINGTON - June 12 - The Bush Administration's decision today to force California to use ethanol as a replacement for the gas additive MTBE will cost taxpayers hundreds of millions of dollars in additional ethanol subsidies and will be a new billion-dollar cash cow for one of America's largest agricultural giants, according to a leading critic of corporate welfare.

To comply with federal requirements imposed by the Clean Air Act of 1990, California will need more than 580 million gallons of ethanol. Currently, the industry receives a 55-cent tax break per gallon of ethanol produced. Today's decision will mean a new $320 million subsidy, bringing total ethanol subsidies to more than $1 billion per year.

"This decision is 100 percent Grade-A political pandering to Archer Daniels Midland and the farm lobby," said Keith Ashdown, spokesman for Taxpayers for Common Sense (TCS). "You would think that this was an election year by the size of this new handout."

Archer Daniels Midland Co. (ADM), a politically powerful company based in Decatur, Illinois, would benefit most from the new ethanol windfall -- not small family farmers in the Midwest, according to the group.

"This decision flies in the face of the Administration's rhetoric on cutting corporate welfare," continued Ashdown. "The cold, hard truth is that this is yet another politically motivated handout to a powerful special interest."

ADM is one of the biggest agricultural companies in the world with more than $14 billion in sales last year. ADM also controls over 55 percent of the U.S. ethanol market. If all 16 states where MTBE is now used were required by EPA to use ethanol instead, total demand for ethanol would more than double.

"The California decision sets a horrible precedent that taxpayers and consumers at the pump should pay more so that profitable companies like Archer Daniels Midland can make even more," said Ashdown.

The proposed switch from MTBE to ethanol highlights the political power of the farm lobby and ADM. ADM has given about $5 million in contributions to federal lawmakers since 1991.

"The saying 'give and you shall receive' seems appropriate today," concluded Ashdown, "ADM gives millions in political contributions and is getting hundreds of millions worth of subsidies in return."

Taxpayers for Common Sense (TCS) is an independent advocate for the American taxpayer. TCS is dedicated to cutting wasteful spending and subsidies in order to achieve a more responsible and efficient government that lives within its means.
