EPA to Hear from Oil Refinery Residents on Review of Clean Air Protections
TX, LA Residents Travel to D.C. To Demand Public Health Protection from Bush Administration
 

WASHINGTON - June 28 -

WHO:
-- LaNell Anderson, Houston, Texas
-- Bob Anderson, Houston, Texas
-- Dagmar Darjean, Mossville Environmental Action Now, Mossville, La.
-- Reverend Roy Malveaux, Beaumont, Texas

WHAT: Statements and interviews available after Meeting with representatives of the Bush administration who are reviewing New Source Review, a cornerstone of the Clean Air Act that requires pollution reduction. Please call 202-667-4500, ext. 209 to arrange an interview after 2:30 p.m. on Friday.

WHERE: Washington, DC at The Environmental Protection Agency, Ariel Rios Building

WHEN: Friday, June 29, at 1:30 p.m. EDT
Statements and interviews after 2:30 p.m.

WHY: The Bush administration recently initiated a 90-day review process to examine New Source Review, a key program in the Clean Air Act that requires new and existing industrial facilities to use modern pollution reduction technology. The administration is reconsidering this program in response to claims by oil and utility companies that the pollution reduction requirements are too stringent. The Wall Street Journal reported June 28th that the Bush administration is doing more than reviewing the program. According to the report, the Justice Department has frozen all pending New Source Review enforcement actions that are part of a major crack down begun under the Clinton administration to stop illegal polluting by oil refineries and dirty power plants.

The six residents each live near sprawling oil refineries in Louisiana and Texas. They are opposed to the Bush energy plan's reconsideration of New Source Review, a key program that can reduce the air pollution released by the huge oil refineries in their communities. Residents are traveling to Washington, DC to personally urge that the EPA continue and strengthen New Source Review enforcement. They will present reports and other data showing the relationship between oil refinery pollution, asthma and other severe health problems suffered by residents in their communities. They will also discuss the need for EPA's continued enforcement of New Source Review and its oversight of Texas and Louisiana environmental agencies, which have a record of poor environmental enforcement.
