Interactive Web-chat With Human Rights And Environmental Activists
   WASHINGTON - April 23 - The U.S. Public Interest Research Group (U.S. PIRG) and an international coalition of human rights groups are calling on the four major oil companies BP Amoco, ExxonMobil, Phillips Petroleum, and Chevron to shelve their plans to drill in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge. Although frequently seen as an environmental issue, these activists see environmental responsibility as key to the preservation of human rights.

As one of the last remaining subsistence cultures in North America, the Gwich’in people’s way of life is being threatened. Gwich’in means “people of the caribou,” the same caribou who provide them food, clothing and shelter. Drilling in the Arctic Refuge would threaten the herd, along with the culture of the Gwich’in people.

These activists will release letters to the oil companies at 11:30 AM Eastern Daylight Time, Tuesday, April 24, 2001, during an online news conference and interactive web-chat at www.SaveTheArctic.com. This online event will also allow people from around the world to ask questions via a moderated, online discussion.

Activists participating in the on-line chat:
- Athan Manuel, Director, PIRG Arctic Wilderness Campaign
- Sarah James, Member of the Gwich'in Steering Committee and the Arctic Village Council
- Oronto Douglas, Nigerian Human Rights attorney and environmental activist
- Steve Kretzmann, Energy and Investment Consultant, International Campaign for Tibet


U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for the state Public Interest Research Groups. PIRGs are non-partisan, non-profit public advocacy organizations that are active across the country.
