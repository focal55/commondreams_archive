Statement of Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger on Secrecy at the President's Commission to Strengthen Social Security
  WASHINGTON - August 22 - The President’s Commission on Social Security is skirting the spirit of our open meeting laws by breaking into closed -door groups to deliberate on changes to the Social Security system. Just because they’re splitting into two subgroups doesn’t mean that the open meeting laws should be summarily put aside. Their legalistic spin doesn’t change the fact that this federal commission is meeting in private when it should be meeting in public.

The Commission’s action creates the appearance that the real work of the Commission is being done behind closed doors, and the public part is just performance art - a kind of dog and pony show staged to make it look like an open and transparent process.

Nobody will have confidence in the Commission if it appears to be meeting in secret just to arrive at and legitimize a predetermined result. Why have a commission at all, if it’s just for public relations and not for public input?

More troubling is that this seems to be a pattern with the Bush Administration. They have shown a general preference for secrecy over openness, and they’ve dragged their feet even when Congress or the GAO comes looking for info. This policy certainly isn’t serving the public interest and frankly, it isn’t serving the Administration all that well either.
