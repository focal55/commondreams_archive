Broad Coalition to Release Joint Principles and Plans for Sept. 30 March and Rally for Global Justice
  WASHINGTON - September 7 - News Advisory:

Environmentalists, students, union members, community activists, religious leaders, women's rights groups, human rights activists and more will announce plans on Tuesday for a peaceful March and Rally for Global Justice, scheduled for Sunday, Sept. 30 during the time of the IMF and World Bank annual meetings in Washington, D.C.

The group will also release a statement of principles and demands which lays out an "alternate vision of globalization: a vision based on justice and solidarity rather than exploitation and inequality." The principles include a focus on Fast Track trade negotiating authority, cancellation of debt for developing nations, IMF and World Bank policy, and the global AIDS crisis.

AFL-CIO President John Sweeney, Feminist Majority President Eleanor Smeal, Friends of the Earth President Brent Blackwelder, Oxfam America President Raymond Offenheiser, a George Washington University student who is a member of United Students Against Sweatshops, a representative of the Mobilization for Global Justice and other representatives of the coalition will be among the speakers.

The March and Rally for Global Justice is scheduled to take place on the Ellipse at noon on Sept. 30. The march route is still being worked out with District of Columbia authorities. Plans for the rally include speakers, music, street theater, face painting, and more.
