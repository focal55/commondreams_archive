`Support Bill Maher' Campaign Delivers Thousands of Protest Messages to ABC, Sears, FedEx
   BERKELEY, CA - October 4 - "Support Bill Maher," a grassroots, online campaign at http://www.MillionFlagMarch.com/bill/, today delivered messages in support of Bill Maher from more than 6,000 people to executives with ABC, Sears, and FedEx. The campaign also posted them at http://www.MillionFlagMarch.com/bill /messages/. The messages were collected over the course of the effort's first 7 days, and several hundred more are still arriving daily.

"Our nation is in the midst of a crisis, and our first priorities must be defending our country and helping the victims of the attack," said John Hlinko, co-founder of the campaign. "But we can't just stand by and allow a small, shrill group to exploit this crisis to unfairly destroy the reputation of a fellow citizen. There are too many real enemies out there - we must not permit opportunists to create false ones."

The grassroots effort was launched on September 25, and is led by: Hlinko, a veteran of MoveOn.org, JustSayBlow.com, MillionFlagMarch.com, and several other online campaigns; Ann McCormick, founder of CompassionateMoms.org; and Ray Simon, head of MischiefMarketing.com. It came in response to the decisions of several ABC affiliates to drop Maher's show, "Politically Incorrect," and of Sears and FedEx to end sponsorship of the program. Those moves in turn came in response to a controversy in which out-of-context remarks by Maher were misinterpreted as supporting the terrorists involved in the September 11th attack, and denigrating the U.S. military. (see www.MillionFlagMarch.com/bill/ for full story).

"There's a belief among some that Maher was somehow praising the terrorists, but anyone who's actually seen the episode or read the transcript quickly sees how absurd that belief is," said Hlinko. "Even if you're not a fan of Maher, you've got to admit that it's incredibly unfair to punish a guy for something he didn't do. If Sears, FedEx and the ABC affiliates choose to continue meting out punishment that isn't deserved, that's their choice - but it'll be our choice to respond to this unfairness by taking our business elsewhere."

The effort is not looking for donations, and instead encourages supporters to send contributions to efforts geared towards helping the victims of the attack (see http://millionflagmarch.com/bill/thanks.htm).

For further information contact John Hlinko at John@MillionFlagMarch.com
