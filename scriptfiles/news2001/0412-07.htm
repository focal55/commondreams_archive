Sierra Club Urges President Bush to End Delays on Protecting Americans From Cancer Causing Dioxin
Statement of Carl Pope, Executive Director, Sierra Club
   WASHINGTON - April 12 - "The Sierra Club is urging President Bush to act quickly to protect Americans from cancer-causing dioxin and stop the delays keeping us in the dark about toxic chemicals in our food. As the Washington Post reported today, chemical, beef and poultry companies are pressuring President Bush to stall an Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) study linking dioxin in food to cancer in people. The Sierra Club is calling on President Bush to stand up to these industries, which contributed heavily to his campaign, and release this decade-long study on the threats of dioxin.

To drag out an already comprehensive process, the chemical, beef and poultry industries are asking President Bush and the EPA to examine yet

more science. But President Bush's budget would cripple EPA's ability to analyze science, slashing $55 million that would be used to study how best to protect our families' health from environmental threats like dioxin. To protect Americans from cancer-causing pollution, President Bush should release the report promptly and take immediate action to safeguard our families' health."

Dioxin, a byproduct of manufacturing and incineration, causes cancer and other neurological and reproductive disorders in people. Because it builds up in the food we eat, almost everyone on the planet carries some dioxin in their bodies already. Currently, no U.S. government standards exist to protect Americans from dioxin-laced food.
