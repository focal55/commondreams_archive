Schumer/McCain Bill Would Provide Consumers with Much Needed Affordable Prescription Drugs
  WASHINGTON - May 1 - U.S. PIRG applauded Senator Schumer (NY) and Senator McCain (AZ) for introducing the Greater Access to Affordable Pharmaceuticals (GAAP) Act at a news conference this morning. The bill would close legal loopholes that brand name drug companies have exploited to keep affordable generic drugs off the market.

"American consumers are desperate for affordable prescription drugs. The Schumer/McCain bill is the prescription American consumers need," said U.S. PIRG staff attorney, Rachel Weintraub.

According to the Congressional Budget Office, generic drugs currently save consumers between $8 and $10 billion each year and enter the market at prices 25% lower than brand name drugs.

"The brand name drug industry is price gouging American consumers by employing a number of questionable tactics: charging American consumers the highest prices in the world and preventing affordable generic drugs from entering the market place," said Weintraub. "This legislation is a significant step toward providing all consumers access to safe and affordable prescription drugs."

The Greater Access to Affordable Pharmaceuticals Act will accelerate generic drug competition by removing incentives for brand name drug companies to pay off potential generic competitors, by requiring full and accurate listing of all relevant patents, by strengthening the citizen petition process, and by making it harder for brand name drug companies to delay FDA's approval process for generic drugs.

U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for state-based Public Interest Research Groups across the country. The state PIRGs are independent, non-profit, non-partisan public interest advocacy organizations.
