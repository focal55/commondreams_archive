Experts Question Which Laws of War are Being Violated in Latest Round of Clashes in the Middle East
   WASHINGTON - February 6 - The current clashes between Israelis and Palestinians have refocused world attention on a conflict many had hoped was nearing resolution. However, most news reports of pitched battles and political proclamations rarely address crucial points of international law. To understand exactly which violations of international humanitarian law are being committed in the region, the Crimes of War Project assigned a former Newsweek correspondent in the Middle East to interview legal experts from Israel, Palestine and the United States.

Among the questions addressed are: Do the current clashes amount to war? Is the Israeli military response justified in the face of Palestinian actions? Are Israel's blockades of Palestinian areas a form of collective punishment? Are armed Israeli settlers legal combatants? Is the intermingling of Palestinian gunmen in amongst stone-throwing youths tantamount to using human shields? The answers to such questions can, we believe, help separate rhetoric from fact by putting the behavior of the parties into the framework of long-established, if often ignored, international legal standards.

Those consulted to help explain the relevance of these important but often confusing laws include:

-- Steven Ratner, University of Texas School of Law and author of "Accountability for Human Rights Atrocities in International Law: Beyond the Nuremberg Legacy" (Oxford U. Press, 1997).

-- Charles Shamas, Palestinian lawyer and Senior Partner, MATTIN Group

-- Eyal Benvenisti, Israeli legal scholar and author of "The International Law of Occupation" (Princeton University Press, 1993).

-- Yaron Ezrahi, political science professor at Hebrew University in Jerusalem and author of "Rubber Bullets: Power and Conscience in Modern Israel" (Farrar, Strauss & Giroux, 1997).

-- Mustafa Barghouthi, physician and president of the Union of Palestinian Medical Relief Committees.

The interviews can be read on http://www.crimesofwar.org/middleeast/index.html

The views presented are those of the individuals and not those of any institution or the Crimes of War Project.

The Crimes of War Project is an independent, non-profit organization solely dedicated to raising awareness of international humanitarian law among the media, policy makers, diplomats, the human rights and humanitarian aid communities, and the general public.
