NWLC Pledges to Hold Ashcroft, Administration Accountable for Assurances on Women's Rights
   WASHINGTON - February 1 - On the heels of the Senate's narrow vote to confirm John Ashcroft as Attorney General, the National Women's Law Center pledged vigilance in holding Ashcroft, and the Bush Administration, accountable for their assurances on women's constitutional rights, civil rights and other important legal protections. Marcia D. Greenberger, NWLC's Co-President, testified against Senator Ashcroft's confirmation before the Senate Judiciary Committee on Jan. 18.

"We will fight the new leadership at the Justice Department and the Administration every step of the way if they attempt to roll back decades of legal advances for women and their families," said Greenberger. "And we will hold the Senate responsible for making sure John Ashcroft keeps the promises those who supported his nomination insist he made."

Greenberger added, "The historic level of grassroots opposition to this nomination sends a clear message that the American people strongly disagree with the appointment of ideologues and extremists who have a record of distorting the law to fit their own aims. We hope that President Bush heard the outcry and will take heed as he selects other important executive branch appointments and nominates judges to the federal bench."

With the confirmation of Senator Ashcroft -- along with the other anti-choice, anti-family planning positions of the Bush Administration and with Roe v. Wade hanging by a 5-4 thread in the Supreme Court -- women are now in danger of losing their right to choose more than at any other time since 1973. The Attorney General's vast powers could allow John Ashcroft to render Roe basically inoperative without his ever having to publicly call for overturning it. Much of this effort could be done outside the light of public or judicial scrutiny.

John Ashcroft's record of antipathy to women's concerns, civil rights and equal opportunity demonstrates that the threat he poses extends beyond the right to choose. Not only did Senator Ashcroft use distortion tactics to sabotage the nominations of Judge Ronnie White and James Hormel, but he also opposed numerous highly qualified female nominees to the federal bench including Margaret Morrow, against whom he also leveled unsubstantiated charges. As Governor, Ashcroft vetoed a maternity leave law that was far more limited in scope than the federal Family and Medical Leave Act he will now be charged with defending as Attorney General, and twice used the line-item veto to seek out and strike even small sums of money for domestic violence programs -- especially troubling given that he will now administer a major program to combat violence against women. "The close votes in the Committee and on the Senate floor strongly demonstrate that the American people do not want the extreme positions and unfair tactics from John Ashcroft's past to re-surface in the future. We'll be working to make sure that doesn't happen," said Greenberger.

The National Women's Law Center is a non-partisan, non-profit organization that has been working since 1972 to advance and protect women's legal rights. NWLC joined an unprecedented, broad-based coalition of women's rights, reproductive rights, civil rights, labor, gun control and environmental groups in opposing the confirmation of John Ashcroft as Attorney General.
