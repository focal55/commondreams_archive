National Environmental Trust Statement on Toxics Treaty
  WASHINGTON - April 19 - Following is a statement of Philip Clapp, president, National Environmental Trust, on President Bush's decision on the toxics treaty:

The President's announcement that he will sign the international agreement on persistent organic pollutants is an important step forward in halting the worldwide spread of dangerous chemicals. Toxics like dioxin persist in the environment and will threaten generations to come.

But this will not defuse the international controversy over the President's unilateral rejection of the global warming treaty. European leaders, Japan and developing nations have united over the past several weeks in their determination to complete and ratify the global warming agreement by next year. It's very likely that the criticism will continue from other leaders when the President goes to the Summit of the Americas in Quebec this weekend.

The National Environmental Trust is a national, non-profit environmental organization providing public policy expertise and educational outreach on national environmental issues.
