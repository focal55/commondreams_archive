Experts Available for Interview on July 14 National Missile Defense Interceptor Flight Test
  WASHINGTON - July 14 - News Advisory:

The Pentagon's Ballistic Missile Defense Organization (BMDO) is scheduled to conduct the fourth test of its ground-based NMD interceptor this Saturday, July 14, between 9 p.m. and 1 a.m. EDT. BMDO will launch an interceptor from the Kwajalein Atoll in the Pacific that will attempt to hit a target launched from Vandenberg AFB in California. Saturday's $100 million test will be a repeat of the July 8, 2000, NMD flight test, which failed to hit the target.

Earlier this week, Bush Administration officials asked Congress to approve a new missile defense research, development and testing program that they say will come into conflict with the 1972 Anti-Ballistic Missile (ABM) Treaty within "months, not years." This reflects the Administration's wish to have a rudimentary national missile defense capability by 2004, whether or not it has been proven to be reliable and effective. Such a crash deployment would provide illusory protection from potential long-range missile threats, while at the same time violating the ABM Treaty and setting off a dangerous action-reaction cycle involving the United States, Russia, and China.
