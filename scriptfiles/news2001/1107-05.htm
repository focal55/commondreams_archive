Statement of Democracy 21 President Fred Wertheimer On Tonight's NRSC Washington Fundraiser
  

WASHINGTON - November 7 - The following is a statement by Democracy 21 President Fred Wertheimer:

The fundraising event being held tonight by the National Republican Senatorial Committee (NRSC) for big donors is not just a return to business-as-usual; it's a return to corrupting- influence-money-as-usual.

This latest quest for big political bucks by the NRSC comes at a time of war, terrorist alerts, anthrax scares and difficult economic times for millions of Americans, and at the equivalent of World Series time for big-money influence in Washington.

As the congressional legislative session heads into its final weeks, high-stakes tax and spending decisions affecting the financial interests of big-money contributors are about to be made in Congress. The NRSC fundraiser is taking place at an optimum time for the buying and selling of influence in Washington.

If you check the NRSC Web site, you will see the following message to donors about tonight's NRSC fundraiser from its Chairman, Senator Bill Frist of Tennessee: "In the immediate aftermath of September 11, the decision was made to temporarily suspend all NRSC fundraising activities. As you likely know, this suspension led to the postponement of our Inner Circle Fall Briefing and Seventh Annual Senate Dinner. As Americans 'get back to work,' we too must move forward and continue our efforts to regain a Republican Senate Majority."

Equating the need for Senators to get back to chasing huge influence-buying contributions with the need for Americans to get back to work is beyond the pale. If Members of Congress want to "get back" to something they ought to get back to cleaning up the corrupt campaign finance system and protecting the integrity of our democracy.

The case for acting quickly in the House on the Shays-Meehan soft money ban bill, the companion legislation to the Senate-passed McCain-Feingold bill, only grows.
