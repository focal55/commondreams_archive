Women Opening Doors for Women Provides Networking Forum for Pro-Choice, Democratic Women
   WASHINGTON - June 13 - Doors will swing wide open for Democratic women throughout the District -- many leading to promising career opportunities at the 12th annual Women Opening Doors for Women, hosted by the Women's Information Network (WIN). This unique event provides a forum for recent college graduates, newcomers to D.C. and women climbing the ladder of success to network and form relationships with mentors and other pro-choice, democratic women.

WIN has made great strides in organizing this event to affirm its commitment to core democratic principles. In support of the organized labor movement, WIN is pleased to hold the opening reception at the AFL-CIO headquarters building. Demonstrating its steadfast commitment to reproductive choice, the featured speaker, Gloria Feldt, President of Planned Parenthood Federation of America will deliver a timely call to action. At this year's WODW, the many accomplishments of Donna Brazile, Campaign Manager, Gore 2000 Campaign, will be honored with a special recognition.

Highlighting the evening are 25 simultaneous, career-themed dinner parties held in the homes of accomplished women in the District. Dinner topics this year range in scope from "A Woman's Place is in the House & Senate" to "The Art of Money: Non-profit Fundraising" to "Web Women, Politics in the 21st Century."

"Providing women with a forum to network and learn from other women motivates our organization," said Amy Haberman Gomez, President of WIN. "WODW is an inspirational event because it brings together women of different social and professional backgrounds who have the same political interests. This event truly demonstrates the power that we can collectively have as women."

The Women's Information Network (WIN) is Washington's premier professional, political, and social network dedicated to the advancement and empowerment of women, with a tradition of pro-choice, Democratic support. Founded in 1989 on the idea that women can and should help women climb the ladder of success, WIN has grown to a membership of nearly 750 women, and now features more than 15 issue and career-specific networks and committees.

Women Opening Doors for Women (WODW) begins with a reception at the AFL-CIO Headquarters at 815 16th Street, N.W. and is followed by 25 simultaneous, career focused, informal dinner parties held throughout D.C. A full list of dinner parties and speakers is available at www.winonline.org. If you are interested in attending one of the dinner parties or would like to interview the chair of this event or one of the many women who volunteered to put it together, please contact Janeen Lawlor at 202-518-8047, janeen@publicinterestpr.com.

The Event is Thursday, June 13, 2001. Reception is from 5:30 until 7 p.m., Dinner Parties are from 7:30 -- 9:30 p.m.
