Sierra Club To Rally At President Bush's Everglades National Park and Tampa Stops
Volunteers Will Protest President's Actions Against Our Public Lands
   EVERGLADES NATIONAL PARK - June 1 - Sierra Club activists will rally at President Bush's photo opportunity outside Everglades National Park and at Legends Field in Tampa on Monday. The activists are calling on President Bush to stop his attacks on our fragile, coastal areas, national wildlife refuges, and other wild lands for damaging energy exploration. Sierra Club members will also protest the Bush administration's review of a recent Air Force decision to prohibit the former Homestead Air Force Base from becoming a major commercial airport.

While President Bush will use Everglades National Park as an environmentally friendly backdrop to announce his choice for the Director of the National Park Service, he is considering opening protected, coastal areas for energy exploration.

Everglades National Park Rally

WHEN: 9:00-11:00 AM, EST, Monday, June 4, 2001

WHERE: Visitors Center of Everglades National Park

Tampa Rally

WHEN: 6:00 PM, EST, Monday, June 4, 2001

WHERE: Legends Field, Tampa, FL
