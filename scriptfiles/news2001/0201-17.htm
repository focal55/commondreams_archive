NARAL Statement on Powerful Opposition Vote Against John Ashcroft
   WASHINGTON - February 1 - NARAL President Kate Michelman today released the following statement on the vote in the Senate to confirm John Ashcroft as the Attorney General.

"The closeness of today's vote demonstrates the strong opposition to John Ashcroft's extreme views and record and the divisive nature of his nomination.

"Today the majority of pro-choice senators made a powerful statement that they find the extreme views and record of John Ashcroft unsuitable for the role of Attorney General; that someone with a long history of unmitigated attacks on women's established constitutional rights is simply unacceptable. The closeness of the vote clearly demonstrates the divisive nature of Bush's nomination.

"Though we did not succeed in defeating Ashcroft, we have established -- and served notice to President Bush -- that any nominee with a record of assault on a woman's right to choose will be met with broad and powerful opposition.

"President Bush's nomination of John Ashcroft unleashed a firestorm across America resulting in historic opposition in the Senate. This serves as a clear and powerful reminder to President Bush that he and his new Attorney General have no mandate from the people to roll back women's constitutional rights.

"NARAL is disappointed that some pro-choice senators elevated politics over principle in voting to confirm Ashcroft. At the same time, we are grateful to those who took courageous and principled stands in voting against him.

"As demonstrated by our campaign in opposition to Ashcroft's nomination, NARAL is prepared to do whatever is necessary to defeat nominations to the Supreme Court and lower circuit federal courts that would threaten a woman's right to choose."
