FACT SHEET: Conflicts of Interest of Non-Federal Employees Participating in DOE's "Rebaseline Validation Review" Committee for the NIF
   WASHINGTON - February 1 - The Chair of the "Large Optics" Subcommittee - a critical area of performance uncertainty for the NIF - was Michel Andre, a senior scientist in the French Megajoule laser project who has extensive contractual relations and engages in joint efforts with the NIF program. Were he to have been critical of NIF, and its optics component, his own program and career would have been adversely affected.

Two other subcommittee members, John Emmett and E. Perry Wallerstein, are former senior laser program officials at the Livermore lab, and longstanding paid consultants to the Livermore laboratory. The fourth member of the subcommittee, Dr. Michelle Shinn, works at DOE's Thomas Jefferson Laboratory in Virginia, whose Director, Dr. Hermann Grunder, until recently chaired Livermore's NIF Programs Review Committee, one of the bodies found most responsible for failing to exert adequate oversight of the project.

Two employees of Dr. Grunder's at the Thomas Jefferson Laboratory - George Neil and Andrew Hutton - chaired the subcommittees reviewing the NIF's "Line Replaceable Units" and "Assembly, Installation, and Commissioning. Each of these subpanels also included a member - Drs. Robert McCrory and Steve Loucks, respectively - from the University of Rochester's Laboratory for Laser Energetics, a major Livermore subcontractor and scientific collaborator on the NIF Project.

Documents obtained by co-plaintiff Tri-Valley CAREs reveal that McCrory and Loucks are also signatories on Livermore NIF subcontracts with the University of Rochester worth millions of dollars. According to an affidavit filed by TVC's executive director, Marylia Kelley, the group has in its possession 47 records, including NIF subcontracts, contract modifications, schedules, and sole source awards, that were signed by McCrory and Loucks or show them as managers or executors for the contracts.

Dr. McCrory has also been a paid individual consultant to the Laboratory Director's Office and to the lab's National Ignition Facility Program Directorate since 1996, and, according to copies of contracts obtained by NRDC under the California Public Records Act, entered into a new contract to consult on the NIF only weeks after his service on the Rebaseline Committee.

The panel on NIF assembly also included another longtime paid private consultant to Livermore - Dr. Damon Giovanielli - who had served only a few months earlier as the chair of the NIF Project's own "Target Physics Program Review Committee," which had concluded, "NIF should be completed to its full 192-beam configuration."

According to the declarations put before the court by Paine and Kelley, other members of the Rebaseline Committee had similar conflicts. Dr. John Peoples and James Renfro were under contract with the Livermore lab to provide consulting services at the time of the review, and Eugene Desaulniers had been a paid consultant to the NIF Project as recently as October 1998. William Barletta, of DOE's Lawrence Berkeley Lab, had previously been tapped by NIF management to promote the project in Congressional meetings.

Corrupting the Advisory Process
"Additional facts surrounding the Rebaseline Review are very telling," says NRDC researcher Christopher Paine, "because they reveal the extent to which DOE is willing to abuse the 'independent' advisory process for purely political purposes." Before Congress had received the Rebaseline Review and other reports purporting to document the Secretary's "certification" of the new $3.5 billion "baseline" NIF construction cost, and before Congress had the opportunity to consider lesser configurations for the facility, DOE preemptively awarded a $230 million cost-plus- award-fee contract on August 31, 2000 to the Jacobs Engineering Group to install the full 192-beam laser infrastructure.

Another 'independent review' supporting the Secretary's certification of the NIF, prepared by a year long "NIF Task Force" of the DOE's main FACA committee - the Secretary of Energy's Advisory Board (SEAB) - was not included in the Congressional certification package because it had not been considered and approved by the full SEAB for transmission to the Secretary. "To fill this gap," Paine said, "the chairman of the NIF Task Force wrote a letter, on SEAB letterhead, directly to the Secretary of Energy, summarizing his view of the favorable conclusions reached by the Task Force, and Secretary Richardson then submitted this letter - a FACA violation -- to Congress as part of his NIF certification package."

When the full SEAB eventually submitted the NIF Task Force's Final Report to the Secretary of Energy -- on November 27, 2000, well after Congress had approved NIF funding for 2001 -- it was accompanied by a letter of dissent from three members of the full SEAB who concluded, "We cannot endorse 'completion' of a $4 billion project irrespective of its ability to perform its intended missions."

A self-described 'key finding' of the NIF Task Force Report is that periodic reviews of progress, similar to the illegal August 2000 review conducted at Livermore, should "be conducted throughout the life of the project." According to Paine, this suggests that DOE is "not only failing to acknowledge past FACA violations in its reviews of NIF, but apparently intends to continue these violations - hence the need for judicial intervention to protect the public interest."
