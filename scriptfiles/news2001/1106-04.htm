More Than $1 Billion in Conservation Funding To Be Considered by Voters on Nov. 6
Election Info Available on LandVote 2001
   WASHINGTON - November 6 - Today, Americans in 14 states are casting votes on more than 150 town, city, county, and state ballot measures to fund more than $1 billion in open space protection.

Elections are being held in Arizona, California, Colorado, Connecticut, Georgia, Massachusetts, Missouri, New Jersey, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Texas, and Washington.

The Trust for Public Land (TPL) and the Land Trust Alliance (LTA) are chronicling these public measures. Descriptions of the measures and election results (as they are confirmed by local officials) are available at both www.tpl.org and www.lta.org.

"While 2001 is an off-year election, citizens and community leaders continue to seek local funding of land protection by placing these measures on the ballot," said Andy McLeod, TPL's director of conservation finance.

"The large number of ballot measures for local and state funding of open space indicates that voters desire to take direct action to raise the funds necessary to save lands essential to their community," said Russell Shay, director of public policy for LTA.

The number of successful open space initiatives or referenda in cities, towns, counties, and states has grown dramatically in recent years. In 2000, voters approved 174 local and state open space measures, creating $7.5 billion in new funding. In 1999, 92 successful measures created $1.8 billion, and in November 1998, 124 successful local and state measures dedicated $8.3 billion in new funding for conservation.

Soon after the elections, LTA and TPL will publish LandVote 2001, a guide to all 2001 ballot questions that dedicate public money for open space. A comprehensive listing can be accessed by:

-- Visiting the Web at www.tpl.org or www.lta.org
-- Calling 617-367-6200, extension 340

The Trust for Public Land, established in 1972, is the only national nonprofit working exclusively to protect land for human enjoyment and well-being. TPL helps conserve land for recreation and spiritual nourishment and to improve the health and quality of life of American communities.

The Land Trust Alliance, founded in 1982, promotes voluntary land conservation across the country and providing resources, leadership and training to the nation's 1,200-plus nonprofit, grassroots land trusts, helping them to protect important open spaces.
