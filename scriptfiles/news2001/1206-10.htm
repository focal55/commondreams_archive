Congress Surrenders Power to Fix Trade Deals
Fast Track is the Wrong Track for America's Environment
   WASHINGTON - December 6 - The Sierra Club today criticized Congress for voting in favor of "Fast Track", also known as Trade Promotion Authority, (H.R. 3005). With this extremely close vote, Congress surrenders its constitutional responsibility to set the course of U.S. trade policy. The Sierra Club opposed the bill because trade agreements increasingly threaten clean air, clean water and public lands both in America and around the world.

"We shouldn't rush through important trade deals without making sure that all Americans' needs are addressed--including protections for the environment and workers. But that's by definition what 'fast track' does," said Carl Pope, Executive Director of the Sierra Club.

Under fast track, Congress gives up the power to fix trade deals made by U.S. negotiators--even if those deals threaten the environment, or workers' rights.

Of special concern to the Sierra Club is that this fast track bill could expand NAFTA-style corporate lawsuits to other nations. Under these rules, global corporations can sue U.S. taxpayers for huge sums of money if they believe America's hard-won environmental, health, and safety laws interfere with their profits.

"Many Members of Congress showed tremendous courage in standing up to the corporate lobby in opposing fast track," continued Pope. "In the wake of this highly partisan vote that defied the will of the American people, our fair trade coalition emerged with significant momentum to ensure that future anti-environmental trade deals never become law. When our health, safety and clean environment are at stake, we should make sure that all sides are heard before we move ahead with these trade deals."
