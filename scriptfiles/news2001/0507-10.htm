Hubert H. Humphrey Civil Rights Award Dinner May 9 At The Hilton Washington & Tower
   WASHINGTON - May 7 - Frank Raines, chairman and CEO of Fannie Mae, Maya Lin, renowned artist and architect, and William L. Taylor, prominent civil rights attorney, will receive the Hubert H. Humphrey Civil Rights Award at the annual dinner of the Leadership Conference on Civil Rights (LCCR) on Wednesday, May 9 at the Hilton Washington and Towers, 1919 Connecticut Avenue, NW, Washington, D.C. A Reception will be held at 5:30 p.m., followed by dinner at 7 p.m. Our Master of Ceremonies for the evening will be ABC-7's News Anchor Maureen Bunyan.

The prestigious Humphrey Award for "selfless and devoted service in the cause of equality" celebrates the legacy of the former United States Vice President, Senator and civil rights pioneer.

The LCCR Humphrey Award Dinner is the largest civil rights dinner in Washington, D.C. Each year a cross-section of our nation's leaders including members of both houses of Congress, officials from the Executive Branch, business people, educators, and advocates for civil and human rights attend the event.

Now in its 51st year, the LCCR is the nation's oldest, most broadly-based civil rights coalition with more than 180 major national organizations representing persons of color, women, children, organized labor, persons with disabilities, older Americans, gays and lesbians, and major religious groups.

The Press is invited to attend. Make arrangements in advance by contacting Natalie Shear, dinner coordinator, at 202-833-4456. For further information on this year's award recipients, visit www.civilrights.org.
