Retaliatory Strikes Inconsistent with American Values
Peace Organization Urges Respect For the Rule of Law
   LOS ANGELES - September 12 - The state's largest peace organization today issued a statement of opposition to potential military strikes in response to the attacks on New York and Washington.

Peter Ferenbach, California Peace Action's Executive Director explained, "Our hearts and our prayers go out to the victims of the attacks in New York and Washington. Many people lost parents, children, sisters, brothers and friends. Nonetheless, there is no value in attacks that are destined to result in a further loss of innocent lives. To act with disregard for the lives of those who are not directly responsible for the attacks on the U.S. lowers us to the same level as those who perpetrated the barbarity of Tuesday's attacks."

Peace Action has mobilized a statewide phone bank to direct people's frustration and anger toward constructive responses. Staff and volunteers are making hundreds of phone calls to provide people with contact information for disaster relief organizations and blood banks in their immediate area. The organization is similarly directing people toward vigils and community gatherings in their area.

"The best response to terrorism, is to affirm community and to call ourselves to a higher appreciation of our humanity. As Gandhi once wrote, 'An eye for an eye leaves the whole world blind'," said Danielle Babineau, Peace Action's Southern California Political Director.

In opposing possible air strikes, California Peace Action notes that such strikes would be a violation of international law. "Compounding terrorism with vigilantism will only make a tragic situation worse," said Ferenbach. "The appropriate course of action is to identify the individuals responsible for these atrocities and bring them to trial, not wreak vengeance upon the innocent and the guilty alike."

Peace Action also pointed out that past attacks on the United States have resulted in assaults on people who were victimized for no reason other than the color of their skin or their national origin. In a statement, the organization declared "We call upon our fellow citizens to vocally and physically oppose the likely violence that will be directed at Moslems and people of Middle Eastern origin."

California Peace Action, with 35,000 members is affiliated with National Peace Action. More information is available at
www.CaliforniaPeaceAction.org and at www.Peace-Action.org.
