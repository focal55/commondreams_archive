Bono's 'Day Off' In Washington
Will Push For Deeper Debt Cancellation,
Other Measures to Help Poorest African Countries
   WASHINGTON - June 13 - Bono, lead singer of U2 and tireless campaigner for the cancellation of third world debt is today taking a day off U2's highly successful Elevation Tour to visit members of Congress and the administration.

In the afternoon Bono will meet with Paul O'Neill, and Sen. Jesse Helms will arrange a lunch to which both Democrats and Republicans will be invited. Earlier in the day Bono will meet Sen. Bill Frist to discuss the links between the debt and AIDS crises. Others he will meet during the day include Secretary Tommy Thompson, Chairman Oxley, Chairman Kolbe, Sen. Pat Leahy, Sen. Orrin Hatch and World Bank President Jim Wolfensohn, amongst others. Senate Majority Leader Tom Daschle will be at the U2 concert on the evening of Thursday, June 14.

In light of the exponential spread of AIDS especially in sub-Saharan Africa Bono will be urging Washington's political leaders to consider what more they must do to rise to the challenge presented by this tragic pandemic.

Throughout the day Bono will discuss how a serious effort to tackle the AIDS crisis must build upon current debt relief measures through a more comprehensive policy package which includes: deeper debt relief, a greater quantity and quality of funding for health care and education in poor countries and access to cheapest essential medicines.

Notes to Editors:

-- So far 23 countries have qualified for some debt relief under the Heavily Indebted Poor Countries (HIPC) Initiative. These countries have seen their annual debt payments reduced by 27 percent, freeing up approximately $750 million per year to help fight poverty in these countries

-- However, even after HIPC the majority of these countries still spend more repaying debts than on healthcare. This is one of the reasons why a solution to the AIDS crisis must include more debt relief.

-- The IMF and WB are now by far the biggest creditors to the qualifying HIPC countries. Rich creditor nations led by the U.S., have agreed to cancel 100 percent of the debts they are owed by the poorest nations. However, the International Monetary Fund and the World Bank are canceling less than half the debts they are owed. As a result the poorest countries still spend more on repaying debts than healthcare. The IMF and WB should now be directed by their shareholders (including the USA) to cancel the debts they are owed by qualifying HIPCs

-- An independent review conducted by London based accountancy firm Chantrey Vellacott, has shown that the IMF and World Bank can afford 100 percent cancellation for qualifying HIPCs.

-- Bono has visited Washington ten times over the last three years to lobby as part of the global Jubilee "Drop the Debt" campaign to cancel the unpayable debts of the world's poorest countries. He has met with Pope John Paul II, Secretary of State Colin Powell, Condoleeza Rice, former President Clinton, Tony Blair of the UK, Jacques Chirac of France, Gerhard Schroder of Germany, IMF Managing Director Horst Kohler and World Bank President James Wolfensohn, among others.
