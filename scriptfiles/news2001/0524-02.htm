National Environmental Trust Statement on Sen. Jeffords' Announcement
   WASHINGTON - May 24 - The following is a statement of Philip E. Clapp, president, National Environmental Trust on Sen. Jeffords' announcement:

Sen. Jeffords clearly did not feel comfortable aligned with a president who thinks G.O.P. stands for gas, oil and pollution.

The loss of the Senate is, in part, a direct consequence of the President's decision to mount a major attack on environmental protections over the past four months, and to promote an energy plan written by the oil, coal and utility industries.

Many in Washington are pointing to personal slights as the reason for the Senator's decision to leave the Republican Party. In reality, Sen. Jeffords cares deeply about education and the environment, as he noted at his press conference today, and his choice was rooted in his dismay that the President has abandoned his campaign commitment to moderation and inclusiveness. Sen. Jeffords has been a major leader on environmental issues for years.

This decision shows just how far away from the political mainstream the President has moved on the environment and on energy policy.
