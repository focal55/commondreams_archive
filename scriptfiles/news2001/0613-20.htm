Stop Global AIDS Now! Activists March On NYC
Demand Action from Rich Countries:
Grants, Treatment Access, Debt Cancellation
  WASHINGTON - June 13 - News Advisory:

WHO:
Global-AIDS-Alliance, ACT UP, Jubilee USA Network, African Services Committee, the Health GAP Coalition, American Jewish World Service, Africa Action, and in cooperation with allies in the Global South.

Thousands of concerned people of conscience including AIDS activists, faith-based organizations, economic justice advocates, students, religious activists, health professionals, local African American congregations, working families and their unions, political leaders and others active in calling for action on global AIDS.

WHEN and WHERE:
11 a.m. on Saturday, June 23, 2001. Marchers will gather at Washington Square Park, Manhattan and march (Noon) to Bryant Park for the Rally (1:30 - 3 p.m.) which will include music and speakers. Additional marchers will join the march beginning at GMHC on 24th Street, a halfway point for those who cannot walk the entire march route.

WHAT:
A mass march and rally as part of A Global Day of Action under the theme, "Stop Global AIDS Now!" to demand action from the U.S. government and other wealthy nations. Organizers are calling for a peaceful, legal assembly without violence to people or property.

The Rally speakers will include people living with HIV and activists from grassroots movements in the Global South issuing calls to action, from: South Africa, Thailand, Brazil, Nigeria, and the United States. Other speakers will be available for a press conference including HIV/AIDS experts and activists from Kenya, Uganda and U.S.-based Africa policy and debt cancellation advocates.

WHY:
Marchers are responding to the upcoming UN General Assembly Special Session on HIV/AIDS (June 25-27) and the Group of Eight Summit in Genoa, Italy (July 20-23, 2001). With HIV transmission increasing exponentially, 13 million orphans and with over 30 million people facing death from untreated HIV/AIDS, the richest countries must increase direct grant aid and implement complete debt cancellation for impoverished countries to support an expanded and comprehensive response.

Demands:

  • The United States Government and economically-advantaged Group of Seven (G7) governments invest billions in grants to the Global AIDS Fund, to national AIDS plans, and to social infrastructure in impoverished countries.
  • The United States Government and the Group of Seven (G7) governments use their influence and voting power to call on the IMF and the World Bank to use their own resources to implement full cancellation of debts owed to them by all impoverished countries heavily impacted by HIV/AIDS.
  • Wealthy countries invest in treatment access strategies and health care infrastructure, including vaccine distribution systems. These strategies include parallel importing, generic production, and a system of global bulk drug procurement.
