Indonesia Human Rights Network To Be Launched at DC Conference
Interviews Available on Current Crises in Indonesia
   WASHINGTON - February 22 - Human rights advocates and experts from across the U.S., Indonesia and elsewhere will gather this weekend in Washington, DC, at the inaugural conference of the Indonesia Human Rights Network (IHRN). Hendardi, a leading human rights activist and Chairman of the Jakarta-based PBHI (The Indonesian Legal Aid and Human Rights Association) will give the keynote address at the conference. Many conference speakers will be available for interviews.

The conference -- to be held at George Washington University, February 23 ­ February 25 --will feature lectures, panel discussions and strategy sessions on promoting human rights and democracy in the troubled Indonesian archipelago.

"With events moving swiftly in Indonesia, a network like ours becomes especially necessary," said Lynn Fredriksson, Interim Coordinator. "We plan to press the new U.S. administration and other policy makers to support human rights, civilian control of the military, and rule of law in Indonesia. With military-backed repression escalating, the Government of Indonesia should receive no U.S. military assistance. The peoples of Indonesia must be free to make their own economic and political choices," she added.

Speakers at the conference include Abigail Abrash (author "Papua: Another East Timor?"); Wiwiek Awiati (Jakarta-based environmental lawyer); Jeff Ballinger (Director of the labor rights group Press for Change); Michael Beer (Director, Nonviolence International); Kurt Biddle (Editor, IndonesiaAlert!); Roger Clark (professor, Rutgers University School of Law); Natércia Godinho-Adams (East Timorese completing Ph.D. in educational psychology); Liem Soei Liong (TAPOL, the Indonesia Human Rights Campaign); Lesley McCulloch (Research Associate at the Centre for Defence Studies, Kings College, London); Octovianus Mote (journalist and West Papuan human rights activist ); Allan Nairn (journalist, currently writing a book on Indonesian military); Constancio Pinto (U.S. Representative of the National Council of Timorese Resistance); Muhammad Saleh (Presidium of SIRA Council Aceh); Umar Santi (vice-chairman of the Dutch foundation HAIN (Help Ambon In Need)); Charles Scheiner (National Coordinator, East Timor Action Network); Dr. Sylvia Tiwon (Associate Professor of Southeast Asian/Indonesian Studies, University of California at Berkeley); Semmy Waileruny (Chairman, Team Lawyers of Churches in Ambon), and Dr. Jeffrey Winters (associate professor of political economy at Northwestern University).

INRN is dedicated to the memory of Jafar Siddiq Hamzah, a founding member of IHRN who was kidnapped, then tortured to death in Medan. Jafar was a human rights lawyer and founder of the International Forum on Aceh.

For additional information see www.indonesianetwork.org.
