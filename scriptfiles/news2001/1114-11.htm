ACORN Releases Report on Predatory Lending, Demands Legislative Action
Upper income African-Americans are more likely to receive a subprime loan than are low-income whites
  WASHINGTON - November 14 - ACORN and ACORN Housing Corporation have released a study documenting the concentration of subprime lending to minority and low-income borrowers. The report analyzes national data and that for 60 metropolitan areas, and it looks at both purchase and refinance loans.

In 2000, 49.9 percent of all conventional refinance loans received by African-American homeowners were from subprime lenders, as were 26.2 percent of the refinance loans received by Latino homeowners, compared to 18.0 percent of the refinance loans received by white homeowners. African-Americans were 2.8 times more likely than white borrowers to receive a subprime loan, and Latinos were 1.5 times more likely to do so. Lower income homeowners of all races receive a disproportionate share of subprime loans. Subprime lenders made 36.47 percent of all conventional refinance loans received by low-income homeowners in 2000, and 25.5 percent of all conventional refinance loans received by low-income white homeowners.

Subprime loans carry higher rates and fees, and in many cases involve abusive features that have come to be known as predatory lending. While not all subprime loans are predatory, all predatory loans are subprime.

ACORN's report last month (http://www.acorn.org/acorn10/communityreinvestment/reports.htm) showed that minority and low-income applicants for prime credit are rejected much more frequently than others. This new report shows that minority and low-income borrowers are overwhelmingly more likely to receive higher priced subprime loans.

The new report, titled "Separate and Unequal," is available at http://www.acorn.org.

The report makes clear that legislation is needed to rein in predatory practices.

-- HUD and the Justice Department need to take action immediately to enforce the fair housing act.

-- The Federal Reserve Board, at a minimum, needs to implement its proposed rule lowering the HOEPA threshold, and collecting APR data on loans.

-- State regulators need to take action against abusive and discriminatory lenders.

"It's a crying injustice when minority borrowers are so many times more likely to be pushed into high cost loans. We have price gouging instead of fair housing. And it's time for it to end," said ACORN National President Maude Hurd. "HUD and the Justice Department need to start enforcing the fair housing act against predatory lending targeting. They need to investigate, and they need to take action against lenders and brokers who are charging minority borrowers more. The Federal Reserve Board put out its proposed rule months and months ago. What are they waiting for? Every day they wait borrowers in our neighborhoods lose their hard earned money, and some of them lose their homes."
