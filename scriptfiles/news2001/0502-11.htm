Bush's Missile Defense Plan: Fire, Aim, Ready!
Statement by Dr. Kurt Gottfried, Chairman, Union of Concerned Scientists
   WASHINGTON - May 2 - "President Bush's plan to deploy a US national missile defense system that will eventually lead to the US withdrawal of the 1972 Anti- Ballistic Missile (ABM) Treaty turns the military order of 'Ready, Aim, Fire' on its head.

"No national missile defense system is anywhere near ready to deploy. The Clinton administration's proposed land-based system, which is to become the backbone of Bush's plan, is furthest in development, but failed two out of three tests and is years behind schedule. The other possible components of the Bush plan -- sea-based and space-based systems and boost phase proposals -- are only on the drawing board or in peoples' imagination. Research should continue, but at this point all proposed systems are either untested or unproven.

"There is no reason to abrogate the ABM treaty anytime soon, since it will take years to determine which, if any, of these NMD systems are viable and worth the security costs of breaking out of the ABM Treaty.

"And those security costs would be extremely large. The prospect of a 'Star Wars'-type NMD system will lead Russia to maintain a large arsenal of offensive missiles on high alert. The US intelligence community predicts that, in response to NMD, China is likely to increase its nuclear force tenfold. And Russia has warned it will withdraw from the START treaties that have eliminated thousands of Russian nuclear-armed missiles. US efforts to control the spread of Russian nuclear weapons and materiel would be in tatters. The NMD 'cure' is far worse than the 'disease'.

"Bush's proposal to reduce the US nuclear arsenal is vitally important and welcome. But Bush's aggressive NMD plan will block deep cuts in Russia's nuclear arsenal and efforts to take their weapons off hair- trigger alert. By focusing on the 'rogue' threat, the Bush administration may exacerbate the key threat to US security today: the danger from the large Russian arsenal on high alert.

"The US should give the highest priority to reducing Russia's nuclear arsenal, to inducing Russia to take its missiles off alert, and to preventing proliferation from Russia. In its urge to rush the deployment of NMD, whether it will be effective or not, the Bush administration has its priorities exactly backward."

NOTE: More than 30 scientists and missile experts will converge at the US Capitol for a press conference tomorrow to tell lawmakers that there is no scientific basis for building any of the proposed NMD systems. The press conference/award ceremony is on Wednesday, May 2, at 1:30 PM in Room 189 of the Russell Senate Office Building.
