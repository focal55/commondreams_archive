Religious Coalition For Reproductive Choice Ads Document Domestic Terrorism Against Women
   WASHINGTON - November 13 - With 480 anthrax threats to abortion providers since September 11, the Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice will make the case in newspaper ads that domestic religious terrorists, like those who attacked America, are waging a blasphemous "religious war."

"Like other extremists who cloak themselves in religion, anti-abortion zealots are certain God wants them to terrorize and even kill for their beliefs," said Reverend Carlton W. Veazey, president of the Religious Coalition, the 28-year-old alliance of mainstream pro-choice religions. "Tragically, America is now feeling the kind of fear abortion providers and women have long known."

The ads will run in the Nov. 14 issue of The Hill and the Nov. 15 issue of Roll Call, Washington-based newspapers geared to opinion leaders.

The Religious Coalition cites statistics on violence and disruption from 1977 to the present compiled by the National Abortion Federation, including seven murders and 343 death threats. "Terrorism in any form -- whatever its ideology, theology, and geographic origin -- has no place in a nation that values tolerance, pluralism, and individual conscience," the ad concludes.

The Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice comprises 40 national organizations from 16 denominations and faith traditions in addition to the national Clergy for Choice Network and 22 state affiliates. Coalition members are theologically diverse but agree that women must be free to make reproductive decisions according to their own conscience and faith, without government interference.
