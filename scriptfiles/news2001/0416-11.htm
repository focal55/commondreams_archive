Interfaith Alliance Joins Religious Leaders To Release Report on Federal Budget Priorities on Tax Day
  WASHINGTON - April 16 - This tax day, religious leaders are raising questions and awareness about the disproportionate amount of federal money flowing into the military in contrast to the lack of funding available for human needs programs that provide health care, education, job training and nutrition programs to the disadvantaged and working poor.

At a press conference at the National Press Club, The Interfaith Alliance (TIA) joined religious leaders from the National Council of Churches of Christ; Network, A National Catholic Social Justice Lobby; the Muslim Public Affairs Council; and Pax Christi USA: National Catholic Peace Movement to unveil Tax Day: How the Federal Government Spends Your Income Tax, a report produced by the National Priorities Project.

The local report provides a detailed analysis of how federal income tax dollars contributed by Washington, D.C., taxpayers are spent on human services within the city in comparison to military and defense funding. According to the data, which analyzes the budget for the 2000-2001 fiscal year, 22 cents of every dollar from D.C. residents' federal income taxes is used for military spending, compared to 2 cents for city housing needs and half a penny for job training.

"The report findings illustrate the disturbing trend to spend almost 50 percent of our discretionary budget on military and defense, compared with fractions on education, nutrition programs or job training," said the Rev. Dr. C. Welton Gaddy, executive director of TIA. "These numbers reflect budget priorities that clearly place a higher moral value on building weapons than adequately funding programs which address the needs of the hungry, suffering and struggling in our midst.

"Our faith traditions instruct us to help, not abandon, those in need. Our tax dollars should be distributed in a manner consistent with our values as a society. How can we increase military spending when 14 million children to go to bed hungry, when there is a critical lack of affordable housing and when public schools lack basics resources like textbooks and teachers?

"Instead of increasing government resources for the working poor, President Bush's faith-based initiative shifts federal responsibility of social services to religious charities through a plan that breeds religious competition, invites the political abuse of religious groups, sanctions government funded religious discrimination and undermines the autonomy of religion."

To raise concerns about the budget priorities and the lack of resources available to those in need, TIA will convene grassroots forums to bring together local religious, civic and government leaders to discuss the impact of President Bush's faith-based proposal on the current needs of local communities. Findings from these forums will be shared with elected leaders and officials in the White House Office of Faith-Based and Community Initiatives.

Founded in 1994, The Interfaith Alliance (TIA) is a non-partisan clergy-led grassroots organization dedicated to promoting the positive and healing role of religion in the life of the nation and challenging those who manipulate religion to promote intolerance.
