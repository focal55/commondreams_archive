Polluters Profiting From Federal Lunch Programs
Sierra Club to Release Information on Pollution, Food Contamination and Worker Safety Violations from Slaughterhouses and Processing Plants
   WASHINGTON - June 6 - The Sierra Club will release a new report on Monday, June 11 entitled, "Spoiled Lunch". The report explains how the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) continuously awards companies who have poor environmental, food safety and worker safety records with hundreds of millions of dollars in federal contracts for federal lunch programs, such as the school lunch program.

Over the past five years the federal government paid these companies more than $500 million for meat for public schools and other federal food programs. The Sierra Club report reviews the records of 19 slaughterhouses and processing plants, owned by 13 companies, that sold meat to the USDA.
