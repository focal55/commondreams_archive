ACORN Denounces Tactics, Profits of Energy Supplier, Demands Relief from Super-High Prices
   WASHINGTON - May 17 - The following statement was released today by ACORN:

"These wholesale energy suppliers are making a killing off consumers who are being hit with outrageous bills that they cannot afford. And the federal government refuses to act," said Maude Hurd, president of ACORN, the Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now.

All across the country families are struggling to pay energy bills that have doubled and tripled. Approximately one million low-income households are unable to pay their utility bills and are facing shut-offs. Meanwhile, energy wholesalers are raking in enormous record profits, with increases of as much as 40 percent in profits in 2000, including huge increases in profits in California. The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission has refused to act to make energy affordable for residential users.

Protests on May 21: -- Sacramento (300 ACORN members from around California) -- Manhattan (125 ACORN members from NY, NJ, CT, MA, RI) -- Washington DC (100 ACORN members from DC, PA, MD) -- Houston (60 ACORN members from Houston, Little Rock) -- Albuquerque (25 ACORN members) -- Chicago (150 ACORN members from Chicago, St. Louis, Toledo) -- Denver (25 ACORN members) -- Portland (40 ACORN members from Portland and Seattle)

Details on the company being targeted will be available May 20 or 21st.

ACORN's Demands: ACORN is demanding that this company use some of its vast profits for an expanded energy assistance program, that this company help leverage federal government funding for energy assistance, and that this company assist in the creation of a conservation program that will supply low-income consumers with home audits, energy-efficient appliances, and other energy-saving steps.

ACORN is the nation's largest community organization of low- and moderate-income families, with over 100,000 member families organized into 500 neighborhood chapters in 40 cities across the country. Since 1970 ACORN has taken action and won victories on issues of concern to our members. Our priorities include: better housing for first time homebuyers and tenants, living wages for low-wage workers, more investment in our communities from banks and governments, and better public schools.
