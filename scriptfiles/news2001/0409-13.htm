Planned Parenthood Calls President Bush's Proposed Elimination of Contraceptive Coverage Disgraceful and Hypocritical
   NEW YORK - April 9 - Following is a statement by Gloria Feldt, president, Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

Contraception is an essential part of basic health care for women. The failure of employers and their insurance providers to cover contraception when other prescriptions are covered is discrimination against women, plain and simple. It is particularly shameful when the employer is the federal government. The proposal in the president's budget, which eliminates contraceptive coverage for federal employees, is a blatant attempt to deny women equitable treatment. It is also the height of hypocrisy for a president who opposes a woman's right to choose to also limit women's access to the very thing that prevents unintended pregnancies and makes abortions less necessary.

This disgraceful measure sends a message to employers, to insurance providers, and to all Americans that it is ok to discriminate based on gender. It must not be allowed to stand.

Planned Parenthood Federation of America is the world's largest and most trusted voluntary family planning organization. It comprises 127 affiliates that operate 875 health centers nationwide, which serve nearly five million Americans annually.
