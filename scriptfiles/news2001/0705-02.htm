Greenpeace Invasion Of U.S. Spy Base Ends
   WASHINGTON / LONDON - July 5 - The two remaining Greenpeace activists occupying a radio mast inside the U.S. spy base at Menwith Hills in the U.K. have ended their two-day protest. More than 100 Greenpeace volunteers invaded the base on Tuesday morning at 6 a.m. (U.K. time) and have occupied various parts of the site for the past two days. Menwith Hills is an early warning base that would become an essential part of President George Bush's Star Wars program, along with a nearby radar facility at Fylingdales and another base in Thule in Greenland.

"The United States cannot ignore the will of the world and this Star Wars program is going to go down as fast as President Bush's approval ratings." said Greenpeace U.S. Disarmament Coordinator, Gordon Clark. "This intense protest clearly demonstrates the level of global outrage over the United State's missile defense plan,"

Activists from the United States, Denmark, the U.K., Australia, New Zealand and Germany took part in the action. The last of the group voluntarily came down from the radio mast because their supplies were exhausted.

Greenpeace announced that this action is part of its intensive campaign to stop the Star Wars program.
