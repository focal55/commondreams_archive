Sierra Club to Protest During President's California Visit
Group Calls on Bush for Quicker, Cleaner Energy Plan and to Protect Wild Lands
The Sierra Club will protest in Los Angeles and Sequoia National Park
  

WASHINGTON - May 25 -

Los Angeles Stop
What: Sierra Club members will join other groups to protest President Bush's Energy Plan. The Bush Energy Plan won't work for California. We need quicker, cleaner, cheaper and safer energy solutions. President Bush should immediately put in place rate caps to stop the price gouging in the state and implement incentives for energy efficiency and responsible production.

When: Tuesday, May 29, 2001
11:30 AM

Where: The intersection of Constellation and Avenue of The Stars on the west side of Avenue of the Stars in the heart of Century City. This location is right next to the Century Plaza Hotel at 2025 Avenue of the Stars.

There are several parking structures around the area, but there's limited free parking at the Century City Shopping and Market place on the corner of Santa Monica Blvd. and Avenue of the Stars.

Who: Carl Zichella, California Regional Director will speak at the rally.


Sequoia National Park Stop
What: Sierra Club members will protest President Bush's visit to Sequoia National Park. Volunteers will line the entrance road to the national park to get out their message that these are "our lands", not "oil lands". The President is considering opening up national monuments, parks, wilderness areas and other wildlife refuges to oil and gas development, mining and logging.

When: Wednesday, May 30, 2001
10:00 AM to 2:00 PM

Where: Along Highway 180, between the small town of Squaw Valley and the Sequoia National Park entrance (depending on where the road block is placed). Sequoia National Park and Squaw Valley are east of Fresno on Highway 180.
