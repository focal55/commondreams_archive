Violence Policy Center Statement In Response To Senate Confirmation Of John Ashcroft As Attorney General
   WASHINGTON - February 1 - The Violence Policy Center (VPC) will be watching closely to see which John Ashcroft shows up for work at the Department of Justice: the John Ashcroft who has consistently opposed even modest gun control laws and worked hand-in-hand with the gun lobby; or the John Ashcroft who promised at his confirmation hearing to conscientiously enforce our nation's gun laws.

The VPC will closely scrutinize Ashcroft's performance enforcing our nation's gun laws. The Violence Policy Center strongly opposed Ashcroft's nomination because of his dismal record on gun violence prevention. In addition, Ashcroft is a member of the National Rifle Association, an organization that has repeatedly expressed hostility to the Federal Bureau of Investigation. The NRA's statements regarding federal law enforcement agents so offended former President George H.W. Bush that he renounced his membership in the organization in 1995.

We will hold Ashcroft to his sworn testimony in which he said: "I do not believe that the Second Amendment prohibits common-sense gun control measures, and if confirmed, as Attorney General I will vigorously defend federal gun control statutes passed by Congress whenever there is a good-faith and conscientious basis for doing so."

The Violence Policy Center also intends to closely monitor the Justice Department's performance in enforcing laws against illegal gun traffickers, corrupt gun dealers, felons in possession of firearms, as well as enforcement of federal bans on assault weapons and machine guns. The VPC will also scrutinize maintenance of the National Instant Criminal Background Check System, which is under the jurisdiction of the Department of Justice.

The Violence Policy Center is a national non-profit educational organization working to reduce gun death and injury in America. For more information please visit www.vpc.org or contact Naomi Seligman at 202-822-8200 ext. 105.
