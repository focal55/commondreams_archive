Nationwide PFAW Ad Campaign Targets Ashcroft's Misrepresentations Under Oath
   WASHINGTON - January 26 - On Tuesday, January 30, a full-page People For the American Way ad in newspapers across the country will pose the question to the American people that their U.S. Senators will be required to answer during the coming days:

"Should a man who misrepresents the facts under oath be our Attorney General?"

The ad details and documents four of the areas in which John Ashcroft misrepresented or evaded the facts of his own record in sworn testimony before the Senate Judiciary Committee. It includes:

-- his bitter battle against voluntary school desegregation in St. Louis; -- his advocacy, while he was Missouri's Attorney General and responsible for upholding the law, of federal legislation to enable states to end women's right to choose abortion; -- his unfair and mean-spirited campaign of distortions against a federal court nominee, Judge Ronnie White; and -- his campaign against the confirmation of James Hormel, a highly qualified gay man nominated to be U.S. Ambassador to Luxembourg.

"John Ashcroft used his testimony to mislead the Senators through evasions, denials, and outright contradictions of the documented public record of his own actions and statements," said Ralph G. Neas, president of People For the American Way. "The question that the Senators must answer is whether a nominee who so distorts and evades the facts under oath should be entrusted with the responsibility of upholding our nation's civil and constitutional rights."

The ad encourages readers to contact their Senators by calling their offices or signing an online petition at PFAW's dedicated website: www.OpposeAshcroft.com. On Tuesday, PFAW delivered over 130,000 petitions from all 50 states to the Senate and is planning to deliver the second installment next week, as tens of thousands of petitions continue to pour in.

The ad is scheduled to appear on January 30 in 11 newspapers: The New York Times, USA Today, the Milwaukee Journal-Sentinel, The (Fargo, ND) Forum, the Bismarck (ND) Tribune, the (Sioux Falls, SD) Argus Leader, the Rapid City (SD) Journal, the Bangor (ME) Daily News; the Portland (ME) Press Herald, the (New Orleans) Times Picayune, and the Hartford Courant. It will also appear in the Thursday, February 1 edition of the twice-weekly Washington, DC paper, Roll Call. Full text of the ad is attached and can be viewed at http://www.OpposeAshcroft.com.

------ Text of Ashcroft ad:

SHOULD A MAN WHO MISREPRESENTS THE FACTS UNDER OATH BE OUR ATTORNEY GENERAL?

John Ashcroft wants to be U.S. Attorney General, the highest law enforcement officer in the land.

But Ashcroft used his sworn testimony before the Judiciary Committee to mislead the Senators. In some cases, he made statements that are directly contradicted by the facts of his record. In others, he used denial or evasion to try to distance himself from extreme actions and statements from his past in an apparent effort to reinvent himself as dedicated only to upholding the law, rather than to any ideological agenda.

What Ashcroft says and what Ashcroft's record shows:

Under oath, Ashcroft said he followed the law and obeyed the courts' orders as Missouri's Attorney General. Transcript of Ashcroft Senate Confirmation Hearings

But during his bitter 10-year legal battle against voluntary school desegregation in St. Louis, a federal court sharply criticized his "continual delay and failure to comply" with court orders and concluded that "the state has, as a matter of deliberate policy, decided to defy the authority of the court." Associated Press, St. Louis Post-Dispatch

Under oath, he said he understood that an Attorney General's role is not to make the laws but to enforce them and implied that he would not use his power as Attorney General to try to take away women's constitutional right to make their own reproductive decisions. Transcript of confirmation hearings

But as Missouri's Attorney General, Ashcroft did not hesitate to push to severely limit that right. In fact, he even testified before Congress to argue for federal legislation that would have had the effect of overturning Roe v. Wade. As a U.S. Senator he sought an abortion ban so sweeping that it would force rape victims to bear their attacker's child and could outlaw birth control pills and IUDs. John Ashcroft now wants the Senators to believe he is ready to put aside these views in favor of enforcing Roe v. Wade and other abortion rulings and laws. Hearings Before Senate Judiciary Subcommittee on S.158 ("Human Life Bill"); St. Louis Post-Dispatch; S.J. Res. 49 ("Human Life Amendment"); S. 2135 ("Human Life Act"); http://www.all.org/activism/pb_basic.htm; Human Events

Under oath, Ashcroft testified about his efforts to defeat the federal court nomination of Judge Ronnie White, the first African American to serve as a Missouri Supreme Court Judge. Ashcroft admitted that he had called White's record "pro-criminal" but said he "did not derogate his background." Transcript of confirmation hearings

But the record of Ashcroft's mean-spirited campaign of distortions against White includes his charge that White would "use his lifetime appointment to push law in a pro-criminal direction, consistent with his own personal political agenda." Congressional Record

Under oath, he said he has not discriminated against gays and did not block James Hormel's confirmation as Ambassador to Luxembourg because Hormel is gay. Transcript of confirmation hearings

But while Ashcroft was waging his campaign against Hormel's confirmation, he was quoted as objecting to Hormel for "promoting a lifestyle" that he said could be "offensive" to people in Luxembourg. News reports show that it was widely acknowledged, even by Republicans, that the opposition to Hormel's confirmation was based on his sexual orientation. Boston Globe; San Diego Union Tribune

John Ashcroft says we can trust him to uphold our fundamental constitutional and civil rights. But with all he's done -- and all that's at stake -- how can we?
