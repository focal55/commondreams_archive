Environmental Groups to Meet with White House
   WASHINGTON - June 1 - The Sierra Club, Natural Resources Defense Council, U.S. Public Interest Research Group and Union of Concerned Scientists will meet with the Administration at the White House on Tuesday, June 5 to discuss a set of energy efficiency, renewable and other cleaner responsible supply options that would assist America in creating and enacting a balanced approach to meeting our energy needs.

The Vice President has stated repeatedly that the Bush energy policy contains 11 of 12 proposals recently outlined by the Sierra Club. Our discussions are intended to identify any areas of agreement and to explore how these initiatives can be pursued without further delay.

We of course recognize that the environmental community and the Administration continue to disagree on major issues. We object to fossil fuel programs that will exacerbate global warming. We oppose plutonium reprocessing and believe that solutions have yet to be found to the waste disposal and safety concerns associated with other nuclear technologies. We oppose drilling in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge and other environmentally critical areas; and we believe that we should strengthen, not weaken, clean air enforcement.

Congressional debate over areas where we disagree may be contentious and prolonged, but we should not delay immediate implementation of any common ground programs we both support. The more we do with efficiency, with renewables, and with other cleaner, responsible fossil fuel proposals, the less our nation will be asked to consider more harmful and risky options.
