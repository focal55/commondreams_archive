If Jeffords Becomes an Independent: Implications for Some Defense and Foreign Policy Issues
  

WASHINGTON - May 23 -

============
Majority Leader:
============
Senator Tom Daschle (D-SD) is Majority Leader rather than Sen. Trent Lott (R-MS)

Significance: If the President decides to support arms control agreements such as the Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty, it will be easier to get those treaties out of committee and onto the Senate floor (although ratification will still be difficult). Controversial Pentagon or State Department nominees may have more difficulty gaining time for full Senate consideration of their nominations.

=====================
Armed Services Committee:
=====================
Senator Carl Levin (D-MI) is Chairman rather than Sen. John Warner (R-VA)

Significance: Probably not much change on military spending levels or for the future of weapons programs (there tends to be bi-partisan support for high military budgets and most weapons), but new hurdles to the Bush Administration's plans to deploy National Missile Defense (i.e., perhaps it will be harder for Bush to win funding this year for constructions of the radar site in Alaska), easier to restore funding for non-proliferationprograms that Bush has cut, somewhat less smooth confirmations for controversial Pentagon nominees, more sympathetic view towards future base closing (BRAC) from Chairman Levin, more likely to hear pro-arms control witnesses and schedule hearings that focus on Democratic priority defense issues.

======================
Foreign Relations Committee:
======================
Sen. Joseph Biden (D-DE) is chairman rather than Sen. Jesse Helms (R-NC)

Significance: Potential rejuvenation of moribund committee, no longer a graveyard of arms control treaties, more likely to hold hearings on major foreign relations issues, more likely to have pro-arms control witnesses on the Anti-Ballistic Missile Treaty, the Comprehensive Test Ban Treaty and other topics, makes a Biden run for President slightly less likely and a Helms retirement slightly more likely.

====================
Appropriations Committee:
====================
Sen. Robert Byrd (D-WV) is chairman rather than Sen. Ted Stevens (R-AK)

Potential significance: Byrd more inclined than Stevens to hike domestic spending at the expense of military spending; he has also been very critical of Pentagon accounting woes, when Chairman Byrd allocates appropriations totals to the various appropriations subcommittees, funding may not be so generous for the Defense Appropriations Subcommittee.

==============================
Defense Appropriations Subcommittee:
==============================
Sen. Daniel Inouye (D-HI) is chairman rather than Sen. Ted Stevens (R-AK)

Significance: Not much, because of similar views of the two Senators, both of whom support high military spending and national missile defense.

====================
Senate Budget Committee:
====================
Sen. Kent Conrad (D-ND) is chairman rather than Sen. Pete Domenici (R-NM)

Significance: Bush budget priorities have more difficulty in the future, the Committee is likely to mark-up a budget resolution next year (this year, the committee failed to produce a budget resolution), somewhat more focus on domestic spending priorities and somewhat less on defense.

==================
Conference committees:
==================
Because Democrats will have a majority of Senate conferees and Republicans a majority of House conferees, the results are likely to be more bipartisan.
