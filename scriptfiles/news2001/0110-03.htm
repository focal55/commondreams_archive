Handgun Control Announces Web Site To Oppose Ashcroft As Attorney General
  WASHINGTON - January 10 - The following was released today by Handgun Control:

-- Handgun Control Announces Web Site To Oppose Ashcroft As Attorney General

-- www.ashcroftandguns.com Details Why Americans Should Be Concerned About Ashcroft's Willingness to Enforce and Defend Nation's Gun Laws

As part of its grassroots effort to defeat John Ashcroft's nomination as Attorney General, the nation's largest gun violence prevention group has launched a re-vamped version of the Web site, www.ashcroftandguns.com. The site, which Handgun Control had set up in 2000 to educate voters about Ashcroft's pro-gun record during his failed Senate re-election campaign, has been revised to address the impact of his extremist positions on the role of Attorney General.

www.ashcroftandguns.com represents the first time in the organization's history that Handgun Control has devoted an entire Web site to the defeat of a nominee for a position in a President's Cabinet. Visitors to the site can read in-depth information on Ashcroft's record, learn why his relationship to the gun lobby and his alarming views on the Second Amendment raise serious doubts about him as Attorney General, and send a letter to their Senators expressing their opposition to the Ashcroft nomination.

"Every American, including every Senator, should visit www.ashcroftandguns.com," said Michael D. Barnes, President of Handgun Control. "Senatorial courtesy is clearly outweighed by this nation's right to an Attorney General who will enforce and defend our gun laws. John Ashcroft should be rejected."

------
Handgun Control, chaired by Sarah Brady, was founded in 1974 by Dr. Mark Borinsky and N.T. "Pete" Shields, two victims of gun violence. Based in Washington, D.C., HCI works with law enforcement, public health, religious, and community groups across the country to strengthen and protect federal, state and local gun control laws, but does not seek to ban all guns. HCI has over 500,000 members and supporters nationwide, making it the nation's largest citizens' gun control lobbying organization. More information about HCI and its sister organization, the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence, can be found on our Web site at www.handguncontrol.org.
