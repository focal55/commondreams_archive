Progressives Organizing For 2002 TANF Reauthorization
Nov. 14 Event To Launch New Grassroots Campaign
   WASHINGTON - November 5 - News Advisory:

Five years after enactment, the legislation known as Welfare Reform faces a crucial test with the current economic downturn. Next year marks Welfare Reform's first Congressional reauthorization -- and this time, low-income families and their allies will not remain silent.

November 14 will mark the start of a movement to repair the gaping holes in our social safety net -- the Make TANF Work! campaign. Leaders from the labor movement, faith community, policy experts and grassroots activists will join to launch a bottom-up movement to bring the voices of America's struggling families and laid-off workers to Washington.
