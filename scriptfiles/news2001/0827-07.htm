Coast-To-Coast Protests to Support 'Democracy Now!'
  BERKELEY, CA - August 27 - Pacifica Radio's most popular show, Democracy Now, has been pulled off the air by Pacifica management, and the show's producers and award-winning host, Amy Goodman, have been suspended without pay. A statement from Goodman's union, the American Federation of Television & Radio Artists, released August 23, reads, "AFTRA has notified Pacifica that it is no longer satisfied that the WBAI studios are a safe and appropriate working environment for the Democracy Now! staff."

In protest against the attacks on Democracy Now, listeners at all five Pacifica radio stations will take to the streets on Tuesday, August 28 in support of the show. "We will not stand by while the Pacifica management tries to get rid of the last progressive national radio show in the United States. Concerned listeners throughout the country will rise up in defense of our show and our radio network," said Andrea Buffa, executive director of Media Alliance in San Francisco. Media Alliance (www.media-alliance.org) and the Pacifica Campaign (www.pacificacampaign.org) put out a call for the protests last week.

At WBAI, the Democracy Now! staff have been physically and verbally attacked by interim station manager Utrice Leid and other WBAI staff members. When Pacifica senior management repeatedly ignored the Democracy Now staff's written requests that something be done about the intolerable atmosphere of intimidation and threats, the DN staff felt they had no choice but to fashion some interim solution. Last week they began producing Democracy Now from Downtown Community Television in Manhattan. But Pacifica has refused to air the shows that are produced at this alternative location.

The Pacifica Foundation has been embattled in recent years as more and more listeners and staff members have accused the organization's leadership of censorship, misuse of funds, and illegal changes to the organization's structure. The situation exploded in the summer of 1999 when Pacifica board members and national managers locked the journalists out of KPFA radio in Berkeley for three weeks using armed security guards. It exploded again in December 2000 when Pacifica locked out journalists and fired long-time staff members at WBAI radio in New York City.

Three separate lawsuits have been filed against Pacifica: by Pacifica local advisory board members, Pacifica listener-sponsors, and five Pacifica national board members. Freelance journalists are on strike against the Pacifica Network News, because Pacifica managers have censored the newscasts and retaliated against journalists who insisted on the editorial integrity of the national news department.

Democracy Now! is Pacifica's most popular and influential show. It has recently broken major stories on a host of controversial issues, including Chevron's role in Nigeria, the Lori Berenson case, and East Timor. But rather than supporting the show and promoting it, Pacifica management has repeatedly attacked the show. In January 2001, Goodman's co-host, Juan Gonzalez, quit the show on-air and announced that he would form the Pacifica Campaign, a national corporate campaign to oust Pacifica Radio's board of directors. Since that time, five of the Pacifica Board members have resigned.
