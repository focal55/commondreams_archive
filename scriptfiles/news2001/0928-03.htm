Afghanistan Crisis Could Involve High Numbers of Child Soldiers
New Report Notes Recruitment in Pakistan's Schools
   WASHINGTON - September 28 - News Advisory:

A report on child soldiers issued today by the Coalition to Stop the Use of Child Soldiers warns that the current crisis may see unprecedented levels of child recruitment and mobilization into military action.

Research conducted by Amnesty International, a member of the coalition, is cited in the report to underscore concern that religious schools in Pakistan have become recruiting grounds for the Taleban. In some cases, the recruitment is involuntary.

Amnesty International USA's Advocacy Director for Asia, T. Kumar, is available for interview and analysis of the use of child soldiers in the region.

Contact Amnesty International at 202-544-0200, ext. 289 or 212-633-4247 to schedule an interview or for further information.
