Congressional Black Caucus Chair Joins Official U.S. Delegation to World Conference Against Racism
   WASHINGTON - August 30 - Congresswoman Eddie Bernice Johnson, Chair of the Congressional Black Caucus, traveled to Durban, South Africa as part of the official U.S. delegation participating in the U.N. World Conference Against Racism, Xenophobia, and Related Forms of Intolerance (WCAR).

"I would like to commend the U.N. for its efforts to eradicate the scourge of racial discrimination worldwide by holding this conference in Durban, South Africa," said Congresswoman Johnson. "This conference presents a wonderful opportunity for the leaders of the world to come together all on one accord to acknowledge the perils of racism and to plan its abolishment."

After much deliberation, President Bush's administration decided to send a State Department delegation, which in addition to Congresswoman Johnson, includes Congressman Tom Lantos (D-CA) and is headed by Michael Southwick, a deputy assistant secretary in the Bureau of International Organizations.

"The United States role in the international community is that of leadership and we impart tremendous influence on a variety of issues," said Congresswoman Johnson. "It is incumbent on the United States to use its power and resources to ensure that this World Conference and any forum that seeks to address the issue of racism achieves its objectives to end this egregious violation here and abroad."

Congresswoman Johnson will join several of her Congressional Black Caucus colleagues who have also traveled to Durban to participate in WCAR.

"Since its inception, the Congressional Black Caucus has championed equality for all through historic struggles for civil rights," said Congresswoman Johnson. "And, in keeping with that tradition, we will remain steadfast in our charge to fearlessly step out and provide leadership in the fight to encourage racial understanding both in America and abroad."

The conference will begin Friday, August 31, 2001 and will end Friday, September 7. Other Congressional Black Caucus Members attending the conference include Rep. John Conyers (D-MI), Rep. Maxine Waters (D-CA), Rep. Sheila Jackson Lee (D-TX), Rep. Juanita Millender-McDonald (D-CA), Rep. Donna Christen-Christensen (D-VI), Rep. Barbara Lee (D-CA) and Rep. DianeWatson (D-CA).
