Shield of Dreams:
Passes Test, But Gets 'Incomplete for Course
   WASHINGTON - July 15 - The National Missile Defense that President George W. Bush's proposes is nothing but a "Shield of Dreams." The current system technology has now passed only two out of four tests. It will be a decade before the U.S. can be confident that Missile Defense will work effectively and reliably in real world conditions.

John Isaacs, of the Council for a Livable World Education Fund, said, "Even factoring in tonight's result, the system still receives an incomplete grade on its report card. Democrats and Republicans in Congress should be highly skeptical of the cost of missile defense in light of its poor performance."

"It is not worth violating a treaty that for thirty years has kept the nuclear peace for a missile shield that doesn't work half the time," continued Mr. Isaacs. "We should not continue to throw money at this 'Shield of Dreams.' We need to focus our limited dollars on other priorities like Social Security and Medicare, prescription drugs and better pay for the military."
