Americans Oppose Personal Social Security Accounts
  WASHINGTON - February 7 - The National Council of Women's Organizations (NCWO) disputes new polling released today claiming a majority of Americans support Individual Accounts carved-out of Social Security. "Polls suggesting massive public support leave out the fine print: taking money out of the system to fund private accounts means cuts in guaranteed benefits for nearly all Americans, increases in the retirement age, and less security for spouses and widows," according to Heidi Hartmann, Ph.D, chair of the Women & Social Security Task Force of the National Council of Women's Organizations.

NCWO, in partnership with seven of its national member organizations, will convene a Summit on Women and Social Security on Feb. 8 at 8:30 a.m. at the Wyndham Washington D.C. (1400 M St., NW) to launch a coordinated campaign to oppose Social Security privatization.

"These kind of misleading polls build on the obvious public support for increasing their wealth in retirement while ignoring the negative impact private accounts will have on the current system" added Hartmann. "You can't maintain the current inflation-adjusted guaranteed benefits that can't be outlived while at the same time taking money out the of system to fund private accounts. The math doesn't add up."

Polling released to the public over the past year shows a dramatic decline in support for private accounts once the trade-off is made clear. According to a national poll, released in September 2000 by AARP, "Support for carve-outs drops sharply when two potential mitigating factors are introduced. If the approach brings with it a reduction in guaranteed Social Security benefits or the possibility of a new government agency, many Americans withdraw their support (55 percent and 58 percent, respectively)." The details of the poll can be found at http://research.aarp.org/econ/sssurvey_1.html

Beginning tomorrow morning, more than 300 leaders of grassroots organizations representing all 50 states will participate in the two days of meetings, training workshops, and Congressional office visits. The attendees will deliver a unified message to Capitol Hill: Individual Accounts carved-out of Social Security will hurt women and families. The conference has been called because national women's groups are concerned that privatization would necessarily jeopardize the guarantee of lifetime, inflation-adjusted benefits that Social Security now provides, and because rates of poverty among older women are already unacceptably high.

The National Council of Women's Organization's (NCWO) is a non-partisan, non-profit coalition of more than 115 women's organizations across the nation collectively representing over six million women. For years, NCWO has convened the leadership of major women's organizations dedicated to focusing on national issues and public policy agendas affecting women. In 1998, NCWO formed the Women & Social Security Project and launched a national education campaign to highlight Social Security's importance to women.
