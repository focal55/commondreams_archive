Bush Tax Cut Plan Catering To Special Interests
   WASHINGTON - February 8 - Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger warned about the influence of high-volume high-profit donors as the Bush tax plan was transmitted to Congress this morning.

"With the current campaign finance system, big money is likely to equal big tax cuts," said Harshbarger. "With all the money special interests poured into the fall campaigns, now is the time to look for a return on their investment. They have paid, now they will play. No one has seen specifics in the tax plan yet, but look out."

The Wall Street Journal today reported President Bush had a "closed-door White House lunch" yesterday to discuss his tax cuts with "generous donors" - 20 industry executives who, according to the Wall Street Journal, contributed more than $14 million to GOP causes in the last campaign cycle.
