Wellstone, Bipartisan Members Push for U.S. to Censure China at UN Human Rights Commission
   WASHINGTON - February 14 - Senators Paul Wellstone (D-MN), Tim Hutchinson (R-AR), Robert Torricelli (D-NJ), Foreign Relations Committee Chairman Jesse Helms (R-NC), Mark Dayton (D-MN), Susan Collins (R-ME), and Robert Smith (R-NH) today introduced a Senate resolution calling on the U.S. government to sponsor a resolution to censure China at the United Nations Human Rights Commission meeting in Geneva next month. A companion resolution was also introduced in the House of Representatives today. At a bipartisan press conference to announce the campaign to promote the U.S. resolution, members of the House and Senate emphasized that China’s dismal record in the area of human rights deserves international scrutiny.

“The Bush Administration is now considering whether to propose a resolution at Geneva condemning China’s human rights abuses. We strongly urge it to do so, and to work vigorously to enlist world support for the resolution. A firm statement from the Senate and the House will help the Administration to establish human rights as a key priority in our international diplomacy and to continue America’s record of leadership at the U.N. Human Rights Commission,” said Wellstone.

The United Nations Human Rights Commission, which meets annually in Geneva, is the only international body responsible for monitoring and reporting on the human rights conditions of all states. Resolutions offered at the Commission both highlight human rights violations and pressure governments to correct them. “China continues to crack down on any perceived threats to the Communist Party, and Chinese authorities have stepped up deplorable human rights abuses, attacking pro-democracy activists, ethnic minority groups, labor activists, and others attempting to exercise free expression. Minority religious groups particularly have been targeted of late, with Chinese authorities destroying places of worship and incarcerating people of faith. The United States cannot be silent, but must lead international condemnation of China’s terrible abuse of its citizens’ fundamental human rights,” said Wellstone.
