PFAW To Announce 13-State Gay Rights Push
  WASHINGTON - February 20 - With anti-gay activity at record levels around the country, People For the American Way will announce plans to step up efforts to protect Americans against hate crimes and discrimination based on sexual orientation.

At a news conference at its Washington, D.C., headquarters, PFAW officials will outline plans to target legislative efforts to 13 states where gay rights bills are in the works or have already been introduced, enlisting PFAW members and activists and working in coalition with other gay rights groups to help build support for these bills.

The PFAW news conference coincides with the release by People For the American Way Foundation of Hostile Climate 2000, the group's annual publication reporting on anti-gay incidents around the country. (For more on the publication, go to http://www.pfaw.org and follow the links for a news release and other information about this year's report.)
