D.C. Police Arrest Greenpeace Activists Anchored to the Interior Building During Norton Confirmation Sessions
   WASHINGTON - January 18 - The D.C. police department arrested three Greenpeace activists today. Two of the activists were anchored to the Interior Building for half an hour, carrying a banner reading, "Bush and Norton, Our Land, Not Oil Land." The police seized the activists when they rappelled down from the building, and took the banner soon after. The international environmental group was protesting Interior Secretary nominee Gail Norton’s anti-environmental record, which includes plans to drill for oil in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge. Calling for president-elect Bush to adopt emergency measures to phase in renewable energy, Greenpeace announced that today’s protest is just the beginning of an intensified campaign to safeguard public lands and the environmental values of the country.

"The police can cut down our banner but we are going to continue to carry our message loud and clear to the Bush Administration and to the oil industry," said John Passacantando, Executive Director of Greenpeace. "America needs to be weaned off of its dangerous dependence on fossil fuel. The technology for viable renewable energy is sitting right under our noses, waiting for a strong commitment from the U.S. government and industry."

Today’s action took place on the first day of Norton’s confirmation hearings and just one day before the official release of the third scientific assessment report from the world’s top climate scientists, predicting global warming increases twice as large as these same scientists predicted five years ago. The report, currently being reviewed by more than 150 governments in Shanghai, China, further projects more extreme weather patterns, floods, droughts, species extinction and human health hazards as a consequence of fossil fuels use such as oil, coal and gas.

Although reserves in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge would supply less than a year’s worth of fuel, Norton has repeatedly gone on record, advocating opening up these pristine lands for drilling and exploration, a move that more than 60% of Americans oppose. (Poll conducted by the League of Conservation Voters Education Fund)

"Bush’s current energy plan is not only a waste of energy but a threat to the planet," said Melanie Duchin, Climate Campaigner, Greenpeace Alaska. "We don’t need oil interests in the White House, we need a leader with a real energy plan to begin emergency initiatives towards a fossil free future."
