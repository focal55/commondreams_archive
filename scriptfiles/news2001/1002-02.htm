Washington Post Columnist Mary McGrory, Environmental Leaders In Congress To Be Honored At LCV's 13th Annual Washington, D.C. Dinner
 

WASHINGTON - October 2 - News Advisory:

The League of Conservation Voters (LCV) will honor Pulitzer Prize-winning Washington Post columnist Mary McGrory for her commentary on the environment and public affairs at its 13th annual Washington, D.C. dinner, to be held at the Mayflower Hotel on Oct. 2, 2001. Sen. Lincoln Chafee (R-R.I.) and Rep. John Lewis (D-Ga.) will also receive awards for their leadership on environmental policy. McGrory is scheduled to speak, along with LCV Chairman Theodore Roosevelt IV, LCV President Deb Callahan, Dinner Co-Chairs Sen. Barbara Boxer (D-Calif.) and Sen. James Jeffords (I-Vt.), Chafee and Lewis, and former White House Chief of Staff and LCV Board Member John D. Podesta. Actor and environmental advocate Robert Redford, who was scheduled to speak, will instead appear at LCV's 12th annual New York Dinner which will be held on Wednesday, Nov. 7, 2001.

What:
13th Annual LCV Washington, D.C. Dinner

Who:
Mary McGrory, Washington Post columnist; Sen. Lincoln Chafee; Sen. Barbara Boxer; Sen. James Jeffords; Rep. John Lewis; LCV Chairman Theodore Roosevelt IV; LCV President Deb Callahan; LCV Board Member John D. Podesta

When:
Reception, 6:30 p.m.; Dinner, 7:30 p.m.; Tuesday, Oct. 2, 2001

Where:
Renaissance Mayflower Hotel, 1127 Connecticut Avenue, N.W.

The non-profit League of Conservation Voters is the non-partisan political voice of the national environmental community. LCV's goal is to protect the environment through political action by helping to elect a pro-conservation majority to Congress and holding federal elected officials accountable for their environmental actions. LCV publishes the National Environmental Scorecard, which rates each member of Congress based on the key environmental votes of the year, as selected by experts from over two dozen mainstream environmental and conservation groups.
