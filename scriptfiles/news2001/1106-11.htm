Sierra Club and Charter Communications Announce Success of Energy Conservation Campaign
Energy Conservation PSAs featuring Backstreet Boy Kevin Richardson and actor/director Harry Shearer Run Over 33,000 Times, at Value of $1 Million
   SAN FRANCISCO - November 7 - The Sierra Club announced the success of its partnership with Charter Communications to provide the public with simple ways to use less energy. To communicate with the public, Charter Communications has run two public service announcements at no charge as a service to communities throughout its systems in California. Since mid-August, the conservation-oriented ads have run approximately 33,000 times at a value of $1 million.

"We are running these public service announcements to help the public understand that it is easy to make a difference during our nation's energy shortages," said Carl Pope, Executive Director of the Sierra Club. "We are thrilled that Backstreet Boy Kevin Richardson, actor/director Harry Shearer and Charter Communications are joining us in this effort."

Backstreet Boy Richardson, who also runs Just Within Reach: An Earth Foundation, is featured encouraging residents to subscribe to green energy and to use clean, renewable, energy resources such as solar power.

Shearer, well known as the voice of Montgomery Burns and Ned Flanders on the Simpsons and for his portrayal as bassist Derek Small in This is Spinal Tap, is featured installing energy efficient lightbulbs and demonstrating other energy saving tips. "The images of Shearer installing compact fluorescent lightbulbs and unplugging power strips shows in a concrete way that it is simple to use energy more efficiently," said Pope.

"Charter Communications is proud to offer our medium to help pass along and promote important energy-saving tips during today's energy crisis," said Tom Schaeffer, Senior Vice President of Operations for Charter Communications Western Region. "Partnering with the Sierra Club gives us an opportunity to work with an organization that has a history of making a difference and whose messages need to be heard by a broader audience."

"The Sierra Club believes that if we make the right energy choices today, Americans can have cleaner air, less global warming pollution, beautiful public lands and reasonably priced power far into the future," said Pope. "We need a balanced energy plan that gives us safer, cleaner, cheaper and more efficient energy solutions. These ads begin to point us in that direction."

The Sierra Club, with more than 700,000 members nationwide, was founded in 1892. It is the nation's oldest and largest grassroots environmental organization. More information about the Sierra Club can be found at http://www.sierraclub.org

Charter Communications, a Wired World company, is among the nation's largest broadband communications companies, currently serving some 7 million customers in 40 states. In California, Charter Communications serves more than 745,000 customers. More information about Charter can be found at www.charter.com.
