Progressive Caucus Demands That Democrats Unify to Halt Bankruptcy Legislation
   WASHINGTON - February 27 - On Wednesday, Feb. 28th at 2pm in the House Radio and TV Gallery at the U.S. Capitol, Rep. Jerrold Nadler and members of the Progressive Caucus will detail how proposed bankruptcy legislation would hurt virtually all the traditionally Democratic-voting constituencies. The members of the Progressive Caucus will also make an appeal to their fellow Democrats not to remain silent on the legislation.

The bankruptcy legislation in question (HR 333) is virtually identical to the Bankruptcy Reform Conference Report opposed by a wide array of groups, as well as newspaper editorial boards last year, and was eventually pocket vetoed by President Clinton. Among the scores of groups that oppose the legislation are the AFL-CIO, Consumers Union, Mothers Against Drunk Driving, National Council of Senior Citizens, National Council for Preservation of Social Security and Medicare, National Organization for Women, Public Citizen, and the National Conference of Bankruptcy Judges.


WHO: Rep. Jerrold Nadler (D-NY), Rep. Dennis Kucinich (D-OH, Caucus Chair), Rep. Bernie Sanders (I-VT) and Members of the Progressive Caucus
WHAT: Press Conference Urging Democratic Opposition on Bankruptcy Legislation
WHERE: House Radio and TV Gallery
WHEN: 2pm, Wednesday, Fed 28th
