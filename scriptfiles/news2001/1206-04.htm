Gun Industry Uses Sept 11 To Sell Weapons
Gun Industry Watch to Launch Counter-Campaign
  WASHINGTON - December 6 - The Alliance for Justice today joined members of Congress to denounce efforts by the gun lobby and gun manufacturers to use the events of September 11 to increase gun ownership in America. Alliance for Justice's student-led Gun Industry Watch will lead a campaign to counter these efforts. "It's time the gun industry stop exploiting tragedy to sell guns," said Alliance for Justice President Nan Aron. "Flooding our streets with deadly weapons will not honor the memory of the victims of September 11, it will only create new victims of senseless violence."

Alliance for Justice's Gun Industry Watch kicked-off a billboard campaign in Los Angeles to counter similar advertisements run by the California Rifle and Pistol Association (NRA affiliate) urging people to arm themselves for safety. The billboard reads: "Society is safer when criminals don't know who's armed." When questioned about the appropriateness and timing of the message, a spokesman said it was "truer than ever" and "in light of what happened in New York City, people have stopped taking for granted their own security."

"We condemn the efforts of the gun lobby and gun makers to exploit people's fears and prejudices to increase gun ownership," said Aron. "It was this blatant attempt to exploit people's anxiety and fear that first convinced us to launch a counter-campaign educating the public as to the dangers of bringing a gun into the home."

Gun Industry Watch research found that gun manufacturers are also using the events of September 11 to sell weapons. Three examples offered at today's press conference:

-- Beretta is selling the "United We Stand" pistol with a percentage of proceeds to go to victims of September 11. The company lists the NYPD and the Survivors Fund of the National Capital Region as the charities they will donate money to.

-- Ithaca Gun is selling a "Homeland Security" model rifle. The company gives a patriotic sales pitch to consumers: "In our current time of national need Ithaca Gun is ready to meet the challenge...In every respect, these new Homeland Security Model shotguns are up to the demanding tasks which lay before us as a nation."

-- Finally, a gun manufacturer called Tromix is publicizing a soon-to-be-released 50 caliber rifle nicknamed the "Turban Chaser". The announcement on the company's website is accompanied by an American flag design.

"Bigotry and exploitation are not American values," added Aron. "And there's nothing patriotic about flooding our streets with lethal weapons."

Gun Industry Watch's student network will also be educating the public about the dangers of guns in the home through letter writing campaigns, protests, op-eds, leafleting, and other activities.

Joining Aron at today's press conference were Reps. Henry Waxman (D-Calif.) and Carolyn McCarthy (D-N.Y.).
