Wellstone Fought Robin Hood-In-Reverse Tax Cuts
Senate Passes Republican Tax Cut Skewed to the Wealthiest
  

WASHINGTON - May 23 - U.S. Senator Paul Wellstone (D-MN) today voted against the Republican tax cut bill (H.R. 1836) following four days of vigorous opposition on the Senate floor. Wellstone, who supports balanced tax relief, said the $1.35 trillion 11-year GOP tax cut is fiscally irresponsible, will eliminate the possibility for future investments in national priorities, and fails the basic test of fairness. He especially warned that the measure opens the door to the return of Reagan-era deficits.

“I believe in tax cuts that reward work, not wealth. Today the Senate went in the opposite direction with this bill. Fully one-third of the benefits of this tax cut will go to the wealthiest one percent of Americans, while only 16 percent of the benefits go to those making $44,000 or less, or 60% of the American people. Where is the fairness in that kind of distribution? These are Robin Hood-in-reverse tax cuts,” Wellstone said.

Americans who earn between $27,000 and $44,000 will get an average tax cut of merely $596. The wealthiest Americans, with an average income of over $900,000, will see an average cut of $44,536. Ten million children (1 in 7 children) live in families that will see no benefit from the legislation because the parents do not earn enough income to qualify for the tax cuts in the bill.

“I remember well the era of deficits. In my ten years in the Senate, something I have learned is that, as a nation, we do not want to return to the era of fiscally irresponsible deficit spending. We know how hard it is to bring them down once deficits are in place. In the outyears, as millions of Baby Boomers prepare to retire, I fear that this tax cut will decimate funding for the Medicare and Social Security on which they will depend,” said Wellstone.

“During debate on this bill, I vocally supported numerous amendments that promised significant tax cuts for working Americans. All were defeated by the Republican majority, sadly, which instead drove through a tax cut lacking in all proportion and balance. The real price to the country of this tax cut, which overwhelmingly benefits the wealthiest, is not the $1.35 trillion as advertised. Rather, when fixes to the Alternate Minimum Tax and interest costs are added in, the real cost will be well over $2 trillion,” Wellstone said.

“As a nation, this tax cut will make it impossible for us to live up to our commitments to invest in our children, to quality education, to affordable health care, and to establishing an affordable Medicare prescription drug benefit for America’s seniors. The Senate made a grave mistake today that may be with us for years to come,” said Wellstone.
