Outsourcing the Drug War in Colombia
CorpWatch releases a State Department contract on its website as part of a special investigative report
   SAN FRANCISCO - May 24 - CorpWatch today released an investigative report, "DynCorp In Colombia: Outsourcing the Drug War," detailing operations of hundreds of contract employees on the frontlines of the drug war in the Andes. If these contractors get caught in an embarrassing crime, or are killed, the U.S. government can deny responsibility for their actions. What's more only a select few in Congress know of their activities and their operations are not subject to public scrutiny or accountability.

By far the largest U.S. contractor in Latin America is DynCorp, headquartered in Reston, Virginia near the CIA, and Pentagon. The company's website promotes it as an Internet technology corporation. Once you dig a little deeper, it becomes clear that this is no ordinary high-tech start up. In fact, DynCorp is on the frontlines of the war on drugs in Colombia.

Investigative reporter Jeremy Bigwood uncovers DynCorp's operations in Colombia for CorpWatch, in this special report:
http://www.corpwatch.org/issues/military/featured/2001/dyncorp.html

As part of this investigation CorpWatch also acquired a copy of DynCorp's $600 million contract with the State Department and posted it on our website:
