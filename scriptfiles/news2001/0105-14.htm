Statement By Earthjustice Legal Defense Fund Executive Director Buck Parker On New Forest Service Roadless Policy
   SAN FRANCISCO - January 5 - "President Clinton has today done more to protect Americans' heritage of public lands than any president since Teddy Roosevelt. The pristine national forest lands at stake provide us with a living legacy of wild forests and wildlife that have been destroyed elsewhere in our country and in much of the world.

"By designating nearly 60 million acres of wild national forests as off limits to further road building and to most logging and oil, gas and mineral development, President Clinton has moved to ensure that these lands and the fish and wildlife they support are preserved for future generations.

"We at Earthjustice are particularly pleased that the Administration has heeded the voices of hundreds of thousands of citizens and has moved to protect roughly nine million acres of Alaska's Tongass National Forest. Now, much of this unique, old-growth rainforest will remain as wild in the future as it is today.

"Some lawmakers, including Rep Jim Hansen of Utah and Sen. Frank Murkowski of Alaska, have said that they will fight the President's actions in court and on Capitol Hill. We hope that common sense will win out and that the fact that this is one of the most publicly supported forest initiatives ever with almost two million comments being registered will prevent a partisan attack on the new policy. If necessary, however, Earthjustice is fully prepared to defend it in any court in the land and on Capitol Hill. Earthjustice has fought to protect roadless areas throughout its 30-year history, and we will continue to fight to ensure that this forest legacy is both upheld and followed."
