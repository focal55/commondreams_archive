More Power Plant Pollution - Coming Soon to Lungs Near You?
News Conference to Highlight How Americans Want Cleaner Energy -- Not Dirtier Air, Mother of Asthmatic Child and Physician Will Speak Out on Weakening the Clean Air Act
  

WASHINGTON - July 5 -

What: The Bush Administration is launching an effort to weaken the Clean Air Act pollution controls -- a move that is unnecessary and bad for Americans' lungs. Utility companies want to gut the Clean Air Act by changing the law so that they can continue to spew high levels of pollution into our air. Beginning July 10, the Bush Administration will hold hearings to consider weakening or suspending a Clean Air Act requirement called New Source Review.

"New Source Review" (NSR) requires companies when expanding or modifying their facilities to either offset any pollution increases with reductions in other sources at the same plant site or obtain a permit demonstrating that best available pollution control technology has been installed. But under the Bush Administration these protections would be weakened or suspended. The EPA is now accepting comments on this issue and four public hearings are scheduled around the country, beginning with one in Cincinnati Ohio on July 10.

The news conference will highlight how effective the New Source Review has been in protecting people from air pollution, and will spotlight other ways to meet our energy needs without sacrificing our children's lungs.

Who: Brett Hulsey, Sierra Club Senior Midwest Representative , Glen Brand, Sierra Club Associate Midwest Representative, Dr. George Leikauf, College of Medicine, University of Cincinnati, Jocelyn van Toor, parent of asthmatic child, Minneapolis resident
