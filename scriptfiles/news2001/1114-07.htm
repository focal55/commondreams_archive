NOW Activists to Picket Senate Office Buildings
Demand Real Economic Stimulus Plan
  WASHINGTON - November 14 - "Feminists will not stand by as ultraconservative lawmakers dole out economic pork to big business and the wealthy while poor women struggle to feed their families," said NOW President Kim Gandy. "Low-income families and laid-off workers need a real economic stimulus plan from Congress, not a financial slap in the face."

Gandy will participate in an informational picket outside the Dirksen Senate Office Building on Thursday afternoon.

Who: NOW President Kim Gandy and other activists

What: Action for Economic Justice

When: Thursday, November 15, 12:30 PM

Where: Outside Dirksen Senate Office Building
Corner of First Street and Delaware Avenue, NE
Washington, D.C.

"If Congress really wants to stimulate spending, then it needs to put resources into the hands of low-income and laid-off workers who, by necessity, will put their money right back into the economy -- and they'll be feeding their families," Gandy said. "Anything less will fail -- and conservatives in Washington who cater to the interests of big business and neglect poor families will be responsible."

For more information or to schedule and interview with Gandy, please contact NOW's Media Relations Office at 202-628-8669, ext. 116.
