Planned Parenthood Opposes Hutchinson-Vitter Legislation Restricting Access To Early Option Abortion Pill
Statement by Gloria Feldt, President
Planned Parenthood Federation of America
  WASHINGTON - February 6 - The reproductive rights and health of American women are being threatened by a handful of legislators who want their personal ideology to replace the judgment of doctors and scientists.

American women and their health care providers had been asking for mifepristone for years because of the clear benefits it offers as a health care option. The FDA approved the early option pill because extensive clinical trials and use in other countries proved beyond doubt that the drug is very safe and very effective. The decision was based on science, plain and simple.

But some members of Congress don't like science that contradicts their ideology. They don't like medicine when it gives women more options instead of taking options away. And when they don't like something, they think their role as legislators gives them the right to interfere with the work scientists, doctors, other health care providers, and anyone else trying to use their knowledge and expertise to serve the people of this society.

Today, they are seeking to restrict access to mifepristone. Tomorrow, it will be all forms of abortion. The day after that, contraception, family planning services, medically accurate sex education, and who knows what else. Enough is enough. It's time to let the scientists, the healers, the professional health care providers do their jobs, and let the legislators stick to theirs.

Planned Parenthood Federation of America is the world's largest and most trusted voluntary family planning organization. It comprises 127 affiliates that operate 875 health centers nationwide, which serve nearly five million Americans annually.
