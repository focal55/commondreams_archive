'Billionaires' To Celebrate Inauguration
'After Buying A President, We're Ready To Cash In'
  WASHINGTON - January 18 - As thousands gather for George W. Bush's inauguration, Billionaires for Bush and More, a coalition of the super-rich, will come together Jan. 20 in Washington, D.C., to celebrate their successful purchase of a president and to remind the incoming administration of the payback they expect.

To celebrate, Billionaires for Bush and More will hold their own inaugural gala and rally Saturday at 9:30 a.m. in Dupont Circle, outside of the north entrance to the Metro Station. After the rally, they will begin their Donor's March to the inaugural parade route.

At the Republican and Democratic conventions in Philadelphia and LA, the Billionaires were out in full force, holding Million Billionaire Marches to demonstrate their influence over the candidates. They were featured in the Washington Post, LA Times, the BBC, Time Magazine, CNN, CBS Evening News, Fox News, and the Associated Press.

"We're thrilled to be here today to install a president who will serve the interests of the outrageously wealthy, said Robert Baron, co-chair of Billionaires for Bush and More. "This election proved that people don't vote, money does!"

"But we are not only in town to celebrate," added co-chair Millie O'Nair. "The President is simply an investment, and now it's time to cash in. We see vast opportunities for profit and increased inequality over the next four years. We are the Billionaires for Bush and More: more tax cuts for the rich, more corporate welfare, more deregulation, more power!"

She added, "Our first priority is to abolish the estate tax. How can we cultivate a sense of entitlement in our children if we can't promise them massive untaxed inheritances?"

Representatives of Billionaires for Bush and More are available for interviews. There will be many humorous photo opportunities at the rally. More information about the organization is available at the website, http://www.billionairesforbushorgore.com

Billionaires for Bush and More is a non-partisan spoof created by United for a Fair Economy to draw attention to the connection between the campaign finance system and growing economic inequality.
