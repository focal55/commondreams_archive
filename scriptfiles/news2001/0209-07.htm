Bush's Plan to Weigh Deep Cuts in Nuclear Weapons is Long Overdue but Ignores Spread of Nuclear Weapons
  WASHINGTON - February 9 - Nuclear Control Institute today released the following statement by NCI President Paul Leventhal:

“President Bush’s decision to consider deep, unilateral cuts in nuclear warheads and missiles is all to the good because the Cold War is over and we need to address present-day threats.

“The biggest threat today is the spread of nuclear weapons and the likelihood that nuclear attacks on the United States will be delivered by boat or by truck rather than ballistic missile.

“Buildup of a ballistic missile defense still leaves us vulnerable to this low-tech threat and might provoke other nations, especially China, to build up their nuclear arsenals.

“Ironically, deep cuts in nuclear weapons raises another danger: what is to become of the plutonium removed from retired warheads?

“The present U.S.-Russian plan is to turn most of it into fuel for commercial power reactors. But if terrorists or radical states get their hands on this plutonium, it can be made into atomic bombs and used against us.

“This is a very big danger, especially in Russia where security measures are notoriously bad. The better course is to directly dispose of warhead plutonium by mixing it with highly radioactive waste and burying it deep in the earth. Processing, transporting and storing it for use in reactors makes it more vulnerable to theft.

“There is already more plutonium in civilian nuclear power programs than in military programs in the world. This has been a neglected area of U.S. non-proliferation policy for decades, and there is still no sign that President Bush is prepared to do anything about it.”
