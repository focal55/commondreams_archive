Putting September 11 in Perspective
AFSC Holds Teach-In on Friday, Oct 19
   PHILADELPHIA - October 15 - Putting September 11 in Perspective will be held Friday, October 19 at Friends Center, 1501 Cherry Street, Philadelphia, PA from noon to 7:00 p.m. The event is sponsored and organized by the Asia International Area Working Group of the American Friends Service Committee (AFSC) and will feature presentations, workshops, videos, and interactive discussion.

"This is an opportunity to explore what the September 11 tragedies and subsequent events mean for our lives, our communities and our work," said organizer Ed Nakawatase of the AFSC Community Relations Unit. "This event will help all of us to work together to understand what happened, why it happened, and how we can respond as activists, as people of faith and people of conscience."

The day will begin at noon with keynote addresses by former Wharton Business School professor Ed Herman, author of The Global Media: The Missionaries of Capitalism, which explores the impact of commercial television on globalization. Other speakers include Phyllis Bennis, Middle East analyst for the Institute for Policy Studies, immigration lawyer and activist Sara Al Fadi, Temple University Law professor David Kairys, Villanova Political Science professor Ann Lesch, and Nubar Hovsepian of the University of Pennsylvania Middle East Center.

Workshop topics will include:

  • Local organizing and humanitarian initiatives for Afghanistan
  • Continuing the Gulf War? Patterns and realities of US intervention in the Middle East/Central Asia region
  • Deconstructing mass media - tools for practical organizing
  • Changing Hearts/Changing Minds: Communicating progressive politics
  • National security, police state/brutality, immigration and civil liberties
  • Youth, militarization and communities of color
  • How have recent events effected the Sikh community?
  • September 11 and the Middle East Peace Process

At 5:30 an open discussion Beyond Peace: Working to create a new movement will be held,

facilitated by Asian Americans United, AWOL, which is a youth organization for conscientious objectors; Jewish Mobilization for a Just Peace and the Nationalities Service Center.

The program is free and open to the public.

Founded in 1917 to provide conscientious objectors with an opportunity to aid civilian victims during World War I, AFSC is grounded in Quaker beliefs respecting the dignity and worth of every person. AFSC has programs in the United States, and in Africa, Asia, Latin America, and the Middle East that focus on issues related to economic justice, peace-building and demilitarization, social justice, and youth. In 1947, the AFSC and the British Friends Service Council received the Nobel Peace Prize on behalf of the Religious Society of Friends, for humanitarian service, work for reconciliation, and the spirit in which these were carried out.

For more information on the American Friends Service Committee, please visit our web site at www.afsc.org.

The American Friends Service Committee is a Quaker organization that includes people of various faiths who are committed to social justice, peace and humanitarian service. Its work is based on the belief in the worth of every person and faith in the power of love to overcome violence and injustice.
