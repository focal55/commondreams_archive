Julia Butterfly Hill, Members Of Congress, Environmental Leaders To Release '10 Most Endangered National Forests' Report
   WASHINGTON - September 7 - News Advisory:

What:
Julia Butterfly Hill, members Of Congress, environmental leaders to release "10 Most Endangered National Forests" report

National Forest Protection Alliance to challenge Bush Administration to end Logging on national forests

Who:
National Forest Protection Alliance & Allied Groups

When:
Tuesday, Sept. 11 at 11 a.m.

Where:
House Triangle (southeast of House Chambers at the U.S. Capitol)
(In case of rain: Cannon 122)

Details:
The National Forest Protection Alliance (NFPA) will release its new report, "America's 10 Most Endangered National Forests" to the national media on Tuesday, Sept. 11 at 11 a.m. at the U.S. Capitol. The report, which paints a grim ecological picture, documents the timber industry's systematic destruction of America's national forests by highlighting the many threats posed from the commercial logging program.

Scheduled attendees are: Rep. Cynthia McKinney (D-Ga.); Rep. Jim Leach (R-Iowa); Julia Butterfly Hill (world-renown forest activist); Rabbi Warren Stone (co-chair of the Religious Campaign for Forest Conservation); Brock Evans (executive director of the Endangered Species Coalition); and representatives from NFPA.

The groups will discuss specific threats to the 10 national forests outlined in the report and demand an end to the Bush Administration's pro-timber, anti-public lands agenda. They will also provide an update on legislation designed to end the commercial logging program -- the National Forest Protection & Restoration Act (H.R. 1494) -- which currently enjoys the support of 103 Members of Congress.

Following the press conference, a rally will be held at 12:30 p.m. at the USFS headquarters, located on the corner of 14th & Independence (red brick building). A delegation will then hand deliver a copy of the report to USFS Chief Dale Bosworth.
