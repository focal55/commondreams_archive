New Report on Internet Governance Released
  

WASHINGTON - August 31 - Common Cause has been teaming up with the Center for Democracy and Technology and academic and technology leaders from around the globe in studying the governance process for ICANN -- the global organization charged with managing the Internet's domain name system and other core technical functions.

Today this collaboration, the NGO and Academic ICANN Study (NAIS), is releasing the definitive study of the structure set up for global public participation in ICANN, the Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers. In 2000 ICANN held an unprecedented global online election for five seats on its board of directors. The NAIS project has been funded by a grant from the Markle Foundation.

"Common Cause has been pleased to bring our perspective and expertise on issues of government openness and accountability to the NAIS project," Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger said. "If ICANN is going to have the credibility and legitimacy it needs to carry out its mission, it should provide for opportunities for broad public participation in the decision making process, and for public representation on the board. We hope ICANN implements the NAIS recommendations."

The full report is available at commoncause.org/icann. For more information, contact the Common Cause Press Office at 202/736-5770.
