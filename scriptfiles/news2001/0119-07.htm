Inaugural Protesters Finalize Plans for Jan. 20th
   WASHINGTON - January 19 - The inauguration of George W. Bush will be heavily attended by protesters as well as by loyal spectators. Listed below are various protest actions being finalized for tomorrow, January 20th. Confirmed times and places for these events are as follows:

Justice Action Movement - Issues: System-wide injustice, electoral reform, corporate influence in politics. Group will meet at Metro Center, 12th & G at 8:30 a.m. And at the "VoterMarch Rally" in Dupont Circle, 10 a.m. Michael Moore and Granny D will attend. JAM will march to Inaugural Parade Route at 11 a.m.

International Action Center - Issue: Death penalty. 14th St. & Pennsylvania Ave. NW; meet at 10 a.m.

National Organization for Women - Issue: Women's rights. Navy Memorial, Pennsylvania Ave. between 8th & 9th Streets NW, (Navy/Archives Metro, Yellow/Green Line); 11:30 a.m.-2:30 p.m.

"Shadow Inauguration" with Rev. Al Sharpton and the Green Party. Issues: Voter disenfranchisement, racial profiling; Stanton Park/Capitol Hill - meet at10 a.m. to march to the Supreme Court.

Black Panthers/Howard University Students - Issues: Disenfranchisement of black voters, anti-drug war, racial profiling; 14th & U Streets NW, meet at 9 a.m., march to 15th St. NW and Pershing Square.

JAM Counter-Inaugural Balls. For tickets, call 202-462-6459 or 202-232-8997, or get them at the door. Dress is casual. Proceeds will benefit the Justice Action Movement protest. $10. Democrazy! - The "People's Dance Party" features Andreas Peter Mueller (Pants Off Records), DJ Sever (Act Your Age) & MC Raff (Syntactics) and DJ Green (Rendevous Lounge) and other DJs. 2K9, located at 2009 8th St. NW. Jah Provides Justice - Selection of live reggae music. Kaffa House , 1212 U St. NW. Auctioneer's Ball - Live music to include Dingleberry Dynasty, Mohawk Barbie and Captain Paradox, and many others. Insomnia, 714 6th St. NW.

On the day of the event, JAM spokespersons will be available for comment and interview.
