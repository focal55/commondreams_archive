Enviro/Human Rights Group bring Youth together for Globalization Summit
  WASHINGTON - January 25 - As part of the effort to ensure that globalization includes protections of human rights and the environment, the Sierra Club and Amnesty International USA are bringing 200 students from around the country to Washington, DC to participate in workshops and meet with their members of Congress. The Youth Summit on Globalization will culminate with a rally at the U.S. Capitol on Monday morning before the students go to meetings with their representatives.

In the past year, there have been several protests regarding the threats that unrestrained free trade can have on the environment and human rights. The Youth Summit on Globalization aims to focus the energy of this movement and give youth the tools they need to really go about making change.

WHAT: Rally at the US Capitol to wrap up Youth Summit with students from around the country.

WHO: Speakers from Amnesty International and Sierra Club and 200 student activists

WHEN: 10:30 AM Monday, January 29, 2001

WHERE: House Triangle, southeast corner of the U.S. Capitol building

Beginning Friday, January 26, the students will meet at the joint Sierra Club/Amnesty International Youth Summit in Washington, DC to plan the defense of the environment and environmental defenders in the face of globalization.

As governments worldwide continue to negotiate trade agreements that ignore environmental and human rights concerns and set their foreign policy in line with the dictates of global corporations rather than human values, the Youth Summit will help build the movement to protect human rights and the environment.

The full youth summit will be taking place January 26-29 at the National 4-H Center, 7100 Connecticut Avenue, Chevy Chase, Maryland. For more information, call David Willett at 202-675-6698 or Alistair Hodgett at 202-544-0200.
