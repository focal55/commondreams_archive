Advance Statement of NET President Philip E. Clapp on President Bush's Proposal to Drill for Oil in Alaska
   WASHINGTON - February 27 - The following is an advance statement of Philip E. Clapp, President, National Environmental Trust on President Bush's proposal to drill for oil in the Alaska National Wildlife Refuge:

What President Bush proposed tonight will do nothing to help consumers at the gas pump.

According to the Department of Energy, there is less than a six-month supply of oil in the Refuge.

The oil wouldn't reach the market for seven to ten years, which is how long it would take to drill, begin production and build a pipeline.

Ninety-five percent of the North Slope of Alaska is already open to oil drilling.

Only five percent of the area is off limits, and that should remain exactly what President Eisenhower made it, a national wildlife refuge.

Philip Clapp, president of the National Environmental Trust (NET), is available tonight and throughout the week for interviews regarding energy, global warming and clean air issues in response to Presidents Bush's first address to congress tonight.
