Business Executives, Religious Leaders, Economists, Scientists Gather to Urge Global Warming Action
   WASHINGTON - June 21 - News Advisory:

What:
Citizens Summit on Climate Change: A broad-based coalition of community leaders -- representing over 100 million Americans and over $300 billion in annual revenues -- will gather in the nation's capital to urge lawmakers to slow global warming. Media are invited to the summit's luncheon event, where leaders of the scientific, economic, business, and religious communities will address these and other key questions:

-- What do scientists think of the president's call for more research and a reliance on voluntary measures to address global warming?
-- Is there a moral dimension to the climate issue?
-- What is the view from economists on the costs and benefits of mitigating global warming?
-- Why is there such a disconnect between some in Washington who say emissions cuts will be trying and those in business who say it won't be difficult?

When:
Monday, June 25
Lunch buffet begins at noon, speakers begin at 12:30 p.m.

Where: National Press Club (529 14th St. N.W.) -- 13th floor --
First Amendment Room

Participants: Scientists, economists, business executives, and religious leaders from 12 states. The 3-day event will include workshops, an interfaith service, and meetings with Congressional representatives and their staff.

Speakers:
-- Father John Chryssavgis, professor of theology, Holy Cross School of Theology, Brookline, Mass.
-- Dr. James J. McCarthy, professor of biological oceanography, Harvard University; co-chair of Working Group II of the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC) Third Assessment Report.
-- Dr. Geoffrey Heal, professor of public policy and corporate responsibility, Columbia University
-- Gary Hirshberg, president, Stonyfield Farms Yogurt
-- Moderator: Howard Ris, president, Union of Concerned Scientists
