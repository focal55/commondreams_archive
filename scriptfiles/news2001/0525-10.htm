NWLC Available For Comment On Expected Tax Vote
   WASHINGTON - May 25 - The tax cut that is expected to pass on Saturday will have a detrimental impact on women and their families, but the retention of a Senate-passed provision to make the child tax credit partially refundable -- making the tax credit available to millions of working families for the first time -- would do more to help low-income families enter and remain in the middle class than any other part of the tax plan, according to the National Women's Law Center. This provision is currently one of the few remaining issues holding up the agreement of House and Senate conferees on the tax bill.

"The size of this tax cut is still too costly, too risky and poses a serious threat to Social Security, Medicare and other safety net programs on which women and their families disproportionately depend -- all for the benefit of a tiny handful of the wealthiest taxpayers," said Nancy Duff Campbell, NWLC co-president. "It is essential that the provision making the child tax credit refundable survives in the tax plan. It is one of the few benefits for low-income families that exists in a tax plan which, as a whole, does very little for them."

Campbell explained that the provision making the child tax credit refundable will allow over 7 million families with over 15 million children to receive some of the child tax credit as a refund, all of whom would otherwise receive no benefit from the doubling of the child tax credit alone, another feature of the tax plan which is expected to pass.

The National Women's Law Center is a co-chair of the Fair Taxes For All Coalition, an unprecedented coalition of more than 500 organizations nationwide working to reduce the size and improve the distribution of the Bush tax plan.
