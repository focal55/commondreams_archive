Sierra Club Calls on Bush to Protect Our Wild Forests
Statement of Sierra Club Executive Director Carl Pope
  WASHINGTON - May 1 - "From the pine forests of Florida to Montana's ponderosas to Alaska's Tongass rainforest, citizens calling for wild forest protection vastly outnumbered those opposed. If President Bush allows our last unspoiled National Forests to be cut down, his actions will be an insult to over a million Americans who took time to urge saving these last fragments for our children to enjoy and explore.

"People want these wild forests saved for hunting, fishing, camping, and providing clean water forever -- Americans don't want to clearcut these places so a few timber and mining companies can profit on taxpayers' backs. American taxpayers lose hundreds of millions of dollars subsidizing logging in our National Forests. No sensible landlord should pay a company to devastate their land and allow the company to take all the profits.

"Despite the overwhelming public support for protecting our last wild forests, President Bush is likely to weaken protections for our last pristine forests. We call on President Bush to respond to the families who hunt, fish, hike, and camp in our National Forests - not to campaign contributors from the timber and mining industry intent on destroying public forests for private profit."
