WWF Web site Gives Everyone a Voice in Saving the Planet
  

WASHINGTON - November 7 - The Conservation Action Network (http://takeaction.worldwildlife.org/), an online advocacy tool sponsored by World Wildlife Fund, allows everyone, worldwide, the opportunity to protect the Earth.

The Conservation Action Network is a free service for individuals from around the world who share a common interest in advancing legislation, policies and programs aimed at maintaining the stability of the planet. Originally created in 1997, the site was recently redesigned to make voicing your opinion easier -- and more effective. Currently, over 34,000 people are enrolled in the network.

"We developed the network to give voice to people who want to protect the environment. The Conservation Action Network increases people's ability to express themselves and get conservation results," said WWF Executive Vice President David Sandalow.

Activists have used the network to help: -- Stop the Russians from commercial hunting of beluga whales -- Pass legislation to protect the Galapagos Islands -- Secure adequate funding for large-scale restoration of the Florida Everglades -- Enact the Rhino and Tiger Labeling bill, which has strengthened protection of endangered tigers and rhinos

Visitors to the site will find various ongoing campaigns with brief explanations regarding the issues as well as links to more information. Friendlier functionality allows visitors to send a prepared letter, written specifically for each action campaign, to their respective senator, congressional representative or other decision makers. Enrolled visitors may send an edited version reflecting their own concerns and may choose to print the letter and send it directly via postal mail. Dynamic new features also allow enrolled visitors to track action campaigns.

"Activists frequently tell us how thrilled they are to be able to stay informed about, and weigh in on, breaking conservation issues. They love being part of something big and of a team that gets results," Debra Prybyla, coordinator for Conservation Action Network said.

Visit the Conservation Action Network at http://takeaction.worldwildlife.org/ for more information about the network and to enroll.

World Wildlife Fund (WWF), known worldwide by its panda logo, leads international efforts to protect the diversity of life on earth. Now in its fourth decade, WWF works in more than 100 countries around the globe.
