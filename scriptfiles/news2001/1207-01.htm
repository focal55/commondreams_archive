Alliance For Justice Condemns Ashcroft For Accusing 'Critics' of Treason and Refusing To Prevent Terrorists From Buying Guns
   WASHINGTON - December 7 - The Alliance for Justice expresses deep concern over Attorney General John Ashcroft's allegation that those who criticize the Bush Administration's decision to use military tribunals to try non-U.S. citizens are "aiding terrorists" as well as eroding national unity. The U.S. Constitution, as the Attorney General certainly knows, defines treason as giving "the Enemies...Aid and Comfort."

Alliance For Justice President Nan Aron stated that, "stifling legitimate debate through denunciation and questioning the Senate's constitutional oversight responsibility gravely erodes the public's confidence that the Attorney General respects the rule of law."

The Alliance is troubled with the Attorney General's refusal to support a request that provisions of the law be amended to close the gun show loophole or allow federal investigators to track sales of firearms to suspected terrorists. Yesterday's New York Times reported several specific instances of terrorists who used the "gun show loophole" -- which allows the untracked sale of guns at gun shows to people who are legally barred from buying firearms -- to illegally purchase guns.

"While the Attorney General has asked Congress to grant the Administration greatly expanded powers in other areas of the law, he refuses either to make such requests or even to explain his refusal to do so when it comes to the sale of guns," added Aron. The same Al Qaeda manuals quoted by the Attorney General and others at the recent hearings included instructions that terrorists use the lack of background checks to buy guns for terrorist activities and for smuggling overseas.
