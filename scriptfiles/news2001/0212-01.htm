Diesel Truck Standards Under Siege: Will The Bush Administration Roll Back New Clean Air Standards
   WASHINGTON - February 12 - In one of its greatest accomplishments, the Environmental Protection Agency recently set tough new standards to clean up dirty diesel trucks and diesel fuel. Our colleagues at the Natural Resources Defense Council noted it was "the biggest vehicle pollution news since the removal of lead from gasoline."

But now, those standards are under siege. Some petroleum refiners want to block the cleanup. And they are pulling out all the stops - going to court, to Congress, and to the new White House.

Will they succeed? Please join us at a media luncheon briefing on Tuesday, February 13, as a unique coalition (including state and local clean-air regulators, automobile makers, health and environmental groups, the BP energy company, the nation's biggest diesel engine maker, the California Trucking Association, and pollution control equipment makers) discusses the status and prospects for this vital effort to protect public health.
