It Is Time To Leave No Child Behind®
Congressional Leaders and The Children's Defense Fund Unveil Comprehensive Bill to End Child Poverty
   WASHINGTON - May 21 - Senator Chris Dodd of Connecticut, Congressman George Miller of California, and Marian Wright Edelman, founder and president of the Children's Defense Fund, will announce a new comprehensive bill to ensure all children in America get what they need to thrive and be safe, healthy, and educated. The Act to Leave No Child Behind provides a solid action agenda to deliver children from poverty, violence, and neglect. It combines the best public and private ideas and proposals into a single, comprehensive measure with 12 specific Titles to improve the lives of children. With 12 million children in America still living in poverty and 10.8 million without health insurance, this act is urgently needed. Although many provisions of the Act are focused on our most vulnerable children, a number of sections, such as environmental safeguards, strengthening public education, and improving the quality of children's health care, would benefit all children, regardless of their economic status.

The mission of the Children's Defense Fund is to Leave No Child Behind® and to ensure every child a Healthy Start, a Head Start, a Fair Start, a Safe Start, and a Moral Start in life and successful passage to adulthood with the help of caring families and communities. CDF provides a strong, effective voice for all the children of America who cannot vote, lobby, or speak for themselves. We pay particular attention to the needs of poor and minority children and those with disabilities. CDF educates the nation about the needs of children and encourages preventive investment before they get sick, into trouble, drop out of school, or suffer family breakdown. CDF began in 1973 and is a private, nonprofit organization supported by foundation and corporate grants and individual donations. We have never taken government funds.
