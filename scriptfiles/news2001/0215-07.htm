Trader Joe's Product Tests Positive for Genetically Engineered Corn
   LOS ANGELES - February 15 - Recent laboratory tests have revealed that Trader Joe’s, a California-based national supermarket chain, uses genetically engineered ingredients in its store brand products, despite customers concerns about the untested technology. A recent letter from Trader Joe’s to Greenpeace states that the store’s office gets "up to a hundred letters a day from customers expressing their concern that Trader Joe’s products contain genetically engineered ingredients."

Genetically altered corn was found in a Trader Joe’s Corn Bread mix bought from a San Francisco Bay Area store earlier this month. Greenpeace, which commissioned the tests, released the results today in front of Trader Joe’s California headquarters. The international environmental group was joined by local groups in calling on Trader Joe’s to eliminate genetically engineered ingredients from its store brands.

"Trader Joe’s customers expect safe foods, not secret genetic experiments hidden in its products," said Heather Whitehead of Greenpeace. "Grocery stores have an added responsibility to remove GE from their store brands. Trader Joe’s works to build trust with its customers, all the while it is working behind its customer’s backs to use and hide GE in its own products."

In January 2000, two national supermarket chains, Whole Foods and Wild Oats markets both announced they would eliminate gene-altered food from their store brand products. Other major food producers such as Gardenburger, Gerber and Heinz have also taken action to eliminate genetically engineered ingredients from their products.

In Europe, Trader Joe’s parent company Aldi has already stopped using gene-altered ingredients in its store brands. "We’re calling on Trader Joe’s to end the double standard," said Andrea Azuma of the Sierra Club Sustainable Food Task Force – Angeles Chapter. "If it can eliminate GE foods in Europe, why not here?"

"Greenpeace’s True Food Network started with Trader Joe’s today but will be challenging other national grocery chains in the future," added Whitehead. Trader Joe’s has stores in 14 states.
