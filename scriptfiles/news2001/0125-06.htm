Press Conference on the Release of "Nearly A Failing Grade: A Report Card on the Health Status of Women and Girls in California"
  

SACRAMENTO, CA - January 25 -

WHAT: Release of "Nearly A Failing Grade," a report spearheaded by The Women's Foundation and co-released with the Los Angeles Women's Foundation, Women's Health Leadership, California Black Women's Health Project, Latino Issues Forum, and Asian Pacific Islander American Health Forum.

The report assesses the state's progress in meeting the health needs of women and girls in four key areas: Basic Health Status, Access to Coverage and Care, Women's Health and the Environment, and California's Policy Framework for Women's Health.

California's Grade: D.

Invited guests: Senator Deborah Ortiz (D-Sacramento); Senator John Burton (D-San Francisco); Assemblywoman Charlene Zettel (R-San Diego); Patricia Chang, President and CEO of The Women's Foundation; Latonya Slack, Executive Director of the California Black Women's Health Project; Diane Littlefield, Executive Director of Women's Health Leadership; and women who have faced problems accessing health care for themselves and their children.

Reporters outside Sacramento can attend this press event via conference call. To register for this call, contact Sue Rochman at 415.437.0414.

The Women's Foundation (TWF) is the oldest and largest philanthropic fund for women in the western United States. In its 22 years of existence, TWF has made more than $7 million in grants to more than 550 organizations that promote and protect the human rights of women and girls in the civil, political, economic, and social arenas.
