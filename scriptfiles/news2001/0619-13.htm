Clinton Joins People For The American Way In Calling Viriginia Voters To Make Their Votes Count
   WASHINGTON - June 19 - Former President Bill Clinton, on behalf of People For the American Way, has been urging voters in Virginia's 4th Congressional District to make a strong showing at the voting booths tomorrow.

Today voters head to the polls in a special election to fill the congressional seat vacated by the passing of Rep. Norman Sisisky. In a recorded telephone message, the former president urges voters to make a difference by exercising their right to vote.

Clinton also provides contact information for PFAW Foundation's "Election Protection" hotline for any possible trouble voters might experience at the polls. In light of the obstacles that plagued the 2000 presidential election, PFAW Foundation launched the nonpartisan Election Protection project to assure that attempts to vote are not stymied at the polls.

"Hello. This is President Bill Clinton. I'm calling you today with a very important message about the upcoming election for Congress on Tuesday, June 19," Clinton says in his get-out-the vote message. "Please go to the polls to vote on Tuesday -- your vote can make the difference. If you experience any trouble at the polls at all, please call the Election Protection hotline at 1-866-OUR-VOTE. That's 1-866-687-8683. The Voters' Bill of Rights is also available by calling 1-866-OUR VOTE. Thank you and please vote on Tuesday."
