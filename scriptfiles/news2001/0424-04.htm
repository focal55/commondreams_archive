AFSC Speaks Out in Opposition of McVeigh Execution
   PHILADELPHIA - April 24 - Citing philosophical opposition to "the taking of human life" and "devaluation of human life in our society," Mary Ellen McNish, general secretary of The American Friends Service Committee (AFSC) and Donald Gann, chairperson of the Board of Directors, today came out in written opposition to both the death penalty and the upcoming federal execution of convicted Oklahoma City bomber Timothy McVeigh.

"In declaring certain human beings absolutely worthless and unfit even to live, our government is officially embracing, legitimizing and acting upon the very sentiments that lead to the occurrence of violence in the first place," McNish said in the prepared statement. She went on to say that the McVeigh execution will "serve only to perpetuate the continued devaluation of human life in our society" and called upon all people to embrace each other as children of God.

The American Friends Service Committee has a long history of opposition to the death penalty having worked more than fifty years to rid the criminal justice system of systemic injustice and to increase awareness about those inequities. AFSC is a Quaker organization that is committed to social justice, peace, and humanitarian service, working in communities throughout the United States and internationally using the power of love and nonviolent struggle to bring about positive social change.

With national headquarters based in Philadelphia, The American Friends Service Committee has regional offices in Atlanta, Baltimore, Chicago, New York, San Francisco, Seattle, Pasadena, California; and Cambridge, Massachusetts.

The American Friends Service Committee is a Quaker organization that includes people of various faiths who are committed to social justice, peace, and humanitarian service. Its work is based on the Quaker belief in the worth of every person and faith in the power of love to overcome violence and injustice.
