Green Party Expects FEC Decision
  

What: Federal Election Commission announcement of its decision on National Committee Status for the Green Party

Where: FEC, 9th Floor Hearing Room, 999 E Street NW, Washington, DC

When: Thursday, November 8, at 10 a.m.

WASHINGTON - November 7 - The Green Party of the United States awaits the decision of the 6-member Federal Election Commission (FEC) on the party's request for recognition as a national party with national committee status. FEC staff have recommended a favorable response to the Commission, which will announce a decision based on this recommendation on Thursday, November 8.

"The Green Party has met all conditions required of previously recognized national committees and is confident of a positive response," said Green Party Media Coordinator Scott McLarty, who will be at the hearing to meet press and to distribute the party's press release on the decision.

The Green Party of the United States is the national political organization of the Greens, in which 33 states are represented, with other states considering or applying for membership. It organized the Green National Convention in Denver in June, 2000, at which Ralph Nader was nominated to run for President, and is recognized among Green Parties around the world.

Media inquiries about national policies and activities of the Green Party should be directed to the Green Party of the United States, through its media representatives as listed in the heading above. To contact state Green Parties, visit the Green Party of the United States web site <http://gpus.org> and follow the links to the state parties and their contacts and web sites.
