EEOC Decision on Genetic Testing a Great Step Forward, But More Work Remains to Prevent Genetic Discrimination
Statement by Judith L. Lichtman, President
National Partnership for Women & Families

  WASHINGTON - April 19 - Yesterday's groundbreaking settlement between the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission and Burlington Northern Santa Fe Railroad Company puts employers on official notice that using genetic testing to discriminate against employees will not be tolerated.

Genetic science is revolutionizing the field of medicine and millions of women and families stand to benefit from improved prevention, detection, and treatment of diseases. But these advances will not help women and families if they fear that by participating in genetic research or taking a genetic test - they risk losing job opportunities, health care, or both.

This agreement is a tremendous step forward in protecting American workers, but more needs to be done. Current federal and state laws do not adequately prevent health insurers or employers from using genetic information to deny job opportunities or health benefits. It is imperative that Congress pass comprehensive legislation that establishes clear protections from genetic discrimination for all Americans.
