NAACP And Other Civil Rights Leaders Call For A National Day of Moral Outrage In Tallahassee, Fla.
   WASHINGTON - January 19 - Kweisi Mfume, President & CEO, the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) today urged citizens to participate in a march and rally in Tallahassee, Florida tomorrow January 20, 2001. The purpose of the march is to allow citizens the ability to continue to express their concern and outrage over voting irregularities that occurred in the recent presidential election.

Mfume said: "The inauguration provides us with the perfect juxtaposition to remind America of the need for a nationwide uniform system of casting and counting ballots. It also gives us pause to reflect again on all of the alleged irregularities that prevented or discouraged people from voting. We congratulate Mr. Bush on his inauguration and wish him well in his attempts to now lead our nation. However, we also call upon him to use the bully pulpit of his new office to work for and support both judicial and legislative remedies."

The march and rally will be held at noon on Inauguration Day, Saturday, January 20 on the steps of the state capital located at Pensacola and Madison Streets in Tallahassee, Florida. Co-sponsoring organizations include the Black Leadership Forum, Rainbow/PUSH Coalition, AFL-CIO, Coalition of Conscience, Congressional Black Caucus, Florida Chapter-Sierra Club, Interfaith Alliance, National Council of Negro Women, National Organization for Women, and the Florida Youth Movement.

Founded in 1909, the National Association for the Advancement of Colored People (NAACP) is the nation's oldest and largest civil rights organization. Its half-million adult and youth members throughout the United States and the world are the premier advocates for civil rights in their communities, conducting voter mobilization and monitoring equal opportunity in the public and private sectors.
