Statement of Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger on Word of an "Unsettling" Settlement Between Justice, Microsoft
   WASHINGTON - November 1 - News emerging about a potential settlement between the Microsoft corporation and the Department of Justice is, to say the least, unsettling. It's unsettling because with all the money that Microsoft has spread around Washington, no one can have confidence this case is being pursued on the merits. It gives new meaning to the phrase "anti-trust."

While the Administration has been playing paddy-cake with Microsoft, Windows XP is hitting the streets - a product critics say threatens to keep new products from the market altogether. Here, XP might as well stand for extra protection - the kind of protection that the soft money system affords its proponents. That state Attorneys General are gearing up to oppose the settlement further raises concerns about theAdministration's approach.

The Microsoft case makes a compelling argument for a national democracy stimulus package, that would include the kind of campaign finance reform that would prevent a company like Microsoft from pouring corporate money into federal campaigns.
