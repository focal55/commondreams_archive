Rally on Capitol Steps for Refundable Child Tax Credit
 

WASHINGTON - May 18 -

When:
1 p.m. to 2:15 Monday, May 21.

Where:
East Steps of the U.S. Capitol.

A hundred members of ACORN from Washington, D.C., Baltimore, and Philadelphia will rally in support of a refundable child tax credit. ACORN, the Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now, is a community membership group of low- and moderate-income families. Many of these families have children and will speak about how they could benefit from a child tax credit.

Marian Wright Edelman, founder and president of the Children's Defense Fund, will also speak at the rally.

Bush advertises his tax cuts as a way to help struggling Americans, but it ignores those most in need. Doubling the income tax credit per child from $500 to $1,000 does nothing for children of hardworking parents who pay payroll tax and sales tax, but who do not earn enough to owe in income tax the amount of the credit for which they qualify. Making the credit fully refundable would allow these families, as well as wealthier ones, to receive $1,000 per child.

A nonrefundable child tax credit misses more than 16 million children, one out of four! More than two-thirds of these children live in working families. Over half of African-American and Hispanic children will not benefit unless the credit is made refundable. And another seven million kids will get no additional help from the doubling of the credit amount. Making the child tax credit fully refundable would lift two million children out of poverty.

ACORN, the Association of Community Organizations for Reform Now, is the nation's largest community organization of low- and moderate-income families, with over 100,000 member families organized into 500 neighborhood chapters in 40 cities across the country. ACORN's website is at http://www.acorn.org.
