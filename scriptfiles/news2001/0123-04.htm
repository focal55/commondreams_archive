Wellstone Opposes Ashcroft
   WASHINGTON - January 23 - “Today I am announcing, after reviewing his record as a Senator and as a public official in Missouri, that I intend to oppose former Senator Ashcroft for Attorney General.

“The Constitution imposes on the Senate a duty to offer advice and consent on the president’s nominations. Because I believe the president should have broad latitude in choosing his advisors, I have voted for many nominees with whom I have disagreed, sometimes forcefully, on policy or philosophy. But the Attorney General has a unique charge as chief law enforcement officer of the nation, and thus senators have a special obligation to scrutinize his record to determine whether what he has said and done over the years suggests a capacity for justice, for dealing fairly with those with whom he disagrees, for being an even-handed arbiter of the law in cases and policy matters large and small which will come before him.

“Many have made the point that not only are John Ashcroft’s policy views far outside of the American mainstream, they are far beyond what President Bush has said he supports. His fierce opposition to abortion, for example, even in the case of rape or incest, goes well beyond Bush administration policy. While true--and while he often effectively imposed an ideological standard on nominees whom he opposed--that is not reason enough to oppose him.

“I’ve known John Ashcroft for some time and I consider him a friend. I especially respect his religious convictions and the commitment to them that he shows in his personal life. As I’ve said elsewhere, that such a friend can hold such harsh views has been difficult. But while I strongly disagree with him on many issues, it is his record of insensitivity to minority rights, including those of racial minorities; and his mishandling of certain nominations that is most troubling.

“There is possibly no issue more important to our future than that of racial reconciliation. But John Ashcroft’s relentless defiance of voluntary school desegregation plans in St. Louis, which prompted repeated rebukes from federal courts; his associations with Bob Jones University and Southern Partisan Magazine; and other similar incidents in his record raise serious questions about his judgment on matters related to race. In my mind, his insensitivity casts doubt on his ability to serve our nation, which must make healing our racial divide a priority, in this role.

“Senator Ashcroft’s handling of certain presidential nominations, particularly his gross distortions of the record of Ronald White as a federal judge, and his efforts to block gay nominee James Hormel as U.S. ambassador to Luxembourg, are troubling. Whether out of animus, which suggests bias; or out of political opportunism, which suggests a lack of judgment, I’ve concluded his handling of these nominees was grossly unjust.

“Enforcement of federal laws protecting the civil rights and voting rights of our citizens, the safety of women carrying out their constitutional right to reproductive services, and many other fundamental rights lies with the Justice Department. Moreover, the Attorney General’s advisory role in the selection of federal judges, including Supreme Court Justices, and the right of the Justice Department to intervene before the Supreme Court means the influence of the Attorney General will extend far beyond the President whom he serves. It is crucial, therefore, that this person be independent and fair-minded, open to arguments from opposing sides, able to take into account the experiences and views of others. I regret that John Ashcroft, despite his many admirable personal qualities, is not the right person for this position.”
