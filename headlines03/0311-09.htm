Published on Tuesday, March 11, 2003 by The Virginian-Pilot
Activist Sentenced to Six Months in Prison for Splashing Own Blood on Pentagon
by Mike Knepler
 

Steve Baggarly won't be feeding the poor this morning.

Baggarly, 37, who heads a free breakfast program in Norfolk, has been sentenced to six months in prison for splashing blood on the Pentagon in protest of a possible war with Iraq.

Baggarly and two other men were convicted Friday by federal magistrate Theresa Buchanan in Alexandria, said Edward Adams, public information officer for the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Virginia.

The incident happened on Dec. 30. The men poured blood -- including their own -- on a door and columns at a Pentagon entrance while 60 other demonstrators diverted a contingent of military police.

The action symbolized ``the bloodiness that's already on the Pentagon,'' Baggarly said in an interview before the trial.

All three men belong to the Catholic Workers, a nationwide lay movement that serves the poor and promotes social justice. The others were Bill Frankl-Streit of the Little Flower Catholic Worker in Louisa County, and Steve Woolford of the Silk Hope Catholic Worker in Siler City, N.C.

Each man was convicted on two charges -- disorderly conduct and violation of preservation of property. Each charge brought a six-month sentence, but they will run concurrently, Adams said.

The men managed to make political statements despite being warned by the court, said Sheila Stumph, who also is a Norfolk Catholic Worker.

``Steve said the blood trail leads from the Pentagon to the courthouse because the court defends the Pentagon,'' she said.

``Even though it was a sad thing, it also was a time of hope because it showed that people are willing to go to jail for what they believe in,'' Stumph said.

Baggarly and his wife, Kim Williams, founded the Norfolk Catholic Worker breakfast program, which is held three times a week on Salter Street, near downtown Norfolk.

Baggarly has been arrested several times before for anti-war demonstrations and spent a year in federal prison for damaging a Navy destroyer in another protest.

Other volunteers will continue the food program, which is held at 7 a.m. on Tuesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays.
