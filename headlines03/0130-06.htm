Published on Thursday, January 30, 2003 by ITV News
London's Feb 15th Peace Rally Ban 'Could Spark Riot'
Peace marchers may riot if they are not allowed to hold a rally in London, an MP has warned
 

The Stop the War Coalition has been told that protesters can march through the capital on February 15 but will be banned from gathering in Hyde Park afterwards.

Labour MP George Galloway said Culture Secretary Tessa Jowell had informed the Coalition of the rally ban.

"This is an extraordinary turn of events. It would amount to an absolute crime against civil liberties in Britain," said Mr Galloway, MP for Glasgow Kelvin and a vehement critic of war in Iraq.

"If what is expected to be half-a-million people come to London to register their opposition to war, they will be allowed to march through the streets of London but they are not allowed to have a rally at the end of it. "

The Stop The War Coalition, estimates that more than 500,000 people will join next month's march.

"I would want to warn Mrs Jowell, she can either choose between half-a-million people at the rally or half-a-million people in a riot, because that is what will surely happen if we tell them that thanks to the British Government they are not allowed to attend a meeting at the end of the march," said Mr Galloway.

The Department of Culture, Media and Sport said the Stop the War Coalition had been refused permission to stage a rally in Hyde Park for safety reasons and the risk of adverse weather would jeopardise the condition of the park.

A spokeswoman said protesters would be allowed to rally elsewhere if they wanted to.

"This is not unusual for this time of year," she added.

"It would not matter who had applied, they would not have been given permission to do it."
