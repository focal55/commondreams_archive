TAKING ACTION:
Raise Your Voice on Iraq Situation

We urge you to write to your local papers, talk in houses of worship, call into talk shows, contact your representatives and the White House.

Talking points:

* The Iraqi people are suffering both as a result of the sanctions the US insists upon and at the hands of Saddam Hussein. The sanctions against Iraq have shattered civil society, the economy and the educational system. The UN International Children's Fund estimates that 4,500 children are dying every month -- one every ten minutes. This is unconscionable.

* We must make a distinction between the government of Iraq and the Iraqi people. The Iraqi people should be provided with the necessities of life -- food, medicine and clean water. The presumed US government policy of using the sanctions as pressure on the Iraqi people to overthrow Saddam Hussein has not worked and is totally immoral. The US should not be using food as a weapon.

* The Clinton administration has been somewhat ambiguous, but has basically said that the sanctions will remain in place no matter what the Iraqi regime does. This creates no incentive for the Iraqi regime to abide by UN resolutions.

* The administration should heed the advice of its allies and genuinely try to find a diplomatic solution to the current crisis. This is no time for people to be proving how macho they can be.

* Are we going to go to war over whether there should be 40 or 20 US inspectors? A way must be found to get the inspectors back in Iraq, ensure that there are no weapons of mass destruction and alleviate the suffering of the Iraqi people.

* The sanctions are not against Saddam Hussein. He remains in power. It is the Iraqi people who are suffering. The sanctions in fact may strengthen his hold on power.

* "Surgical strikes" don't exist in this world. If the US bombs, targets will be missed and, as in the past, civilians will die.

* Iraq had and did not use chemical weapons during the Gulf War. While it is the cause of much debate, many experts believe that Gulf War Syndrome was caused by the US military bombing Iraqi storage facilities of chemical weapons. What untold suffering will be unleashed if the US military begins bombing such Iraqi facilities?

* Double standards have led to triple and quadruple standards. In 1991, the US insisted that a war be waged to implement UN resolutions against Iraq. Since that time, the UN and its resolutions have been ignored on Israel's oppression of the Palestinians and its occupation of Lebanon. Now, suddenly, the US again discovers the UN as an instrument of foreign policy. The UN is a hollow shell that doesn't have any teeth except when it comes to Arab countries.

* Israel is the all time champion of violating UN Security Council resolutions, but the US doesn't place sanctions or bomb it. Instead, the US gives it billions of dollars each year.

* On the issue of weapons of mass destruction, Israel has never allowed serious inspection of its estimated 200 nuclear weapons. The resolution that created UNSCOM also called for a Mideast free of nuclear weapons. Israel's massive arsenal must be on the table. So long as Israel has this massive arsenal, the region will be unstable and other countries will try to match it.

* A case can be made that the sanctions are a weapon of mass destruction. The rest of the world wants to see some greater alleviation of the suffering of the Iraqis. The "food for oil" deal is totally insufficient and does not address the infrastructure, like water and electrical facilities.

* The media are right to complain that Iraq is not letting many of them in now. But where have they been for the last six years? As the Iraqi people have been suffocating, they have been silent. Now, the only time Iraqi civilians are shown on US TV is when they are at Saddam Hussein's palace.

* Iraqis are humans. If you cut them, they bleed. If you suffocate them, they choke. If you starve them, they die. This happens whether or not there are cameras there.
