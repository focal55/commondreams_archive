Published on Monday, September 9, 2002 by Agence France Presse
Canada Will Not Support US Military Action to Topple Saddam: Manley
 

Canadian Deputy Prime Minister John Manley said his country will not support US military action against Iraq aimed at toppling its government.

"We have not signed on for the change-the-regime movement in Iraq," Manley said Sunday in an interview with CTV television.

"They would be going in without Canadian support, but I'm not so sure they would be going in alone," he added.

The comment came ahead of a US-Canadian working summit in Detroit, Michigan, where President George W. Bush and Prime Minister Jean Chretien were to discuss Monday the situation in Iraq and US charges that Baghdad poses a threat to the international community by secretly building up its arsenal of weapons of mass destruction.

The meeting is seen as part of the Bush administration's attempt to rally international support for its possible military campaign against Iraq.

But Manley said Canada was not "in the camp" with British Prime Minister Tony Blair and others "who say there should be a preemptive attack."

"We have said there should be, in order to consider this part of the war against terrorism, evidence that Iraq is somehow connected to al-Qaeda," said the deputy prime minister.

The international Islamic terror network al-Qaeda and its leader, Osama bin Laden, are blamed for the September 11 attacks on the United States, which left about 3,000 people dead.
