Published on Thursday, May 18, 2000 at 8:26 PM by the Associated Press
Lott Calls On Daschle To Rein In Wellstone
by Frederic J. Frommer
 
WASHINGTON - Senate Majority Leader Trent Lott wants his Democratic counterpart, Tom Daschle of South Dakota, to rein in Sen. Paul Wellstone for challenging how Lott runs the Senate, according to a report in a Capitol Hill Web publication.

Lott, a Republican from Mississippi, told Congressional Quarterly Thursday that Daschle isn't doing enough to control his ``more volatile members,'' specifically Wellstone, D-Minn., and Sen. Dick Durbin, D-Ill.

The two were ``up on the edge'' of violating Senate decorum when they attacked the majority leader on Wednesday for not allowing Democrats to offer amendments on the floor, Lott said.

``I'm just tired of that,'' he said. ``I'm not going to just stand around and let them take potshots at me.''

Lott's comments came a day after an unusually explosive exchange in which Wellstone said that Lott ``wants to run the Senate as the House of Representatives,'' a reference to the tighter controls the House exerts on debate.

``I think the Senate is losing its capacity to have the discussions, to have debate, and to have its vitality,'' said Wellstone, one of the Senate's feistiest members who often blocks bills he opposes.

An emotional Lott said he felt ``personally maligned, and I do not appreciate it ... I am getting really tired of people questioning my commitment to the Senate and to the opportunity for debates, and that I am trying to be a rules committee of one.''

He said Democrats keep offering time-consuming resolutions that take away from the business of the Senate.

Judging from Daschle's response on the Senate floor, it's not likely he will try to muzzle the two liberal Democrats.

``The way the Senate is being run is wrong,'' an angry Daschle said Wednesday. ``No majority leader in history has attempted to constrain Senate debate as aggressively as Senator Lott has chosen to do.''

``He is trying to protect his members so they don't have to vote on tough issues,'' Daschle continued. ``Let's get it out on the table.''

On Thursday, Lott said Daschle should have a talk with Wellstone. Daschle's spokeswoman, Ranit Schmelzer, said that's not likely to happen.

And Wellstone himself was unrepentant.

Speaking on the Senate floor, Wellstone said that if Lott blames him for instigating Wednesday's exchange, ``I will be proud to take the blame. It's a discussion we needed to have.''

``What I wanted to say was I don't intend to be silent,'' he added. ``I intend to issue challenges and speak out.''
