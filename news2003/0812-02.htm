Call for Nominations for the Sixth Mario Savio Young Activist Award
 
GUERNEVILLE, CA - August 11 - The Mario Savio Memorial Lecture Fund is seeking nominations for the sixth Mario Savio Young Activist Award. This award carries a cash prize of $3000, divided equally between the recipient of the award and the organization of the awardee.

The award, named for the Berkeley Free Speech Movement leader who died in 1996, is presented each year to a young person who has shown a deep commitment to human rights and social justice and a proven ability to transform this commitment into effective action. The recipient should demonstrate leadership ability, creativity, and integrity. (Past recipients are listed below.)

To be eligible for the award this year, a nominee should be:

a) between 18 and 30 years of age by November 1, 2003;

b) engaged in social change activism; focus may be issues of economic or social justice, human rights, civil rights, peace etc.;

c) acting not alone but within a larger community/organizational/social movement context;

d) considered by peers within that context as an inspirational leader or motivating force;

e) willing and able to attend award ceremony in Berkeley, CA, on November 20 of this year (expenses paid).

Nominations may be sent by e-mail, fax or post and must include the following:

1) Completed nomination form (printed below)

2) Description of organization (brochure or 1-2 paragraphs)

3) Two letters of recommendation, 1-2 pages. One of these letters should be from someone outside the candidate¹s organization.

4) Optionally, nominators or candidates may submit any other material that would present a deeper picture of the candidate (e.g., articles by or about the candidate, videos made by or about the candidate and his or her work, etc.).

Finalists for the award may be asked to submit a 1-2 page personal statement and/or have a personal interview (by telephone if necessary).

Nominations for the 2003 award must be received by September 3, 2003. They may be e-mailed to savio@sonic.net, faxed to 707-823-7028, or mailed to Mario Savio Memorial Lecture, PO Box 152, Guerneville, CA 95446. Finalists will be notified by October 1. No materials will be returned.

Previous award winners:

1998 - Michael Leon-Guerrero, Southwest Organizing Project

1999 - Nikki Bas, Sweatshop Watch

2000 - Jia-Ching Chen, JustAct

2001 - Jim Keady, Educating for Justice and NikeWages.org

2002 - Harmony Goldberg and Genevieve Gonzales, SOUL
