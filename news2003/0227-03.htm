Reverend Simeon Nartoomid Offers Alternative Proposal to War with Iraq
 
SANTA FE, N.M. - February 27 - Following is a statement by Rev. Simeon Nartoomid on his proposed alternative to war with Iraq:

I propose that the U.S.A. offer to the people of Iraq an aid package in the amount of the estimated cost of the proposed war (said to be 100 billion USD). The aid package would be to rebuild the country from its state of deterioration. I will not detail all that encompasses as most people are aware of what is needed there. The stipulation associated with this aid package would be that Saddam Hussein must step down from power and go into exile for the remainder of his life.

I believe it is highly likely that some of the following would occur as a result of such a sincere (and it must be sincere and followed through with) proposal on the part of the U.S.A.: 1) Saddam Hussein would lose what support he has from his own people in the Arab world; 2) if he did not step down from power he would likely be removed by some means by people in his country or neighboring countries; 3) the U.S.A. would likely have much greater support from its allies in such an action, and our allies would also likely help pay the bill of such an aid package wherein they are reluctant to do so for the war action; 4) the amount of money involved could be paid out over a period of time, say 5-10 years, therefore reducing the immediate financial burden on the economies of those nations contributing, not the least of which would be the U.S.A.; 5) the U.S.A. would set an example for the world, truly transforming its Super Power status into one of being a Super Helping Brother/Sister status, therefore changing our world image in the process; 6) the purposes of the oil industry would still be served as they could establish cooperation with a new regime for the benefit of both Iraq and the other nations of the world, which still need the use of crude oil coming from that region; 7) the U.S.A. and its allies could cooperate in the effort to establish a new regime such as occurred for Afghanistan, and rebuild the country of Iraq, therefore further establishing a sense of global community and goodwill; 8) the tensions and conflicts between Israel and Palestine would be more easily dealt with because the U.S.A. would have demonstrated, in action not just words, the way to properly deal with potential conflict and, therefore, would gain further respect from both the Israelis and Palestinians in their counsel on such affairs; 9) the de-polarization which would take place between the U.S.A. and its allies and Iraq would ripple throughout the world, and, in particular, in the conflict-torn Middle East having a positive overall effect for the entire world; 10) the Bush administration would have found a viable alternative and "out" for its previous stance without losing face.

I believe there is much more than this that would come about as a result of implementing such a proposal, but these possibilities are of themselves enough. If only one or two of these potentials were real possibilities, that alone should justify a serious consideration for implementation.
