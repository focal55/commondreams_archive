Renewable Energy Creates Jobs
200,000+ High-Tech Jobs From Growing Renewable Energy Market

 
TORRANCE, CA - July 29 - As a solution to the state job crunch, clean energy advocates and policy makers called for continued development of California’s renewable energy industry as a means of providing a job boost to the state.

“Renewable energy technologies have the potential to grow California’s economy and provide thousands of high-tech, high-paying jobs,” said Ross Hammersley, Los Angeles Campaign Director for Environment California Research and Policy Center, a statewide environmental advocacy organization. “With the right programs, California could develop a “Green Energy Valley,” similar to Silicon Valley, while bringing all the environmental benefits of clean, locally produced energy.”

According to the report by Environment California Research and Policy Center released today, Renewable Energy and Jobs: Employment Impacts of Developing Markets for Renewables in California, state renewable energy programs such as the Renewable Portfolio Standard and programs to put more solar power on new homes, would greatly increase demand for renewable energy equipment and services, encouraging California companies to expand their operations and tap into a growing international market. Currently, there are more than 50 companies involved in the renewable energy industry located in the Los Angeles region with over 2,500 employees.

“By creating a clear market demand for renewable energy, policies that boost demand for renewable energy make is possible for companies to attract the investments needed to expand our operations, increase production and hire more workers,” said Vahan Garboushian, President & Founder of Amonix, Inc., a PV design and manufacturing company in Torrance. “Over the past five years, we’ve expanded our local operations by 300% as a result of an increased demand in renewable energy.”

Environment California Research and Policy Center’s report details how capitalizing on the expanding local and foreign markets for renewable energy would benefit for the state economy. Policy leaders also pointed out that given the rise in natural gas prices, California’s primary fuel source for electricity, expanding renewable energy markets also helps stabilize prices and reliability.

“Given the state’s economic and energy situation, growing our renewable energy industry is exactly the direction we need to be headed right now,” said S. David Freeman, Chairman of the California Power Authority. “With the Energy Action Plan, recently adopted by California’s three state energy agencies, we hope to double our clean energy achieving the 20% renewable energy goal by 2010. Doing so will bring more jobs to California the next decade."

Specifically, the report concludes:

· Full realization of the Renewable Portfolio Standard goal of 20% would create an estimated 119,000 person-years of employment for Californians over the lifetimes of the plants built through 2017. The Energy Action Plan would bring about these jobs by 2010.

· Overseas renewable energy markets would create an estimated 4,300 new jobs for Californians in 2010 and 9,700 in 2017. From 2003-2017, this would total 78,000 person-years of employment.

In addition to the state’s renewable portfolio standard, the report also looked at California’s growing market for solar photovoltaic (PV) systems, concluding that jobs from a steady growth in the use of solar panels would add 2,700 person-years of employment. A more dramatic growth in the PV market would bring about the greatest job benefits since solar creates more jobs than all other clean energy technologies.

“Solar power not only creates pollution-free and reliable electricity, but it creates more jobs per unit of energy than all other technologies,” said Hammersley. “By expanding our solar market, we could export solar panels like we export vegetables, computer programs and Hollywood movies.”
