ICHV Criticizes GOP Leadership for Efforts to Legalize Deadly Assault Weapons
Offers Strong Support for Conyers-McCarthy Bill
 
CHICAGO - May 14 - The Illinois Council Against Handgun Violence (ICHV) today announced its strong support for the "Assault Weapons Ban and Law Enforcement Protection Act of 2003," legislation introduced last Thursday by U.S. Representatives Carolyn McCarthy (D-N.Y.) and John Conyers (D-Mich.). The legislation would renew a 1994 federal law banning certain assault weapons. More importantly, the bill would significantly strengthen current law to address limitations in the ban that have allowed the gun industry to circumvent the law.

"Gun manufacturers have found ways to skirt the law and continue making these deadly products that are attractive to criminals," stated Elizabeth Coolidge, ICHV Board Chair. "The Conyers-McCarthy bill will strengthen the existing ban so that gun makers can't get away with breaking the law."

For the past decade, the gun industry has successfully circumvented the existing law by designing and marketing assault weapons that incorporate slight modifications in order to evade the ban. The gun industry markets a wide variety of such "post-ban" assault weapons that incorporate features that are the essence of an assault weapon-design characteristics that make it easy for a shooter to simply point, as opposed to carefully aim, a weapon to quickly spray a wide area with a lethal hail of bullets. Today, post-ban versions of AK-47s and AR-15s, guns banned by name in the 1994 law, are flooding the civilian market. A post-ban AR-15 clone, manufactured by the Bushmaster corporation, was used by the Washington, DC-area snipers to kill 10 and injure three in October 2002.

The McCarthy-Conyers bill would keep gun makers from modifying weapons in order to evade the law. But House Republican leaders have stated that they will do everything in their power to prevent a vote from taking place on this bill. Recent statements by House Majority Leader Tom DeLay (R-Tex.) clearly show that the GOP-controlled House would allow the current ban to expire, and that "(w)e have no intention of bringing it up" for a vote.

"We are urging House Speaker Dennis Hastert to address this issue and allow a vote on this critical measure," said Coolidge. "We all know how much influence the pro-gun lobby has in Washington, D.C. But if Speaker Hastert does not allow a vote on this bill, he will be showing the American public in no uncertain terms that he and the House Republicans are puppets of the gun lobby and the NRA."

The current assault weapons ban will end on September 13, 2004, unless Congress and the president act to pass new legislation. Contrary to House Republicans, both President Bush and Attorney General John Ashcroft have voiced support for renewal of this measure.

"We believe that assault weapons have no place in our communities, and the American public agrees with us," stated Coolidge. "The public needs to know that the House Republican leadership does not have our best interests in mind when they want to legalize deadly weapons."

The Illinois Council Against Handgun Violence (ICHV) is a statewide non-profit educational organization working to stop gun death and injury in Illinois. Please call the ICHV office at 312-341-0939 or visit ICHV's website at http://www.ichv.org for more information.
