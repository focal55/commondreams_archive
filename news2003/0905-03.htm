Jim Hightower's 'Thieves' Mounts Guerilla Attack on Best-Seller Lists
 

AUSTIN, TX - September 5 - With all the hoopla over the Fox-Franken lawsuit and ensuing success of "Those Lying Liars...", the mainstream media has missed the big story in the publishing world in the last month: the surprise success of Jim Hightower's new book -- THIEVES IN HIGH PLACES: THEY'VE STOLEN OUR COUNTRY AND IT'S TIME TO TAKE IT BACK (Viking Press) -- which has vaulted onto the New York Times Best-Seller List and onto lists all across the country.

In its first weeks on the shelves, THIEVES IN HIGH PLACES has vaulted to

* #9 on the New York Times Best-Seller List,

* #6 on the Booksense List [for independent booksellers], and

* #3 on the San Francisco Chronicle List.

Utilizing grassroots networks, guerilla marketing, independent media and the internet, Hightower has mobilized thousands across the country to act with a fervor that resembles an election campaign. Since the book's August 18 on-sale date, Hightower has been touring the country, speaking before standing-room-only crowds that range from 400-1,500. Before the current tour is over, Hightower will have visited more than 30 cities and spoken at more than 50 events.

"The Bushites, Wobblycrats, and Corporate Kleptocrats don't give a damn about the workdaday majority of Americans," Hightower says, "and I'm out to rally our people's grassroots power so we can take our country back from these greedheads and boneheads."

In every town, Hightower is collaborating with local grassroots activist groups to raise awareness on key issues, plus he's teaming with TrueMajority and GOTV organizers to channel energy from the crowds into direct political action.

"The best weapon we have against these THIEVES is humor and hope, both of which will be in ample supply at these events," Hightower says.

For more information on Hightower's tour schedule, which includes stops in New York, Boston, Washington DC, San Francisco, Seattle, Portland, Los Angeles, Chicago, Minneapolis, Kansas City, Philadelphia and Austin, Texas, visit http://www.jimhightower.com/road/.
