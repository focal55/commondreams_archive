Selma, Ala., 200 Volunteers Join to Improve Homes of Seniors, Disabled, Low-Income Residents; Kick-off June 9 at Selma High School
 
WASHINGTON - June 6 - The Selma community, as many as 200 volunteers from across the U.S., and a leader from the National League of Cities will help kickoff a community-wide effort to improve homes for low-income, senior, and disabled residents of Selma June 8-14.

An estimated 30 local residents will benefit from the work of the adult and teen volunteers at the Deep South Workcamp. The location of the workcamp is Ward 8, the Weed and Seed Target Area. The workcamp will provide free home repairs through the Group Workcamp program, sponsored locally by the Jonathan Daniels Community Development Corporation, the City of Selma Weed and Seed, and Selma High School. Repairs offered through the workcamp include limited roofing, porch and wheelchair ramp construction, interior and exterior painting, weatherization, and other work.

"The citizens of Selma are privileged to have Deep South Workcamp volunteers in Selma," said James Perkins, mayor of Selma. "Through this volunteer home repair program, our citizens are receiving direct benefits from our relationship. From the city to all participants and stakeholders, thank you. And to the families who will benefit, congratulations."

"This represents about 6,400 hours of volunteer labor - worth at least $40,000 - to the community," said Joyce M. Kendrick, executive director of the Jonathan Daniels Community Development Corporation.

A kickoff at Selma High School (2180 Broad St.) will be held on June 9 at 8 a.m. featuring Mayor James Perkins, representatives from the Jonathan Daniels Community Development Corporation, the City of Selma, the Group Workcamps Foundation, and the National League of Cities.

James Hunt, city councilmember for Clarksburg, West Virginia, and representing the National League of Cities, will attend the kickoff on Monday. Hunt chairs NLC's Advisory Council and he is manager of the West Virginia Housing Development Fund's Clarksburg Office. The National League of Cities has provided technical assistance to nearly 30 cities around the country since 1998 in its Strengthening Partnerships for Housing Opportunities Program.

The Jonathan Daniels Community Development Corporation is a not-for-profit organization providing services in affordable housing, home buying education, financial literacy, neighborhood safety and leadership. The City of Selma Weed and Seed promotes an improved quality of life in Ward Eight by building pride in the community, promoting ownership in neighborhood safety, and community development. The Group Workcamps Foundation is a nonprofit, interdenominational Christian volunteer home-repair organization headquartered in Loveland, Colorado.

The National League of Cities is the largest national organization for American cities. NLC serves as a resource and advocate for 18,000 cities, towns and villages of all sizes. http://www.nlc.org.
