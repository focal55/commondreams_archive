The Accidental Activist Launches Tour In Honor Of Hiroshima & Nagasaki Victims
 
BERKELEY, CA - August 8 - Who: Upon These Boards (UTB) presents What: THE ACCIDENTAL ACTIVIST, a play written and performed by Kathryn Blume, co-creator of The Lysistrata Project with music by Eliza Ladd

When: August 8 & 9, 2003 at 7:30pm

Where: The Aurora Theatre Company, 2081 Addison (at Shattuck), Berkeley

In remembrance of Nagasaki’s nuclear devastation Upon These Boards (UTB) will launch the national tour of Kathryn Blume’s anti-war play, THE ACCIDENTAL ACTIVIST, at the Aurora Theatre, 2081 Addison Street (at Shattuck) in Berkeley. Show time is 7:30pm Friday and Saturday. Tickets are $10-$20, available online at www.frantix.net or call 415-621-1216.

UTB brought their acclaimed Lysistrata Project-Berkeley to the Berkeley Rep, with a cast of over 100 actors, in March 2003. Recently they have produced the Art is Peace series including the unveiling of the hot new play INKWELL COMMUNIQUES also at the Berkeley Rep. They are a Troupe who present live events (plays, readings, salons & symposia) in innovative ways. Their work focuses mainly on classical material and adaptations of time-honored works, illuminating the human condition yet also prizes the epic sweep of a Modern or Contemporary piece of theatre. UTB'S artistic staging director, Randall Stuart, director of the Berkeley version of the Lysistrata Project, felt compelled to bring Blume out to the Bay Area, so that we all might enjoy her message.

THE ACCIDENTAL ACTIVIST was written in response to Kathryn Blume’s experience as the co-creator of the Lysistrata Project, this solo piece, directed by Michaela Hall, is laced with optimistic socio-political observations and deft sketches of determined women of the world. Blume recounts a story that's part fact, fantasy & caffeine-induced madness. This irreverent and irrepressible performer imagines how she might possibly make a difference on a planet that couldn't care less

Performances are both Friday and Saturday evening at 7:30pm. The show runs one hour and twenty minutes with no intermission.

Reservations are strongly recommended. Performances are at the Aurora's beautiful and intimate space on Berkeley's "theatre row" - a few steps from BART. Suggested donation is $20 and there is discounted seating at $10 suggested donation. . A generous portion of the proceeds from this performance will benefit Oxfam. Get your tickets online at www.Frantix.net (they never charge a service fee)or 415-621-1216 or 866-372-6849
