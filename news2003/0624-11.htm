Rallies Planned for Day of U.S. Supreme Court Ruling in Historic Case Against Texas Sodomy Law
 
NEW YORK - June 24 - The National Gay and Lesbian Task Force, in conjunction with the NYC Gay & Lesbian Anti-Violence Project, is coordinating a celebration or protest rally in New York City at 7:00pm on the day the United States Supreme Court announces its decision in Lambda Legal's case challenging the Texas "Homosexual Conduct" law. The decision is expected to be released on the morning of Thursday, June 26.

WHAT: Celebration or Protest Rally

WHEN: 7:00 pm, Thursday, June 26 (unless something extraordinary happens with the court's schedule)

WHERE: Sheridan Square (West 4th Street between Christopher and Grove)

WHY: Fifteen states still criminalize anal and oral sex between unmarried persons. Four of these states criminalize this behavior only if it occurs between persons of the same sex. The challenge before the court involves the Texas statute, which applies only to same-sex behavior.

If the court upholds the Texas sodomy statute and reaffirms its infamous 1986 Bowers v. Hardwick decision, it will be a devastating blow to equal rights for LGBT people. Such a result - reinforcing the second class citizenship of LGBT people - will demand an angry protest.

On the other hand, the court could strike down the Texas sodomy law for two reasons - first, that it is an unconstitutional invasion of privacy; and second, that by singling out gay people, it violates the Constitution's guarantee of equal protection under the law. If the ruling is based on privacy rights, it will be a victory for all people and be a body blow to governmental authority to criminalize private, consensual intimate relations between unmarried persons.

If the decision is based on equal protection grounds only, it will strike down the laws of four states (including Texas) that prohibit same-sex consensual behavior only (while upholding the government's right to criminalize consensual intimate relations). This ruling will also be a major victory for us and a significant defeat for those who seek to deny equal rights for LGBT people.

The Task Force is also involved with similar rallies in:

Los Angeles:

Rally in West Hollywood Park on the day of the decision, at the corner of Santa Monica and San Vincente Boulevards

Washington, DC:

Rally at 7 p.m. on the day of the decision, at Dupont Circle Park

Boston:

Rally in Boston at 6:30 p.m. on the day of the decision at Copley Square, Boylston between Clarendon and Dartmouth

To view a partial list of other planned rallies across the country, go to the Lambda Legal Web site.
