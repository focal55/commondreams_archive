Sierra Club Applauds Senator Durbin for Pushing Real Fuel Economy Increase
 
WASHINGTON - April 7 - The Sierra Club today strongly applauded Senator Richard Durbin (D-IL) for spearheading an attempt to reduce our oil dependence by making real changes to fuel economy standards. Senator Durbin is introducing a bill that would require automakers to achieve a fleet-wide average of 40 miles per gallon (mpg) for passenger vehicles. The move comes amid heightened awareness about the role of oil in U.S. foreign policy, particularly towards the Middle East.

"American troops are right now risking their lives in a war that is at least in part about oil," said Daniel Becker, director of the Sierra Club's Global Warming and Energy program. "Responsible leaders such as Senator Durbin understand that we need to make ourselves less reliant on oil. Raising fuel economy standards is the biggest single step we can take to do that."

By raising fuel economy standards to 40 mpg, the U.S. would save 3 million barrels of oil a day - more than we currently import from the Persian Gulf, and could hope to get from the Arctic Refuge, combined. Cutting our oil consumption would improve national security, reduce global warming pollution, and save consumers money at the gas pump as gas prices continue to skyrocket.

Automakers have the technology to comfortably get to 40 mpg. By adding existing components like the integrated starter generator, and the variable valve control engine, they could significantly improve the fuel economy of all cars, trucks, and SUVs, without compromising performance, safety, or affordability. These fuel-saving technologies are already being used successfully on select models. Making these technologies standard would allow automakers to meet a 40 mpg requirement, and would put the U.S. auto industry at the forefront of the race for fuel efficient vehicles.

Today's attempt to raise fuel economy standards comes almost a year after Congress caved to an aggressive lobbying effort by the automakers by rejecting a similar proposal. Since that time, a spike in gas prices and war in the Persian Gulf have placed a renewed focus on the need to reduce our oil consumption.

"Senator Durbin deserves immense credit for putting the long-term interests of national security and the environment over the short-term interests of automakers," continued Becker.
