FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
MAY 1, 2003
12:01 PM
 CONTACT: Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice (RCRC)
 Marjorie Signer, 202-628-7700 ext. 12
RCRC and Texas Minister Applaud Cloture Vote on Owen Nomination
 
WASHINGTON - May 1 - The following statement will be delivered today by Reverend Rigby, pastor of St. Andrew's Presbyterian Church, Austin, Texas, on behalf of the Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice:

On behalf of the national Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice and its Texas affiliate, I applaud the Senators who this morning voted against cloture on the nomination of Priscilla Owen to the Fifth Circuit Court of Appeals. To these Senators, I say thank you for standing up for women's constitutional rights and against the Bush court-packing scheme.

I have come to Washington today from Texas -- a part of the country that will be most affected by Justice Owen's confirmation. I wish to speak on behalf of people of faith who believe that women have not only the right but also the responsibility to make their own reproductive choices.

Priscilla Owen's record on the Texas Supreme Court demonstrates that she is a judicial activist who has no hesitation about imposing her own harsh and rigid beliefs about abortion rights on others.

Her opinions regarding judicial bypass for minors seeking abortion in the series of Jane Doe cases are particularly chilling to me as a person of faith.

In the Jane Doe 1 case, Justice Owen questioned whether Doe, a college-bound senior who was pregnant and had received information about her options from friends, family, a parenting teacher, and Planned Parenthood, was aware of the religious implications of abortion, despite the fact that the law in no way involves religious considerations. Justice Owen wrote:

"I would require a minor to indicate to the court that she is aware of and has considered that there are philosophic, social, moral and religious arguments that can be brought to bear when considering abortion."

Justice Owen's opinion was cruel to the minor girl and insensitive and callous to differing beliefs and values. Her opinion went far beyond the Texas statute and the intent of the legislature. She disregarded not only the law but also the conscience of a minor girl who looked to the court to protect and safeguard her rights.

It is offensive to me, as a minister who trusts women to make moral decisions such as whether to terminate a pregnancy, that this statement presumes religious considerations would always be against abortion. In fact, many religions believe the opposite -- that abortion may be the most responsible, most moral decision in some cases.

Justice Owen's writings show lack of awareness about beliefs other than her own, a clear problem in a judicial system that serves people of all faiths and backgrounds. As Jane Doe 1 shows, Justice Owen would place her ideology regarding reproductive rights over the law.

That is tyranny, not justice.

I implore the Senate to continue to use whatever means the Constitution allows to block this nominee, whose record shows she will deprive women of the right of conscience over their personal decisions.

Reverend Rigby is Pastor of St. Andrew's Presbyterian Church, Austin, Texas, and a member of the RCRC of Texas.

The Religious Coalition for Reproductive Choice, founded in 1973, comprises 39 national organizations from 15 denominations, including the Episcopal Church, Presbyterian Church (USA), United Church of Christ, United Methodist Church, Unitarian Universalism, and Reform and Conservative Judaism. The Texas RCRC is one of 24 affiliates throughout the nation.
