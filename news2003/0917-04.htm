Senate Goes On Record For The Third Time Against Drilling In The Arctic Refuge
 

WASHINGTON - September 17 - Statement of Athan Manuel, Director, PIRG Arctic Wilderness Campaign

The U.S. Senate has once again rejected drilling in the coastal plain of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge, one of America's last wild places. Today's release of the Durbin-Feingold letter to the conference committee, when combined with Senator Snowe's Republican letter, demonstrates that at least 43 Senators oppose including drilling in the Arctic Refuge in the final energy bill.

This marks the third time in less than 20 months that the U.S. Senate has rejected drilling in America's Arctic. In March 2003 and April 2002 the Senate rejected attempts to allow drilling in the area.

It is our hope that Sen. Domenici and other drilling supporters on the conference committee will finally recognize what is obvious to a majority of Americans—it just does not make sense to ruin one of America's last wild places for a minuscule amount of oil.

The PIRGs applaud Sens. Richard Durbin, Russ Feingold, and Olympia Snowe for circulating the letters, and congratulate the 43 Senators who signed them. They send a loud and clear message to the Bush administration and their allies in Congress: stop pushing to allow oil and gas drilling in the Arctic Refuge.

The Arctic Refuge deserves continued protection. Congress should pass an energy bill that does not allow for drilling in the Arctic Refuge and other public lands, but instead focuses on cleaner, more efficient, renewable sources of energy.

U.S. PIRG is the national lobby office for the state Public Interest Research Groups. State PIRGs are non-profit, non-partisan public interest advocacy groups.
