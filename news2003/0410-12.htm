Brady Center Report:
Gun Makers, Dealers Knowingly Feed Illegal Gun Market
Evidence from Lawsuits Reveals Industry Misconduct
 
WASHINGTON - April 10 - Previously secret gun industry documents, statements from industry whistle-blowers, and sworn testimony from a host of industry executives obtained in lawsuits show that firearms industry businesses have actively and knowingly allowed guns to be sold into the illegal market, according to a report released today by attorneys at the Brady Center to Prevent Gun Violence.

The report, entitled Smoking Guns: Exposing the Gun Industry's Complicity in the Illegal Gun Market, discusses specific documents and deposition testimony that the industry unsuccessfully tried to keep confidential in court proceedings across the country.

"This reports shows that U.S. firearms manufacturers and gun dealers maintain a distribution system that gives felons access to unlimited arsenals of weapons," said Michael Barnes, president of the Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence. "It's unconscionable to think that the United States Congress is moving rapidly towards granting wide-ranging immunity to an industry that knows how to stop arming our nation's most dangerous criminals, but refuses to change."

Smoking Guns was released one day after the U.S. House of Representatives voted to grant the gun industry unprecedented immunity from lawsuits and limit the legal rights of gun violence victims.

"Before the Congress votes to give the gun industry special immunity from legal accountability, it should get to know the companies it seeks to protect," said Dennis Henigan, Director of the Brady Center's Legal Action Project. "Because of lawsuits targeted by this legislation, the dark underside of the gun industry is exposed for all to see. We now know the sad story of an industry that has aided and abetted the illegal gun market for years and has profited handsomely from it."

The report is available on the Web site of the Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence United with the Million Mom March, at http://www.bradycampaign.org. A media summary follows this release.

The Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence United with the Million Mom March, and its sister organization the Brady Center to Prevent Gun Violence, are dedicated to reducing gun violence in the United States through education, outreach, grassroots mobilization, civil litigation against the gun industry, political action and support for common sense gun safety laws on the local, state and national levels. Formerly known as Handgun Control, the organization fought for passage of the Brady Law, the assault weapons ban and other reasonable laws. Sarah Brady, wife of former Reagan Presidential Press Secretary Jim Brady, serves as Chair of the Brady Campaign.
