Baltimore, MD, Resident To Go On Trial For Nonviolent Protest At Nation's Capitol Opposing Attack On Iraq
 
WASHINGTON - June 11 - On Monday, June 16, 2003, Baltimore, MD, resident Ellen Barfield will go on trial for an act of civil disobedience at the U.S. Capitol on March 17, 2003. During the last days of buildup before the attack on Iraq, Barfield joined a procession to the Capitol, in order to demand that Congress prevent the impending attack.

"At a time when we most need to protect and practice our Constitutional rights to peacefully assemble and to petition our government, we were instead confronted with an arbitrary police line that prevented us from approaching the Capitol," said Barfield. "Although we posed no threat of violence to anyone, we were prevented from going to the Capitol steps purely because of our political speech. Tourists were clearly being allowed to go up the steps that were decided to be off limits for us."

The procession, organized by many groups including the DC Iraq Pledge of Resistance, included roughly 200 people. They carried homemade signs opposing war, photos of Iraqi civilians who would suffer in thelooming attack, and copies of voided blank checks for Congress. A total of 51 were arrested for crossing a Capitol police line in their attempt to bring to the steps of the Capitol their request that Congress take back the blank check for war it gave George Bush in the fall.

For those four who asked for a trial, the charge could result in a fine of $300 or up to 90 days in jail.

The protesters followed a strict code of nonviolence, and interaction with the police was cordial, in spite of the police obstruction of the protesters' rights.

The trial will start at 9am in Courtroom 120 of the Superior Court of the District of Columbia, 500 Indiana Avenue in Washington, DC.

"Attacking Iraq was an immense violation of international law, and made us less safe in the United States," added Barfield. "It is disappointing to find that even protesting the war is now against the law in many instances, but I intend to do everything in my power to continue the struggle through the legal process and through continued actions like the one we took on March 17."

Barfield will also stand trial on Wednesday, June 18 in the same court for a similar action at the White House on March 19 as the attack on Iraq was beginning, and on Friday June 20 in Alexandria, VA, for an action at the Pentagon on Good Friday, April 18.
