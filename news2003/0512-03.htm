Tax Cuts Now Will Hurt Women and Children Later, Association of Maternal and Child Health Programs Statement
 
WASHINGTON - May 12 - Following is a statement of Deborah Dietrich, acting executive director of the Association of Maternal and Child Health Programs on tax cuts:

"Today is an alarming day for women, children and families. The tax cuts passed by the Senate Finance Committee mean there will be less money for important programs that serve our most vulnerable citizens -- low-income, at-risk mothers and children. These families rely on public health programs to get basic prenatal care, immunize their children and diagnose chronic diseases.

"There is no room in today's economy for tax cuts. By increasing deficits and undermining the nation's fiscal stability, tax cuts threaten important national programs that serve families, like the Maternal and Child Health Block Grant, bioterrorism preparedness and funding for welfare programs.

"With looming cuts in Medicaid, increased costs for welfare programs and now tax cuts, state governments are in dire straights. They face the worst fiscal crisis since World War II. Many states tie their income tax levels to federal levels. Should these tax cuts become law, states will experience unplanned drops in their revenues, leaving programs vulnerable to emergency cuts throughout the year.

"States can't do it alone. They need additional funds now to ensure that low-income women receive prenatal care, that hungry children are fed and that families of children with special health needs have access to health care.

"This tax proposal is paid for with fiscally unsound budget gimmicks. To include the dividends adjustment and achieve the $350 billion cut, the Senate and the Bush Administration used some pretty creative accounting - talk about fuzzy math. The proposal doesn't even take into account the cost of war in Iraq.

"Don't be fooled. This tax cut will help wealthy Americans. While upper-class businessmen get tax breaks on their capital gains, low-income women and children are threatened with cuts to public health programs, Medicaid and the State Children's Health Insurance Program (SCHIP).

"During difficult economic times, it's easy to be swept up by the fantasy of lower taxes. But it's precisely during these difficult times that we have to reach out to those most in need. Passing this tax bill now means cuts later to the vital social programs that protect our most vulnerable citizens."

The Association of Maternal and Child Health Programs (AMCHP) represents state public health leaders who work to promote the health and well-being of all women, children and families, including those with special health care needs.
