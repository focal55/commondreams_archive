 CONTACT:  US PIRG / Sierra Club

Energy Bill: The Sequel
Could it be Worse than the Original?
Tuesday Morning Briefing on Key Environmental Issues in the Energy Bill
 

WASHINGTON - May 2 - Press Advisory

Sierra Club--Natural Resources Defense Council--US PIRG--The Wilderness Society--Union of Concerned Scientists--National Environmental Trust--Physicians for Social Responsibility

The Senate is poised to once again take Americans down the path of adestructive, polluting and expensive energy bill. Will the Senate choose tosave oil or increase giveaways to polluters? Will the Senate protect ordevastate our nation's pristine areas? How will this debate impact otherenvironmental protections for future generations? And like most sequels,could this year's energy bill actually be worse than last year's?

Find out everything you need to know about one of the most contentiousissues to come before the U.S. Senate by attending a morning press briefing on energy policy. Experts from America's leading environmental organizations will frame the essential issues that will face the Senate as it debates the energy bill, including: oil savings, polluter subsidies, renewable energy, electricity, global warming, threats to public lands andthe politics driving these issues.

A very short introduction will be followed by a question-and-answer session for journalists.

WHAT: Press Briefing on the Senate Energy Bill (Bagels and Coffee will be served.)

WHEN: Tuesday, May 6th, 9:30 AM

WHERE: The Hart Senate Office Building room 216

WHO:
-- Sierra Club: Daniel Becker, Director of Global Warming and Energy Programs
-- Natural Resources Defense Council: Dan Lashof, Climate Center Science Director
-- U.S. PIRG: Anna Aurilio, Legislative Director
-- The Wilderness Society: Dave Alberswerth, Director of BLM Lands Program
-- Union of Concerned Scientists: Marchant Wentworth, Clean Energy Representative

Additional experts from these and other environmental groups will be available for Q&A.
