Members of Congress, Students and Educators to Host Press Forum Highlighting Negative Impacts of Student Drug Provision
Nearly 100,000 Students Have Been Denied Need-Based Financial Aid, Some for Possession of Single Marijuana Cigarette, Thousands Organize for National Day of Action
 

WASHINGTON - April 3 -

News Advisory:

On April 10th, 2003, thousands of students from approximately 100 colleges and universities across the country will be participating in the Day of Action on the Higher Education Act Drug Provision, organized by Students for Sensible Drug Policy and the United States Student Association. Since its inception in 1998, the Higher Education Act (HEA) drug provision has denied need-based financial aid for higher education to nearly 100,000 students. Aid may be delayed or denied due to minor drug convictions such as possession of a single marijuana cigarette.

Learn more about the National Day of Action, how the 1998 Amendments to the Higher Education Act negatively impacts students, and the campaign to repeal the 1998 drug provision of the HEA at an upcoming press forum:
