Illinois Veterans Fight to Save Health Care
 
BATAVIA, IL - April 10 - A Veterans Town Meeting will draw hundreds of veterans from throughout Illinois in a campaign to protect health care benefits they were promised when they answered their country's call. Congressional representatives have been invited to this important meeting at VFW Post 1197 in Batavia, Ill., on Thursday. April 17, 2003, from 7-9 p.m.

Years of inadequate budgets have forced drastic cuts in health care services to veterans. The Department of Veterans Affairs (VA) health care system is unable to meet the needs of veterans as funding shortfalls have led to a rationing of health care through reduced services, lengthy delays and veterans being turned away completely. Illinois is home to some 925,000 veterans. According to VA statistics, some 200,000 veterans across the country are waiting over six months for a primary health care appointment.

"Together with our fellow veterans from the American Legion and Disabled American Veterans we have called this Town Meeting to focus public attention on the need for full and predictable funding for veterans' health care," Mike Carlson, commander of Batavia VFW Post 1197 said.

The Veterans Town Meeting has been called to urge the Illinois Congressional delegation to support mandatory funding for VA health care.

"The only way veterans will have access to timely, top quality and readily accessible health care is to remove VA healthcare from the appropriations process and mandate a fair and necessary budget for VA," Charles Brimm, commander of the VFW Department of Illinois said.

"This Veterans Town Meeting shows that we were united in battle and that we are united again to defend the VA health care system. No veteran should ever be left behind," Carlson said.
