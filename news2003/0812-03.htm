 CONTACT:  U.S. PIRG

White House Nominee To Head EPA Opposes Clean Air Protections, Wilderness Preservation, And Action To Halt Global Warming
 

WASHINGTON - August 12 - Statement of Emily Figdor, Clean Air Advocate:

The Bush administration has a dismal record of failing to protect America's air, land, and water. Unfortunately, the nomination of Utah Governor Mike Leavitt to head the EPA is just more of the same.

Governor Leavitt has been a vocal supporter of the Bush administration's so-called "Clear Skies Initiative," a bill that would endanger public health and the environment by allowing old and dirty power plants to pump millions of additional tons of pollution into our skies.

Leavitt supports the Bush's administration's do-nothing approach to global warming and opposes the Kyoto Protocol.

In April 2003, Leavitt cut a secret deal with Interior Department Secretary Gale Norton that will leave millions of acres of pristine lands open to mining, drilling, road-building, and other development.

The Bush administration should reverse course and stand with the public not the polluters.

U.S. PIRG is the national advocacy office for the state Public Interest Research Groups. State PIRGs are non-profit, non-partisan public interest advocacy groups.
