Potential Livermore Lab Espionage Incident Report Missing from Files
 
WASHINGTON - April 15 - According to a whistleblower and back up documents, another potential espionage incident involving a Chinese national occurred under the watch of the Lawrence Livermore Counter-Intelligence Chief who is now under investigation. Documents detailing the incident are mysteriously missing from official files.

A whistleblower from the Lawrence Livermore National Lab (LLNL) came to POGO over a year ago with concerns of security problems at Livermore. One of his allegations involved what he believed was a potential espionage case involving a Chinese national who was an employee at the lab. When he raised questions about why nothing was being done to pursue this case, and other concerns of the security force, he was forced to resign.

Now that questions are being raised about the reliability of the recently resigned Counter-Intelligence Chief at Livermore, William Cleveland, for being sexually involved with an alleged Chinese double agent, this case has taken on new relevance.

Mark Danielson was a member of the SWAT team at Livermore in 1998. According to a July 8, 1998 Incident Report #980708-05, during a random check at the South Gate of the trunk of a vehicle belonging to an employee who was a Chinese national, the officers found a Kodak camera and a Sony tape recorder under a blanket. They also found a briefcase containing a laptop computer, belonging to another Livermore employee. According to Danielson, this computer was later determined to contain classified information. All three of these items are prohibited from being brought into the lab. The security officers were ordered by higher-ups to give the items back to the detained employee, and allow him to enter the facility. According to Danielson, he was miffed by this action. When he complained about the handling of the case, DOE Security Czar Eugene Habiger, wrote “a check of that database showed no identical or similar incident for the entire period during which Mr. Danielson was employed.” William Cleveland was responsible for investigating such incidences at Livermore at the time.

“I’d like to see the Department of Energy and the University of California explain this one,” said POGO Senior Investigator Peter Stockton.

William Cleveland, a former FBI official in San Francisco, was the lead agent on a number of Chinese espionage investigations, including “Tiger Trap” in the 1980s which involved the transfer of classified information on the neutron bomb to China. In 1993, he left the Bureau to become the Chief of Counter-Intelligence at Lawrence Livermore National Lab, which is operated by the University of California. According to published reports, he became intimate with an alleged FBI double agent, and has been stripped of his clearance and access to the lab.
