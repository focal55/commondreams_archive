TomPaine.com Takes on the FCC's Rush to Judgment
 
WASHINGTON - May 21 - TomPaine.com today challenges FCC Chairman Michael Powell's push to adopt new rules for media ownership. "Why is it in the national interest to put control over information into fewer hands?" asks the Op Ad, published today in The New York Times. TomPaine.com also challenges the Commission's independence and asks whether it can make an objective decision in the public interest. TomPaine.com will publish Powell's responses to these, and other questions, if he provides them.

The Op Ad frames the regulatory issue in the context of its broadest impact: "Informed and diverse discourse is the oxygen of democracy. The body politic suffocates without it. Michael Powell ... is supposed to protect civic discourse. It's threatened now -- just six companies own most print, radio, Internet and television media outlets. Mr. Powell thinks that's too much diversity....he thinks Americans can trust a few elite CEOs to tell us what we need to know to govern ourselves."

The Op Ad charges that with increased media concentration there will be less information and more "infotainment."

Questions about the agency's independence will be raised tomorrow when the Center for Public Integrity releases a new investigation and database that will reveal just how cozy the FCC is with media owners.

Two FCC Commissioners have asked for a one-month delay beyond Powell's June 2 deadline and even though such requests are normally granted, Powell has refused.

To view the ad, visit http://www.tompaine.com/op_ads/.

TomPaine.com is a nonprofit, nonpartisan Internet journal. Since 1999, its online content and ads have been praised by Rolling Stone, Forbes.com, the Columbia Journalism Review, Chicago Tribune, PC Magazine and many others.
