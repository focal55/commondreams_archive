Justice for Gun Victims Praises Howard Dean for Opposing Legal Immunity for Gun Industry; Calls on Other Candidates to Do Same
 
WASHINGTON - April 10 - Justice for Gun Victims today praised former Vermont Governor Howard Dean for opposing attempts to immunize the gun industry from legal action. Dean's announcement came during a Children's Defense Fund forum featuring all nine Democratic presidential candidates, and just hours after the full U.S. House of Representatives passed H.R. 1036, a bill that shields gun makers and dealers from legal action even if they act irresponsibly.

In response to a question by journalist Mark Shields, Dean said, as president, he would veto H.R. 1036 and any legislation that singles out the gun industry for special protection. Dean also voiced support for renewing the federal assault weapons ban, which expires in 2004, and for closing the gun show loophole.

Justice for Gun Victims urged the other eight presidential candidates to strongly oppose H.R. 1036 and S. 659, its Senate companion.

"Governor Dean's opposition to granting the gun industry special legal protections shows how extreme and unwise this measure is," said Joshua Horwitz, executive director of the Coalition to Stop Gun Violence, the organization spearheading the Justice for Gun Victims campaign. "We urge the rest of the Democratic presidential field to join Governor Dean in standing up for the rights of gun violence victims."

H.R. 1036 was rushed through the House in an effort to throw out a case filed against several gun makers and dealers by the NAACP. The trial, currently being held in a Brooklyn federal court, seeks no monetary damages -- only the reform of gun industry practices that aid criminal access to guns.

On Monday, Robert Ricker, a former high-ranking gun industry insider, testified in the NAACP trial that some gun makers and dealers have known for years that their business practices aid criminal gun access, but have chosen to do nothing about it.

Justice for Gun Victims is a collaboration of domestic violence prevention, civil rights, and gun violence prevention organizations dedicated to stopping the gun lobby's attempt to grant the firearms industry legal immunity. For additional information, visit http://www.justiceforgunvictims.org.
