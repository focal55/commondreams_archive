NLC Council on Youth Education and Families Meeting in Nashville to Discuss Head Start Reauthorization
 
NASHVILLE, TN - May 15 - The National League of Cities (NLC) Council on Youth Education and Families will meet in Nashville, Tenn., May 16, 2003, to discuss the reauthorization of the Head Start program, its success stories, and the Bush Administration proposed changes. New Haven Conn., Mayor John DeStefano, NLC's President, chose early childhood development as the top issue for his year as president.

"Since the inception of the program in 1965, more than 20 million children and their families have benefited from the Head Start program," said Nashville Mayor Bill Purcell, chair of the Council and host of the meeting. "While Head Start has always been a bi-partisan legislative success story, this year marks the most critical point in its history. An important part of the success of Head Start is the partnership the federal government has had with local agencies to serve young children and their families.

The NLC opposes the administrations plan to block grant the Head Start program to the states for several reasons:

-- Community control would be undermined by giving states more authority over Head Start; -- Block-granting to states will weaken program performance standards; -- Block-granting will require more money for administration, reducing funds available for children; and -- State control of Head Start is not necessary for state collaboration on programs.

NLC's position is that Head Starts comprehensive approach must be maintained and is best accomplished by keeping it at HHS. The key to Head Start's success is the full range of services provided to at-risk children, namely, nutrition, immunizations, health/dental care, parental involvement, early learning in literacy, numeracy and socialization skills.

"Head Start funding should be increased to provide learning opportunities to all eligible children," said Mayor DeStefano. "Head Start is a proven investment and the federal government should increase its financial commitment. Administration proposals to block grant Head Start funding to states and move its administration from the Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) to the Department of Education (DOE), coupled with inadequate funding commitment, could seriously undermine Head Start."

The National League of Cities is the largest national organization for American cities. NLC serves as a resource and advocate for 18,000 cities, towns and villages of all sizes, from New York City to Bee Cave, Texas, which collectively serve 225 million people. Visit http://www.nlc.org.
