FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
SEPTEMBER 12, 2003
3:38 PM
 CONTACT: 9/11 Environmental Action
 Kimberly Flynn, Cell: 718-812-8220
Press Conference Monday 9/15 10:00 Will Call for Investigation of WTC Contaminants, Proper Cleanup, and Health Care for Those Affected
 
NEW YORK - September 12 - On Monday, September 15 - Downtown Residents, Workers, Physicians & Community Advocates Demand the Truth About Toxic Hazards from the WTC Disaster and Cleanup of All Homes and Workplaces.

Inspector General’s Report Confirms that EPA Has Yet to Carry Out a Proper Testing and Cleanup Program in Lower Manhattan and Brooklyn. The health of New Yorkers must come first, before any “competing concerns”of politics and business.

What: Community residents, workers, and others reject the rationale that the health of tens of thousands of people should have been put at risk so that Wall Street could re-open, and demand:

  • A full investigation into how accurate information was withheld about toxic hazards from the WTC disaster
  • Proper testing and cleanup of affected homes and workplaces in compliance with all federal environmental & worker safety laws
  • Health care for all of those suffering from WTC-related illnesses, now and in the future

When: 10:00 am, Monday, Sept 15, 2003

Where: Steps of Federal Hall, corner of Wall and Broad Streets.

Who: Stephen M. Levin, M.D., Co-Director, Mount Sinai-Selikoff Center for Occupational and Environmental Medicine

Suzanne Mattei, Executive for Sierra Club national field office in NYC

Joel Shufro, Executive Director, NY Committee for Occupational Safety & Health

Jo Polett, downtown resident, member 9/11 Environmental Action

Bob Gulack, office worker, Woolworth Building, NTEU

Mae Lee, Chinese Progressive Association

Pawel Kedzior, Local 78, LIUNA

And other residents and workers will be present with their stories.
