Iraq Peace Team Members Travel From Bagdad to Amman;
Available for Interviews About Life in Bagdad During the War
 
AMMAN - April 2 - Departing from Iraq has now become increasingly unsafe and costly. Communication within and beyond Iraq has been effectively silenced. Facing these conditions, the Iraq Peace Team has elected to send half of our members outside of Iraq to give eye-witness accounts of the calamity that now afflicts the Iraqi people.

The Iraq Peace Team is a project of Voices in the Wilderness and Christian Peacemaker Teams to maintain an ongoing presence in Iraq during the war, in order to be a voice for the Iraqi people to the West. IPT is not a "human shields" group. For more information on IPT and team members remaining in Iraq, please visit our website at: http://www.iraqpeaceteam.org

Team members arrived in Amman Tuesday evening to:

i-immediately give witness to their experiences at the bedsides of victims, at the homes of families devastated by the bombing campaign, and within the settings where they have lived during the first two weeks of the war

ii-undertake efforts to further the antiwar movement, worldwide, which calls for an immediate halt to the aggression presently waged against Iraqi civilians

Consistent with the principles of the Iraq Peace Team, our group will continue, in Baghdad, to be independent of both government policy and financing. It is hoped that we will be a small light in a very dark scene.
