Latin Patriarch of Jerusalem Holds Press Conference During New York City Visit
 
NEW YORK - July 28 - WHO: Latin Patriarch of Jerusalem, His Beatitude Michel Sabbah. Msgr. Sabbah is a key figure in the Middle East peace process. He is the former President of the Middle East Council of Churches, experienced with ecumenical and inter-religious dialogue. Msgr. Sabbah has repeatedly called the attention of the international community to the root causes that spawn the conditions of hatred and violence—especially in Israel/Palestine, but also across the globe—such as the growing disparities between the rich and poor, the denial of human dignity, the lack of respect for human rights and fundamental freedoms, social injustice, intolerable refugee situations, internal and external displacement and physical or psychological oppression. In his own country, Msgr. Sabbah continues to call for greater justice for the Palestinian people so that Israelis and Palestinians can live together in peace and mutual trust. He has been the president of Pax Christi International since 1999.

Joining Msgr. Sabbah for the press conference will be Etienne De Jonghe, Secretariat of Pax Christi International, and Marie Dennis, Director of Maryknoll Office for Global Concerns and Vice-President of Pax Christi International. Pax Christi International is the international Catholic peace movement with sections in over 30 countries.

WHAT: Press conference.

WHEN: Friday, August 1, 2003 at 11:30 a.m.

WHERE: United Nations Church Center, Second Floor, 777 U.N. Plaza (corner of 44th Street and First Avenue), New York City.

WHY: Msgr. Sabbah will be addressing the current situation in Palestine/Israel, his analysis of the “road map” for peace in the Middle East, the impact and role of the Catholic Church in the Middle East, and the role of the Bush Administration in the peace process. Msgr. Sabbah is in the U.S. for meetings in Washington D.C. and New York City. Prior to the press conference, Msgr. Sabbah will have just completed participation in a conference on global peacemaking, Preemptive Peace: Beyond Terrorism and Justified War at St. John’s University. On Friday evening, August 1, he will be the keynote speaker at Pax Christi USA’s national assembly, A World on the Brink: A Gated Empire or Global Solidarity?, also at St. John’s University.
