Clean Air Trust To EPA's Jeffrey Holmstead: Read Your Own EPA Press Releases
 
WASHINGTON - April 23 - EPA's flurry of cheery pre-Earth Day announcements caused us to stop and think. The truth is, we have been puzzling for some time about the remarks of EPA Assistant Administrator Jeffrey Holmstead, who has argued repeatedly that the Clean Air Act's new source review program does not reduce emissions (1). We find his diehard views a curious contrast to various EPA press releases (including one this week involving Virginia Electric Power) touting pollution reductions from enforcement of new source review.

So we decided to list and add up the results of all the press releases in one place, so the casual reader can decide who's right -- Jeffrey Holmstead, or his EPA press office. (Hint: EPA's own press releases list 1.3 million tons of annual emissions reduced by new source review settlements in the past three years. All to be done within a decade. The final resolution of one announced settlement is still pending. To put this in context, that's actually more cleanup -- and a lot sooner -- than EPA says would be accomplished by its very positive and much-touted proposal to clean up diesel heavy equipment and fuel -- 950,000 tons by 2030. Jeffrey, are you paying attention? It seems like we're doing pretty well by enforcing the law you want to change.)

(1) Take, for example, this passage from BNA's Daily Environment Report, dated September 7, 2001: "...In contrast, the new source review program does not achieve emissions reductions at existing sources, Holmstead said...`For the vast majority of these sources that have essentially continued to operate in the same way, it doesn't get any emissions reductions from those facilities,' Holmstead said. `They can continue to operate as long as they want to.'" Of course, the totals listed in this release are only those resulting from legal settlements. Pollution reductions from those following the law would be additional.

(2) This is a company projection, as reported by Cox News Service. It is consistent with EPA's press release.

Jeffrey, please take note!
