Planned Parenthood Tells Senate to Stop the Court Stacking
 
WASHINGTON - April 28 - The following statement of Gloria Feldt, president of Planned Parenthood Federation of America, on judicial nominations, was released today:

This coming week, the Senate will tackle a triumvirate of judicial nominees who oppose women's reproductive freedom. All three nominees must be rejected.

The president's nominee to the Eastern District of Arkansas, James Leon Holmes, has worked throughout his career to eradicate women's reproductive freedom. He served as president of Arkansas Right to Life and as secretary of the Unborn Child Amendment Committee.

Judge Carolyn Kuhl, nominee to the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals, used her position in the Reagan-era Justice Department to advocate for the reversal of Roe v. Wade. While in private practice, Kuhl argued in favor of a "gag rule" that would have prohibited health care providers who receive Title X funds from counseling women about abortion.

Texas Supreme Court Justice Priscilla Owen has demonstrated her willingness to dismantle women's civil rights by legislating from the bench. Last year, the Senate Judiciary Committee did an extensive review of Justice Owen's record. After careful deliberation, her nomination to the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Fifth Circuit was rejected. And her nomination should be rejected again now that the president has resubmitted it.

The U.S. Senate must give civil rights more than lip service by rejecting all of these nominations.

President Bush is poisoning the judicial system, weaving a pernicious web of attacks on women's reproductive rights. We call on the Senate to require every judicial nominee to affirm fundamental civil rights, including a woman's right to make her own childbearing choices. And we call on the president to stop stacking the courts against women's civil rights.

Planned Parenthood Federation of America is the nation's largest and most trusted voluntary reproductive health care organization. Planned Parenthood affiliates serve nearly five million women, men and teenagers every year.
