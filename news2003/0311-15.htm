PBM Drug Discount Cards Provide Significant Savings to Uninsured Seniors;
Cards Save Hundreds of Dollars a Year on Prescription Drugs
 
ARLINGTON, VA - March 11 - Drug discount cards offered by pharmacy benefit managers (PBMs) provide significant relief to uninsured senior citizens who are paying the highest prices for their prescription drugs, according to a comprehensive new study released today by Brandeis University. The study, based on actual claims data from pharmacies, found that using a PBM discount card saves senior citizens money - an average of 15 percent off the retail price of medications charged by pharmacies for each prescription filled. These savings are realized at chain and independent pharmacies in both rural and urban states, according to Brandeis, and are in addition to any discounts uninsured seniors may receive from pharmacies.

"Today's Brandeis study shows conclusively that uninsured senior citizens -- who can least afford the high drug prices charged by retail pharmacies -- benefit from using PBM discount cards. This is true whether they live in rural or urban areas of America," said LaVarne Burton, President of the Pharmaceutical Care Management Association (PCMA). "In addition to these savings revealed in the Brandeis report, PBMs are also able to offer senior citizens improved safety and convenient access to medications."

Specifically, the Brandeis study found the average savings achieved through PBM discount cards for generic medications was 26 percent, or $7 for each prescription, and the average discount for brand name drugs was 14 percent, or $11 for each prescription. The overall average discounted price for prescription drugs purchased using a discount card was 15 percent, or $10 dollars for each prescription filled. Burton noted, "Drug discount cards, when offered in conjunction with a partially funded pharmacy benefit, would likely result in even greater cost savings for seniors."

Brandeis also found that discount cards save senior citizens who require multiple medications, as most seniors do, hundreds of dollars for a year's supply of drugs. In one profile, Brandeis found that a Medicare participant who has mild heart failure, high cholesterol and osteoporosis and whose doctor prescribes furosemide, Lipitor, Fosamax and hydrocodone as needed for joint pain would be charged $2,542 a year for these medications by her local pharmacy. If that same senior had a discount card the savings would be $525, or 21 percent.

The Brandeis study is a more comprehensive examination of the savings achieved by PBM discount cards than a December 2001 General Accounting Office report. Whereas the GAO report was based on a survey of prices for 17 common medications, Brandeis examined more than 3.5 million claims for 124 commonly used brand and generic drugs submitted for payment by pharmacies participating in eight discount card programs. These claims contained the actual usual-and-customary prices retail pharmacies would have charged senior citizens if they did not have a PBM discount card. As a result of this more comprehensive data, Brandeis found the actual savings resulting from the use of PBM discount cards were more significant than those determined by GAO.

The Brandeis study's findings are particularly relevant since most proposals to provide a Medicare prescription drug benefit have gaps that would require senior citizens to pay significant out of pocket expenses. This study is further evidence that private-sector innovations such as PBM drug discount cards could help reduce seniors' out of pocket expenditures.

For a link to the full Brandeis report, please visit http://www.PCMAnet.org.

PBMs are companies that administer nearly 70 percent of the more than three billion prescriptions dispensed in the United States each year. Utilizing discount negotiations, generic drugs, high-tech safety procedures and mail service, PBMs are the best way for America to get prescription drugs.

PCMA represents pharmacy benefit managers and their health care partners in pharmaceutical care. PCMA provides leadership and standards of excellence to assure the availability of quality, safe, and cost-effective pharmaceutical care services.
