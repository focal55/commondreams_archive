LCV: House Energy Bill Ignores U.S. Priorities;
GOP Energy Plan Fails to Reduce Dependence on Foreign Oil or Bolster Economy
 
WASHINGTON - April 8 - This week the U.S. House of Representatives will debate an energy plan (H.R. 6) that, if passed, would seriously weaken America's energy future and economic security, according to the League of Conservation Voters.

The bill includes provisions to give $12.1 billion in tax breaks to polluting industries such as oil and gas, but would do next to nothing to help Americans use fuel more efficiently or obtain more energy from renewable sources. H.R. 6 would also open the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge to oil and gas drilling and allow energy companies unprecedented access to millions of acres of federal lands for oil and gas exploration.

"Republican House leaders have ignored America's security needs by pushing an energy bill that fails to reduce our dependence on foreign oil or strengthen our economy," said Deb Callahan, League of Conservation Voters president. "Voters want a cleaner, healthier, safer, and more affordable energy future, but the Republican energy plan rewards the oil and gas industry with billions of dollars in tax breaks and unfettered access to our natural treasures."

A March 2003 Gallup poll found that 60 percent of Americans want a solution to our energy problems that focuses on conservation of existing energy supplies over greater production of oil and gas. The poll also showed that 73 percent of Americans favor higher fuel efficiency standards for automobiles. Republican drafters of this energy plan failed to include either in H.R. 6. Instead, the bill focuses on rewarding the oil, gas, and coal industries with $12.1 billion in tax breaks and the promise of much more revenue from drilling and mining on public land. According to the Center for Responsive Politics, the oil and gas industry invested $19.2 million in Republican campaigns in 2002 and $27 million in 2000 -- almost 80 percent of their total campaign contributions.

The nonprofit League of Conservation Voters (LCV) is the political voice of the national environmental and conservation community. LCV is the only national organization working full-time to hold Congress and the president accountable for their environmental actions.
