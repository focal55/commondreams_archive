Virtual Wednesday in Washington Against Tax Cuts for Rich
Thousands to Tell Congress: Reject Tax Cuts for Millionaires
 
WASHINGTON - May 12 - News Advisory:

Hundreds of thousands of people from across the country will call, email and fax Congress Wednesday to urge that Congress reject tax cuts that sacrifice protections for children to pay for massive new tax cuts for the rich. The Children's Defense Fund will join with the AFL-CIO, Moveon.org, the Fair Taxes for All Coalition and people all across the country to tell Congress to oppose irresponsible tax cuts.

Black Entertainment Television reported (Apr. 10) that thousands of Wednesday in Washington student activists handed out "$62,500 checks to a Millionaire from their college fund" to Senators and Reps. The Washington Post (Eilperin, Mar. 24) reported that "hundreds of thousands" of Wednesday in Washington activists jammed the Capitol switchboard in March to stop the Bush Administration's budget and tax war on children.

Based on a similar initiative used by the Civil Rights Movement of the 1960s, the Children's Defense Fund's Wednesdays in Washington efforts call on people to take action through personal visits, phone calls, letters, e-mail, faxes, prayers, and other activities to alert leaders about what can be done to truly Leave No Child Behind(r).

VIRTUAL WEDNESDAY IN WASHINGTON

DATE: Wednesday, May 14

TIME: All day

LOCATION: Media representatives interested in covering a phone bank location should contact Toby Chaudhuri at 202-662-3609 or tchaudhuri@childrensdefense.org

PARTICIPANTS: Hundreds of thousands of activists
