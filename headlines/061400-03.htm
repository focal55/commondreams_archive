Published on Wednesday, June 14, 2000 at 2:12 PM by the Associated Press
Ralph Nader Wins Endorsement Of Big California Nurses Union
 
WASHINGTON - Green Party presidential hopeful Ralph Nader picked up the endorsement of a big nurses' union in California, a state where his candidacy might have an impact on the general election.

The California Nurses Association, a 31,000-member union based in Oakland, praised Nader as committed to universal health care, patients' rights and expanded federal health insurance for Americans.

"He is the real thing, and he deserves the support of caregivers," Kay McVay, president of the association, said in a statement Wednesday.

Nader has been doing better than expected in some national polls, with some showing him at 4 to 5 percent in a statistical tie with Reform Party contender Pat Buchanan. But an ABC News-Washington Post poll released Tuesday indicated that including Buchanan and Nader in a four-way race did not alter the dynamics of the race between Republican George W. Bush and Democrat Al Gore significantly.

Nader, who has run for president several times in the past, earlier this month again selected American Indian activist Winona LaDuke as his vice presidential running mate.
