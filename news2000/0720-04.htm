For Tosco, Does 'Green' Mean Green?
 
WASHINGTON - July 20 - The following was released today by Clean Air Trust:

CLEAN AIR BULLETIN

TO: EDITORS AND REPORTERS COVERING THE ENVIRONMENT
FROM: Frank O'Donnell
DATE: July 20, 2000

For Tosco, Does 'Green' Mean Green?

The nation's major oil companies began reporting the second-quarter profits this morning. First out of the gate was the Tosco Corp., which reported that profits jumped 71 percent from the same period a year ago. The $145 million in second-quarter profits was nearly double the $74.9 million that Tosco made in the first three months of this year.

Today's announcement was in line with recent analysts' expectations. Based on an analysis of other forecasts, we are expecting that major oil companies will announce that they collectively made more than $12 billion in profits in the second quarter. (Profit announcements will continue into early August.)

Tosco, the nation's largest independent refiner, is an outspoken supporter of the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency's proposal to clean up diesel fuel in order to clean up diesel trucks and buses. Not coincidentally, Tosco is the oil company that receives the highest "buy" rating by stock analysts surveyed by the First Call Company.

For Tosco, does being "green" (environmentally) lead to green (money)? There is evidence that it does. For while Tosco has had its share of environmental problems (for example, it has come under sharp criticism for emissions from its Linden, N.J. refinery), the company clearly is positioning itself to profit by tougher standards on fuel. For example, Tosco recently announced it would purchase a large refinery in Louisiana. The company noted the purchase would enable it to supply large volumes of low-sulfur gasoline and diesel fuel -- and make money. "As a result of these efforts (including sale of another refinery), Tosco should show strong growth not only in 2000 but continue into 2001 and beyond," Tosco's chairman and CEO Thomas P. O'Malley said today.

Other oil companies take note: you, too, can clean up and be profitable.
