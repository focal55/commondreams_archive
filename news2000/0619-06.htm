VPC Web Site Decimated by Pro-Gun Hacker
 
WASHINGTON - June 19 - This morning, the Violence Policy Center (VPC) learned that its Online Resource Center web site, www.vpc.org, was completely erased by a pro-gun hacker who replaced the site with one featuring a bomb, skull and crossbones, obscenities, and links to pro-gun web sites, including the National Rifle Association.

The hacked web site states, "VPC just got shot in the head." The VPC has reported this violation of federal law to the Federal Bureau of Investigation and Violence Policy Center Executive Director Josh Sugarmann today released the following statement:

The complete destruction of the VPC's Online Resource Center web site is the latest example of the extremism of the pro-gun fringe. The web site, which was the leading source in the nation for information on the gun lobby, gun industry, and gun death and injury in America, was a resource for policymakers, activists, the media, and the general public. This destruction of property and erasure of information is a gauge of the fear and desperation of pro-gunners and a measure of the lengths to which they will go. Only fact-based information can counter the lies of the gun lobby. The gun lobby promises us that their supporters are "law-abiding citizens." This act of Internet terrorism proves that such claims are hollow.

Copies of the home page of both the original and now-hacked VPC web site are available from the VPC.

------
The Violence Policy Center is a national nonprofit educational organization working to reduce gun death and injury in America.
