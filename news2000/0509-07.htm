Budget Watch Group Criticizes Bush's Social Security Plan
 
WASHINGTON - May 9 - Social Security privatization recently joined "rebuilding America's defense" on presidential candidate George W. Bush's campaign plate. However, a close examination shows that Bush's plan will likely create a "death spiral" for Social Security and national defense that would harm seniors and soldiers alike.

"While Wall Street brokers have done wonders for the private economy, their track record is quite different when it comes to comes to government security programs," says Scott Nathanson, Executive Director of Citizens for a Responsible Budget (CRB), a D.C.-based budget watchdog group. "Bush's plan is to take an idea that failed our national security and foist it on Social Security."

CRB's first issue of its New View briefing paper series, entitled 'Wall Street, Weapons Contractors, & Privatization' shows how investment brokers' involvement with defense policy was supposed to benefit soldiers and taxpayers alike, but failed miserably. "Defense contractors and Clinton administration officials were pushed by investment brokers to consider massive mergers," the paper says. "Defense specialists promised that these mega-mergers would produce streamlined production and research facilities that could produce next-generation weapons at a far lower cost to the taxpayer."

Once the mergers cleared anti-trust hurdles, "Wall Street bought every kind of defense stock..." But while Wall Street rode the tide of swelling defense stocks, the General Accounting Office concluded last year that these mergers created no cost savings for the American taxpayer. Further, Pentagon officials complained that the lack of competition caused by these mergers actually harms weapons development. As one retired official said, "I always got more new ideas when I have several companies trying to think up products to win a contract."

According to Nathanson, "Gov. Bush is singing that same siren song to the taxpayer all over again. Brokers are once more in position to make a killing off of the taxpayer, this time by making commissions on privatized Social Security accounts irregardless of market fluctuations. Meanwhile, those who need the "security" of Social Security the most will get a cut in base benefits, and an increase in retirement age. It seems like quite a risk given what happened last time around."

Nathanson concluded that Bush's privatization efforts plus his plans to dramatically boost weapons spending creates a two-headed monster that would eat at both sides of Social Security. "A massive increase in new weapons procurement will without doubt leach from the budget surplus that should provide for the solvency of Social Security. We will have more national and social security for less money if we provide for the readiness of our soldiers rather than the desires of defense contractors, and keep Wall Street from meddling in yet another of our nation's most important programs."
