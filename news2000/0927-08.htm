Democrats Admit Defeat; Nader on Illinois Ballot
 
CHICAGO, IL - September 27 - Today the Democratic machine, in defeat, withdrew all of their objections to the petitions submitted by Ralph Nader’s presidential campaign in Illinois. Admitting that Nader campaign volunteers have collected more than the required 25,000 signatures for ballot access, the Democrats withdrew their objection at a court hearing in Chicago Sept. 27.

The state board of elections is expected to next week officially place Nader on the Illinois ballot. The state board counted at least 27,698 valid signatures.

“This is a tremendous victory for the more than 150 different volunteers who have worked tirelessly on this battle for the ballot in Illinois for the past few weeks,” said Dan Johnson-Weinberger, coordinator for the Nader 2000 Campaign in Illinois.

“This victory is infusing the campaign with tremendous energy as we are selling tickets for our Chicago Super Rally.”

The Chicago Super Rally—the fifth in a series of Nader super rallies that are drawing audiences of more than 10,000 -- will take place on Oct. 10 at 7:30 p.m. at the University of Illinois at Chicago Pavilion.

A press conference will be held tomorrow at the Nader 2000 field office in Chicago to discuss the ballot victory and the Chicago Super Rally.  The press conference will take place Thursday, Sept. 28, at 11 a.m. at 59 East Van Buren, 14th floor.  For more information, please call 312-588-0974.
