NARAL Hails Approval Of Mifepristone (RU 486) as Major Advancement In Women's Health
 
WASHINGTON - September 28 - The following was released today by NARAL:

After more than a decade of delays at the hands of anti-choice activists and lawmakers, mifepristone, commonly known as RU 486, today received final approval from the FDA. NARAL President Kate Michelman immediately hailed the announcement as "a tremendous advance for women's health and reproductive rights."

"Mifepristone's approval is one of the most significant advances in women's reproductive health that we have seen in decades," said Michelman. "Its approval today is yet another milestone on the long road to women's reproductive freedom and equality in this country."

"The FDA's action today opens doors long closed to American women, but the benefits of mifepristone will reach far beyond the realm of reproductive freedom and choice," said Michelman. "The potential mifepristone holds for the treatment and possible eradication of numerous diseases could mean an end to the suffering of millions of Americans."

More than 500,000 women in Europe have used mifepristone safely for over a decade. It was proven safe and effective following clinical trials here in the U.S. Mifepristone is effective for terminating early pregnancies up to 49 days from the beginning of a woman's last menstrual cycle. It has also shown promise in the treatment of numerous medical conditions, including breast cancer, HIV, Cushing's syndrome, and endometriosis.

In praising the FDA's action, Michelman also pointed out the tenuous nature of mifepristone's approval status.

"As we celebrate this victory, we must remain vigilant. Anti-choice lawmakers in the House repeatedly tried to block FDA approval of mifepristone and are unlikely to halt their efforts now. George W. Bush has also stated his opposition to mifepristone and has said he would be 'inclined not to accept' the FDA's ruling if it approved the drug," said Michelman. "Al Gore supports the approval of mifepristone. We must ensure that our next president supports making this important reproductive health option available to American women. We must also return the House to the hands of a leadership that will work to expand women's reproductive health options, not curtail them."
