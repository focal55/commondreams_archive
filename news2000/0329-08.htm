'Don't Duck Contraceptive Coverage,' ZPG's Kostmayer Urges Bush, Lawmakers
 
TALLAHASSEE, FL - March 29 - Family planning campaigner Peter H. Kostmayer, executive director of the environmental organization Zero Population Growth, is urging Governor Jeb Bush and state law makers to "end an appalling discrimination against Florida women" and support an all-out effort to require health insurers to comprehensively cover contraceptives.

"Full contraceptive coverage is good for women, good for business and good for equality," says Kostmayer, a former seven-term U.S. Congressman.

The effort, if translated into law, would make it illegal for health insurers to refuse women coverage of all five FDA-approved methods of contraception (see note A). It would slash health costs for millions of women statewide. It would also provide a formidable weapon against the vexing problem of unintended pregnancy.

"Half of all the pregnancies in America every year are unintended. 30 percent of all the births result from unintended pregnancy. That means that in Florida, almost 60,000 births are the result of unintended pregnancy. Equally disturbing is the fact that women have to pay almost 70 percent more than men in out-of-pocket healthcare costs. Access to affordable contraceptives is the key to breaking this cycle. A comprehensive contraceptive coverage law would change the situation dramatically," Kostmayer stressed.

Opponents of the legislation only examine the short term costs of providing contraceptive coverage but fail to mention the big medium and long-term savings. For example an insurance industry study concluded that providing comprehensive coverage for contraceptives would cost only $16 per person per year as compared to $5,512 for prenatal care and delivery. And while the vast majority of insurance plans cover child birth and abortion, they do not cover the relatively inexpensive contraception.

Kostmayer added, "Since thousands of abortions in Florida result from unintended pregnancy, even anti-choice lawmakers can join us in supporting this critical effort."

------
Note (A): Five FDA-approved methods of contraception: oral, injectable, inserts, IUD and diaphragm.

Zero Population Growth (ZPG) is America's premier non-profit environmental organization working to educate people about the impact of rapid population growth. ZPG believes that by tackling population growth, our nation can improve and sustain a better quality of life for everyone living here. ZPG's Executive Director is Peter H. Kostmayer-the former U.S. Congressman and EPA Regional Administrator.
