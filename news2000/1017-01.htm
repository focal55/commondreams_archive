Demonstrations Target CITIGROUP in 50 Cities
Groups Call on Banking Giant to Stop Funding Environmental Destruction
 

NEW YORK/SAN FRANCISCO - October 17 - In more than 50 cities around the world today

WHO: Rainforest Action Network, Inner City Press/Community on the Move, New York Direct Action Network, and university student organizations from more than 30 campuses.

WHAT: Demonstrations in more than 50 cities across the country targeting Citigroup. This first International Day of Protest targeting Citigroup will draw public attention to the preeminent role of the US’s largest financial institution in funding environmentally destructive projects around the globe. Student organizers will be adding thousands of names to the list of some 10,000 students across the U.S. who already pledged to boycott Citigroup credit cards and bank accounts.

WHEN: Tuesday, October 17, 2000, in San Francisco: 12 Noon

WHERE: At Citibank branches and university campuses in more than 50 cities across the US including New York, Boston, New Haven, Washington DC, Seattle, San Francisco, Chicago, and Los Angeles. In San Francisco: Citicorp Bldg., corner of Sansome and Post

WHY: Organizations at the forefront of recent non-violent mobilization protesting corporate-led globalization (WTO, World Bank/IMF, and the Republican and Democratic National Conventions) are now turning their attention to Citigroup. Citi is the largest financial institution in the United States and the #1 bank-roller of environmental destruction resulting from corporate globalization. Through financing and underwriting projects, Citi promotes ecological disasters around the globe, from the cutting of the last ancient Redwood groves in California, to the construction of crude oil pipelines through pristine ecosystems in Cameroon. Despite the size of its extensive funding portfolio, Citigroup has virtually no social or environmental criteria to screen the projects it backs. Students across the U.S. are joining the international campaign to demand that Citigroup turn around its environmental practices and become the industry leader environmental sustainability.

For more information on the Citigroup’s environmental and social damage around the globe, please visit: www.ran.org
