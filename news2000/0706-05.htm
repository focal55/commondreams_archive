Common Cause Calls For Disclosure for Convention Host Committees
 

WASHINGTON - July 6 - The following is the body of a letter sent yesterday from Common Cause President Scott Harshbarger to the heads of the "host committees" of the Republican and Democratic National Conventions. The letter calls on Noelia Rodriguez, President of LA Convention 2000, and Karen Dougherty Buchholz, President of Philadelphia 2000, to take the steps necessary to ensure prompt and full disclosure of the reported $70 million raised by the committees.

July 5, 2000

According to recent reports, almost $70 million has been raised by the host committees of the Republican and Democratic National Conventions. The level of convention fundraising is on pace to shatter all previous records. The large majority of this money is being contributed by special interests, many of which have pressing issues before Congress or the Clinton administration.

Given the unprecedented amount that these interests are contributing, Common Cause calls on the host committees which are receiving the money to take the necessary steps to ensure full and prompt disclosure of the money.

As the recently enacted legislation addressing the funding of Section 527 entities makes plain, there is overwhelming bipartisan support for maximum disclosure of money in the political process.

We recognize that convention host committees are required to disclose contributions 60 days after the convention. This is inadequate to serve the public interest. We ask that the host committee respect not just the letter of this regulation, but the spirit of full and immediate disclosure, and publicly commit to disclose the name of any contributor and amount of the contribution within 24 hours of receipt by the committee. Further, this information should be made available to the public by posting on the Internet.

We recognize that many of the donors who have already contributed have allowed their names and contributions to be disclosed. However, we urge you to fully embrace the values of openness and accountability, by publicly committing to full and immediate disclosure of all contributions - both monetary and in-kind - as soon as they are received.

Sincerely,

Scott Harshbarger
