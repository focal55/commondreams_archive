FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
FEBRUARY 29 , 2000
4:39 PM
 CONTACT:  Handgun Control
Statement Of Sarah Brady Regarding Michigan Grade School
 
WASHINGTON - February 29 - The following is a statement by Sarah Brady, of Handgun Control Inc., regarding the Michigan grade school shooting:

"Today a six-year-old girl went to elementary school and didn't come home. She was shot dead by a classmate, age 7, who shouldn't have been able to get a gun but did, largely in part because of our nation's weak gun laws continue to allow children such easy access to firearms. We don't yet know where, why or how the young shooter was able to get a handgun and bring it to school, but we do know he didn't buy it at a mall toy store with his allowance. Quite possibly the shooter found the gun sitting in his own home; which is usually the case when young children shoot others.

"This horrible tragedy should send a clear message to lawmakers in Michigan and around the country: they should quickly pass child access prevention or 'safe storage' laws that make it a crime to leave a loaded firearm where it is accessible by children. Consider this: a 1997 study by the Journal of American Medical Association found that state CAP laws save lives and reduce unintentional deaths of children by firearms by an average of 23 percent. The study also demonstrated that these laws are particularly effective in protecting children younger than ten."

"Today's shooting should also send an important message to parents, especially those who own guns: that keeping a gun in the home can be deadly. Sadly, tragic stories of accidental or unintentional shootings from the careless storage of guns at home are all too common. They happen every day. It happened only last July in nearby Pontiac, Michigan, when a two-year-old boy was killed as he and his three-year-old brother played with a gun in their bedroom with their seven-month-old sister nearby.

"And lastly, the responsibility for shootings like these do not stop at the hands of the gun owner. Why are that gun makers manufacturing weapons that a six-year old child can fire? This makes no rational sense. When will gun makers realize that they bear a responsibility to make sure that their products do not mete out preventable deaths, and that they do not warrant nor deserve special protection from the law to avoid that burden? Instead of safeguarding the gun makers, we should be childproofing the guns.

"This is another senseless tragedy which could have been prevented. With such easy access to guns in our society and our schoolyards turning deadlier, more and more parents are worried for the safety of their children. That's why, on May 14, Mothers Day, I am joining thousands of mothers, grandmothers, dads and concerned citizens who are lending their voices to a growing movement - the Million Mom March Across America - calling on Congress to pass tougher gun control laws to help keep guns out of the hands of children and criminals. We are united in our resolve to make sure that tragedies, like the one today in Mount Morris Township, Michigan, do not happen again. No mother should have to wait for that six-year old at the door, only to be told she will never be home again."

------
Handgun Control, chaired by Sarah Brady, is the nation's largest citizens' gun control lobbying organization. Based in Washington, DC, HCI works to enact stronger federal, state and local gun control laws, but does not seek to ban handguns. Founded in 1974, HCI has more than 400,000 members nationwide and works with local groups around the country to enact and protect reasonable gun control laws. More information about HCI and its affiliated organization, the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence, can be found on our web site at http://www.handguncontrol.org.
