Sunshine's Free, Say Activists, What's Oil Costing Us?
Demonstrators at the World Petroleum Congress Install Solar and Wind Energy System
 
CALGARY, ALBERTA - June 12 - Concerned citizens kicked off this week's much anticipated demonstrations at the World Petroleum Congress (WPC) by installing a state-of-the-art solar and wind energy system in front of the conference center where oil industry heavyweights opened their oil congress. Two 400-watt wind turbines flanked two 350-watt solar panels below a banner reading "Sunshine's Free, What's Oil Costing Us?" The clean energy system powered a PA system for speakers and music as well as videos of petroleum's damaging impacts.

Meanwhile, the festive and peaceful "Rally for Oil Accountability and Responsibility" of some 2500 people traveled through downtown Calgary and arrived at Olympic Park where the renewable energy system was installed. Marchers carried colorful banners depicting the repression, social and environmental injustice suffered in the interest of oil companies by communities around the world, from Ogoni of Nigeria to the Lubicon of Canada.

"Clean energy solutions, such as this solar and wind unit, are ready and available," explained local Calgarian Tak Dirks. "It is time for Big Oil to get out of its business of global warming, human rights violations and environmental degradation and see that better energy options for society exist."

"Oil Corporations continue to destroy the health and well-being of communities around the world," said Leslie Weigl, from Edmonton who helped install the solar power system. "The Calgary demonstrations are part of a global movement to demand that Big Oil stop exploiting human rights and the environment."

The World Petroleum Congress, an organization of more than 50 member nations which account for some 90 percent of global oil production and 85 percent of oil consumption, is meeting in Calgary this week. The WPC serves as a top industry forum for charting the future of petroleum as well as brokering key oil and gas deals. Oil ministers, corporate CEOs and some 3000 other industry leaders are in Calgary for the Congress.

Local, national and international activists have organized a week of non-violent protests, speakers forums and teach-ins to coincide with the WPC in order to draw critical attention to the damaging climate, human rights and environmental impacts of society's unnecessary dependence on fossil fuels. Protestors wish to send a peaceful and clear message to the industry and public: End injustice and climate change caused by oil. The time is now to move beyond petroleum and invest in readily available, renewable energy solutions.
