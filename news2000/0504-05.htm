Japanese Prime Minister Dogged By Debt Campaigners On First Official Tour
 
WASHINGTON - May 4 - Supporters of debt cancellation for the poorest countries will be haunting Prime Minister Mori throughout his whirlwind tour of G8 nations in Europe and North America this week.

Tomorrow (5/5), Jubilee 2000/USA supporters will be peacefully demonstrating outside the White House when Mr. Mori arrives to meet with President Clinton. Demonstrators will be present beginning at 9:20; Mr. Mori's visit is scheduled for 9:40am. Jubilee 2000 Campaigners have become increasingly frustrated by the failure of the G8 world leaders to meet the debt promises made almost a year ago in Cologne, Germany.

Campaigners are not protesting the visit of Prime Minister Mori. Rather, the intent is to respectfully call on the Prime Minister, as Chair of the G8, to make the Okinawa summit truly historic: by canceling the debts of the poorest countries before the end of the year; and by including representatives from the indebted nations in these discussions.

The Organization of African Unity (OAU) and G77 (leaders of the poorest nations) have both said that they should be at the table when decisions are being taken which affect the lives of the people that they represent.

Dan Driscoll-Shaw, director of Jubilee 2000/USA said: "Jubilee 2000 supporters will be present outside the meetings of Prime Minister Mori and all his G8 counterparts to draw attention to the failure of the world's leaders to live up to their promises on debt cancellation. Our campaign is certainly pressuring the US Congress to play its part. Yet, we are also appealing to Prime Minister Mori to set the stage for an historic Okinawa summit which clears the way for a debt-free future for the poorest in the world".

Jubilee 2000 is an international movement active in more than 50 countries and campaigns for the cancellation of the unpayable debts of the world's poorest countries in this year. 17 million people worldwide support Jubilee 2000, including the Dalai Lama, the Pope, Ruyichi Sakamoto, U2's Bono, Radiohead and supermodel Naomi Campbell.
