Sierra Club's Dorsey Responds To Gore Environmental Record Article Mischaracterization
 

WASHINGTON - June 1 -

To: Interested Reporters
From: Allen Mattison, Sierra Club Media Relations Director
Date: June 1, 2000

This week, numerous newspapers published a story reporting on an email sent from a Sierra Club board member to colleauges six months ago discussing the Gore-Bradley presidential primary. That story did not report on the board member's current assessment of the Gore-Bush presidential race. Below is a letter to the editor from that board member outlining his thoughts on the current Presidential race.

May 31, 2000

To the Editor:

I was disappointed to see that you published an article focusing on a memo I wrote six months ago about my concerns regarding Al Gore's environmental record. I think it is unfair and misleading to take an outdated e-mail written in the context of the Gore versus Bill Bradley race, and to use it the context of the Gore versus George Bush race.

My original memo was part of a vigorous and heated debate inside the Sierra Club -- months ago. But there has never been a debate inside the Club on Mr. Bush's status as environmental villain No. 1.

Bush's henchmen at the Republican National Committee, who distributed this dated e-mail, failed to ask me my opinion on the worrisome governor's lackluster environmental leadership.

Under Gov. Bush's sinister tenure, Texas has racked up a lot of firsts:

#1 in the Emission of Ozone-Causing Air Pollution Chemicals;

#1 in Toxic Chemical Releases into the Air;

#1 in the Use of Deep Well Injectors as method of Waste Disposal;

#1 in Counties Listed in the Top 20 of Emitting Cancer Causing Chemicals;

#1 in Total Number of Hazardous Waste Incinerators;

#1 in Environmental Justice Title 6 complaints;

#1 in the Production of Cancer-Causing Benzene and Vinyl Chloride;

and #1 in the Largest Sludge Dump in the Country.

The record speaks for itself. Voting for Bush is the kiss of ecological and planetary death! To do so is to risk the lives of our children and our future.

Yours respectfully,

Michael Dorsey
Director, Sierra Club National Board
Ann Arbor, Michigan
734-945-6424
