US Congressional Commission Calls for Total Debt Cancellation
 

WASHINGTON - March 8 - Jubilee 2000/USA supports the unanimous decision of the Meltzer Commission Report to call for total debt cancellation for the world's most impoverished countries.

In its only unanimous decision, the Commission writes, "the International Monetary Fund, the World Bank and the regional development banks should write-off in their entirety all claims against highly indebted poor countries (HIPCs)." This decision coincides with the Platform of Jubilee 2000/USA, which calls for the definitive cancellation of all crushing foreign debt in the world's most impoverished countries in this Jubilee Year of 2000.

Congress set up the Commission in the fall of 1998, the last time the Congress allocated funds for the International Monetary Fund (IMF). The bipartisan Commission was established to consider the future of the major international financial institutions. While the report was approved 8-3, the call for debt cancellation, in its entirety, was the only unanimous decision of the group.

The report recommends that the IMF, in the future serve only as a quasi lender of last resort. It states that, in this plan "there would be no need for detailed conditionality (often including dozens of conditions) that has burdened IMF programs in recent years and made such programs unwieldy, highly conflictive, time consuming to negotiate, and often ineffectual."

This criticism refers to the Structural Adjustment Programs that Jubilee 2000 campaigners in the Global South have identified as destructive to sustainable development. The Platform of the Jubilee 2000/USA Coalition states that as debts are canceled, there should be no accompanying conditions that exacerbate poverty.

Jubilee 2000/USA is also pleased to see that the Report calls for an increase in "transparency and accountability". We know, from our partners in the Global South, that broad sectors of civil society are qualified and ready to participate in new financial relations in such a context.

The Campaign welcomes the Commission's conclusions. However, Jubilee 2000/USA opposes any interpretation or use of this report that might contribute to a slowing down of the international process, now underway, to cancel foreign debt. The suggestions of this Commission should, instead, help to strengthen and speed up the process already in motion.

"This report will contribute to the momentum, brought about in great part by the Jubilee 2000 movement worldwide, to cancel all the foreign debt of the world's most impoverished countries in Africa, Latin America and Asia. In that way this Year of 2000 will become truly a Jubilee Year," declared Dan Driscoll-Shaw, National Coordinator of Jubilee 2000/USA.

Jubilee 2000/USA is made up of environmental, faith-based and human rights groups that are calling for debt cancellation in this Jubilee Year of 2000. Together with the AFL-CIO, Jubilee 2000/USA is planning a National Mobilization in Washington on April 9th, when they will form a human chain around the Nation's Capitol. In this way they will symbolize the chains of debt that bind so many in our world and the need to break those chains now.
