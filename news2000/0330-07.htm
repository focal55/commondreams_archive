Auto Industry Professes Concern About Fuel Economy While Blocking Increase in Fleet-wide Standards
 

WASHINGTON - March 30 - Statement of Katherine Silverthorne, Staff Attorney:

If the auto industry is really serious about improving fuel economy, they should stop blocking the Department of Transportation from closing the loophole in miles per gallon (CAFE) standards. Cars are currently required to achieve 33% more miles per gallon than Sport Utility Vehicles, minivans and other light trucks. This loophole allows automakers to put inferior fuel-saving technology on light trucks costing the families that own them an estimated $27 billion dollars extra at the gas pump this year.

Chairman Frank Wolf (VA) has included an auto industry-supported rider on the Department of Transportation appropriations bill for the past 5 years effectively freezing miles per gallon standards and prolonging the light truck loophole.

President Clinton and Vice President Gore should speak out against this rider before it is included in this year’s budget package. If the Department of Transportation funding bill contains this rider when it arrives at the White House this year, the President should protect American consumers and veto it.

The Partnership for a New Generation of Vehicles has funneled more than a billion taxpayer dollars to the Big 3 automakers to produce a production prototype "supercar" by 2003. Meanwhile, Honda and Toyota, neither of whom received any of this funding are already selling vehicles that get 70 miles per gallon.

Increasing CAFE standards is the most effective way to reduce our dependence on foreign oil, reduce global warming pollution, and save people money at the gas pump. For the auto industry to claim to be a part of the solution while they block these standards is greenwashing. 30-30-30 U.S. PIRG is the national office for the State Public Interest Research Groups. State PIRGs are non-profit, non-partisan consumer and environmental advocacy groups active across the country.
