Davos: Groups Launch "Citizens Compact" On The UN And Corporations
 

DAVOS, SWITZERLAND - January 28 - Non-governmental groups present in Davos to monitor the World Economic Forum today challenged the United Nations and business leaders to endorse a "Citizens Compact" on the relationship of the private sector to the United Nations.

"The World Economic Forum in Davos represents the same kind of unaccountable corporate power against which citizens protested in Seattle," said Olivier Hoedeman, Director of Corporate Europe Observatory in Amsterdam. "Today as the WEF holds its closed sessions, we are asking Kofi Annan and the UN system to join us in taking the first step toward correcting that imbalance."

The Citizens Compact comes exactly one year after UN General Secretary Kofi Annan challenged business leaders to embrace his "Global Compact" with the UN. This year, the UN is launching a website regarding the new corporate "partnerships" with business. UN High Commissioner Mary Robinson will address the WEF regarding business and human rights and progress in the Global Compact. Since last year, non-governmental groups have protested the UN's implementation of these new partnerships, for example the UN Development Programme's proposed Global Sustainable Development Facility (GSDF). NGO concern has helped stall the GSDF. Various UN agencies also have relationships with McDonald's, Disney, Chevron, BP Amoco, and the International Chamber of Commerce.

"The growing public opposition to the model of globalisation promoted by the WTO and the WEF points to the potential of the UN as a more democratic institution that can defend human and environmental rights" said Kenny Bruno, Research Associate with Transnational Resource & Action Center (TRAC). "The UN is our best hope to monitor and hold accountable the giant companies that control so much of our economies and our lives." The Citizens Compact stresses the need for the UN to develop a legal framework, including monitoring, to govern corporate behavior around the world.

The Citizens Compact is endorsed by more than 70 organizations on six continents.

Olivier Hoedeman will comment on the 'Citizens Compact' at a media briefing organised by the NGO forum "Public Eye on Davos", Saturday 29/1 at 18.00. Venue: Dutch Astma Clinic, Scalettastrasse 19, Davos (next to the Congress Centre).

Background information on the 'Citizens Compact' is available on the websites of Corporate Watch and Corporate Europe Observatory .
