Handgun Control, Center to Prevent Handgun Violence Name Former Congressman Michael Barnes New CEO
 
WASHINGTON - February 3 - Former Maryland Congressman Michael D. Barnes was chosen on Feb. 1 by unanimous vote of the Boards of Directors of Handgun Control and the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence to succeed Robert Walker as Chief Executive Officer of both organizations. Mr. Barnes will formally join the organizations as President on March 1, after resigning from his partnership at the law firm of Hogan & Hartson and his chairmanship of several national and regional policy organizations.

Best known for his advocacy of human rights and democracy as chairman of the House Sub-Committee on Western Hemisphere Affairs during the Central American civil wars of the 1980's, Mr. Barnes also led the national fight against drunk driving and worked closely with MADD to establish more stringent legal standards and penalties for driving under the influence. At that time Mr. Barnes was named "one of the two dozen most influential Members of the House" by the Almanac of American Politics and as the "most influential member of Congress from Maryland or Virginia" by the Washington Post. A graduate of the University of North Carolina, a J.D. from George Washington University Law School and a student of international economics at the Graduate Institute of Higher International Studies in Geneva, Switzerland, Mr. Barnes also served in the U.S. Marine Corps from 1967-69.

After leaving the House in 1986, Mr. Barnes continued to involve himself in foreign policy as a private attorney, serving as the principal international advocate for the successful restoration of democracy in Haiti, and serving as counsel for the governments of Chile, Cyprus, Panama and South Korea. Mr. Barnes also currently serves as Chairman Emeritus of the Center for National Policy; as Co-Chairman of the NAFTA Congressional Caucus and Forum; as Co-Chairman of the U.S. Committee for the United Nations Development Programme; as Chairman of the U.S.-Panama Business Council, and as Chairman of the Maryland Economic Growth, Resource Protection and Planning Commission.

"Like most Americans, I am fed up with the tragedy of gun violence which kills tens of thousands of our citizens every year," said Mr. Barnes. "In my home state of Maryland, more people die from gun violence than from traffic accidents. We clearly need to take common-sense steps to control guns in order to protect all Americans, particularly our children. I am honored and excited to be able to play a role in this crucial effort to save lives."

Mr. Barnes was selected as HC/CPHV president after a national search process initiated when current president Robert Walker decided to leave the organizations last summer. At a time when the gun control issue has regained political and public urgency, the Directors looked for a candidate with significant national political and management experience.

"Michael Barnes' stature and experience with important national and international political issues made him the inevitable choice to lead Handgun Control and the Center at this critical time," said Sarah Brady, who chairs both organizations. "As we work towards the next generation of sensible gun laws, we need a leader with vision, with understanding and with the courage to take on the gun lobby. Michael Barnes is that leader, and I am overjoyed to welcome him to our crusade."

------
Handgun Control, chaired by Sarah Brady, is the nation's largest citizens' gun control lobbying organization. Based in Washington, D.C., HCI works to enact stronger federal, state and local gun control laws, but does not seek to ban handguns. Founded in 1974, HCI has more than 400,000 members nationwide and works with local groups around the country to enact and protect reasonable gun control laws. More information about HCI and its affiliated organization, the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence, can be found on the web site at www.handguncontrol.org.

------
The Center to Prevent Handgun Violence, chaired by Sarah Brady, was founded in 1983 to reduce gun violence through education, legal advocacy, research, and outreach to the entertainment community. Based in Washington, D.C., CPHV's national initiatives include prevention programs for parents and youth on the risks associated with guns, legal representation for gun violence victims, work with the entertainment community to encourage deglamorization of guns in the media, and research of the risks associated with guns and the efficacy of gun control laws. More information about CPHV and its affiliated organization, Handgun Control, can be found on the web site at www.cphv.org.
