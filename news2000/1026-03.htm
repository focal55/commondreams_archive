New NARAL Ad Warns Voters on Nader:
'A Vote for Nader Helps Elect George W. Bush
 
PORTLAND, OR - October 26 - Today in Portland, NARAL President Kate Michelman unveiled NARAL's new ad, which warns that a vote for Ralph Nader could propel George W. Bush into the White House. The ad begins running today in Portland, Oregon and Minneapolis- St. Paul, Minnesota, and tomorrow in Madison, Wisconsin.

"NARAL has made a strategic decision to confront the Nader threat head on. We will reach out to voters to be sure they have all the facts and understand the full impact of their vote," said Michelman. "The threat to a woman's right to choose is too grave to do otherwise."

"George W. Bush is anti-choice and as president could appoint enough Supreme Court justices to overturn Roe vs. Wade. Like it or not, a vote for Ralph Nader will help to elect Bush," said Michelman.

"This is not about the integrity of Ralph Nader and his supporters. It is about reminding voters that the unintended effect of a vote for Nader could be the end of a woman's right to choose," added Michelman.

Ralph Nader remains at 5 percent in the polls nationally, but his popularity in just a few states could tip the Electoral College in George W. Bush's favor.

The NARAL ad is set to air in key electoral states between now and Election Day, including Oregon (7 electoral votes), Minnesota (10), and Wisconsin (11). The ad buy complements the over $5 million NARAL is currently spending on its direct voter contact program in 15 target states reaching 2.1 million pro-choice swing voters.
