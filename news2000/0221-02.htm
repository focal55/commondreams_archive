Nader Announces Bid for Presidency
Iowa Green Party Consider Options
 
IOWA CITY - February 21 - The Iowa Green Party now has four candidates to choose from for the Green Party presidential nomination in the 2000 election. Today, consumer advocate and former Green Party nominee Ralph Nader joined Steven Gaskin and Joel Kovel in the contest for the party's selection. Jello Biafra (http://www.greens.org/jellobiafra/) is also on the ballot in two states. An endorsement from the Iowa Green Party is expected at the middle of March.

Once a nominee is chosen, the Greens will focus on getting qualified ballot status in Iowa, alongside the Democrat and Republican parties. 1500 signatures are needed from ten counties throughout the state by August 17.

"A presidential campaign presents a great opportunity for the Green Party to establish itself as a real alternative to the sound-bite image of the current "front-running" candidates," Holly Hart, secretary of the coordinating committee of the Iowa City Green Party said. "The issues the Green Party stand for needs to be discussed on the national level."

The candidates:

Steven Gaskin: www.stephen2000.org
Gaskin is founder of "The Farm," the countrie's largest successful commune. His planks include universal healthcare, campaign and election reform, choice in education, the decriminalization of marijuana and the amnesty of marijuana prisoners not involved with guns or hard drugs, and the tenet that a corporation is not a person.

Joel Kovel: http://www.greenplace.org/pages/kovel_for_president.htm
Kovel, a psychiatrist by training, is currently a professor at Bard College, the author of numerous books, and a long-time political activist. A member of the New York State Green Party since 1990, Kovel is campaigning for universal healthcare, a major initiative to combat global warming, democratization of the global economy, and far-ranging campaign finance and political reforms.

Ralph Nader: www.votenader.com , www.nader2k.org
Nader's campaign is expected to focus on the need to curb the growing powers of corporations, promote the adoption of a single payer health care system, oppose the multi-billion dollar bailout of the nuclear industry, restore democracy to the political and economic process, and support strong environmental protection. Unlike his campaign four years ago, Nader plans to do extensive fund-raising and campaign appearances.

Iowa Green Party in other news: Russell Lovetinsky, an Iowa Green Party member will be running for state representative in House District 46.
