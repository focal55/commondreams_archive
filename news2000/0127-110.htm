Great Lakes Toxic Buffet Shows Public Health Threats
 

WASHINGTON - January 27 - On January 31, the Sierra Club and Great Lakes United will display a toxic fish buffet to show in vivid detail how Great Lakes pollution still poses dangers to public health. The US Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) is currently negotiating a multi-million dollar clean-up plan, and this toxic buffet is part of a larger effort to urge for stronger plans that will eliminate Great Lakes pollution that has threatened public health for too many years.

WHO:
Dr. Brian McMarry, McMaster University Charlotte Caldwell, Great Lakes Indigenous Environmental Network Margaret Wooster, Great Lakes United Emily Green, Sierra Club Great Lakes Program

WHAT:
Sierra Club and Great Lakes United will display a Great Lakes toxic fish buffet and urge for effective clean-up plans of the Great Lakes -- to protect these natural treasures, for our families, for our future.

WHERE:
The Murrow Room, 13th floor National Press Club 529 14th St. NW Washington, D.C.

WHEN:
January 31, 2000 9:30 a.m.

Despite numerous calls over the past 30 years to clean up toxic hot spots around the Great Lakes, this pollution is still severe enough to render most local fish too dangerous to eat. Dangerous amounts of mercury, PCB's and other toxic contaminants still slip silently into the lakes from nearby industries and abandoned dumping spots. Fish that swim in this toxic soup are now contaminated, and pose a particularly insidious threat to the health of pregnant women, children and subsistence fishermen. With the federal government working out the details of a multi-million dollar clean-up and prevention plan, now is the time to make the Great Lakes' waters clean and safe from toxic pollution, and ensure that these poisons no longer threaten our families.

Excellent visuals will be available.
