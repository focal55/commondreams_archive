Joseph Stiglitz Addresses World Bank Social Security Policy
Former World Bank chief economist, leading experts to address Bank's global promotion of Social Security privatization
 

WASHINGTON - March 30 - As finance ministers and others travel to Washington for the spring meetings of the World Bank and IMF, the Center for Economic and Policy Research, the 2030 Center, and the Institute for America's Future will host a forum to discuss the World Bank's role in promoting Social Security and public pension privatization across the globe. The featured speaker will be Joseph Stiglitz, former chief economist at the World Bank. Dr. Stiglitz, one of America's most prominent economists, has also served as Chair of President Clinton's Council of Economic Advisors.

Also providing commentary and analysis will be Representative Peter DeFazio (D-OR), Richard Trumka, Secretary-Treasurer of the AFL-CIO, and Dean Baker, Co-Director of the Center for Economic and Policy Research. Hans Riemer, Director of the 2030 Center and Roger Hickey, Co-Director of the Institute for America's Future, will provide introductions and overview. Heidi Hartmann, Director of the Institute for Women's Policy Research, will moderate the discussion.

DATE: Wednesday, April 5, 2000

TIME: 10:00 am - 12:00 (noon)

PLACE: U.S. Capitol, Room HC-9

RSVP: Laura Jones, 2030 Center (822-6526 or laura@2030.org)

Over the last decade the World Bank has encouraged dozens of countries to replace, or avoid creating, pension systems that provide guaranteed benefits (such as the U.S. Social Security system). The World Bank has instead promoted systems of privately managed individual accounts. The panel will consider, on the basis of economic evidence, whether the World Bank's focus on privatization is justified, and whether the Bank's aggressive agenda of publications and reports, "how to" seminars, technical assistance, and loans is appropriate.
