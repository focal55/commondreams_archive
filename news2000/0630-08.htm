Sierra Club Urges Clinton To Veto Bill With Anti-Environmental Riders
 

WASHINGTON - June 30 -

Dear Reporter,

The Sierra Club has called upon President Clinton to veto the Military Construction appropriations bill because it contains two anti-environmental riders. One would prevent the EPA from completing new rules to curb polluted runoff, which is the principal reason why 40% of the nation's waters are unfit for swimming, fishing and aquatic habitat. The other is a fiscally irresponsible rider that would exempt the environmentally damaging Wilson Bridge project from requirements that construction begin only after all funding has been appropriated or committed.

The Sierra Club is disturbed that some members of Congress are using must-pass legislation to sneak in irresponsible and anti-environmental riders.

A copy of the letter to President Clinton follows below.

For more information, contact Ed Hopkins at 202-675-7908

June 30, 2000

President William Clinton The White House 1600 Pennsylvania Avenue, NW Washington DC 20500

Dear President Clinton:

On behalf of the Sierra Club's more than 600,000 members, I greatly appreciate the strong position that you have historically taken in insisting that must-pass appropriations bills be free of anti-environmental riders. I urge you to uphold this important principle and veto the Military Construction appropriations bill when it reaches your desk. It contains two outrageous anti-environmental riders that Congress never debated on the merits because they were included only in the final conference report.

One rider, slipped into the conference report late Wednesday night, attacks the Clean Water Act by preventing the EPA from completing and implementing a proposed EPA rule to implement the Act's Total Daily Maximum Load (TMDL) program. This rule is a long-delayed attempt to improve water quality in lakes and rivers not suitable for fishing, swimming, aquatic life or drinking water supply. A strong TMDL rule is critical to cleaning up the 40 percent of waters that do not support fishing and swimming. Without an effective TMDL program, EPA and the states will not have the tools they need to clean up the most significant sources of water pollution, and the nation's progress in cleaning up our waters will come to a halt.

Backroom deals resulted in another rider that would exempt the proposed replacement of the I-495 Wilson drawbridge (just south of Washington, D.C.) from compliance with federal requirements set forth in TEA-21 that such federal capital projects begin construction only after all funding has been appropriated or otherwise committed. An additional $170 million would be appropriated, in addition to the $900 million already appropriated for this project termed by a Washington Post business columnist as a "highway construction lobby boondoggle." For many reasons, not the least of which are its escalating costs, we strongly oppose exempting the Wilson Bridge replacement from complying with TEA-21. We also oppose appropriating any additional funds unless the replacement is re-designed with rail, not HOV lanes and the expensive interchanges HOV lanes would require. Incorporating rail into the design is the only way to fully meet increased transportation demand at this busy crossing.

I recognize that this bill contains funding for many critical programs. Nonetheless, I urge you to veto it and demand that Congress immediately enact a Military Construction appropriations bill free of anti-environmental riders.

Sincerely,

Carl Pope Executive Director
