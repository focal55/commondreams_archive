HRC Urges California Candidates Seeking Federal Office To Publicly Oppose Ill-Conceived Knight Initiative
Candidate's Public Stands Key Factor in Endorsements for the 2000 Elections
 

WASHINGTON - February 2 - In a letter, Elizabeth Birch, executive director for the Human Rights Campaign, urges fair-minded California candidates seeking U.S. House of Rep. And Senate seats to show their leadership by publicly opposing the ill-conceived Knight Initiative/Proposition 22. Already, hundreds of public officials and leaders from many religious and civil rights groups have publicly voiced their opposition. Each California candidate's public stands on the Knight Initiative will be a key factor in HRC's endorsements for the 2000 election, says the HRC.

"Legally, this measure seeks to ban marriage from a group that is already excluded, as lesbians and gays are not allowed to wed in California or any other state," said HRC's Political Director Winnie Stachelberg. "This measure is nothing more than the politics of division and exclusion and we must work with Californians to defeat it."

Already, the HRC has donated $87,000 to the No on Knight/No on Proposition 22 campaign. Funding for the No on Knight campaign's political director as polling, hosting house parties, and participating in GOTV efforts have been made possible in tandem with the HRC's Western Field Organizer and its California State Coordinators.

Public officials who have voiced their opposition include democratic presidential candidates Bill Bradley and Al Gore, Gov. Gray Davis, Sens. Barbara Boxer and Dianne Feinstein, Rep. Tom Campbell, California Mayor Susan Golding and many members of the California legislature and U.S. House delegation.

HRC's political action committee is one of the nation's largest and has donated more than $1 million to fair-minded, pro-gay federal candidates in the 1998 elections, of which $140,000 went to candidates in California. As a member of the National Freedom to Marry Coalition, HRC works with statewide groups to defeat anti-gay and anti-marriage proposals. For more information on the campaign to defeat the Knight Initiative including endorsees visit the No On Knight website at www.NoOnKnight.org.

The Human Rights Campaign is the largest national lesbian and gay political organization with members throughout the country. It effectively lobbies Congress, provides campaign support and educates the public to ensure that lesbian and gay Americans can be open, honest and safe at home, at work and in the community.
