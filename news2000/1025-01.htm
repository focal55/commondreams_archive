Clean Air Trust TV Spot Urges Gov. Bush to Support Clean Air for Our Kids
 
MILWAUKEE, WI - October 25 - The non-profit Clean Air Trust began airing a television spot in Milwaukee today that urges Texas Gov. George W. Bush to "support strong national clean air standards for our kids."

The Trust, a non-partisan clean-air watchdog and grass roots advocacy group, noted that in 1997 Gov. Bush personally tried to weaken the U.S. EPA's attempt to set tougher national health-based clean air standards.

(The EPA standards have been put on hold following lawsuits filed by numerous industry groups. One of the legal briefs in the case was written by ormer White House Counsel C. Boyden Gray, said to be one of Gov. Bush's environmental advisors. The U.S. Supreme Court will hear arguments in the case on Election Day, November 7.)

The commercial, which will run on Milwaukee television stations through November 6, notes that the air in Houston is so polluted that some schools use smog meters to see whether it's safe for kids to play outside.

It also notes that Texas now has twice as many industrial plants violating clean air rules as any other state, and that Houston has overtaken Los Angeles as the nation's smoggiest city.
