Amnesty International USA To Congress: Don't Rush The $1.3 Billion Colombia Aid Package
 
WASHINGTON - March 7 - Amnesty International USA (AIUSA) and other organizations have urged the U.S. House and Senate leadership to slow down consideration of a proposed $1.3 billion, mainly military, aid package for Colombia.

In a letter to the congressional leadership, AIUSA and seven other groups expressed their concern that the accelerated nature with which Congress is considering President Clinton's Colombia aid package can not allow for the package's full repercussions to be addressed.

"The rush to approve President Clinton's package is dangerous given the enormity of the package, the ramifications to U.S. policy of deepening involvement in the war, the potential for a humanitarian catastrophe, and even more human rights violations," AIUSA Executive Director Dr. William F. Schulz, and other groups representatives wrote in the letter.

Some of the issues and unanswered concerns that the organizations' letter raises include:

-- objectives and benchmarks;
-- effectiveness in reducing the flow of drugs;
-- the counterinsurgency dimension of US involvement in the Colombian war;
-- human rights concerns, especially about the military's link to paramilitary groups;
-- effect on the peace process;
-- choice of military vs. economic aid;
-- choice of military vs. police aid;
-- choice of expensive Blackhawk helicopters vs. cheaper alternatives;
-- burden sharing with the international community;
-- projection of the strategy beyond current Pastrana administration.

Meanwhile, the House Committee on Appropriations is scheduled to "mark-up" the emergency supplemental appropriation request on Thursday, March 9.

"All sides of the Colombia debate have raised important concerns that the administration has failed to address," said Carlos Salinas, AIUSA advocacy director for Latin America and the Caribbean. "The administration's lack of clarity reflects the same lack of clarity we observe on the part of the Colombian government. This confusion will only spell disaster if the aid is rushed through Congress. It is time for Congress to exercise its important checks and balances duties on the executive branch."
