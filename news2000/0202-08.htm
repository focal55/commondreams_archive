Greenpeace Welcomes FTC Ruling To Oppose BP-Amoco–ARCO Merger
 
WASHINGTON - February 2 - Greenpeace welcomes the Federal Trade Commission’s decision to oppose BP Amoco’s merger with Atlantic Richfield Corp (ARCO). But the international environmental organization stresses that as the merger now heads to federal court, the FTC must broaden its review to include consideration of environmental impacts the merger may bring, particularly in Alaska.

The BP Amoco–ARCO merger threatens yet greater oil expansion into the Arctic, which will result in more global warming and a greater danger of oil spills. Already Arctic regions are under duress from global warming, as sea ice has thinned by some forty percent over the last forty years, threatening the habitat of Arctic wildlife, including polar bears.

"The FTC has the obligation and the authority to examine environmental threats presented by mergers," said Dr. Iain MacGill, senior analyst for Greenpeace. "As for BP Amoco, we ask why a company whose stated goal is to provide energy without damaging the environment is pursuing more Arctic oil and fueling global warming."

Earlier in the day, ten Greenpeace activists dressed in polar bear costumes demonstrated outside the FTC building in Washington. The "polar bears" carried placards reading "Paws off the Arctic", "Stop the Polar Meltdown" and "Go Solar." Photos have been offered to Reuters, AFP and Associated Press.

Greenpeace also noted that today it received a letter from BP Amoco’s vice-president of corporate governance stating that a shareholder resolution filed by Greenpeace, the US Public Interest Group (U.S. PIRG) and socially responsible investors fulfils the legal requirements to be included with the notice of the company’s AGM in April. The resolution forces BP Amoco to allow its shareholders to choose between more Arctic oil expansion or a revolutionary investment in solar power. This letter is available upon request.
