Drilling in the Arctic Refuge is NOT an Energy Policy
 

WASHINGTON - April 6 - Statement of Athan Manuel, PIRG Arctic Wilderness Campaign, on the Senate Vote on the Roth Arctic Refuge Amendment:

Today's vote against the Roth Arctic amendment is a cynical gesture meant to please BP Amoco and ExxonMobil. Opening the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge for oil drilling will not solve our energy problems or lower the price of gas. The Arctic Refuge, the only part of America's Arctic that is not open for oil and gas drilling, should be preserved, not plundered.

Senators voted 51 to 49 to table an amendment offered by Senator Philip Roth (R-DE) that would have stripped the Senate Budget resolution of language that would have assumed revenue from leasing and drilling activities in the coastal plain of the Arctic Refuge.

We are very disappointed in the votes cast by Senators Gordon Smith of Oregon and Daniel Patrick Moynihan of New York, who sided with Big Oil over America's Arctic. Senator Smith originally voted with Roth, then switched his position ten minutes later and voted against the amendment. Senator Moynihan is a past cosponsor of legislation to preserve the Refuge from drilling.

The U.S. will never drill its way to energy independence. At current rates of consumption there is enough oil in the Arctic Refuge to last the U.S. for about six months. Any oil thought to be in the Refuge would take more than 10 years to reach U.S. consumers.

If the Senate really cared about energy independence they would vote to increase corporate auto fuel economy standards. The U.S. needs an energy policy that focuses more on energy efficiency and renewables than on ruining some of the last unspoiled areas left in the U.S. We have plenty of oil rigs -- and plenty of pollution from the burning of fossil fuels -- but there is only one Arctic National Wildlife Refuge.
