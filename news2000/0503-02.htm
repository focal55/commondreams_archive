AFSC Calls for the U.S. Justice, Department of Defense To Halt Their Imminent Operation To Arrest The People Peacefully Occupying the U.S. Military's Enclave on Vieques
 
PHILADELPHIA - May 3 - The American Friends Service Committee (AFSC) today called for the U.S. Justice Department of Defense to halt their imminent operation to arrest the people peacefully occupying the U.S. military's enclave on Vieques, the Puerto Rican island used for decades for livefire military training.

"The deal brokered by President Clinton to allow resumption of the bombing, gunnery practice and military training exercises flies in the face of common sense and the will of the Puerto Rican people," said Martin Gárate, International Programs director of the Quaker organization.

"The President should instead be negotiating in good faith for the swift return of the military-held land to the people of Puerto Rico, and effective measures for the environmental clean-up of its devastated ecosystems," added Gárate.

The AFSC has supported the efforts by the people of Vieques to recover their island since the 1970s. The current situation remains as critical as described by AFSC spokesperson Betty Nute during a testimony before the UN Committee on De-Colonization on August 6, 1979. Nute noted that when the U.S. Navy expropriated Vieques sixty years ago,

…residents were forced to live in the central part of the island between a bombing range and a large ammunition dump….the Navy's use of the island has destroyed reefs, polluted waters, denied local fisherman access to the best fishing grounds. Noise disrupts schooling and other activities, affecting residents emotionally…We have, and pledge to continue to call on our own government to end the occupation of Vieques.

In the last year a peaceful, non-violent, citizens' movement has arisen in Puerto Rico and on Vieques to press the United States to end its hold on the island. The movement cuts across political, ideological, religious, age and class lines, unifying Puerto Ricans. Serious research and planning efforts are underway by Puerto Rican citizens--scientists, lawyers, religious figures, environmentalists, community leaders and peace activists to assess the environmental damage and cleanup needs. Proposals for a land trust and environmentally sound development planning to benefit Vieques residents are underway as well.

"For the United States to push its way back into training exercises on Vieques is a serious mistake," said Gárate. "Even if Navy and FBI officials do expel the peaceful protesters, it will not halt their struggle to recover the land.

AFSC recently issued a four-part study of US military policy and presence in Latin America and the Caribbean, Still Pulling Strings, which includes, "Colonial Legacy and Military Strategy: The US Military in Puerto Rico." (the studies are available from AFSC)

 

The American Friends Service Committee is a Quaker organization that includes people of various faiths who are committed to social justice, peace, and humanitarian service. Its work is based on the Quaker belief in the worth of every person and faith in the power of love to overcome violence and injustice.
