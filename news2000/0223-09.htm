San Diego City Council Opposes Anti-Gay Marriage Initiative
 

SAN DIEGO - February 23 - The San Diego City Council by a 7-2 vote last night adopted a resolution putting the second largest city in California on record as opposing Proposition 22, the so-called Limits on Marriage initiative on California’s March 7 ballot. The resolution was recommended by the City’s Human Relations Commission and sponsored by Christine Kehoe, San Diego’s first openly lesbian council member.

The resolution states that the Knight Initiative would “cause divisiveness and run contrary to … the right and opportunity of all people to be free from discrimination based on sexual orientation.” Many Council members echoed this sentiment in their statements explaining their opposition to Prop 22 and several referred to a San Diego Union-Tribune editorial which called Prop 22 a solution in search of a problem and encouraged readers to vote “No” in March.

"I know that gay and lesbian San Diegans have suffered a great deal of pain and confusion and feelings of unfairness around Prop 22," said Councilmember Kehoe. "Human dignity does not thrive in an atmosphere of contempt, mockery or stereotyping. Prop 22 does not protect traditional marriage, but it does back all gays and lesbians into a corner of denial and scorn.”

"That's why I asked the City Council to go on record opposing the initiative. The bigotry it enflames is painful and unfair to gay and lesbian San Diegans--our constituents. It promotes prejudice and can lead to hate and even violence," Kehoe stated.

Speaking before a Council chamber nearly filled with supporters sporting “No on Knight” stickers – many of whom had waited more than six hours for the item to come to a vote – proponents of the resolution cited the fact that Prop 22 would not change California law banning same sex marriage. Supporters did note, however, that passage of the Knight Initiative could be used to overturn non-discrimination laws, domestic partnerships, and other rights and benefits currently legally available to gay and lesbian couples.

“You don’t have to support same sex marriage to oppose Prop 22,” says San Diego “No on Knight” campaign spokesperson Rick Foglia. “You just have to reject divisive, mean-spirited politics and believe that government should not intrude into people’s personal lives. That’s why the San Diego City Council, whose members are primarily Republican, have gone on record against the Knight Initiative.”
