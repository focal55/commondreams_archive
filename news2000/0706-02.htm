Friday Night's Missile Test Is Rigged, Experts Say
 
WASHINGTON - July 6 - News Advisory:

WHAT: National Missile Defense (NMD) media briefing
Friday, July 7 11 a.m.

On The Threshold of NMD Decision, Deep Dissent On Technology And "Rogue State" Missile Threat

On Friday night (July 7), the last National Missile Defense (NMD) test before a deployment decision takes place. On Friday morning, July 7, MIT scientist Ted Postol and other analysts critical of National Missile Defense (NMD) will join CEO's representing Business Leaders for Sensible Priorities, for a BLSP-hosted media briefing. Key weapons experts and business leaders alike will speak on the deficiency and duplicity of the tests and global consequences of the White House's rush to deployment. With the intelligence community, military leadership and the Administration internally divided over the seriousness of the missile threat, the deployment decision is mired in political controversy and misinformation on points of scientific fact.

Nevertheless, the White House has said that pending results of Friday's test, it will give NMD deployment "a limited green light" to begin construction of x-band radar in the Aleutian Islands.

WHEN: Friday, July 7, 11 a.m. -- 12:30 p.m.

WHERE: National Press Club, 14th and F St, NW, Washington DC
Lisagor Room

Featured speakers include:

Ted Postol, MIT scientist and ballistics expert who debunked overblown claims for the Patriot missile during the Gulf War, and who more recently brought to national attention charges that NMD tests had been rigged and knowingly falsified to cover up the fact that NMD interceptors cannot distinguish between warheads and decoys. He will release a letter to President Clinton with additional charges at the briefing.
 

Bruce Blair, president, Center for Defense Information, formerly a senior fellow at the Brookings Institution. A MacArthur Fellow, he is a leading expert on U.S. and Russian security policy specializing in nuclear command and control issues.

Alan Kliegerman, CEO of AkPharma Inc., board member Business Leaders for Social Priorities, a group of 600 CEOs that has come out against NMD deployment and is hosting the briefing.

Jack Mendelsohn, executive director, Lawyers Alliance for World Security, former Deputy Director, U.S. Arms Control and Disarmament Agency, and former senior official, U.S. Mission to NATO.

Leon Sigal, director, Northeast Asia Cooperative Security Project, Social Science Research Council. He is a leading authority on the Korean "rogue state threat," a signatory to a recent letter from China experts to the Clinton Administration arguing against NMD deployment, author of several books on U.S.-North Korean relations, and a former editorial writer for The New York Times.

In addition, other noted NMD experts will be in attendance for side interviews and available by phone for comment on the results of the Friday evening tests.
