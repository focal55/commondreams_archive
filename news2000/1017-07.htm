Fact Sheet: Gov. Bush On Oil: Myth Vs. Fact
 
WASHINGTON - October 17 - The following was released today by the Alaska Wilderness League:

GOVERNOR BUSH ON OIL: MYTH VS. FACT

In anticipation of tonight's presidential debate, the Alaska Wilderness League today offered the following corrections to Governor George W. Bush's energy plan and his continued call for the opening of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge for drilling.

---

MYTH: "...Open only 8 percent of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge to environmentally responsible exploration, which could replace the oil that U.S. now imports from Iraq."

FACT: Bush is actually proposing to allow drilling in the entire area that the Regan Administration's own scientists called the "biological heart" of the Arctic Refuge, the 1.5 million-acre coastal plain. He neglects to point out that 95 percent of Alaska's oil rich North Slope is already open to oil exploration or drilling. The refuge coastal plain is the only area along Alaska's Arctic coast protected from oil drilling.

MYTH: "The vice president ... would rather protect this refuge than gain the energy, but this is a false choice...We can do both -- taking out the energy and leaving only footprints."

FACT: The oil industry made the same promise before the Exxon Valdez oil spill. It's simply not believable. The Arctic Refuge's landscape is so fragile that vehicle tracks from nearly two decades ago are still visible from the air. Even with the best technology, drilling the refuge would require hundreds of miles of pipelines, roads, airports, and other facilities. Oil development in Alaska's Arctic already sprawls over 1,000 square miles -- an area bigger than Rhode Island. At Prudhoe Bay, there's an average of more than 400 spills a year and twice as much air pollutants emitted as Washington, D.C.

MYTH: "America, more than ever, is at the mercy of foreign governments and cartels -- at the mercy of big foreign oil."

FACT: Bush neglects to point out that the biggest oil operator on Alaska's North Slope is in fact a foreign, multinational oil company, British Petroleum. The U.S. actually imports much of its oil from Canada, Mexico, and other non-OPEC countries. We also export about 1 million barrels of oil and refined petroleum products every day. And since a ban on Alaska oil exports was lifted in 1995, North Slope oil has been shipped to China and other foreign nations.

MYTH: "On the Clinton-Gore watch, Saddam Hussein's Iraq has become a major supplier of oil to America. This means that one of our worst international enemies is gaining more and more control over America's economic future."

FACT: The Clinton Administration has actually increased oil and gas production from U.S. federal lands. It has even leased 4 million acres on Alaska's North Slope -- an area far larger than the coastal plain of the refuge. New discoveries have extended the life of Alaska's oil fields until 2040 and more than 30 trillion cubic feet of natural gas (the energy equivalent of 6 billion barrels of oil) is available from already developed Alaska fields. Still, the U.S. simply can't drill its way to energy independence or lower prices. We have less than 3 percent of the world's known oil reserves. Even if we plundered every national park, wildlife refuge, and coastline, we would still be importing more than half our oil. Only by decreasing our dependence on oil can we become less dependence on imported oil. A modest increase in the fuel economy of our nation's cars and light trucks of just 3 miles per gallon would save more than a million barrels of oil per day -- far more than might be in the Arctic Refuge.
