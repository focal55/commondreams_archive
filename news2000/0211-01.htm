The Human Rights Campaign Endorses Al Gore For President
Endorsement Earned through Long History of Actions Supporting Equality
 

WEST HOLLYWOOD, CA - February 11 - The Human Rights Campaign, the nation's largest gay and lesbian political organization, today endorsed Al Gore for president of the United States at a West Hollywood, Calif. press conference attended by the vice president. While his Democratic primary opponent Bill Bradley has taken honorable stands on gay issues, Gore earned the endorsement through his long, well-documented history of tangible actions in support of equality, says HRC.

"Al Gore understands that words create dreams, but actions create results," said HRC Executive Director Elizabeth Birch at the press conference. "He has demonstrated a thorough understanding of our issues, not just rhetorically but historically. He has a long, tangible record of support and has championed our quest for freedom both in word and deed."

Gore best demonstrated his willingness to stand by his convictions in 1996 when the Employment Non Discrimination Act (ENDA) -- which would prohibit job discrimination based on sexual orientation -- was up for a Senate vote. Gore actively lobbied his former Senate colleagues to pass this legislation and vowed, in the event of a tie, to return to Washington by helicopter to cast the decisive vote.

"It is still legal to fire people in 39 states just because they are gay," said Birch. "Al Gore has proven that he will actively use the power of his office to help end this injustice. If Congress passes ENDA, Gore has pledged to sign it into law when he is elected President."

Gore and his wife Tipper have been in the forefront in the fight against AIDS and the spread of HIV at home and abroad. Both he and Tipper visited and read names at the AIDS quilt. Tipper has served as the chair of the Washington, DC AIDS walk. Gore has fought within the Clinton administration to expand Medicaid coverage for people with AIDS. Gore has committed to using our nation's resources to develop an AIDS vaccine and provide money for better, life saving drugs.

Gore also supports the Hate Crimes Prevention Act, which would help combat the violence that killed James Byrd Jr. and Matthew Shepard. Gore has appointed openly gay men and lesbians to high level positions in both his vice presidential and campaign staffs. Additionally, he supports gays serving openly in the military.

The next president will pick nominees for the Supreme Court who will make crucial decisions that will profoundly affect our lives. During the Clinton/Gore administration the president signed a historic executive order banning federal government workplace discrimination based on sexual orientation. Comments made by both John McCain and George W. Bush suggest that policies such as this might be in jeopardy if either one wins the oval office.

"There is a lot at stake in the next presidential election," said Birch. "The outcome will determine whether we take monumental strides forward or significantly prolong our fight for fairness. If Gore is elected president, we will have someone in the oval office who is committed to uniting our country and ensuring equality for each and every person."

The Human Rights Campaign is the largest national lesbian and gay political organization with members throughout the country. It effectively lobbies Congress, provides campaign support and educates the public to ensure that lesbian and gay Americans can be open, honest and safe at home, at work and in the community.
