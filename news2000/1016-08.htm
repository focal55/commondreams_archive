Nader Campaign To Launch Clean Up Crew To Scour Corporate Influence From Politics
Cross-Country Van Tour Kicks Off Today
 
WASHINGTON - October 16 - Big money in politics has dirtied up the American democratic process, and Green Party presidential candidate Ralph Nader is today launching a fleet of vans to help clean up the problem.

Dubbed the “Corporate Influence Clean-Up Crew,” the cleaners will cross the country in a fleet of vans to draw attention to the problem of dirty money in politics and offer solutions. The vans were launched from Washington, D.C. today.

“As part of our pledge to bring the campaign to every area of the country, the vans will travel throughout the south spreading the word about how corporate influence is undermining our democracy,” said Nader 2000 campaign manager Theresa Amato.

Campaign finance reform is a major theme of Nader’s candidacy. Nader – who this week announced that his campaign has already reached its $5 million fundraising goal -- accepts no corporate soft money contributions and no political action committee contributions. The $5 million and counting has been raised entirely through individual contributions averaging less than $100 each.

Nader is the only major presidential candidate who supports full public financing of public elections. “Year after year, big business invests in politicians and political parties by giving them millions of dollars, and in return, those businesses get corporate welfare, weak law enforcement and tax breaks worth billions of dollars. The biggest single obstacle to honest, just government action – government of, by and for the people – is the corruption of special-interest money in our election campaigns,” Nader said.

Al Gore and George W. Bush Al Gore and George W. Bush have both benefited from millions of dollars in corporate soft money and PAC money contributions. Neither candidate supports full public funding of elections.

Nader’s Corporate Influence Clean-Up Crew will travel through 12 southern states throughout the remainder of the campaign to call attention to the perils of cash-register politics. Armed with janitorial props and thousands of bars of soap, the three vans will travel to Kentucky, Missouri, Iowa, Nebraska, Arkansas, Tennessee, Louisiana, Missouri, Alabama, Georgia, South Carolina and Florida. The vans will make two to three stops per day, conducting events including street theatre, rallies, meetings with local supporting groups and the occasional political prank.
