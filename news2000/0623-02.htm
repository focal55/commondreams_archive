The Othmer Institute at Planned Parenthood of New York City Releases Abortion Polling Data
 
NEW YORK - June 23 - The Othmer Institute at Planned Parenthood of New York City today released the first in a series of results of its nationwide poll of American attitudes on abortion and the soon to be decided Supreme Court case, Stenberg v. Carhart.

The survey affirms that abortion is a very important issue in this country. So important that 7 out of 10 Americans are concerned about safeguarding a woman's right to choose abortion.

Further, Americans show little ambivalence when asked who should decide whether or not an individual woman should have an abortion: 75 percent of Americans believe that the woman should make the decision herself or in consultation with her doctor. And a strong majority of Americans support the 1973 decision in Roe v. Wade.

On the eve of the Supreme Court's decision in the most important abortion case in the last eight years, Nebraska's Stenberg v. Carhart, 81 percent of Americans report that they are unfamiliar with this case.

When asked if they would support or oppose a law such as the one in Nebraska that prohibits certain kinds of pre-viability abortion procedures, even though the doctor believes that the procedure is the safest for the woman, a plurality of Americans said they would oppose such a law.

Further, when informed that if the Supreme Court upholds the Nebraska law, doctors would be prevented from using the abortion procedure they consider the safest to protect the life and health of the woman, opposition to the law dramatically increased to a majority of Americans -- with a 20 point increase in respondents expressing strong opposition to the law.

The results of this survey should serve as a clarion call to politicians -- that the vast majority of Americans believe in and support the right of an individual woman to decide whether or not to terminate an unwanted pregnancy. Anti-choice politicians waste time and money dividing the country over abortion. They fail to accurately represent the views of their constituents and undermine the health and wellbeing of women and their families.

Global Strategy Group conducted the survey during the period of June 9-18. 807 respondents were polled nationwide. The poll has a margin of error of +/- 3.4 percent.

1. How concerned are you about safeguarding a woman's right to choose an abortion -- would you say very concerned, somewhat, not very or not at all concerned.

29 percent Somewhat concerned
10 percent Not very concerned         27 percent Net unconcerned
18 percent Not at all concerned
4 percent Don't know/Refused

2. As you may know, Roe v. Wade is the 1973 U.S. Supreme Court law which made abortion legal in the United States. This court decision made it legal to end a pregnancy before fetal viability -- that is, before the fetus can live outside the woman's body. The decision also said that abortions could not be performed after viability except to save the life or health of the woman. Would you say you support or oppose this decision? (IF SUPPORT or OPPOSE ASK: Do you strongly support/oppose or only somewhat support/oppose?)

41 percent Strongly support         61 percent Net support
20 percent Somewhat support
8 percent Somewhat oppose         29 percent Net oppose
22 percent Strongly oppose
10 percent Don't know/Refused

3. Which of the following statements comes closest to how you feel about who should decide whether or not an individual woman should have an abortion?

45 percent The woman in consultation with her doctor should decide.
29 percent The woman should decide.
22 percent No one should be able to decide because abortion should be outlawed
2 percent The woman and man should decide
1 percent Depends on what month / how far along
* Other (specify)
2 percent Don't Know/Refused

As you may know, within the next couple of months, the United States Supreme Court is expected to rule on a case involving the issue of abortion. The Court will rule on a Nebraska law that criminalizes certain kinds of abortion procedures that are commonly
performed during the first and second trimesters of a pregnancy-that is before a fetus is viable, or able to live outside of the woman's body. These procedures are used when the doctor believes that such a procedure is the safest one for the woman. The Court will decide whether these restrictions are legal or not.

4 percent Very familiar 16 percent Net familiar
12 percent Somewhat familiar
10 percent Not very familiar 81 percent Net unfamiliar
71 percent Not at all familiar
4 percent Don't know/Refused

5. Would you support or oppose a law such as the one in Nebraska that would prohibit certain kinds of pre-viable abortion procedures, even though the doctor believes that the procedure is the safest for the woman?

21 percent Strongly support prohibiting certain pre-viability abortion procedures
15 percent Somewhat support prohibiting certain pre-viability abortion procedures
27 percent Somewhat oppose prohibiting certain pre-viability abortion procedures
19 percent Strongly oppose prohibiting certain pre-viability abortion procedures
18 percent Don't know/Refused

37 percent Net support prohibiting

45 percent Net oppose prohibiting
