 CONTACT:  Mobilization for Global Justice

Protesters Get Smart
 Friday Devoted to Teach-ins and Training as Thousands Rev Up for April 16
 
WASHINGTON - April 13 - Protesters are following up a week of mass rallies and victories with a day of intensive education for Sunday's demonstrations. With teach-ins, press conferences, and civil disobedience training taking place throughout the city, protesters plan to be better-prepared than ever before to make their message heard. Here's where you can get the story:

Making Our Voices Heard:
INTERNATIONAL ACTIVISTS AND SCHOLARS join forces to explain how the I.M.F. and the World Bank create poverty, not prosperity. Press conference with speakers from Zimbabwe, the Philippines, Uganda, Haiti, the U.S. and more beginning at 8:30 a.m., National Press Club, First Amendment Rm; Soren Ambrose, (202) 210-0792.
DIVERSE YOUNG LEADERS explain why the movement for global justice is spreading like wildfire with America's youth. Hear the voices of the next generation at Noon, St. Stephen's Church, 16th St. & Newton, N.W. Loren Finkelstein, (202) 285-6760 for more info.

Teach-ins – What's Wrong with Globalization and How We're Going to Fix It:

WORLD'S LEADING CRITICS of globalization from every continent come together to explain the sins of the IMF and World Bank. This event is already sold out, but there will be room for press – get there early! All day: 9:00 a.m-10:30 p.m. at Foundry Methodist Church, 1500 16th St. N.W. International Forum on Globalization at (415) 229-9269 for more info.

SWEATSHOPS are the order of the day in countries where the World Bank and the I.M.F. set agendas. Find out why and what to do about it with students, activists, and workers from around the world. All day at St. Stephen's Church, 16th St. &Newton, N.W. Melinda St. Louis, (202) 544-9355 for more info.

MORE TRENCHES, LESS FAIR TRADE – that's the result of the World Bank's "dry canal" network stretching from Mexico, through Nicaragua to Colombia and beyond, wiping out rainforest and local economies all along the way. World Bank says "Trust us"; Nicaraguan activist Suyen Barahona and others say "No thanks." Get the facts behind the fury: Noon, Protest Headquarters, on Florida Avenue between 13th & 14th N.W.

Specialized Training
Activists are preparing for Sunday's mass action by getting special training fit for the occasion:

JUST-ACT youth organization for global justice will teach kids and older activists the basics of civil disobedience and non-violence from 9:00 a.m.-2:00 p.m. at the Community for Creative Non-Violence, 425 Second St. N.W. Colin Rajah, (510) 334-4750 for more info.

LEGAL OBSERVERS will make sure direct action protesters' rights are respected. Training at 8:00 p.m. GWU Law School Rm. L-302. Zachary Wolfe, (202) 318-0855

Tune in tomorrow for another press release announcing SATURDAY'S action, or check www.a16.org.
