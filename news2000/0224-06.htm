Gore's 1992 Promise to Make 'No Net Loss' of Wetlands Broken, Campaign Contributors Could Benefit
 
WASHINGTON - February 24 - The following was released today by Friends of the Earth:

Gore's Environmentalism For Sale?

A 1992 promise by Vice-President Gore to ensure "no net loss" of U.S. wetlands was broken last spring when the White House endorsed a draft state-federal plan that would permit the destruction of 500 acres of New Jersey wetlands and allow the construction of a shopping mall by the Mills Corporation.

According to the Feb. 28 issue of The American Prospect, the proposed shopping mall project "would not only decimate a recovering wildlife refuge, but also mar the region's human landscape by creating traffic jams and urban sprawl." Plans to develop the Meadowlands have been denounced for over 10 years by environmentalists and more than 1000 New Jersey residents. According to the Friends of the Earth Political Action Committee, this revelation is especially disturbing in the wake of a $43,000 contribution to the Gore 2000 campaign by executives, contractors and family members of the Mills Corporation.

"Real convictions should not be set aside for campaign contributors," said Friends of the Earth PAC president, Brent Blackwelder. "Al Gore is allowing the public to conclude that his environmentalism has a price tag on it."

Blackwelder also expressed concern that today's endorsement of Gore by the New York League of Conservation Voters and an earlier endorsement by Robert F. Kennedy, Jr. -- who is an outspoken critic of the proposed Meadowlands project is turning a blind eye to the Mills Corporation deal. The American Prospect quoted Kennedy as saying, "It is disappointing to see Al Gore succumb to this kind of mercenary approach."

"Despite promises to the contrary, the Clinton-Gore administration has allowed nearly every harmful wetland decision remaining from the Reagan/Bush era to remain in place," said Blackwelder, citing more than a dozen examples of unsound wetland decisions. "Is this what we can expect from Al Gore in the future? The answer, unfortunately, appears to be 'yes.'"
