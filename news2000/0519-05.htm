Congressional Leaders Send Letter Decrying PNTR for China as Trading Away Environment
 
WASHINGTON - May 19 - Congressional environmental champions today joined leading environmental groups in denouncing Permanent Normal Trade Relations (PNTR) with China. The coalition, led by members of the House Natural Resources Committee, Minority Whip David Bonior, Friends of the Earth, Sierra Club and The Humane Society pledged to vigorously oppose a trade agreement which turns a blind eye toward environmental protection.

"We should neither put our seal of approval on this flawed agreement, nor give up our annual opportunity to scrutinize the environmental implications of U.S.-China trade," says the letter sent today by Reps. DeFazio (D-OR), Miller (D-CA), Andrews (D-NJ), Bonior (D-MI), Pelosi (D-CA), Stark (D-CA), Kaptur (D-OH) and Woolsey (D-CA).

The letter cites a litany of missed opportunities to address vital environmental problems like trade in endangered species, global warming and potential destruction caused by invasive species. It stresses that the U.S. should have sought assurances that China would not seek to weaken endangered species regulations and invasive species protections once it enters the World Trade Organization (WTO). The letter also notes that the trade deal failed to promote the export of clean, renewable energy technology to China, where two million people die annually due to air pollution.

Environmentalists have accused the U.S. of negotiating a trade deal that prioritizes benefits to big business at the expense of the planet.

"The trade deal is a 250 page smoking gun that proves how U.S. trade policy has been captured by corporate polluters and fat cat campaign contributors," said Friends of the Earth president Brent Blackwelder. "The Administration found time to negotiate tariffs on golf carts and weasel tail hair, but didn't even put anything on the table that remotely addressed the safety of the air and water we depend upon for survival."

Friends of the Earth joined the Sierra Club, the Humane Society, Earthjustice Legal Defense Fund, Defenders of Wildlife, Rainforest Action Network, American Lands Alliance, and the Center for International Environmental Law last week in urging Congress to vote against PNTR. Many congressional opponents of PNTR, most recently Rep. Andrews (D-NJ), have cited environmental concerns as a key deciding factor.

On Tuesday May 23rd, environmental groups will join with the labor and human rights organizations for a protest vigil at the Capitol urging Congress to vote against against PNTR for China.
