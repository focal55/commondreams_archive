Hall Questions Clinton Administration's Criticism of U.N.'s Iraq-Based Humanitarians
 
WASHINGTON - February 17 - U.S. Rep. Tony P. Hall, D-Ohio, today questioned the comments of the U.S. Department of State's spokesman on the resignation of top U.N. officials in Iraq over the humanitarian situation there. Text of his letter to Secretary of State Madeleine Albright follows:

Dear Madame Secretary:

I read with concern the comments of your spokesman in response to the resignations of two leaders of humanitarian work in Iraq.

I have visited scores of nations where people suffer from hunger, malnutrition, disease, and other problems that often are rooted in poor decisions by their political leaders. I have met thousands of courageous people, like Hans von Sponeck and Jutta Purghart, working against almost impossible odds to help those in need overcome the challenges they face. And during my time in Congress I have worked extensively with United Nations organizations and generally found them to be conscientious and careful in their evaluation of situations such as those the people of Iraq now endure.

It appears to me that nothing Mr. Von Sponeck or Ms. Purghart has noted differs from the reports of UNICEF, the International Red Cross, and other respected organizations that have examined what is happening in Iraq today. Your spokesman's vilification of these individuals is unfair and appears to be an effort to stifle the expression of legitimate concerns about our country's policies toward Iraq. If they accurately reflect your position, Madame Secretary, I hope you will reconsider it.

The United States generally manages to work its will through the United Nations without resorting to the sort of vitriol against U.N. employees that Mr. Rubin directed against these respected humanitarians. Even in Congress, reasonable men and women are able to disagree; I urge you to protect the United Nations' reputation as a forum for discussion and consensus-building that is at least as free of personal attacks.

Thank you for your attention to my concerns, Madame Secretary, and for your continued leadership.

Sincerely,

Tony P. Hall
Member of Congress
