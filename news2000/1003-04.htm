The Hallmark to Governor Bush's Gun Safety Policy: Licensing Felons to Carry Concealed Handguns
 
WASHINGTON - October 3 - The following was released today by Handgun Control and the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence:

One of the first pieces of legislation signed by George W. Bush as Governor, which allows the carrying of concealed handguns in public, came under fire today from the Los Angeles Times. Today's Times story, "Felons Get Concealed Gun Licenses under Bush's 'Tough' Law," again demonstrates the serious problems created by the carrying concealed handgun legislation in Texas. According to the Times' in-depth research, Texas has licensed more than 400 criminals with prior convictions since the inception of the concealed-handgun program, and more than 3,000 other licensees have since been arrested. Governor Bush signed the law permitting concealed-carry in 1995, and in 1997 signed another law removing churches, hospitals, nursing homes and amusement parks from the list of places where carrying concealed handguns was prohibited.

In a letter to Jim Brady in 1995, the Governor wrote that "(he) does not believe allowing responsible, law abiding citizens who are trained and licensed to carry weapons for their personal protection will lead to an increase in crime." Despite the Governor's hopes, a recently released report from the Violence Policy Center demonstrated that Texans with concealed-carry licenses were 66 percent more likely to be arrested for firearms violations than Texans who did not have licenses.

"The LA Times has now demonstrated conclusively what other research has suggested," said Sarah Brady, Chair of Handgun Control. "Lax laws that do not permit the police to exercise discretion about who can carry hidden handguns let criminals through the cracks. And even citizens without prior convictions carrying handguns in their waistbands and purses are tempted to misuse them in the heat of the moment."

"Governor Bush has allowed dangerous criminals to carry concealed handguns, and put his own state's citizens at risk," Mrs. Brady said.

In a 1999 form letter sent out by the Bush campaign in response to inquiries on the gun issue, the Governor echoes the gun lobby's mantra of 'tough laws and long sentences' for those who use guns to commit crimes. In the letter from the campaign, Bush also wrote about the carrying concealed handgun law, "I believe this legislation is helping make Texas a safer place to live."

Nevertheless, as pointed out by both the Houston Chronicle (12/8/99) and The Wall Street Journal (3/29/00), more than 700 criminals who were known to possess guns -- because they used them to pass the training standards for concealed handgun licenses -- were not prosecuted by state authorities, despite the applicants' failure of the background check process and prohibition from gun ownership.

"Concealed Truth," a report released by the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence this year, demonstrated that since peaking in 1992, the violent crime rate dropped 30 percent (through 1998) in states that restrict or prohibit carrying concealed handguns compared to a decline of less than 20 percent in states that eased restrictions on carrying hidden guns in public as a way to attack crime in their communities. The disparity in crime reduction is even greater for robbery -- the crime theoretically most affected by changes in carry laws similar to those implemented in Texas. In states that only issue concealed carry permits to individuals who can demonstrate the need to carry a concealed handgun or prohibit the practice altogether, the robbery rate declined 44 percent -- nearly twice as much as the decline in states with Texas-style laws.

"It's no wonder that the NRA's chief lobbyist has said publicly that Governor Bush has never denied them any part of their legislative agenda," Mrs. Brady said. "Concealed-carry of hidden handguns was at the top of the NRA's wish list throughout the '90s. The danger it presented was always clear. Now that the danger is objectively verified, we urge Governor Bush to reconsider his support for the dangerously permissive carrying of concealed handguns."

For more information about the issue of carrying of concealed handguns or to read more about the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence's "Concealed Truth" report, please visit Handgun Control's Web site at http://www.handguncontrol.org. You can also find more information on Texas Governor George W. Bush's record on guns at http://www.bushandguns.com.

Handgun Control, chaired by Sarah Brady, was founded in 1974 by Dr. Mark Borinsky and N.T. "Pete" Shields, two victims of gun violence. Based in Washington, D.C., HCI works with law enforcement, public health, religious, and community groups across the country to strengthen and protect federal, state and local gun control laws, but does not seek to ban all guns. HCI has more than 500,000 members and supporters nationwide, making it the nation's largest citizens' gun control lobbying organization.
