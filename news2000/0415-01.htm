FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
APRIL 15, 2000
12:20 AM
 CONTACT:  Moblilization for Global Justice
MGJ Media Center (202) 789-5419
Citizen-Activists Debate IMF/World Bank
Street Action Builds as Protesters Meet IMF/World Bank Reps in Public Debate
 
WASHINGTON - April 15 - IMPORTANT NOTE: Mobilization Convergence Space on Florida St. closed to press after 1pm Saturday.

High-ranking officials from the World Bank and the IMF will square off in a public debate with representatives of the thousands of protestors pouring into the city here for Sunday's massive demonstration against corporate globalization. Elsewhere, activists will take the protest into the streets – with civil disobedience, live music, parties and street festivals.

Global Citizens vs. IMF/World Bank – The Debate:
Njoki Njoroge Njehu, director of Mobilization for Global Justice co-sponsor 50 Years is Enough, and renowned Filipino scholar Walden Bello are ready to take on the World Bank and IMF in a challenge of the minds. High-ranking officials from the Bank and IMF have promised to attend. Get down to the nitty-gritty and watch the words fly at what promises to be the key debate of the biggest anti-IMF/World Bank protests in US history.
When: 3-5:00 p.m., Saturday, April 15
Where: D.C. Jewish Community Center, 1529 16th St. N.W.
More info: Soren Ambrose, (202) 210-0792

Doctors Say Corporate Globalization is Bad For Your Health – M.D.s Speak Out:
IMF/World Bank policies aren't just bad for your pocketbook – they're bad for your health, say physicians world-wide. IMF/World Bank austerity measures gut health programs around the globe and undermine international efforts to alleviate the spread of mass epidemics. Dr. Quentin Young of Physicians for a National Health Program will lead a march to the World Bank to demand health justice.
When: 7:15 p.m., Saturday, April 15
Where: Meet at Georgetown U., McNeir Auditorium, 37th & Q St. N.W.
More info: Darius Silvin (202) 319-7753

World Bank, IMF Fan Flames of AIDS Epidemic – ACT UP! Fights Back:
Bank and IMF Structural Adjustment Programs gut health care and other key social services in 'developing' countries. That means shutting down AIDS response programs. Speakers from ACT UP! and the HIV Community Coalition will provide the facts on how corporate globalization exacerbates the global AIDS epidemic and impedes treatment and education programs.
When: 1-3:30 p.m., Saturday, April 15
Where: All Soul's Church, 16th St. @ Columbia, N.W.
More info: Rob Weissman, (202) 387-8030

Also tomorrow: Anarchist Guerilla Gardening with Kids! Steelworkers, Students Unite (and build a stage for Sunday's rally)! Mass Parties to Prepare for Mass Demonstrations!
