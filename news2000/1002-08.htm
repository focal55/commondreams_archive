Pizza Economics Launches Web Site, Delivers Pedagogical Pizzas at Presidential Debates
 
WASHINGTON - October 2 - Privatizers are feeding the public a lot of misinformation about Social Security. That's why Pizza Economics, a new grassroots educational initiative, is launching a web site http://www.pizzaeconomics.org, and delivering pizzas state-to-state and at the Presidential debates.

Pizza Economics is getting out one message: "No matter how you slice it, privatization of Social Security means cuts, cuts and MORE cuts."

The Pizza Economics Team, made up of union activists, youth and retiree groups, is serving pedagogical pizzas to participants at the Presidential debate in Boston on Oct. 3 to educate the public about the importance of preserving Social Security and the dangers of privatization. In Boston, Pizza Economics is teaming up with the American Federation of Teachers Retirees, the Gray Panthers and 2030 Action to warn people that privatization means sliced benefits.

According to a recent Century Foundation study, authored by some of the nation's foremost economists, diverting 2 percent of payroll taxes into individual accounts will slice 40 percent of people's Social Security benefits -- for young and old alike. Pizza Economics cuts through the complex economics of Social Security by delivering "privatized pizzas" that are missing four pieces from every pie demonstrating visually and unmistakably how the benefits of widows, children and retirees would be slashed under privatization schemes.

"We're launching a pizza education movement, " says Karen Friedman, the founder of Pizza Economics. "While the whole Social Security pie is filled with guaranteed, inflation-adjusted lifetime benefits, privatized pizzas are expensive, half-eaten, thin-crusted substitutes."

Pizza Economic partners include 2030 Action, the National Committee to Preserve Social Security and Medicare (NCPSSM), AFSCME, the American Federation of Teachers Retirees, the Gray Panthers, and the Campaign for America's Future. In coming weeks, 2030 Action will be holding pizza parties in New Mexico, California and Pennsylvania to help publicize their new report about the impact of privatization on young people. NCPSSM is holding pizza parties for their members in Michigan, Arkansas and across the country as part of their Legacy Campaign.
