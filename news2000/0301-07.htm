Institute for America's Future, Century Foundation to Host Discussion on New Book on Transforming American Politics
 

WASHINGTON - March 2 -

News Advisory:

WHAT:
The Institute for America's Future and The Century Foundation will host a discussion to discuss an important new book about transforming American politics, "The Missing Middle: Working Families and the Future of American Social Policy."

WHO:
-- Author Theda Skocpol
-- Comments by Rep. Rosa DeLauro (D-CT)
-- Introductions by Roger Hickey, Institute for America's Future, and Ruy Teixeira, The Century Foundation.

DATE:
Thursday, March 2

TIME:
2-4 p.m.

WHERE:
National Press Club, Lisagor Room
Washington, D.C.

DETAILS:
Join the Institute for America's Future and The Century Foundation for a discussion with Theda Skocpol, author of a new book, "The Missing Middle," as she explores the potential of a new politics focused on the "Missing Middle" of American society.

In this important new book, Skocpol points out that conservatives have forced the political debate to the right. American politics revolves around whether or not to cut taxes for the very rich, whether to cut programs for the very poor, whether we should dismantle the social insurance programs for the very old.

She makes a strong case that, despite millions of dollars spent on right wing propaganda, the American people are rejecting false trade-offs that would allow the dismantling of universal social programs. For example, she explains why Americans aren't buying the idea that Social Security and Medicare should be dismantled. They have rejected a well-funded campaign designed to promote a war between generations because Americans know that all generations benefit from these universalistic programs.

Skocpol argues that, even while progressives defend programs for the oldest, the youngest, and the poorest, now is the time to advance new policies to enhance security and opportunity for the "missing middle" -- the average working parents of modest means. Skocpol concludes that the time is ripe to end the polarized and fragmented debates of today. Taking lessons from the most successful past social efforts, she identifies ways the missing middle can reclaim American politics and shape a new social policy in the coming century.

Theda Skocpol is Victor S. Thomas Professor of Government and Sociology at Harvard University and author of Boomerang: Health Care Reform and the Turn Against Government (Norton), among other books, and co-editor (with Stan Greenberg) of The New Majority: Toward a Popular Progressive Politics. The Missing Middle is a Century Foundation Book. (W.W. Norton, Publisher. January/cloth/ ISBN 0-393-04822-5/6 1/8" X 9 1/4" /256 pages/CURRENT AFFAIRS)

Copies of the book will be available to members of the media in attendance. RSVP to Kim Ostrowski at 202-955-5665.
