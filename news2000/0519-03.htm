Sierra Club Outraged at GOP and Dem. Leadership Over Rider
Congress Once Again Blocks Even Study of Cutting Auto Pollution
 
WASHINGTON - May 19 - Statement of Daniel Becker, Director, Sierra Club Global Warming and Energy Program

"The Sierra Club is outraged that the Republican leadership has yet again prevented action to cut auto pollution, we are profoundly disappointed that the Democratic leadership didn't fight back; we urge the President to veto the DOT appropriations bill to remove the rider. Once again, Congress has used a rider to bar the Department of Transportation from even studying raising Corporate Average Fuel Economy (CAFE) standards. With fuel economy at its lowest level since 1980, action is clearly needed.

"A bipartisan coalition of more than 100 Representatives signed onto a letter urging increased fuel economy standards. This is a strong showing of support for tackling this urgent problem. These Representatives heard the demand from Americans who want to save money at the gas pump, cut our addiction to foreign oil and reduce global warming pollution. Polls show that an overwhelming majority of Americans want to improve miles per gallon standards for SUVs -- including SUV owners themselves.

"This is the first major anti-environmental rider of the 21st Century. It is essential that the Republican leadership stop polluting our laws with riders. It is equally essential that when they do, the Democratic leadership stand and fight. And when Congress doesn't do its job, the President must use his veto power.

"In 1975, Congress passed the CAFE law to conserve oil and improve the nation's gas mileage. From 1975 to 1989, CAFE doubled the nation's fuel economy. Since then, fuel economy has remained stagnant. Every year since 1995, a rider has been attached to the Transportation Appropriations bill to prevent the Department of Transportation from examining the need to improve CAFE standards."
