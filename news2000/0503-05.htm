Enviros Challenge Clinton/Gore Falsehoods Over Dolphin-Safe Ruling
 

SAN FRANCISCO - May 3 -

Prepared by:
David Brower and David Phillips, Earth Island
Joshua Floum, Partner, Legal Strategies Group
William Snape, Senior Counsel, Defenders of Wildlife

On April 11, 2000, a coalition of environmental groups, including Earth Island Institute, Defenders of Wildlife, Humane Society of the U.S., and ASPCA, won a major victory for dolphins in the San Francisco federal court of Judge Thelton Henderson.

Judge Henderson ruled in "Brower v. Daley" that the Clinton/Gore Administration violated the law when they weakened the standards for allowing tuna to be labeled "dolphin safe."

"(The Court) concludes" Judge Henderson wrote, "...the (Commerce) Secretary acted contrary to the law and abused his discretion when he triggered a change in the dolphin safe label standard on the ground that he lacked sufficient evidence of significant adverse impacts." Judge Henderson restored strong "dolphin safe" standards favored by environmental groups and chastised the government for ignoring the best available scientific evidence.

A copy of the full decision by Judge Henderson can be found at http://www.cand.uscourts.gov/.

The Clinton/Gore Administration, a vitriolic Mexican government, and Greenpeace Mexico have issued official statements which misstate the legal ruling and vilify Judge Henderson, the pro-dolphin coalition, and the U.S. legal system. These false claims must be challenged.

In a press release dated April 12, 2000 ("Commerce Secretary Disappointed at Federal Court Ruling"), the Clinton/Gore Administration claims that "The completed portions of the research study did not demonstrate that depleted dolphin stocks are significantly adversely affected by the encirclement fishing practice."

This statement is false. The extensive research conducted by biologists for the National Marine Fisheries Service (NMFS) concluded that depleted dolphin populations are not recovering as expected, and in fact some populations may be continuing to decline. Furthermore, the NMFS scientists concluded that "the information suggests but by no means conclusively that the fishery has been the source of significant adverse impact on these two (depleted dolphin) populations." (NMFS Report to Congress, March 1999).

As Judge Henderson states in his powerful written decision: "...the scientific information that was available from the mandated studies -- that is, from population abundance surveys and the stress literature review -- and other relevant sources, all pointed in the direction of a significant adverse impact."

It is clear that the Clinton/Gore Administration blatantly ignored their own scientists in order to weaken the standards for the "dolphin safe" tuna label. The Mexican tuna millionaires and free trade bureaucrats won out at the expense of dolphins and American consumers.

The Mexican government's statement also mischaracterized the ruling ("Mexico Says San Francisco Court Ruling on Tuna Ban Misinformed, Unfair," April 13, 2000), claiming: "Judge Henderson's ruling is not only unfair but totally misinformed, since he omitted considering all of the scientific evidence that the U.S. Government presented..." Again, this statement is completely false -- Judge Henderson had before him every piece of relevant U.S. Government scientific evidence and cited them extensively in his 32-page written decision. All the studies, furthermore, reach conclusions totally opposite those sought by the Mexican tuna industry and the Clinton/Gore Administration. In fact, no credible scientific information was obtained suggesting any cause, other than the chasing and netting of dolphins in the tuna fishery, for the failure of depleted dolphin populations to recover.

Greenpeace Mexico inexplicably claimed (Associated Press April 12, 2000) that authorities "...should not pass off protectionist measures in the name of conservation." Since the ruling applies equally to U.S. and Mexican tunaboats, it cannot constitute a trade "protectionist measure" by any stretch of the imagination.

Furthermore, Greenpeace Mexico claimed that dolphin-safe fishing practices kill "thousands of marine species," ignoring the facts of the case:

  • The large foreign tuna fleets that set nets on dolphins also routinely set their nets on floating objects, with high bycatch of nontarget species, when the opportunity is present, and they have done so for decades. Only with the success of the "dolphin safe" campaign have tuna fishing nations raised the "red herring" of nontarget fish losses in "dolphin safe" fishing practices.

  • Tellingly, there have been no efforts, either by the Clinton/Gore Administration or by the Mexican government, to restrict net sets on logs or other floating objects, where the highest bycatch occurs, by the massive fleets of Mexico, Venezuela, and Colombia. These nations' vessels continue to set nets without restraint on dolphins, on schools of tuna, and on floating objects with high bycatch.

  • The "dolphin safe" campaign, which includes a prohibition on the use of high seas drift nets, has made a major worldwide contribution to the reduction of bycatch and ridding the oceans of senselessly destructive fishing practices. Organizations supporting the strong and verifiable "dolphin safe" label have been in the forefront of efforts to protect the entire ocean ecosystems of the world, including whales and dolphins.

  • The Clinton/Gore Administration, the Mexican Government, and Greenpeace Mexico are not telling the truth about dolphins and the tuna fishery, nor are they telling the truth about Judge Henderson's landmark decision to ensure the integrity of the "dolphin safe" tuna label.

The American public deserves better.
