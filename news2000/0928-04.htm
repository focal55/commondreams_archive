Martin Sheen Stars in New Handgun Control Ads Criticizing Bush Record
 
WASHINGTON - September 28 - Handgun Control today released two new issue advocacy ads, featuring Martin Sheen of the Emmy-award winning drama The West Wing, which criticize the pro-gun record of Governor George W. Bush and urge viewers to check the facts on the gun records of other elected officials. Sheen donated his time and appearance to Handgun Control.

The ads, which were produced by York-Zimmerman, Inc., direct viewers to the bushandguns.com and handguncontrol.org websites to learn more about the records of Governor Bush and other elected officials. With an initial buy of several hundred thousand dollars, the ads will appear in Cleveland, Milwaukee and St. Louis as well as in the Washington, D.C. market beginning this weekend.

"We are extremely grateful that a star of Mr. Sheen's stature would take the time to contribute his appearance to this vital message," said Sarah Brady, chair of Handgun Control. "Mr. Sheen's long record of social activism speaks for itself. By speaking of the need for sensible gun laws at this time, he will help call attention to those elected officials whose records reflect the positions and power of the gun lobby."

Earlier this month, Handgun Control spent $1.4 million -- a record for the group -- on another ad about Governor Bush's record. Additionally, the Handgun Control Voter Education Fund has and will run ads in key races around the country.

---
MEDIA NOTE: Mr. Sheen will not be available for comment or interviews, and has asked us to let you know that "the ads speak for themselves."

------
Handgun Control, chaired by Sarah Brady, was founded in 1974 by Dr. Mark Borinsky and N.T. "Pete" Shields, two victims of gun violence. Based in Washington, D.C., HCI works with law enforcement, public health, religious, and community groups across the country to strengthen and protect federal, state and local gun control laws, but does not seek to ban all guns. HCI has more than 400,000 members nationwide, making it the nation's largest citizens' gun control lobbying organization. More information about HCI and its sister organization, the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence, can be found on our website at http://www.handguncontrol.org.
