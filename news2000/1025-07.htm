Sierra Club Launches Independent Expenditure Campaigns
 
WASHINGTON - October 25 - The Sierra Club today launched independent expenditure campaigns in three Congressional races: Washington's second district, Ohio's 12th district and Utah's second district. The goal of these campaigns is to elect pro-environment candidates in these open seats. The Sierra Club is spending approximately $300,000 on these three independent expenditure campaigns.

"We need Members of Congress who will clean up our air and water, stand up to big polluters and preserve our wild places," said Dan Weiss, Political Director, Sierra Club. "By supporting pro-environment candidates in these critical districts, we are working to push environmental issues to the forefront in the 107th Congress."

The Sierra Club's campaigns will include television and radio ads about the records of the candidates; direct mailings; phone banks and grassroots Get-Out-The Vote activities.

In WA-02, the Sierra Club is campaigning for Democrat Richard Larsen and contrasting his support for clean water programs at the local and state level against his opponent, State Representative John Koster, who has consistently voted against environmental protection programs. "John Koster has voted to allow building landfills on top of our fragile supplies of drinking water," said Bill Arthur, Regional Director for Washington, Sierra Club. "His voting record clearly shows that Mr. Koster is no friend of clean water and a healthy environment and this is why Sierra Club is supporting Richard Larsen."

Columbus City Councilwoman Maryellen O'Shaughnessy, who the Sierra Club is supporting in OH-12, is battling State Representative Pat Tiberi. Tiberi has voted to allow more pollution in Ohio's rivers and streams and to exempt many Ohio waterways from the Clean Water Act. "Ms. O'Shaughnessy's record speaks for itself. She's been a staunch supporter of clean water standards and she believes in smart growth, not haphazard sprawl," said David Scott, Chairman of the Sierra Club's Ohio Chapter.

And in UT-02, the Sierra Club is backing Jim Matheson in his race against Derek Smith. Smith is opposed to the only realistic funding for expanded mass transit in Utah's second district and he supports the construction of the sprawl-inducing Legacy Highway. "People in Utah are very conscious of the environmental threats that sprawl brings with it and Jim Matheson understands this. He supports smart growth and measures to curb suburban sprawl," said Claire Gardner, Political Chairwoman for the Utah

Chapter of the Sierra Club. "But Derek Smith supports the Legacy Highway, a clear threat to our natural environment and our quality of life here in Utah."

"In each of these open seat races, there is a stark contrast between the environmental records of the candidates," said Weiss.

Through these independent expenditure campaigns, the Sierra Club will continue its support of candidates who will support the environment. By using independent expenditure campaigns, the Sierra Club may legally spend as much money necessary to win these seats. However, there can be no coordination or communication between the candidates and their staff and the Sierra Club. The Sierra Club closely adheres to all federal campaign laws while running independent expenditure campaigns.
