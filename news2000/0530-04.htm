Sierra Club Applauds Gore's Vision of 'Environmental Decade'
 
WASHINGTON - May 30 - The Sierra Club today applauded Vice President Al Gore's vision of an "environmental decade" to begin the 21st century. In his address on the shores of Lake Michigan, Gore supported important initiatives such as protecting our nation's remaining wild forests, encouraging smart growth and opposing efforts to weaken our clean air and water laws.

"We strongly applaud Vice President Gore for articulating a bold vision for protecting the environment in the 21st century," said Sierra Club Executive Director Carl Pope. "We're especially pleased with his support for protecting our wild forest roadless areas, including the Tongass National Forest in Alaska. The new draft Forest Service plan does not yet protect our wild forests from logging and other destructive activities. Gore's vision is the type of bold leadership America needs to protect its remaining wild forests for future generations to enjoy, explore and discover."

The Sierra Club is urging citizens to attend public hearings this summer to urge the U.S. Forest Service to provide full protection for all unspoiled roadless areas of 1,000 acres or larger from all destructive activities, including the Tongass.

In addition, Gore called for proactive steps to clean up polluted water bodies, such as the Great Lakes and our nation's dirtiest power plants.

"We're glad Vice President Gore is once and for all dispelling the myth that there is a trade-off between clean air and water and a world-class economy," Pope said. "We encourage other leaders to voice their support for protecting our environment; for our families, for our future."

Gore made the comments in a speech where he received the endorsement of the League of Conservation Voters. The Sierra Club is currently undergoing its grassroots endorsement process, surveying the views of its 65 Chapters and its leaders and members.
