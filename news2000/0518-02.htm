Japan's Mistreatment of Activists Sparks Global Protests
Protest Today at Noon at Japanese Embassy
 
WASHINGTON - May 18 - Today, in a heated response to the Japanese government's raids, threats and human rights violations against environmental activists in Tokyo last week, a dramatic protest is taking place at noon in front of the Japanese Embassy.

An International Day of Action was declared by Greenpeace, and supporters in more than 15 countries have gathered at Japanese embassies demanding the immediate release of four imprisoned activists. While no formal charges have been filed against the activists, they have been detained in prison for nine days. Families of the activists arrived in Tokyo today, but no decision will be made on the activists' fate until May 20.

Last Friday, a judge ordered the four Greenpeace activists, moved to the notorious "Daiyo Kangoku" prison system. In a recent report, The United States Human Rights Committee condemned this system for its practice of long, unmonitored interrogations, verbal abuse, and depriving prisoners of sleep. After the arrests the Greenpeace office in Tokyo and the Greenpeace flagship the "Rainbow Warrior" were raided and property seized.

Executive Director of Greenpeace International, Thilo Bode said: "This is a blatant attempt to silence legitimate peaceful protest against dioxin emissions into the environment. I call on the Japanese authorities to release our activists who were simply exercising their international right to freedom of speech in alerting the world to the dangers of incineration and dioxin emissions."

The four activists, were arrested in Tokyo on May 9, after a non-violent action highlighting the problems of dioxin emissions into the environment. Japan has the highest levels of dioxin emissions in the world today as a consequence of having more than 2000 waste incinerators, more than any other country. Dioxins are toxic substances created during the incineration process and are linked to multiple cancers as well as non-cancerous effects such as endometriosis and immune system damage. They are toxic in extremely minute quantities and are categorized as known human carcinogens. Amnesty International has also condemned Japanese detention of the activists. In a statement, it said, "The heavy handed treatment of these peaceful protesters is an obvious attempt to silence and intimidate environmental activism. The activists are being held in continuous detention without charge or trial, a practice which is unacceptable by international human rights standards."

The protest is being held today at noon at the Japanese Embassy in Washington, D.C. at 2520 Massachusetts Ave. N.W., between California and Bancroft.
