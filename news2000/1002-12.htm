Commission-Sponsored Debates Illegal Under Federal Rules
Poll Finds Bush/Gore Ignore Major Concerns of Most Voters
 
WASHINGTON - October 2 - News Advisory:

A new poll, conducted by the Center on Public Attitudes, a social science group associated with the University of Maryland, reveals that despite a robust stock market, Americans overwhelmingly want the Presidential candidates to debate issues of basic equality and fairness that are largely ignored in the current elections. "As our new poll reveals, the American public is deeply concerned by issues including labor rights and environmental considerations in international trade, the gap between the rich and the poor, and excessive military spending. Issues which will be largely ignored by tonight's debate duopoly by the Republican and Democratic parties," said John Cavanagh of the Institute for Policy Studies.

-- Press Briefing followed by Freedom for Sale Trail
Oct. 3, at 12 noon
Boston Commons, meet at the Park Street Station (Red Line)
Boston, MA (press are welcome to join Freedom for Sale Trail tour following the briefing)

-- Suing the Commission on Presidential Debates: Why the 2000 Debates are Illegal
Oct. 2 at 9 p.m.
Harvard Law School, 101 Hauser Hall

Speakers include:

-- Jamin Raskin, director of the Appleseed Commission on Electoral Reform at American University and a leading scholar on the history and current structure on the Commission on Presidential Debates.
-- Chuck Collins, executive director of the Boston-based United for A Fair Economy, a non-partisan citizen organization working to reduce economic inequality and increase shared prosperity.
-- Amy Quinn, Institute for Policy Studies, an independent research organization, to outline the new poll results
 

Speakers will address:

-- How the exclusion of third party candidates will lead to one of the lowest debate viewerships and voter turnout in years
-- How the current funding and operational structure of the Commission on Presidential Debates is illegal under Federal Election Commission rules
-- Why the first Presidential debates of the new millennium will be met by public frustration and protest as citizens feel locked out of debate forums

"Given the tremendous impact debates can have on elections, it is a crime that they have been entrusted to the private Commission on Presidential Debates which is entirely controlled by the two major parties and their corporate sponsors", said Jamin Raskin, head of an independent task force on Presidential Debates convened by American University. "A more inclusive approach to the debates would allow any candidate who has 5 percent support in national polls or who national polls show a majority of the public wants included."
