Setting the Record Straight on the Second Amendment
 
WASHINGTON - February 14 - News Advisory:

WHAT:
On April 7, 1999, Judge Sam Cummings of the Northern District of Texas became the first federal judge in more than 40 years to overturn a gun control law on the grounds that the Second Amendment protects the individual right to own firearms. Judge Cummings' ruling in U.S. vs. Emerson not only violated the long-standing rule that requires lower courts to follow the lead of appellate courts, but also ignored strong opposing legal and historical scholarship on the language of the Second Amendment.

The Emerson decision has given new vigor to the gun lobby's campaign of misrepresenting the Second Amendment as guaranteeing a broad, individual right to bear arms and precluding the enactment of reasonable gun control laws.

Some of America's most acclaimed scholars will shatter the gun lobby's myth of the Second Amendment by presenting academic studies of the Amendment's historical context. These historians will refute the individual rights theory promulgated by the gun lobby, discuss the historical context in which the amendment was crafted, including its relationship to English law, and speak directly to the Emerson decision.

This timely symposium will be invaluable to understanding the arguments surrounding Emerson as the Fifth Circuit Court of Appeals considers the federal government's appeal of the decision.

WHO:
-- Professor Lois Schwoerer (retired), George Washington University
-- Professor Don Higginbotham, University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill
-- Professor Saul Cornell, Ohio State University
-- Professor Michael Bellesiles, Emory University
-- Introduction: David W. Clark, Jackson, Miss., former chair of the ABA Coordinating Committee on Gun Violence
