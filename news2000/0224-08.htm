Activists And Their Giant Mosquito Demand:
“What’s Your Plan To Solve Global Warming?”
 

DOBBS FERRY, NY - February 24 - Today, as the New York League of Conservation Voters endorsed Gore as their environmental candidate, activists concerned about global warming gathered outside in what should have been the cold with their thirty foot model of the world enveloped by an infectious disease-carrying mosquito. These non-partisan campaigners have followed the presidential candidates throughout the early primary states. Along the way, the campaigners have traveled through drought-ridden Iowa, snow-lacking New Hampshire and now balmy, fifty degree New York, urging the candidates to inform the public about their plans to solve global warming.

"All the candidates have stated publicly that global warming is a threat. We are here to ensure that they continue to commit to a dialogue about their plan to solve the problem,” said Kate Simmons, Global Warming 2000 New York field staff member.

According to the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency, temperatures could increase from 1.6-6.3 degrees Fahrenheit by the year 2100. Diseases that thrive in warmer climates, such as dengue and yellow fever, malaria, encephalitis and cholera are likely to spread due to the expanded ranges of mosquitoes and other disease-carrying organisms. New York City experienced a small taste of what global warming could mean when an encephalitis outbreak occurred in the city this past August.

Activists have not been alone in their efforts to create a dialogue about global warming solutions during the early primaries. Over 100 local elected officials and 400 scientists have joined in calling on the candidates to lay out their plans to address global warming. Lizann Altman, a Southampton town council member stated that, "Here in Southampton, we are very concerned that global warming-induced sea-level rise will negatively impact our coastal community. This is an essential issue for all the presidential candidates to debate."

Throughout the month of March, Global Warming 2000 activists will continue to urge the candidates to lay out comprehensive plans to solve global warming. Working with local elected officials, scientists, health specialists and the religious community, activists will use visibility tactics such as giant inflatable mosquitoes and other public demonstrations to keep the pressure on the candidates and the public aware of the global warming threat in their communities. Yesterday, one team activist even feigned fainting into the arms of Vice President Gore to highlight the impacts of heat related illness due to global warming in Florida.

"The presidential candidates need to be aware of the health and environmental impacts of global warming," said Matt Stembridge, Global Warming 2000 New York Campaign Director. "No matter where they are, with which party they are affiliated, or with whom they are meeting, we will continue to urge each candidate to further present their own plan to solve global warming.” Digital image of the inflatable prop is available at the Ozone Action website: www.ozone.org
