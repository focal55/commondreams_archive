Comedian Al Franken Leads Planned Parenthood National Day of Action
Pro-Choice Activists Lobbying in Washington
 

WASHINGTON - March 22 - Planned Parenthood Federation of America (PPFA) is launching its first national Congressional Day of Action on Thursday, March 23, and, with help from comedian Al Franken, is urging pro-choice activists to make sure their views are heard.

Those who can't lobby in person in Washington can get involved by logging onto the Planned Parenthood Web site, www.plannedparenthood.org. Activists will be greeted on the site with a special message and call to action from Al Franken, will have the opportunity to chat with such congressional leaders as Sen. Barbara Boxer (D-CA), Rep. Ellen Tauscher (D-CA), and Rep. Anthony Weiner (D-NY), and will be able to send free faxes to their members of Congress.

"We're expecting hundreds of pro-choice, pro-family planning people from around the country to be walking the halls of Congress," said PPFA President Gloria Feldt. "We also want to dramatically add to their voices by generating thousands of emails and faxes on the same day. On-line activism is part of Planned Parenthood's overall advocacy strategy which includes work on Capitol Hill, in local communities, and on-line. As one of the first organizations to overlay all three, we consider this an important step in reenergizing pro-choice America and making our voices heard in the political process," Feldt added.

The Congressional Day of Action Online will call on Congress to take three important actions to protect the health and well being of women here and abroad: approve the Equity in Prescription Insurance and Contraceptive Coverage Act (EPICC); increase funding for Title X, America's Family Planning Program; and remove the Global Gag Rule, the restrictions that have been attached to international family planning assistance.

The virtual lobbying day is part of the Responsible Choices. Action Agenda, a unique campaign that uses technology to attract and mobilize supporters at the grassroots level and to involve more people in the political process. Supporting the virtual and physical lobbying day will be a full-page ad in the March 23 Washington Post. In addition, flyers will be distributed at metro stops around the city at rush hour on Wednesday, March 22.

The Congressional Day of Action is one of many activities scheduled during the PPFA annual conference. From March 22 through March 25, more than 1,000 staff members, volunteers, and supporters from across the country will gather for a Public Policy Summit, followed by three days of working meetings, culminating in an awards banquet.

All events will take place at the Grand Hyatt Washington, across from the convention center in downtown Washington, DC. To arrange coverage, call PPFA press officers Sandra Jordan (202-973-6395) or Colleen McCabe (212-261-4729).
