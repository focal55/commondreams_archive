Peolple for the American Way: SupremeCourtVote.org Campaign In Final Countdown To Nov. 7
 
WASHINGTON - October 16 - In the final weeks before the election, People For the American Way (PFAW) is hosting or taking part in rallies and public forums in key battleground states around the country to urge voters to look to the Supreme Court when they cast their vote for President.

Five special Supreme Court Forums -- in New York, San Francisco, St. Louis, Detroit, and Cleveland -- will bring together speakers and activists to discuss the potential impact of the next President's Court appointments on reproductive rights, civil rights, gun control, the environment, disability rights, workers' rights, and much more.

In addition, PFAW's leadership will fan out around the country to participate in a variety of other events to bring home to Americans the impact that the next two or three Court appointments could have, especially if they are in the ideological mold of Justices Antonin Scalia and Clarence Thomas, the two right-wing justices Gov. George W. Bush says he admires. Vice President Al Gore has also been clear: he will seek to appoint Justices who are committed to preserving individual rights.

"The choice Americans make in this election could determine the direction of the Supreme Court for the next 40 years," said Ralph G. Neas, President of People For the American Way. "The Court can keep us moving ahead -- or it could back the clock on half a century of progress."

PFAW is distributing thousands of organizing kits and mobilizing activists around the country to create their own forums, rallies and panel discussions around the Supreme Court issue. For further information, see the schedule below and the special PFAW Web site on this issue, http://www.supremecourtvote.org
