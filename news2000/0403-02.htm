Half-Million-Postcard Blizzard to Hit Capitol
Sierra Club to Deliver 600,000 Comments Urging Congress to Protect Giant Sequoia; Rep. Sam Farr (D-CA), Other Leaders to Press for Strong National Monument
 
WASHINGTON - April 3 - Representative Sam Farr (D-CA) will join with the Sierra Club on Wednesday to urge President Clinton to adequately protect California's giant sequoia forests from destruction by nearby logging. Later this month, President Clinton is expected to designate a National Monument protecting up to 400,000 acres of giant sequoia forests, but details are still being finalized.

If President Clinton's National Monument designation fully protects the giant sequoia, his action would fulfill the century-old dream of Sierra Club founder John Muir, who called these forests "nature's masterpiece."

Current U.S. Forest Service policy protects the sequoia trees themselves, but permits other tree species surrounding the giant sequoia to be logged. And contrary to what most people believe, over half the remaining giant sequoias have no permanent protection. Logging around the sequoia groves has the potential to kill the sequoia just as an ax or a chainsaw does. Because sequoia trees are very vulnerable to disruption, protecting the forest around the sequoias will ensure these towering trees can survive in their healthy, natural habitat.

Speaking at Wednesday's press conference will be Rep. Sam Farr (D-CA), who is leading the Congressional fight for the Giant Sequoias, and Joe Fontaine, chair of the Sierra Club's Yosemite Committee, who has worked for decades to save the sequoia forests. Protecting the sequoia has been one of the Sierra Club's top goals for a century.
