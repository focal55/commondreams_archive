President Clinton To Address PFAW
and Progressive Leaders On October 24
Evening Event Will Stress Dangers of Rightward Shift on Supreme Court
 
WASHINGTON - October 23 - President Bill Clinton will address leaders of the progressive community at a special evening event hosted by People For the American Way on Tuesday, October 24.

The evening will include a panel discussion of the most important and far-reaching domestic issue in this year’s presidential election: what kind of justices the next President will choose for the U.S. Supreme Court. That's because the Court is now closely divided on fundamental constitutional issues and the next President is likely to have the chance to pick as many as 2-3 Justices, enough to shift the balance on key issues such as privacy, civil rights, reproductive rights, environmental protection, gun control, disability rights, among others. It has now been more than six years since the last Supreme Court appointment, the longest interval in 177 years.

WHEN: Tuesday, October 24, 2000

Reception: 7:00 p.m.

Presidential address, followed by Supreme Court Panel: 8:00

WHERE: National Education Association

16th & M Streets, NW

PANEL: James Carville, Democratic strategist

Mary Frances Berry, Chairperson, U.S. Commission on Civil Rights

Ralph G. Neas, President, People For the American Way

"With so many important rights riding on just one or two votes, it's no exaggeration to say that the future of the Supreme Court makes this the most important election in over half a century," said Neas. "We are urging all Americans to look at this issue carefully when they make their judgment about who they want for President."
