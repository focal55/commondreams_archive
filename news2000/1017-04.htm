Bush Campaign Still Misleads On Issue Of Whether Federal Hate Crimes Law Should Cover Sexual Orientation
Bush Officials Refuse to Answer Directly Mother of Slain Gay Hate Crime Victim
 
WASHINGTON - October 17 - The Human Rights Campaign today admonished Texas Gov. George W. Bush for continuing to refuse to answer directly whether current federal hate crimes law should cover sexual orientation. Judy Shepard, mother of slain Wyoming college student Matthew Shepard, asked the question again in a new HRC radio advertisement, and officials for the GOP presidential candidate have yet to give a straightforward answer, according to HRC.

“The Bush campaign’s response to Shepard’s question is the greatest Texas dance since the two-step, and shows a wanton display of political maneuvering,” said HRC Political Director Winnie Stachelberg. “It is amazing that the Bush campaign can’t answer the simple question of whether they support sexual orientation being added to current federal hate crimes law. Hate crime victims and their families deserve a simple, honest answer. Unfortunately, the Bush campaign has not been forthcoming.”

"Texas has a hate crimes law on the books…The individuals who murdered James Byrd Jr. received some of the toughest penalties available, including the death penalty for two and life in prison for the other,” Bush campaign officials today told the Washington Post in response to the HRC ad. The governor supports the bill put forth by Sen. Orrin Hatch, R-Utah, “because it helps states prosecute hate crimes without dramatically expanding the role and the reach of the federal government,” the officials said.

“What the Bush campaign failed to mention is that the Hatch bill does not include sexual orientation which leads us to wonder if he does not believe gay and lesbian Americans should receive the same protection as other people from hate crimes,” said Stachelberg. “Furthermore, although Texas has had a weak penalty enhancement statute since 1993, it also does not include sexual orientation. Legislation was introduced in 1998 that would have strengthened the existing state law, while also adding sexual orientation, but Bush opposed it.”

Bush’s apparent opposition to adding sexual orientation is even more striking in light of the Oct. 15 release of the FBI’s Uniform Crime Reports for 1999, the latest year for which statistics are available. As overall serious crime continued to decrease for the eighth consecutive year, hate crimes based on sexual orientation have continued to rise, and increased 4.5 percent from 1998 to 1999.

Further, reported hate crime incidents based on sexual orientation have more than tripled since the FBI began collecting statistics in 1991- comprising 16.7 percent of all hate crimes for 1999 at 1,317, according to the FBI data. Hate crimes based on sexual orientation continue to make up the third highest category after race and religion, which make up 54.5 and 17.9 percent, respectively of the total, 7,876.

The new HRC radio campaign is currently playing in media markets where Bush is traveling, beginning today in St. Louis, Mo. The ad will also run in other cities he visits on the campaign trail until he answers the question posed by Shepard, HRC said. A spending figure for the campaign is not available due to the open-ended nature of the run.

The radio ad features Shepard speaking by telephone and is directed at Bush. The text of the ad follows:
    “Governor Bush, my name is Judy Shepard. Two years ago my son Matthew was murdered because he was gay. Last week you said in the debate that you opposed the Kennedy hate crimes bill. I want to know why? It has nothing to do with punishment. It has everything to do with giving law enforcement new tools in combating hate violence. Your own state received federal help in convicting the murderers of James Byrd Jr. Law enforcement in Jasper, Texas, received $284,000 to help them with the crime.

    “In Laramie, Wyoming, the sheriff’s department had to layoff four deputies because of the cost of investigating and prosecuting my son’s murderers. The detectives got the job done, but they support the Kennedy hate crimes bill because they did not get the same federal assistance. Why? Because sexual orientation is not covered in current hate crimes law.

    “I am asking American families to call you, Governor, at (512) 463-2000 and ask why you said you support Senator Hatch’s bill which is much weaker and excludes sexual orientation. Do you support including sexual orientation in federal hate crimes law? Governor Bush, we deserve an answer.

    "Paid for by the Human Rights Campaign."

The Human Rights Campaign is the largest national lesbian and gay political organization, with members throughout the country. It effectively lobbies Congress, provides campaign support and educates the public to ensure that lesbian and gay Americans can be open, honest and safe at home, at work and in the community.
