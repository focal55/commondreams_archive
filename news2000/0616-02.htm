Website Promotes Climate-Neutral Vacations
Planting trees online helps slow global warming
 
WASHINGTON - June 16 - In the wake of a new federal report outlining the significant effects global warming will have in the United States, American Forests is showing travelers how they can have "greener," more environmentally friendly summer vacations by planting trees to reduce carbon dioxide produced while on vacation. Carbon dioxide (CO2), from the burning of fossil fuels such as gasoline, jet fuel, and coal-generated electricity, is the primary greenhouse gas contributing to global climate change. The nation's oldest conservation organization has posted three vacation scenarios on its website, www.americanforests.org, and calculated the number of trees necessary to plant to offset the CO2 generated by these trips.

According to American Forests, a family of four on a 2-week "family-style" vacation that includes 1,200 miles of driving, can offset their climate impact by planting 9 trees, less than the cost of one-half tank of gasoline. The tree number increases to 14 for a couple on a 2-week "first class" vacation to a destination 2,500 miles away. A "back to nature" camping and canoe trip for four for the same number of days can be offset for just 5 trees.

"Vacations are important for personal and family well-being and for other reasons," said Deborah Gangloff, American Forests' executive director. "We're simply pointing out that a lot of greenhouse gases are produced from our daily activities including vacations. As people become more aware of these impacts, many will realize that offsetting their carbon dioxide emissions is good for environment and only takes a click of the mouse.

 

Vacation Scenarios: Global ReLeaf trees to offset CO2 emissions:
1. First Class - Two people flying 5,000 miles Roundtrip, luxury accommodations, and activities. 1 week = 10 trees
2 weeks = 14 trees
2. Family Style - Family of four driving 1,200 miles (@ 20 mpg), moderate accommodations, visits to amusement parks, boating, etc. 1 week = 6 trees
2 weeks = 9 trees
3. Back to Nature - Four people driving 800 miles (@15 mpg); 2 nights in motels per week, others camping, cooking w/ propane stove, canoeing, hiking. 1 week = 3 trees
2 weeks = 5 trees

 

Climate change is one of the most serious environmental issues facing the planet today. A federal report released June 12 documents the potential consequences this century from the increasing greenhouse gases we produce every year. According to the new report, Climate Change Impacts on the United States, average temperatures are likely to rise 5-10 degrees Fahrenheit in the U.S. creating urban heat waves, more smog, warmer winters, and more extreme storms that will result in degraded river systems and more polluted runoff. Coastal erosion and more frequent ocean storms will put additional pressures on seaside communities. Warmer waters will have adverse effects on wildlife such as salmon. In addition to directly removing CO2 from the atmosphere, strategically planted trees help counteract many of these impacts, as well.

While the types of vacations people take vary greatly, the one thing all vacations have in common is their use of energy. Whether we get there by car, bus, train, or airplane, we're using fossil fuels that emit climate-change inducing CO2. Nearly 20 pounds of CO2 is produced for every gallon of gasoline used in a vehicle. A 2,000 mile drive in a vehicle that gets 20 miles per gallon produces 2,000 pounds of carbon dioxide, about the same amount that American Forests estimates one of its trees will remove from the atmosphere over 40 years. The average 1,600-mile flight by commercial airliner also generates about the same amount of CO2 per passenger.

In addition to planting trees to help make vacations more environmentally friendly, American Forests makes it easy to calculate, and then offset, the CO2 production of a household for an entire year with its online Climate Change Calculator, also found at www.americanforests.org. Users input information including the type and amount of energy used in their home and the number of miles they drive in a year. The Calculator then determines how many new trees are needed to offset these emissions. A number of businesses and corporations are also using American Forests' Climate Change Calculator to determine the number of trees to plant to offset environmental impacts ranging from business travel to those of their entire operations.

The organization also analyzes satellite images and its own CITYgreen software to calculate the value of tree cover in cities and towns. Healthy tree cover is essential to maintaining the green infrastructure necessary for overall environmental and community health. Among other things, trees provide cooling shade that reduces the urban heat island effect. This is valuable since higher urban temperatures increase the amount of smog, result in the use of more fossil fuels to power air conditioners, and are a direct threat to public health.

American Forests makes planting trees easy through its website, www.americanforests.org. The trees cost $1 each and are planted in forest restoration projects that provide wildlife habitat for endangered species, reduce erosion, clean the air, and filter polluted runoff. American Forests will plant more than 3 million trees this year in Global ReLeaf projects to restore native longleaf pine forests in Florida, to improve water quality in the Chesapeake Bay, salmon streams in the Pacific Northwest, and even to create tiger habitat in Siberia. The ability to plant trees online is a major convenience for people who want to improve the environment, but may not have the time or space to plant trees themselves.

###

American Forests, founded in 1875, is the oldest national nonprofit citizen conservation organization. Today it is a world leader in planting trees for environmental restoration. The goal of Global ReLeaf 2000 is to plant 20 million trees for the new millennium. So far, more than 13 million trees have been planted. Trees planted with American Forests are also helping to meet the goals of Millennium Green, a project of the White House Millennium Council. The Vacation Climate Change Calculator is featured in the Summer issue of American Forests magazine.
