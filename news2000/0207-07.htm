Release of LCV's 1999 National Environmental Scorecard
 
WASHINGTON - February 7 - News Advisory:

The League of Conservation Voters (LCV) will release the 1999 National Environmental Scorecard at a 10 a.m. news conference, Thursday, Feb. 10, at the National Press Club.

Every member of the U.S. House and Senate will be given a rating for their environmental votes during the first session of the 106th Congress.

Based on the consensus of experts from more than 20 respected environmental, conservation and sportsmen groups, LCV's National Environmental Scorecard assigns a percentage score to each member of Congress based on the year's key environmental votes. LCV has produced a Scorecard for each Congress since 1970.

LCV president Deb Callahan will present the 1999 Scorecard findings and analysis to reporters and answer Scorecard-related questions at Thursday's news conference.

The nonprofit League of Conservation Voters is the bipartisan political voice for environment. LCV is the only organization working full-time to inform citizens about the environmental voting records of Members of Congress. An interactive, on-line version of the 1999 Scorecard will be available at http://www.lcv.org.
