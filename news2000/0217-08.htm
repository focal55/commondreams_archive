HCI: Hill Republicans Again Playing Chicken on Guns?
 
WASHINGTON - February 17 - The following was released today by Handgun Control Inc.:

Reports that Senate Judiciary Chairman Orrin Hatch is considering dropping gun safety legislation from the juvenile justice bill again demonstrate that the Congressional Republican leadership is out of touch with what Americans want for public safety.

Last year, after the Columbine tragedy, the Senate passed juvenile justice legislation with provisions that would help keep guns out of the hands of juveniles and criminals. The Senate voted to restrict possession of assault weapons by juveniles, prevent violent juveniles from purchasing firearms as adults, eliminate the sale of large ammunition magazines and, most important and with a dramatic tie-breaking vote by Vice-President Gore, close the gun-show loophole by requiring background checks for all gun show purchases. The House, however, after voting for an artfully worded amendment that would actually have widened the gun-show loophole, dropped all gun provisions from the bill and left it to a House-Senate conference to restore the integrity of the legislation. With a lot of help from the gun lobby, that legislation has now been stalled since last summer.

Stripping the gun provisions out of the juvenile justice bill would not go unnoticed in an election year in which both Democratic presidential candidates support much more stringent regulation of gun access, while the three remaining Republican candidates have long records of pro-gun votes and support. Recent polls demonstrate as much as 89% support for closing the gun show loophole and as much as 74% support for the gun licensing system proposed by President Clinton in the State of the Union address. In 1998 and 1999, a referendum closing the gun-show loophole passed in Florida, while a referendum to allow the carrying of concealed weapons failed in Missouri despite massive NRA support. Survey after survey demonstrates widespread public support for common-sense gun control, particularly among women and suburban voters.

"Talk about shooting yourself in the foot," said Sarah Brady, Chair of Handgun Control. "If the Republican leadership in Congress tries to pass juvenile justice legislation without gun control provisions, they will continue to be seen as the party that puts gun lobby money ahead of the safety of our children. We welcome a renewed Congressional debate on stopping juvenile gun violence, and so, I suspect, will the American people."

------
Handgun Control, chaired by Sarah Brady, is the nation's largest citizens' gun control lobbying organization. Based in Washington, D.C., HCI works to enact stronger federal, state and local gun control laws, but does not seek to ban handguns. Founded in 1974, HCI has more than 400,000 members nationwide and works with local groups around the country to enact and protect reasonable gun control laws. More information about HCI and its affiliated organization, the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence, can be found on our website at http://www.handguncontrol.org.
