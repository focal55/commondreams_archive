League of Conservation Voters: Bush Energy Proposal Criticized By League of Conservation Voters
 
WASHINGTON - September 29 - Calling George W. Bush’s energy proposal short sighted and a significant threat to the environment, the League of Conservation Voters (LCV) today rejected Bush’s false premise that clean air, clean water and the nation’s natural treasures must be sacrificed in order to have a successful energy policy.

Bush’s energy policy is fueled by concern for protecting oil and gas interests, not the interests of the majority of voters who want stronger clean air and water protections, said LCV President Deb Callahan.  His proposal would do little to reduce the nation’s reliance on oil, while posing a serious threat to one of North America’s last-remaining pristine wilderness ecosystems.  His call for the development of clean coal technologies is nothing more than an oxymoron.

According to the League, Bush’s plan would open Alaska’s Arctic National Wildlife Refuge to oil and gas development.  Often referred to as America’s Serengeti, the Arctic refuge is home to nearly 200 species of wildlife, including polar bears, caribou, wolves and more than 100 species of migratory birds.  Habitat destruction associated with oil development, not to mention air and water pollution and oil spills could irreversibly compromise this globally important wilderness area. 

The plan also offers ineffective long-term solutions for reducing U.S. oil dependence.   While Vice President Al Gore’s energy plan would offer tax breaks, grants, low-interest loans and other financial incentives to increase the reliability of the nation’s electricity grid and spur the use and development of cleaner technologies, Bush’s plan continues to press for short-term drilling solutions.

Bush’s announcement is bad news for public health and bad news for the environment, Callahan said.  We face a clear choice on the environment and energy in this presidential election.  While Bush and Richard Cheney are busy satisfying the oil and gas interests that have lined their campaign coffers with more than $1.5 million, only Gore and Joe Lieberman are busy addressing the energy and environmental problems facing the nation.  American’s deserve a president that will look for long-term solutions to both our energy and environmental needs, not one who is focused on short-term economic solutions that ignore the environment completely.   

                         

The League of Conservation Voters is the political voice for the national environmental and conservation community. LCV works to elect pro-environment candidates to federal office, and is the only group dedicated full-time to holding federal officials accountable on the environment.  LCV was the first national environmental organization to endorse Gore for president.  Environmental information about all of the presidential candidates can be accessed at the LCV web site at http://www.lcv.org.
