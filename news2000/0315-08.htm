Major Medical Student Organization to Rally on Steps of U.S. Capitol for National Health Insurance
 

WASHINGTON - March 15 -

News Advisory:

As part of its mission to 'involve its members in the social, moral and ethical obligations of the profession of medicine,' the American Medical Student Association (AMSA), the nation's largest, independent medical student organization will lead a rally on the steps of the U.S. Capitol, titled "Speak Up, America! Health Care Is Our Right." The rally will take place on the West Steps of the U.S. Capitol. on Thursday, March 16, at 3 p.m.

AMSA will present speakers dedicated to the cause of universal health care and national health insurance. Scheduled to attend are:

-- Rep. Henry Waxman (D-Calif.)
-- Rep. James McDermott (D-Wash.)
-- Rep. Tammy Baldwin (D-Wisc.)
-- Rep. Danny Davis (D-Ill.)
-- Rep. John Tierney (D-Mass.) (invited)
-- Quentin Young, M.D., national coordinator, Physicians for a National Health Program
-- Clarita E. Herrera, M.D., past president, American Medical Women's Association
-- Walter Shervington, M.D., national president, National Medical Association
-- Vincent DeMarco, executive director, Maryland Citizen's Health Initiative

Medical student attendees will come to the rally in their white coats, symbolizing their commitment to their chosen profession, while recognizing that change is necessary to create the system they want to practice in for the future.

During the rally, AMSA will have nearly 1000 participants holding coffee cups with eight pennies inside. For just eight cents per hour or $2 per day, per average middle-income family, or about the cost of one cup of coffee, everyone in the United States would have comprehensive, quality health care, and would not incur additional costs such as co-pays, premiums, deductibles or prescription costs. This symbolic representation of the cost of national health insurance will be used to promote this issue to the U.S. Congress and the general public.

Following the rally, medical student attendees will be visiting their members of Congress to solicit support for a bill that would encourage state innovation toward a goal of universal health care.
