Senate Ensures Anti-Choice Terrorists Do Not Escape Fines, Damages for Clinic Violence
 
WASHINGTON - February 2 - Following is a statement today by Gloria Feldt, president of Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

Today the U.S. Senate passed an amendment (80-17) to the bankruptcy reform law forcing defendants prosecuted under the Freedom of Access to Clinic Entrances Act (FACE) to pay their legal judgments. Until now, many convicted under the FACE law have hidden their assets and declared bankruptcy to escape paying their debt to society. These terrorists will now be forced to face the full force of the law; steep monetary penalties combined with prison time should prove a great deterrent to violent attacks, including death threats, anthrax threats, assaults, bombings, and shootings.

FACE has proven highly effective in ending violent and intimidating anti-abortion activities. Now those who attempt force, the threat of force, or physical obstruction to injure, intimidate, or interfere with providers of reproductive health care services or their patients will be held financially responsible. They will also held responsible for damaging or destroying the property of a reproductive health care facility.

All of us who truly care about the health and well being of Americans must work together to ensure that women can continue to make responsible choices about their lives and health, and that doctors can work and live without fear. Planned Parenthood will continue to work closely with law enforcement agencies and officers to prevent these crimes against patients, staff, volunteers, and clinics, and to ensure swift prosecution of violators of FACE.

Planned Parenthood applauds Senators Schumer (D-N.Y.), Reid (D-Nev.), Snowe (R-Maine) and Jeffords (R-Vt.) for their efforts to make anti-choice criminals pay for their crimes against women.
