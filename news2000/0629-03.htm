Sierra Club Applauds McCain Lieberman Campaign Finance Win
 
WASHINGTON - June 29 - The Sierra Club today applauded the campaign finance reform victory won by Sens. John McCain (R-AZ), Joseph Lieberman (D-CT) and Russell Feingold (D-WI) today when the United States Senate voted to adopt their proposal to require disclosure of the contributions and expenditures by so-called "527" organizations. The bill's passage occurred two days after the House of Representatives passed its 527 reform disclosure bill.

"The Sierra Club congratulates Senators McCain, Lieberman and Feingold for winning the first battle in the long war for campaign finance reform," said Carl Pope, Sierra Club executive director. "Today's vote broke the stranglehold against reform in the U.S. Senate. While the 527 disclosure provision is just the first step toward comprehensive campaign finance reform, it's an important one. The next critical step to reforming our shameful campaign finance system is for the Senate to pass the comprehensive McCain-Feingold bill and ban soft money contributions to political parties, which are expected to dwarf expenditures by 527 organizations. We call on Sen. Majority Leader Trent Lott to promptly allow a free and fair vote on the comprehensive McCain-Feingold bill."

The McCain-Lieberman-Feingold disclosure bill would require organizations that conduct issue advocacy activities under Sec. 527 of the Internal Revenue Code to report on the contributions and expenditures of their programs. Currently, organizations with 527's do not have to disclose the source of funds or spending when broadcasting issue-advocacy ads or undertaking other activities.

The Sierra Club strongly supports campaign finance reform, including the disclosure of 527 donors, the ban on soft money donations to political parties, and public financing of campaigns. The Sierra Club successfully worked for House passage of the bill by Reps. Chris Shays (R-CT) and Marty Meehan (D-MA) to ban soft-money contributions to political parties and establish restrictions on issue advocacy efforts. The Sierra Club also supported efforts by Rep. Lloyd Doggett (D-TX) to successfully pass the 527 disclosure bill in the House.

While working towards reform, the Sierra Club is conducting issue-advocacy efforts under the existing rules. The organization has a 527 and expects to spend $8 million in at least two dozen Congressional sites and the Presidential campaign this year. The Sierra Club's Environmental Voter Education Campaign will educate the public about candidates' environmental records, and urge the public to ask their officials to take specific actions to support the environment.
