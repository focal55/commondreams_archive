Missouri Legislature Ignores Concerns About Women's Health
 
WASHINGTON - September 16 - The following was released today by NARAL:

The Missouri Senate today voted to override Gov. Mel Carnahan's veto of the so-called "Infant Protection Act," the latest in a long line of attempts by the Missouri state legislature to curtail a woman's right to choose. Gov. Carnahan vetoed the bill last June because it did not contain an exception to protect the health of the woman, outlawed some of the safest and most commonly used abortion procedures, and provided a legal defense for those who attack or murder abortion providers. Proponents of the bill, which is one of the most extreme of its kind, have openly stated that it is designed to be a "test case" to challenge Roe vs. Wade.

NARAL President Kate Michelman, sharing the governor's concerns about the bill's broad-ranging effects, spoke out against the legislature's action.

"Today, the Missouri legislature has dealt a tremendous blow to the health and safety of Missouri's women, the doctors who serve them, and the freedom to choose," said Michelman. "The so-called Infant Protection Act' purports to ban only one type of abortion, but will actually result in the near elimination of abortions in the state of Missouri. Shockingly, the Act also provides an automatic legal defense for those who use violence against physicians who perform abortions, allowing such criminals to claim they were acting to prevent crimes against others in this case, a fetus. I applaud Gov. Carnahan for his principled stand in opposing this unprecedented bill and for his steadfast support of women and their doctors."

Missouri NARAL Executive Director M'Evie Mead expressed her concern for the bill's impact on the women of Missouri.

"The impact of this bill will be felt immediately. Even now, staff at Missouri's clinics, concerned about the impact of this vaguely worded law on doctors and patients and fearful of the violence that may result from its enactment, are referring patients to out-of-state facilities as they prepare to shut down," said Mead. "The women of Missouri will be left out in the cold, some having to travel hundreds of miles for what should be a safe, legal, and accessible medical procedure."

"The so-called 'Infant Protection Act' is another attempt by abortion opponents to strike at the very foundation of Roe v. Wade," continued Michelman. "Its proponents have admitted it is designed to serve as a 'test case,' a vehicle by which Roe vs. Wade may be overturned.
