Feeding Frenzy At Taxpayer Trough; Sierra Club Report Reveals Factory Farms Receive Millions of Tax Dollars to Pollute
 
WASHINGTON - September 15 - The Sierra Club today released a national report that reveals the enormous taxpayer giveaways benefiting wealthy factory hog and chicken farms, and the costs the public pays in return -- polluted air and water, a major public health hazard, and the loss of family farms. The Sierra Club's report on factory farms, Corporate Hogs at the Public Trough, highlights part of the nationwide pattern of public money going to these wealthy polluters.

"Governments are spending millions of taxpayer dollars to bring a pollution nightmare into our neighborhoods," declared Ken Midkiff, coordinator of the Sierra Club's Clean Water Committee. "In state after state, while factory farms rake in the financial benefits, Americans are paying the environmental, health and social costs."

"That giant sucking sound is the factory-farm industry taking a hold of the government teat," said Kathryn Hohmann, Director of Sierra Club's Environmental Quality Program.

From Utah's red-rock country to Maryland's Chesapeake Bay watershed, Concentrated Animal Feeding Operations (CAFOs) have benefited from millions of tax dollars for road improvements, railroad spurs, worker training and waste-water treatment plants. In return, residents are forced to boil their tap water, inhale horrendous odors, and sell their family farms. Their lakes, rivers and water supplies are contaminated with livestock waste. And CAFOs are contributing to bacterial infections resistant to normal antibiotic treatment.

"This money is not creating economic development -- it is creating environmental destruction. Instead of holding these polluters accountable for polluting our water and air, governments are offering these factories tax breaks, direct and indirect subsidies, and even outright cash outlays," Midkiff said.

Christine Johnson, a poultry grower formerly under contract with Perdue, stated: "Billion-dollar livestock factories are receiving large sums of taxpayer money while family farmers are run out of business. We've tried to maintain our own farm, but Perdue has simply made it too difficult. Meanwhile, our favorite places to swim and fish are polluted and dangerous to our children's health."

Corporate Hogs at the Public Trough studies 10 corporations as examples of a nationwide problem of the special favors that CAFOs receive and the pollution they cause. The Sierra Club report profiles:
  • Cagle's, Inc., Kentucky
  • Circle Four Farms, Utah
  • DeCoster Farms, Iowa
  • Murphy Family Farms, Illinois
  • Perdue Farms, Maryland
  • Premium Standard Farms, Missouri
  • Prestage Farms, Mississippi
  • Seaboard Corporation, Oklahoma
  • Smithfield Foods, North Carolina
  • Tyson Foods, Inc., Arkansas

    "It's time to protect America's water and support family farms, not factory farms," concluded Midkiff.
