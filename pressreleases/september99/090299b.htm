Amnesty International USA Statement on New York City Police Shooting of Gary Busch
 
WASHINGTON - September 2 - Following is a statement today by Curt Goering, senior deputy executive director of Amnesty International USA, on the New York City Police shooting of Gary Busch:

STATEMENT BY CURT GOERING
Amnesty International USA

The fatal shooting of a mentally ill man by four New York City police officers on Monday raises serious concerns about the training that officers receive to handle incidents involving the mentally ill and about the use of excessive force by the New York Police Department (NYPD).

In its 1998 "United States of America -- Rights for All" report, Amnesty International called for federal, state and local authorities, including metropolitan police departments, to ensure that training programs involve instruction for coping with emotionally disturbed individuals. While city figures show that the NYPD receives some 38,000 calls a year involving mentally disturbed people, published reports indicate that just 4,000 of these calls are handled by the Emergency Services Unit, the only unit that receives specialized training in this area. In contrast, most officers receive just 16 hours of academy training in procedures for handling the mentally ill. In the Busch case, police on the scene reportedly called the Emergency Service Unit, which unfortunately arrived after the shooting.

While reports indicate that Gary Busch struck an officer on the scene with a hammer, we are concerned that four of the officers found it necessary to disarm Busch with 12 bullets -- 11 of them hollow points, known to inflict more harm than standard bullets. This distressing incident once again underscores the critical need for New York City authorities to take effective action to prevent further tragedies and to comply with international standards on the use of force and firearms by police officers.
