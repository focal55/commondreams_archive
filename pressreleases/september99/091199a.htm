Rev. Jesse Jackson To Conduct "Free Vieques Rally" At St. Cecilia Church In NYC; Human Rights Leader To Call For End To U.S. Colonialism In Puerto Rico And Applauds Release Of FALN Members
 
CHICAGO - September 11 - St. Cecilia Church, 106th Street at Park and Lexington in New York City, will be the setting for a special rally calling for the U.S. military to end its occupation of the Puerto Rican island of Vieques when the Reverend Jesse L. Jackson Sr. speaks from its pulpit on Sunday, September 12 at 4:00 p.m. (est).  The human rights leader will join Puerto Rican religious leaders, peace activists, scholars, and others in an event designed to bring the crusade to U.S. shores.

"The U.S. has no right to own a part of another nation, and it must end its colonial posturing," Jackson said today from his national headquarters here. "We must return the land of Puerto Rico to the Puerto Ricans. The military has a right to be there only as defenders of liberty, but not as colonizers of the land and its people."

Vieques, a 52-square mile island just off the coast of Puerto Rico, is the largest area in the hemisphere for U.S. military exercises with live ammunition. To date, it remains the only place where bombing occurs near a substantial civilian population. Earlier this summer, one civilian was killed and four others were injured when a Marine F/A-18 dropped two 500-pound bombs near their work area. Additionally, due to damage to the environment, residents have a high rate of cancer and birth defects, and the fishing (one of the biggest industries) has suffered due to contamination of the sea life.

Three weeks ago, Jackson and key staff traveled to Puerto Rico to aid the island's campaign of ending "U.S. occupation" remove the land mines and live ammunition and cleanup the environment.

Ironically, it what some deemed suspicious timing to take the thunder out of Jackson's August 12-13 visit, President Bill Clinton announced clemency conditions for the release of 11 Puerto Rican political prisoners. This past weekend, the group accepted the offer and returned to their community. Jackson said that in many ways the plight of the FALN members were directly connected to the U.S.' ongoing occupation Vieques, and the nation's suspended
status as part state and part colony.

Saying that the media must work with the Puerto Rican people to dispel the myth that the FALN was comprised solely of terrorists, the human rights leader recalled that "none of them were convicted of murder, but rather by association in an organization the U.S. helped create," he said. "Yes, we condemn terrorism, it is cowardly and, yes,  want the freed men and women to honor the conditions of their clemency.

"We must bring closure to the FALN controversy by addressing the issue of Vieques," Jackson added. "The tensions were created by the U.S. because it refuses to acknowledge the will of the (Puerto Rican) people to breathe free.  FALN was created because of what has been perceived as U.S. occupation. In an act of good faith, the first step should be for the U.S. to leave Vieques and grant Puerto Rico its liberty."

Although in terms of voting the country splits evenly on whether the Caribbean commonwealth should be an independent nation, all political party, religious and civil rights leaders agree on the issue of Vieques. The clarion call includes a measure that would restore two-thirds of the island owned by the U.S. Navy to the government of Puerto Rico.

"The people of Puerto Rico have spoken," Jackson said. "We must honor their voices and free Vieques now. It is the duty of every American who enjoys freedom and liberty to join this fight. It is our duty-no one is free, until all of us are free."
