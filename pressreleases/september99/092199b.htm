So-Called "True Choice" Equals No Choice
 
WASHINGTON - September 21 - Anti-choice, anti-family planning members of Congress have introduced the Women and Children's Resources Act. The effort will redirect federal attention away from successful, yet under-funded publicly funded family planning programs. The move ensures that low-income women will have no choice in determining their reproductive destiny.

The so-called "True Choice" bill, introduced today by Senator Rick Santorum (R-PA) and Reps. Mary Bono, (R-CA), Joseph Pitts (R-PA), and Anne Northrup (R-KY), is cynical and misguided. For the first time, the federal government will be subsidizing biased counseling, denying women a full range of reproductive choices. Not only does the bill direct federal funds to so-called "crisis pregnancy" groups and religious organizations that provide only one option for pregnant women, the bill denies vulnerable women counseling on contraceptive services.

Family planning organizations offer many of the same services this bill claims it will offer, the difference being family planning organizations provide all options to pregnant women, including carrying a pregnancy to term, counseling, pre-natal care, pre-natal referral, and adoption referrals.

With teen pregnancy rates and abortion rates declining rapidly in the 1990's due to increased access and use of contraceptives, we have statistical proof what the pro-family planning community has been saying all along — prevention works.
  • Teen birth rate in the U.S. declined 16% between 1991 and 1997.
  • The number of abortions performed in the U.S dropped 11% between 1992 and 1996.
Since 1974, the Title X Family Planning Program has been on America's front line in the fight to prevent unintended pregnancy, providing contraceptives, counseling and other reproductive health care services to 6.5 million low-income women per year. Publicly funded family planning successfully avert 1.5 million unintended pregnancies and 632,000 abortions annually.

Family planning organizations provide a full slate of critical reproductive health care services to low-income women including routine pap smears, cervical cancer screening, breast cancer screening, STI screening and treatment, HIV/AIDS screening, contraceptive services, and abortion and adoption counseling.

Clearly, continued investment in publicly funded family planning is critical. While publicly funded family planning currently serves 6.5 million women, the current level of funding is woefully inadequate in that 16.5 million women need publicly supported contraceptive services.

The bottom line is, this bill is pointless: the only reason to introduce this bill now is to create political firestorm where none exits.
