Physicians Call on Congress to 'Stop Gun Violence Now!'
 
WASHINGTON - September 16 - The following was released today by Physicians for Social Responsibility:

Another day, another shooting. "Gunman Opens Fire; Innocent Victims Die." With more than 200 million guns in America -- more than one gun per adult -- it's really just a matter of time before we read the same headline again.

"With a gun in every night stand and in every jacket pocket, why should we be surprised when people get killed?" said Robert K. Musil, Ph.D., president and CEO of Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR). "What kind of society allows people to walk around with semi-automatic weapons? There have been nine mass shootings in 1999. We know what's needed to prevent the next tragedy, but our do-nothing Congress refuses to act."

According to Physicians for Social Responsibility, the only way to prevent these kinds of tragedies is to reduce the number of guns. PSR advocates major changes in the ways that guns are sold, distributed and regulated. They support gun buyback programs such as the Clinton administration's $15 million initiative. They want to end the practice of police re-circulating seized weapons and see every confiscated gun destroyed. They want a crack down on domestic gun trafficking and greater community education about the dangers of keeping a gun in the home.

"These are not radical ideas," says Dr. Musil. "These actions will make our streets, schools, and churches safer. On behalf of Physicians for Social Responsibility, I call on Congress to stand up to the National Rifle Association and gun manufacturers and others that are lobbying against sensible gun control policies. Obviously the current policies are not protecting us. If we have any hope of stopping this epidemic, we must take a different course of action than what we've doing now."

Available for Interviews
Dr. Robert K. Musil is Executive Director of Physicians for Social Responsibility, a national nonprofit organization working to prevent gun violence through public education and sensible gun control policies. Dr. Musil is on the steering committee of the Handgun Epidemic Lowering Plan (HELP), a coalition of health organizations engaged in efforts to reduce gun violence. He worked with Senator Robert Toricelli (D-N.J.) to help build support for the Firearms Safety and Consumer Protection Act of 1999. He has conducted TV debates with spokespersons from the NRA and appeared on numerous national TV and radio programs.
