Emergency Demonstration to Highlight Plight of East Timorese; U.N., U.S. Must Address Humanitarian Crisis Inside and Outside East Timor Now
 
NEW YORK - September 18 -

** When: Sunday, September 19, 2 pm

** Where: Across from the United Nations, Dag Hammarskjold Plaza, (47thSt. and 1st Ave), New York City

** What: Demonstration and Rally

** Sponsored by the East Timor Action Network and members of the Portuguese American community in the northeast

Hundreds will converge on United Nations headquarters on Sunday, September 19 at 2 p.m. to highlight the plight of the people of East Timor. Demonstrators will come from throughout the northeastern United States.

The protest takes place as the a multi-national force readies to deploy in East Timor and on the eve of the opening of the U.N. General Assembly. Demonstrators will call for the immediate withdrawal of all Indonesian military, police and militias from East Timor; an intensified humanitarian effort for all East Timorese; and international protection and repatriation of East Timorese forced into exile in West Timor and elsewhere in Indonesia.

"Even as the U.N. prepares to send a force into East Timor, the killing goes on," said John M. Miller of the ETAN. "Humanitarian aid to those facing starvation in the mountains must be stepped up --with or without Indonesia's permission. The massive turnout and overwhelming vote for independence on August 30 emphasized for all the world that Indonesia rule over East Timor has no legitimacy."

"We have a special concern for those forcibly deported from East Timor. Reports of conditions in the camps in West Timor are extremely disturbing," said Miller. "The military-controlled militias have been executing pro-independence Timorese and food and health care are scarce. These people must not be abandoned."

Please note: international observers who have recently returned from East Timor are available for interviews.
