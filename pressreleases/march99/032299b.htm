Rep. Tony Hall to Release Results of Nationwide Survey on Food Banks
 
WASHINGTON - March 22 - News Advisory:

U.S. Rep. Tony P. Hall (D-Ohio), will release the results of his second nationwide survey of food banks on Wednesday and introduce the Congressional Hunger Center's "Mickey Leland Hunger Fellows," who have spent the past six months working in food banks around the nation.

Hall's 1998 survey found that a growing number of Americans are turning to food banks for help. The 1999 survey finds that trend is continuing -- and that the staple foods that food banks have counted on in the past now provide them with a precarious foundation. The 1999 survey also finds that despite the strong U.S. economy, lines at soup kitchens and other charities continue to lengthen, and more of those waiting are working people and senior citizens.
------
Date: Wednesday, March 24
Time: 10:30 a.m.
Place: Room H-313, U.S. Capitol, Washington, D.C.

The Mickey Leland Fellows joining Hall bring their field experience to policy placements in Washington. The communities in  which the Fellows served include:

Wilmington, Del.

Jeffersonville, Ind.
Detroit, Mich.
St. Paul, Minn.
Grand Forks, N.D.
Incline Village, Nev.
Logan, Ohio
Lubbock, Texas
Seattle, Wash.
