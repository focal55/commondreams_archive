HOPE for Africa: The New Direction in Trade
 
WASHINGTON - March 4 - Join the Campaign for America's Future for a press conference on the HOPE for Africa Act, featuring:

Robert L. Borosage, Co-director, Campaign for America's Future

Congressman Jesse Jackson Jr. (D-IL)

Thea Lee, Assistant Director of Public Policy, AFL-CIO*

Jerome Levinson, Distinguished Lawyer in Residence, American University Law School

Lori Wallach, Director, Public Citizen's Global Trade Watch

Ronald Walters, Professor, Afro-American Studies Dept., University of Maryland-College Park

* invited

Questions and discussion to follow brief presentations.

Date: Tuesday, March 9, 1999

Time: 9:00 AM (Breakfast to be served)

Where: The Capitol, Room HC-9 (House side)

What: Press Conference for Media, Congressional Staff, invited leaders

Prominent leaders from Congress, citizen groups, and academia have come together to support the HOPE for Africa Act (H.R. 772). As an alternative to the African Growth and Opportunity Act, the HOPE for Africa Act outlines an equitable, sustainable, and responsible approach to promoting economic development in Africa.

The HOPE for Africa Act outlines a new responsible direction in trade policy, a stark contrast to the mistakes made in NAFTA. As economies across the globe struggle to deal with crippling recession, decisions in U.S. trade policy take on ever-greater importance. The debate on HOPE is the first round of the contentious fight on trade that will define this political year.

The Campaign for America's Future is a center of progressive research and education, founded by over 100 prominent Americans to make the economy work for working people. The Campaign is directed by Robert Borosage and Roger Hickey.
