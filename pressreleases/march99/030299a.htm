New Amnesty International Report Documents Abuse of Women Inmates In US Prisons
 
WASHINGTON - March 2- News Advisory:

WHAT: News conference

WHEN: Thursday, March 4 - 10 a.m.

WHERE: National Press Club
Murrow Room
14th and F Streets, N.W.
Washington, D.C.

Details:
Groping. Extorting sex for favors. Rape. Retaliation. Shackling during labor. Denial of medical care.

These human rights abuses, visited daily upon women in U.S. prisons and jails, are documented in a new Amnesty International report to be released next week here and by Amnesty chapters around the world.

Over the past decade, the population of women inmates has tripled, and in some jurisdictions quadrupled. Local, state and federal corrections officials have made few major adjustments to address the proliferation of women behind bars.

Consider these facts:

-- Approximately 70 percent of the guards in the U.S. women's federal prison system are male; conversely, in Canada 91 percent of the guards are female.
-- 13 states do not outlaw sexual contact between women inmates and their jailers; in states where there are laws, enforcement is lax.
-- There are no policies prohibiting male guards from performing body pat searches on women inmates.
-- Some prisons have no gynecological services.
-- Pregnant women who pose no risk of escape or violence are routinely shackled, even during labor.

To secure a copy of the embargoed report, an executive summary or to arrange an interview with an Amnesty spokesperson, victims or expert, contact Gwen McKinney or Brenda Jones at 202-833-9771.
