Statement On Death Of Justice Harry A. Blackmun; Nation Will Miss 'Great Advocate Of Church-State Separation And Freedom Of Conscience,' Says AU Leader
 
WASHINGTON - March 4 - The Rev. Barry W. Lynn, executive director of Americans United for Separation of Church and State, issued the following statement today concerning the death of Supreme Court Justice Harry A. Blackmun:

"We were saddened to learn of the death of Justice Harry Blackmun. Justice Blackmun was one of the Supreme Court's great advocates of church-state separation and freedom of conscience. His commitment to the cause grew steadily throughout his tenure on the court, and his writings on the subject reflected keen insight and common sense mixed with flashes of wit.

"Justice Blackmun was a strong supporter of individual religious freedom rights and wisely insisted that government should have no role in promoting or subsidizing faith. He opposed mandatory programs of religious worship in public schools, the display of sectarian symbols by government on public property and use of tax funds for private sectarian schools. At the same time, he zealously safeguarded the right of Americans to engage in the religion of their choosing without undue government interference or fear of state-sponsored discrimination.

"Justice Blackmun summed up his church-state philosophy quite eloquently in his concurring opinion in 1992's Lee v. Weisman, a case challenging school-sponsored religious worship during public school graduation ceremonies.

"'The mixing of government and religion can be a threat to free government, even if no one is forced to participate,' Justice Blackmun wrote. 'When the government puts its imprimatur on a particular religion, it conveys a message of exclusion to all those who do not adhere to the favored beliefs. A government cannot be premised on the belief that all persons are created equal when it asserts that God prefers some.'"

"Shortly after he retired from the court, Justice Blackmun attended an Americans United banquet where he accepted the Madison-Jefferson Award, AU's top award. At that time he said, 'Over our history there are always those who want to take this wall of separation and remove a brick here or there or damage it more than that. I think one has to be vigilant and constantly on the alert."

"Harry Blackmun was a towering figure in American law. He will be greatly missed."
