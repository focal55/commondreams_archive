Nation's Ten Most Endangered Rivers to be Announced April 12; Press Conference to be Held Around the Country
 
WASHINGTON - March 8 - News Advisory:

American Rivers will release its fourteenth annual report, America's Most Endangered Rivers of 1999, on Monday, April 12th at a series of press conferences which will take place around the country.

The report ranks the ten most endangered rivers in the country, which this year are facing the most serious and immediate environmental abuses, and provides a snapshot of the state of rivers today. It identifies and discusses the sources of the abuses that this year include:

-- Sprawl which chews up floodplains and river banks and pollutes
rivers with toxic runoff

-- Dams which block rivers and kill fish

-- Population growth and development in arid areas which depletes
water supplies

-- Mountaintop mining which dumps millions of tons of debris into
rivers and streams

The Most Endangered Rivers Report of 1999 also examines actions
that should be taken to mitigate these problems.
