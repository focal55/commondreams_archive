Handgun Control Fact Sheet on 'NRA: Getting It Wrong Again'
 
WASHINGTON - MAY 1 - The National Rifle Association (NRA) held its annual convention today, May 1, 1999, in Denver, Colo. Major speeches delivered by the NRA's executive vice president, Wayne LaPierre, and its president, Charlton Heston, contained notable errors or inconsistencies. Here are a few:

Child Safety Locks

What the NRA said today: "We support and encourage the distribution and development and use of safety locks, trigger locks, gun safes or any voluntary means necessary and appropriate to keep firearms away from or inoperable by those who shouldn't have them. We've always condemned anything less." The NRA's Executive Vice President, Wayne LaPierre, May 1, 1999.

What the NRA said after President Clinton proposed that manufacturers be required to equip every new handgun with a child safety lock: "The Clinton proposals mean no hunting, no self-defense and no safety....President Clinton's proposals for trigger locks on all firearms encourage all Americans to abandon armed, lawful self-defense in our homes, including federal law enforcement agents." The NRA's Washington lobbyist Tanya Metaska, March 5, 1997.

What the NRA said after the U.S. Senate defeated an amendment that would have required gun manufacturers to package a child safety lock with every handgun sold: "It was a great day for gun safety in America." The NRA's Washington lobbyist Tanya Metaksa, July 21, 1998.

Requiring Gun Owners to Safety Store Firearms

What the NRA (Wayne LaPierre) said today: "We've always supported holding adults responsible for willfully, recklessly allowing access to firearms." May 1, 1999.

What the NRA has opposed in the past: 1) various state child access prevention (CAP) bills that would hold adults responsible if they improperly store their weapons in a manner that a child could reasonably be expected to gain access to it; and 2) an amendment offered in Congress by Sen. Durbin on July 22, 1999 that would hold a gun owner criminally responsible (up to one year in jail) if the owner stores a loaded firearm in a place where the owner "knows, or reasonably should know, that a juvenile is capable of gaining access to the firearm without the permission of the parent," and a child "obtains access to the firearm and thereby causes death or bodily injury to the juvenile or to any other person."

Unsupervised Access to Firearms by Youth

What the NRA (Wayne LaPierre) said in Colorado today: "The National Rifle Association believes in no unsupervised youth access to guns, period."

What the NRA opposed in Colorado six years ago: A proposal by Gov. Roy Romer to ban juvenile possession of handguns by juveniles under the age of 18, except for certain supervised activities such as hunting, firearm instructions or target practice. After a fierce legislative fight, the NRA supported a compromise bill that was later signed by the Governor, but the NRA's lobbyist dismissed the legislation as a "modern-day, snake-oil remedy."

Felons and Guns

What the NRA supported today: prosecuting felons caught in illegal possession of a firearm.

What the NRA has also supported: federal legislation that: 1) would allow the Treasury Department to use taxpayer dollars to restore firearm privileges to convicted felons who petition to have their rights restored; and 2) would make it easy for convicted felons to redeem illegal guns hocked at pawn shops by doing away with the requirement that pawn shops conduct Brady background checks on those seeking to redeem firearms.

Hitler Quote on Gun Control

What the NRA (Charlton Heston) attributed to Adolph Hitler: "This year will go down in history! For the first time, a civilized nation has full gun registration! Our streets will be safer, our police more efficient, and the world will follow our lead into the future!"

What http://www.urbanlegends.com says about that quote: "....This quotation, however, effective it may be as propaganda, is a fraud....This quotation, often seen without any date or citation at all, suffers from several credibility problems, the most significant of which is that the date often given has no correlation with any legislative effort by the Nazis for gun registration, nor would there have been any need for the Nazis to pass such a law, since gun registration laws passed by the Weimar government (in part to address street violence between Nazis and Communists) were already in effect."


Handgun Control, chaired by Sarah Brady, is the nation's largest citizens' gun control lobbying organization. Based in Washington, D.C., HCI works to enact stronger federal, state and local gun control laws, but does not seek to ban handguns. Founded in 1974, HCI has more than 400,000 members nationwide and works with local groups around the country to enact and protect reasonable gun control laws. More information about HCI and its affiliated organization, the Center to Prevent Handgun Violence, can be found on our Web site at www.handguncontrol.org.
