CONTACT: Congressman Bob Clement
26 Congressional Democrats Urge President Clinton To Call For 72 Hour NATO Bombing Halt
 
WASHINGTON - May 27 - A coalition of Congressional Democrats organized by Tennessee Congressman Bob Clement and Illinois Congressman Rod Blagojevich, released a letter today calling on President Clinton to impose a 72-hour cease-fire in the NATO bombing campaign in Yugoslavia. The letter included signatures of more than two dozen Democratic House Members who have supported the NATO bombing campaign. Clement and Blagojevich made the announcement during a press conference at the U.S. Capitol.
 
"We are deeply concerned about the current situation in Kosovo and believe that it is time to give diplomacy a chance," said U.S. Rep. Clement. "The best way to demonstrate our commitment to resolving this conflict is a 72-hour cease-fire. It is time for the United States and NATO to work with the Russians and others to negotiate a peace settlement."

Blagojevich recently returned from the successful mission to free three U.S. soldiers in captivity, led by Rev. Jesse Jackson. Clement, a Colonel in the Tennessee Army National Guard, toured Bosnia last year, and saw first-hand the devastation and destruction in the war torn region.

"Make no mistake- we support the current mission in Yugoslavia. And we support NATO," said Clement. "But we also support every effort at reaching a diplomatic solution for ending the Kosovo conflict. It is time for the United States and NATO to show some real courage and humility and allow diplomatic negotiations a chance to succeed."

Members who have signed the letter include: U.S. Reps. James Barcia (MI); Rod Blagojevich (IL); Leonard Boswell (IA); Michael Capuano (MA); Julia Carson (IN); Bob Clement (TN); John Conyers (MI); Jerry Costello (IL); Elijah Cummings (MD); Danny Davis (IL); Anna Eshoo (CA); Sam Farr (CA); Sheila Jackson-Lee (TX); Carolyn Cheeks Kilpatrick (MI); Gerald Kleczka (WI); Jim McDermott (WA); Michael McNulty (NY); Carrie Meek (FL); Gregory Meeks (NY); George Miller (CA); Major Owens (NY); Donald Payne (NJ); David Phelps (IL); Bobby Rush (IL); Mark Udall (CO); and Melvin Watt (NC).
