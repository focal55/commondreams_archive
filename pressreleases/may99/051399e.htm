Anti-war Protesters Picket New York Dems
 
WASHINGTON - May 13 - Anti-war activists will protest at the local offices of two members of New York's congressional delegation Friday, May 14, to protest US bombing of Yugoslavia. The Tribeca office of liberal Rep. Jerrold Nadler will be the site of a noontime picket and street theater action; the Jackson Heights, Queens, office of first-term congressmember Joseph Crowley will see a 4:30 p.m. protest.

Rallying around the slogans, "No to NATO bombing! End the Expulsions!," the congressional actions are part of an ongoing protest against violence in the Balkans. Recently liberal members of Congress—often considered doves—have come under fire for their congressional votes supporting the war in Yugoslavia. In Burlington, Vermont, twenty-five protestors occupied the office of Bernie Sanders, Congress' only independent member. They were arrested after 45 minutes.

"At the cost of hundreds of lives and billions of dollars, Clinton's policy in the Balkans has failed," said Chris Ney, disarmament coordinator at the War Resisters League, one of the action organizers. "New York's Congressional delegation needs to hear the clear message that if they favor partisanship over principle, they will lose the support New Yorkers who want a peaceful solution to the conflict."

"Time and again in Latin America and the developing world, we have seen that the US military does not respect human rights," said Phil Josselyn of the Committee in Solidarity with the People of El Salvador, a sponsor of the action. "Calling this military intervention a humanitarian mission represents one of the most cynical claims to come from Washington in recent memory."

Both protests will take place Friday, May 14.
