Just 5 Days Until People In Your Community Take A Stand For Children
 
WASHINGTON - May 28 - News Advisory:

WHAT: Stand For Children Day events nationwide on and around June 1, will highlight the urgent need to improve children's education in and out of the classroom. Communities across America are organizing rallies, marches, after-school center and school renovations, literacy drives and more to raise awareness about children's education, child care, out-of-school time, and health needs and increase support for local, state, and national measures to support children. Stand For Children Day '99: "Ready to Learn, Ready to Succeed" will also join people worldwide via a "Cyber-Stand For Children" on the Stand For Children Web site (www.stand.org)

WHO: Local volunteer Stand For Children Day organizers across the country.

WHERE: 1,600 events in all 50 states, the District of Columbia and Guam. The five states with the largest number of events are:

California -- 209
South Carolina -- 94
Illinois -- 71
Pennsylvania -- 71

WHEN: Most events will be held on Stand For Children Day, June 1 or May 29-31/June 5-6. Contact the Stand For Children press office to find events in your area or visit our web site at www.stand.org.

WHY: Two out of three of eighth grade students in the United States are not reading at proficient levels; one out of three children are behind a year or more in school; and one in eight children never graduate from high school. More than 14 million children attend schools that are in major disrepair, and 7 million children attend schools with life-threatening safety-code violations.
