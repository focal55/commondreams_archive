Physicians Call for Congress to Act on Public's Cry for Gun Restrictions
 
WASHINGTON - May 7 -Citing recent poll data following the worst school shooting in this country’s history, Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR) today demanded that Congress heed their constituents’ cries for stronger gun laws to end the rampant gun violence that is killing America’s children. PSR calls for Congress to pass tough legislation when they take up gun control debate Monday.

"It is clearly a case of dereliction of duty if this Congress turns a deaf ear to the public’s cry for responsible gun legislation," says Robert K. Musil, Ph.D., Executive Director and CEO of PSR. "The American people realize that until we restrict the ready access to guns in this country the bloody tragedies of Littleton, Jonesboro, and Springfield will continue. And that blood will be on the hands of the members of Congress who fail to act."

Musil cited a study released yesterday by the Johns Hopkins University Center for Gun Policy and Research confirming that the majority of gun owners, as well as non-owners, strongly favor legislation that regulates firearms, imposes safety standards, and decreases availability of guns in this country. The study reveals that more than 60 percent of gun owners and greater than 75 percent of the general public support policy measures that impose mandatory registration of handguns, mandate health and safety standards for the gun industry, require background checks and waiting periods for all gun sales (including private sales through gun shows and on the Internet), and hold gun owners liable for negligence that allows guns into children’s hands.

According to PSR, the passage of the President’s Gun Enforcement and Accountability Act and several other bills awaiting Congressional action are a critical step toward preventing the 34,000 deaths, 100,000 injuries and 4 billion dollars in treatment costs attributed to gun violence each year. Without major changes in the ways that guns are designed, sold and distributed, physicians will continue to see the victims of gun violence in their emergency rooms and morgues.

PSR calls on Congress to take 3 immediate actions to reduce gun violence:

  • Regulate the firearms industry. Impose regulations on the design, manufacturing and distribution of firearms and firearm products. Pass Senate bill 534, Firearms Safety and Violence Prevention Act, and Senate bill 443, Gun Show Accountability Act.
  • Make guns safer and less accessible to children. Require the safe storage of guns, prohibit the carrying of concealed weapons, and require mandatory waiting periods for firearm purchases. Pass the President’s Gun Enforcement and Accountability Act.
  • Educate the community about the dangers of guns in the home. Create a national firearm injury surveillance system. Pass Senate bill 735, Children’s Gun Violence Prevention Act.

"Gun violence is an epidemic, but it is preventable," says Dr. Musil. "These are concrete steps that Congress must take to protect the public health and safety before there are more Littletons."
