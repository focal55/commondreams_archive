Chuck Brady Re-Elected As National Sierra Club President
 
SAN FRANCISCO - May 22 - The Sierra Club has re-elected Chuck McGrady for a second one-year term as President. McGrady's nomination was seconded by David Brower, former Sierra Club Executive Director. Brower had previously announced his interest in becoming President, but declined nomination in today's meeting. After his re-election, McGrady expressed his appreciation to the thousands of Sierra Club volunteers across the country whom he credits for making the Sierra Club the most powerful grassroots environmental organization in the country. McGrady lives in Flat Rock, North Carolina and directs Falling Creek Camp, a summer camp for boys in Tuxedo, North Carolina.

"I am grateful for this opportunity and I look forward to continuing to build the Sierra Club's campaigns to protect America's environment, for our families and for our future," said McGrady. "In my work as President, I will be thinking of my own young children and the hundreds of children that attend my summer camp each year. Protecting the environment means protecting their future."

In a Saturday meeting in San Francisco, the organization's Board of Directors elected McGrady to a second one-year term as President, the highest office in the volunteer-run organization. The President chairs the Board of Directors and is responsible for oversight of all activities of the organization. Before becoming President in 1998, McGrady held several national volunteer positions in the organization, as well as leading environmental campaigns for the Sierra Club in North Carolina and Georgia.

In his first year as President, McGrady led the organization's work on four major national campaigns: ending commercial logging on National Forests, protecting wildlands, stopping sprawl, and protecting America's water from factory farm pollution.

Other members of the Sierra Club's Executive Committee were also elected on Saturday. They are: Nick Aumen of Palm Beach County, Florida as Vice-President, Charlie Ogle of Eugene, Oregon as Secretary, Anne Ehrlich of Palo Alto, California as Treasurer, and Jennifer Ferenstein of Missoula, Montana as Fifth Officer.
