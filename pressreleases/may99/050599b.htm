New Century Alliance to Outline Criteria for Social Security Reform May 6 at NPC
 
WASHINGTON - May 5 - On Thursday, May 6 at 9:30 a.m. at the National Press Club, the the New Century Alliance for Social Security will hold a press conference at which policy experts and leaders of citizen groups will announce a joint statement of the criteria they say must guide any possible Social Security reform plan. They will also evaluate the new Republican proposals.

Two proposals -- "No pain" Archer-Shaw plan and Gregg-Kolbe "classic privatization" proposal -- will be examined by experts, citizen leaders from civil rights, women, labor and senior groups.

Details:
At least two incompatible Social Security privatization plans have been advanced by groups of (mostly) Republican lawmakers in recent weeks. Each claims to solve alleged problems facing Social Security by drastically changing it. In light of these plans, is reform possible this year?

Speakers include:
-- Roger Hickey, IAF & New Century Alliance
-- Gerald McEntee, American Federation of State County and Municipal Employees
-- Brent Wilkes, League of United Latin American Citizens
-- Paul Marchand (a), The Arc and Consortium for Citizens with Disabilities
-- Robert Greenstein, Center for Budget & Policy Priorities
-- Martha Burk, Center for the Advancement of Public Policy & Washington Feminist Fax Net
-- William Spriggs, National Urban League
-- Bill Dodds, National Council of Senior Citizens
-- Joy Simonson, Older Women's League

Date: Thursday, May 6

Time: 9:30 a.m. (Breakfast to be served)

Where: National Press Club, Lisagor Room

What: Press conference for media, citizen leaders & guests.

Brief presentations to be followed by questions and answer session ------ Editors note: RSVP to Erik Cole at 202-955-5665
