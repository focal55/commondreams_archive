Civilians Take Brunt Of Latest NATO Refusal To Negotiate End Of War; Peace Action Point To Growing War Against Civilians
 
WASHINGTON - May 3 - "It is outrageous that NATO is escalating the bombing campaign in the face of overtures for a negotiated settlement. That NATO's response to the release of the three captured service men is to start a wide-area B-52 bombing campaign shows a brazen disregard for the lives and property of civilians in Yugoslavia. We call on the leaders of NATO to declare an immediate cease-fire," said Gordon S. Clark executive director of Peace Action, the nation's largest grassroots peace organization

"This is now the fifth time that the U.S. has refused legitimate opportunities to halt the bombing and see if it could halt the killing on all sides," noted Clark. "We have rejected cease-fire proposals from the Greek government, the German government and the World Council of Churches, a cease-fire offered by Milosevic himself, and now this. Why is it not possible for NATO to at least attempt a 24 hour cease-fire to see how Milosevic responds?"

The group noted the rapidly increasing number of civilian deaths caused by the NATO bombing. Over the weekend a NATO missile destroyed a bus, killing at least 35 civilians. Other errant missiles have also destroyed civilian neighborhoods and killed civilians in the past week.

In addition, the destruction of Yugoslavia's oil refineries has flooded the Danube River with oil, clogging the cooling system of Bulgaria's Kozloduy nuclear power plant downstream and threatening a Chernobyl-like accident. Recent reports of NATO's bombing of electric plants suggest that all of Belgrade is now without power - including homes, hospitals, factories and other facilities necessary for existence.

"This ill-advised NATO bombing campaign not only precipitated a major refugee crisis, destabilizing the region, but has quickly snowballed into an all-out war against a civilian population and a threat to countries around it. This bombing violates every known convention of international law and must be stopped now, using any opportunity available" added Mr. Clark.

Peace Action is joining other major national groups such as the American Friends Service Committee, Fellowship of Reconciliation and Pax Christi to mobilize citizens demanding an end to the bombing. Multiple actions are being planned for the month of May and will culminate with a major rally in Washington, DC on June 5.

Peace Action is the nation's largest grassroots peace and disarmament organization with 50,000 members nationwide. Since 1957, Peace Action, formerly Sane/Freeze, has organized for the abolition of nuclear weapons, an end to the international arms trade and a redirection of U.S. tax dollars from the Pentagon budget to community needs.
