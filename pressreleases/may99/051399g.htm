Double Pro-choice Victory For Military Women Stationed Overseas
 
WASHINGTON - May 13 - The following was released today by NARAL:

Women in the military stationed overseas will have greater access to safer abortion for the first time in five years if two amendments passed by the House Armed Services Subcommittee on Military Personnel today become law. Until today's vote, anti-choice language in the annual Defense Department Reauthorization Act had prevented military servicewomen and dependents from obtaining abortions at overseas military facilities, even if they paid for the procedure with their own funds. The only exception to the ban had been for abortions necessary to save the life of the woman, leaving military servicewomen who were victims of rape and incest to fend for themselves.

Today, the Subcommittee repealed the ban on privately-funded abortion services at overseas U.S. military hospitals and added rape and incest exceptions to the ban on the use of public Department of Defense funds for abortions for military women and their dependents stationed overseas. This is the first year pro-choice lawmakers have been able to muster the votes necessary to repeal the discriminatory ban and the first year that an exception for victims of rape and incest has been added to the ban.

"Pro-choice groups and lawmakers have struggled for five years to give military women stationed overseas the same access to quality reproductive health care as women living in the United States," said NARAL president Kate Michelman. "The Subcommittee's actions today represent an important step in that direction and will serve to protect the reproductive health of the women who risk their lives to protect this country.

"Being a member of this nation's military, or a dependent of a member of the military, should not mean you are relegated to second-class citizenship when seeking treatment as a victim of rape or incest," Michelman continued. "Military women and their dependents have a right to expect access to the same range of services whether stationed overseas or here in the U.S."

The amendment adding the rape and incest exceptions, introduced by Steven Kuykendall (R-Calif.), passed 11-7. The amendment repealing the ban on privately-funded abortions was introduced by Loretta Sanchez (D-Calif.) and passed on a vote of 10-8. The Defense Department Reauthorization Act, with the two amendments attached, now goes to the full Armed Services Committee for consideration.

The Sanchez amendment does not require the Department of Defense to pay for abortions, but rather repeals the current ban on privately-funded abortions at U.S. military facilities overseas written into permanent code by the 104th Congress in 1996. The Kuykendall Amendment would require the Defense Department to pay for abortions only in cases of rape, incest, and life endangerment in a manner similar to the Hyde Amendment here in the U.S. The Hyde Amendment bans the use of public Medicaid funds for abortions for low-income women, but includes exceptions for victims of rape, incest, and life endangerment.
