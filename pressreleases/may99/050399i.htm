ARCO Shareholders Support Arctic Refuge, 4.7% Vote in Favor of Canceling Drilling Plans
 
WASHINGTON - May 3 - 4.7% percent of ARCO shareholders voted in favor of a resolution to protect the Coastal Plain of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge from oil drilling at the company’s annual meeting today in Los Angeles, California. The percentage of votes will allow shareholder proponents of the resolution to re-introduce the proposal at next year's annual meeting.

The final vote was 4.7 percent in support of the resolution,7.5% percent abstained, and 87.7% percent in opposition.

The resolution was filed by the Green Century Equity Fund, United States Trust Company of Boston, and the Mercy Consolidated Asset Management Program. It was filed in concert with the PIRGs’ Arctic Wilderness Campaign.

"Today ARCO shareholders joined the growing chorus of Arctic Refuge supporters," said Athan Manuel, Campaign Director of the PIRGs’ Arctic Wilderness Campaign. "Five percent of the owners of the second-largest oil company operating in Alaska just called on ARCO to save the Arctic Refuge from drilling."

The resolution calls on ARCO to unconditionally cancel any future plans for oil drilling in the coastal plain, 1002 area, of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge and immediately stop the expenditure of any corporate funds targeted to achieve this objective."

“We call on ARCO to acknowledge the growing shareholder support for preserving one of the most pristine and unique ecosystems in the nation," said Tracy Graham of the Green Century Capital Management. "We look forward to negotiating with ARCO on canceling their drilling plans for the Arctic Refuge."

Last week 10 percent of Chevron shareholders voted in favor of a similar resolution. ARCO and Chevron are two of the companies, along with BP Amoco and Exxon, that have expressed the most interest in drilling in the Refuge.

Support for the Arctic continues to grow. In addition to a strong showing from ARCO and Chevron shareholders, a record number of Senators support protecting the Refuge. Last week, 22 Senators cosponsored Senator Roth’s Arctic Wilderness bill, S. 867, to permanently protect the coastal plain of the Refuge.
