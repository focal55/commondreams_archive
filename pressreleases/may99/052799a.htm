Activists Gather for 2nd Human Rights Action Camp May 28-June 2
 
WASHINGTON - May 27 - News Advisory:

More than 100 activists from international and domestic human rights and environmental groups are gathering this week for a unique "direct action" training camp -- one designed to sharpen their skills to accomplish constructive and non-violent social change in their communities and around the world.

The 2nd Human Rights Action Camp will offer six days of "direct action" workshops and training from May 28-June 2 at Glen Ora Farm in Middleburg, Va. (about an hour outside of Washington). International participants are expected from such human rights "hotspots" as Kenya, Nicaragua, Hungary, Mexico, China, Bangladesh and Nigeria. Activists will live and work together in a tent city, taking part in workshops on Human Rights and Environmental Law, Strategic Campaigning and the Impacts of Economic Globalization. Technical training sessions include nonviolence, urban climbing, protests and blockades, Internet activism, and political theater.

--
NOTE: The Human Rights Action Camp offers compelling visuals and powerful individual stories. Members of the media are welcome to attend the Human Rights Action Camp and to interview Camp participants.

Designated spokespeople for the Camp and media greeters will be available on-site. Media credentials are required to cover this event. Credentials and a full schedule of activities are available from any of the above contacts.

EVENT: 2nd Human Rights Action Camp

DATE: Friday, May 28-Wednesday, June 2

PLACE:
Glen Ora Farm (home of Elaine Broadhead, host of the 1999 Human Rights Action Camp)
5297 Hulberts Lane
Middleburg, Va.
(one hour outside of Washington, D.C.)

--
DIRECTIONS to Glen Ora Farm from Washington D.C.:

Take Route 66 West from D.C. (about 20 minutes without traffic).
Follow signs for Route 50 West and bear right at the exit.

Take Route 50 West approximately 15 miles to Middleburg, Va. (Middleburg is a small town with only one traffic light). Turn left at the first light onto Madison Road. Proceed for approximately one mile to the crossroads (Parsons Road will be to the left and Hulberts Lane will be to the right).

Turn right onto Hulberts Lane, which is a dirt road. Proceed for one-half mile to the first driveway on the left. Look for two stone gateposts and a sign for Glen Ora Farm. Drive in and park as directed.
