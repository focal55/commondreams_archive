Statement by Planned Parenthood President on Kopp Indictment
 
WASHINGTON - May 6 - Following is a statement today of Gloria Feldt, president, Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

The fact that James Kopp remains at large is a reminder of how far we as a nation still have to go in stemming the tide of violence against abortion providers. Threats continue to come in daily. Attacks on facilities that provide family planning or abortion services are weekly events. How many more deaths will it take to make stopping anti-choice violence the priority it should be for law enforcement at every level?

In April alone, arsonists caused damage at three different women's health centers -- two in Wisconsin and one in New Mexico. A suspect, recently released from prison after serving time for a prior clinic arson, has been arrested in the New Mexico attack. No arrests have been made in the Wisconsin arsons.

In March, a bomb designed to kill and maim damaged a woman's health center in Asheville, N.C. It was mere chance that prevented deaths, injuries and tremendous destruction. No one has been named as a suspect. That bombing received far less attention in the press than Operation Rescue's sorry traveling circus, which drew only a handful of participants, but a gaggle of journalists, much to the irritation of most Buffalo residents.

Operation Rescue, ever playing to the cameras, announced its Buffalo protest within days of Dr. Slepian's murder. The group refused to acknowledge any moral responsibility for such an outrageous act, and in fact blames the slaying on Dr. Slepian himself.

Anti-choice extremists across the country who have been fined for illegal activities also continue to dodge responsibility for their actions by declaring bankruptcy. Congress has a chance this week to end the practice of using bankruptcy to avoid paying judgments under the FACE act. Planned Parenthood will deliver 200,000 petitions to Congress tomorrow demanding action against anti-choice violence. But we expect anti-choice legislators otherwise known for their "law and order" stances to invoke their usual double standard and go soft on anti-choice criminals.

We hope that the announcement of formal charges against James Kopp will lead to increased vigilance and bring us one day closer to apprehending Dr. Slepian's assassin. With Eric Rudolph still at large, there are now two men wanted in connection with anti-choice murders who remain on the loose. Law enforcement officials across the country must support the work of the federal task force in doing everything possible to eradicate the scourge of domestic terrorism. The thousands of people who go to work every day dedicated to serving the reproductive health needs of America's women deserve no less. Women must be able to exercise their constitutionally protected right to choose when and if to become a parent in an environment of love, support and safety.
