Wellstone Amendment to GOP Juvenile Crime Bill; Seeks to Address Disproportionate Minority Confinement
 
WASHINGTON - May 18 - Senator Paul Wellstone, a longtime advocate for children, introduced an amendment today to the GOP Juvenile Crime Bill (S. 254) which would maintain and strengthen the current federal law which requires that states identify and address the problem of disproportionate confinement of minority children (DMC) in America's jails. Together with Senator Edward Kennedy, Wellstone joined a broad coalition of civil rights' and children's advocates at a Washington, D.C. media conference to urge support for passage of the amendment.

"This Republican bill undermines efforts now underway to deal with the disproportionate confinement of minority youth in our nation's jails. In practically every state, minority children are over-represented at every stage of the juvenile justice system, particularly in secure confinement. Further, they receive unequal treatment within the system. The authors of S.254 are resistant to mentioning `race' in federal legislation, and have gutted the current requirements on states to address this issue. Efforts to remedy the disparate treatment of minority youth will be seriously undermined as a consequence. I fear that our juvenile justice system will fail to become one which treats every youth fairly and equitably, regardless of race and ethnicity. We can do better than this as a nation, and we must," Wellstone said.

Current law directs states to identify the extent to which disproportionate minority confinement exists, to assess the reason that it exists, and to develop intervention strategies to address the causes for DMC. As a result, 40 states to date are implementing or developing promising intervention plans to address this problem. The Republican Juvenile Crime bill, S. 254, would significantly weaken this requirement, greatly reducing the likelihood that states will take steps to identify and correct the problem. The current law does not require and has never resulted in the release of juveniles, and does not require numerical quotas for arrest or release of any youth from custody based on race.

The Wellstone/Kennedy Amendment would uphold the spirit of current law, requiring states to take steps to reduce the disproportionate number of children of color who come into contact with the juvenile justice system.

The Wellstone/Kennedy Amendment is supported by the Children's Defense Fund, NAACP, ACLU, Child Welfare League of America, Youth Law Center, National Mental Health Association, and many other groups.
