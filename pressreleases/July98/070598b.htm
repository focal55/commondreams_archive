 Statement by American Federation of Teachers President on National Education Association Vote on Principles of Unity
NEW ORLEANS - July 5 - The following is a statement by American Federation of Teachers (AFT) President Sandra Feldman on the National Education Association (NEA) vote on Principles of Unity:

"Although today's vote was obviously disappointing, there is no doubt that something very positive for public education developed in our four years of negotiations. The two organizations learned that, despite their different histories and cultures, they also have much in common. In fact, the NEA and AFT have even begun working on some of these common problems together, among them: school infrastructure, school safety and discipline and teacher quality.

Certainly, both organizations share the goals of improving education for America's 50 million students, fighting for the dignity and well-being of the nation's educators, and of course, putting an end to vouchers and other privatization attempts that can't possibly succeed for the majority of our students.

I wish my colleague, President Bob Chase, and his members the best of luck, as they, like us, continue these crucial battles."
