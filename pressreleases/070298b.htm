 Save the Children Urges Global Response to Emergency in Sudan; More Than Two Million at Immediate Risk
WESTPORT, Conn. - July 2 - The Save the Children Alliance today warned that the lives of more than two million people in Sudan are in dire need of food and other emergency assistance.

In the face of worsening conditions and widening famine zones, the Alliance calls upon the international community to come to the aid of vulnerable children and families in Sudan.

Several years of drought, the threat of yet another crop failure this autumn, and the effects of decades of civil war have left much of the population without food, shelter, and other resources vital to survival. In many areas of the country, deaths from starvation are increasing at alarming rates.

The Save the Children Alliance is an international organization of 25 independent members that work in more than 100 countries worldwide and have been in Sudan since 1950. Its work in Sudan combines the efforts of several member organizations, including the U.S., U.K., Sweden, Norway, and Denmark.

Dr. Bob Arnot, a trustee for Save the Children (U.S.) and a medical correspondent for NBC News, has just returned from Bahr-el-Ghazal, where the situation is most critical. ``In civil wars like this, it is always the children who are sacrificed,'' he states. ``As a doctor, there is nothing worse than to stand and watch these children die. I come from a world with advanced medicine and technology, but I felt powerless to save these lives.'' Dr. Arnot will bring the Sudan crisis to the attention of the American people through newscasts and documentaries throughout the coming week.

Conditions in the south are most acute where armed conflict is the worst, forcing large numbers of people to flee their homes. Some migrate from one emergency food drop to another. Others move northward, where there is less fighting, but just as few resources and services. Far from home and unable to provide for themselves, many now are entirely dependent upon external support for their survival. More and more unaccompanied children are arriving at feeding centers, often malnourished and ill. The Alliance reports that more than half of children in the town of Wau, located in Bahr-el-Ghazal, are extremely malnourished and that nearly a quarter of these children die as a result of their condition.

Save the Children Alliance members are providing both emergency aid to people affected by constant civil unrest as well as longer-term assistance to help families rebuild their communities and better provide for themselves. The Alliance is responding to the current emergency by meeting the immediate needs of children and families whose lives are in danger; helping communities rebuild and prepare for the future; and promoting a cease-fire and permanent peace.

``The world community has yet to realize the extent of the suffering and devastation currently taking place in Sudan,'' said Charles MacCormack, president of Save the Children (U.S.). ``But the reality is that the lives of millions of children are now at stake. We simply must do everything within our power to save the children.''

Meeting immediate needs of children and families in Sudan

Save the Children Alliance members currently work in areas in the south, Bahr-el-Ghazal and Upper Nile, as well as the Transitional Zone within the States of North and South Kordofan, located in south-central Sudan. The Alliance provides emergency aid to children and families in need through:

Emergency food
High energy biscuits
Supplemental feeding of malnourished children
Survival kits: pots, pans, mosquito nets, blankets, fishing equipment, and
cloth.

Helping communities rebuild

In addition to meeting the basic needs of children in crisis, the Save the Children Alliance helps communities develop ways to mitigate the effects of continuing and future crises and to promote a healthy environment for their children. Because it is important to provide families with the tools they need to rebuild their lives, members provide the following community services:

Family reunification
Distribution of seeds and tools for resettlement
Replacement of livestock lost in droughts and war
Support for large-scale immunization campaigns
Training of community health workers

A Call to Action: Toward a permanent peace

Until there is peace in Sudan, there will be no semblance of stability, security, hope, or future for children and families. The Save the Children Alliance urges that:

-- All parties in Sudan agree to a cease-fire and negotiate a lasting, peaceful settlement.
-- All parties respect international law regarding the rights of children to receive humanitarian assistance.
-- The international community ensures that humanitarian efforts are fully resourced and supported.
-- The U.S. government support all regional initiatives to bring about a lasting peace.
-- All humanitarian organizations share resources and more effectively coordinate efforts among famine zones and war-affected regions both in the north and south.


Save the Children (U.S.) has set up the Sudan Emergency Appeal, a 24-hour telephone line to accept contributions towards the collective effort of Save the Children throughout Sudan. The number is 1-800-403-0505.
