Congresswoman McKinney and Dikembe Mutombo Deliver Hope to People of Democratic Republic of Congo: "Time is now to stop exchange of gunshots and start exchanging of medical supplies"
 
DECATUR, GA - August 26 - Congresswoman Cynthia McKinney (D-GA) and Atlanta Hawks center Dikembe Mutombo travel to the Democratic Republic of Congo today on a medical fact finding mission to this war-torn country. Mr. Mutombo, creator of the Dikembe Mutombo Foundation, has the goal of improving the health, education and quality of life in the Democratic Republic of Congo. The major project for the Foundation is the construction of a 300 bed general hospital in the capitol city of Kinshasa.

While in the Democratic Republic of Congo, Congresswoman McKinney will also assess the status of the peace process for the US House International Relations Committee. This country, located in central Africa, has been tangled in a war with the neighboring countries of Uganda and Rwanda since August 1998. The Columbia Broadcast System (CBS) is sending a television crew from its feature news program "60 Minutes" to accompany Congresswoman McKinney.

While in Kinshasa, Rep. McKinney and Mr. Mutombo will witness the distribution of medical and pharmaceutical supplies donated from the United States. Among the highlights of the fact finding mission will be a visit to local hospitals administering polio vaccines to children. "The Democratic Republic of the Congo has 10 million children who need to be vaccinated against this disease. Our goal with this visit is to replace the killing gun shots with life saving polio shots," stated Rep. McKinney.
