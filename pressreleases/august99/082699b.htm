Greenpeace Denounces Food Industry Double Standard on Gene-Altered Ingredients
 
WASHINGTON - August 26 - Greenpeace is calling on Nestlé, McDonalds, and Pillsbury to end their double standards in selling genetically engineered foods to European consumers and U.S. consumers. These food companies keep the use of genetically engineered ingredients secret from U.S. consumers while making pledges to European consumers to avoid those ingredients.

Test results just released by the largest consumer group in the U.S., Consumers Union (C.U.), revealed the presence of genetically engineered ingredients in foods sold in the country by Nestlé, McDonalds and Pillsbury, among others. In the past few months Greenpeace has requested information from these and over 40 other food companies regarding their policies on using genetically engineered ingredients. To date, none of these companies have responded.

The Nestlé product that tested positive was the company’s Carnation Alsoy infant formula. In response to similar testing by Greenpeace, Gerber, the leading baby food maker in the U.S., announced last month that it would no longer use genetically engineered ingredients in its products. C.U. also found evidence of genetically engineered ingredients in McDonalds McVeggie Burger and Pillsbury’s Old El Paso brand corn taco shells.

“In Europe food companies assure their customers that they will not be guinea pigs,” said Greenpeace Genetic Engineering Issues Specialist Charles Margulis. “Yet these same companies continue to keep Americans in the dark about this genetic experiment in our food. They simply cannot justify this double standard when doctors and scientists are warning that these ‘secret ingredients’ may not be safe.”

Numerous opinion polls have shown that Americans want to avoid genetically engineered foods. A Time magazine poll early this year found that over 80 percent of readers favored labeling of GE foods, and 58 percent said they would avoid GE foods if it were labeled.

In a form letter, Nestlé acknowledges that it does “use some genetically grown ingredients in some products.” However, none of its product labelling contains this acknowledgment. Nestlé also states “In countries where public opinion rejects ingredients derived from genetically modified crops…Nestlé respects the consumer’s preference and will provide customers, in as far as technically possible, with products that do not contain these ingredients.” McDonalds in the UK released a statement in June 1999, stating, “We can confirm that we do not use genetically modified products…, or ingredients containing GM [genetically modified] material…. We are working with our suppliers to secure sustainable, non-GM sources.” This same promise has not been made to U.S. consumers.
