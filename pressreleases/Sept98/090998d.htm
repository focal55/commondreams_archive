Campaign Exposing Billions in Congressional Military Pork To Be Unveiled at News Conference Thursday
WASHINGTON - September 9 - News Advisory:

-- Military and Business Leaders, Champion Hog Caller to Denounce End-of-Session Attempt at New Weapons Spending

-- Full-Page New York Times Ad, Grassroots Efforts Will Target Military Pork for Congressional Leadership -- New Budget-Buster Threatens Social Security Fix
------
WHAT: Corporate CEOs join former top military leaders to protest an end-of-session attempt in Congress to add billions of dollars in military spending the Pentagon didn't ask for and doesn't need.

WHEN: 11 a.m., Thursday, Sept. 10.

WHERE: U.S. Capitol, House Triangle.

WHO: -- Rear Adm. Eugene Carroll, U.S. Navy (Ret.).
-- Lawrence Korb, former Assistant Secretary of Defense under President Ronald Reagan (written statement).
-- Ben Cohen, co-founder, Ben & Jerry's Ice Cream, and other business leaders.
-- Virginia Moyer, five-time Virginia State Champion Hog Caller (Women's Division).
VISUALS:
-- Champion Hog Caller rings in the pork barreling season.
-- Full-page New York Times advertisement depicting the Capitol as the "Congress Cafe -- Washington's Finest Pork BBQ."

Sponsored by Business Leaders for Sensible Priorities.
