Sierra Club Experts To Comment On President Clinton's Plan To Invest In America's Wildlands
 
SAN FRANCISCO - January 11 - In light of President Clinton's anticipated announcement of initiatives to preserve and protect America's natural heritage, Sierra Club experts will conduct a press conference via telephone to comment on the plan and provide background information to reporters covering the announcements.

WHAT: Tomorrow, President Clinton is expected to announce a major investment in America's special wild places, recreation areas, and wildlife habitat.

WHO: Debbie Sease, Sierra Club Legislative Director
Melanie Griffin, Director Sierra Club Public Lands Program

WHEN: 1:30 PM EST Tuesday, January 12, 1999

HOW: To join the call, just dial 1-800-266-7218. You will be asked for the password #50970.

Since John Muir founded the Sierra Club in 1892, the organization has been fighting to protect and restore undeveloped and vulnerable lands across America. From the grassroots to the halls of Congress, we are working to secure permanent protections for sensitive areas such as the Alaska's coastal plains, California's desert, Florida's Everglades, New England's forests, the sensitive wildlife habitat of the Northern Rockies and millions of acres of wildlands from coast to coast.

To learn more about the Sierra Club is working to protect America's natural wildland heritage, please visit our web site at http://www.sierraclub.org/
