Sierra Club Welcomes Plans To Limit Sprawl; Conservation Groups Call Gore Initiatives "Good First Step"
 
SAN FRANCISCO - January 11 - The Sierra Club today applauded Vice President Al Gore's announcement of a new Administration plan aimed at reducing suburban sprawl.

"The Gore initiatives to encourage the development of urban parks and protect farmlands are a good first step toward addressing suburban sprawl, which is now the fastest growing threat to our environment," explained Sierra Club Executive Director Carl Pope.

Pope explained that the initiatives proposed by the Vice President potentially reverse a fifty year trend of federal policies and programs -- including road building, flood control and sewer subsidies -- that helped create sprawl.

Between 1970 and 1990, more than 19 million acres of rural land were developed. Every year, America loses 400,000 acres a year to development. And the rate of development is increasing along with the traffic congestion and air and water pollution it creates.

"We believe controlling sprawl is essential to preserving our quality of life and communities, and protecting our environment. While today's plan moves in the right direction, we would encourage the Administration to combine these kinds of smart investments with the elimination of traditional policies that encourage sprawl. For example -- withholding funding for projects that promote sprawl, which is what the EPA did last week when they put a stop to the sprawling legacy highway that would have destroyed the wetlands outside of Salt Lake City. The Administration has the power to both encourage smart growth policies and programs and to pull the plug on projects that promote sprawl. They must do both to curb sprawl," Pope concluded.
