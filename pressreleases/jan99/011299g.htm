President's Open Space Initiative A Step Forward, But Permanent Fund Needed
 
WASHINGTON - January 12 - Today, President Clinton announced an increase in spending to preserve open spaces by expanding federal land purchases and giving millions of dollars to states for urban parks and land conservation. The package includes $1 billion to protect green and open spaces, including $440 million to acquire open land such as wilderness, forests, deserts, and coastlines, and nearly $600 million for states and communities to use to protect parks, farms, urban and suburban lands and recreation areas, and other wild areas. The specifics of the proposal will be unveiled as part of the President’s Budget on February 1st.

"This is an encouraging first step by the Administration to protect our wilderness and wildlife and preserve our open spaces. However, if we are to achieve permanent protection for wildlife and open spaces, the Administration needs to find a permanent funding source," said Gene Karpinski, Executive Director, U.S. PIRG.

This initiative comes on the heels of legislative proposals pending in the House and Senate to dedicate about half of the yearly Outer Continental Shelf (OCS) receipts from oil and gas drilling to LWCF, coastal impact act, and state game programs.

"The current OCS proposals in the Congress are seriously flawed, because they include incentives encouraging offshore oil drilling," said Ms. Shultz. "We hope that with this land protection initiative, the

Administration will oppose the current legislative proposals and work to promote a proposal that will fund important land acquisition and wildlife protections that will not encourage destructive off shore oil drilling," stated Ms. Shultz.

###

U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for the state PIRGs. PIRGs are non-partisan, non-profit environmental and consumer watchdog organizations that are active around the country.
