Labor Movement to Hold National MLK Weekend Celebration in Birmingham; Union Members to Serve Local Community to Honor Dr. King
 
WASHINGTON - January 12 - On Thursday, January 14 through Monday, January 18, more than 600 union members and leaders from around the nation will gather in Birmingham -- a cradle of the civil rights movement -- to honor Dr. Martin Luther King with a weekend of activities including a march, community service, educational seminars, song and celebration.

On Saturday, January 16th hundreds of union members will fan out across Birmingham to feed the homeless, paint community centers, build homes for the needy and serve at a church. They will reconvene that evening for a reception and tour of the Civil Rights Institute.

AFL-CIO President John Sweeney will speak at the worship service on Sunday, January 17th at Sixteenth Street Baptist Church. Thousands will gather for a march on Monday, January 18th starting at City Hall which will be followed by a rally inside Sixteenth Street Baptist Church. Throughout the weekend, union members will educate one another in seminars on such topics as workers' right to organize and the link between religion and labor.

Union members chose Birmingham as the host city for their celebrations because the city holds a prominent place in the history of civil rights. Union members will highlight Dr. King's efforts to ensure that all American workers have the right to organize a union to improve their lives and to be treated with dignity and respect in the workplace. Last year's celebrations were in Memphis.

Many of the nation's top labor leaders will be in attendance including: AFL-CIO President John Sweeney, AFL-CIO Executive Vice President Linda Chavez-Thompson, George Becker, President United Steelworkers of America (USWA), Ed Fire, President International Union of Electrical Workers (IUE), James Hoffa, President International Brotherhood of Teamsters (IBT), Mike Monroe, President of the Painters Union (IBPAT), Cecil Roberts, President of the Mineworkers (UMWA), Boyd Young, President of the Paperworkers Union (UPIU), Clayola Brown, Vice President, UNITE, Bill Lucy, Secretary-Treasurer, AFSCME, and Gloria Johnson, President of the Coalition of Labor Union Women (CLUW).
