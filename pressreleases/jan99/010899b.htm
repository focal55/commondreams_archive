'Follow the People,' Ad Urges Senate; 'State of the Union' Campaign Launched
 
WASHINGTON - January 8 - A full-page ad in today's Washington Post calls on the Senate to listen to the people and stop the impeachment process.

Signing the ad were more than 50 national leaders, actors, musicians, authors and other nationally known figures, including Detroit Mayor Dennis Archer, actress Glenn Close, historian Henry Louis Gates, Jr., authors Norman Mailer, Toni Morrison and Kurt Vonnegut, musician Carly Simon, former Texas Gov. Ann Richards and Elie Wiesel, recipient of the 1986 Nobel Prize for Peace.

The ad announces a day of action against impeachment for Jan. 19 - the day President Clinton is scheduled to deliver the annual State of the Union message to Congress. PFAW is mobilizing activists across the country to hold rallies, forums, teach-ins and house parties across the United States. The theme of the day's events will be, "The State of the Union: It's Up to Us."

"The people are clamoring for this partisan witch hunt to end," said PFAW President Carole Shields. "It's time for Republicans in the Senate to rise above the rancor. Through our national mobilization, we will help make sure the people's voices are heard in the halls of government."

Rallies are currently planned for Philadelphia, Burlington, Chicago, Seattle, Columbus, Ohio, Minneapolis, Detroit, Los Angeles, and Atlanta.

PFAW also announced it has launched a new web page, www.stateoftheunion.org and a new toll-free phone number, 1-877-866-6836. Both the web site and the toll-free number will help local activists across the country mobilize and plan rallies in their communities.
