Sierra Club Air Campaign Targets Five House Members With Radio Ads On Anti-Environmental Budget Vote
 
WASHINGTON - April 5 - The Sierra Club today launched radio ads to inform residents of five congressional districts that their Representatives voted for a federal budget proposal that would force devastating cuts in programs that protect our air, water, land, parks and wild places. Targeted at Reps. Jim Kolbe (AZ-5), Heather Wilson (NM-1), Steve Chabot (OH-1), Don Sherwood (PA-10), and Virgil Goode (VA-5), the ads are part of Sierra Club's on-going effort to hold Members of Congress accountable when they vote to harm the environment.

"These five Members of Congress are batting 0-for-3 on the environment this year," said Sierra Club Executive Director Carl Pope. "When Members of Congress vote to harm the environment, Sierra Club will hold their feet to the fire. On the flip side, if they consistently vote to protect clean air and clean water, we'll praise them and make sure their constituents know they are doing the right thing."

The radio ads tell listeners that their local Congressperson voted for devastating cuts to environmental programs when he or she supported the budget proposal that passed the House of Representatives on March 25. (See attached script.) They also voted against an amendment that would have restored the cuts and provided resources for the Lands Legacy and Livable Communities initiatives. In addition, the ads urge listeners to call their Representative to voice their displeasure with the vote and tell the Representative to protect the environment on the next budget vote. Each of these five Members also cast anti-environmental votes on February 11 by opposing amendments from Henry Waxman (D-CA) and Sherwood Boehlert (R-NY) to the Mandates Information Act (H.R. 350).

"The people who live in these places want protections that guarantee the water they drink and the air they breathe will be clean and safe," Pope said. "These five Representatives voted to gut funding for environmental protections, and with Members home for Congress' spring recess, their constituents will have a chance to speak up to demand better performance on environmental issues in the future."

On March 25, the House of Representatives passed a budget that would slash funding for environmental programs by an estimated 12 percent in Fiscal Year 2000 and 28 percent in Fiscal Year 2004. These extreme cuts would undo years of progress made toward providing a clean and safe environment for all Americans.

After the votes on the Waxman and Boehlert amendments, Sierra Club aired radio ads targeting Reps. Kolbe, John Sununu (NH-1) and Wilson, and published newspaper ads in the districts of Reps. James Rogan (CA-27) and Gary Condit (CA-18).
