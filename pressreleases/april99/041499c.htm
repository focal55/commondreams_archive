U.S.-NATO Bombing Campaign Is Creeping Along: 'We've Seen Target Creep, Mission Creep, And Policy Creep'
 
WASHINGTON - April 14 - Congresswoman Cynthia McKinney (D-GA-4th), Member of the International Relations and Armed Services Committees, says the U.S. policy on Kosovo is one creeping policy after another.

First, we saw target creep: at the beginning of the bombing campaign, the U.S. targeted sights in and around Kosovo, but has incrementally decided to expand the bombing campaign into Belgrade and beyond.

Second, there is mission creep, because we started the bombing campaign to force Milosevic to sign the Rambouillet peace proposal, and now we seem to have jettisoned Rambouillet and I hear talk about toppling Milosevic. Finally, policy creep has seen the objective for Kosovo move form autonomy to talk of independence.

"U.S. policy has been a moving target," says Congresswoman McKinney. "Plan A for this Administration seems to rely on the use of force rather than the use of diplomacy to solve the world's hot spot problems."

"Reports show that the Serbs have expanded the war beyond the Yugoslavia borders, Is the U.S. going to follow them with war planes? Milosovic's unwarranted, unconscionable, and unjust campaign of violence against the people of Kosovo must be stopped, but not by turning the Balkans into Europe's filling fields. The U.S. is well down the slippery slope toward war," concludes Congresswoman McKinney.
