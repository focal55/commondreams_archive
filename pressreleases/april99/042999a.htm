Teenager Challenges Philip Morris CEO Geoffrey Bible INFACT's Kraft Boycotters Mobilize Nationwide
 
RICHMOND, VA - April 29 - Corporate "watchdogs" in 38 states and at least five countries are converging on Philip Morris facilities and product retailers for a nationwide Day of Action coinciding with the tobacco giant's annual meeting in Richmond today. Thirteen-year-old Kendra Banks of Hamilton Middle School in Seattle, Washington, winner of INFACT's nationwide Letter Writing Contest for kids, will read her letter to board members, shareholders, and executives of Philip Morris explaining why she boycotts Kraft Macaroni & Cheese.

Kendra Banks and other children and teens across the country wrote letters to Robert Eckert, CEO of Philip Morris's Kraft Foods, to highlight the ownership of Kraft Macaroni & Cheese by Philip Morris and to protest the tobacco transnational's aggressive marketing of tobacco, especially to young people. "I'm going to encourage others who buy your products," Kendra writes, "to leave them on the grocery shelves where they belong!"

The contest was part of the national corporate watchdog organization INFACT's Tobacco Industry Boycott, targeting Philip Morris's Kraft and RJR's Nabisco food brands since 1994. The Boycott applies direct economic pressure on the two largest US-based tobacco corporations to stop their marketing and promotion of tobacco to kids.

"Today's nationwide and international actions demonstrate the rapid expansion of the Kraft Boycott," says INFACT Executive Director Kathryn Mulvey. "Because the tobacco industry has no credibility of its own, Philip Morris needs the wholesome image of Kraft. INFACT's Boycott threatens that image. The role of organized consumer pressure in crumbling the RJR Nabisco cookie indicates the threat to Philip Morris posed by widespread consumer action."

Philip Morris documents released as a result of the Minnesota lawsuit against the tobacco industry confirm the corporation's fear of the Boycott. One memo prior to the Boycott's launch warned that it would force the tobacco corporation "to fight with everything we have." Today INFACT is unveiling preview copies of its 1999 People's Annual Report, Secrets & Lies: How Philip Morris and RJR Nabisco are Responding to INFACT's Tobacco Industry Boycott. The report provides an insider look at the corporate response to the early phases of this well-organized grassroots campaign. INFACT's analysis illustrates Philip Morris's concern about Boycott pressure, and reveals the corporation's public relations strategy of claiming Kraft's independence from tobacco. "The bad news," notes one memo about INFACT's Campaign, "is that this has the potential to go beyond the tobacco products and into our other operating divisionsànamely food and beer products. That's one area where the Philip Morris corporate name has kept many people away from our other companies' products."

Philip Morris closely monitors consumer participation in INFACT's Campaign. In 1994, corporate representatives worried, "They began with a postcard writing campaign and apparently have gotten a hold of our 800 number. What next?"

Next for INFACT is a moving billboard linking Kraft Macaroni & Cheese to its tobacco parentùand urging consumers to join the Boycott. "INFACT's billboard reclaims public space from the tobacco industry, following the recent removal of cigarette billboards across the US under the terms of last year's settlement with 46 states suing to recover health care costs," says INFACT's Mulvey. The Boycott Kraft billboard is making the rounds in Richmond this morning, then traveling to Washington, DC--home of the notorious public relations firm Burson-Marsteller.

Philip Morris hired Burson-Marsteller in 1993 to investigate INFACT and develop strategies to respond to the Boycott. Burson, a subsidiary of advertising giant Young & Rubicam, became the engine behind the National Smokers Alliance.

Exposure of Burson-Marsteller is just one element of mounting Campaign momentum, as growing numbers of INFACT activists all over the country call and write to Philip Morris. Kendra Banks' classmates in Seattle will be handing out Boycott leaflets at a grocery store near her school and speaking with the store manager about why retailers should support the Boycott. Hundreds of other leafleters nationwide will reach shoppers in their neighborhoods with the message that Kraft Macaroni & Cheese comes from the Marlboro men. At Kraft headquarters in Northfield, Illinois, INFACT volunteers will display the Human Toll of Tobacco Banner depicting people who suffer or have died from tobacco-related illnesses, then deliver Boycott messages from consumers to Kraft executives.

INFACT supporters and members of endorsing groups are paying a visit to Philip Morris/Kraft sites in San Leandro, California; Atlanta, Georgia; Phoenix, Arizona; New York City, and dozens of other locations. Throughout the day of action, thousands of Kraft boycotters are calling in to the Kraft consumer phone line to demand that Philip Morris' food business stop fronting for tobacco interests. INFACT volunteer Kathy Remias of Cleveland, who is organizing an action at the Philip Morris/Kraft facility in her city says, "The Boycott of Kraft is a great way for people like me to take on a huge corporation like Philip Morris and force it to stop its abuses."

INFACT activists and allies are also dropping in on Philip Morris facilities in the Czech Republic, Zimbabwe, Argentina, New Zealand, Spain, and Mexico. Corporate executives will receive copies of Global Aggression: The Case for World Standards and Bold US Action Challenging Philip Morris and RJR Nabisco, INFACT's 1998 People's Annual Report. Within weeks after last year's annual meeting at which INFACT exposed Philip Morris's international marketing abuses, CEO Geoffrey Bible pledged that his corporation would "align our marketing and selling practices here in the USA and overseas so that we cannot be accused of marketing cigarettes to youth."

Founded in 1977, INFACT's purpose is to stop life-threatening abuses of transnational corporations and increase their accountability to people around the world. INFACT is known for its successful Nestl‰ and GE Boycott Campaigns.
