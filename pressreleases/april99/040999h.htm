Arming KLA Not A Solution to Kosovo Problem, Says Scientists' Group
 
WASHINGTON - April 9 - The following was issued today by the Federation of American Scientists:

Arming KLA Not a Solution to Kosovo Problem

In this decade, the Kosovo Liberation Army (KLA) finally succeeded in destabilizing the uneasy balance in Kosovo between ethnic Albanian moderates and repressive Serbs by provoking, a few years ago, major Serb reprisals. In the traditional guerrilla fashion, the KLA knew these reprisals would isolate Albanian moderates, like Ibrahim Rugova, and swell KLA ranks with angry Albanians. In their effort to polarize the struggle, they did not hesitate, where necessary, to kill Albanian moderates and, according to human rights groups, took Serb hostages and conducted extra-judicial executions. This group is, in fact, the terrorist organization which, at the outset, the U.S. government termed it. And such a group, once armed, could not subsequently be controlled.

Arming the KLA would also invite the Russians to arm the Serbs and, from a diplomatic point of view, would make it harder to hold NATO together in this struggle. And it would prevent the contemplation of a range of possible solutions short of taking Kosovo away from Serbia. Indeed, the main value of discussing arming the KLA army is the incentive this provides to Serbia to settle the conflict. Because one thing is certain: if a fully armed KLA got control of Kosovo, it would never stop short of insisting on Kosovo independence. In addition, the KLA would use any such victory to fight subsequently to detach the Western part of Macedonia to secure the greater Albania for which it has called -- or even to widen the war if it began to lose so as to force others to join the war.

Arming the KLA would interfere with fully international inspection and occupation forces in Kosovo because the Russians would not agree to participate if the KLA were being backed by NATO. And it would not permit the U.S., or NATO, to drop out of the struggle entirely simply because the KLA, without air power, could not, by any stretch of the imagination, defeat the Serbs. With all this in mind, arming the KLA means allying ourselves with them. And this seems, for the above reasons, especially inappropriate.

It is difficult to foreclose options in the absence of any clear strategy with which to fight and win the war. But it does seem that the KLA does not fit within any plausible strategy. However, the Serbs should be reminded that, if the U.S. were to withdraw from the struggle, the field would be left to the KLA, armed by private contributions, and that this is something that Serbia would definitely not want.

-- Reviewed and Approved by the FAS Executive Committee.
