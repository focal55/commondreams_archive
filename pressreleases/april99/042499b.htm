Board Of Trustees Shuns Hunger Strikers
 
PITTSBURGH - April 24 - Hunger strikers continued in their 13th day despite continuing symptoms of fatigue, headaches, irritability and one hospitalization. On Friday April 23rd, the Executive Committee of the Board of Trustees met for approximately 90-minutes privately followed by a ten-minute session open to the public. Only Board members were allowed to speak during the public portion and all ignored the presence of ten hunger strikers waiting for action. At this meeting the Board approved funding for new housing for its students, but refused to even consider providing full benefits to all University employees. Members of the Equal Rights Alliance also met with Robert Dunkelman, Secretary of the Board of Trustees, to discuss meeting with individual members of the Board of Trustees to prepare a public forum.

The lack of public input into the Board of Trustees' Friday meeting further illustrates their lack of public accountability and interaction. The Board is essentially self-selecting; members are not publicly responsible, and are not involved in the day to day activities of the University. However, they make decisions that affect the entire university community and beyond. A handful of Board members are responsible for taking discriminatory actions and magnifying those actions by attacking a civil rights ordinance. These actions fly in the face of the founding principle of this nation: democratic accountability. While the Equal Rights Alliance understands and appreciates the Trustees' public service, the concept of accountability is a way to help them make better decisions.

We encourage Chancellor Nordenberg and the members of the Board of Trustees use their power to call an emergency meeting of the Board to create the forum where students, faculty, staff, media, and community members can voice their concerns about domestic partner benefits and the impact of the University's policy. This is an emergency. The actions of a few members of the Board ignore the health of hunger strikers, show contempt for the entire university community, and threaten decades of civil rights progress.

Chairman Connolly described the Hunger Strike as an extortion tactic. Extortion, ironically, is the same tactic the handful of Board members has used. Presumably by threatening to pull their contributions if Pitt extends medical benefits (as has happened when limited same-sex benefits were granted), they have pushed the University into the position of threatening to eliminate statewide civil rights protections unless Deborah Henson sacrifices her own. By using the unquestioned authority of his office to promote his own political views, Chairman Connolly's actions are at best an abuse of power and at worse they resemble tyranny. Members of the Equal Rights Alliance decided to act on an issue, which does not directly benefit them. They are standing up for the civil rights not only of campus employees, but also the citizens of Pittsburgh. In so doing, they are pushing to create a community of tolerance for the entire city.
