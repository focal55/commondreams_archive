Just the Facts: Tax Burdens on Typical Families
 
WASHINGTON - April 13 - Press Conference:
Just the Facts: Tax Burdens on Typical Families
10:30 a.m., Thursday, April 15, 1999
National Press Club East Room

On Thursday, April 15 at 10:30 a.m., in the East Room of the National Press Club, the Center on Budget and Policy Priorities will hold a press conference to assess how much average Americans pay in taxes and to critique a Tax Foundation report being released earlier that morning.

The press conference will examine the methodology the Tax Foundation uses in computing what it calls "Tax Freedom Day" -- the day until which the Foundation says the average American must work to pay taxes. Each year from 1993 through 1998, the Tax Foundation has claimed the average American's tax rate has increased, with "Tax Freedom Day" being pushed later into the year.

Iris J. Lav, the Center's deputy director, will discuss Center findings supported by data from sources such as the Congressional Budget Office, the Treasury, and the Joint Committee on Taxation on how much Americans are actually paying in taxes.

The press conference also will address such questions as:
- How does the Tax Foundation arrive at its picture of an average family's tax burden?
- Do the Tax Foundation's claims consistently overstate the amount families pay in taxes?
- Is there evidence of a trend toward higher taxation among families, as the Tax Freedom Day methodology has suggested?
- How do the Tax Foundation's claims regarding tax burdens compare to Congressional Budget Office and Joint Tax Committee estimates?

The Center on Budget and Policy Priorities is a nonpartisan research organization and policy institute that conducts research and analysis on a range of government policies and programs, with an emphasis on those affecting low- and moderate-income people. It is supported primarily by foundation grants.
