Union of Concerned Scientists Advisor Available for Comment on NATO Intervention in Kosovo
 
WASHINGTON - April 2 - News Advisory:

Ambassador Jonathan Dean, advisor to the Union of Concerned Scientists on International Security Issues, is available for comments on the NATO intervention in Kosovo.

Ambassador Dean was the Chief U.S. Representative at the NATO-Warsaw Pact force reduction negotiations between 1978-1981 and is author of "Ending Europe's Wars."

Ambassador Dean on the conflict in Kosovo:

"The United States and other NATO countries can gradually increase the pressure on Milosevic by continuing the bombing, talking about ground force intervention in Kosovo, deciding on ground force intervention and then assembling the forces for intervention. This process may take up to three months, with further ethnic cleansing and refugee flows from Kosovo in the interim. "

Or, the NATO countries can go for a solution that could be effective within a few days by returning to the Rambouillet formula for a peacekeeping presence in Kosovo, but switching away from NATO and then back to the UN as the peacekeepers in Kosovo. "

This approach would give all the parties something. Milosevic would not have to agree to NATO occupation of Kosovo. Russia could claim to be the originator of the solution and could affect its implementation from its position in the Security Council. The NATO allies would achieve the outcome in substance they are striving for without losing more Kosovar, Serb or NATO lives."
