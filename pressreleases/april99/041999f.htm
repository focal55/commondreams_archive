Sierra Club Members Protect Wildlands For Earth Day
 
WASHINGTON - April 19 - To celebrate Earth Day, Sierra Club members across America will take actions to protect their local wildlands. These local wildlands are spotlighted in a new Sierra Club national report, SPARE America's Wildlands: The Sierra Club Plan to Protect our National and Neighborhood Wild Places and Open Spaces, which examines the movement to protect America's open spaces on both the national and neighborhood scales.

After nearly two decades playing defense to slow environmental destruction, Americans have begun fighting for a more ambitious, pro-active wilderness agenda. A wilderness revival is underway, a grassroots movement of people working to protect not only grand national treasures such as the Everglades, Utah's wilderness, the Northern Rockies, Sierra Nevada, the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge, and the Maine woods, but also neighborhood open spaces that are locally important.
