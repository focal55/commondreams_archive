New Auto Pollution Rules Expected To Give The Green Light To Clean Cars
 
WASHINGTON - April 30 - Statement of Rebecca Stanfield, Clean Air Advocate, On the Anticipated Release of the EPA’s New Auto Pollution Standards:

U.S. PIRG applauds the EPA for proposing the tough new auto pollution standards as we anticipate that President Clinton’s will announce in tomorrow’s radio address. Requiring new cars to be clean cars is the single most important thing the Clinton Administration can do this year to protect the public from air pollution. Under EPA’s proposal, if preliminary reports are accurate, the typical new car will be nearly 90% cleaner than cars currently on the road.

While this rule is an important milestone on the road to cleaner air, it has a way to go before it crosses the finish line. We are concerned that several aspects of the Tier 2 proposal could cause delay in achieving its clean air benefits, and would perpetuate existing loopholes. In particular, bigger and dirtier SUVs will have until 2009 before they have to meet clean car standards and dirty diesel cars will be permitted to emit more pollution than gasoline cars. In addition, the final proposal should provide stronger incentives for the commercialization of advanced technology vehicles, such as electric or fuel cell automobiles.

U.S. PIRG and a growing coalition of environmental and health groups across the country will be campaigning in communities throughout the summer to send a strong message to the Clinton Administration that cleaner cars are a top priority for the American public.

U.S. PIRG is the national lobbying office for the State PIRGs across the country. The State PIRGs are non-profit, non-partisan consumer and environmental advocacy groups.
