"We Laughed! We Cried! But Mostly We Cried!" -- BP Amoco CEO Wins Earth Day "Oscar" for Acting Like an Environmentalist
 
NEW YORK - April 22 - In response to the environmental leadership award presented today to BP Amoco CEO John Browne at an Earth Day ceremony at the United Nations, Greenpeace gave Browne an Academy Award for "Best Impression of an Environmentalist." Greenpeace said behind Browne's portrayal of BP Amoco as a leader in solar energy lies a company with far greater investment in dirty fossil fuels that are causing global warming.

"As director of BP Amoco, John Browne has created an environmental fantasy of epic proportions," said critic Gary Cook of the Greenpeace Climate Campaign. "In reality, BP Amoco's commitment to reducing greenhouse gases is like the make-believe world of the 'The Truman Show.'"

The budget for 'Titanic' pales in comparison to BP Amoco's budget for oil: 99.95% of its recent investments are in fossil fuel concerns, while solar investments have actually declined to less than .01% of the overall company portfolio. Even with its recent acquisition of the solar company Solarex, BP Amoco's performance in clean energy still leaves environmental audiences underwhelmed. For example:
  • BP Amoco spent 588 times more on buying the oil company ARCO for $26.5 billion, than it has for Solarex.
  • BP Amoco spent $100 million, more than double this solar investment on legal/advisors fees for buying ARCO.
  • BP Amoco has stated that the merged company will spend $5 billion in the next five years on exploration and production in Alaska, up from the combined $4.5 billion that the two companies spent in the past five years.
  • For every $10,000 BP Amoco spent on oil exploration and development in 1998, $16 was spent on solar energy.

    Greenpeace is actively opposing BP Amoco's drilling and exploration in Alaska, particularly its Northstar project. If completed, Northstar would be the first offshore production oil field in the Arctic Ocean. "We cannot afford to burn 75% of the fossil fuels we've already discovered, if we are to avert catastrophic global warming," Cook explained. "Yet John Browne wants to spend $4 billion to open up new oil fields in the Arctic."

    Greenpeace recently launched SANE BP -- Shareholders Against New Exploration -- a group of BP Amoco shareholders who are demanding that the company shift their investments away from oil exploration and into renewable energy such as solar and wind.

    Last week, Alaska Natives attended the BP Amoco shareholders meeting in London where they confronted company executives about the devastating impacts of global warming on their environment and traditional lifestyles. The Arctic is currently warming at a rate 3-5 times faster than the global average, damaging wildlife and causing alarming rates of glacial melt.

    "At this rate," Cook concluded, "we may soon have to give John Browne another award for his leading role in the re-make of 'Some Like It Hot.'"
