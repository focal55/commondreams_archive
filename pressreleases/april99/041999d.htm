Arms Trade Policy: Problems And Solutions
 
WASHINGTON - April 19 - Press Advisory

Luncheon Briefing on Wednesday, April 28

DEMILITARIZATION FOR DEMOCRACY
BRITISH AMERICAN SECURITY INFORMATION COUNCIL
THE COUNCIL FOR A LIVABLE WORLD EDUCATION FUND

Members of the press are invited to attend a luncheon briefing on Capitol Hill for the release of Arms Un-Control: A Record Year for U.S. Military Exports, a report by Demilitarization for Democracy.

Speakers will also address current congressional and international policy approaches to limiting the spread of weapons. A briefing paper on the status of the European Union's Arms Trade Code of Conduct, written by the British American Security Information Council and Saferworld, will also be released.

This luncheon will take place:

DATE: Wednesday, April 28
TIME: 12-1:30 p.m.
PLACE: TBA

Invited speakers include: Jan Willem Bertens (Belgian Member of the European Parliament, sponsor of the European Union's Code of Conduct, and co-author of the report's foreword), non- governmental experts, such as Bobby Muller of the Vietnam Veterans of America Foundation, Steve Rickard of Amnesty International (representing the Nobel Peace Laureates' Code of Conduct campaign), Ambassador Robert White of the Center for International Policy (representing Dr. Oscar Arias, also a foreword co-author), and congressional leaders on arms trade policy such as Representatives Cynthia McKinney, Nita Lowey, Dana Rohrabacher, Chris Smith, John Porter, Jim McGovern, and Senator John Kerry.

As each speaker will offer just two or three minutes of perspective on policy implications, there will be ample time for questions.

Please call Paul Olweny at 319-7191 x 11 or e-mail pdd@clark.net to reserve a seat.
