HRC'S Donna Red Wing Honored By The Interfaith Alliance; Commitment To Faith and Fairness Earns Red Wing Walter Cronkite Award
 
WASHINGTON - April 12 - Human Rights Campaign National Field Director Donna Red Wing was honored as a recipient of the prestigious Walter Cronkite Faith and Freedom award Friday night. The award is given annually by the Interfaith Alliance, an interdenominational religious organization which counters the religious right and strives to bring civility and compassion into civic affairs.

"Today we honor two outstanding Americans who challenged the religious right's attempts to restrict religious freedom under the banner of religion by manipulating and narrowly defining the principles of Christianity," said former news anchor Walter Cronkite at a Manhattan press conference.

Red Wing received the award for having created an interfaith service for supporters of gay and lesbian equality. Rev. Chriss H. Doss, director of the Center for the Study of Law and the Church at Sanford University in Birmingham, Ala. also received an award. Doss encouraged public acceptance of a court ruling against a state school prayer law.

The award recipients were chosen by a six-person committee, which includes former President Gerald R. Ford; Bill Moyers, journalist; Rev. Joan Brown Campbell, General-Secretary, National Council of Churches of Christ in USA; Rep. John Lewis, D-Ga.; Cokie Roberts, news anchor; and Andrew Young, Chairman, Goodworks International.

"As a nation, our commitment must be to treat each other fairly, ensuring that all Americans – including gay Americans – are equally protected from discrimination," said Red Wing at the ceremony. "It is simply a matter of treating others as we wish to be treated. If we each do our part, we can make real the promise of fairness."

Red Wing has a long history of working for gay and lesbian equality. Before joining HRC, she was a senior consultant to the Gay & Lesbian Alliance Against Defamation. She is also GLAAD's former field director and community affairs director. From 1989 to 1993, Red Wing was executive director of the Lesbian Community Project in Portland, the largest lesbian organization in the Pacific Northwest. During that time, she helped lead the fight against Measure 9, a virulently anti-gay ballot measure that would have amended Oregon's constitution to declare homosexuality "abnormal and perverse" behavior. As a result of her efforts, The Advocate magazine named Red Wing its 1992 Woman of the Year. From 1985-1989, Red Wing was executive director of the Child Assault Prevention Project of Central Massachusetts.

The Human Rights Campaign is the largest national lesbian and gay political organization, with members throughout the country. It effectively lobbies Congress, provides campaign support and educates the public to ensure that lesbian and gay Americans can be open, honest and safe at home, at work and in the community.
