15,000 Students Take Community Action Through The 15th Annual hunger Cleanup; Students Dedicate Additional Resources in Special Effort to Aid Kosovo Refugees
 
WASHINGTON - April 9 - Fifteen thousand students from nearly 200 high schools and colleges are volunteering on such projects as cleaning shelters and painting food banks, as part of the 15th Annual Hunger Cleanup, a project of the National Student Campaign Against Hunger and Homelessness.This year’s Hunger Cleanup will include a special fundraising effort to raise an additional $10,000 for food, water, and supplies for the Kosovo Refugees, through Oxfam America’s relief fund.

The Annual Hunger Cleanup is one of the largest student-run community service fundraisers in the country. Through the Hunger Cleanup, volunteers raise hourly sponsorships for their time painting shelters, gleaning fields, cleaning soup kitchens, and planting community gardens.

"Students have the vision to not only help their local communities, but also to help people in need in another part of the world. Here is the next generation of community leaders and advocates for a better society," observed Julie Miles, Executive Director of the National Student Campaign Against Hunger and Homelessness (NSCAHH), sponsor of the Annual Hunger Cleanup and a project of the PIRGs.

Highlights From Across the Country:

* Mrs. Tipper Gore will join students from Castle College in New Hampshire in painting a food pantry in Derry, NH as part of Hunger Cleanup activities.

* The Cal Marching Band will kickoff UC Berkeley’s Hunger Cleanup. More than 250 volunteers will work with BOSS (Building Opportunities for Self-Sufficiency) and other sites to paint and cleanup sites such as transitional housing units and an emergency shelter for women and children who are HIV positive.

* More than 850 volunteers will work as part of Fairfield University’s Hunger Cleanup, which is truly a campus-wide event, drawing nearly every campus club and sports team, including the football team, men’s and women’s basketball and tennis teams, the golf team, and women’s volleyball team. Both the City of Fairfield and state of CT have proclaimed April 10th, Hunger Cleanup Day.

* UW Madison’s Hunger Cleanup will involve 250 volunteers who will work in the homes of low-income elderly community members to help with spring cleaning. Congresswoman Tammy Baldwin will speak at the students’ kickoff event.

Fundraising increases the impact of the Cleanup by helping agencies in need. Fifty percent of the funds raised are donated to local organizations; 35 percent of money raised supports NSCAHH; and 15 percent will fund international projects through the International Development Exchange. A special collection will be taken to raise $10,000 to benefit Kosovo Refugees. Over the 14 year history of the Hunger Cleanup, 100,000 student volunteers have raised more than $1 million to combat hunger and homelessness locally, nationally, and internationally.

###

Founded by the student Public Interest Research Groups (PIRGs) and USA for Africa in 1985, the National Student Campaign Against Hunger and Homelessness is the largest student network in the country fighting hunger and homelessness through education, service and action. The Campaign sponsors several national programs and provides support and training to student leaders through projects manuals, on-site trainings, phone consultations, and a national conference. NSCAHH also functions as a clearinghouse of information and organizational contacts for students and the community.
