Seventh "Golden Leash" Awarded: Sen. Rick Santorum (R-PA) Cited for his Campaign Finance Ties To Companies Profiting from Medical Savings Accounts
 

HARRISBURG, PA - April 29 - For his ties to the special interests that fund his campaign, Senator Rick Santorum (R-PA) was dubiously honored with the "The Golden Leash Award" today by Public Campaign at a press conference in Harrisburg.

Santorum is one of the most outspoken champions in Congress of Medical Savings Accounts (MSAs), a health insurance experiment that economic experts and consumer groups argue help the healthy and wealthy and disadvantage the ill and poor. Since 1991, Santorum has accepted nearly $91,000 from companies in the MSA business, through PACs and large ($200+) contributions from executives. Santorum's two largest MSA donors were Pennsylvania-based Mellon Bank and the Golden Rule Insurance Company, based in Indiana. Golden Rule is the main lobbying force behind MSAs in Washington.

The Golden Leash Award is a modern incarnation of former Senator William Proxmire's legendary Golden Fleece, which highlighted government waste and abuse.

"Senator Santorum's actions on behalf of the very companies that would profit most from MSAs - and which have rewarded him handsomely with campaign contributions - are proof that special interests have greater influence over politicians than the public who elects them," said Ellen Miller, executive director of Public Campaign.

"It's time that politicians like Sen. Santorum be held accountable for the campaign contributions they take and what they do for them in return."

MSAs are like medical Individual Retirement Accounts, tax-free investment accounts used to cover medical expenses. MSAs are usually accompanied by some sort of catastrophic insurance, a high-deductible insurance policy that pays for medical bills over a set amount.

MSAs are a lose-lose proposition for sick and poor people. They must either pay higher premiums for traditional health care (which is likely to happen if MSAs are widely adopted by the healthy), or, if they purchase MSAs, pay more out of their pockets to satisfy the high deductible on their catastrophic health care plan. For a low-income family - or even a middle-class family - the deductible can be a substantial portion of income.

In Santorum's home-state of Pennsylvania, where the median household income is $34,437, a family could spend 13 percent of its yearly income on doctors' bills before the catastrophic plan begins to cover costs. Consumers Union predicts that if 25 percent of healthy people enrolled in MSAs, then premiums for traditional policies would increase by 63 percent. That increase is likely to be borne by the less well-off and the ill.

Wealthy people with MSAs can not only better afford to pay high deductibles, they also benefit more from a tax free account since they are in a higher tax bracket. And the greater the tax breaks enjoyed by the wealthy, the more money the Federal Treasury (i.e. taxpayers) lose.

Sen. Santorum first introduced legislation establishing MSAs in 1991, when he was a House member, and has since sponsored or cosponsored 11 separate bills that promote MSAs. A demonstration program established by Congress in 1996 for MSAs sunsets in the year 2000. Sales of the MSAs under the pilot program have been tepid and MSA company executives complain that people aren't buying them because the pilot program is too limiting (only the self-employed and people working in firms of 50 or fewer employees may participate), so the MSA lobby is going to need all the friends on Capitol Hill it can find to make changes.

Public Campaign's Golden Leash Award focuses public attention on politicians who do particularly egregious favors for their cash constituents, highlighting their captivity to special interests at the expense of average voters, taxpayers and the public at large. It was Senator Proxmire - a member of Public Campaign's National Advisory Board - who said that Congress has a "golden leash" around its neck due to the campaign contributions that have grown exponentially through the years. Past recipients include Rep. Bill McCollum (R-FL), Sen. Chris Dodd (D-CT), Rep. Bill Thomas (R-CA), Sen. John Breaux (D-LA), Rep. Charlie Stenholm (D-TX) and Senators Jeff Sessions (R-AL), Richard Shelby (R-AL) and Rep. Bob Aderholt (R-AL).
