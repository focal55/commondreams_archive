New Independent Film Tells Story Of Rural Gay Priest
 
PORTLAND, ME - April 9 - A new documentary film tells the story of a Catholic Priest as he discovers his homosexuality and struggles with family relation ships, his vows to the church and his self acceptance.

The film, entitled "Tangible Fathers" will make its broadcast premiere on the Maine PBS independent film series at 11 PM on May 13, 1999 and run throughout the Independent Series1 summer season. The one hour film has been screened in independent theatres, by Pride and other LGBT&Q organizations / events and by university film and LGBT studies organizations in New England. The film is now being marketed nationally to independent theatres, Pride & LGBT & Q groups as well as university film and LGBT studies groups.

"Tangible Fathers" explores "Matthew's" life from the birth of his spirituality in his pre-teen years, through the discovery of his sexuality in his early adulthood, to his present day struggle with accepting his sexual identity in the light of his role as a man of the cloth.

In the light of Hollywood's recent wave of gay romantic comedies "Tangible Fathers" offers a cold reminder of what life as a homosexual is like in rural Christian America.

During Production of Tangible Fathers:
Still employed by the church and having second thoughts about making the film, "Matthew" decides he can not reveal his identity to the audience, after over a year of development and production. This turn of events drives Tangible Fathers' personal "memoir" style. Using various experimental film textures and digital imagery we follow "Matthew's" life from the birth of his spirituality to his present day struggle with loneliness, anonymous public sexuality and the internal conflicts with his vows to the church. After presiding over a same gender commitment ceremony and a straight "forbidden" wedding ceremony that gets him called into the chancellor's office, we are with him when he decides that he needs to leave the priesthood in order to be true to himself and "the true nature of love and spirituality".

The Producer:
Bob has Associate Produced for National Geographic Television Specials, Turner Broadcasting Network and the Discovery Channel as well as Producing and Co-Producing several projects for Maine Public Broadcasting. "Tangible Fathers" is Bob's first independent film that has been three years in the making.

"Tangible Fathers" was sponsored by The Equity Institute of Maine. Funding came from many community organizations, family, friends and personal resources.

Bob Poirier, originally from Biddeford, Maine now resides in Portland. Screenings can be scheduled through Allied Artists Inc. 207-828-4030
