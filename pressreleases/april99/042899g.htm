Jesse Jackson And Religious Leaders Make Humanitarian Mission To Yugoslavia; Group To Deliver Messages To Captured U.S. Servicemen
 
WASHINGTON - April 28 - A delegation of Christian, Muslim and Jewish leaders led by the Rev. Jesse Jackson, president of the Rainbow/PUSH Coalition, is traveling to Belgrade Wednesday on a humanitarian mission which will include a visit to the three captured U.S. soldiers. Jackson will deliver tape-recorded messages from the families of the servicemen and meet with Yugoslavian religion leaders.

The inter-religious delegation includes leaders from Protestant, Roman Catholic, Jewish, Muslim and several Eastern Orthodox communions as well as U.S. Congressman Rod Blagojevich (D-IL) and Rev. Dr. Joan Brown Campbell, general secretary of the National Council of Churches. The group plans to leave from Dulles Airport on Wednesday, April 28. The departure time and travel route are fluid and are subject to immediate change.

"This is a pilgrimage of faith, not of politics," said Rev. Jackson. "We go to Belgrade as religious leaders who are concerned about all who suffer in that region. We also expect to visit the three American soldiers captured and held by Yugoslav Army forces and to share our prayers for their safety and swift release."

"We plan to meet with religious leaders there, with whom we have long ties," said Dr. Campbell. "The breadth of our delegation reveals our deep concern as people of faith for the captured soldiers and for refugees and victims of violence on all sides. Should we get to see the three soldiers, we are bringing along personal messages to them from their families as well as a Bible for each of them signed by the religious leaders in the delegation."

In addition to the religious leaders, Jackson has organized a group of doctors and health care practitioners who are also planning a humanitarian mission to bring medical supplies to Albanian refugees.

Rev. Jackson and Dr. Campbell have contacted each the families of Staff Sgt. Andrew Ramirez of Los Angeles, Staff Sgt. Christopher Stone of Smiths Creek, Michigan, and Specialist Steven Gonzales of Huntsville, Tex. The three soldiers were part of the United Nations peacekeeping force when they were captured along the border with Macedonia on March 31.

The U.S. Christian, Muslim and Jewish leaders go to Belgrade to support the religious community in Yugoslavia and at their invitation. The state purpose of the religious mission to Belgrade is as follows:
  • Hopes to meet with the American soldiers now held as prisoners to take personal messages to them from their families and to gather messages from them to bring back to their families.
  • Hopes to present a bible to each of the prisoners and share with the captives prayers for their safety and swift release.
  • Plans to visit the leaders of Yugoslavia's many religious communities to support their pastoral leadership as they aim to heal wounds rather than foster them.
  • Will speak plainly about the destructive force of war that harms ethnic Albanians and Serbs alike.

  • Will speak out on the destructiveness of animosity between God's children. Hopes to carry back a message to be shared with the Americans religious community from the Yugoslavian religious leaders.
