Wellstone Urges Antitrust Action to Address Market Concentration, Low Prices in Farm Economy
 
SOUTH ST PAUL, MN - April 18 - At an historic town meeting in South St. Paul with agricultural producers from across the Midwest and federal antitrust officials, Senator Paul Wellstone continued to push for antitrust action to address the growing market concentration in the Midwest farm economy. Justice Department Antitrust Chief Joel Klein and USDA Under Secretary Michael Dunn came to Minnesota for the meeting Sunday to hear firsthand the stories of farmers and producers about the economic effects of the growing market concentration in the Midwest farm economy.

"We're in the midst of a crisis of American agriculture," Wellstone said. "The growing concentration of economic power in the hands of a precious few firms threatens the existence of the family farm structure in American agriculture. The current economic structure harms farm families and has a ripple effect, putting the squeeze on our rural communities. It holds potential dangers to consumers, food safety and the environment nationwide. And it undermines basic democratic principles by concentrating extreme economic and political power in the hands of a small number of large and apparently unaccountable agribusiness corporations."

"I am pleased that Joel Klein and Mike Dunn took time out of their busy schedules to come to Minnesota to hear the concerns of farmers and producers in the Midwest. I urge them to take these accounts seriously, and step up their efforts to take antitrust action where it is appropriate," Wellstone said. "This historic gathering is a huge first step toward reversing the current trend toward concentration in the agriculture economy. We will continue to marshal evidence of anti-competitive practices and continue to organize until we succeed in restoring free enterprise to the free enterprise system."

"Farmers and producers recognize the connection between record low prices and the growing market concentration in agriculture. Family farmers feel the economic effects of these conglomerates muscling their way to the dinner table. We need to get the price up for farmers and antitrust action is an essential part of that effort. The underlying crisis in our agricultural economy will not go away unless we alter the basically anti-competitive structure of the entire food and agribusiness sector. Four corporate packing houses control more than 60 percent of all hogs slaughtered in this country. Hog farmers are facing extinction while the packers are in hog heaven," Wellstone said.

Wellstone, with the help of senators from the Upper Midwest, spearheaded the effort to bring federal antitrust officials out to the Midwest to meet with farmers and state and local officials about these issues. On January 12, 1999 Wellstone signed a letter with six other Democratic farm state senators to urge Attorney General Janet Reno to initiate an immediate, full-scale antitrust investigation into the growing vertical integration and concentration of the nation's agricultural and livestock markets. On November 24, 1998 Wellstone wrote letters to Attorney General Janet Reno and to Chairman Robert Pitofsky of the Federal Trade Commission, urging close and careful review of the Cargill/Continental merger filing for possible anti-competitive effects as covered by the Hart/Scott/Rodino antitrust act.
