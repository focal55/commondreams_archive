PSR Says Colorado Gun Tragedy Was Predictable and Preventable
 
WASHINGTON - April 22 - Littleton is not the first town to be torn apart by gun violence and, without swift and decisive action by the 106th Congress, it won't be the last. Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR) calls on Congress to take immediate action to stop the gun violence that is sweeping the nation.

"The tragic event yesterday in Littleton, Colorado, was both predictable and preventable," says Robert K. Musil, Ph.D., Executive Director of PSR. "Where did these children get this arsenal of weapons? Until we restrict the ready access to guns in this nation, the blood will continue to flow."

According to PSR, gun manufacturers are a big part of the problem. One shotgun manufacturer is actually marketing to young people with psychedelic paint on their products. Without major changes in the ways that guns are sold and distributed, physicians will continue to see the victims of gun violence in their emergency rooms and morgues.

Only cancer, heart disease, and motor vehicle accidents cause more deaths than guns. A recent study by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention indicates that nearly one in every ten male high school students reported carrying a gun in the past thirty days.

PSR urges 3 immediate actions to help address the problem of gun violence:
  • Regulate the firearms industry. Impose regulations on the design, manufacturing and distribution of firearms and firearm products.
  • Make guns safer and less accessible to children. Require the safe storage of guns, prohibit the carrying of concealed weapons, and require mandatory waiting periods for firearm purchases.
  • Educate the community about the dangers of guns in the home. Encourage dialog among parents, teachers and children on issues of guns and violence.

    "We are not helpless in the face of gun violence," says Dr. Musil. "These are concrete steps that Congress can take to protect the public health and safety. On behalf of PSR's Board of Directors and our 15,000 members, we call on Congress to take action NOW!

    PSR urges support for eight bills that are currently before Congress. A fact sheet describing these bills is available on request.
