Chevron, ARCO Shareholders to Vote on Resolutions to Protect the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge
 
WASHINGTON - April 27 - Shareholders for Chevron and ARCO have the opportunity to protect one of America’s last wild places, the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge, at their annual meetings this week. Shareholder resolutions have been introduced that call on the two companies to "unconditionally cancel any future plans for oil drilling in Coastal Plain, 1002 Area, of the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge and immediately stop the expenditure of any corporate funds targeted to achieve this objective.”

“Passing the Arctic shareholder resolution would make Chevron and ARCO industry leaders in the fields of corporate and environmental responsibility,” said Athan Manuel of the U.S. Public Interest Research Group (U.S. PIRG). “It would preserve a unique and fragile ecosystem and dramatically enhance each company’s image with the American public.”

Caribou, muskoxen, wolves, polar, brown and black bears, and hundreds of thousands of migratory birds rely on the wilderness habitat that the Refuge provides. The Gwich’in, which means “people of the caribou,” have lived in and around the Refuge for thousands of years. Their subsistence culture depends on the caribou herd. The Chevron annual meeting is April 28, 1999 in San Ramon, California.

The ARCO annual meeting is May 3, 1999 in Los Angeles, California. The Arctic resolution is item 5 on the Chevron proxy form and item 3 on the ARCO proxy card.

ARCO is the second-largest oil producer and the largest U.S.-based oil producer in America’s Arctic. The Los Angeles-based company is one of the three principal owners of the Trans Alaska Pipeline System, controlling approximately 20 percent. Chevron controls less than 2 per cent of the oil from Alaska’s North Slope. The oil industry already has access to 95 per cent of America’s Arctic.

“Consumers want to support companies that do not harm the environment or disturb traditional cultures,” continued Manuel. “We feel that each company would benefit significantly from positive publicity if shareholders were to vote in favor of canceling drilling plans for the Arctic.”

The Chevron resolution was filed by Trillium Asset Management (formerly Franklin Research and Development). The Green Century Equity Fund, on behalf of the Domini Social Index Portfolio, filed the resolution with ARCO. U.S. PIRG, the state PIRGs’ national lobbying office, is coordinating a national campaign to protect the Arctic Refuge from drilling. Jonathan Soloman, the chairman of the Gwich’in Steering Committee will attend the ARCO shareholder meeting.
