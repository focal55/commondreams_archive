DC Web Women Announces 'Take Our Daughter on the Web' Events
 
WASHINGTON - April 13 - DC Web WomenSM (DCWWSM), a non-profit professional organization serving women in new media industries, is sponsoring the third annual "Take Our Daughters on the Web" event series on April 21 in conjunction with San Francisco Women on the Web (SF WoW).

DCWW volunteers will offer web technology training throughout the D.C. Metro area in order to expand the career horizons of girls and familiarize them with increasingly common Internet technologies. On the same day, SFWoW will sponsor corresponding events in the San Francisco Bay Area.

Take Our Daughters on the Web (TODWeb) springs from the traditional Take Our Daughters to Work Day sponsored by the Ms. Foundation. Initiated three years ago, TODWeb grew out of the desire of the DCWW membership to encourage girls to consider careers in new media. This year, the involvement of SF WoW elevates TODWeb to a national event.

"SF WoW's mission is to support and empower the fast-growing community of women working in the Internet and new media industries. TODWeb is the perfect way to reach out to the next generation of computer-savvy women and encourage them to become leaders in these fields," said Cyd Harrell, Point Woman for SF WoW.

"Take Our Daughters on the Web is an opportunity for DCWW to help the future generation of women realize the benefits and earning potential of careers in new media," said Jeanne Marshall, president of DCWW. "We are interested in being both a career resource and a community resource to women of any age, and TODWeb helps us fulfill this mission."

"Community service is a foundation of the DCWW philosophy," said Sonia Zamborsky, DCWW outreach director. "We're excited about the opportunity to help girls visualize careers in new media industries."

DCWW is offering 2-hour workshops at five locations throughout the region, including Maryland and Virginia. Each workshop will serve 10-12 girls, who will learn about the Web, online safety issues, careers in the wired world and how to build a web page. Each girl will create her own page, which will be posted to the DCWW website.

Corporate sponsors have volunteered to contribute training rooms, teaching assistance and giveaway items. Among the sponsors are Decision Systems Technologies, Rockville, Md., WestLake Internet Training, Arlington, Va., and NetMasters, Washington, D.C. Also contributing are Georgetown University Networked Media Center in Washington, D.C., and the University Maryland, College Park Campus.

For more information on Take Our Daughters on the Web, please e-mail DCWW at info@)dcwebwomen.org, visit the website at www.dcwebwomen.org.

Headquartered in Washington, D.C., DC Web Women (DCWW) is a non-profit professional organization serving women in new media industries. DCWW provides a forum for women involved or interested in new media. In addition to sponsoring monthly workshops, seminars and lectures, DCWW runs a listserv that functions as a powerful information exchange tool and job clearinghouse. DCWW focuses strongly on career development, and is a leading employment source in the area for both recruiters and job seekers. DCWW members strive to be role models for young women and girls and to promote women in new media.
