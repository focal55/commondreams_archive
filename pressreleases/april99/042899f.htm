Human Chain of Protesters to Surround NRA Annual Meeting
 
WASHINGTON - April 28 - As National Rifle Association (NRA) members gather for their annual meeting on Saturday morning, May 1 at the Adam's Mark Hotel in Denver, they will confront a human chain of protesters surrounding the hotel. Sponsored by the Colorado Coalition Against Gun Violence, the rally is expected to draw thousands of demonstrators to protest the NRA's promotion of the sale and use of guns and their opposition to gun control policies and safety regulations.

Physicians for Social Responsibility (PSR), a national organization representing 15,000 members, is part of the Coalition and has played a role in organizing the protest. PSR members know first-hand what it means to treat the victims of gun violence and are speaking out on the urgent need for prevention. They advocate three immediate actions

-- Impose strict regulations on the design, manufacturing and distribution of firearms.

-- Require the safe storage of guns, prohibit the carrying of concealed weapons, and require mandatory waiting periods for firearms purchases.

-- Educate the public about the dangers of guns. Encourage dialogue among parents, teachers, and children on issues of guns and violence.

"The tragic event in Littleton was both predictable and preventable," says Robert K. Musil, Ph.D., executive director of PSR. "More than a million children have access to guns in their homes. A recent study by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention indicates that nearly one in every ten male high school students reported carrying a gun in the past 30 days. Until we restrict the ready access to guns in this nation, the blood will continue to flow."

-- Available for Interviews Dr. Robert K. Musil is executive director of Physicians for Social Responsibility, a national nonprofit organization working to prevent gun violence. Dr. Musil is on the steering committee of the Handgun Epidemic Lowering Plan (HELP), a coalition of health organizations engaged in a wide range of efforts to reduce gun violence. He is currently working with Senator Robert Torricelli (D-N.J.) to help build support for the Firearms Safety and Consumer Protection Act of 1999. Dr. Musil has conducted television debates with spokespersons from the National Rifle Association and appeared on numerous TV and radio programs including ABC World News Tonight, NPR's All Things Considered, and the Michael Jackson Show. He has held teaching positions at several major universities and is currently an adjunct professor at American University.
