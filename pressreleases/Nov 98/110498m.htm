Former Sen. Paul Simon Predicts U.S., Worldwide Water Crisis in New Book
 

WASHINGTON - November 4 - News Advisory:

A new book published this week titled "Tapped Out" by former U.S. Sen. Paul Simon details a coming water shortage in states like California, Florida, Texas and 22 countries around the world brought about by over consumption of existing supplies and population growth.

Sen. Simon will discuss his new book at the Population and Environmental Policy Forum at the American Association for the Advancement of Science, 1200 New York Ave., N.W., Ravelle Room (second floor), Washington, D.C., between 12:30-2:30 p.m. on Monday, Nov. 9.

Simon, a former two-term U.S. Senator from Illinois, now heads the Public Policy Institute at Southern Illinois University.
