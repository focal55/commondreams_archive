Election Analysis 1998
 
WASHINGTON - November 3 - MEDIA ADVISORY – ELECTION DAY 1998

Election Analysis 1998
Statements by Planned Parenthood Federation of America

WHAT: Election Analysis 1998

WHO: Gloria Feldt, President of the Planned Parenthood Action Fund & Nina Miller, Director, Planned Parenthood Action Fund & Federal PAC

WHEN: Ongoing, beginning November 3, 1998

Planned Parenthood Action Fund (PPAF) engaged in an unprecedented level of electoral activity during Election 1998. The following highlights some of the PPAF’s activities:

Independent Expenditure Campaigns:

Brian Baird – Washington-3, Lane Evans – Illinois-17, Joe Hoeffel – Pennsylvania-13, Roxanne Qualls – Ohio-1, Tom Udall – New Mexico-3.

PAC Contributions:

In PPAF’s first year of PAC activity, 167 U.S. House and Senate candidates were endorsed, 127 of which received between $1,000 to $10,000. All of the candidates PPAF supported through independent expenditures received PAC contributions. Highlights are as follows:

SENATE RACES: Sen. Barbara Boxer – California, Sen. Russ Feingold – Wisconsin, Sen. Patty Murray – Washington, and Rep. Charles Schumer - New York.

HOUSE RACES:

Incumbents: Rep. Lois Capps – California-22, Rep. Maurice Hinchey – New York-26, Rep. Loretta Sanchez – California-46, Rep. Brad Sherman – California-21, Rep. Adam Smith – Washington-9, and Rep. John Tierney – Massachusetts-6.

Challengers: Shelley Berkley – Nevada-1, Lily Eskelsen – Utah-2, Baron Hill – Indiana-9, Jay Inslee – Washington-1, and David Wu – Oregon-1.

Ballot Initiatives:

The Action Fund also contributed to the campaigns fighting anti-choice ballot initiatives in Washington (abortion ban) and Colorado (abortion ban and a restriction on minors’ access to abortion.)
