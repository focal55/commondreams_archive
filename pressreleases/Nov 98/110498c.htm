American Friends Service Committee to Accept Donations for Assistance to Hurricane Mitch Victims in Honduras
 
WASHINGTON - November 4 -

The American Friends Service Committee (AFSC) announced today that it will begin accepting financial donations to assist victims of Hurricane Mitch in Honduras. The storm has left an estimated 100,000 people homeless and the extent of damage from wind and flooding is still being assessed. Rain continues to fall, worsening the flooding and mudslides all over the country.

Donations will be distributed through AFSC's programs and program partnerships working in Honduras, including the Mennonite Social Action Committee, and will be used to support local recovery efforts. Funding will provide food and materials most needed, especially blankets, sheets, children's clothes, medicines and canned food.

"The storm has brought tremendous devastation to many parts of Honduras, but the northern coast, the Bay Islands and other low-lying areas have been hit especially hard," said David Bronkema, of AFSC's Latin America and Caribbean Programs. "Major rivers have overflowed, wiping out 26 bridges, destroying roads and electric lines, and have carried away thousands of houses. Many people are still stranded, waiting to be rescued."

AFSC programs in Honduras include the wide-ranging COMAL network of small farmers and community stores engaged in an alternative trade project that seeks to tie together rural producers and consumers. One hundred fourteen groups in this network are in the areas hit hardest by the hurricane, and will help channel relief to those who need it.

Donations can be made to the Hurricane Relief Fund, American Friends Service Committee, 1501 Cherry Street, Philadelphia, PA. 19102.

#####

The American Friends Service Committee is a Quaker organization which includes people of various faiths who are committed to social justice, peace and humanitarian service. Its work is based on the belief in the worth of every person and faith in the power of love to overcome violence and injustice.
