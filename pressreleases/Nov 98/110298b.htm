Common Cause Releases Election Night Campaign Finance Kit
 
WASHINGTON - November 2 - News Advisory:

Common Cause has released an Election Night Campaign Finance Kit examining the financing of the 1998 elections. Included in the kit are the following Common Cause studies:

House races: 87 percent of House incumbents in financially uncompetitive races; 95 incumbents unopposed altogether.

Senate races: Senate candidates raise record $243 million throughSept. 30, 1998; incumbents enjoy a 10-to-1 PAC advantage over challengers.

Soft money: National parties raise $162 million in soft money through Oct. 14, 1998; Republicans outraise Democrats by $28 million.

Common Cause spokespeople, including Common Cause President Ann McBride, are available on Monday and Tuesday to discuss the financing of House and Senate campaigns in 1998, and to discuss the issue of money in politics in general.

To schedule an interview, or to receive the Election Night Campaign Finance Kit, call the Common Cause Press Office at 202-736-5770.
