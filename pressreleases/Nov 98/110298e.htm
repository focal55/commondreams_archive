United Methodist Social Action Agency Joins Fast for Farmworkers
 
WASHINGTON - November 2 - This is a statement of Jaydee R. Hanson, assistant general secretary of the General Board of Church and Society of The United Methodist Church:

The General Board of Church and Society calls on United Methodists to fast in solidarity with farm workers, the day after Thanksgiving, November 27th. This one-day fast will call attention to the unfair wages and deplorable living and working conditions of farm workers, who toil in the fields picking the fruits and vegetables that we eat each day. It will also give us the opportunity to donate the money that we would have used for food to a farmworker organization of our choice.

We recognize that the most fundamental needs of farm workers are the needs for dignity, for self-determination and for self-organization. Of working people in the United States, farm workers have consistently been among the most poorly paid, housed and educated. Most live below the poverty level and lack access to adequate health care. They have been systematically excluded from all, or nearly all, of the social and economic benefits enjoyed by other US workers.

Immigrants, especially, migrant workers, face the brunt of legislated xenophobia. The US Congress tried to pass a new agricultural immigrant program that would have worsened the substandard working conditions that farmworkers already face. Although this attempt failed, we are certain that Congress will try again.

For more than 70 years, the United Methodist Church has been in ministry with national farm worker support organizations. United Methodist policy on farm workers declares that the Church’s most important role must be an advocate with and supporter of the efforts of farm workers. The teachings of Jesus calls us to show special concern for the poor and the oppressed. We have sought to improve the living conditions of farm workers and will continue to press for better educational opportunities, housing, a living wage, and full coverage by all social legislation for farm workers (Book of Resolutions1996, Special Needs of Farm Workers resolution).

Fasting is an ancient religious tradition. The founder of the Methodist movement, John Wesley, fasted often and gave the money he saved to the poor. We call on all United Methodists to fast in solidarity with farm workers. Let us fast, pray and work for justice for the people who work to put food on our tables.

###

Only General Conference speaks for the entire denomination. The General Board of Church and Society is the international public policy and social action agency of The United Methodist Church
