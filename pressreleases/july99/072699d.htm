Wellstone Welcomes White House Announcement on Increased Funding for Vets Health Care, But Says `We Must Do Better'
 
WASHINGTON - July 26 - Senator Paul Wellstone welcomed the announcement made today by the Clinton Administration that it will seek an additional $1 billion for veterans' health care in the upcoming fiscal year 2000 budget, but said the request does not go far enough. The White House budget office is to request the additional funds for veterans health care from Congress today.

"I applaud the Administration for reconsidering its prior request, but we must still do a lot better than this. This increase gets our veterans only one-third of the way to where health care funding should be. For the past three years, veterans have received flat-line budgets from this President and the Congress which threaten to impose real hardships on our nation's citizen-soldiers. I join my friends in the veterans' community in continuing to call on the President and Congress to support an increase of $3 billion above the current budget for veterans' health care," Wellstone said.

Four days ago Wellstone released a hard-hitting report which attracted national attention by showing that the Clinton administration's proposed budget would result in major cutbacks in staff and reduced services for veterans. The Wellstone report found that 20 of the 22 VA health care networks would experience funding shortfalls if Congress accepts the administration's proposal to provide $17.3 billion for VA medical facilities next year, about the same as this year.

"At the same time that we are celebrating the announcements of huge budget surpluses, it is outrageous that our veterans would be denied timely, quality care because of insufficient funding. Make no mistake, our veterans have earned their health care benefits on battlefields around the world," Wellstone said.

The Administration's earlier annual budget request represented the fourth straight flat-line budget for veterans health care. The Independent Budget, a detailed analysis of the needs of the VA health care system, found that a $3 billion increase over last year's budget is needed to provide full funding for veterans. The Independent Budget is created bt the Veterans of Foreign Wars, the Disabled American Veterans, the Paralyzed Veterans of America, and AMVETS, and is endorsed by Wellstone and dozens of groups nationwide.
