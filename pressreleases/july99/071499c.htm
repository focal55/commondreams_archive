Wellstone Amendment Guarantees Patients' Choice `Point of Service' Plan Ensures Choice, Continuity and Protection for Consumers
 
WASHINGTON - July 14 - As the Senate swung into the third day of its long-delayed debate on how to curb unfair health insurance practices, U.S. Senator Paul Wellstone called for action to guarantee that consumers get the access to the care they need, choice and continuity. Wellstone announced today his intention to introduce a key amendment ensuring patients the option of a true `point-of-service' plan, supported by a coalition of over 130 consumer and provider groups that includes every major medical association and patient advocacy group in America, and all of his Democratic colleagues in the Senate.

The Wellstone `Point of Service' amendment to the Patients Bill of Rights legislation would give patients the ability to choose providers and services outside their plan's network, at little or no additional cost to the employer. If a health plan decides to cut the number of its participating doctors, or if an employer changes the company's health plan, the `Point of Service' measure would allow patients to continue seeing their existing doctor.

"When Americans need to see a doctor, they should enjoy the right to see the doctor of their own choice. All Americans -- regardless of whether they work for a large company or a small business -- must be able to choose a point-of-service option at the time of enrollment. It is shameful that Americans today do not have that right, and will not have that right unless we pass the Democratic patient protection bill," said Wellstone.

Choosing a point-of-service option at the time of enrollment allows a patient to continue current relationships with providers whom they rely upon and trust. The Wellstone `point-of-service' option is the ultimate consumer protection against poorly-managed health plans.

"It is well known that patients do best when they have confidence in their doctors, and clearly choice is the key component of consumer satisfaction with the health care system. It is not only the sickest and most disabled among us that need this crucial protection -- and they surely do-- all of us need this protection," Wellstone said.

While the Republican bill prevents employers with more than 50 employees from offering only one closed-panel HMO, it does not require that consumers have choice outside the offered plans, even if they are willing to pay the additional cost. A choice of two closed-panel health plans is not true point-of-service and does not guarantee choice.
