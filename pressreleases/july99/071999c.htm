Greenpeace U.S.A. Joins Global Opposition to Plutonium Fuel Shipments
 
WASHINGTON - July 19 - Greenpeace activists protested the pending shipment of hazardous, highly radiotoxic nuclear fuel from Europe to Japan. Several of the protesters held a giant banner that read “Nuclear Free Seas” in front of the Japanese embassy while others carried placards and posters that read “Stop Plutonium Shipments”. This demonstration is part of an international day of action by a number of environmental and anti-nuclear groups opposed to the shipment.

Two ships, the Pacific Teal and the Pacific Pintail, are due to load the plutonium fuel (MOX) in Barrow, northern England, and the French port of Cherbourg. The plutonium fuel contains more nuclear weapons-usable material than in the Indian nuclear weapons program. The vessels' departure date and route are being kept secret by the nuclear industry.

Meanwhile, two Greenpeace vessels, the MV Greenpeace and the SV Rainbow Warrior, are on stand-by monitoring the ports of Barrow and Cherbourg in France respectively. Caribbean states and the New Zealand Government have already expressed formal opposition to this shipment, the first commercial plutonium fuel shipment to Japan and nearly 50 nations have objected to previous nuclear transports.

"It is clear that there is wide-spread public and governmental opposition to the shipment of plutonium fuel, which puts nuclear non-proliferation objectives and the global environment at risk," said Greenpeace spokesperson, Carwil James. "Currently East Asia is one of the most dangerous areas in the world, it is essentially a plutonium powder keg. That is why Greenpeace is calling for an immediate stop to these shipments.”

Previous nuclear shipments have taken three different routes: around the Cape of Good Hope in Southern Africa and up through the Tasman Sea between Australia and New Zealand, Cape Horn on the tip of Latin America and also through the Caribbean and the Panama Canal.

British Nuclear Fuels Limited (BNFL) is currently preparing to load one ship in Barrow, in the English north-west, with a cargo of eight plutonium fuel elements containing nearly 500 pounds of plutonium. A second ship in Barrow will travel to the French port of Cherbourg where it will be loaded with 32 MOX fuel elements containing more than 480 pounds of plutonium. The two ships are then expected to rendezvous at sea, off the French Atlantic coast, and continue together on the 20,000-mile voyage to Japan.

Greenpeace is the leading independent organization that uses peaceful and creative activism to protect the global environment.
