Planned Parenthood Applauds House Support for UNFPA
 
WASHINGTON - July 21 - Statement by Gloria Feldt, President Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

Planned Parenthood Federation of America (PPFA) applauds the House of Representatives' vote to restore U.S. funding to the United Nations Population Fund (UNFPA). The bipartisan decision (221-198) to provide $20 million to UNFPA in the next fiscal year saw Congress making a major commitment to improve the health and rights of women around the world.

The vote, the first strong pro-choice vote of the legislative session, not only will make a profound difference in the lives and health of women and families internationally, it will restore U.S. leadership in international family planning.

The House has put the lives and needs of women above politics. I am delighted to see common sense overcome rigid ideology in Congress.

For too long, the House has been held hostage by its anti-choice members. Opponents of international family planning have denied basic reproductive health care to women overseas by claiming UNFPA funds supported the coercive family planning policies of the Chinese government. This was a gross misrepresentation of the facts. They know this isn't about China, it's about blocking family planning and a woman's right to determine when to start a family and how large her family will be. If anti-choice members want to debate family planning, I say let's debate it. We know family planning works and we know it prevents abortion. If anti-choice members want to stop abortions, logic says they should support family planning. That they do not is unconscionable.

There are legitimate human rights concerns about Chinese policies. But denying basic reproductive health care services has been an illogical, and highly punitive, way to address them. When Congress voted to withhold funding for UNFPA last year, they did nothing to punish the Chinese government or effect a change in policy. They did hurt countless women in 150 countries that relied on UNFPA for family planning.

The need for US support is clear:
  • In the current fiscal year for which there is no U.S. funding for UNFPA, 870,000 women have been denied access to modern contraception;
  • Of these, 500,000 women have faced unintended pregnancy, leading to 1,200 maternal deaths, 22,000 infant deaths, 234,000 unwanted births, and 200,000 abortions.
  • Without contraception, countless thousands have died needlessly from sexually transmitted diseases.


    • Planned Parenthood will continue to work strenuously on behalf of the fundamental right of each individual, throughout the world, to make responsible choices about his or her fertility, regardless of income, marital status, race, ethnicity, sexual orientation, age, national origin, or residence.
