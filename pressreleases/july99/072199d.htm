Wellstone To Release Report Showing "Devastating" Impact on Veterans' Health Care; Budget Cuts in 2000 Will Close Doors to Thousands of Vets, Slashing Staff in VA Hospitals
 
WASHINGTON - July 21 - Senator Paul Wellstone will hold a news conference on Thursday, July 22 at 11:30 am at the Senate Swamp to release a report with Senator Tim Johnson (D-SD) and leaders of the veterans' community showing in detail the "devastating" impact of the Clinton Administration's flat-line VA health care budget request for FY 2000.

The report contains data from each of the twenty-two Veterans Integrated Service Networks (VISNs) around the country summarizing the effects of the proposed funding level requested by the Administration. The FY 2000 budget will generally result in shortfalls in funding necessary to provide current health care service levels for our nation's veterans at VA facilities in every state in the nation.

WHAT: News Conference to Release a Report Showing in Detail the Impact on Veterans Health Care Resulting From President Clinton's `Flat-Line' FY 2000 Budget Request.

WHEN: Thursday, July 22 11:30 am

WHERE: Senate Swamp

WHO:
Senator Paul Wellstone (D-MN)

Kenneth Steadman, VFW Executive Director
Dave Gorman, Disabled American Veterans Executive Director
