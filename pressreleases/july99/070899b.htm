Voices for Planned Parenthood Returns to Lilith Fair 1999
 
NEW YORK - July 8 - The premiere summer concert event for many music lovers kicks off in Vancouver, BC today. For the third year in a row, Planned Parenthood Federation of America (PPFA) is teaming up with Lilith Fair to educate concert-goers about reproductive health and freedom.

"The generosity of Lilith Fair provides a remarkable opportunity for Planned Parenthood to offer vital reproductive health information, link young people to health care centers and mobilize supporters," PPFA President Gloria Feldt said.

Vox: Voices for Planned Parenthood

To mobilize young adults, Planned Parenthood has launched Vox: Voices for Planned Parenthood sm. Vox is a nationwide program that seeks to increase awareness about the threats to reproductive health and freedom and engage 18-to 30-year-olds in the civic life of our nation.

"Through Vox, our voices for Planned Parenthood national mobilization campaign works with musical artists and the music industry to inspire young people to raise their pro-choice voices and register voters," PPFA President Gloria Feldt added.

The plannedparenthood.org Web site and the recently-launched teenwire.com Web site are cyber resources, a click away to learn more about VOX, the summer tour schedule, critical reproductive health information and the Planned Parenthood Responsible Choices Action Network.

Interactive Kiosk at Concerts: A Link to the Planned Parenthood Responsible Choices Action Network

This year Planned Parenthood on-site concert activities will include an interactive recruitment kiosk, featuring a computer with the plannedparenthood.org Web site and a sign-up page for the Planned Parenthood Responsible Choices Action Network, which alerts its members about important reproductive health issues in Congress and links them to their representative in Washington, D.C.

The 1999 Planned Parenthood national concert tour outreach program this summer will be on the road in three Canadian provinces and concert-setttings in more than 20 states nationwide. The festivals include Barenaked Ladies, Ani DiFranco, Santana and Mana, and the Vans Warped Tour. The Woodstock Festival booked for three days later this month from July 23-25 in Rome, New York, is among the major tour events that will include Vox interactive activities for concert-goers.
