List of Top Bush Fund-Raisers
 
WASHINGTON - July 20 - The list of 115 top fund-raisers for Texas Gov. George W. Bush's (R) presidential campaign has been posted by the Center for Responsive Politics as a public service at:
http://www.opensecrets.org/2000elect/other/bush/pioneer.htm.

The list of "Pioneer" volunteer fund-raisers for Bush, released by the campaign late Monday, includes everybody who has raised at least $100,000 from individuals and/or PACs for the campaign. Federal campaign finance laws limit contributions per election to $1,000 per individual and $5,000 per political action committee, but there are no restrictions on the amounts that individuals can raise on behalf of a campaign.

Campaigns are not required to disclose the names of their fund-raisers. The Bush campaign released the information at the urging of reporters and public policy organizations.

The Pioneers include several present and former public officials, including Michigan Gov. John Engler and U.S. Rep. Jennifer Dunn (R-Wash.), and a large number of business leaders. The list released this week includes those who have raised at least $100,000 so farm, and it is expected to grow dramatically as other Bush fund-raisers hit that target.

According to the campaign, this preliminary list of Pioneers produced about 40 percent of the record $37 million Bush collected in donations during the first quarter of this year.

The Center for Responsive Politics is a non-partisan research organization in Washington, D.C. that tracks money in politics, and its effect on elections and public policy. The Center conducts computer-based research on campaign finance issues for the news media, academics, activists, and the public at large.
