Dirty-Air Budget 'Rider' May be Offered Today
 
WASHINGTON - July 26 - TO: EDITORS AND REPORTERS COVERING THE ENVIRONMENT
FROM: Frank O'Donnell
DATE: July 26, 1999

THERE THEY GO AGAIN!
POLLUTERS PREPARE STEALTH ATTACK!
DIRTY-AIR BUDGET `RIDER' MAY BE OFFERED TODAY

With much of the Eastern half of the nation wheezing since May under "Code Red" alerts, coal-based power polluters are preparing a stealth attack against a key smog cleanup strategy.

The dirty-air assault is expected this afternoon as a congressional subcommittee examines the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency's budget. At industry's request, a member of that panel, Rep. Joseph Knollenberg (R-Mich.), is preparing to introduce an amendment or "rider" that would cripple EPA's ability to reduce smog-forming pollution from electric power plants. (Please don't hesitate to call me at 202-785-9625 if you'd like a copy of the amendment or would like to discuss the issue.)

Here are the other House members who will be asked to vote on the clean air gut job: Rep. James Walsh (R-N.Y.); Rep. Tom DeLay (R-Texas); Rep. David Hobson (R-Ohio); Rep. Rodney Frelinghuysen (R-N.J.); Rep. Roger Wicker (R-Miss.); Rep. Anne Northrup (R-Ky.); Rep. John E. Sununu (R-N.H.); Rep. Alan Mollohan (D-W.Va.); Rep. Marcy Kaptur (D-Ohio); Rep. Carrie Meek (D-Fla.); Rep. David Price (D-N.C.); and Rep. Robert Cramer (D-Ala.). All of the subcommittee's members live in states that have breached national health standards for smog this summer. Ironically, Rep. Knollenberg's state has been among the dirtiest.

Blue-Ribbon MTBE Panel to Report Tomorrow

Tomorrow a blue-ribbon federal panel is expected to recommend changes in the federal reformulated gasoline program, including a call to eliminate mandatory levels of oxygen additives (including the controversial MTBE) in gasoline. Significantly, the panel reportedly will also urge that reformulated gasoline maintain its clean-air benefits.

Those benefits have been substantial. According to the EPA, reformulated gasoline has sharply reduced emissions of toxic pollutants (such as benzene) from motor vehicles -- the equivalent of taking nearly 15 million cars off the road. Additional benefits are expected with Phase 2 of the clean-gasoline program, scheduled to start next year. Reformulated gasoline also has produced major reductions in emissions of smog- forming volatile organic compounds and nitrogen oxides and is expected to produce additional reductions during Phase 2.

State-by-state breakdowns of those benefits are available for: California, Connecticut, Delaware, the District of Columbia, Illinois, Indiana, Kentucky, Maine, Maryland, Massachusetts, New Hampshire, New Jersey, New York, Pennsylvania, Rhode Island, Texas, Virginia, and Wisconsin. Please don't hesitate to call if you'd like details.
