Business leaders Applaud F-22 Funding Cuts; Urge Congress To Set New Priorities
 
WASHINGTON - July 21 - Cutting $1.8 billion from the expensive and unnecessary F-22 Raptor program is a good first step towards trimming Pentagon waste, say members of Business Leaders for Sensible Priorities, and Congress should take the next logical steps to reduce such wasteful spending and instead invest in the needs of America's children.

"This is a plane the Pentagon doesn't need and U.S. taxpayers can't afford," said Dr. Lawrence Korb, former assistant secretary of defense under President Ronald Reagan and a senior advisor to Business Leaders for Sensible Priorities. "By applying the same scrutiny to other Cold War weapons, Congress could trim tens of billions of dollars a year off the current budget."

Dr. Korb last year authored a report, "Why a Cold War Budget Without a Cold War?," an analysis of Pentagon program expenditures that recommends trimming about 15 percent, or $40 billion, a year from current Pentagon spending. The F-22 was one of the programs Dr. Korb called unnecessary.

Ben Cohen, founder of Business Leaders for Sensible Priorities and co-founder of Ben and Jerry's Homemade, Inc., urged House Members to stand firm for reduced F-22 funding when the appropriations bill goes to Conference Committee, and he added that Congress should seize this opportunity to re-direct some of the savings to important domestic needs.

"The federal budget is our nation's checkbook and should reflect our priorities," Cohen said. "We need to invest in our kids, not Pentagon waste. Dumping this unnecessary Cold War holdover and using the money we save to build communities would be a great start."

Cohen also said Congress should apply the same standards to other costly and unnecessary Pentagon spending, such as America's overlarge nuclear weapons inventory or plans for new aircraft carriers and attack submarines. For example, Cohen said, the $2.1 billion it would cost to build one new attack submarine would build 350 elementary schools.

Business Leaders for Sensible Priorities is a national education campaign aimed at redirecting federal budget priorities to invest in necessary domestic needs, such as education and child health care, and paying for those investments by reducing wasteful Pentagon spending, like the F-22. The campaign now has more than 500 business executives and retired military personnel on its membership rolls.
