Kenyan Students Defend Native Forest; Green Party requests Sanctions to Defend Forest
 
LAWRENCE, MA - February 2 - The Greens/ Green Party USA, Forest Issues Coordinator and national co-spokesperson Lionel Trepanier, called upon the U.S. President and Vice President today, to "make clear US policy to embargo any project destructive of forest ecosystems in Kenya. President Moi of Kenya, now must be made stop the police state repression of peaceful protest."

Background:

Brave students in Kenya's capital blocked a major street with stones and tree trunks. Three hundred students staged the protest of government plans to cut the Karura Forest, a rare indigenous forest just outside the capital, Nairobi, the Tribune wire service reported. Environmental activists say government officials gave friends of President Daniel arap Moi's ruling party a lease to construct homes Kenya's few native forests that have not yet been cut down. Riot police, on January 31, 1999, fired tear gas and beat students with clubs to break up the second day of protests.

Isaack Otieno Kenyan Green activist in exile in the Hague, reports Kenyan ecofeminist Wangari Maathai, was recently beaten by State police when she led the members of the public to the forest to plant tree seedlings.

As Greens we demand our Government stand with the Kenyans against persistent Police harassment of innocent and heroic civilians who are fighting to save their natural world, even as we engage our own government to investigate the murder of forest activist, David Chain, redwood activist.

As Greens /Green Party USA - and as internationalists working in solidarity with democratic, anti-militarist, and ecological movements around the world to build a peaceful, just, free, democratic, and ecologically sound world -:

We call on the US to influence international agencies like the World Bank, private banks, and trade boards for a halt to all current international funding and policies that may promote destruction for forest ecosystems in Kenya;

We, also call for direct diplomatic pressure by the US government on Kenya;

We further call upon our leaders to demand an end to all cutting in ancient forests and significant native forests.
