Oregon Jury Strikes Blow Against Anti-Choice Terrorism
 
NEW YORK - February 2 - Statement by Gloria Feldt
President, Planned Parenthood Federation of America:

The verdict issued today by an Oregon jury in Planned Parenthood v. ACLA, sends an important message—that Americans stand united against terrorism and threats of terrorism.

"The jury saw anti-choice Wanted Posters for what they are—a hit list for terrorists," said Gloria Feldt, president of Planned Parenthood Federation of America (PPFA). "The American people—represented by this jury—have clearly stated that our society will neither tolerate violence, nor tolerate intimidation by domestic terrorists who issue thinly veiled threats of violence. Whether these threats are posted on trees or on the Internet, their intent and impact is the same: to threaten the lives of doctors who courageously serve women seeking to exercise their right to choose abortion."

Doctor David Gunn and Dr. John B. Britton were shot to death after being targeted individually on wanted posters. Dr. George Tiller, one of those named on the "Deadly Dozen" poster, was wounded by gunshots. The name of Dr. Barnett Slepian was posted on the Nuremberg Files Web site—with a line through it to indicate his murder—within hours after he was cruelly slain in his home by a sniper last October. Dr. Slepian's killer is still at large, as is the perpetrator of the deadly clinic bombing a year ago in Birmingham, Alabama. Planned Parenthood filed this suit in 1995, but the threat of violence remains quite real today.

"These defendants care nothing about our democratic freedoms," said Feldt. "They respect neither the rights of women nor the lives of the doctors who care for women. Their threats of violence and their public 'justification' of murder are meant to terrorize and intimidate, so that others cannot exercise their own rights. They have intentionally created a climate of fear and violence, yet they cowardly refuse to accept any legal or moral responsibility for it. Today's verdict strikes back in defense of our doctors, our patients, and our constitutional right to provide important health services and information, free from fear."

"The jury could see the tremendous harm done to the physicians as individuals, and to the women's health care institutions that were the targets of these threats," said PPFA Director of Litigation Roger Evans, who was co-counsel for the plaintiffs. "We will use this judgment to try to deny these people financial resources for any future dirty work. This case should leave no doubt in the minds of those who threaten our liberties that Planned Parenthood, together with other people of conscience, will take every step available within the law to defend our freedoms, and to protect our patients, our doctors, and our clinics. We could not have succeeded in this case without the expert and tireless pro-bono work of the New York law firm Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton, and Garrison, and the Portland law firm Davis, Wright, and Tremaine. We are tremendously grateful for their help."

"We cannot restore the lives of those who have been killed so that American women no longer die from unsafe, illegal abortions," said Feldt. "But this verdict reminds us all that while Americans have a great diversity of thoughts and feelings about abortion, we speak with one voice to renounce domestic terrorists."
