NMA Says 'No' to Washington State Over Affirmative Action Vote, Pulls 2001 Convention from State
 
WASHINGTON - February 2 - The following was released today by the National Medical Association:

Today, the National Medical Association (NMA), the largest black medical society representing the interests of more than 20,000 African-American physicians promoting quality healthcare for African Americans, officially pulled their 2001 Convention from Washington State because of the passage of an anti-affirmative action ballot measure, Initiative 200.

The recently passed legislation dismantles affirmative action programs in Washington. The health implications of this initiative will significantly impact community medical services and public health. Thus, "such legislative enactment is counter to the basic tenets upon which the National Medical Association was founded more than 100 years ago," says NMA's executive director, Lorraine Cole, Ph.D.

Efforts to find a relocation site are under way. Potential sites include Denver, Miami, Nashville, Orlando, Philadelphia, New York and Baltimore. As the economic benefit of the NMA convention to its host city has reached as high as $5.7 million, NMA is confident that a new host city will be secured in a timely fashion.
