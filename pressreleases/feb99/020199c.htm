President's New Budget Endorses ILO's Activities
 
WASHINGTON - February 1 - Following through on his State of the Union pledge to "put a human face on the global economy," the President's new budget today includes $25 million to support a new initiative by the International Labor Organization (ILO) to address the challenges of globalization.

The ILO is a specialized United Nations agency that promotes better working conditions, job creation, and human rights globally through international standards, compliance oversight, technical assistance, and research. The ILO's unique "tripartite" structure--with 174 member countries represented by workers, employers and governments--has helped advance economic and social stability since its creation in 1919.

Now world leaders are looking to the ILO to take on new challenges and to ensure, in the words of President Clinton, that the new global economy "works for working people." Today's budget request for the ILO follows President Clinton's January 12 announcement that his Administration would support the creation of "a new arm of the [ILO] to work with developing countries to put in place basic labor protections, safe workplaces, and the right to organize, so that workers everywhere can enjoy the advantages of a strong social safety net."

Commenting on the President's announcement, ILO Washington Office Director Anthony G. Freeman said: "President Clinton and Congress offered strong bipartisan support last year for our work on child labor through the International Program to Eliminate Child Labor (IPEC), and U.S. business and labor leaders came together to develop an historic ILO Declaration and monitoring system to bring basic human rights to today's global workplace.

We look forward to continuing bi-partisan support this year, so that we can make major strides towards the ILO's goal of building stable societies."

News about the Declaration, efforts to ban abusive child labor, and other ILO work of interest to Americans is available at the new ILO-U.S. web site ( www.us.ilo.org ), launched this week. The site offers regular news updates, links to the ILO's Geneva headquarters, timely information on ILO labor standards and constituent services, and an "ILO Kids" micro-site explaining the child labor problem to concerned children in the U.S.
