Ben & Jerry's Co-Founder Dons Nightshirt on National TV in Attempt to Wake Up Washington to Misguided Spending Priorities
 
WASHINGTON - February 4 - Ben Cohen, co-founder of Ben & Jerry's Ice Cream and leader of a national group of business leaders working to shift federal funding from the Pentagon to education and health care, will appear today on ABC's "The View" dressed in a nightshirt that depicts these lopsided funding priorities.

Cohen will appear with co-founder and friend Jerry Greenfield as guests of supermodel Christie Brinkley on the daytime television talk show. Wearing a nightshirt emblazoned with the "Move Our Money" logo and a federal budget pie diagram, Cohen hopes to bring more attention to his favorite cause.

"I guess I should first apologize to the American public for exposing them to the sight of myself in a nightshirt, especially with a supermodel standing next to me," said the XXL Cohen.

"But if the public will look beyond this study in physical contrasts, they'll see a very important message. We need to stand up to the defense contractors and their lobbyists and invest in our communities so we can modernize schools and provide health care for our kids."

This week in Washington, D.C., Cohen and supporters constructed an 11-foot ice cream pie at the U.S. Capitol to demonstrate to the media and the public that the Pentagon now gets more than half of all federal discretionary spending. President Clinton has proposed increasing defense spending by more than $100 billion in the next five years, even though America's Cold War nemesis, the Soviet Union, no longer exists.
