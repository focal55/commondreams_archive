Smog Threat ‘98: New Report Documents Over 5000 Violations of EPA’s New Smog Standard This Summer
 
WASHINGTON - October 5 -

What:

Public health and environmental organizations will release a new report by U.S. PIRG and the Clean Air Network documenting the more than 5000 violations nationwide of the health standard for smog pollution. The report compiles and analyzes air quality data recorded between May and early September for twenty-six states and Washington, DC, and  more limited data from an additional fifteen states.    Ozone or "smog" air pollution sends tens of thousands of Americans to the emergency room each summer. 

Who:
Rebecca Stanfield, Energy Advocate, U.S. PIRG
Jayne Mardock, Clean Air Network
Dr. Alfred Munzer, Director of Pulmonary Medicine, Washington Adventist Hospital and past President of American Lung Association

When:
Thursday, October 8, 1998,  9:30 a.m.

Where:
Garfield Park, On First street, SE, five blocks south of Cannon office building, one block south of the corner of First and E streets, SE.
The site is just across First street from the power plant that serves the electric needs of Congress.  In case of rain, the press conference will be held in the conference room of the Natural Resources Defense Council, 1200 New York Ave. NW, Suite 400, Washington, DC

EXCELLENT VISUALS:  Examples of both major sources of smog producing air pollution -- an old, coal-burning power plant and automobiles driving on a major highway -- converge to form the backdrop for the press conference.  A day-care facility is only 100 yards away.
