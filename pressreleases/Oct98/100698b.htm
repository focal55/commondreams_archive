American Friends Service Committee Joins "Jubilee 2000/USA" Campaign Seeking Debt Relief for World's Poorest Countries
 
PHILADELPHIA, PA - October 6 - The American Friends Service Committee (AFSC) will join a growing number of religious and social justice organizations in a world-wide campaign calling for cancellation of the crushing international debt of impoverished countries by the beginning of the millennium, Kara Newell, AFSC's Executive Director, announced today. The campaign, Jubilee 2000/USA, involves grass roots organizing, education and advocacy on policy issues.

"No vision of a just and peaceful world can be realized without a comprehensive plan to cancel the overwhelming debt problem faced by the developing world," Newell said. "We look forward to working closely with the organizations allied in this historic effort, which we believe offers a significant opportunity for alleviating poverty and injustice in heavily indebted nations."

Jubilee 2000/USA was launched in Denver at the time of the Summit of the Group of 7 Governments in June 1997. The campaign grew out of a project of the Religious Working Group on the World Bank and the International Monetary Fund, a coalition of representatives of over 40 U.S. Catholic and Protestant organizations concerned with debt relief. The Jubilee 2000 movement is based on inspiration from the book of Leviticus in the Hebrew Scriptures. The book describes a Year of Jubilee every 50 years to rectify social injustice: slaves are freed, land is returned to original owners and debts are canceled.

AFSC's Board of Directors recently approved a measure to co-sponsor Jubilee 2000/USA during its September meeting in Philadelphia. AFSC will participate in the campaign as a member of the National Steering Committee through its Washington D.C. office director, James Matlack.

Angela Berryman, Coordinator of AFSC's Latin America/Caribbean Peace Education Programs, said that the Committee's participation in Jubilee 2000/USA is an extension of AFSC's long-standing commitment to economic justice work in the US and around the world. AFSC's Chicago Economic Justice Program director will serve as the mid-west coordinator for the campaign.

Berryman noted that AFSC staff have been collaborating with groups in Africa and Latin America on educational and organizing campaigns to raise awareness of the detrimental effects large debt repayment programs have on poor countries. For example, she said that in the world's 37 poorest countries, health and education spending per person has gone down 50 and 25 percent respectively. Among African countries, combined spending on interest is about four times higher than amounts spent on health care.

"Clearly, this (Jubilee 2000) effort to forgive debt is not charity, but rather a just and sensible response to the desperate situation in much of the developing world, especially in sub Saharan Africa," Berryman said.

The American Friends Service Committee is a Quaker organization which includes people of various faiths who are committed to social justice, peace and humanitarian service. Its work is based on the belief in the worth of every person, and faith in the power of love to overcome violence and injustice.

More information on Jubilee 2000/USA is available on their web site.
