Open Letter to President Clinton Looking Ahead To Palestinian-Israeli October Talks
 
WASHINGTON -

October 1, 1998
         
President William J. Clinton
The White House
Washington, DC
                  
Dear Mr. President,
                   
      Thank you for your resolve expressed in recent days to help peace survive and grow stronger in the Middle East. Churches for Middle East Peace (CMEP) welcomes your renewed commitment to work with Israel and the Palestinian Authority to ensure that the 1993 Declaration of Principles and 1995 Interim Agreement establishing a framework for security and self-determination are implemented.  As President of the United States, your involvement is crucial for a just and lasting peace in the region to become reality.
                         
      Palestinians and Israelis need a vision of where the peace process is going.  Churches for Middle East Peace encourages you to assure the Palestinians that their recognition of the State of Israel is consistent with their own right of self-determination, including the possibility of a Palestinian state. Similarly, CMEP urges you to assure the Israelis that their compliance with U.N. Security Council Resolution 242 is consistent with their right "to live within secure and recognized boundaries free of threats or acts of force."
              
     Mr. President, like you, we abhor the violence that has so marked the relationship between Israel and the Palestinians.  We are deeply concerned about the great many innocent people who have suffered and live with fear. The high priority you give to security and combating terrorism is understandable; we ask, however, that these security measures be grounded in an appropriate regard for human rights. Israel's demolition of homes, destruction of olive groves,  confiscation of land and expansion of settlements must cease.  All these actions, which violate international law and needlessly provoke Palestinians, lead to violence.
         
      We stand with you in your commitment to end the Arab boycotts of Israel and to secure additional aid for the Palestinians.  Immediate improvement of the Palestinian economy, however, requires the cooperation of Israel in dismantling the crippling restrictions currently in place. Greater economic integration of the region is crucial to a lasting peace. That will be impossible to achieve if Palestinians continue to be denied their economic, political and human rights.
                   
      The leaders of  twenty Christian denominations and organizations wrote to you in May urging "active, determined efforts by the U.S. to reinvigorate the peace  process."   These Christian leaders expressed their conviction "that a resolution of  the question of Jerusalem is essential for peace and cooperation among the three Abrahamic faiths." As the possibility of final status negotiations approaches, we again encourage you to offer a vision for Jerusalem that respects the human and political rights of both Palestinians and Israelis, as well as the rights of the three religious communities.
         
      Final status negotiations will also move the issue of Palestinian statehood and sovereignty into discussion.  We express our concern that a Palestinian entity be territorially and economically viable and founded on international law and human rights covenants and conventions.
          
      We encourage you and Secretary Albright to provide strong leadership in the illusive quest for peace as you bring Israeli and Palestinian leaders together in October.  The role of principal sponsor of the peace process that falls upon your Administration is of profound importance to the conflicting parties, the international community and the American people.
         
Sincerely,
Executive Committee
Churches for Middle East Peace
         
Mark B. Brown, Chair             Thomas Hart          J. Daryl Byler
         
Ted Keating, S.M.                Robin Ringler
