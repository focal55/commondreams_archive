Religious, Civil Rights Groups Accuse Christian Right of Accelerating Anti-Gay Bias
 
WASHINGTON - October 13 - The following was released today by the National Gay and Lesbian Task Force:

Under the guise of compassion, the Christian Right is intensifying its anti-gay campaign at a time when hate crimes against lesbians and gay men -- like the fatal assault on 22-year-year Wyoming college student Matthew Shepard -- are on the rise, a new study shows.

The report, Calculated Compassion: How the Ex-Gay Movement Serves the Right's Attack on Democracy, documents for the first time that groups like the Family Research Council and Christian Coalition are promoting the bogus notion that people can be "converted" to heterosexuality as camouflage for a new effort to strip lesbians and gay men of their basic civil rights.

The report will be issued on Thursday. It is a collaboration of three organizations representing distinct perspectives on the subject: Political Research Associates, a Boston-based independent research center that studies anti-democratic movements; the National Gay and Lesbian Task Force, a leading civil rights organization; and Equal Partners in Faith, a national network of religious leaders.

Representatives of the three organizations and others will hold a press conference to detail the report's findings at 11 a.m. on Oct. 15 at the New York Avenue Presbyterian Church, 1313 NewYork Ave., N.W., in Washington, D.C.

Copies of the report and other information will be available there.
