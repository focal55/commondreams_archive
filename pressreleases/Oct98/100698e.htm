Will the 1998 Election Be Stolen by Scandal? Americans Want Straight Talk About Issues But Focus on Scandal Threatens to Overwhelm the Debate
 
WASHINGTON - October 6 - Media Advisory

Polling research shows that most voters want the 1998 election to be about the issues and choices that will affect the future of working families - Social Security, education, health care, and a clear plan to protect the American economy and jobs from the threat of world financial crisis.   These are issues that provide the basis for a progressive resurgence.  Instead, we have gotten a 24 hour diet of scandal, investigations, and impeachment posturing.

The scandals consuming Washington have transformed the political debate this fall. The fallout has muffled the core concerns of working Americans - on key issues such as Social Security, education, health care and good jobs - issues that provide the basis for a progressive resurgence.

A media seminar & breakfast hosted by the Campaign for America’s Future.

Join leading opinion analysts -

Diane Feldman, Stan Greenberg, Celinda Lake, Ron Lester, and Guy Molyneux

in a discussion of the attempt to steal the election.

The analysts are co-authors of Straight Talk, a new publication by the Campaign for America's Future, designed to help activists and political leaders reach out to a new progressive majority. They will discuss how the scandals have transformed the political debate this fall – and the implications for the future.
