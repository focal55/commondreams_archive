NPCA Says Discovery Trails at Risk; Rep. Bereuter Initiative's Fate Tied to Controversial Anti-Environment Bill
 
WASHINGTON - October 2 - An initiative by Rep. Douglas Bereuter (R-Neb.) to establish extended interstate trails is jeopardized by its connection to a large "omnibus" bill in the House of Representatives that contains numerous environmentally destructive provisions.

The National Parks and Conservation Association (NPCA) is recommending that Rep. Bereuter remove the trails provision from the larger bill and press the House to pass it separately.

A system of protected National Discovery Trails would be established under Bereuter's provision, the cornerstone of which would be a 6,000 mile, coast-to-coast American Discovery Trail. NPCA, the nation's leading park advocacy group, supports the new trails initiative but believes it may be doomed by its association with the large and hotly contested omnibus bill.

"It is wrong to hold Discovery Trails hostage to a bad bill that attacks many other protected areas," NPCA President Tom Kiernan said.

NPCA and many of the nation's leading environmental and conservation organizations oppose the "Omnibus National Parks and Public Lands Act of 1998," (H.R. 4570) which was introduced with only 12 days left in the congressional session. The Clinton administration has threatened to veto the bill if it is passed by Congress.

Although H.R. 4570 also includes provisions, such as National Discovery Trails, that would benefit national parks and public lands, they are overshadowed by the many environmentally destructive sections of the bill. These include the sale of national park lands, the declassification of wilderness areas, irresponsible logging in national forests, and costly giveaways of federal water projects. The bill also would weaken the president's authority to create national monuments in order to protect threatened federal lands. Leading environmental groups have identified a total of 23 measures in the bill with damaging environmental impacts.

NPCA has been urging members of Congress who have beneficial, non-controversial provisions in the omnibus bill to persuade the House leadership to allow them to move forward on their own.

"Congress has procedures for passing important, but relatively small pieces of legislation," Kiernan said. "Unfortunately, that process has broken down and good bills are running out of time. Rep. Bereuter should work with the Republican leadership to ensure that National Discovery Trails are not caught in the train wreck that is coming."

-----

The National Parks and Conservation Association (NPCA) is America's only private nonprofit citizen organization dedicated solely to protecting, preserving, and enhancing the U.S. National Park system. An association of "Citizens Protecting America's Parks," NPCA was founded in 1919 and today has nearly 400,000 members.

A library of national park information, including fact sheets, congressional testimony, position statements, press releases and media alerts, can be found in NPCA's "Press Room" on the World Wide Web at http://www.npca.org .
