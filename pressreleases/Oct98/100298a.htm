Environmental, Human Rights Groups to Rally Outside World Bank/IMF Annual Meetings
 
WASHINGTON - October 2 - News Advisory:

Environmental and human rights organizations will stage a giant puppet show and rally on the opening day of the World Bank and International Monetary Fund's Annual Meetings at the Marriot Wardman Park Hotel.

Friends of the Earth, 50 Years is Enough, Rainforest Action Network and other groups are protesting World Bank funding for projects that benefit multinational corporations, devastate the environment and hurt the poor. Activists are urging the World Bank to drop a planned loan to Exxon and Shell for a controversial oil pipeline between the African nations of Chad and Cameroon.

The rally will feature giant puppets, including an impersonation of World Bank President James Wolfensohn, and colorful banners. Speakers include Nigerian Oronto Douglas, former attorney for Ken Saro-Wiwa. Saro-Wiwa was killed by the Nigerian Government for speaking out against environmental pollution and human rights abuses associated with Shell Oil's activities in Nigeria. Activists from Cameroon will also be available to speak to journalists.

The Chad-Cameroon pipeline sponsors include Exxon, Shell and Elf, three of the wealthiest corporations in the world. Despite their size, funding for the project hinges on $365 million in loans from the World Bank. The pipeline is the largest construction project in West Africa, and will produce severe, irreversible environmental damage as it cuts through hundreds of miles of rainforest and wildlife areas. Additionally, a wide variety of human rights violations have been associated with the project. In Chad's Doba oil field region, at least 80 people were killed by the government in late 1997. In March 1998, another 100 unarmed civilians were massacred according to Amnesty International.
