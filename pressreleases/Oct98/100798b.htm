Women to Speak Out Against Rape, Other Crimes Committed by U.S. Military Personnel in Asia
 
WASHINGTON - October 6 -

News Advisory:

Women from the Philippines, Korea, Japan (Okinawa) and all over the United States will come to Washington, D.C., on Oct. 8 to speak out against rape, murder, sexual exploitation and other crimes committed by U.S. military personnel in Asia and the severe negative effects of military budget priorities within the United States.

A press conference will be held on Friday, Oct. 9 at 10 a.m.at National Press Club, 14th and F streets, N.W., Washington,D.C.

--"There has been no government help for the many women who used to work in bars and clubs near the bases in the Philippines or for their Amerasian children." Aida F. Santos, Women's Education Development Productivity and Research Organization (WEDPRO), Philippines

-- "The U.S. Army displays a certain arrogance in claiming that it came here to protect Korea and yet disrespects Korean citizens." Yu Jin Jeong, the National Campaign for the Eradication of Crime by U.S. Troops in Korea, Seoul, South Korea.

-- "Prostitution and rape are the military system's outlets for pent up aggression and methods of maintaining control and discipline --the target being local community women." Suzuyo Takazato, Okinawan Women Act Against Military Violence,Okinawa, Japan

-- "It's not just Asia...the military harms plenty of people in this country. Social programs in health and education, programs which especially benefit women and children, have been cut while the military budget remains sacrosanct....For many young African Americans there are three options: the military, the drug trade or incarceration -- what kind of choice is that?" Margo Okazawa-Rey, professor of social work, San Francisco State University

--- These four women and other members of the East Asia/U.S. Women's Network Against U.S. Militarism will meet in Washington Oct. 8-13. On the agenda will be a Congressional briefing, meetings at the State Department and Department of Justice, briefings and brown bag lunches at various organizations and universities in Washington, as well as two days of closed working meetings for participants. --- Congressional briefing sponsored by Rep. John Conyers (Mich.) and Barbara Lee (Calif.) of the Progressive Caucus: Tuesday, Oct. 13, 2p.m., Rayburn Building, Room 2222
