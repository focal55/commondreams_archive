Planned Parenthood Announces Unprecedented Effort to Change the Face of Congress; Congress is Out of Step with America
 
WASHINGTON - October 6 - The Planned Parenthood Action Fund (PPAF) today announced its first-ever endorsement of congressional candidates, its first PPAF Congressional Scorecard, and details of its first federal PAC activities. In an effort to change the face of Congress, the Planned Parenthood Action Fund and Federal PAC will spend more than $1 million to identify and educate voters, and contribute to campaigns of pro-choice and pro-family planning candidates.

"It’s time for a change," said Gloria Feldt, president of the PPAF at a press conference today. "Congress has voted 84 out of nearly 100 times in the last three years to eliminate or restrict family planning and reproductive choice. Let’s put a stop to political battles over abortion and focus on the real solution to unintended pregnancies: prevention. And that means making family planning available and affordable to all women and men."

Planned Parenthood has endorsed 152 U.S. House and Senate candidates.* All endorsed incumbents have 100 percent pro-choice voting records and all endorsed challengers have a 100 percent pro-choice score on the Planned Parenthood candidate questionnaire. Some 118 of the endorsed candidates will receive PAC contributions ranging from $1,000 to the maximum allowed - $10,000 - for the primary and general elections. This is the first election cycle that Planned Parenthood has activated its federal PAC; the PAC is expected to raise a half-million dollars before Election Day.

The PPAF Congressional Scorecard* evaluates the position of each member of Congress based on 10 key votes and gives a score from zero to 100 percent. The Scorecard has been mailed to 150,000 Planned Parenthood Action Fund members and will be used in targeted campaigns nationwide.

The PPAF has targeted key swing House districts for independent expenditure campaigns. While the target list is confidential, each campaign includes voter identification, direct mail, including Planned Parenthood’s new voter guide, and phone contacts to motivate voters. The overall budget for the independent expenditure campaigns is a half-million dollars.

"Congress is simply out of step with American voters – and Jon Fox (R, PA-13), who has received a 92% score from the Christian Coalition, is a prime example," said Feldt. "Americans, including voters in the 13th District, deserve to know the facts about who they elect to represent them, and Joe Hoeffel is 100 percent pro-choice and pro-family planning."

The following polling data demonstrates the overwhelming support for family planning in our country:

  • Over 70 percent of Americans favor more funding for family planning, but Congress won’t adequately fund it.
  • Two out of three Americans support confidential teen access to family planning, but Congress tries to block it.
  • Over 60 percent of Americans favor requiring health plans to cover contraceptive methods, but Congress doesn’t act.
  • 82 percent of Americans want sex education in schools, but Congress spends millions of tax dollars on one-sided programs that teach only abstinence.
  • Two out of three Americans believe abortion should remain accessible to women, but Congress tries relentlessly to block access.

"It isn’t the role of the government to turn personal, medical matters into political issues.

But the congressional leadership has made the voting booth as essential as a family planning clinic if we want to ensure access to reproductive health care," said Feldt. "Every day, Planned Parenthood helps people make responsible choices about their reproductive health. We want to support candidates who understand each individual’s right to make those responsible choices."
