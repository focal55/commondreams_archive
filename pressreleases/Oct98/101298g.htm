Images of Africa: Stereotypes and Realities:
October 22, 23, 23, 1998
 
UPPER MONTCLAIR, NJ - October 12 - 142 speakers from Africa, Europe, the USA and Canada are expected at this international conference which will take place on the campus of Montclair State University, Upper Montclair, New Jersey.  Over the course of three days and from the perspectives of such widely different fields as religion, literature, sociology, medical history, classics, anthropology, etc. they will re-examine and deconstruct many of the stereotypes that have traditionally haunted and distorted western perceptions of Africa, while providing ways of understanding African culture in a more fruitful and realistic way.

As one of its main highlights, the conference will feature a keynote banquet address by Martin Bernal, internationally renowned author of the controversial book Black Athena who will speak on the "European Images of Africa: A Tale of Two Names, Ethiop and N-------."  Bernal, professor of Government at Cornell University, rose to fame beyond academic circles on the publication of the first volume of his provocatively
named book.  In it he suggested that the traditional historical view of ancient Greece as the fount of "civilization" is in fact a fabrication of modern western scholarship arising from racial prejudices that have not credited ancient Egypt, and, by extension, Africa as a whole, with their rightful due: the ancient Greeks knew that they had borrowed much of their culture from their southern neighbors ... how come western historians have since forgotten this?

CONFERENCE PROGRAM HIGHLIGHTS

THURSDAY, OCTOBER 22
The conference begins in the morning of Thursday, October 22, with an opening ceremony and a plenary session which will feature Congressman Donald Payne of New Jersey, and 6 scholars who will be given the task of "Assessing Africa's Third Millennium: Political, Cultural and Socio-Economic Prospects".
This will be followed in the afternoon (1:00-5:00) by five concurrent sessions dealing with various Africa-related themes.
FRIDAY, OCTOBER 23
Friday, the main day of the conference, will feature seven concurrent sessions on various themes. The sessions will run from 9:00am to 5:00pm.
Martin Bernal's keynote address will take place on this day at 6:15pm during the banquet offered for the occasion.
SATURDAY, OCTOBER 24
Five concurrent sessions running from 9:00am to 4:00pm will be featured on this last day of the conference. 
The sessions will be followed at 7:00pm by an African Dance performance by the UMOJA DANCE COMPANY.
