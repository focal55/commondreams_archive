Conservationists to Unveil 1998 'Dirty Dozen' Targets
 
WASHINGTON - June 22 - News Advisory:

The League of Conservation Voters (LCV) Action Fund will kick off its 1998 election season by announcing the first six targets of its "Dirty Dozen" campaigns on Tuesday, June 23, at 10 a.m. The "Dirty Dozen" targets are among the most vulnerable anti-environment candidates running for Congress and are pegged for defeat through LCV Action Fund's independent voter information campaigns.

LCV Action Fund debuted the "Dirty Dozen" campaigns in 1996, successfully providing the margin of difference to defeat seven of its 12 anti-environment targets. The "Dirty Dozen" helped defeat such candidates as former Sen. Larry Pressler of South Dakota, the only incumbent senator to lose his seat in 1996.

Deb Callahan, LCV president, and Mike Hayden, LCV board chairman and former Republican governor of Kansas, will announce the first set of Democratic and Republican "Dirty Dozen" targets and provide insight into the political power of the environment as an issue in the 1998 elections. "Dirty Dozen" campaign consultants also will be available to answer journalists' questions.

Who: Deb Callahan, LCV president

When: Tuesday, June 23, 10 a.m.

Where: National Press Club
529 14th St., N.W.

Washington, D.C.
(Continental breakfast will be served)
------
The League of Conservation Voters is the non-profit bipartisan political voice for the environment. LCV is the only national environmental or conservation organization dedicated full-time to informing voters about the environmental records of members of Congress. For each Congress, LCV publishes the National Environmental Scorecard, which rates how each U.S. senator and representative votes on key environmental issues. The League of Conservation Voters Action Fund is LCV's political action committee.
