Senator Kent Conrad, William Gates, Sr., Release Survey on Estate Tax Repeal
Americans Support Reforming, Not Repealing Estate Tax
 

WASHINGTON - June 10 -

WHAT:
William Gates, Sr., Senator Kent Conrad (D-ND), and Americans for a Fair Estate Tax will release a new poll conducted by Greenberg, Quinlan & Rosner on American¹s perceptions of estate tax repeal.

Survey shows support for estate tax repeal lags far behind other tax reform proposals, saving Social Security and Medicare, and creating a prescription drug benefit for seniors. Most voters support reforming, not repealing the estate tax.

WHEN:
WEDNESDAY, JUNE 12, 9:30 A.M.

WHERE:
608 Dirksen Senate Office Building, United States Capitol

TO LISTEN TO THE PRESS CONFERENCE REMOTELY,
CALL 1-800-451-7724 AND ASK FOR PASS CODE V871.

WHO:
** Senator Kent Conrad (D-ND)

** Americans for a Fair Estate Tax (AFET):
AFET is a broad-based non-partisan coalition of nonprofit groups advocating for estate tax reform that would ensure that family farms and small businesses are not unfairly taxed while keeping 98 percent of taxpayers exempt.

** William H. Gates, Sr., Responsible Wealth:
William H. Gates Sr. is chairman and CEO of the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, the largest foundation in the world. Mr. Gates has served as trustee, officer and volunteer for more than two dozen Northwest organizations and is presently a member of the board of directors of the United Way of America.

WHY:
While serving the interest of just 6,000 Americans, permanent estate tax repeal would weaken America¹s commitment to protect Social Security, pass Medicare prescription drug relief, and improve the educational system for our children. In the next few days, the U.S. Senate will be faced with an important choice. It can pass harmful legislation that will shift tax burdens from the 2% of the super-wealthy to the 98% of Americans not now paying the estate tax, reduce federal revenue needed for key community-based programs, and decrease charitable giving all to help the wealthiest Americans or it can make the commitment to serve the interest of the American people.
