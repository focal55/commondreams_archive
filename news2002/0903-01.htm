Ben of Ben and Jerry's to Ride Through NYC in TrueMajority Parade Featuring a 12-Foot 'Pentagon' Pig, Sept. 4
 
WASHINGTON - September 3 - As the Sept. 11 anniversary approaches, Ben Cohen's TrueMajority campaign aims to unite citizens who believe America's response to Sept. 11 has been inadequate.

In perhaps the ultimate example of taking a political message to the streets, Ben Cohen of Ben and Jerry's will ride a giant, grunting, and eye-popping pig around New York Sept. 4. Ben and the parade vehicles will be at New York's Union Square, 14th and Broadway, Sept. 4. from 11 a.m. to 2 p.m.

The purpose of the parade is to unite and activate Americans who believe that in the wake of 9-11, our nation must become more compassionate, fair, and just. The vehicles in the parade include:

Rolling Piggies: Depicting Washington's mixed-up budget priorities, a 12-foot rolling piggy bank represents the Pentagon budget. It pulls two small piggy banks -- proportionately representing America's budget for "Education" and "World Hunger."

Tree and Double-Decker School Bus: Another parade vehicle sports a tree on its roof that can spring to life to emphasize the need for environmental sustainability. A double-decker school bus calls for doubling federal education spending. A trailing RV and other vehicles provides detailed information about inflated Pentagon spending and other mixed-up federal budget priorities.

The parade is sponsored by the TrueMajority, a free e-service that monitors Congress. Twice a month, at critical moments, TrueMajority members receive a short e-mail alert about an important issue pending in Washington. With the click of a mouse, members can send a fax to their Congressperson.

"Our government's response to 9/11 is inconsistent with the values of the majority of American citizens," says Ben Cohen, co-founder of Ben and Jerry's and President of the TrueMajority. "We want give a voice to millions of Americans who agree with us."

A reception for Ben and parade designer Stefan Sagmeister is scheduled for Wed. September 4, 6:30 to 8:30 p.m. at All Souls Church, 80th Street and Lexington Avenue. For information, call 212-243-3416.
