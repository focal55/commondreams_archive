Statement of Staff Attorney Rebecca Stanfield on Passage of the Clean Power Act by the Senate Environment and Public Works Committee
 
WASHINGTON - June 27 - U.S. PIRG applauds Senator Jeffords and Senator Lieberman for their leadership in advancing the Clean Power Act. We urge the full Senate to take up this critical bill as expeditiously as possible. Each year that the Congress fails to take action to clean up power plant pollution means another year of smog and soot, acid rain, mercury poisoning and global warming that we cannot afford.

The significance of this vote cannot be overstated. Today the Administration's approach to power plant pollution - burying our heads in the sand regarding global warming -- was rejected. A majority of EPW Senators insisted that carbon dioxide must be addressed as part of any comprehensive power plant clean-up measure. We hope that at this juncture, the Administration will abandon its obstinate refusal to engage in a discussion on the details of a policy that includes mandatory carbon dioxide emission reductions for the power sector.

It is worth restating the reasons that this legislation is so critical:
* More than 18,000 premature deaths caused by fine particle soot emitted by power plants would be prevented each year;
* Hundreds of thousands of asthma attacks triggered by smog-forming power plant emissions would be prevented each year;
* Forests and lakes from Maine to Georgia would be able to recover from the impacts of acid rain;
* Contamination of fish throughout the nation with the dangerous neurotoxin mercury would be dramatically reduced;
* The largest source of global warming pollution in the nation would begin to chart a course toward the emission reductions necessary to prevent devastating climate changes.

Today marks an important milestone in a battle for public health, the environment, and the future of our planet. Now it is time to begin the work to take the next step in the full Senate.

U.S. PIRG is the national lobby office of the state Public Interest Research Groups, which are nonprofit, nonpartisan public interest advocacy organizations active across the country.
