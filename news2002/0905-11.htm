From Rio to Johannesburg: A Trail of Broken Promises
Activists want to know why governments failed to make renewable energy development a priority
 
RIO DE JANEIRO - September 5 - Greenpeace activists hung the banner "RIO+10 = 2nd CHANCE?" across the arms of the Christ the Redeemer (on Corcovado) in Rio de Janeiro today to protest against world leaders’ failure to secure the future of the planet at the Earth Summit in Johannesburg that ended yesterday, September 4, 2002.

"The outcome of the energy negotiations epitomizes the failure of world governments to ensure a clean and sustainable future for the generations to come. Government leaders did nothing to ensure that two billion people currently living without electricity will soon have access to clean, renewable energy," said Marcelo Furtado, of Greenpeace.

The proposal set forth in Rio, ten years ago, which called for a target of 10 percent renewables by 2010, was blocked by the United States, Japan and the member countries of the Organization of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (OPEC). Instead, the final text coming out of the WSSD in Johannesburg, contains no targets or timetables for the implementation of clean energy which is instrumental in protecting the climate. Additionally, the text promotes large-scale hydro projects and transference of fossil fuel technologies to developing countries, and does not exclude the possibility of nuclear energy projects.

"Humanity cannot remain silent after the failure of Rio+10. We should all question the governments that killed Brazil’s proposal as well as the targets and timelines for implementation of clean, renewable energy. Powerful oil exporters and consumers are frying our future and the oil industry is behind it, using greenwashing to hide its dirty and extremely dangerous games," said Frank Guggenheim, Executive Director of Greenpeace Brazil. "What’s worse, governments are letting them get away with it and globally subsidize the fossil fuel and nuclear industries to the tune of 250-300 billion U.S. dollars per year 2."

As part of today’s protests, Greenpeace activists will deliver the "mortal=remains" of the Johannesburg Earth Summit to those responsible for the failure of the Earth Summit: the world's multinational fossil fuel corporations. Ironically, they are all gathered at the World Petroleum Congress in Rio, the host city to the first Earth Summit.

Greenpeace will continue to press world governments to create the necessary conditions to implement clean and sustainable energy and to guarantee a safe future, free from environmental devastation.
