Brady Campaign and Million Mom March Honor Our Nation's Law Enforcement Officers During National Police Week
 
WASHINGTON - May 13 - The 11th Annual National Police Week will begin Monday, May 13 at 8 p.m. with a candlelight vigil at The National Law Enforcement Officers Memorial in Washington, D.C. The week-long tribute to America's federal, state and local police officers, honors those who died in the line of duty and those who continue to protect and serve everyday at great personal risk. The Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence united with The Million Mom March (MMM) is proud to announce its support for this event and we encourage a nation to come together in honor of our fallen officers.

"No one knows the danger of gun violence better than America's law enforcement," said James Brady, former Press Secretary to President Ronald Reagan, gun violence victim and survivor. "I urge all Americans to stop and share a moment of silence in remembrance of these fallen hero's who have unselfishly given the ultimate sacrifice in their quest for public safety."

"Each year our nation recognizes those men and women who have given the ultimate sacrifice of their lives so that each of us can live safe in our communities," said John Shanks, Law Enforcement Relations spokesman for The Brady Campaign and MMM. "Please join all of us at the Brady Campaign and MMM in honoring those individuals during National Police Week."

According to the National Law Enforcement Officers Memorial Fund (NLEOMF), more than 100 police officers die in the line of duty every year and gun violence is the leading cause of those deaths. The Brady Campaign to Prevent Gun Violence united with The Million Mom March is committed to drastically reducing, and even erasing, this yearly statistic by creating a safer America through common-sense gun laws.

To learn more about National Police Week, call the NLEOMF Information Line at, 202-737- 3400 or log onto: http://www.nleomf.org. For more information about preventing gun violence in America, log onto: http://www.bradycampaign.org or http://www.millionmommarch.org.
