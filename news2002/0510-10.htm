Emergency Mobilization for Palestine; Two Day Event Includes Eyewitnesses from Palestine and 'Lobbying Congress for Peace'
 
WASHINGTON - May 10 - As Palestinians attempt to rebuild their nation, Arab Americans, civil rights activists and individuals concerned about peace and Palestinian rights, will convene in Washington for an emergency mobilization on May 13 and 14. The two-day event will focus on the crisis confronting Palestinians and what Americans who support peace can do to work for a balanced U.S. policy.

On Monday, May 13, participants will hear from Nabil Shaath, Palestinian Authority Minister of Planning and International Cooperation, Michael Tarazi and Diana Buttu, legal advisors in the Palestine Liberation Organization's (PLO) Negotiations Affairs Department, Adam Shapiro and Huwaida Arraf, founders of the International Solidarity Movement (ISM), and renowned pollster John Zogby. They will also hear from local community leaders around the U.S. who are working to raise awareness of Palestinian rights.

Tuesday, May 14, the group will focus its attention on "Lobbying Congress for Peace." On the heels of Congress'passage of one-sided, non-binding resolutions supporting Israel, activists will be briefed by Reps. Nick Rahall (D-W.Va.) and Darrel Issa (R-Calif.) on congressional efforts regarding the Mideast. They will also hear from policy experts. Following these briefings, constituents will meet with their respective members.
