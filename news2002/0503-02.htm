CRP Launches CapitalEye.org
 
WASHINGTON - May 3 - The non-partisan Center for Responsive Politics announces a new Web site dedicated to news and analysis of money-in-politics issues - www.capitaleye.org. For nine years, the Center's Capital Eye newsletter has educated its readers about the role of money in our political system. Now Capital Eye is on the Web and will be updated regularly with original news stories that closely examine fundraising by politicians and parties and the special interests that are funding U.S. elections.

Stories posted today include:

"Wartime Profiteering" - A look at the special interests that top the list of post-Sept. 11 soft money donors.

"Mission Impossible" - An analysis of Nevada's costly battle against deep-pocketed energy interests over nuclear waste storage in Yucca Mountain.

"Charitable Intent?" - A report on how lawmakers rushed to return contributions from Enron, but seemingly have far fewer concerns about keeping money from Arthur Andersen.

Other features on CapitalEye.org include "Press Clips," a look at the money-in-politics angle of some of the week's top news stories. Future editions of Capital Eye will include analyses of campaign fundraising at the state level, exclusive money-in-politics interviews with key political figures, and more.

http://www.capitaleye.org/


OPENSECRETS.ORG NOMINATED FOR A WEBBY

The Center's award-winning Web site, OpenSecrets.org, has been nominated for a prestigious Webby Award in the category of Best Politics site. As the winner of last year's award, OpenSecrets.org will defend its crown at the Sixth Annual Webby Awards ceremony, June 18 at San Francisco's Legion of Honor.

Called the "Oscars of the Internet," the Webby Awards are the leading international honors for Web sites and individual achievement in creativity and technology. The awards are determined by the International Academy of Digital Arts & Sciences.

As a nominee for a Webby Award, OpenSecrets.org is also eligible for a People's Voice Award. Show your support of this invaluable resource! Vote now at the link below through June 7.

http://www.webbyawards.com/peoplesvoice/
