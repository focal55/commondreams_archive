Israeli Government Refuses to Release Illegally Detained U.S. Citizens
 
BETHLEHEM - May 15 - Five Americans--four men and one woman--were illegally detained by the Israeli authorities on May 2nd and May 9, respectively. They are being held in Ramle prison-- a prison notorious for its human rights abuses, especially those against women and children. The Americans have not been charged with any crime. Their detention comes at the height of International humanitarian efforts to deliver food and water to besieged Palestinian civilians. To protest their illegal detention, the Americans have been on hunger strike for over a week (one man for two weeks), and have been refusing water for most, if not all, of the last 6 days. The US embassy has consistently refused to help these U.S. citizens and has attempted to force their parents to pay their deportation costs, despite the fact that each of them already has a return ticket.

The men were all arrested whilst trying to take humanitarian aid to besieged Palestinian civilians in the Church of the Nativity in Bethlehem (now released);

the American woman, Kristen Schurr, was one of the 10 humanitarian workers who brought food and water into the Church of Nativity-she was detained when the standoff ended. She reports that she is being beaten and repeatedly strip searched;

they were arrested by the Israelis in Bethlehem, which is in 'area A,' within which Israel has no legal jurisdiction. Access to legal counsel has been repeatedly denied to all five;

all five Americans have agreed to leave the country, yet the Israeli government continues to detain them, and now wishes to interrogate Nathan Musselman.

The five Americans are: Kristen Schurr of New York, NY and Seattle, WA; Trevor Baumgartner of Seattle, WA and Berkeley, CA; Nathan Mauger of Spokane, WA; Thomas Koutsoukos of Lakeforest, IL; and Nathan Musselman of Roanoke, VA.

Two of the American hostages, Trevor Baumgartner and Thomas Koutsoukos, released a statement from Ramle prison this morning:

"We remain in prison because the Ministry of Interior, after making an agreement then breaking it, has been seeking to manipulate our parents and ourselves in to purchasing new flight tickets, rather than letting us use our own as our fellow internationals have, or purchasing them themselves, as is their legal obligation. Toward this end, Nathan Mauger and Nathan Musselman have been separated from us and each other, despite repeated assurances that this would not happen. This morning we were informed that when we leave, Nathan Musselman is to remain behind for interrogation by Shabak (Israeli internal security). We find this wholly unacceptable."
