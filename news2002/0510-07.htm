American Family Voices Recognizes Anniversary of Denial of Access to Energy Task Force Records
 
WASHINGTON - May 10 - American Family Voices (AFV) today recognized the one-year anniversary of Congress' request and the Bush Administration's denial of access to White House Energy Task Force records. Congressional investigators are seeking answers to whether the White House allowed industry to shape energy policy and whether steps could have been taken to prevent the collapse of Enron.

On May 10, 2001, the Comptroller General ordered Vice President Cheney to provide the General Accounting Office (GAO) with "full and complete access" to records of meetings between the Bush administration and private-sector members of its Energy Task Force. Following the collapse of Enron, the White House opted to protect their corporate supporters by hiding behind legal-procedure and only releasing selective documents.

"The American people and those hurt by Enron's collapse are looking for answers, and the only response from the White House is selective disclosure and more lawsuits," said Mike Lux, president of American Family Voices. "The Vice President is clearly attempting to bury his relationship with Enron and big business in legal procedure."

The GAO cites a number of federal laws which specify that federal energy policy should be developed in a manner that takes into account a broad and diverse spectrum of publicly-solicited views. To date, the White House has provided only a select few edited documents to Congress, adding to speculation that they allowed corporate interests to shape energy policy.

"Only after they believed it to be politically advantageous and legally required, did the White House feel the need to provide just enough information about the energy task force meetings to the court system to deflect the concerns of a handful of environmental lobbies, Lux added. "Their selective disclosure flies in the face of the thousands of Americans adversely affected following the collapse of Enron. Vice President Cheney must come clean on what he is trying to hide."

American Family Voices (AFV) is a 501(c)4 non-profit, issue advocacy organization created to focus attention on the economic and family issues most important to the quality of middle class working families' lives. AFV members include advocates for consumer, healthcare, education, children, seniors, civil rights and labor issues.
