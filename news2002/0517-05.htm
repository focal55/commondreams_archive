Bush Administration Decides to Leave Wild Areas of Alaska's Tongass Rainforest Open to Clearcut Logging
Statement of Carl Pope, Executive Director of Sierra Club
 
WASHINGTON - May 17 - "Despite overwhelming public support for protecting the last wild areas of Alaska's Tongass National Forest, the Forest Service has instead chosen a timber industry-favored plan for the Tongass. The Bush Administration's plan does not recommend protecting a single acre of wild forest land as Wilderness.

"The Tongass -- home to grizzly bears, salmon and wolves -- is the largest remaining temperate rainforest on Earth. But 700 square miles of the Tongass have already been clearcut, and logging continues to threaten the remaining wildlands and wildlife. With this decision, the Bush Administration has given the green light to the timber industry to forge ahead with destructive logging projects in this pristine rainforest. Right now, the Forest Service is planning 33 large-scale, industrial timber sales in roadless areas of the Tongass National Forest.

"Today's decision was made in spite of the overwhelming support of the American people who want our last wild forests protected for future generations. Now is the time for the American people to weigh in again to call for a plan that protects millions of acres of Alaska's last remaining rainforests."
