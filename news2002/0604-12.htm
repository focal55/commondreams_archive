Israeli-Palestinian Bereaved Families Forum to Hold Briefing for Congress & Media
 
WASHINGTON - June 4 - The Israeli-Palestinian Bereaved Families Forum for Peace will hold a briefing on Capitol Hill on Thursday, June 6, 9:30AM-11:30 AM in 441 Cannon House Office Building.

The briefing, entitled "Beyond Mourning: Toward a Peaceful Solution to the Israeli-Palestinian Conflict," will feature Mr. Yitzhak Frankenthal and Mr. Ibrahim Bushnaq. Mr. Frankenthal founded the Bereaved Families Forum in 1994 following the abduction and murder of his 19 year-old son Arik by Hamas terrorists. Mr. Bushnaq, who lost a nephew to the conflict, is the coordinator of the group’s Palestinian bereaved families.

The Bereaved Families Forum is composed of the Parents’ Circle, an organization of more than 200 Israeli families whose children have died in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict and who have joined together in a unique partnership to advance peace, tolerance and democracy. The Parents' Circle promotes peace and coexistence through education for tolerance and compromise. By promoting the Jewish moral obligation to preserve and value human life above all else, the Parents' Circle is at the forefront of explaining the human toll paid because of the absence of peace.

In 1995, The Parents’ Circle established a partnership with Palestinian bereaved families in Gaza and established the Israeli – Palestinian Bereaved Families Forum for Peace. In addition, the Bereaved Families Forum works with groups of bereaved families all over the West Bank.

The work of the Bereaved Families Forum is predicated upon a compelling message: "If we, who have lost our children, the most precious thing of all, can sit down with our enemies to discuss a way to end this bloody conflict, then our leaders must be able to as well." Toward that end, the forum has met numerous times with presidents and prime ministers, ministers and diplomats to pressure leaders to make peace. They have also carried out the only joint media campaign for peace to take place in Israel and the Palestinian Authority simultaneously. The main activity of the Families Forum is to publicly campaign for a peace agreement that will resolve the conflict and open a new chapter in Israeli-Palestinian relations.
